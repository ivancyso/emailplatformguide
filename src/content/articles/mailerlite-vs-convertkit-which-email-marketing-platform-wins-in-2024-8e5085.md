# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate 2024 Showdown for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business or content creation efforts. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams. Both are popular for their user-friendly interfaces and powerful features, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, core features, automation capabilities, segmentation, analytics, integrations, and support. We'll provide practical advice to help you decide based on your subscriber count, budget, and goals.

Whether you're starting with a small list or scaling up, testing both via their free plans or trials is the best first step. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the deciding factor. Both platforms offer transparent, subscriber-based pricing without hidden fees for most features.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite stands out for affordability, especially for beginners:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes basic automation, forms, and landing pages.
- **Growing Business Plan**: Starts at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 1,000-2,500, and higher tiers up to enterprise levels.
- **Advanced Plan**: $21/month base, adding A/B testing, web push notifications, and priority support.

Practical tip: If your list is under 1,000, stick with the free plan indefinitely while testing campaigns. Upgrade only when you hit limits.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit targets creators with a focus on monetization:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 10,000 subscribers (recently expanded), but limited to 30-day broadcasts only—no ongoing sequences.
- **Creator Plan**: $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, $29 for 500-1,000, scaling to $2,900+ for 500k+.
- **Creator Pro Plan**: Adds advanced reporting, subscriber scoring, and one-click upsells from $25/month base.

ConvertKit's free tier is generous for list-building but pushes upgrades for full automation. Annual billing saves 20% on both.

**Winner on Price**: MailerLite for small lists under 1,000 due to its robust free plan. ConvertKit edges out for larger creator lists with commerce tools.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop vs Creator Workflow

### MailerLite's Intuitive Editor
MailerLite's drag-and-drop builder is beginner-friendly. Add blocks for images, text, buttons—no coding needed. Mobile-responsive templates (50+) are customizable in minutes. Practical advice: Use their template library to clone and tweak proven designs, saving hours on first campaigns.

### ConvertKit's Visual Canvas
ConvertKit uses a "visual automation" canvas for emails and sequences. It's simple for tagging subscribers but the email editor feels more code-like (though drag-and-drop improved recently). Best for those comfortable with workflows.

**Winner**: MailerLite for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for workflow-savvy users.

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools Head-to-Head

### Email Templates and Design
- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates, photo editor, RSS campaigns. Embed videos easily.
- **ConvertKit**: Simpler templates focused on plain-text authenticity for creators. Strong for newsletters.

Tip: For e-commerce, MailerLite's product blocks integrate better with stores.

### Automation and Sequences
Both excel here, but differently:

#### MailerLite Automations
Trigger-based: Welcome series, abandoned cart, birthday emails. Unlimited steps, time delays, and conditions. Free plan includes basics.

#### ConvertKit Automations
Tag-based sequences shine for creators (e.g., "7-day course" drips). Re-engagement campaigns auto-tag inactive subs.

Practical advice: Map your customer journey first—use MailerLite for simple e-com flows, ConvertKit for content funnels. Test with 100 subs to refine.

### Segmentation and Tagging
- **MailerLite**: Groups, tags, custom fields. Dynamic segments update automatically.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tagging (actions trigger tags). Subscriber scoring in Pro.

**Winner**: ConvertKit for nuanced creator segmentation.

### Landing Pages and Forms
- **MailerLite**: Unlimited popups, forms, landing pages (HTML editor). A/B testing on paid plans.
- **ConvertKit**: Forms, landing pages, checkouts. Native commerce for digital products.

Tip: Use MailerLite popups for lead gen on blogs; ConvertKit for selling courses.

### Analytics and Reporting
Both track opens, clicks, bounces:
- **MailerLite**: Revenue tracking, geography reports, spam tests.
- **ConvertKit**: Revenue per subscriber, link tracking, churn reports (Pro).

Review weekly metrics: Aim for 20-30% open rates. Low? Segment better.

## Integrations and Support

### Integrations
- **MailerLite**: 250+ via Zapier, native WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics.
- **ConvertKit**: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable, podcast hosts. Zapier too.

Practical: If you're on WordPress, both plugins work seamlessly.

### Customer Support
- **MailerLite**: Email/chat (paid priority), extensive help center, Facebook group.
- **ConvertKit**: Email/ticket, creator-focused resources, webinars.

Both score high (4.5+ on G2/Capterra). Start with docs before support.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
Small businesses, e-commerce stores, bloggers under 5k subs needing affordability and design. Migrate if you outgrow free—export/import is CSV-easy.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Content creators, podcasters, course sellers prioritizing sequences and sales. Ideal for 1k+ subs with digital products.

Practical migration advice: Export from current tool as CSV, import subscribers (match fields), recreate top automations first. Run A/B tests on 10% of list post-switch.

## Conclusion
MailerLite vs ConvertKit? No one-size-fits-all. MailerLite wins for budget-conscious beginners with visual appeal (start free today). ConvertKit dominates for creators scaling revenue through tags and commerce.

Assess your needs: List size? E-com or content? Test both—sign up, send a campaign, review analytics after 1k sends. Whichever boosts opens/revenue by 10%+ is your winner. For most under 2k subs, MailerLite's value shines. Questions? Check their sites for latest pricing.
