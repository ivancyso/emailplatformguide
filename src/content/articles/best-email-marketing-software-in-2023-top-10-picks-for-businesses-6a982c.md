# Best Email Marketing Software in 2023: Top 10 Picks for Businesses

Choosing the right email marketing software is crucial for businesses looking to nurture leads, boost engagement, and drive sales. With countless options available, it can be overwhelming to pick the best one. This roundup focuses on the top 10 email marketing platforms based on real user feedback, core features, scalability, and value for money. We'll cover key criteria to evaluate them, detailed breakdowns of each tool, and practical tips to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store owner, or marketing team lead, these recommendations cater to various needs and budgets. All data is drawn from official sites and established reviews as of late 2023—no hype, just facts.

## What Makes the Best Email Marketing Software?

Before diving into the list, understand the essentials. The best platforms excel in deliverability, ease of use, automation, analytics, and integrations.

### Key Features to Prioritize

- **Drag-and-Drop Editors**: Intuitive builders for non-designers, like Mailchimp's template library.
- **Automation Workflows**: Trigger emails based on user actions, e.g., welcome series or abandoned cart reminders.
- **Segmentation**: Divide lists by behavior, location, or purchase history for targeted campaigns.
- **Analytics**: Track open rates, clicks, conversions, and revenue per email.
- **Integrations**: Connect with CRM (e.g., Salesforce), e-commerce (Shopify), or analytics (Google Analytics).
- **Deliverability**: High inbox placement rates, often 95%+ with tools like ActiveCampaign.

### Pricing Considerations

Most offer freemium tiers or start at $10-50/month. Factor in contacts/subscribers, as costs scale. Free plans suit beginners (under 1,000 subs), while enterprises need unlimited sends.

### Practical Advice: Testing Platforms

1. Sign up for free trials (most offer 14-30 days).
2. Import a test list and build a sample campaign.
3. Run A/B tests on subject lines (aim for 20-30% open rates).
4. Check mobile responsiveness—60% of emails are opened on phones.
5. Review support: Live chat beats email tickets for quick fixes.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Compared

Here's our curated list, ranked by overall versatility, user ratings (from G2/Capterra), and suitability for growing businesses.

### 1. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign stands out for advanced automation and CRM integration. Ideal for mid-sized businesses.

**Pros**:
- Powerful machine learning for predictive sending.
- 870+ integrations.
- Deep reporting on ROI.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve.

**Pricing**: Starts at $29/month (1,000 contacts). Free trial.

**Best For**: E-commerce with complex funnels. Practical tip: Use site tracking to automate based on page visits.

### 2. Mailchimp

The most popular choice for beginners, with a massive template library.

**Pros**:
- Free plan up to 500 contacts/1,000 sends/month.
- Excellent audience insights.
- Pop-up forms and landing pages included.

**Cons**:
- Automation limited on free tier.

**Pricing**: Free to $299+/month.

**Best For**: Startups. Tip: Leverage behavioral targeting for 25% higher engagement.

### 3. Klaviyo

E-commerce powerhouse, especially for Shopify users.

**Pros**:
- Revenue tracking per flow.
- Advanced segmentation (e.g., VIP customers).
- SMS integration.

**Cons**:
- Geared toward stores, less for B2B.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts; $20+/month.

**Best For**: Online retailers. Tip: Set up abandoned cart flows—recover 10-15% of lost sales.

### 4. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Creator-focused with simple automations.

**Pros**:
- Visual automation builder.
- Tagging for sequences.
- One-click upsells.

**Cons**:
- Basic analytics.

**Pricing**: Free to $29/month.

**Best For**: Bloggers/influencers. Tip: Use broadcast emails for time-sensitive offers.

### 5. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels.

**Pros**:
- Conversion funnel builder.
- AI email generator.
- High deliverability.

**Cons**:
- Interface feels dated.

**Pricing**: $19/month (1,000 contacts).

**Best For**: Agencies. Tip: A/B test landing pages integrated with emails.

### 6. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Affordable transactional + marketing emails.

**Pros**:
- Unlimited contacts on paid plans.
- Chat/SMS add-ons.
- EU GDPR compliant.

**Cons**:
- Fewer templates.

**Pricing**: Free; $25/month.

**Best For**: SMBs. Tip: Automate transactional emails like order confirmations.

### 7. Constant Contact

User-friendly for non-profits/small biz.

**Pros**:
- Event management.
- Social media tools.
- 60-day free trial.

**Cons**:
- Pricey for large lists.

**Pricing**: $12/month.

**Best For**: Local businesses. Tip: Use list-building tools at events.

### 8. Drip

Behavioral automation for e-com.

**Pros**:
- Workflow timelines.
- Customer lifetime value tracking.

**Cons**:
- No free plan.

**Pricing**: $39/month.

**Best For**: High-volume stores.

### 9. AWeber

Reliable veteran with strong support.

**Pros**:
- Spam testing.
- RSS-to-email.

**Cons**:
- Dated design.

**Pricing**: $19/month.

**Best For**: Long-term users.

### 10. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free tier within CRM ecosystem.

**Pros**:
- Seamless CRM sync.
- Personalization tokens.

**Cons**:
- Upsell pressure.

**Pricing**: Free; $20/month for pro.

**Best For**: Inbound marketers.

## How to Choose and Implement the Best Email Marketing Software

1. **Assess Needs**: Solo? Mailchimp. E-com? Klaviyo.
2. **Migrate Data**: Export CSV from old tools; use built-in importers.
3. **Compliance**: Follow CAN-SPAM/GDPR—double opt-in mandatory.
4. **Optimize Campaigns**: Subject lines under 50 chars; 20-40% image-text ratio.
5. **Measure Success**: Aim for 20-30% opens, 2-5% clicks. Use UTM tags.

Practical setup: Start with a welcome sequence (3-5 emails) to boost retention by 30%.
