# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can be tough when you're building your email list and automating marketing. Both are popular for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small businesses, offering free plans and powerful features. This in-depth comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide based on your needs.

We'll cover practical advice for getting started, like testing free trials and focusing on your audience size. No hype—just real insights from their core offerings.

## Quick Overview of MailerLite

MailerLite is an affordable, beginner-friendly platform launched in 2010. It's designed for simplicity with drag-and-drop tools. Key strengths include:

- **Free plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month.
- **Paid plans**: Start at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20 for 1,000, etc.
- **Standout features**: Responsive email editor, automation workflows, popups/forms, landing pages, and basic e-commerce integrations.

Practical tip: If you're under 1,000 subscribers, start free. Use their popup builder to grow your list organically on your website.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

| Feature/Plan | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|--------------|------------|------------|
| Free Tier | 1,000 subs, 12k emails/mo | 1,000 subs, unlimited emails (time-limited sends) |
| Entry Paid | $10/mo (500 subs) | $15/mo (up to 1k, but scales) |
| Mid-Tier | $20/mo (1k subs) | $29/mo (1k-3k subs) |

MailerLite wins on raw affordability for small lists—perfect for bootstrappers. ConvertKit's pricing jumps faster but includes creator-specific tools like subscriptions.

**Advice**: Calculate based on your list size. For 500 subs sending weekly, MailerLite saves $5-10/mo. Both offer forever-free tiers, so test without commitment.

## Feature-by-Feature Breakdown

### Email Editor and Templates

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 80+ responsive templates. No coding needed; preview on mobile instantly.

ConvertKit's editor is block-based, flexible for custom designs. Fewer templates (around 50), but excels in personalization via tags.

**Winner**: Tie—MailerLite for speed, ConvertKit for customization. Tip: Import your logo and branding early for consistent newsletters.

### Automations and Workflows

MailerLite offers simple if/then automations, A/B testing, and RSS-to-email. Great for basic sequences like abandoned carts.

ConvertKit shines with visual builders for complex sequences (e.g., tag-based drips). Supports subscriber scoring.

**Practical advice**: Start with a 5-email welcome series. ConvertKit if you segment by behavior; MailerLite for set-it-and-forget-it simplicity.

### Landing Pages and Forms

Both have builders:
- MailerLite: Unlimited popups, forms, and sites (paid plans). High customization.
- ConvertKit: Forms, landing pages, and checkouts integrated seamlessly.

Tip: Embed forms on your blog. Track conversions with UTM tags for Google Analytics.

### Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite: Open/click rates, bounce handling, revenue tracking.
ConvertKit: Detailed subscriber activity, tag performance, A/B insights.

**Advice**: Monitor unsubscribe rates (<0.5% ideal). Use data to refine subject lines—test 2-3 variations monthly.

### Integrations and E-commerce

MailerLite: 100+ (Stripe, WooCommerce, WordPress).
ConvertKit: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable; native commerce.

For Shopify users, both work, but ConvertKit edges for digital sales.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

MailerLite: 4.9/5 on ease (Capterra). Clean dashboard, 24/7 live chat, email support.

ConvertKit: 4.7/5, steeper learning for tags but excellent academy/resources. Priority email support on paid.

**Tip for beginners**: Watch MailerLite's 5-min tutorials. ConvertKit users: Master tagging to boost engagement 20-30%.

## Pros and Cons

**MailerLite Pros**:
- Cheaper long-term.
- Unlimited images/bands in emails.
- EU-based (GDPR compliant).

**Cons**:
- Less advanced segmentation.
- Fewer creator tools.

**ConvertKit Pros**:
- Powerful automations.
- Creator economy focus.
- Great for courses/newsletters.

**Cons**:
- Higher cost at scale.
- Editor less visual for some.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Small businesses/e-commerce stores under 5k subs.
- Budget-conscious users needing quick setup.
- Example: A local bakery automating promos—simple, cost-effective.

**Get started**: Sign up free, build a popup, send your first campaign in 30 mins.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators, coaches, podcasters.
- Those with digital products needing tags/sequences.
- Example: A blogger selling e-books—automate sales funnels.

**Get started**: Free trial, import contacts, set a sequence.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

**Overall Winner**: MailerLite for most (affordability + ease), ConvertKit for creators (depth).

Practical roadmap:
1. Audit your needs: List size? Product type?
2. Test both free plans for 2 weeks.
3. Export/import lists easily (CSV standard).
4. Track ROI: Aim for 30-40% open rates.

Both platforms deliver 99% deliverability. Whichever you pick, focus on value-driven content. Your audience will thank you.
