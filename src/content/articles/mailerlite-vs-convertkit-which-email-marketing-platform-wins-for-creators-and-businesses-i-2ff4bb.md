# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. In this MailerLite vs ConvertKit showdown, we'll break down key aspects like pricing, features, usability, and support to help you decide which tool suits your needs—whether you're a solopreneur, blogger, or small business owner.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing tool. Both platforms offer transparent plans, but they cater to different scales.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite stands out for affordability, especially for beginners. It includes a **free plan** for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—perfect for testing without commitment. Paid plans start at **$10/month** for 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20 for 1,000, and higher tiers like Advanced at $21/month for 500 subs with extras like A/B testing and webhooks.

No contracts, monthly billing, and annual discounts (up to 24% off). Additional subscribers cost extra, but it's cost-effective for lists under 10,000.

**Practical tip**: Start free to build your list via popups and forms, then upgrade seamlessly as you grow.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) focuses on creators with plans starting at **$15/month** for up to 300 subscribers (Creator plan) or **$29/month** for 500 (Creator Pro). The free plan was discontinued, but a 14-day trial is available. Higher tiers like Expert ($79/month for 1,000 subs) unlock commerce and advanced monetization.

Annual billing saves 20%. It's pricier for larger lists but includes unlimited emails and sends.

**Practical tip**: If your revenue exceeds $50K/year, ConvertKit's Creator Pro unlocks payouts and subscriptions, justifying the cost for serious creators.

**Winner**: MailerLite for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit for revenue-focused pros.

## Ease of Use and Interface

Both platforms prioritize simplicity, but their designs reflect user bases.

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with a clean dashboard resembling Google Workspace. Setup takes minutes: import contacts, design campaigns, and launch automations via blocks.

ConvertKit uses a visual builder with "blocks" for sequences and broadcasts. Its tagging system shines for segmentation without lists—tag subscribers based on behavior (e.g., "opened ebook").

**Practical advice**: Test both trials. MailerLite suits visual designers; ConvertKit excels for automation-heavy workflows like welcome sequences.

## Core Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite offers 80+ responsive templates, a drag-and-drop editor with JSON editing for pros, and photo/video blocks. Previews across devices are seamless.

ConvertKit's builder is block-based, emphasizing plain-text options for higher deliverability. Fewer templates (around 50), but optimized for creators with commerce embeds.

### Automation and Workflows

MailerLite: Trigger-based automations (e.g., abandoned cart), RSS campaigns, and multi-step workflows. Advanced plan adds time-based delays and A/B splits.

ConvertKit: Powerful sequences act as drip campaigns. Rules trigger actions like tagging or selling. Pro plans include subscriber scoring and paid newsletters.

**Tip**: Use MailerLite for simple popups/lead magnets; ConvertKit for evergreen funnels like course launches.

### Segmentation and Analytics

MailerLite segments by groups, clicks, opens, or custom fields. Analytics cover opens (45% avg), clicks (3-5%), bounces, with heatmaps on Advanced.

ConvertKit's tagging enables hyper-personalization (e.g., "podcast listener"). Reports show revenue per subscriber, unsubscribe reasons, and A/B insights.

### Landing Pages and Forms

Both build unlimited pages/forms. MailerLite includes popups, exit-intent, and fixed bars. ConvertKit focuses on opt-in forms tied to sequences, with checkout pages on Pro.

### Integrations

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress, Google Analytics, Stripe.

ConvertKit: Strong with creator tools (Gumroad, Teachable), plus Zapier and native commerce.

**Practical advice**: Integrate MailerLite with e-commerce (WooCommerce); ConvertKit with membership sites (Memberful).

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive academy (videos/tutorials), and Facebook community. Response times under 2 hours.

ConvertKit: Email/ticket support (business hours), knowledge base, creator-focused webinars, and a vibrant forum. Slower for free users.

**Winner**: MailerLite for responsive help.

- Lists under 5,000
- Need for popups/landing pages
- Budget under $20/month

**Real-world example**: A local bakery uses MailerLite's free plan for weekly newsletters and popup signups, growing to 800 subs without costs.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators/bloggers/podcasters
- Revenue-generating newsletters
- Complex automations
- Scaling beyond 1,000 subs

**Real-world example**: A course creator automates sequences post-opt-in, tagging buyers for upsells, hitting $10K/month revenue tracked in-dashboard.

## Migration and Getting Started

Switching? Both offer CSV imports. MailerLite's migration tool is free; ConvertKit provides guides. Always clean your list first to avoid bounces.

**Tip**: Export test segments to compare deliverability—MailerLite edges out at 98-99%, ConvertKit at 97% per user reports.

## Conclusion: Your Best Pick

**MailerLite wins for affordability and ease**, ideal if you're starting or keeping costs low while accessing robust tools like automations and popups.

**ConvertKit triumphs for creators**, with unmatched tagging, sequences, and monetization to turn subscribers into paying fans.

Assess your list size, budget, and goals: under 1,000 subs and simple needs? MailerLite. Creator economy focus? ConvertKit. Both deliver value—sign up for trials today and A/B test campaigns to see real engagement lifts.

Ultimately, the "best" depends on you. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-40%) and conversions post-switch. Happy emailing!
