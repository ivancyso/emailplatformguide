# MailerLite Review 2024: Features, Pricing, Pros & Cons for Beginners

If you're searching for an **email marketing platform** that's easy to use, budget-friendly, and packed with essentials, you've likely landed on **MailerLite**. In this comprehensive **MailerLite review**, we'll dive deep into its features, pricing, strengths, weaknesses, and whether it's the right fit for your business. Targeted at people choosing email marketing platforms, this guide draws from official documentation, user feedback on sites like G2 and Capterra, and hands-on testing insights.

MailerLite stands out for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams who need reliable tools without complexity. Let's break it down.

## What is MailerLite?

MailerLite is a Lithuania-based email marketing service launched in 2010, serving over 2 million users worldwide. It focuses on simplicity and affordability, offering drag-and-drop email builders, automation workflows, landing pages, and popups. Unlike enterprise-heavy platforms like HubSpot, MailerLite prioritizes accessibility for beginners.

Key differentiators include a forever-free plan (up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month), high deliverability rates (around 99% per their reports), and GDPR compliance for EU users. It's not the flashiest, but it gets the job done for growing subscriber lists without breaking the bank.

## Key Features of MailerLite

MailerLite packs a solid feature set for its price point. Here's a practical breakdown:

### Drag-and-Drop Email Editor

The visual editor lets you create responsive emails without coding. Choose from 80+ templates categorized by industry (e.g., e-commerce, newsletters). Practical advice: Start with a template, customize blocks for images/text, and use A/B testing for subject lines. Previews work across devices, ensuring mobile optimization—crucial since 50%+ of emails are opened on phones.

### Automation Workflows

Build if/then sequences like welcome series or abandoned cart reminders. The editor is flowchart-based, making it intuitive. Example: Trigger an email 3 days after signup if no purchase. Integrates with Stripe/WooCommerce for e-commerce automations. Tip: Segment lists by behavior (e.g., opened last campaign) to boost open rates by 20-30%.

### Landing Pages and Popups

Create unlimited landing pages with forms, optimized for conversions. Popups include exit-intent and scroll-triggered options. Advice: A/B test popup copy—"Get 10% off" often outperforms generic signups. Embed forms on websites via JavaScript snippets.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Tag subscribers and segment by custom fields (e.g., location, purchase history). Analytics dashboard shows opens, clicks, bounces, and revenue tracking. Pro tip: Use heatmaps to see click hotspots and refine CTAs.

### Integrations

Connects to 1,000+ apps via Zapier, plus native ones like WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics. For e-commerce users, sync orders automatically.

Other notables: RSS-to-email campaigns, one-click unsubscribes, and image editor.

## MailerLite Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

MailerLite's pricing is subscriber-based, with no daily send limits on paid plans.

- **Free Plan**: 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes core features but watermarked templates and basic support.
- **Growing Business ($10/mo)**: 1,000-2,500 subs, unlimited emails, removes watermarks, adds landing pages/popups.
- **Advanced ($20/mo)**: Adds automation, A/B testing, webhooks.
- **Enterprise**: Custom for 300k+ subs.

Billed annually for 10-20% discounts. Practical advice: Start free to test imports/migrations (supports CSV, integrations). Upgrade when hitting limits—scales linearly without surprise fees. Compared to Mailchimp's higher tiers, it's 50-70% cheaper for similar features.

## Pros and Cons of MailerLite

### Pros
- **Affordable**: Best free tier in the industry.
- **User-Friendly**: 4.6/5 on G2; minimal learning curve.
- **High Deliverability**: Authentication tools (DKIM/SPF) included.
- **Responsive Support**: 24/7 chat/email; quick responses per reviews.
- **No Contracts**: Cancel anytime.

### Cons
- **Limited Advanced Features**: No deep behavioral scoring like ActiveCampaign.
- **Basic Reporting**: Lacks predictive analytics.
- **Template Variety**: Fewer options than ConvertKit.
- **API Limits**: 10k calls/hour on lower plans.

User reviews (Capterra: 4.7/5 from 3k+): Praise ease/pricing; some miss multichannel (SMS absent).

## Who Should Use MailerLite?

Ideal for:
- **Bloggers/Content Creators**: RSS campaigns shine.
- **Small E-com Stores**: Affordable automations.
- **Non-Profits/Starters**: Free plan perfect for testing.

Avoid if you need: CRM depth (try ActiveCampaign) or design-heavy newsletters (Beehiiv better).

Practical setup guide:
1. Sign up free, import list via CSV.
2. Verify domain for better deliverability.
3. Build first campaign: Template > Personalize > Schedule.
4. Set automation: Triggers > Actions > Test send.
5. Monitor analytics weekly, clean bounces monthly.
