# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Omnisend against Mailchimp makes sense for many users. Both platforms cater to businesses looking to nurture leads, drive sales, and engage customers through email. But they shine in different areas: Mailchimp is a generalist powerhouse, while Omnisend specializes in ecommerce. This in-depth comparison breaks down their features, pricing, usability, and more to help you decide.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend is an email and SMS marketing platform designed primarily for ecommerce stores. It integrates seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce. Launched with a focus on omnichannel marketing, Omnisend allows users to send emails, SMS, and push notifications from one dashboard.

Key strengths include pre-built workflows for abandoned cart recovery, welcome series, and post-purchase campaigns. It's particularly useful for online retailers who want to leverage customer data for personalized messaging. Omnisend's popup builder and segmentation tools help capture leads and segment lists based on behavior, such as browsing history or purchase patterns.

Practical advice: If your business relies on an online store, start by connecting Omnisend to your ecommerce platform. Use their drag-and-drop editor to create product-based emails that highlight upsell opportunities.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing since 2001, evolving from simple newsletters to a full marketing automation suite. It's ideal for small to medium businesses, bloggers, and non-profits. Mailchimp offers audience management, templates, automation, and even landing pages and ads.

Its user-friendly interface appeals to beginners, with thousands of pre-designed templates. Advanced users appreciate behavioral automations, A/B testing, and integrations with CRM tools like Salesforce or Google Analytics.

Practical tip: For beginners, Mailchimp's free plan is a great entry point. Import your contacts, select a template, and send your first campaign within minutes. Always clean your list first to improve deliverability.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a critical factor when choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp.

### Omnisend Pricing

- **Free Plan**: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month.
- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts), scales with contacts.
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts), adds advanced segmentation and A/B testing.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing.

Omnisend charges based on contacts and emails sent, with fair usage for SMS. No contracts, monthly billing.

### Mailchimp Pricing

- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month (with branding).
- **Essentials**: $13/month (500 contacts).
- **Standard**: $20/month (500 contacts), includes automations.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced features.

Mailchimp's free tier is more generous for emails but limits features. Costs rise quickly with larger lists.

**Verdict**: Omnisend is often cheaper for ecommerce users with high-volume sends due to unlimited emails in paid plans. Mailchimp suits low-volume senders on a budget.

Practical advice: Calculate your monthly contacts and send volume using each platform's calculator. Factor in SMS costs if needed—Omnisend bundles them efficiently.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Builders and Templates

Both offer drag-and-drop editors. Mailchimp has more templates (hundreds), customizable for any industry. Omnisend focuses on ecommerce with dynamic product blocks that pull live inventory data.

**Winner**: Omnisend for stores; Mailchimp for general use.

### Automation and Workflows

Omnisend excels with ecommerce-specific automations like cart abandonment (up to 7 emails), win-back, and cross-sell. It supports multi-channel (email + SMS).

Mailchimp's automations are versatile: customer journeys, RSS-to-email, and behavioral triggers. However, complex workflows require higher tiers.

Practical tip: Test automations with a small segment. For Omnisend, set up cart recovery immediately—recovery rates can hit 10-20% with good timing.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Omnisend uses predictive segmentation based on purchase data. Mailchimp offers tags, groups, and merge fields for personalization.

**Edge**: Omnisend for behavioral ecommerce data.

### Integrations

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Zapier, Facebook, etc.). Omnisend has 140+, prioritizing ecommerce (Klaviyo alternatives aren't direct, but it covers Shopify deeply).

### Analytics and Reporting

Both provide open rates, clicks, and revenue tracking. Omnisend's ecommerce reports show attributed revenue per campaign. Mailchimp offers comparative reports and Einstein AI insights (Premium only).

Practical advice: Track revenue per recipient (RPR) in Omnisend for ROI. In Mailchimp, use A/B testing reports to refine subject lines—aim for 20-30% open rates.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is intuitive for non-marketers, with guided tours. Omnisend has a steeper learning curve but ecommerce-focused onboarding.

Support: Mailchimp offers 24/7 email/chat (paid plans); Omnisend provides priority support and a knowledge base.

**Practical tip**: Use free trials (14-30 days) to test navigation. Watch tutorials for advanced features like custom JSON in Omnisend.

## Pros and Cons

### Omnisend Pros
- Ecommerce-optimized automations
- Unlimited emails in paid plans
- Multi-channel (SMS/push)

Cons:
- Less ideal for non-ecommerce
- Fewer templates

### Mailchimp Pros
- Generous free plan
- Vast template library
- Broad integrations

Cons:
- Expensive at scale
- Feature-locked behind tiers

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Choose Omnisend if**:
- You run an online store (Shopify/Woo).
- Need SMS and advanced ecommerce flows.
- Prioritize revenue tracking.

**Choose Mailchimp if**:
- You're a beginner or small business.
- Need general marketing tools (social ads, sites).
- Have a diverse audience beyond ecommerce.

Practical advice: Audit your needs—ecommerce volume? Go Omnisend. Multi-channel general campaigns? Mailchimp. Migrate data carefully using CSV exports.

## Conclusion

Omnisend vs Mailchimp boils down to your business type. Omnisend wins for ecommerce with superior automations and value at scale, while Mailchimp remains the go-to for versatility and ease. Both deliver results when used strategically: focus on list hygiene, personalization, and testing.

Start with free trials, align with your goals, and scale accordingly. Whichever you pick, consistent campaigns will boost engagement and sales. Ready to choose? Sign up today and elevate your email marketing.
