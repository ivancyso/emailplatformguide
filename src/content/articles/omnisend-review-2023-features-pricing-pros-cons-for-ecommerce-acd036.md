# Omnisend Review 2023: Is It the Best Email Marketing Tool for Ecommerce?

If you're running an ecommerce store and struggling with customer engagement, you've likely come across Omnisend. This all-in-one marketing automation platform specializes in email, SMS, and push notifications, making it a favorite among Shopify and WooCommerce users. In this comprehensive Omnisend review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, ease of use, and whether it's worth your investment. Whether you're a beginner or scaling your business, we'll provide practical advice to help you decide.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is a marketing automation platform designed primarily for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it focuses on omnichannel communication—combining email, SMS, web push notifications, and popups into one dashboard. Unlike generalist tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend tailors its features for online stores, with deep integrations for platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and Magento.

It's not just about sending emails; Omnisend emphasizes automation workflows that recover abandoned carts, welcome new subscribers, and nurture leads. With a free plan available, it's accessible for small businesses testing the waters.

**Practical Tip:** Start with Omnisend's free plan if you have under 250 contacts. It includes core features like email campaigns and basic automations, letting you evaluate without commitment.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs a punch with ecommerce-specific tools. Here's a breakdown:

### Drag-and-Drop Email Builder

The visual editor is intuitive, offering pre-built templates optimized for mobile. You can customize with product blocks that pull live data from your store—think dynamic images and prices.

**Practical Advice:** Use product blocks for cart recovery emails. Link directly to the abandoned items to boost recovery rates by up to 20-30% based on industry benchmarks. Test A/B subject lines within the builder for higher opens.

### Advanced Automation Workflows

Omnisend shines in automations: welcome series, abandoned cart, back-in-stock alerts, and cross-sell campaigns. The workflow builder uses if/then logic for segmentation.

**Pro Tip:** Set up a multi-step abandoned cart flow: First email after 1 hour (reminder), second after 24 hours (discount offer), third after 72 hours (urgency closeout). Segment by cart value—offer bigger discounts to high-value carts.

### SMS and Push Notifications

SMS campaigns have high open rates (98% average). Omnisend handles opt-ins compliantly (TCPA/GDPR). Web push works for browser notifications without apps.

**Practical Advice:** Combine SMS with email in omnichannel journeys. For flash sales, send push first, email second, SMS last for non-openers. Track ROI via unified reporting.

### Popups and Forms

Wheel of fortune, exit-intent popups, and spin-to-win increase list growth. Forms embed anywhere.

**Tip:** A/B test popup timing and incentives. Offer 10% off for emails, 15% for SMS opt-in. Aim for 5-10% conversion rates on popups.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Hyper-segment by behavior (purchased, viewed), tags, or custom data. Reports show revenue per channel.

**Advice:** Create segments like 'VIP customers' (3+ purchases). Send exclusive previews. Use heatmaps in reports to refine campaigns.

### Integrations

Seamless with Shopify, WooCommerce, Google Analytics, Facebook, Klaviyo alternatives. API for custom needs.

## Omnisend Pricing: Transparent Tiers

Omnisend uses a contact-based model:

- **Free:** Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month.
- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts), unlimited emails, basic automations.
- **Pro:** $59/month (500 contacts), advanced segmentation, A/B testing.
- **Enterprise:** Custom, for high-volume.

No setup fees; pay per SMS credits separately (~$0.01/message US).

**Practical Advice:** Calculate costs: For 1,000 contacts, Standard is ~$36/month. Factor SMS at scale. Switch tiers anytime—no contracts.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-focused: Syncs orders/inventory automatically.
- Omnichannel: Email + SMS + push in one place.
- Generous free plan.
- 24/7 support via chat/email.
- High deliverability (99%+ claimed).

### Cons
- SMS costs add up for high volume.
- Learning curve for complex workflows.
- No native social media posting.
- Limited to ecommerce (not ideal for B2B).

**Real-User Insight:** On G2/Capterra, Omnisend scores 4.6/5 from 1,000+ reviews, praising automation ease but noting occasional integration glitches.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Perfect for DTC brands, Shopify stores (1,000-50,000 contacts). If you sell physical/digital products and want quick ROI from cart recovery, it's ideal. Avoid if you're non-ecommerce or need heavy B2B CRM.

**Decision Framework:** 
1. Need omnichannel? Yes → Omnisend.
2. Budget under $50/month? Free/Standard.
3. Scaling to 10k+ contacts? Pro plan.

## Practical Setup Guide

1. **Sign Up:** Connect store (2 mins).

3. **Build First Automation:** Abandoned cart (template).

5. **Test & Launch:** Preview on devices.
