# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Picks for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing hikes, limited free tier, and complex interface have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're dealing with scaling costs or needing advanced automation, it's time to explore options that offer better value, superior deliverability, and user-friendly designs.

In this roundup, we'll cover the top 10 **Mailchimp alternatives** tailored for small businesses, creators, e-commerce stores, and agencies. Each includes key features, pricing, pros/cons, and practical advice on when to choose it. All data is based on official sites as of 2024—always verify current plans.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month, with paid plans starting at $13/month but jumping sharply for larger lists (e.g., $299/month for 10k contacts). Users often cite poor automation, slow support, and forced upgrades as pain points.

**Practical advice**: Audit your needs first. Calculate current costs vs. alternatives using their pricing calculators. Test deliverability with free trials—aim for 95%+ inbox rates. Prioritize platforms with Zapier integrations if you use multiple tools.

## 1. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
- Unlimited contacts on free plan (300 emails/day).
- Advanced automation, SMS, chat, and CRM.
- Drag-and-drop editor, A/B testing, transactional emails.

### Pricing
Free forever; paid from $25/month (20k emails).

### Pros/Cons
**Pros**: Affordable scaling, multi-channel. **Cons**: Steeper learning for beginners.

**Best for**: SMBs wanting all-in-one marketing. **Advice**: Use for high-volume transactional emails—integrates seamlessly with WooCommerce.

## 2. MailerLite

### Key Features
- Free up to 1,000 subscribers/12,000 emails/month.
- Pop-ups, landing pages, automation workflows.
- High deliverability (99% reported).

### Pricing
Free; paid from $10/month.

### Pros/Cons
**Pros**: Intuitive, cheap, great templates. **Cons**: Limited reporting vs. enterprise tools.

**Best for**: Bloggers and small lists. **Advice**: Leverage website pop-ups for list growth—pair with Google Analytics for tracking.

## 3. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
- Deep automation (if/then branches, site tracking).
- CRM, sales pipelines, machine learning scoring.
- 900+ integrations.

### Pricing
From $29/month (500 contacts).

### Pros/Cons
**Pros**: Powerful for personalization. **Cons**: Overkill for simple newsletters.

**Best for**: E-commerce and lead nurturing. **Advice**: Map customer journeys first—use conditional content to boost opens by 20-30%.

## 4. ConvertKit (now Kit)

### Key Features
- Creator-focused: tagging, broadcasts, commerce tools.
- Visual automations, subscriber scoring.

### Pricing
Free to 300 subscribers; from $29/month.

### Pros/Cons
**Pros**: Simple for solopreneurs. **Cons**: Less robust analytics.

**Best for**: Content creators. **Advice**: Segment by tags (e.g., 'ebook buyers') for targeted sequences—increases revenue per subscriber.

## 5. Klaviyo

### Key Features
- E-commerce powerhouse: flows, segments, A/B.
- Predictive analytics, revenue tracking.

### Pricing
Free to 250 contacts/500 emails; scales with usage.

### Pros/Cons
**Pros**: Shopify native. **Cons**: Expensive at scale.

**Best for**: Online stores. **Advice**: Set up abandoned cart flows immediately—recovers 10-15% lost sales.

## 6. GetResponse

### Key Features
- Webinars, funnels, AI email generator.
- Conversion funnels, landing pages.

### Pricing
Free trial; from $19/month.

### Pros/Cons
**Pros**: All-in-one. **Cons**: Interface feels dated.

**Best for**: Agencies running webinars. **Advice**: Use funnel builder for lead magnets—track ROI with UTM parameters.

## 7. Constant Contact

### Key Features
- Event management, social ads, AI recommendations.
- Mobile app for on-the-go edits.

### Pricing
From $12/month (500 contacts).

### Pros/Cons
**Pros**: Beginner-friendly support. **Cons**: Higher costs for extras.

**Best for**: Non-profits/small biz. **Advice**: Integrate with Facebook for ads—simplifies multi-channel campaigns.

## 8. Drip

### Key Features
- E-com automations, customer data platform.
- Workflow builder, revenue experiments.

### Pricing
From $39/month (500 people).

### Pros/Cons
**Pros**: Advanced segmentation. **Cons**: No free plan.

**Best for**: DTC brands. **Advice**: Use revenue tracking to justify spend—focus on LTV over opens.

## 9. AWeber

### Key Features
- Legacy reliability, RSS campaigns, quizzes.
- Strong deliverability.

### Pricing
Free to 500 subscribers; from $19.99/month.

### Pros/Cons
**Pros**: Stable, good support. **Cons**: Basic automations.

**Best for**: Long-term newsletters. **Advice**: Automate RSS-to-email for consistent sending—saves time.

## 10. Omnisend

### Key Features
- Omnichannel (email/SMS/push), pre-built workflows.
- Product recommendations.

### Pricing
Free to 250 contacts/500 emails; from $16/month.

### Pros/Cons
**Pros**: E-com focused value. **Cons**: Limited non-ecom use.

**Best for**: Shopify stores. **Advice**: Enable SMS for cart recovery—higher engagement than email alone.

## Practical Advice for Choosing a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Budget Check**: For <5k contacts, MailerLite/Brevo win. Scale to 50k+? ActiveCampaign/Klaviyo.
2. **Test Deliverability**: Use GlockApps or Mail-Tester during trials.
3. **Migration Tips**: Export Mailchimp CSV, import directly—most support it. Clean lists first (remove inactives).
4. **Key Metrics**: Track open rates (25-30% avg), clicks (2-5%), unsubscribes (<0.5%).
5. **Integrations**: Essential for CRMs (HubSpot), e-com (Shopify), analytics (GA4).
6. **Compliance**: Ensure GDPR/CCPA tools like double opt-in.

Start with 2-3 trials. Run a test campaign to the same segment across platforms.

## Conclusion

The best **Mailchimp alternative** depends on your audience size, tech comfort, and goals. Brevo and MailerLite shine for affordability, while ActiveCampaign and Klaviyo dominate automation. Don't stick with Mailchimp out of habit—switching can cut costs by 50%+ and boost engagement.
