# Best Mailchimp Alternatives: Why Switch and Top Picks for 2024

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but rising costs, limited automation in lower plans, and a clunky interface have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're dealing with bloated pricing after recent changes or need advanced features like deep automations and CRM integration, it's time to explore options. This roundup covers the top 10 Mailchimp alternatives, focusing on real-world usability for small businesses, e-commerce stores, and creators. We'll break down features, pricing, pros/cons, and practical tips to help you choose.

## Why Look for Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month, with paid plans starting at $13/month but scaling quickly—up to $350/month for 50,000 contacts. Recent pricing hikes and sunset of legacy plans frustrate users. Common pain points include:

- **Limited automation**: Basic triggers only in free/standard plans.
- **Deliverability issues**: Average inbox rates around 80-85%.
- **Steep learning curve**: Editor feels dated.

Switching can save 30-50% on costs while unlocking better tools. According to EmailToolTester, 40% of Mailchimp users migrate for affordability and features.

### Key Factors to Evaluate
- **Pricing model**: Per contact vs. per send.
- **Automation depth**: Behavioral triggers, A/B testing.
- **Integrations**: With Shopify, WordPress, etc.
- **Ease of migration**: Import tools and support.

## Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)
Brevo offers unlimited contacts on free plans (300 emails/day). Paid starts at $25/month for 20,000 emails.

**Pros**:
- Excellent deliverability (95%+).
- Built-in SMS/chat.
- Advanced automation workflows.

**Cons**:
- No drag-and-drop for landing pages.

**Practical Advice**: Ideal for transactional emails. Migrate by exporting CSV from Mailchimp and using Brevo's importer—test with 10% of list first.

### 2. MailerLite
Affordable at $10/month for 1,000 subscribers, unlimited sends.

**Pros**:
- Intuitive editor.
- Pop-ups/forms included free.
- High deliverability.

**Cons**:
- Limited CRM features.

**Practical Advice**: Great for bloggers. Use their website builder for quick landing pages; segment lists by engagement to boost open rates 20%.

### 3. ActiveCampaign
Starts at $29/month for 1,000 contacts.

**Pros**:
- Machine learning automations.
- Deep CRM/sales tools.
- 99% deliverability.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve.

**Practical Advice**: For sales teams, set up 'if/then' automations (e.g., cart abandonment). Integrate with Zapier for 6,000+ apps; split test subject lines weekly.

### 4. Klaviyo
Free up to 250 contacts; $20/month after.

**Pros**:
- E-commerce focus (Shopify native).
- Predictive analytics.
- Revenue tracking.

**Cons**:
- Less for non-ecom.

**Practical Advice**: Sync Shopify data for flows like welcome series—increase revenue 15-30%. Use dynamic product blocks in emails.

### 5. ConvertKit
$15/month for 300 subscribers.

**Pros**:
- Creator-friendly tagging.
- Visual automations.
- Monetization tools.

**Cons**:
- Basic reporting.

**Practical Advice**: Tag subscribers by content interest; automate broadcasts. Export Mailchimp tags directly—grow list with incentives like lead magnets.

### 6. GetResponse
$19/month for 1,000 contacts.

**Pros**:
- Webinar integration.
- Conversion funnels.
- AI email generator.

**Cons**:
- Interface overwhelming.

**Practical Advice**: Build funnels for courses; A/B test CTAs. Clean bounces pre-migration to maintain sender reputation.

### 7. Constant Contact
$12/month for 500 contacts.

**Pros**:
- Event management.
- Social ads.
- Beginner-friendly.

**Cons**:
- No custom HTML.

**Practical Advice**: For nonprofits/events, schedule RSVPs. Use AI subject optimizer for 10-15% open rate lifts.

### 8. Drip
$39/month for 1,000.

**Pros**:
- E-com automations.
- Customer journey builder.
- Workflow scoring.

**Cons**:
- Pricier for small lists.

**Practical Advice**: Score leads by actions; integrate with ReCharge for subscriptions. Personalize with first-name merges.

### 9. Omnisend
Free to 250 contacts; $16/month.

**Pros**:
- Omnichannel (SMS/push).
- Pre-built workflows.
- E-com templates.

**Cons**:
- Limited free analytics.

**Practical Advice**: Automate post-purchase upsells; segment by purchase history. Test SMS for 98% open rates.

### 10. Moosend
Free to 1,000; $9/month.

**Pros**:
- Unlimited emails.
- Page builder.
- A/B testing free.

**Cons**:
- Newer, fewer integrations.

**Practical Advice**: Use landing pages for lead gen; track ROI with UTM tags. Migrate in batches to monitor deliverability.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: E-com? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Creators? ConvertKit.
2. **Budget Check**: Calculate TCO—Brevo/MailerLite for savings.
3. **Trial Everything**: Most offer 14-30 day trials; import sample list.
4. **Deliverability Test**: Send to Gmail/Yahoo; aim for 90%+.
5. **Support Quality**: Live chat > email tickets.

**Pro Tip**: Use tools like GlockApps for inbox placement pre/post-switch.

## Practical Migration Guide

- **Backup Data**: Export audiences, campaigns, automations from Mailchimp.
- **Map Fields**: Align custom fields (e.g., tags to segments).
- **Rebuild Automations**: Prioritize high-ROI ones like welcome series.
- **Warm IP**: Start with low volume (10% list) to build reputation.
- **Notify Subscribers**: Announce change to reduce unsubscribes.

Expect 1-2 weeks; tools like ActiveCampaign offer migration services.

## Conclusion

The best Mailchimp alternative depends on your scale and goals—Brevo for versatility, ActiveCampaign for power users, Klaviyo for Shopify stores. All outperform Mailchimp in affordability and features for most. Start with a trial, migrate strategically, and watch engagement soar. Ready to switch? Pick one, test, and optimize. Your inbox (and wallet) will thank you.
