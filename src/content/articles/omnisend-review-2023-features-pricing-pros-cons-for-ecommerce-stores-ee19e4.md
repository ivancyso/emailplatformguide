# Omnisend Review 2023: Is It the Best Email Marketing Tool for Ecommerce?

If you're running an online store and looking to boost sales through email marketing, Omnisend might have crossed your radar. As an ecommerce-focused platform, it stands out by combining email, SMS, and push notifications into one dashboard. In this comprehensive Omnisend review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, ease of use, and whether it's worth your investment. Whether you're on Shopify, WooCommerce, or another platform, we'll help you decide if Omnisend fits your needs.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an all-in-one marketing automation platform designed specifically for ecommerce businesses. Launched in 2014, it helps store owners create targeted campaigns across multiple channels without needing separate tools. Unlike general email platforms like Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes omnichannel marketing—email, SMS, web push notifications, and popups—all tailored for driving revenue.

It's particularly popular with Shopify users due to seamless integrations, but it supports over 140 platforms including BigCommerce, WooCommerce, and Magento. The platform uses customer data from your store to segment audiences and trigger personalized automations, making it a go-to for scaling sales.

**Practical Tip:** Start by connecting your store's API key during signup. This pulls in customer data automatically, saving hours of manual import.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs a punch with features built for ecommerce growth. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Builder and Templates

The drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, even for beginners. It offers 250+ responsive templates optimized for mobile, with ecommerce-specific blocks like product carousels and order summaries. You can A/B test subject lines, content, and send times directly in the builder.

**Practical Advice:** Use dynamic content blocks to show abandoned cart items or personalized recommendations. Test sending emails at peak customer times (e.g., evenings for fashion stores) to lift open rates by 20-30%.

### Automation Workflows

Omnisend shines in automations. Pre-built workflows for welcome series, abandoned carts, back-in-stock alerts, and post-purchase upsells are ready to launch in minutes. Custom workflows use if/then logic based on behavior, like browsing history or purchase value.

For example, a cart abandonment flow sends an email reminder after 1 hour, an SMS after 24 hours, and a discount push notification after 48 hours. Data shows these recover up to 17% of lost sales on average.

**Pro Tip:** Segment by RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary value) for hyper-targeted flows. High-value customers get VIP discounts, while lapsed buyers receive win-back offers.

### SMS and Push Notifications

Not just email—Omnisend includes SMS for high-open-rate messages (98% average) and web push for browser notifications. SMS campaigns are compliant with TCPA regulations, and push works without apps.

**Practical Advice:** Reserve SMS for urgent promos like flash sales. Combine with email for 3x higher engagement. Always include opt-out links to maintain compliance.

### Popups and Landing Pages

On-site popups capture emails with exit-intent triggers, spin-to-win wheels, and time delays. These feed directly into your contact list for immediate nurturing.

### Analytics and Reporting

Track revenue per recipient, ROI, and campaign performance with ecommerce-specific metrics. Heatmaps show what links get clicks, and cohort analysis reveals long-term customer value.

**Tip:** Set up custom dashboards for key KPIs like ROAS (Return on Ad Spend). Review weekly to refine underperforming segments.

## Omnisend Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

Omnisend offers a free plan for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month—perfect for testing. Paid plans start at:

- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts) – All core features.
- **Pro:** $59/month (500 contacts) – Adds A/B testing, advanced reporting.
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing for 100k+ contacts.

Pricing scales by contact list size, not sends, which is cost-effective for growing lists. SMS and push have pay-per-use rates (e.g., $0.015/SMS in US).

**Practical Advice:** Calculate costs using their pricing calculator. Migrate from free Mailchimp tiers to save 50%+ as your list grows, since Omnisend unlimited sends beat per-email pricing.

No long-term contracts; monthly billing with a 14-day trial on paid plans.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-first design with deep store integrations.
- Omnichannel support boosts engagement.
- User-friendly interface; quick setup (under 30 minutes).
- Strong automation ROI—users report 2.4x average revenue lift.
- 24/7 support via chat/email for paid users.

### Cons
- SMS/push costs add up for high-volume senders.
- Free plan limits scalability (no automations).
- Advanced segmentation requires Pro plan.
- Less suited for non-ecommerce (e.g., B2B newsletters).

**Real User Insight:** On G2 and Capterra, Omnisend scores 4.6/5 from 1,000+ reviews, praised for ease and results but dinged for occasional integration glitches.
