Mailchimp is the most recognizable name in email marketing, but it's not always the best fit. Pricing has increased significantly, the free plan has shrunk, and some features are locked behind expensive tiers.

If you're looking for a Mailchimp alternative, here are 10 platforms worth considering.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Common reasons people leave Mailchimp:
- **Pricing:** Mailchimp has increased prices multiple times. The Standard plan starts at $13/month for just 500 contacts.
- **Free plan limitations:** The free plan now caps at 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month — down from 2,000 contacts and 10,000 emails.
- **Complexity:** Mailchimp has added so many features that the interface can feel cluttered for users who just want simple email marketing.
- **Affiliate restrictions:** Mailchimp has strict policies around affiliate marketing content.

## The Best Alternatives

### 1. MailerLite — Best Overall Alternative

**Why switch:** [MailerLite](/mailerlite-review) does everything Mailchimp does for less money, with a cleaner interface.

- Free plan: 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month (2x Mailchimp's free tier)
- Paid plans from $9/month
- Clean drag-and-drop editor
- Good automation capabilities
- Built-in website and landing page builder

**Best for:** Anyone who wants Mailchimp-like features at a lower price.

### 2. Kit (ConvertKit) — Best for Creators

**Why switch:** If you're a blogger, podcaster, or course creator, [Kit's](/convertkit-review) tag-based system and creator tools are a better fit than Mailchimp's list-based approach.

- Free plan: 1,000 subscribers
- Visual automation builder (better than Mailchimp's)
- Built-in commerce for digital products
- Creator Network for growth

**Best for:** Content creators who want simplicity and powerful automation.

### 3. AWeber — Best for Reliability

**Why switch:** AWeber has been around since 1998 and offers better deliverability, more templates (700+), and phone support.

- Free plan: 500 subscribers
- 700+ email templates
- Affiliate marketing friendly
- Phone support available

**Best for:** Small businesses that value proven reliability and customer support. Read our full [AWeber review](/aweber-review) for a detailed assessment.

### 4. Brevo (Sendinblue) — Best for Multi-Channel

**Why switch:** Brevo combines email, SMS, WhatsApp, and live chat in one platform. Mailchimp requires separate tools or paid add-ons for these channels.

- Free plan: 300 emails/day
- Email + SMS + WhatsApp + chat
- Built-in CRM
- Transactional email included

**Best for:** Businesses that communicate across multiple channels.

### 5. Moosend — Best for Ecommerce

**Why switch:** Moosend offers ecommerce-specific features (product recommendations, cart abandonment) at a fraction of Mailchimp's price.

- Plans from $9/month
- AI product recommendations
- Pre-built ecommerce automations
- Real-time analytics

**Best for:** Online stores that want affordable email marketing with ecommerce features.

### 6. ActiveCampaign — Best for Advanced Automation

**Why switch:** ActiveCampaign's automation capabilities far exceed Mailchimp's (see our [ActiveCampaign vs Mailchimp](/activecampaign-vs-mailchimp) comparison). If you've outgrown Mailchimp's Customer Journey Builder, this is the upgrade.

- Plans from $29/month
- Most powerful automation builder available
- Built-in CRM
- 900+ integrations

**Best for:** Growing businesses that need sophisticated marketing automation.

### 7. GetResponse — Best for Webinars + Email

**Why switch:** GetResponse combines email marketing with webinar hosting — a unique combination that Mailchimp doesn't offer.

- Free plan: 500 contacts
- Built-in webinar platform
- Landing page builder
- Conversion funnel tools

**Best for:** Businesses that use webinars as part of their marketing strategy.

### 8. Constant Contact — Best for Events

**Why switch:** Constant Contact excels at event marketing with built-in RSVPs, ticketing, and event management that Mailchimp lacks.

- Plans from $12/month
- Event management tools
- Social media posting
- Good template library

**Best for:** Organizations that regularly host events.

### 9. Omnisend — Best for Ecommerce Automation

**Why switch:** Omnisend is built specifically for ecommerce with deep Shopify/WooCommerce integration, SMS, and push notifications.

- Free plan: 250 contacts, 500 emails/month
- Pre-built ecommerce workflows
- SMS and push notifications
- Product picker for emails

**Best for:** Shopify and WooCommerce stores. Read our [Omnisend review](/omnisend-review) for a deeper look, or compare it against [Klaviyo](/klaviyo-review), the other top ecommerce option.

### 10. Systeme.io — Best All-in-One

**Why switch:** Systeme.io combines email marketing with course hosting, funnel building, and affiliate management. It replaces multiple tools.

- Free plan: 2,000 contacts
- Email + funnels + courses + affiliate management
- No transaction fees on sales
- Generous free tier

**Best for:** Solopreneurs who want one platform for everything.

## How to Migrate from Mailchimp

Most alternatives make migration straightforward:

1. **Export your list** from Mailchimp (Audience > All contacts > Export)
2. **Import to your new platform** (CSV upload is universal)
3. **Recreate your automations** (most platforms offer migration guides)
4. **Update your forms** on your website
5. **Test everything** before fully switching

Most platforms also offer free migration assistance for larger accounts.

## The Bottom Line

The best Mailchimp alternative depends on your needs. For most people, **MailerLite** offers the smoothest transition with lower pricing. **Kit** is the clear winner for creators, and **ActiveCampaign** is the upgrade path for power users.
