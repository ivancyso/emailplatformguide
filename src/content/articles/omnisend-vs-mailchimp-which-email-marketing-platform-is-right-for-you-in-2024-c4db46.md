# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform is Right for You in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's customer engagement and sales. With so many options, pitting Omnisend against Mailchimp highlights two popular choices: Mailchimp's beginner-friendly interface and broad appeal versus Omnisend's ecommerce-centric, omnichannel focus. This comparison dives into their features, pricing, usability, and more to guide your decision.

Whether you're a small business owner, ecommerce store manager, or marketer scaling campaigns, understanding these tools' strengths will help you pick the winner for your needs.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing since 2001. It's known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor, making it accessible for non-technical users. Mailchimp offers a forever-free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month, ideal for startups testing the waters.

Key strengths include:
- **Robust email templates**: Hundreds of customizable designs.
- **Audience segmentation**: Basic to advanced options based on behavior and demographics.
- **Automation**: Drip campaigns, abandoned cart recovery, and welcome series.

However, as your list grows, pricing escalates, and advanced ecommerce features require higher tiers or integrations.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend targets ecommerce brands, launched in 2014, and excels in multichannel marketing (email, SMS, push notifications). It's designed for platforms like Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce, with seamless integrations.

Standout features:
- **Omnichannel workflows**: Automate across email, SMS, and web push in one flow.
- **Product recommendations**: AI-driven personalization for higher conversions.
- **Free plan**: Up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, with paid plans starting affordably.

Omnisend shines for stores wanting to recover lost sales through targeted messaging.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a critical factor when deciding between Omnisend and Mailchimp.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month.
- **Essentials**: $13/month (5,000 contacts) to $350+/month (50,000+).
- **Standard**: $20/month+ with better automation.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced A/B testing and support.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts and sends, with overages adding costs. SMS and deeper automations cost extra.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free**: 250 contacts, 500 emails/month.
- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts) scaling to $59/month (10,000).
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts) up to $1,059/month (100,000+), unlocking SMS and advanced segmentation.

Omnisend uses a contact-based model, making it more predictable. SMS credits are included in Pro plans, offering better value for multichannel users.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your monthly contacts and sends. For ecommerce with SMS needs, Omnisend often proves cheaper long-term. Test free plans to gauge fit without commitment.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp's is more polished for general marketing with creative templates. Omnisend's builder integrates dynamic product blocks, pulling live inventory from your store—perfect for ecommerce promotions.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journey Builder for behavioral triggers like purchases or site visits.
- **Omnisend**: Superior pre-built workflows for cart abandonment (up to 40% recovery rates reported in case studies), post-purchase, and win-back campaigns. Multichannel branching (e.g., email if no open, then SMS) sets it apart.

**Tip**: For high-volume automations, map your customer lifecycle first. Use Omnisend if 30%+ of revenue comes from repeat buyers.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp segments by tags, groups, and engagement. Omnisend adds ecommerce-specific segments like cart value or product views, with hyper-personalization via merge tags and AI content generation.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp provides detailed reports on opens, clicks, revenue. Omnisend tracks ROI per channel, attributing sales accurately across touchpoints.

**Practical Advice**: Review past campaign data. If tracking revenue per email is key, Omnisend's ecommerce analytics give clearer insights.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners—its interface feels like a content editor. Setup takes minutes.

Omnisend has a slight learning curve for multichannel but offers guided onboarding, video tutorials, and 24/7 chat support even on free plans.

**Tip**: Spend 30 minutes in each demo account. Import a test list to build a campaign and simulate automations.

## Integrations

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Omnisend focuses on ecommerce: native Shopify, Magento, Klaviyo alternatives, plus Google and Facebook.

Both support Zapier for custom needs. Omnisend's API is robust for developers.

**Practical Advice**: List your top 5 tools (e.g., Shopify, Google Sheets). Prioritize platforms with native syncs to avoid data silos.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket support; priority for paid plans.
Omnisend: Live chat 24/7, email, knowledge base, and dedicated managers on Pro.

Users praise Omnisend's responsiveness for ecommerce troubleshooting.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- Free plan generosity.
- Excellent for non-ecommerce.
- Creative templates.

**Cons**:
- Pricing jumps with scale.
- Limited multichannel.

### Omnisend
**Pros**:
- Ecommerce powerhouse.
- Cost-effective multichannel.
- Strong automations.

**Cons**:
- Less ideal for non-stores.
- Fewer general templates.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp** if: You're a blogger, service business, or beginner needing simple emails. Budget under $20/month initially.
- **Choose Omnisend** if: Running an online store, need SMS/push, or focus on revenue recovery. Scaling to 10k+ contacts.

**Migration Tip**: Export lists from one to the other via CSV. Rebuild automations carefully to avoid disruptions. A/B test campaigns post-switch.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out Mailchimp for ecommerce due to multichannel automation and ROI-focused features, while Mailchimp remains the go-to for versatile, easy marketing. Evaluate based on your business type—start with free trials to send test campaigns.

Ultimately, the best platform aligns with your goals. For sales-driven ecommerce, Omnisend; for broad marketing, Mailchimp. Whichever you pick, consistent testing and segmentation will boost results.
