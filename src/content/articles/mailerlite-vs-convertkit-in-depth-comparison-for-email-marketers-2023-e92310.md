# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. For bloggers, creators, and small business owners, MailerLite and ConvertKit stand out as popular, creator-friendly options. Both offer automation, beautiful templates, and segmentation without the complexity of enterprise tools like Mailchimp.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, and real-world applicability. Whether you're migrating from free tools or scaling up, you'll get practical advice to decide which fits your workflow. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms use tiered plans based on subscriber count, with free tiers for testing.

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation and drag-and-drop editor.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers (scales up).
- **Advanced**: $20+/month with A/B testing, webhooks.

MailerLite shines for budget-conscious users. No transaction fees on paid plans, and you can send unlimited emails on higher tiers within fair use.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (recently reduced from 1,000), basic forms and broadcasts.
- **Creator**: $15/month for 300 subscribers, unlocks sequences and tagging.
- **Creator Pro**: $29+/month with advanced reporting, subscriber scoring.

ConvertKit charges per subscriber more aggressively as lists grow. It's pricier for larger audiences but justifies it with creator-specific tools.

**Practical Advice**: If under 1,000 subscribers, start with MailerLite's generous free plan. ConvertKit suits those willing to invest early in premium automations. Calculate your costs using their pricing calculators—factor in growth projections.

## Ease of Use: User Interface and Onboarding

Both have intuitive dashboards, but differences emerge in setup.

### MailerLite: Beginner-Friendly Drag-and-Drop
MailerLite's editor is block-based, like Canva for emails. No coding needed for responsive designs. Onboarding includes video tutorials and templates optimized for high open rates.

**Pro Tip**: Use their popup builder for list growth—integrates seamlessly with WordPress via plugins.

### ConvertKit: Creator-Centric Simplicity
ConvertKit uses a visual automation builder (like a flowchart). Tagging replaces traditional segments, making subscriber journeys intuitive for content creators.

**Pro Tip**: Import contacts easily from Google Sheets; their tagging system excels for segmenting engaged vs. inactive subscribers.

MailerLite edges out for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for those familiar with funnels.

## Core Features: Head-to-Head

### Email Editor and Templates
- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates, photo editor built-in. Strong for visual campaigns.
- **ConvertKit**: Simpler editor focused on text-heavy newsletters. Commerce add-on for selling digital products directly.

**Advice**: Test templates with your branding—preview on mobile.

### Automations and Sequences
- **MailerLite**: Trigger-based automations (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart). RSS-to-email for bloggers.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced sequences with if/then logic. Ideal for nurture sequences post-opt-in.

ConvertKit wins for complex creator funnels; MailerLite for straightforward workflows.

### Segmentation and Tagging
ConvertKit's tags allow dynamic segmentation (e.g., 'purchased-course'). MailerLite uses groups and custom fields—simpler but effective.

**Practical Tip**: Segment by engagement: Send re-engagement campaigns to opens <20%.

### Landing Pages and Forms
Both offer unlimited pages/forms.
- MailerLite: Popups, embedded forms, countdown timers.
- ConvertKit: Native checkout, incentive forms (e.g., free lead magnet).

Integrate with Zapier for both—connect to Stripe, Google Analytics.

### Analytics and Deliverability
- MailerLite: Open/click rates, bounce handling, domain authentication.
- ConvertKit: Revenue tracking, subscriber value scores.

Both boast 99%+ deliverability with SPF/DKIM setup. **Advice**: Warm up your domain gradually.

## Integrations and Support

- **Integrations**: MailerLite (500+ via Zapier), ConvertKit (native with Gumroad, Teachable).
- **Support**: MailerLite (chat, email, knowledge base). ConvertKit (email, extensive help docs, creator community).

**Tip**: Join their Facebook groups for user tips.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
Small businesses, bloggers starting out, or anyone prioritizing affordability. Perfect if you need quick popups and unlimited sends without complexity.

**Example Use Case**: E-commerce store owner using RSS campaigns for product updates.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Content creators (podcasters, course sellers) needing sequences and tagging. If monetization is key, its commerce features save time.

**Example Use Case**: Newsletter author automating lead magnet delivery and upsells.

**Migration Advice**: Export CSV from your current tool. Both support one-click imports. Test with 100 subscribers first.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage and style. MailerLite offers unbeatable value and simplicity for growing lists affordably. ConvertKit empowers creators with deeper automations and monetization, despite the premium price.

No clear 'winner'—audit your needs: Budget? Go MailerLite. Creator funnels? ConvertKit. Start free trials (MailerLite: 30 days; ConvertKit: 14 days) and track metrics like open rates.
