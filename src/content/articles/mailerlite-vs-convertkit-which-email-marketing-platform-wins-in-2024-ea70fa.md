## Introduction

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. In this MailerLite vs ConvertKit comparison, we'll break down two popular tools favored by creators, bloggers, and small businesses. Both offer automation, beautiful templates, and segmentation, but they cater to slightly different needs.

MailerLite emphasizes affordability and simplicity, with a generous free plan. ConvertKit (rebranded as Kit) focuses on creators with powerful tagging and sequencing. We'll compare pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more to help you decide. This guide draws from official docs, user reviews on G2 and Capterra, and practical testing insights.

## Pricing Breakdown

Pricing is a top concern for those starting out. Here's how they stack up (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates).

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, forms, and landing pages.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $9/month for 500 subs (billed annually), scales to $89 for 10,000 subs.
- **Advanced**: From $20/month, adds A/B testing, web push notifications.

No transaction fees on payments, and high deliverability (99%+ claimed).

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers (limited to 10,000 emails/month? Check site). Basic broadcasts and forms.
- **Creator**: Starts at $15/month for up to 300 subs, $29 for 1,000. Unlimited emails in paid plans.
- **Creator Pro**: From $25/month, adds advanced reporting, subscriber scoring.

ConvertKit charges based on subs only, making it predictable but pricier for larger lists. No free plan beyond basics for high-volume users.

**Practical Advice**: If you're under 1,000 subs and bootstrapping, MailerLite's free tier lets you test without commitment. ConvertKit suits creators willing to invest early for unlimited sends.

## Feature Comparison

Both platforms excel in core email marketing, but differences shine in automation and integrations.

### Email Editor and Templates
- **MailerLite**: Intuitive drag-and-drop builder with 80+ responsive templates. Photo blocks, columns, and custom HTML. Previews across devices.
- **ConvertKit**: Clean visual editor with blocks for text, images, buttons. Creator-focused templates for newsletters, courses. Strong mobile optimization.

**Winner**: Tie—both user-friendly. Test MailerLite for speed, ConvertKit for storytelling flows.

### Automation and Workflows

### MailerLite Automations
- Trigger-based: Welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement.
- Visual workflow builder: If/then branches, delays, A/B splits (paid).
- RSS-to-email for bloggers.

### ConvertKit Automations (Sequences)
- Tag-based triggers: Subscribers auto-move through sequences based on actions (e.g., opens, clicks).
- Advanced rules: Commerce integrations for upsells.
- Visual Automations builder in Pro.

**Practical Advice**: Use MailerLite for simple drip campaigns (e.g., new subscriber → 5-email welcome). ConvertKit shines for complex creator funnels like lead magnet → course upsell.

### Segmentation and Tagging
- **MailerLite**: Groups, tags, custom fields. Behavioral segments (e.g., opened last 30 days).
- **ConvertKit**: Tag-driven segmentation—gold standard for creators. Predicts tags based on behavior.

ConvertKit edges out for nuanced audience management.

### Landing Pages and Forms
Both offer popups, embedded forms, and one-page sites:
- MailerLite: Unlimited pages on all plans, A/B testing.
- ConvertKit: Forms, landing pages, checkouts (Pro).

**Tip**: Integrate with WordPress via plugins for seamless list growth.

### Integrations
- MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress, Shopify.
- ConvertKit: Strong with creator tools (Gumroad, Teachable), Zapier, Google Analytics.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

MailerLite's interface is minimalist—learn in minutes. Drag-and-drop feels like Canva.

ConvertKit has a steeper curve due to tagging but includes tutorials and a Creator Network for tips.

**Advice for Beginners**: Start with MailerLite's 30-day money-back. ConvertKit's free trial helps migrate.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

- **MailerLite**: Email/chat support (paid), extensive help center, 4.6/5 on Capterra. 99.85% deliverability.
- **ConvertKit**: Priority email/phone (Pro), active community. 99.5%+ deliverability, inbox placement tools.

Both score high (G2: MailerLite 4.7, ConvertKit 4.5).

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros**:
- Cheaper long-term.
- Free plan generous.
- Fast setup.

**Cons**:
- Fewer creator-specific features.
- Limited advanced analytics (paid).

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:
- Superior tagging/automation.
- Unlimited sends.
- Creator community.

**Cons**:
- Higher cost.
- No perpetual free plan for growth.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
Small businesses, bloggers, e-commerce starters needing affordable basics. Ideal if budget < $20/mo and list < 5,000.

**Practical Steps**:
1. Sign up free.
2. Import CSV.
3. Build welcome automation.
4. A/B test subject lines (aim 20-30% open rates).

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Content creators, course sellers, podcasters with engaged audiences. Best for tag-heavy strategies and revenue funnels.

**Practical Steps**:
1. Use free trial.
2. Set up sequences for lead magnets.
3. Tag openers/clickers.
4. Integrate commerce for one-click upsells.

## Migration and Testing
Switching? Both offer CSV imports and Zapier. Test with a small segment: Send identical campaigns and track opens/clicks.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage and style. MailerLite wins for cost-conscious users wanting quick wins—perfect for scaling from zero. ConvertKit dominates for creators building lifetime value through smart automations.

No one-size-fits-all; trial both. Track metrics like 25%+ opens, 3%+ clicks. Whichever you pick, consistent value-driven emails beat perfect tools.
