# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Free & Paid Options for Email Marketing

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation in lower tiers, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're facing high fees, poor deliverability, or need more advanced features like deep segmentation or ecommerce integrations, it's time to explore options.

In this roundup, we'll cover the top 10 Mailchimp alternatives tailored for small businesses, creators, ecommerce stores, and agencies. Each includes key features, pricing (as of 2024), pros/cons, and who it's best for. We'll focus on real-world usability, migration tips, and selection criteria to help you switch smoothly.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 subscribers and 1,000 emails/month, with paid plans starting at $13/month but escalating quickly (e.g., $299/month for 10k subs). Advanced automations and A/B testing are locked behind higher tiers. Common pain points include:

- **Pricing hikes**: Legacy users saw 30-50% increases.
- **Deliverability issues**: Spam complaints rose post-2023 updates.
- **Limited customization**: Rigid templates and weak personalization.

Switching can save 20-60% on costs while gaining better tools. Always export your Mailchimp list via CSV before migrating.

## 1. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
Brevo combines transactional emails, marketing automation, SMS, and live chat. Unlimited contacts, drag-and-drop editor, and 40k emails/month on free plan.

### Pricing
Free forever (300 emails/day). Paid from $25/month (20k emails).

### Pros
- Excellent deliverability (98%+).
- Multi-channel (email + SMS).
- Easy migration from Mailchimp.

### Cons
- Automation workflows less visual than competitors.

**Best for**: SMBs needing transactional emails. Migrate by importing CSV and remapping tags.

## 2. MailerLite

### Key Features
Simple editor, pop-ups/forms, automation, and landing pages. High deliverability and A/B testing on all plans.

### Pricing
Free up to 1,000 subs/12k emails. Paid from $10/month.

### Pros
- Affordable scaling.
- Intuitive for beginners.
- Strong website builders.

### Cons
- Fewer ecommerce integrations.

**Best for**: Bloggers and small lists. Practical tip: Use their migration tool for seamless transfer.

## 3. ConvertKit (now Kit)

### Key Features
Tag-based segmentation, visual automations, creator-focused forms, and payouts for newsletters.

### Pricing
Free up to 300 subs. Paid from $15/month.

### Pros
- Perfect for creators/substack-style.
- One-click upsells.

### Cons
- No free images library.

**Best for**: Content creators. Advice: Recreate sequences with tags instead of lists for better engagement.

## 4. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
Advanced CRM, machine learning scoring, 870+ integrations, site tracking.

### Pricing
From $29/month (500 contacts).

### Pros
- Powerful automations.
- Deep personalization.

### Cons
- Steep learning curve.

**Best for**: Agencies/marketers. Tip: Start with their 14-day trial; map Mailchimp automations carefully.

## 5. Klaviyo

### Key Features
Ecommerce-first: Revenue tracking, dynamic product feeds, flow builder for abandoned carts.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails. Paid from $20/month.

### Pros
- Shopify/WooCommerce native.
- Predictive analytics.

### Cons
- Overkill for non-ecom.

**Best for**: Online stores. Integrate directly via API for real-time sync.

## 6. GetResponse

### Key Features
Conversion funnels, webinars (up to 1,000 attendees), AI email generator, ecommerce tools.

### Pricing
Free up to 500 contacts. Paid from $19/month.

### Pros
- All-in-one (autoresponders + funnels).

### Cons
- Interface feels dated.

**Best for**: Course creators. Use their importer for quick setup.

## 7. AWeber

### Key Features
Proven deliverability, tagging, custom HTML, integrations with 700+ apps.

### Pricing
From $19/month (500 subs), free trial.

### Pros
- Reliable support.
- Legacy-friendly.

### Cons
- Basic automations.

**Best for**: Long-time marketers.

## 8. Moosend

### Key Features
Unlimited emails, AI optimization, landing pages, ecommerce automation.

### Pricing
Free up to 1,000 subs. Paid from $9/month.

### Pros
- Cost-effective.
- Open analytics.

### Cons
- Newer player.

**Best for**: Budget-conscious teams.

## 9. Omnisend

### Key Features
Omnichannel (email/SMS/push), pre-built workflows, hyper-customization.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails. Paid from $16/month.

### Pros
- Ecom-focused flows.

### Cons
- SMS add-on costs extra.

**Best for**: Retailers.

## 10. Zoho Campaigns

### Key Features
Zoho CRM integration, workflow automation, RSS campaigns.

### Pricing
Free up to 2,000 subs/6k emails. Paid from $4/month.

### Pros
- Integrates with Zoho suite.

### Cons
- Limited templates.

**Best for**: Zoho users.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

### Step 1: Assess Your Needs
- **List size/budget**: MailerLite/Brevo for <10k subs.
- **Automations**: ActiveCampaign/Klaviyo.
- **Ecommerce**: Klaviyo/Omnisend.

### Step 2: Check Deliverability & Compliance
Look for 95%+ rates, GDPR tools, and authentication (DKIM/SPF). Test with free trials.

### Step 3: Migration Best Practices
1. Export Mailchimp audience CSV.
2. Clean data (remove bounces).
3. Use built-in importers.
4. Notify subscribers of switch.
5. Test campaigns.

### Step 4: Key Metrics to Track
Open rates (20-30% benchmark), click-through (2-5%), unsubscribe (<0.5%). Tools like these often improve these via better segmentation.

Pro tip: Run A/B tests on subject lines post-migration—expect 10-20% uplift with advanced personalization.

## Conclusion

The best Mailchimp alternative depends on your niche: Brevo or MailerLite for simplicity and savings, ActiveCampaign for power users, Klaviyo for shops. Most offer free plans/trials, so test 2-3 before committing. Switching can cut costs by half while boosting engagement—start with a trial today and reclaim your email strategy.
