# Omnisend Review 2024: Best Email Marketing Tool for Ecommerce Stores?

If you're running an online store and struggling with customer engagement, you've likely come across Omnisend. This email marketing platform specializes in ecommerce, helping businesses like yours send targeted emails, SMS, and push notifications. In this Omnisend review, we'll dive deep into its features, pricing, ease of use, and whether it's the right choice for your business. Whether you're on Shopify, WooCommerce, or another platform, we'll provide practical advice to help you decide.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform designed primarily for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it focuses on helping store owners nurture leads, recover abandoned carts, and drive repeat sales through automated campaigns across email, SMS, and web push notifications. Unlike general-purpose tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend tailors its features to online retailers, integrating seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and Magento.

It's particularly popular among small to medium-sized businesses (SMBs) because of its user-friendly interface and free plan. As of 2024, Omnisend powers over 70,000 brands worldwide, sending billions of messages annually. No coding required—drag-and-drop editors make it accessible for non-marketers.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend stands out with ecommerce-specific tools. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Automation Workflows

Omnisend's pre-built workflows are a game-changer. Set up abandoned cart recovery in minutes: it automatically sends a reminder email 1 hour after abandonment, followed by SMS if no action. Practical tip: Customize the delay timers based on your audience—test 30 minutes for high-traffic days.

Other workflows include welcome series, post-purchase upsells, and win-back campaigns for inactive customers. Use dynamic blocks to insert product images and prices directly from your store. Pro tip: Segment by purchase history to boost open rates by 20-30%.

### SMS and Web Push Notifications

Beyond email, Omnisend supports SMS for urgent messages like flash sales (opt-in required for compliance). Web push works for browser notifications, even if users aren't subscribed to email. Advice: Start with SMS for cart recovery—conversion rates can hit 20-40%.

### Advanced Segmentation and Personalization

Segment lists by behavior: page views, purchases, or custom events. Hyper-personalize with first-name tags or product recommendations powered by AI. Integrate with Google Analytics for richer data. Tip: Create segments for "VIP customers" (3+ purchases) and send exclusive offers—expect higher loyalty.

### Product Recommendations and Popups

Omnisend's popup builder captures emails at checkout or exit-intent. AI-driven recommendations suggest "frequently bought together" items. Practical setup: Embed the popup script on your site via a single code snippet; A/B test headlines for 10-15% lift in signups.

### Reporting and Analytics

Track revenue per recipient, ROI, and campaign performance. Heatmaps show where subscribers click. Export data to CSV for deeper analysis in Google Sheets. Note: Advanced reports unlock on Pro plans.

## Omnisend Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

Omnisend offers a free plan for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month—perfect for testing. Paid plans are contact-based:

- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts) up to $59/month (10k). Includes most automations, SMS credits extra.
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts) to $382/month (10k). Adds API access, custom events, dedicated support.
- **Custom**: For 100k+ contacts, with volume discounts.

No contracts; monthly billing. SMS costs ~$0.02/message in the US. Tip: Calculate costs by estimating growth—many users stay on Standard for years. Compare to Klaviyo (more expensive) or ActiveCampaign (less ecommerce-focused).

## Ease of Use and Integrations

Onboarding takes under 10 minutes: Connect your store, import contacts, and launch a workflow. The dashboard is intuitive, with a clean mobile app for monitoring. Integrates with 140+ apps: Shopify, WooCommerce, Facebook, Google Ads, Zapier.

Practical advice for setup:
1. Sign up and connect your store.
2. Import historical orders for segmentation.
3. Build your first workflow (welcome series).
4. Test on a small segment before full send.

Support is email/chat (24/7 on Pro), with a robust knowledge base and video tutorials.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-centric features save time.
- Generous free plan.
- High deliverability (98%+ inbox rate).
- Excellent automation ROI—users report 2-5x revenue growth.

### Cons
- SMS credits not unlimited (buy add-ons).
- Reporting depth limited on Standard.
- No native landing pages (use third-party).
- Steeper learning for complex custom events.

Real user feedback from G2/Capterra: 4.7/5 stars, praised for ease and support.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for:
- Shopify/WooCommerce stores under 50k contacts.
- Brands focused on automation over manual campaigns.
- Businesses wanting SMS/push without multiple tools.

Not for: Enterprise needing white-label or agencies managing clients (limited seats).

Alternatives: Klaviyo (more advanced, pricier), Mailchimp (cheaper but basic), Brevo (general-purpose).

## Practical Tips to Maximize Omnisend

1. **Compliance First**: Use double opt-in; include unsubscribe links. Follow GDPR/CAN-SPAM.
2. **A/B Testing**: Test subject lines (e.g., "Your Cart Misses You" vs. "Forgot Something?")—aim for 20%+ open rates.
3. **Timing Optimization**: Send emails 8-10 AM weekdays; SMS afternoons.
4. **Content Best Practices**: Short subjects (under 50 chars), mobile-optimized templates, clear CTAs.
5. **Scale Smart**: Monitor unsubscribe rates (<0.5% ideal); clean lists quarterly.

Case study example: A fashion store recovered 15% of abandoned carts, adding $10k/month revenue.
