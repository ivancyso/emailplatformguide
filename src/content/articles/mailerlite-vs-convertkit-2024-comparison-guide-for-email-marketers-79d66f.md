# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: 2024 Comparison Guide for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you grow and engage your audience. For bloggers, creators, small business owners, and solopreneurs, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both tools offer powerful features for building email lists, sending newsletters, and automating campaigns without needing coding skills.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, core features, automation capabilities, ease of use, integrations, and customer support. We'll use real-world data from their official sites (as of late 2024) and user feedback from platforms like G2 and Capterra. No hype—just practical advice to help you decide which fits your needs.

Whether you're migrating from free tools like Mailchimp or starting fresh, this guide covers it all.

## Pricing: Which Offers Better Value?

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both MailerLite and ConvertKit use tiered plans based on subscriber count, with free options to test the waters.

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month (1,000 subs), scales to $20 for 2,500 subs. Unlimited emails.
- **Advanced**: $21/month (1,000 subs) adds A/B testing, web push notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for 200k+ subs.

MailerLite shines for affordability—email sends are unlimited on paid plans, and there's no overage fees.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers (previously 300), with forms and basic broadcasts.
- **Creator**: $15/month (300 subs), $29 for 1,000. Unlimited emails, advanced automations.
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month (300 subs), adds revenue tracking, subscriber scoring.
- **Enterprise**: Custom for high-volume users.

ConvertKit's free plan expanded in 2024, but paid tiers are pricier than MailerLite for similar subscriber counts. It's monthly billing only, no annual discounts mentioned currently.

**Practical Tip**: For under 1,000 subs, start with either free plan. MailerLite wins for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit if you need creator-specific tools like commerce integrations early.

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools Head-to-Head

Both platforms excel in no-code email building, but differences emerge in templates, segmentation, and extras.

### Email Editor and Templates
- **MailerLite**: Drag-and-drop editor with 80+ responsive templates. Photo blocks, columns, and custom HTML. Strong mobile preview.
- **ConvertKit**: Visual builder focused on clean, minimalist designs. 100+ templates optimized for creators. Easier for plain-text newsletters.

**Advice**: MailerLite for visually rich emails (e.g., e-commerce); ConvertKit for simple, text-heavy broadcasts like newsletters.

### Forms, Popups, and Landing Pages
Both offer embeddable forms, exit-intent popups, and landing pages.
- MailerLite: Unlimited popups, rule-based triggers (scroll depth, time on page). High-converting landing pages with A/B testing on paid plans.
- ConvertKit: Native forms with tagging on signup. Popups via third-party (e.g., OptinMonster integration). Landing pages geared toward product sales.

**Pro Tip**: Use MailerLite's popups for aggressive list growth; ConvertKit's tagging for personalized follow-ups.

### Segmentation and Tagging
- MailerLite: Groups, segments based on behavior, custom fields.
- ConvertKit: Advanced tagging system—auto-tag based on actions (e.g., "purchased course"). Subscriber scores in Pro plan.

ConvertKit edges out for behavioral segmentation, ideal for course creators.

## Automation: Sequences and Workflows

Automation is where creators thrive.

### MailerLite Automations
- Drag-and-drop builder for workflows like welcome series, abandoned cart.
- Triggers: Signups, opens, clicks, purchases.
- Time delays, A/B splits on Advanced plan.

### ConvertKit Sequences
- Renowned for "Sequences"—drip campaigns with visual mapping.
- Advanced: If/then branches, ending triggers.
- Commerce: Automate upsells post-purchase.

**Practical Advice**: Map your customer journey first. ConvertKit for complex funnels (e.g., blog-to-course); MailerLite for straightforward nurture sequences. Test with 100 subs before scaling.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

MailerLite: Intuitive dashboard, 5-minute setup. Extensive video tutorials, 24/7 live chat.
ConvertKit: Creator-friendly interface, but steeper learning for tagging. Great migration tool from other platforms, email support (24-48 hours).

User ratings (G2, 2024): MailerLite 4.6/5, ConvertKit 4.5/5. MailerLite praised for simplicity; ConvertKit for power users.

**Tip for Beginners**: Import a CSV list and send a test broadcast. MailerLite feels faster out-of-gate.

## Integrations and Advanced Tools

- **MailerLite**: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress plugin, Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel. E-commerce: WooCommerce, Shopify.
- **ConvertKit**: Strong for creators—Gumroad, Teachable, Patreon. Zapier, native commerce for Stripe/PayPal.

Both deliverability ~99%. MailerLite has better EU compliance (GDPR tools).

## Customer Support and Community

- MailerLite: Live chat (instant), email, knowledge base. Highly rated.
- ConvertKit: Email/ticket (1-2 days), podcast, creator community. No live chat on base plans.

**Advice**: If you're solo, prioritize MailerLite's chat. Creators love ConvertKit's resources.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Small businesses/e-commerce stores
- Budget starters (under 5k subs)
- Popup-heavy growth strategies
- Teams needing quick support

**Real Example**: A boutique shop uses MailerLite for cart recovery, saving 20% on tools vs competitors.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers/podcasters selling digital products
- Complex automation needs
- Revenue-focused creators
- Users valuing tagging over popups

**Real Example**: A course creator automates a 7-email sequence, boosting conversions 15% via tags.

## Migration Tips

Switching? Both offer CSV imports and Zapier transfers.
1. Export from old tool.
2. Recreate top automations.
3. Test sends to 10% of list.
4. Update website forms.

MailerLite's wizard is beginner-friendly; ConvertKit auto-maps tags.

## Conclusion: The Verdict

**MailerLite wins for affordability and ease**—perfect if you're growing a list cost-effectively with popups and simple automations. Start here if budget < $20/mo.

**ConvertKit excels for creators** with advanced sequences and tagging, justifying the premium for product sellers.

Test both free plans for a week. Track metrics like open rates (aim 30%+) and growth. For most solopreneurs, MailerLite's value tips the scale, but scale to ConvertKit as revenue grows.
