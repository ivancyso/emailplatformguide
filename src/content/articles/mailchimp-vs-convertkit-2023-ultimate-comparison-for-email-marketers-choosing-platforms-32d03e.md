# Mailchimp vs ConvertKit: Which Email Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing between Mailchimp and ConvertKit can feel overwhelming when you're building your email list. Both are powerhouse email marketing platforms, but they cater to slightly different audiences. Mailchimp shines for small businesses and e-commerce stores with its robust free plan and drag-and-drop tools. ConvertKit, on the other hand, is tailored for creators like bloggers, podcasters, and course sellers, emphasizing advanced automations and subscriber tagging.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, usability, integrations, support, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur or scaling a team, you'll get practical advice to pick the right tool. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: Free Plans to Enterprise

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer accessible entry points, but their structures differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's forever-free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends—ideal for startups testing the waters. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for Premium (10,000+ contacts). Key perks include unlimited emails on higher tiers, but expect contact-based billing with overage fees.

**Practical Tip:** If your list is under 500 and you're sending basic newsletters, stick to free. For e-commerce automation, upgrade to Standard ($20+/month) early.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit ditched its free plan in recent years but offers a 14-day free trial. Creator plan starts at $9/month (up to 300 subscribers), jumping to $29/month for 1,000 subs. The top Creator Pro tier hits $79/month for 10,000 subs, with enterprise options available.

No send limits, which is a boon for frequent broadcasters. Annual billing saves 20%.

**Practical Tip:** Creators with growing lists love ConvertKit's predictable pricing. Budget for it if you need tagging from day one; trial it alongside Mailchimp to compare.

**Winner:** Mailchimp for beginners on a $0 budget; ConvertKit for value at scale.

## Ease of Use and User Interface

### Mailchimp: Beginner-Friendly Drag-and-Drop
Mailchimp's interface is intuitive, with a dashboard resembling popular design tools. The email builder is block-based—drag images, text, buttons effortlessly. Onboarding wizards guide new users through list setup and first campaigns.

However, advanced automations feel clunky for power users.

**Practical Advice:** Perfect if your team lacks design skills. Spend 30 minutes building a professional newsletter.

### ConvertKit: Clean but Learning Curve for Tagging
ConvertKit's minimalist design prioritizes function over flash. Forms and landing pages are simple to create, but the tagging system (for segmentation) requires upfront planning.

Visual automations use flowcharts—logical once learned.

**Practical Advice:** If you're tech-savvy or follow tutorials, master it in a day. Use their templates to speed up.

**Winner:** Mailchimp for sheer simplicity; ConvertKit for streamlined creator workflows.

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools Head-to-Head

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp boasts 100+ responsive templates across industries, with a content studio for blogs-to-emails. High customization without coding.

ConvertKit offers fewer (around 50) but optimized for creators—clean, mobile-first designs with incentive boxes for lead magnets.

**Tip:** Export Canva designs to Mailchimp; use ConvertKit's for quick opt-ins.

### Automation and Sequences
ConvertKit excels here with subscriber journeys: tag-based triggers send personalized sequences (e.g., welcome series post-signup). No expiration on automations.

Mailchimp's customer journeys are solid for basics like abandoned carts but less flexible for complex nurturing.

**Practical Advice:** Creators: ConvertKit sequences convert 20-30% better for courses. E-com: Mailchimp's behavioral triggers shine.

### Segmentation and Analytics
ConvertKit's tagging beats traditional segments—apply multiple tags per subscriber for hyper-personalization (e.g., 'podcast listener + course buyer'). Reporting shows opens, clicks, revenue.

Mailchimp uses groups/tags too, with strong e-com analytics (revenue per email) and A/B testing on all plans.

**Tip:** Track ROI in both; ConvertKit for engagement depth, Mailchimp for sales metrics.

### Forms, Landing Pages, and Popups
Both handle these well. Mailchimp's forms embed easily; ConvertKit's are conversion-optimized with A/B testing built-in.

**Winner:** Tie, but ConvertKit edges for creator funnels.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps: Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics, Facebook Ads. Native e-com tools like product blocks.

ConvertKit connects to 100+, focusing on creator stack: Teachable, Gumroad, YouTube, Patreon. Strong Zapier support.

**Practical Advice:** E-com/business? Mailchimp + Shopify = seamless. Creators? ConvertKit + podcast hosts accelerates growth.

## Customer Support and Resources

Mailchimp: 24/7 email/chat on paid plans, extensive help center, free courses.

ConvertKit: Priority email support (faster on Pro), live chat, creator-focused academy with webinars.

**Tip:** Both responsive; ConvertKit feels more 'human' for niche advice.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp vs ConvertKit?

**Pick Mailchimp if:**
- You're a small business/e-com store under 2,000 subs.
- Need free entry and quick setups.
- Prioritize design variety and sales tracking.

**Pick ConvertKit if:**
- You're a creator/blogger/podcaster.
- Want tagging for personalized sequences.
- Plan long-term audience building.

**Migration Tip:** Export CSV lists easily between both. Test with a small segment first.

## Conclusion: Your Next Steps

Mailchimp vs ConvertKit boils down to your goals. Mailchimp democratizes email for everyone with its free tier and polish—start here if budget-tight. ConvertKit empowers creators to nurture superfans through smart automations, justifying the cost for higher engagement.

No one-size-fits-all; trial both (Mailchimp free, ConvertKit 14 days). Track opens, clicks, conversions for 30 days post-switch. Whichever you choose, consistent value-driven emails grow lists 4x faster than sporadic blasts.
