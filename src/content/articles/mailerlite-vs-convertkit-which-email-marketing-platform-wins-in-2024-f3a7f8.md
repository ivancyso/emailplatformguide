# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. For solopreneurs, bloggers, small business owners, and content creators deciding between MailerLite and ConvertKit, this in-depth comparison breaks it down. We'll cover pricing, core features, automation capabilities, ease of use, integrations, and more to help you pick the winner for your needs.

Both platforms are popular for their user-friendly interfaces and powerful features tailored to growing audiences. MailerLite emphasizes affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit focuses on creators with advanced tagging and sequencing. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing platform. Both MailerLite and ConvertKit offer free plans, but they scale differently as your subscriber list grows.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite stands out for its budget-friendly tiers. The free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month, including access to basic automation and a drag-and-drop editor. Paid plans start at around $10 per month (billed annually) for up to 500 subscribers, scaling gradually:
- Up to 2,500 subs: ~$20/mo
- Up to 10,000 subs: ~$50/mo

Higher tiers include advanced features like A/B testing and custom HTML. Annual billing saves 10-20%, making it ideal for bootstrapped businesses.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit's free plan covers up to 1,000 subscribers with unlimited emails, but lacks some premium features. Paid plans begin at $15/month for the Creator tier (up to 300 subs, billed annually it's lower), jumping to $29 for 500-1,000 subs:
- Up to 3,000 subs: ~$79/mo
- Up to 10,000 subs: ~$199/mo

ConvertKit also offers a Creator Pro plan for commerce features like subscriptions and one-click upsells, starting higher. It's pricier but justifies costs for high-volume creators.

**Practical Advice:** If your list is under 5,000 subscribers and budget is tight, MailerLite wins on cost—saving you hundreds yearly. ConvertKit suits those willing to invest in creator-specific tools like paid newsletters.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Interface

Both platforms prioritize intuitive designs, but differences emerge in setup and daily workflows.

MailerLite's dashboard is clean and beginner-friendly. New users can send their first campaign in minutes via the drag-and-drop editor. Navigation is straightforward, with quick access to lists, automations, and analytics.

ConvertKit uses a visual builder with tagging instead of traditional lists, which has a slight learning curve but streamlines segmentation for creators. The interface feels modern, with a focus on sequences and broadcasts.

**Practical Advice:** Test both free plans. MailerLite for quick starts; ConvertKit if you're comfortable with tags for personalized journeys.

## Feature Breakdown

### Email Editor and Templates

MailerLite's editor is responsive and mobile-optimized, with 80+ free templates. It supports dynamic content blocks and RSS-to-email for bloggers.

ConvertKit offers a block-based editor with creator-focused templates for newsletters and promotions. It's excellent for text-heavy emails but less image-heavy than MailerLite.

**Winner:** Tie—MailerLite for visuals, ConvertKit for simplicity.

### Automation and Workflows

#### MailerLite Automations
Workflows are visual and no-code, supporting triggers like sign-ups, opens, and clicks. Free plan includes basic automations; paid unlocks A/B testing and multi-step sequences. Great for welcome series or abandoned cart (with integrations).

#### ConvertKit Automations
The standout feature: Visual automations with tagging. Build sequences triggered by actions, like "if subscriber buys, tag as customer." Re-engagement campaigns are powerful for dormant lists.

**Practical Advice:** Use MailerLite for simple drip campaigns. Opt for ConvertKit if you need sophisticated subscriber journeys, e.g., tagging podcast listeners for exclusive content.

### Segmentation and List Management

MailerLite uses groups and segments based on behavior, location, or custom fields.

ConvertKit's tag system is superior for creators—tag by interests (e.g., "podcast fan") for hyper-targeted sends, reducing unsubscribes.

### Landing Pages and Forms

MailerLite shines with unlimited free landing pages, popups, and forms—no extra cost. Embeddable and customizable.

ConvertKit includes basic forms and landing pages in higher plans; more focused on email than websites.

**Practical Advice:** Building opt-ins? MailerLite's free popups integrate seamlessly with your site.

### Analytics and Reporting

Both provide open rates, clicks, and revenue tracking. MailerLite offers heatmaps and bounce details. ConvertKit excels in subscriber activity reports, showing tag history.

## Integrations and E-commerce

MailerLite integrates with 1,000+ apps via Zapier, including Stripe, WordPress, Shopify. Native e-commerce for WooCommerce.

ConvertKit connects to Gumroad, Teachable, and has built-in commerce for digital products, subscriptions, and one-click upgrades.

**Practical Advice:** Selling courses? ConvertKit's commerce edges out. For general e-com, MailerLite suffices.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive knowledge base, and Facebook community.

ConvertKit: Email support (priority for paid), creator-focused resources, webinars, and a vibrant podcast.

Both score high on Trustpilot (4.5+ stars).

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros:** Affordable, free landing pages/popups, easy editor, high deliverability.
**Cons:** Less advanced tagging, fewer creator-specific tools.

### ConvertKit
**Pros:** Powerful automations/tags, commerce features, creator community.
**Cons:** Higher cost, steeper learning for beginners.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
- Small businesses, bloggers under 10k subs.
- Those needing cheap landing pages and forms.
- Budget-conscious teams wanting quick wins.

**Practical Tip:** Start with MailerLite's free plan to build your list, then migrate if scaling to creator tools.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
- Content creators, podcasters, course sellers.
- Users prioritizing sequences and tagging.
- Willing to pay for long-term revenue growth.

**Practical Tip:** Leverage ConvertKit's sequences for lead magnets—automate content upgrades to boost conversions by 20-30% (based on user reports).

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

In the MailerLite vs ConvertKit showdown, **there's no one-size-fits-all winner**. MailerLite takes the crown for affordability, ease, and website tools—perfect for most small audiences. ConvertKit dominates for creators needing advanced personalization and monetization.

**Recommendation:** Audit your needs. Under 5k subs and simple automations? Go MailerLite. Creator economy focus? ConvertKit.

Sign up for both free trials today. Track your first campaign's metrics: opens >25%, clicks >3% indicate a good fit. Whichever you choose, consistent value-driven emails will grow your business.
