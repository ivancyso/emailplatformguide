# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate 2024 Comparison Guide

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, drive sales, and grow your business. In this head-to-head comparison of MailerLite vs ConvertKit, we'll break down key factors like pricing, features, ease of use, and more. Both tools cater to creators, bloggers, small businesses, and e-commerce owners looking to build email lists effectively. Whether you're a beginner or scaling up, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email tool. Both platforms offer straightforward plans, but they differ in structure and generosity.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite stands out for its generous free plan: up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. No credit card required, making it perfect for testing. Paid plans start at $10/month (billed annually) for up to 500 contacts under the 'Growing Business' tier, scaling to $20/month for 2,500 contacts. Higher tiers go up to enterprise levels. Additional perks include unlimited emails on paid plans (within fair use) and no overage fees. Practical tip: If you're under 1,000 subs, stick with free to experiment with automations before committing.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (rebranded as Kit) discontinued its perpetual free plan in recent years, offering a 14-day free trial instead. Plans start with 'Creator' at $15/month (annually) for up to 300 subscribers, jumping to $29/month for 1,000. 'Creator Pro' adds advanced features like subscriber scoring at $29/month for 300 subs. Prices scale with list size—e.g., $79/month for 5,000 on Creator. They include unlimited emails and sends. Advice: Budget-conscious users may find ConvertKit pricier for small lists; calculate based on your projected growth using their pricing calculator.

**Winner on Pricing:** MailerLite for startups and bootstrappers due to the free tier.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Both platforms prioritize intuitive interfaces, but user experience varies.

MailerLite's dashboard is clean and beginner-friendly, with a drag-and-drop editor accessible from day one. Onboarding includes quick-start guides and video tutorials. Navigation is straightforward: campaigns, automations, and sites all in a sidebar.

ConvertKit targets creators with a visual composer and tag-based system. Setup involves connecting your site and importing contacts seamlessly. Their 'Creator Network' offers resources, but the learning curve is steeper for tagging vs lists.

**Practical Advice:** Start with MailerLite if you're new to email marketing; use ConvertKit if you're comfortable with segmentation from tools like ActiveCampaign.

## Email Editor and Templates

### MailerLite Editor

Features a responsive drag-and-drop builder with 80+ templates. Blocks for images, buttons, and custom HTML. Mobile preview ensures compatibility. Pro: Photo editor built-in for quick tweaks.

### ConvertKit Editor

Visual builder with pre-built templates for newsletters, broadcasts, and sequences. Strong on personalization (e.g., first name, recent purchase). Supports custom CSS for advanced users.

Both deliver high inbox rates (MailerLite ~99%, ConvertKit similar via authentication). Tip: Test templates on multiple devices; use A/B testing (available in both) for subject lines.

## Automation and Workflows

Automation is where these tools shine for nurturing leads.

### MailerLite Automations

Visual workflow builder for triggers like sign-ups, abandoned carts. Supports A/B testing in automations. Examples: welcome series, re-engagement campaigns. Integrates with e-commerce for behavioral triggers.

### ConvertKit Automations (Sequences)

Tag-based automations excel for creators. Build sequences triggered by actions (e.g., form submit → tag 'interested' → send nurture series). Advanced: subscriber scoring in Pro plans predicts engagement.

**Advice:** Use MailerLite for simple drip campaigns; ConvertKit for complex creator funnels like course launches.

## Segmentation and List Management

MailerLite uses groups and segments based on activity, opens, clicks. Easy CSV imports and cleaning tools.

ConvertKit's tagging system is powerful—no lists needed. Apply multiple tags per subscriber (e.g., 'blog-reader', 'podcast-fan'). Segments pull from tags for targeted sends.

**Pro Tip:** Clean lists monthly (both have tools); aim for <1% bounce rate to protect deliverability.

## Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

### MailerLite

Unlimited landing pages, popups, and forms on all plans. Drag-and-drop with A/B testing. High-converting templates for lead magnets.

### ConvertKit

Forms, landing pages, and checkouts included. Native commerce for selling digital products. Popups via 'Grow' tools.

**Practical Advice:** Embed forms on your site (WordPress plugins available); track conversions with UTM parameters.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native with WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce. API for custom.

ConvertKit: Strong with creator tools (Ghost, Memberful), Zapier, plus commerce integrations (Stripe, Gumroad).

Both support webhooks. Tip: Prioritize platforms you already use to avoid data silos.

## Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite: Detailed reports on opens, clicks, revenue tracking. Heatmaps for engagement.

ConvertKit: Revenue per subscriber, tag reports, A/B insights. Pro: forecasting.

**Advice:** Set up Google Analytics goals alongside for full-funnel tracking.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive knowledge base. High ratings (4.8/5 on Capterra).

ConvertKit: Email support (priority for Pro), podcasts, courses. Community-focused.

Both have Facebook groups. Tip: Join communities early for templates and tips.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite Pros

- Free plan
- Affordable scaling
- All-in-one (sites, forms)

**Cons:** Fewer creator-specific features.

### ConvertKit Pros

- Tag-based power
- Creator tools (monetization)
- Excellent sequences

**Cons:** Higher cost, no free plan.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Beginners/small lists
- Budget-focused SMBs
- Need for popups/landing pages

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers/podcasters
- Digital product sellers
- Advanced segmentation needs

**Migration Tip:** Both offer easy imports; test with a small segment first.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage and style. MailerLite wins for affordability and simplicity—ideal if you're starting or keeping costs low. ConvertKit edges out for creators needing sophisticated tagging and sales funnels. Evaluate your list size, features required, and trial both. Whichever you pick, focus on consistent value-driven emails: aim for 1-2 sends/week, personalize, and analyze results. Start building your list today—your audience awaits!
