# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform is Right for You in 2023?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools designed to help businesses grow through targeted campaigns, automations, and customer engagement. But they cater to slightly different needs: Mailchimp excels in general-purpose marketing for small businesses and beginners, while Omnisend shines in ecommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur, ecommerce store owner, or marketing team lead, you'll get practical advice to decide which platform fits your workflow. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing since 2001. It's known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor, vast template library, and robust analytics. With over 12 million users, it's ideal for newsletters, promotional emails, and basic automations.

**Key Strengths:**
- Beginner-friendly interface.
- Free plan for up to 500 contacts.
- Advanced segmentation and A/B testing.

**Best For:** Bloggers, service-based businesses, and non-ecommerce brands starting out.

Practical tip: Use Mailchimp's audience dashboard to segment lists by engagement—send re-engagement campaigns to inactive subscribers to boost open rates by up to 20% based on industry benchmarks.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend, launched in 2014, focuses on ecommerce marketers. It supports email, SMS, web push, and popups in one platform, with deep integrations for Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce. It's praised for pre-built workflows that automate cart recovery and post-purchase flows.

**Key Strengths:**
- Omnichannel marketing (email + SMS + push).
- Ecommerce-specific automations.
- Generous free plan (500 emails/month, unlimited contacts).

**Best For:** Online stores needing to recover lost sales and nurture customers across channels.

Practical tip: Set up Omnisend's abandoned cart workflow immediately after integrating your store—it typically recovers 10-15% of lost sales without extra effort.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major factor when scaling. Here's a side-by-side look at their plans (as of 2023; always check official sites for updates):

| Feature/Plan | Mailchimp | Omnisend |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| **Free Tier** | Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/mo | Unlimited contacts, 500 emails/mo, 60 SMS credits |
| **Essentials/Standard** | $13/mo (500 contacts) | Standard: Free; Pro: $16/mo (500 contacts) |
| **Intermediate/Pro** | $20/mo (500 contacts) | Pro scales by sends: $59/mo (10k emails) |
| **Premium/Unlimited** | $350+/mo | Custom enterprise |
| **SMS Costs** | Pay-per-use (~$0.015/text) | Included credits, then pay-per-use |

Mailchimp's pricing jumps with contacts and features (e.g., automation in higher tiers). Omnisend bills by emails/SMS sent, making it cheaper for high-volume ecommerce with fewer contacts.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your monthly sends first. If under 500 emails, Omnisend's free plan wins. For 10k+ contacts with low sends, Mailchimp's flat rate might save money. Test both free tiers to project costs.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is polished with 100+ responsive templates. Omnisend offers similar ease but ecommerce-optimized templates (e.g., product blocks that pull live inventory).

Winner: Tie for beginners; Omnisend for stores.

**Tip:** Customize templates with dynamic content blocks to personalize based on purchase history—increasing click-through rates by 14% per studies.

### Automations
Mailchimp has customer journeys for triggers like sign-ups or birthdays. Omnisend's 200+ pre-built workflows (welcome series, win-back) are more ecommerce-focused, with SMS branching.

Winner: Omnisend for advanced automation.

**Tip:** Start with single-trigger automations (e.g., Mailchimp's abandoned browser) before multi-step journeys to avoid overwhelming new subscribers.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both segment by behavior, location, and tags. Omnisend adds product-based segmentation (e.g., "viewed shoes"). Mailchimp's merge tags enable deep personalization.

**Tip:** Use behavioral data for hyper-personalization—"Hey [Name], loved those sneakers?" can lift opens by 26%.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp's reports track ROI, revenue per email. Omnisend emphasizes ecommerce metrics like attributed revenue and cart recovery value.

Winner: Omnisend for ROAS-focused users.

### Omnichannel Features
Omnisend includes SMS (US/Canada/EU), push notifications, and popups. Mailchimp adds SMS in higher plans but lacks native push.

**Tip:** Combine email + SMS for 8x higher engagement; use Omnisend for this if ecommerce.

## Ease of Use: Which is More Beginner-Friendly?

Mailchimp's interface feels like a consumer app—setup in minutes. Omnisend has a slight learning curve for omnichannel but wizards guide you.

**Practical Advice:** Beginners: Mailchimp. Ecommerce pros: Omnisend. Spend 30 minutes on tutorials; both have 24/7 chat support.

## Integrations and Apps

Mailchimp: 300+ (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).
Omnisend: 100+ focused on ecommerce (Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives, Google Ads).

**Tip:** Prioritize native store integrations to sync data automatically—reduces manual imports and errors.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for paid; priority phone for Premium.
Omnisend: 24/7 live chat, email, and dedicated managers for Pro+.

Both score high (4.5+ on G2), but Omnisend edges for responsive ecommerce help.

**Tip:** Join communities like Reddit's r/Emailmarketing for peer advice alongside official support.

## Pros and Cons

**Mailchimp Pros:** Free plan, templates, all-in-one (landing pages, ads).
**Cons:** Expensive scaling, limited SMS.

**Omnisend Pros:** Ecommerce automations, omnichannel, better free tier.
**Cons:** Less general templates, SMS costs add up.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp if:** You're a beginner, non-ecommerce, need simple newsletters, or value a huge template library.
- **Choose Omnisend if:** You run an online store, want SMS/push, or prioritize revenue recovery automations.

**Migration Tip:** Export lists via CSV. Test campaigns on a small segment first to check deliverability.

## Conclusion

Omnisend vs Mailchimp boils down to your business type. Mailchimp offers broad accessibility for general marketing, while Omnisend delivers ecommerce power with omnichannel edge. For most online stores, Omnisend's specialized features justify the switch—especially with its free tier.

Start with free trials: [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com) or [Omnisend](https://omnisend.com). Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and ROAS after 30 days. Whichever you pick, consistent testing and segmentation will drive results.
