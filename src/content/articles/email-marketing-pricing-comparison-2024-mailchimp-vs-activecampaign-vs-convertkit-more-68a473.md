# Email Marketing Pricing Comparison 2024: Which Platform Wins on Value?

Choosing the right email marketing platform isn't just about features—it's about fitting your budget while scaling with your business. In this comprehensive email marketing pricing comparison, we'll break down costs for popular tools like Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, GetResponse, Brevo (formerly Sendinblue), and Klaviyo. We'll focus on real pricing as of mid-2024 (always check official sites for updates), subscriber-based tiers, and value for money. Whether you're a solopreneur with 500 subscribers or a growing team with 10,000+, this guide helps you decide.

We'll cover pricing structures, hidden costs, free plans, and practical tips to avoid overpaying. All data is based on standard monthly billing; annual plans often save 15-20%.

## Understanding Email Marketing Pricing Models

Email platforms charge based on:
- **Subscribers**: Core metric—more contacts mean higher costs.
- **Emails sent**: Some use 'credits' or send limits.
- **Features**: Automation, A/B testing, or e-commerce integrations add premiums.

Common models:
- **Freemium**: Free tier for small lists (e.g., Mailchimp up to 500 subs, 1,000 emails/mo).
- **Tiered plans**: Scale by subscriber count (e.g., ActiveCampaign).
- **Pay-as-you-go**: Brevo's credit system.
- **Unlimited sends**: Rare, but ConvertKit offers it on higher plans.

**Practical advice**: Estimate your list growth. Start free, but factor in 20-50% annual growth. Calculate total cost of ownership (TCO): pricing + time spent on setup/migrations.

## Best Value by Budget and Use Case

- **Under $20/mo (<1,000 subs)**: Mailchimp or ConvertKit free tiers. Practical: Build lists via popups.
- **$20-100/mo (1k-10k subs)**: Brevo or GetResponse—watch credits.
- **Scaling teams (10k+)**: ActiveCampaign for automations.
- **E-commerce**: Klaviyo if revenue >$1M/year.

**Pro tip**: Use calculators on sites (e.g., ActiveCampaign's). Negotiate enterprise discounts for 50k+ subs.

## Hidden Costs and Savings Tips

- **Overages**: Mailchimp charges extra subs.
- **Add-ons**: SMS ($0.01/msg), domains.
- **Migration**: Free tools like Zapier; budget 4-8 hours.

**Savings advice**:
1. Clean lists quarterly (remove bounces)—saves 20-30%.
2. Annual billing.
3. Start trials simultaneously.
4. Track ROI: Aim for $38 revenue per $1 spent (industry avg).
5. Avoid 'feature creep'—match needs.

## Conclusion

No one-size-fits-all in email marketing pricing comparisons. Mailchimp wins beginners, ActiveCampaign power users, ConvertKit creators. For most, start with subscriber projections: under 5k? Go Brevo/GetResponse. Larger? ActiveCampaign's value shines.

Action step: List your subs, must-haves (e.g., automations?), and budget. Test 2-3 platforms' trials. Reassess yearly as lists grow.
