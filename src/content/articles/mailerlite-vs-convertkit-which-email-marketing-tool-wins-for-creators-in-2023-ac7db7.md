# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: In-Depth Comparison for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business. Both MailerLite and ConvertKit are popular choices for creators, bloggers, and small businesses. MailerLite emphasizes affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit focuses on powerful automation for content creators. In this head-to-head, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Here's how they stack up based on current plans (as of late 2023; always check official sites for updates).

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite offers a generous free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Paid plans start at $10/month for 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20 for 1,000 subs, and higher tiers like Advanced at $21/month for 500 subs with extras like A/B testing and web push notifications. It's pay-as-you-grow, with monthly or annual billing (20% discount annually). No subscriber caps beyond plan limits—costs rise predictably with list size.

**Practical tip:** Start free to test campaigns. Upgrade only when you hit limits. Ideal for bootstrapped solopreneurs.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) has no free plan but offers a 14-day trial. The Creator plan starts at $9/month for up to 300 subscribers (annual billing), jumping to $29/month for 1,000 subs. The Creator Pro plan begins at $29/month for 300 subs, adding advanced automation and reporting. Prices increase with subscribers: e.g., $79/month for 5,000 on Creator. Annual saves 20%.

**Practical tip:** If your list is under 300, it's competitive, but costs escalate faster than MailerLite for larger lists. Budget for growth.

**Winner:** MailerLite for affordability, especially starters. ConvertKit if you need pro features early.

## Ease of Use and Interface

### MailerLite

Intuitive drag-and-drop editor, clean dashboard. Beginners set up campaigns in minutes. Mobile-responsive templates and a visual automation builder make it accessible. No steep learning curve—great for non-tech users.

**Advice:** Use the template library for quick starts. Customize with blocks for images, buttons, and text.

### ConvertKit

Creator-friendly but more powerful. The visual builder is straightforward, yet tagging/subscriber management feels advanced. Forms and landing pages are simple to embed.

**Advice:** Leverage the 'sequences' for drip campaigns. It rewards familiarity but might overwhelm absolute newbies.

**Winner:** MailerLite for sheer simplicity.

## Features Breakdown

### Email Templates and Design

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, photo editor integration. Strong customization without coding.

ConvertKit: Fewer templates (focused on clean, minimalist designs). Excels in personalization via tags.

**Tip:** Both support HTML for pros. Test mobile previews always.

### Automation

MailerLite: Trigger-based automations (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart). RSS-to-email for bloggers.

ConvertKit: Sequences shine—automated email courses with tagging. Rules-based triggers like 'if opens X, send Y.' More flexible for creators.

**Practical advice:** For newsletters, ConvertKit's tagging segments readers by behavior (e.g., 'read podcast email'). MailerLite uses segments/groups simpler.

### Forms, Popups, and Landing Pages

Both offer embeddable forms, popups, and pages. MailerLite includes exit-intent popups free; ConvertKit's are incentive-focused (e.g., lead magnets).

**Tip:** A/B test popups. MailerLite's free plan includes basics; ConvertKit unlocks more in Pro.

### Segmentation and List Management

MailerLite: Groups, segments by activity/location. Simple but effective.

ConvertKit: Tag-based (e.g., 'podcast listener'). Broadcast to tags dynamically.

**Advice:** ConvertKit scales better for engaged audiences; import CSVs easily in both.

## Integrations and E-commerce

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WooCommerce, custom API. Stripe for payments.

ConvertKit: Strong with WordPress, Gumroad, Teachable. Zapier too, plus commerce features like one-click upsells.

**Tip:** Check Zapier for niche tools. Both handle GDPR compliance well.

## Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite: Open/click rates, bounce tracking, revenue per email (with integrations).

ConvertKit: Detailed subscriber activity, revenue tracking, A/B insights (Pro).

**Practical advice:** Track 'revenue per subscriber' in ConvertKit for creators selling courses. Use MailerLite's heatmaps for engagement.

## Customer Support

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, extensive help center/videos.

ConvertKit: Email support (faster for paid), Creator Network community, podcasts/resources.

**Winner:** MailerLite for immediacy.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite Pros

- Free plan

- Affordable scaling

- Easy editor

- Popups included

**Cons:** Less advanced tagging; automation not as nuanced.

### ConvertKit Pros

- Creator-optimized automations

- Tagging power

- Monetization tools

**Cons:** Pricier; no free tier.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Beginners/small lists (<5k subs)

- Budget-conscious users

- Simple newsletters/popups

**Migration tip:** Export CSVs from old tools, import segments. Test a welcome automation first.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators/bloggers

- Complex sequences/upsells

- Growing to 1k+ engaged subs

**Tip:** Start with sequences for lead nurturing. Integrate with your CMS early.

## Final Verdict

MailerLite wins for affordability and ease—perfect if you're testing waters or running lean. ConvertKit edges out for creators needing automation depth and monetization, despite higher costs.

Ultimately, trial both (MailerLite free, ConvertKit 14 days). Consider your list size, tech comfort, and goals. For most small businesses, MailerLite's value shines; scale to ConvertKit later.
