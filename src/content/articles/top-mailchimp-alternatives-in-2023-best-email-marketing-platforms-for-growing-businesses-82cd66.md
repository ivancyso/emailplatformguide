# Top Mailchimp Alternatives in 2023: Best Email Marketing Platforms for Growing Businesses

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but as businesses grow, many seek **Mailchimp alternatives** due to rising costs, limited automation depth, or better user experiences elsewhere. If you're evaluating platforms for newsletters, automations, or e-commerce campaigns, this guide compares proven options.

We'll cover why switch, top alternatives with practical pros/cons, selection tips, and a conclusion to help you decide. All recommendations are based on current features, pricing (as of late 2023), and user feedback from sources like G2 and Capterra.

## Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp excels for beginners with its drag-and-drop editor and free tier (up to 500 contacts). However, drawbacks include:

- **Pricing jumps**: Free plan limits; paid starts at $13/month but scales quickly (e.g., $299/month for 10k contacts).
- **Automation limits**: Basic workflows on lower plans.
- **Deliverability concerns**: Some users report higher spam rates post-2023 updates.

Alternatives often offer unlimited sends, advanced segmentation, or e-commerce focus at competitive prices. Ideal for solopreneurs, SMBs, or agencies needing scalability.

## Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives Compared

Here's a head-to-head of leading platforms. We prioritize ease of use, automation, integrations (e.g., Shopify, WordPress), and ROI.

### 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for**: Transactional emails + marketing automation.

Brevo combines SMTP with marketing tools. Pricing: Free up to 300 emails/day; paid from $25/month (unlimited contacts).

**Key Features**:
- Drag-and-drop builder.
- Advanced automation (e.g., abandoned cart).
- SMS/WhatsApp integration.
- 99% deliverability.

**Pros**: Generous free plan; multi-channel. **Cons**: Interface can feel cluttered.

**Practical Tip**: Migrate via CSV import. Test automations with their 14-day trial—perfect if Mailchimp's contact limits frustrate you.

### 2. MailerLite

**Best for**: Bloggers and small teams on a budget.

Affordable and intuitive. Pricing: Free to 1,000 subscribers; $10/month for 500 contacts.

**Key Features**:
- Pop-up forms and landing pages.
- Basic automation + A/B testing.
- Website builder included.

**Pros**: Unlimited emails on paid plans; high deliverability. **Cons**: Fewer e-commerce tools.

**Practical Tip**: Use their migration wizard for seamless Mailchimp switch. Segment lists by engagement for 20-30% open rate boosts.

### 3. ActiveCampaign

**Best for**: Advanced automation and CRM.

Powerhouse for sales funnels. Pricing: $29/month (1,000 contacts).

**Key Features**:
- Machine learning site tracking.
- 700+ integrations.
- Deep CRM + deal pipelines.

**Pros**: Predictive sending; robust reporting. **Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

**Practical Tip**: Start with templates for lead nurturing. Integrate with Zapier for custom workflows—ideal if Mailchimp feels too basic.

### 4. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

**Best for**: Creators and course builders.

Creator-focused. Pricing: Free to 300 subscribers; $29/month.

**Key Features**n
- Visual automations.
- Tagging/subscriber scoring.
- Native commerce (sell digital products).

**Pros**: Simple for newsletters. **Cons**: Limited templates.

**Practical Tip**: Use broadcast sequences for launches. Tag-based segmentation simplifies personalization over Mailchimp lists.

### 5. Klaviyo

**Best for**: E-commerce (Shopify users).

E-com giant. Pricing: Free to 250 contacts; scales by revenue.

**Key Features**:
- Revenue tracking.
- Flow builder for SMS/email.
- Predictive analytics.

**Pros**: ROI-focused; 45% revenue lift reported. **Cons**: E-com only vibe.

**Practical Tip**: Install pixel for cart recovery. A/B test flows—great Mailchimp upgrade for DTC brands.

### 6. GetResponse

**Best for**: All-in-one marketing.

Pricing: $19/month (1,000 contacts).

**Key Features**:
- Webinars + funnels.
- AI email generator.
- Conversion funnels.

**Pros**: 30-day trial. **Cons**: Overwhelming dashboard.

**Practical Tip**: Build funnels for higher conversions. Export Mailchimp data easily.

### 7. AWeber

**Best for**: Long-term reliability.

Veteran platform. Pricing: $19/month.

**Key Features**:
- Loyal customer support.
- E-commerce integrations.
- A/B testing.

**Pros**: Stable deliverability. **Cons**: Dated UI.

**Practical Tip**: Use SparkPost for sends. Good for non-profits.

### 8. Constant Contact

**Best for**: Events and nonprofits.

Pricing: $12/month.

**Key Features**:
- Event management.
- Social ads.

**Pros**: Beginner-friendly. **Cons**: Pricey per contact.

**Practical Tip**: Promote events seamlessly.

### 9. Omnisend

**Best for**: Omnichannel retail.

Pricing: Free to 250; $16/month.

**Key Features**:
- SMS/push notifications.
- Pre-built workflows.

**Pros**: E-com templates. **Cons**: Limited free.

**Practical Tip**: Automate post-purchase.

### 10. Moosend

**Best for**: Affordable automation.

Pricing: Free to 1,000; $9/month.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited emails.
- Landing pages.

**Pros**: Cheap scaling. **Cons**: Newer player.

**Practical Tip**: Use JSON triggers for devs.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative: Practical Guide

1. **Assess Needs**: Newsletters? Pick MailerLite. E-com? Klaviyo.
2. **Budget Check**: Calculate TCO—e.g., ActiveCampaign saves via automation ROI.
3. **Test Migration**: All offer importers. Backup lists first.
4. **Trial Run**: Send test campaigns; monitor opens (aim >25%).
5. **Integrations**: Ensure Shopify/CRM compatibility.
6. **Support**: Live chat wins (Brevo excels).
7. **Scalability**: Unlimited sends matter for growth.

**Pro Tip**: Track metrics pre/post-switch. Use UTM tags for attribution.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ActiveCampaign, and Klaviyo offer better value for most users, with superior automation and pricing. Start with free trials—migrate in under an hour. Your choice depends on audience size and goals, but ditching Mailchimp often unlocks 2-3x engagement.
