# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for Email Marketers in 2024

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are popular platforms, but they cater to slightly different audiences. Mailchimp is a long-standing favorite for beginners and small businesses with its intuitive interface and free tier. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines for ecommerce stores with advanced omnichannel features like SMS and push notifications.

In this comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're scaling an online store or nurturing leads for a service business, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been around since 2001 and powers millions of campaigns. It's known for its drag-and-drop editor, pre-built templates, and robust audience segmentation. The free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month, making it accessible for startups.

Key strengths include:
- **Audience Management**: Tag-based segmentation and behavioral triggers.
- **Content Creation**: Hundreds of mobile-responsive templates.
- **Analytics**: Detailed reports on opens, clicks, and revenue.

However, as you grow, costs rise quickly, and advanced automations require higher tiers. Practical tip: Start with Mailchimp's free plan to test basic newsletters, but evaluate scaling needs early.

## Overview of Omnisend

Launched in 2014, Omnisend targets ecommerce brands with multichannel marketing. It integrates seamlessly with Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce, focusing on cart recovery and personalized flows.

Standout features:
- **Omnichannel**: Email, SMS, web push notifications in one dashboard.
- **Pre-built Workflows**: Abandoned cart, welcome series, post-purchase upsells.
- **List Building**: Popups, signup forms, and API access.

The free plan covers 250 contacts and 500 emails monthly. It's ideal if your business relies on sales funnels. Advice: Use Omnisend's recovery workflows to boost revenue—studies show they recover 10-15% of lost carts on average.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major differentiator. Here's a side-by-side for 500 contacts (billed monthly):

| Feature/Plan       | Mailchimp                  | Omnisend                  |

| Starter/Essentials| $13/mo                    | $16/mo (Standard)        |

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your contact growth. For <1,000 contacts, Mailchimp is cheaper. Over 2,500? Omnisend saves money with fair usage (no send limits on paid plans). Always check current pricing on their sites, as it fluctuates.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly with 100+ industry templates. Customization is straightforward, but code blocks are limited on lower plans.

Omnisend uses a similar editor but emphasizes dynamic content blocks that pull product data from your store. It has fewer templates (~50) but they're ecommerce-optimized.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for variety; Omnisend for product feeds. Tip: Import custom HTML in both for branded designs.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp's automations include customer journeys, RSS campaigns, and A/B testing (Standard plan+). It's solid for basic sequences.

Omnisend dominates with 200+ pre-built workflows, including cart abandonment (sends email + SMS), win-back, and cross-sell. Behavioral triggers like 'viewed product' are native.

**Winner**: Omnisend. Advice: Set up Omnisend's cart recovery first—it emails after 1 hour, SMS after 24, increasing conversions by layering channels.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Both segment by tags, opens, and purchases. Mailchimp adds predictive segmentation (Premium). Omnisend excels in ecommerce segments like 'high-value customers' via revenue data.

Tip: Use merge tags for first-name personalization in both to lift opens by 20-30%.

### SMS and Push Notifications

Mailchimp offers SMS on Premium ($350+/mo), limited to transactional messages.

Omnisend bundles SMS and web push from Standard ($16/mo), with automation support.

**Winner**: Omnisend for multichannel. Practical: Combine email + SMS for 98% open rates on urgency campaigns like flash sales.

### Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp provides campaign ROI, unsubscribe trends, and Google Analytics integration.

Omnisend tracks revenue per channel, with ecommerce attribution (e.g., email-driven sales).

Both export CSV. Tip: Link to Google Analytics in settings for full-funnel insights.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp wins for non-tech users—setup takes minutes, with guided tours.

Omnisend requires store integration but offers video tutorials and 24/7 chat.

**Advice**: Mailchimp for solopreneurs; Omnisend if you're comfortable with Zapier for custom setups. Test both free plans side-by-side.

## Integrations and Apps

Mailchimp: 300+ native (Shopify, WordPress, Google Workspace) + Zapier.

Omnisend: Deep ecommerce focus (Shopify, Magento) + 140+ apps, including Facebook Lead Ads.

Both support webhooks. Tip: Use Zapier to connect either to CRMs like HubSpot for lead syncing.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket (paid plans), vast knowledge base.

Omnisend: 24/7 live chat/email, priority for Pro.

**Winner**: Omnisend for responsiveness. Advice: Join their Facebook groups for community tips.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Mailchimp if**:
- You're a beginner or service-based business.
- Budget < $20/mo initially.
- Need simple newsletters.

**Pick Omnisend if**:
- Running an online store.
- Want SMS/push.
- Prioritize automations.

Migration tip: Export CSV from one, import to the other. Re-tag segments to avoid data loss. Test a small campaign first.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce with superior automations and multichannel support, while Mailchimp remains the go-to for ease and affordability in general marketing. Assess your goals: revenue-focused? Omnisend. Quick setup? Mailchimp.

Both offer free trials—sign up today and run a test campaign. Track metrics like open rates (aim >25%) and conversions. Whichever you choose, consistent testing will maximize ROI. Need more help? Check their docs or comment below.
