# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate 2024 Comparison

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business or content creation efforts. For solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams deciding between MailerLite and ConvertKit, this head-to-head comparison breaks down key factors like pricing, features, usability, and support. Both tools excel in automation and audience growth, but one might suit your needs better based on budget and scale.

We'll dive into practical advice to help you decide without hype—just real insights from their official offerings as of 2024.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. MailerLite offers a generous free plan, making it ideal for startups testing the waters.

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes basic campaigns, forms, and automations.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $9/month (billed annually) for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 2,500 subs.
- **Advanced**: From $18/month annually for high-volume senders with extra features like A/B testing and web push notifications.

Practical tip: If you're under 1,000 subscribers, stick to the free tier to build your list without costs. Upgrade only when you hit limits.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit (rebranded elements but core same) focuses on creators with tiered plans:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (limited features like no advanced automations).
- **Creator**: Starts at $15/month (annually) for up to 500 subs, up to $29 for 1,000.
- **Creator Pro**: From $25/month annually, adding revenue tracking, subscriber scoring, and one-click upsells.

Practical tip: ConvertKit's pricing jumps faster for growing lists—calculate based on your projected growth. Annual billing saves 20% on both.

**Winner on Price**: MailerLite for beginners and budget-conscious users; ConvertKit if you need pro creator tools early.

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools for Growth

Both platforms handle emails, but differences shine in automation and segmentation.

### Email Campaigns and Design

**MailerLite**: Drag-and-drop editor with 80+ templates. Responsive designs, photo editing, and RSS-to-email campaigns. Supports plain text for authenticity.

**ConvertKit**: Visual builder with blocks for custom layouts. Strong for newsletters with inline images and purchase links.

Practical advice: Test MailerLite's editor if you want quick, mobile-first designs; ConvertKit for storytelling-focused newsletters.

### Automation and Sequences

**MailerLite**: Visual automation builder for workflows like welcome series, abandoned carts. Time delays, tags, and A/B splits.

**ConvertKit**: Excels here—'Sequences' for drip campaigns, tagging for segmentation, and 'Visual Automations' in Pro. Integrates with commerce for upsells.

Practical tip: Use ConvertKit sequences for course launches (e.g., Day 1: Intro email, Day 3: Bonus). MailerLite suffices for simpler e-commerce flows.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

**MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, popups, and forms on all plans. Drag-and-drop, A/B testing, and embedded options.

**ConvertKit**: Forms, landing pages, and checkouts. Pro adds recommendation blocks.

Practical advice: MailerLite's popups convert better for lead gen (e.g., exit-intent triggers). ConvertKit shines for product sales pages.

### Integrations and Analytics

Both integrate with Zapier, Stripe, WordPress. MailerLite adds Google Analytics tracking; ConvertKit has open/click reports and revenue per subscriber.

Practical tip: If e-commerce heavy, ConvertKit's commerce tools track earnings directly. For general analytics, MailerLite's heatmaps help optimize.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Daily Workflow

**MailerLite**: Intuitive dashboard, 5-minute setup. No steep learning curve—great for non-techies. Mobile app for campaigns.

**ConvertKit**: Clean interface but tagging system requires practice. Strong tutorials for creators.

Practical advice: Start with MailerLite if switching from Mailchimp (similar UI). ConvertKit rewards time investment for advanced segmentation.

User reviews (from G2/Capterra): MailerLite 4.6/5 (ease), ConvertKit 4.5/5.

## Customer Support and Community

**MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive help center. Free migration service.

**ConvertKit**: Email support (faster on paid), creator community, webinars.

Practical tip: MailerLite for immediate help; ConvertKit for peer learning via their Facebook group.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros**:
- Free plan generosity
- Affordable scaling
- All-in-one (sites, popups)

**Cons**:
- Less creator-specific (no subscriber scoring)
- Automation less visual than rivals

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:
- Powerful for courses/newsletters
- Revenue optimization tools
- Tagging mastery

**Cons**:
- Higher cost for small lists
- No phone support

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Bootstrapping, need free tools, focus on popups/landing pages, or simple automations. Ideal for bloggers/small e-com.
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Creator selling digital products, need sequences/upsells, or plan rapid growth.

Practical migration advice: Export CSV from current tool, import to trial accounts. Test a campaign with 10% of list first.

## Conclusion

MailerLite edges out for affordability and ease (perfect for 80% of new users), while ConvertKit dominates for serious creators willing to invest. Evaluate your subscriber count, automation needs, and budget—sign up for free trials today.

Both deliver 99% deliverability and GDPR compliance. Whichever you choose, consistent value-driven emails grow lists 20-30% yearly.
