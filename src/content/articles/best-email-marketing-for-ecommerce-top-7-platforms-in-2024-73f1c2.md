# Best Email Marketing for Ecommerce: Why It Matters in 2024

Email marketing remains one of the highest-ROI channels for ecommerce businesses, with average returns of $36 for every $1 spent according to DMA benchmarks. For online stores, it's essential for customer retention, abandoned cart recovery, and personalized promotions. If you're running a Shopify, WooCommerce, or BigCommerce store, choosing the right platform can make or break your campaigns.

This roundup focuses on the best email marketing for ecommerce, highlighting platforms with strong automations, segmentation, and integrations. We'll cover key features, pricing, pros/cons, and practical advice to help you select the ideal tool.

## Key Features to Prioritize for Ecommerce Email Marketing

When evaluating platforms, look for ecommerce-specific capabilities:

### Abandoned Cart Emails
Recover lost sales with automated sequences triggered by cart abandonment. Top tools integrate directly with your store to pull real cart data.

### Product Recommendations
Dynamic recommendations based on browsing history or past purchases increase AOV (average order value) by 10-30%.

### Advanced Segmentation
Segment by purchase history, location, or behavior for hyper-targeted campaigns.

### Integrations
Seamless connections to Shopify, WooCommerce, Klaviyo flows, SMS, and analytics tools like Google Analytics.

### Automation Workflows
Welcome series, win-back campaigns, and post-purchase upsells.

### Deliverability and Compliance
High inbox rates with dedicated IPs and GDPR/CCPA tools.

Practical tip: Start with platforms offering free trials or tiers to test integrations without commitment.

## Top 7 Best Email Marketing Platforms for Ecommerce

### 1. Klaviyo

Klaviyo is the gold standard for ecommerce email marketing, especially Shopify users. It excels in data-driven personalization using first-party customer data.

**Key Ecommerce Features:**
- Native Shopify integration syncs orders, products, and customer events.
- Advanced segments like "added to cart but didn't purchase."
- AI-powered flows for abandoned carts (recover 10-20% of lost sales).
- Product recommendation blocks with revenue tracking.

**Pricing:** Free up to 250 contacts and 500 emails; paid plans start at $20/mo for 500 contacts, scales by contacts sent.

**Pros:** Unmatched analytics, A/B testing, revenue attribution.
**Cons:** Steeper learning curve; costs rise quickly for large lists.

**Practical Advice:** Use Klaviyo's pre-built flows for quick wins. Track ROI via their dashboard to justify scaling.

### 2. Omnisend

Omnisend is built for ecommerce, combining email, SMS, and push notifications in one platform.

**Key Ecommerce Features:**
- One-click Shopify/WooCommerce setup.
- Omnichannel automations (e.g., cart abandonment via email + SMS).
- Pre-made ecommerce workflows like browse abandonment.
- Pop-up builder for list growth.

**Pricing:** Free for 250 contacts/500 emails; Standard at $16/mo for 500 contacts, unlimited emails.

**Pros:** Generous free plan, easy drag-and-drop editor, SMS included.
**Cons:** Fewer advanced analytics than Klaviyo.

**Practical Advice:** Pair SMS with email for 6x higher open rates on recovery campaigns. Segment by device for mobile-optimized sends.

### 3. Mailchimp

Mailchimp is a beginner-friendly staple with solid ecommerce tools via its integrations.

**Key Ecommerce Features:**n- Shopify app for product sync and abandoned carts.
- Customer Journey Builder for automations.
- Behavioral targeting and product blocks.

**Pricing:** Free up to 500 contacts/1,000 emails; Essentials at $13/mo for 500 contacts.

**Pros:** Intuitive interface, large template library.
**Cons:** Ecommerce features feel bolted-on; higher tiers needed for advanced segmentation.

**Practical Advice:** Ideal for startups. Use their ecommerce templates and test subject lines with multivariate testing.

### 4. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign shines in deep automations and CRM-like features for scaling ecommerce.

**Key Ecommerce Features:**
- Site tracking for behavior-based emails.
- WooCommerce/Shopify integrations.
- Machine learning for send times and predictions.

**Pricing:** Starts at $29/mo for 1,000 contacts.

**Pros:** Powerful if/then automations, site messaging.
**Cons:** No free plan; complex for beginners.

**Practical Advice:** Build win-back sequences for inactive customers (e.g., no purchase in 90 days). Integrate with your CRM for full-funnel tracking.

### 5. Drip

Drip is ecommerce-centric with a focus on revenue recovery.

**Key Ecommerce Features:**
- Frictionless Shopify integration.
- Workflow builder for upsells/cross-sells.
- Customer lifetime value scoring.

**Pricing:** Starts at $39/mo for 2,500 contacts.

**Pros:** Revenue-focused analytics.
**Cons:** Pricier for small lists.

**Practical Advice:** Use their revenue per visitor metric to optimize campaigns.

### 6. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo offers transactional + marketing emails affordably.

**Key Ecommerce Features:**
- Shopify sync, automation blueprints.
- SMS and WhatsApp.

**Pricing:** Free up to 300 emails/day; paid from $25/mo.

**Pros:** Unlimited contacts on some plans.
**Cons:** Editor less polished.

**Practical Advice:** Great for transactional emails like order confirmations.

### 7. GetResponse

GetResponse provides webinars + ecommerce tools.

**Key Ecommerce Features:**
- Abandoned cart add-on, product recommendations.

**Pricing:** From $19/mo for 1,000 contacts.

**Pros:** All-in-one with landing pages.
**Cons:** Weaker native integrations.

**Practical Advice:** Use for funnel nurturing pre-purchase.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing for Your Ecommerce Store

1. **Assess Your Tech Stack:** Shopify? Prioritize Klaviyo/Omnisend. WooCommerce? ActiveCampaign.
2. **List Size & Budget:** Free tiers for <1,000 contacts (Omnisend, Mailchimp).
3. **Test Integrations:** Spend a week migrating a test list.
4. **Measure Success:** Track open rates (25%+), click rates (2-3%), revenue per email ($0.50+).
5. **Compliance:** Ensure double opt-in and easy unsubscribes.

Practical tip: Benchmark against industry averages—ecommerce open rates average 18-25%. A/B test everything from send times (Tues-Thurs best) to personalization.

## Conclusion

For the best email marketing for ecommerce, Klaviyo leads for advanced users, while Omnisend wins for ease and value. Start with your pain points: cart recovery? Go Omnisend. Deep personalization? Klaviyo. Test 2-3 platforms via free trials, integrate fully, and monitor revenue impact.
