# Best Email Marketing Software 2024: Top 10 Picks for Businesses

Choosing the right email marketing software is crucial for businesses looking to nurture leads, boost engagement, and drive sales. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to pick the best email marketing software that fits your needs, budget, and scale. In this roundup, we evaluate the top 10 platforms based on real user feedback, key features like automation, deliverability, and integrations, and practical value for small businesses to enterprises.

We'll cover what to look for, detailed breakdowns of each tool, a comparison, and tips to select the ideal one. All recommendations are based on established platforms with proven track records—no hype, just facts to help you decide.

## What to Look for in the Best Email Marketing Software

The best email marketing software balances usability, power, and affordability. Key factors include:

- **Deliverability Rates**: High inbox placement (aim for 95%+).
- **Automation Capabilities**: Drip campaigns, behavioral triggers.
- **Template Library and Editor**: Drag-and-drop for non-coders.
- **List Management**: Segmentation, cleaning tools.
- **Analytics**: Open rates, clicks, revenue tracking.
- **Integrations**: With CRM like Salesforce, e-commerce like Shopify.
- **Pricing**: Free tiers or low entry for startups.
- **Support**: 24/7 chat, resources.

Consider your audience size (e.g., under 1,000 subscribers?) and goals (newsletters vs. sales funnels).

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp remains a leader for beginners due to its intuitive interface. It offers a forever-free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month. Features include drag-and-drop builder, A/B testing, and basic automations like welcome series.

**Pros**: Excellent templates (hundreds pre-built), audience insights, e-commerce integrations (Shopify, WooCommerce).
**Cons**: Advanced automation locked behind higher tiers; pricing jumps after free plan ($13/month for 500 contacts).

Ideal for small businesses sending newsletters. Deliverability is strong at ~90-95%.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign excels in automation and CRM integration, making it best email marketing software for sales teams. Starts at $29/month for 1,000 contacts.

**Key Features**: Advanced if/then automations, site tracking, machine learning for split testing.
**Pros**: Deep personalization, 4X ROI reported by users via detailed reporting.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve; no free plan.

Practical tip: Use its conditional content for dynamic emails that adapt to user behavior.

### 3. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo is affordable with transactional email strengths. Free plan: 300 emails/day. Paid from $25/month.

**Features**: Multichannel (SMS, WhatsApp), unlimited contacts on free, automation workflows.
**Pros**: High deliverability (99% claimed), easy segmentation.
**Cons**: Template editor less polished.

Great for e-commerce with built-in chat and ads.

### 4. ConvertKit

Tailored for creators and bloggers. Free up to 1,000 subscribers; $29/month after.

**Highlights**: Visual automations, landing pages, subscriber tagging.
**Pros**: Simple tagging over lists, commerce tools.
**Cons**: Lacks advanced reporting.

Tip: Tag subscribers by interests for hyper-targeted broadcasts.

### 5. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars. Starts at $19/month for 1,000 contacts.

**Features**: Conversion funnels, AI email generator, ecommerce tools.
**Pros**: 99% deliverability, strong A/B testing.
**Cons**: Overwhelming dashboard.

Use for full marketing suites.

### 6. Constant Contact

User-friendly for nonprofits/small biz. $12/month starter.

**Features**: Event management, social ads, AI text generator.
**Pros**: Mobile app, robust support.
**Cons**: No free plan, pricier for large lists.

Practical: Auto-resend to non-openers boosts opens by 10-20%.

### 7. AWeber

Veteran platform with strong deliverability. $19/month for 500 subscribers.

**Features**: RSS-to-email, landing pages, spam testing.
**Pros**: Loyal support, tag-based automation.
**Cons**: Dated interface.

Reliable for consistent senders.

### 8. Klaviyo

E-commerce powerhouse. Free up to 250 contacts; scales with revenue.

**Features**: Predictive analytics, dynamic product feeds.
**Pros**: Shopify native, revenue tracking.
**Cons**: Expensive for non-ecom.

Tip: Flow builder for abandoned cart recovery (recaptures 15-30%).

### 9. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free with CRM. Paid from $20/month.

**Features**: Smart content, full CRM.
**Pros**: Seamless with sales tools.
**Cons**: Limited standalone.

Best if using HubSpot ecosystem.

### 10. Moosend

Affordable automation. Free up to 1,000 subscribers; $9/month.

**Features**: Page builder, API access.
**Pros**: Unlimited emails on paid, fast setup.
**Cons**: Smaller community.

Great budget pick.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing Software for Your Needs

1. **Assess Your List Size**: Free plans suit <2,000 subs (Mailchimp, Brevo). Scale to ActiveCampaign for growth.
2. **Define Goals**: Newsletters? Mailchimp. Sales automation? ActiveCampaign/Klaviyo.
3. **Budget Check**: Start free, monitor costs per contact.
4. **Test Deliverability**: Use tools like Mail-Tester pre-launch.
5. **Trial Everything**: Most offer 14-30 day trials—import a test list, build a campaign.
6. **Integrate Early**: Ensure compatibility with your stack (e.g., WordPress plugins).
7. **Compliance**: GDPR/CCPA tools mandatory.

Practical advice: Segment lists (e.g., by engagement) to hit 30%+ open rates. Clean lists quarterly to maintain sender reputation. A/B test subjects for 20% uplift.

## Conclusion

The best email marketing software depends on your stage: Mailchimp for starters, ActiveCampaign for power users, Klaviyo for shops. Prioritize trials and match features to goals for maximum ROI. With email delivering $42 per $1 spent (DMA stats), investing time here pays off. Start with a free plan today and scale as you grow.
