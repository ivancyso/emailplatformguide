# Best Email Marketing Tools for Ecommerce in 2024: Top 7 Picks to Drive Sales

Email marketing remains one of the highest-ROI channels for ecommerce businesses, with average returns of $36 for every $1 spent according to DMA data. For online stores, the right platform handles abandoned cart recovery, personalized product recommendations, and post-purchase upsells seamlessly. If you're choosing an email marketing tool for ecommerce, focus on integrations with Shopify, WooCommerce, or BigCommerce, advanced segmentation, and automation flows.

This roundup covers the best email marketing for ecommerce based on real user reviews from G2 and Capterra, pricing transparency, and features tailored to scaling stores. We'll dive into each tool's strengths, practical use cases, and tips to implement them effectively.

## Why Email Marketing is Essential for Ecommerce Success

Ecommerce brands face high customer acquisition costs via paid ads, making retention critical. Email captures 20-30% of revenue for many stores through targeted campaigns. Key benefits include:

- **Abandoned cart emails**: Recover 10-15% of lost sales.
- **Win-back campaigns**: Re-engage lapsed customers.
- **Product recommendations**: Boost AOV by 20-30% using purchase history.

Choose platforms with ecommerce-specific templates, behavioral triggers, and analytics to track revenue per email (RPV).

## Top 7 Best Email Marketing Tools for Ecommerce

### 1. Klaviyo: Best Overall for Shopify Stores

Klaviyo excels in ecommerce with deep Shopify integration, pulling real-time customer data for hyper-personalized flows. Features include:

- Dynamic product feeds for recommendations.
- Advanced segmentation (e.g., by CLV, browsing behavior).
- A/B testing on subject lines and content.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts; paid starts at $20/mo for 500 contacts, scales with contacts and features.

**Practical Advice**: Set up an abandoned cart series: Email 1 at 1 hour (reminder), Email 2 at 24 hours (discount), Email 3 at 72 hours (urgency). Track open rates >25% and conversion >5% as benchmarks. Integrate with Google Analytics for full-funnel attribution.

**Pros**: Superior analytics (predicted CLV), SMS add-on.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve for beginners.

### 2. Omnisend: Best for Multichannel (Email + SMS)

Omnisend is built for ecommerce, offering pre-built workflows for carts, browses, and birthdays. It supports Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce.

Key features:

- Omnichannel campaigns (email, SMS, push).
- Pop-up builders for list growth.
- Revenue tracking per campaign.

**Pricing**: Free for 250 contacts; Standard at $16/mo, Pro at $59/mo.

**Practical Advice**: Use the "Wheel of Omnisend" popup to segment subscribers by interest (e.g., apparel vs. accessories). Run cart recovery SMS for 2x higher open rates than email. Aim for 1-2% list growth weekly via incentives like 10% off first purchase.

**Pros**: Intuitive automation builder, strong templates.
**Cons**: SMS costs extra per message.

### 3. Mailchimp: Best for Beginners and Small Stores

Mailchimp's user-friendly interface suits startups with basic ecommerce needs. It integrates with most platforms and offers Classic Automations.

Highlights:

- Customer Journey Builder for flows.
- Product recommendations block.
- Behavioral targeting.

**Pricing**: Free up to 500 contacts/1k emails; Essentials $13/mo.

**Practical Advice**: Start with the abandoned cart automation: Trigger on exit-intent, personalize with viewed items. Segment by purchase frequency (e.g., VIPs get exclusive previews). Test send times—midweek afternoons often perform best for ecommerce.

**Pros**: Generous free tier, easy migration.
**Cons**: Advanced segmentation locked behind higher plans.

### 4. ActiveCampaign: Best for Advanced Automation

ActiveCampaign shines in deep automations and CRM-like features, ideal for mid-sized ecommerce with complex customer journeys.

Features:

- Site tracking for behavior-based emails.
- Machine learning for send-time optimization.
- Split testing.

**Pricing**: Lite $29/mo for 1k contacts.

**Practical Advice**: Build a "post-purchase nurture" sequence: Thank-you email > upsell > review request > loyalty program invite. Use conditional content to show different offers based on past buys. Monitor engagement scores to clean inactive lists quarterly.

**Pros**: Powerful if/then logic.
**Cons**: Overkill for simple needs.

### 5. Drip: Best for High-Volume Personalization

Drip focuses on revenue recovery with ecommerce workflows and Lift for incrementality testing.

Standouts:

- Customer data platform integration.
- Workflow analytics.
- Shopify Plus certified.

**Pricing**: Starts at $39/mo for 2.5k subscribers.

**Practical Advice**: Implement "browse abandonment" emails with exact product images. Use revenue per recipient metric to prioritize top performers. A/B test urgency phrases like "Only 3 left" vs. "Limited stock."

**Pros**: Granular reporting.
**Cons**: No free plan.

### 6. Klaviyo Alternatives: Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo offers affordable transactional + marketing emails with ecommerce automations.

**Pricing**: Free up to 300 emails/day; paid from $25/mo.

**Practical Advice**: Pair transactional emails (shipping updates) with marketing for higher deliverability. Segment by cart value for tiered discounts.

**Pros**: Low cost, SMS included.
**Cons**: Fewer ecommerce templates.

### 7. Glew.io: Best for Analytics-Heavy Stores

Glew integrates email with full ecommerce analytics.

**Pricing**: Custom, starts ~$79/mo.

**Practical Advice**: Use cohort analysis to target lapsed segments with win-back offers.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing for Your Ecommerce Store

1. **Integrations**: Prioritize native support for your platform (e.g., Klaviyo for Shopify).
2. **Scalability**: Check contact-based vs. send-volume pricing.
3. **Deliverability**: Look for 95%+ rates; test with GlockApps.
4. **Support**: Live chat for Pro plans.
5. **Trial**: Most offer 14-30 day trials—import a test list.

Budget tip: Start free tiers, upgrade as list grows past 1k.

## Practical Tips for Ecommerce Email Campaigns

- **List Hygiene**: Remove bounces monthly; re-engagement for opens <10%.
- **Subject Lines**: Keep under 50 chars, use emojis sparingly (e.g., 🚀 Flash Sale).
- **Timing**: Send 8-10 AM recipient time zone.
- **Compliance**: Add one-click unsubscribe, GDPR forms.
- **Metrics to Track**: Open rate 20-30%, CTR 2-5%, Revenue per email $0.50+.
- **A/B Testing**: Rotate 2-3 variants weekly on high-volume sends.

Pro move: Sync with Google Optimize for landing page tests tied to emails.

## Conclusion

For the best email marketing for ecommerce, Klaviyo leads for advanced Shopify users, while Omnisend and Mailchimp suit multichannel beginners. Evaluate based on your store size, tech stack, and goals—most offer free trials to test ROI firsthand. Implement one automation today, like abandoned carts, to see quick wins. Focus on value-driven content to build long-term loyalty and scale revenue sustainably.
