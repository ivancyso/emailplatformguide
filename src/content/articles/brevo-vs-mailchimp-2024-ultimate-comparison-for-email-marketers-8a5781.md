# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses and marketers looking to grow their email lists. Both are popular platforms with robust features, but they cater to slightly different needs. This in-depth comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, integrations, support, and more to help you decide based on your specific goals, like audience size, budget, and technical expertise.

Whether you're a solopreneur starting out or scaling a team, we'll provide practical advice on when to choose each tool. Let's dive in.

## Quick Overview

**Brevo** is an all-in-one platform emphasizing transactional emails, marketing automation, SMS, and CRM. It's known for affordability and high deliverability, especially for e-commerce.

**Mailchimp** is a longstanding leader with intuitive drag-and-drop editors, strong audience insights, and a massive template library. It's ideal for beginners but can get pricey at scale.

Both offer free plans, making them accessible for testing.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is a top decision factor. Here's a practical breakdown based on current plans (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates).

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo uses a contact-based model with daily email limits on free tiers:
- **Free**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day (~9,000/month), basic automation.
- **Starter**: $25/month for 20,000 emails/month, up to 5,000 contacts.
- **Business**: $65/month for 20,000 emails, unlimited contacts, advanced features like A/B testing.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume needs.

Practical tip: If you send infrequent campaigns, Brevo's free plan scales better than Mailchimp's subscriber-limited free tier. Add-ons for SMS or chat are affordable (~$10-20/month).

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp charges per audience size:
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month, basic features.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts, 5,000 emails/month.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, advanced automation.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for 10,000+ contacts, full features.

Practical tip: Mailchimp jumps in cost as your list grows (e.g., $350 for 10k contacts). Great for small lists, but switch to Brevo if scaling without budget hikes.

**Winner**: Brevo for cost-effectiveness at scale; Mailchimp for tiny lists.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp shines with 100+ professional templates and a beginner-friendly drag-and-drop builder. Customize with dynamic content blocks.

Brevo offers 40+ responsive templates and a block-based editor. It's simpler but less visually polished out-of-the-box.

**Advice**: Use Mailchimp if design is your focus; Brevo if you prioritize speed.

### Automation Workflows
Both excel here:
- **Mailchimp**: Journey Builder for multi-step automations (e.g., abandoned cart, re-engagement).
- **Brevo**: Advanced workflows with triggers like web behavior, plus transactional emails integrated seamlessly.

**Advice**: E-commerce users pick Brevo for SMS/email combos; content creators love Mailchimp's behavioral triggers.

### Audience Management and Segmentation
Mailchimp's audience dashboard uses tags, groups, and predictive scoring for hyper-targeted sends.

Brevo's CRM syncs contacts automatically, with dynamic lists based on activity.

**Advice**: Segment lists weekly—both tools reduce unsubscribes by 20-30% with proper use.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp provides detailed reports on opens, clicks, revenue tracking.

Brevo tracks deliverability deeply (bounces, spam rates) and revenue per email.

**Advice**: Monitor open rates >20%; tweak subjects if below.

### Additional Features
- Brevo: Built-in SMS, WhatsApp, chat, landing pages.
- Mailchimp: E-commerce integrations, ads manager.

**Winner**: Tie—Brevo for multichannel; Mailchimp for marketing depth.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is polished and intuitive—no coding needed. Onboarding tutorials are excellent for newbies.

Brevo is straightforward but has a slight learning curve for automation setup. Mobile app is strong for both.

**Practical tip**: Start with Mailchimp's free trial if you're non-technical; Brevo if comfortable with basic tech.

## Integrations and API

Both integrate with 100+ tools:
- **Mailchimp**: Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics, Facebook Ads.
- **Brevo**: WooCommerce, Zapier, custom API for transactional sends.

**Advice**: Use Zapier for gaps. Brevo's API is developer-friendly for custom apps.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

**Support**:
- Mailchimp: Email/ticket for paid; chat for higher tiers.
- Brevo: 24/7 live chat/email, priority for paid.

**Deliverability**: Both >95%. Brevo edges out for transactional (IP warming tools); Mailchimp for marketing lists.

**Advice**: Warm up IPs gradually (start 100/day) to avoid spam folders.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo
**Pros**:
- Cheaper at scale.
- Multichannel (email + SMS).
- Strong transactional support.

**Cons**:
- Fewer templates.
- Free plan email cap.

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- User-friendly.
- Great analytics.
- Huge ecosystem.

**Cons**:
- Expensive for large lists.
- No native SMS.

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Brevo** if: E-commerce store, need SMS/transactionals, budget-conscious, >1k subscribers.
- **Choose Mailchimp** if: Beginner, content-focused, small list (<5k), prioritize design.

**Migration tip**: Export CSV from one, import to other—takes <1 hour. Test campaigns first.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp? Brevo wins for affordability and multichannel growth, while Mailchimp excels in ease and creativity. Assess your list size, send volume, and features needed. Start with free plans: sign up for both, import 100 contacts, run a test campaign. Track metrics for a week to decide.

For most growing businesses, Brevo offers better long-term value without sacrificing core functionality. Whichever you pick, consistent testing and list hygiene are key to 30%+ open rates.

Ready to choose? [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) or [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com).
