# Top Email Marketing Platforms for Nonprofits in 2024: Free Plans, Discounts & Donor Tools

Email marketing remains one of the most cost-effective ways for nonprofits to connect with donors, volunteers, and supporters. With tight budgets, nonprofits need platforms that offer generous free plans, steep discounts, or specialized features like donation integrations and compliance tools. This roundup focuses on proven email marketing platforms tailored for nonprofits, helping you choose the right one for your mission.

Whether you're running fundraising campaigns, event invitations, or newsletters, these tools prioritize high deliverability, easy segmentation, and automation to maximize impact without breaking the bank.

## Why Email Marketing is Essential for Nonprofits

Nonprofits rely on relationships. Email marketing allows you to nurture those relationships at scale. According to industry benchmarks from sources like Nonprofit Tech for Good, email boasts open rates of 25-30% for nonprofits—far higher than social media.

Key benefits include:
- **Cost Efficiency**: Reach thousands for pennies per email.
- **Measurable ROI**: Track opens, clicks, and donations directly.
- **Personalization**: Segment by donor history for targeted appeals.
- **Compliance**: Built-in tools for CAN-SPAM and GDPR adherence.

In 2024, with rising ad costs, email is your lifeline for recurring donations and volunteer mobilization.

## Key Features to Look for in Nonprofit Email Platforms

When selecting a tool, prioritize these features:

### Nonprofit-Specific Pricing
Many platforms partner with TechSoup or GuideStar for verification, offering 15-50% discounts or free tiers. Always check eligibility.

### Advanced Segmentation and Personalization
Tag donors by giving level, location, or engagement. Dynamic content swaps appeals based on past behavior.

### Automation Workflows
Set up welcome series for new subscribers, thank-you sequences post-donation, or lapsed donor re-engagement.

### Integrations
Connect with CRMs like Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud, donation platforms (Donorbox, Classy), or event tools (Eventbrite).

### Reporting and A/B Testing
Monitor campaign performance with revenue attribution and test subject lines for better opens.

### Mobile Optimization and Templates
Drag-and-drop builders with nonprofit-themed templates save time.

## Top 8 Email Marketing Platforms for Nonprofits

Here are the leading options, based on user reviews from G2, Capterra, and nonprofit forums. We've focused on verified nonprofit perks.

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp is a nonprofit favorite due to its user-friendly interface and TechSoup discounts (up to 25% off paid plans). Verified nonprofits get access to higher free limits: up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends on the free plan, with discounts scaling to enterprise.

**Pros**:
- Intuitive drag-and-drop editor.
- Strong automation for donor journeys.
- Integrates with 250+ apps, including DonorPerfect.

**Cons**:
- Free plan limits advanced features.
- Recent pricing changes reduced some perks.

Ideal for small nonprofits starting out. Practical tip: Use behavioral segmentation to target major donors with custom appeals.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo offers nonprofits unlimited contacts on free plans (300 emails/day) and 15% discounts via TechSoup. No subscriber cap on paid tiers makes it scalable.

**Pros**:
- Excellent deliverability (99%+).
- SMS and chat integrations for multi-channel.
- Advanced automation at no extra cost.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve for automations.

Great for mid-sized orgs. Tip: Automate post-event thank-yous with donation links to boost immediate giving.

### 3. Constant Contact

Constant Contact provides 20-50% nonprofit discounts and a 60-day free trial. It's praised for event management tools.

**Pros**:
- Mobile-optimized templates.
- Built-in event registration and RSVP tracking.
- AI-powered subject line generator.

**Cons**:
- Higher pricing post-discount.

Perfect for volunteer-driven nonprofits. Advice: A/B test event invites—personalized ones see 40% higher attendance.

### 4. ActiveCampaign

Discounts up to 20% for nonprofits. Powerful for complex automations.

**Pros**:
- Machine learning for predictive sending.
- Deep CRM integrations.
- Site tracking for engagement scoring.

**Cons**:
- Overkill for beginners.

For data-savvy teams. Tip: Score leads by engagement to prioritize high-value donors.

### 5. HubSpot

Free CRM with email marketing (2,000 sends/month free). Nonprofits get priority support and custom onboarding.

**Pros**:
- All-in-one CRM + email.
- Custom reporting dashboards.
- Free forever for basics.

**Cons**:
- Advanced features pricey.

Best for growing orgs. Integrate with fundraising pipelines for seamless tracking.

### 6. ConvertKit

20% lifetime discount for nonprofits. Creator-focused but adaptable.

**Pros**:
- Tagging and visual automations.
- Landing pages for campaigns.
- High deliverability.

**Cons**:
- Less robust reporting.

Suited for advocacy groups. Tip: Use broadcasts for urgent appeals with one-click donations.

### 7. Keap (formerly Infusionsoft)

Tailored for small nonprofits with sales pipelines. Discounts available.

**Pros**:
- Opportunity tracking for pledges.
- E-commerce for ticketed events.

**Cons**:
- Complex setup.

### 8. Moosend

Generous free plan (unlimited emails to 1,000 subs) and nonprofit outreach for custom deals.

**Pros**:
- Affordable scaling.
- Pop-up forms for list growth.

**Cons**:
- Fewer integrations.

## How to Choose the Right Platform for Your Nonprofit

1. **Assess Your Size**: Under 1,000 contacts? Start free with Mailchimp or Moosend.
2. **Budget Check**: Verify TechSoup eligibility for discounts.
3. **Test Integrations**: Ensure compatibility with your CRM/donation tools.
4. **Trial Run**: Most offer 14-60 day trials—import a sample list and simulate campaigns.
5. **Support Needs**: Look for nonprofit specialists (e.g., HubSpot's team).

Practical advice: Start with list hygiene. Remove bounces quarterly to protect sender reputation.

## Best Practices for Nonprofit Email Campaigns

- **Subject Lines**: Keep under 50 chars, use urgency ("Your Gift Doubled by 5 PM").
- **Content**: Storytelling over sales—share impact stories with photos.
- **Frequency**: Weekly newsletters, monthly appeals; monitor unsubscribes (<0.5%).
- **Calls to Action**: Clear buttons like "Donate Now" linking to secure pages.
- **Compliance**: Always include physical address, opt-out links.
- **Analytics**: Track not just opens, but revenue per email sent.

Example workflow: New subscriber → Welcome email with video → 30-day nurture → Year-end appeal.

## Conclusion

For nonprofits, the best email marketing platform balances affordability, ease, and power. Mailchimp and Brevo shine for beginners, while ActiveCampaign and HubSpot suit scaled operations. Prioritize tools with nonprofit pricing to stretch your dollars further.
