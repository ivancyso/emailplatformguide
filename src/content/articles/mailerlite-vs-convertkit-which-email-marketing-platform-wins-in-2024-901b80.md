# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business or content creation efforts. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams. Both are popular for their affordability and creator-friendly features, but they cater to slightly different needs.

In this head-to-head, we'll break down pricing, ease of use, key features like automations and segmentation, integrations, support, and more. Whether you're migrating from free tools or scaling up, this guide provides practical advice based on real user experiences and official docs. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer free plans, but their structures differ.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite starts free for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Paid plans begin at $10/month for 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20 for 1,000, and higher tiers like Advanced at $21/month for more features.

- **Key perks**: Unlimited emails on higher plans? No, but high limits. Includes websites, popups, and forms.
- **Practical tip**: Ideal if you're testing waters—upgrade seamlessly without data loss.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit's free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers with basic automations (now called Visual Automations). Creator plan starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, $29 for 1,000. Max plan at $79/month for 10,000 subs adds advanced commerce.

- **Key perks**: Native selling tools like paid newsletters.
- **Practical tip**: If revenue from emails is your goal, ConvertKit's commerce features justify the cost.

**Winner?** MailerLite for bootstrappers (cheaper entry), ConvertKit for revenue-focused creators. Always calculate based on your list size—use their calculators.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Interface

Both have intuitive dashboards, but let's compare.

### MailerLite's User-Friendly Design
Drag-and-drop editor is beginner-proof with 80+ templates. Campaign setup takes minutes. Automation builder uses blocks for if/then logic.

- **Pro**: Clean, no clutter. Mobile app for on-the-go.
- **Advice**: Start with their tutorials—import contacts in CSV easily.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Flow
Visual automations shine: drag subscriber journeys. Forms and landing pages are simple, but tagging system requires learning (subscribers get tags for segmentation).

- **Pro**: Intuitive for sequences (drip campaigns).
- **Advice**: Watch onboarding videos; tagging beats lists for personalization.

**Winner?** Tie—MailerLite for absolute newbies, ConvertKit for those comfortable with tags.

## Core Features: Emails, Automations, and Segmentation

### Email Builder and Templates
MailerLite: Responsive drag-and-drop, A/B testing standard. Rich text, images, JSON feeds for blogs.

ConvertKit: Block-based editor, strong for newsletters. Inline images, purchase buttons.

**Tip**: Test deliverability—both score high (MailerLite ~99%, ConvertKit similar).

### Automations

#### MailerLite Automations
Workflows trigger on signups, opens, clicks. Includes RSS-to-email, abandoned cart (with integrations).

- **H3 Strength**: Multi-step with delays, goals.
- **Advice**: Use for welcome series—set tag on signup, send sequence.

#### ConvertKit Automations
Visual Automations (formerly Sequences) are powerhouse: tag-based triggers, endless loops.

- **H3 Strength**: Re-engagement for inactive subs.
- **Advice**: Segment by tags (e.g., 'purchased') for hyper-targeted sends.

**Winner**: ConvertKit edges for complex creator funnels.

### Segmentation and Analytics
MailerLite: Groups, tags, custom fields. Analytics show opens, clicks, revenue tracking.

ConvertKit: Tag-based (powerful), broadcasts vs sequences. Reports track earnings.

**Tip**: For ecom, ConvertKit; for simple lists, MailerLite.

## Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

MailerLite bundles websites (landing pages), popups, forms—all unlimited on paid plans. Easy AB testing.

ConvertKit: Forms, landing pages, checkouts. No popups natively (integrate).

**Practical advice**: Need popups for lead gen? MailerLite wins. Selling digital products? ConvertKit's commerce.

## Integrations and Ecommerce

Both integrate with Zapier, WordPress, Shopify.

- MailerLite: 250+ native, Stripe for payments.
- ConvertKit: Gumroad, Teachable focus; commerce for subscriptions.

**Tip**: Check API docs for custom needs.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. High ratings (4.7/5 Capterra).

ConvertKit: Email support (priority on paid), Creator Network community, podcasts.

**Winner**: MailerLite for instant help.

### ConvertKit

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Small businesses, agencies needing websites/popups.
- Budget-conscious with <5k subs.
- **Advice**: Great for courses via integrations.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers, podcasters monetizing newsletters.
- Need tag-based personalization.

**Advice**: Perfect if 80% of revenue is email-driven.

## Migration and Testing Tips

Both allow easy imports (CSV). Test free plans:
1. Import 100 subs.
2. Build a campaign/automation.
3. Check analytics.

Use UTM tags for tracking signups.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit? No one-size-fits-all. MailerLite excels in affordability and all-in-one tools for growing lists. ConvertKit dominates for creators prioritizing automations and monetization.

Assess your needs: list size, revenue goals, tech comfort. Start free on both—most users pick after a week. Whichever you choose, focus on value: deliver helpful content to boost opens (aim 30%+).
