# MailerLite vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Tool Wins in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email list or scaling campaigns. Both are popular platforms trusted by small businesses, bloggers, and e-commerce stores. Mailchimp has long been a household name with its robust features, while MailerLite gains traction for its affordability and simplicity.

This head-to-head comparison dives into pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more. We'll provide practical advice based on real user needs, like starting with a free plan or automating abandoned cart emails. By the end, you'll know which tool fits your audience growth stage.

## Pricing Breakdown: MailerLite vs Mailchimp

Pricing is often the first deal-breaker. Both offer free plans, but they differ in limits and paid tiers. All prices are monthly, billed annually for discounts.

### MailerLite Pricing

- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups. Ideal for testing without commitment.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $9/month for 500 subscribers (scales to $89 for 50,000). Unlimited emails, advanced automation, A/B testing, and web push notifications.
- **Advanced**: From $23/month for 500 subscribers. Adds dedicated IP, 24/7 support, and custom domains.

MailerLite's strength? Predictable pricing based solely on subscribers—no extra fees for sends. Practical tip: If your list is under 1,000, stick to free and upgrade seamlessly as you grow.

### Mailchimp Pricing

- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month. Basic templates and landing pages, but limited automation (single-step only).
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts (up to $350 for 50,000). Always-on automation, behavioral targeting, and customer journey builder.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts. Adds advanced automation, A/B testing, and detailed reports.
- **Premium**: Starts at $350/month for 10,000 contacts. Includes multivariate testing and segmentation.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts *and* features, which can surprise users. Pro advice: Calculate your monthly sends first—Essentials caps delivery at 10x your list size.

**Winner?** MailerLite for budget-conscious users; Mailchimp for feature-heavy needs despite higher costs.

## Feature Comparison: What Can Each Do?

Both platforms cover email essentials, but depth varies.

### Email Editor and Templates

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 80+ responsive templates. Customize blocks easily—no coding needed. Practical use: Duplicate campaigns for A/B tests in seconds.

Mailchimp's editor shines with 100+ templates and merge tags for personalization. It's more visual but can feel cluttered. Tip: Use Content Studio for optimizing subject lines pre-send.

### Automation Workflows

MailerLite offers multi-step automations like welcome series or re-engagement. Trigger on tags, opens, or clicks. Free plan includes basics; paid unlocks RSS-to-email.

Mailchimp's automation is powerful: Customer Journeys map complex paths (e.g., cart abandonment + upsell). Free plan limits to one-step; Standard+ needed for branching.

Advice: Beginners start with MailerLite's simple flows; e-commerce pros pick Mailchimp.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

MailerLite bundles a website builder, popups, and forms (fixed/embedded). Convert visitors effortlessly—free plan includes unlimited.

Mailchimp has landing pages and forms, but popups require third-party integrations. Standard plan unlocks more customization.

### Analytics and Reporting

Both track opens, clicks, bounces. MailerLite adds revenue tracking and heatmap-style click maps. Mailchimp excels in comparative reports and ecommerce insights.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly?

MailerLite wins for simplicity. Clean dashboard, quick setup (import lists in minutes). No steep learning curve—perfect for solopreneurs juggling tasks.

Mailchimp's interface is feature-rich but busier. Navigation improves with use, but onboarding tutorials help. Practical tip: Use Mailchimp's free courses if switching from another tool.

User surveys (e.g., G2 ratings: MailerLite 4.6/5, Mailchimp 4.5/5) show MailerLite edging out for ease.

## Customer Support and Resources

MailerLite: Email/chat support (paid: 24/7 live chat), extensive knowledge base, and Facebook group. Response times: 1-2 hours.

Mailchimp: 24/7 email/phone (paid plans), huge help center, webinars. Free users get community forums only.

Pro tip: Test support pre-signup—both offer demos.

## Integrations and Deliverability

MailerLite integrates with 250+ apps (Stripe, Shopify, WordPress). Deliverability ~99% per their stats.

Mailchimp: 300+ integrations (Google Analytics, Facebook). Proven 99.5% deliverability, backed by scale.

Both comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM. Advice: Verify domain authentication early to boost inbox rates.

Case: An online store automates post-purchase upsells, tracking ROI via Mailchimp's ecommerce dashboard.
