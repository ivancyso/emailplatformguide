# Email Marketing Pricing Comparison 2024: Which Platform Wins for Your Budget?

Choosing the right email marketing platform isn't just about features—it's about value for money. With prices varying by subscriber count, send limits, and extras like automation, this 2024 comparison breaks down costs for popular tools. We'll focus on real pricing (as of mid-2024, check official sites for updates), key factors, and advice for businesses from startups to enterprises. Whether you're at 500 contacts or 50,000, find the best fit without overpaying.

## Key Factors to Consider Before Comparing Prices

Pricing isn't one-size-fits-all. Here's what impacts your choice:

- **Subscriber Count**: Most platforms charge based on contacts. Estimate growth to avoid mid-tier jumps.
- **Monthly Sends**: Free tiers limit emails; paid plans scale.
- **Features per Tier**: Basic lists vs. advanced automation, A/B testing, or e-commerce integrations.
- **Deliverability & Support**: Higher prices often mean better inbox rates and priority help.
- **Billing Flexibility**: Monthly vs. annual (10-20% discounts common).
- **Hidden Costs**: Add-ons like landing pages or SMS.

**Practical Tip**: Start with your monthly email volume and 6-month growth projection. Use free trials (most offer 14-30 days) to test real costs.

## Pricing Breakdown of Top Email Marketing Platforms

We'll compare entry-level paid plans for ~500-1,000 contacts, scaling to 10k. Data from official sites (prices USD/month, billed annually where applicable).

### Mailchimp

- **Free**: 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/mo, basic templates.
- **Essentials**: $13 (500 contacts, 5k sends), scales to $75 (10k contacts).
- **Standard**: $20 (500), $110 (10k), adds automation/retargeting.
- **Premium**: $350+ (10k), for large teams.

**Best For**: Beginners. Strong templates, but automation locked behind higher tiers. Watch send limits.

### ActiveCampaign

- **Lite**: $29 (500 contacts, unlimited sends), email automation.
- **Plus**: $49 (500), site tracking, machine learning.
- **Professional**: $149 (500), advanced splits.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

**Best For**: Automation-heavy users. Unlimited sends justify price for mid-sized lists. Excellent CRM integration.

### Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

- **Free**: 300 emails/day (~9k/mo), unlimited contacts.
- **Starter**: $25 (20k emails/mo, 5k contacts? Wait, volume-based: scales with sends).
- **Business**: $65 (20k), removes Brevo branding, API.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

**Best For**: High-volume senders on budgets. Transactional emails shine; no contact limits in free.

### ConvertKit (Creator-focused)

- **Free**: 300 subscribers (or <1k with annual), unlimited sends? Up to 10k sends.
- **Creator**: $15 (300 subs), broadcasts/sequences.
- **Creator Pro**: $25 (300), advanced tagging.

**Best For**: Solopreneurs/bloggers. Simple pricing, unlimited sends above free.

### GetResponse

- **Starter**: $19 (1k contacts), unlimited sends? 10 campaigns.
- **Marketer**: $59 (1k), funnel builder.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

**Best For**: All-in-one with webinars. Good for e-com.

### Klaviyo

- **Free**: 250 contacts/250 sends.
- **Email**: $20 (500 contacts), scales ~$0.03/contact avg.
- **SMS/Email**: Higher.

**Best For**: E-commerce. Revenue-based discounts.

### AWeber

- **Free**: None now; Lite $19 (500 contacts), unlimited sends.
- **Unlimited**: $149 (2.5k contacts).

**Best For**: Legacy users wanting simplicity.

## Best Platforms by Budget and Use Case

### Under $20/mo (Starters, <1k Contacts)
- **Top Pick**: Mailchimp or ConvertKit. Free tiers ease entry. Advice: Migrate if you hit limits—export is easy.

### $20-50/mo (Growing Lists, Automation)
- **Top Pick**: Brevo or GetResponse. Unlimited/ high sends without contact penalties. Tip: Prioritize deliverability (Brevo ~98%).

### $50+/mo (Advanced, E-com)
- **Top Pick**: ActiveCampaign or Klaviyo. ROI from automations pays off. Calculate: If one abandoned cart email saves $100, it's worth it.

**Practical Advice**:
1. **Audit Needs**: List must-haves (e.g., segmentation? Go ActiveCampaign).
2. **Test Deliverability**: Use tools like Mail-Tester during trials.
3. **Scale Smart**: Annual plans lock savings; negotiate enterprise.
4. **Avoid Traps**: Watch overage fees (rare but exist). Free tiers for testing.
5. **ROI Focus**: Track open rates (aim 20-30%); cheap tools with poor delivery cost more long-term.

## Common Pitfalls and Savings Tips

- **Overestimating Contacts**: Many overestimate; start low.
- **Feature Bloat**: Don't pay for unused webinars.
- **Switching Costs**: Data migration free, but time-intensive—plan 1-2 days.
- **Savings Hack**: Use promo codes (10-20% off first year common). Bundle with hosting.

**Pro Tip**: For agencies, white-label options (e.g., ActiveCampaign Partner) add revenue streams.

## Conclusion: Pick the Right Value, Not Just the Cheapest

Mailchimp wins for sheer ease and free start, ActiveCampaign for power users, Brevo for volume deals. Your best pick depends on contacts (under 5k? Budget options), sends (unlimited? ActiveCampaign), and goals (e-com? Klaviyo). Always trial, project 12 months ahead, and monitor ROI.
