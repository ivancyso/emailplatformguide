# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp—the two popular platforms—helps narrow it down. This comparison dives into pricing, features, usability, and more, tailored for small businesses, freelancers, and growing teams deciding between Brevo and Mailchimp.

Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling up, we'll break down real-world differences based on official specs, user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, and practical use cases. Let's get into it.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo is an all-in-one platform emphasizing affordability and multichannel marketing. It supports email, SMS, chat, and CRM features in one dashboard. Key strengths include unlimited contacts on paid plans and strong transactional email capabilities, making it ideal for e-commerce stores sending order confirmations alongside campaigns.

Brevo's free plan allows 300 emails per day with no contact limit, which suits testing without commitment. Paid plans start at $25/month for 20,000 emails. Users praise its drag-and-drop editor and automation workflows for nurturing leads.

Practical tip: If your business relies on transactional emails (e.g., receipts, password resets), Brevo's API integration shines, reducing reliance on separate tools.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, a veteran since 2001, is renowned for its intuitive interface and robust audience segmentation. It's popular among content creators, e-commerce brands, and agencies due to pre-built templates and behavioral triggers.

The free plan covers up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Essentials plan begins at $13/month for 500 contacts. Mailchimp excels in analytics like open rates and click tracking, with integrations for Shopify, WordPress, and Google Analytics.

Practical advice: For beginners, Mailchimp's onboarding tutorials make launching your first campaign straightforward—perfect if you're new to email marketing.

**Practical advice:** Calculate your needs. If you have 10,000 contacts and send monthly newsletters, Brevo might save 30-50% vs Mailchimp. Use their pricing calculators for precision.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Templates and Design
Both offer drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp has 100+ responsive templates optimized for mobile. Brevo provides similar customization with A/B testing built-in on all plans.

Winner: Tie for most users, but Brevo edges for transactional templates.

### Automation and Workflows
Mailchimp's automation is tag-based and journey-focused, great for e-commerce (abandoned cart emails). Brevo's workflows include wait steps and SMS triggers, supporting multi-channel sequences.

Practical tip: Test automations with small segments first. For drip campaigns, Brevo's unlimited contacts prevent overage fees.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp leads with behavioral segmentation (e.g., purchase history). Brevo uses custom fields and dynamic content effectively.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp offers detailed heatmaps and revenue tracking. Brevo provides real-time stats and ROI calculators.

### Integrations
Mailvo (Brevo) connects to 300+ apps via Zapier; Mailchimp to 1,500+ natively, including advanced e-com tools.

**Practical advice:** Prioritize integrations matching your stack. Shopify users lean Mailchimp; general CRM users prefer Brevo.

## Ease of Use: Brevo vs Mailchimp

Mailchimp's interface feels polished and beginner-friendly, with a creative vibe. Brevo is functional but slightly more technical, rewarding experienced users.

G2 ratings: Mailchimp 4.5/5 for ease; Brevo 4.4/5. Switchers from Mailchimp note Brevo's learning curve but appreciate its power.

**Tip:** Start with free trials (both offer 14-30 days). Import a test list to build a campaign and gauge comfort.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Both maintain high deliverability (95%+). Brevo's dedicated IPs on higher plans boost this for large volumes. Mailchimp includes domain authentication setup.

GDPR compliance is standard. Brevo's EU base appeals to privacy-focused users.

Practical advice: Warm up new domains gradually (start with 100 sends/day) to avoid spam filters on either platform.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for paid; chat for higher tiers. Brevo: 24/7 chat/email on Starter+, knowledge base for all.

User reviews favor Brevo's responsiveness, especially for non-US hours.

- Budget-conscious SMBs
- High-volume transactional senders

Example: An online store sending 50,000 emails/month saves $200+ yearly with Brevo.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- E-commerce with complex journeys
- Design-focused creators
- Teams needing deep analytics

Practical tip: If revenue tracking is key, Mailchimp's e-com reports justify the cost.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to priorities: Brevo wins on affordability and multichannel for growing businesses; Mailchimp on polish and e-com depth for creatives. Neither is "best" universally—audit your volume, features, and budget.

**Next steps:** Sign up for free trials, migrate a small list, and A/B test campaigns. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) over 30 days. For most solopreneurs, start with Brevo; agencies often stick with Mailchimp.
