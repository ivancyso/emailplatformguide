# Best Email Marketing for Ecommerce: Top 7 Tools to Boost Sales in 2024

Email marketing remains one of the most effective channels for ecommerce businesses. With average ROI often cited around 42:1 by industry benchmarks from sources like Litmus and DMA, it's no wonder store owners prioritize it. For ecommerce specifically, tools that handle abandoned carts, product recommendations, and customer segmentation shine brightest.

If you're choosing an email marketing platform, focus on integrations with Shopify, WooCommerce, or BigCommerce; automation capabilities; and ecommerce-specific templates. This roundup covers the best email marketing for ecommerce based on real user feedback from G2, Capterra, and official docs—no hype, just facts.

## Why Ecommerce Needs Specialized Email Marketing Tools

Standard newsletters won't cut it for online stores. Ecommerce demands dynamic content like personalized product feeds, behavioral triggers (e.g., post-purchase upsells), and SMS integration for higher open rates.

Key features to look for:
- **Abandoned cart recovery**: Automates emails to recapture 10-20% of lost sales on average.
- **Segmentation**: Based on purchase history, browsing behavior, or location.
- **Integrations**: Seamless with your CMS to pull real-time inventory.
- **Analytics**: Revenue tracking per campaign.

Practical tip: Start with your store's pain points. High cart abandonment? Prioritize recovery flows. Low repeat purchases? Focus on win-back sequences.

## Top 7 Best Email Marketing Tools for Ecommerce

### 1. Klaviyo: The Ecommerce Powerhouse

Klaviyo is built for ecommerce, with deep Shopify and Magento integrations. It excels in data-driven flows like abandoned carts (users report 15-30% recovery rates) and revenue-focused automations.

**Key Features**:
- Predictive analytics for churn prediction.
- One-click integrations with 350+ apps.
- A/B testing on subject lines and send times.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts; paid starts at $20/mo for 500 contacts, scales with contacts.

**Pros**: Superior segmentation; tracks revenue attribution accurately.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve for beginners.

Best for: Growing Shopify stores. Setup tip: Enable Facebook pixel sync for cross-channel tracking.

### 2. Omnisend: All-in-One for Multichannel

Omnisend combines email, SMS, and push notifications, ideal for ecommerce pop-ups and cart recovery.

**Key Features**:
- Pre-built workflows for welcome series, browsed-product emails.
- Omnichannel reporting.
- Free plan with unlimited emails.

**Pricing**: Free for 500 emails/mo; Standard at $16/mo for 500 contacts.

**Pros**: User-friendly drag-and-drop; strong WooCommerce support.
**Cons**: Advanced segmentation requires Pro plan ($59/mo).

Practical advice: Use their popup builder to grow lists by 20-30% via exit-intent offers.

### 3. Mailchimp: Reliable Starter Option

Mailchimp is ubiquitous, with solid ecommerce automations via its integrations.

**Key Features**:
- Customer Journey Builder for tagging-based automations.
- Product recommendation blocks.
- Basic abandoned cart (with Shopify connect).

**Pricing**: Free up to 500 contacts/1,000 emails; Essentials $13/mo.

**Pros**: Intuitive interface; huge template library.
**Cons**: Ecommerce features lag behind specialists; per-email pricing can add up.

Tip: Leverage behavioral targeting with purchase data sync for repeat buyer nurturing.

### 4. ActiveCampaign: Automation Experts

ActiveCampaign focuses on deep automations, great for complex ecommerce funnels.

**Key Features**:
- Site tracking for behavior-based emails.
- Machine learning for send-time optimization.
- CRM integration for full customer profiles.

**Pricing**: Starts at $29/mo for 1,000 contacts.

**Pros**: Powerful if/then logic; splits tests everything.
**Cons**: No native SMS; interface feels dated.

Advice: Build a post-purchase flow: Thank you > upsell > review request > loyalty.

### 5. Drip: Revenue-Focused for DTC Brands

Drip targets direct-to-consumer ecommerce with customer data platforms.

**Key Features**:
- Workflow builder with revenue per email tracking.
- Dynamic yield for personalized content.
- Shopify/BigCommerce apps.

**Pricing**: Starts at $39/mo for 2,500 subscribers.

**Pros**: Excellent for segmentation by lifetime value.
**Cons**: No free plan; higher cost.

Pro tip: Use their scoring to prioritize high-value segments.

### 6. Klaviyo Alternatives: Brevo (Sendinblue)

Brevo offers transactional emails alongside marketing, cost-effective for scaling stores.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts on pay-per-email.
- Automation with ecommerce triggers.
- SMS add-on.

**Pricing**: Free up to 300 emails/day; Starter $25/mo.

**Pros**: Affordable scaling; API flexibility.
**Cons**: Templates less polished.

Tip: Ideal for international stores with multi-language support.

### 7. Glew.io: Analytics-Driven

Glew integrates marketing with full ecommerce analytics.

**Key Features**:
- Automated campaigns based on CLV.
- Multi-channel attribution.

**Pricing**: Custom, starts ~$79/mo.

**Pros**: Holistic dashboard.
**Cons**: Less standalone email focus.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing for Your Ecommerce Store

1. **Assess Your Tech Stack**: Shopify? Klaviyo or Omnisend. WooCommerce? Omnisend or ActiveCampaign.
2. **Budget Check**: Under $50/mo? Mailchimp or Omnisend free tiers.
3. **List Size**: Small? Free plans. Large? Contact-based like Klaviyo.
4. **Test Integrations**: Most offer 14-30 day trials—import a test list and build one flow.

Practical framework:
- Week 1: Migrate lists and test sync.
- Week 2: Launch abandoned cart (expect 10% lift).
- Ongoing: A/B test subjects (emojis boost opens 5-10%).

## Practical Tips for Ecommerce Email Success

### H3: Master Segmentation
Divide by RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary): New buyers get onboarding; lapsed get win-back with 40% off.

### H3: Optimize Timing
Sends Tuesdays-Thursdays, 8-10 AM user timezone. Tools like Klaviyo auto-optimize.

### H3: Compliance First
GDPR/CCPA: Use double opt-in; include clear unsubscribes. Avoid purchased lists.

### H3: Content Best Practices
- Subject lines: 6-10 words, personalized ("Hey [Name], your cart awaits").
- Mobile-first: 80% opens on phone.
- Value over sales: 80/20 educational-to-promotional ratio.

Track opens, clicks, revenue—not just opens. Aim for 20-30% open rates, 2-4% clicks.
