# Top Mailchimp Alternatives in 2023: 8 Best Options for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're facing high fees, need advanced automation, or want better deliverability, switching platforms can save money and boost results.

In this roundup, we evaluate eight proven **Mailchimp alternatives** based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, official pricing pages, and feature comparisons. We'll cover key features, pricing, pros/cons, and practical tips for each. Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or agency, there's an option here for you.

## Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but jump quickly—e.g., $299/month for 50,000 contacts. Alternatives often offer unlimited sends, better segmentation, and e-commerce integrations at lower costs.

Common pain points:
- **Pricing opacity**: Bills can surprise with overages.
- **Automation limits**: Basic journeys on lower tiers.
- **Deliverability**: Some report drops post-2023 updates.

Switching makes sense if your list exceeds 2,000 or you need CRM-like features. Pro tip: Export your Mailchimp list as CSV before testing alternatives—most import seamlessly.

## 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
Brevo combines email, SMS, chat, and CRM in one dashboard. Standout: Unlimited contacts on paid plans, transactional emails, and automation workflows with A/B testing.

### Pricing
Free: 300 emails/day. Paid from $25/month (20k emails). Scales affordably.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Generous free tier, excellent deliverability (98%+), EU GDPR compliant.
**Cons**: Interface can feel cluttered for beginners.

**Best for**: SMBs with transactional needs. Practical advice: Use their drag-and-drop editor for quick campaigns; integrate with WordPress via plugin for auto-sync.

## 2. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
Advanced automation, machine learning for predictions, site tracking, and 900+ integrations (Shopify, Salesforce).

### Pricing
Starts at $29/month (500 contacts). Lite plan lacks reporting.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Powerful CRM + email, split testing, conditional content.
**Cons**: Steep learning curve; no free plan beyond trial.

**Best for**: Marketers needing deep personalization. Tip: Set up "if/then" automations for cart abandonment—users report 30% recovery rates.

## 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

### Key Features
Creator-focused: Visual automations, landing pages, subscriber scoring, and commerce tools.

### Pricing
Free up to 1,000 subs. Creator plan $29/month.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Simple for newsletters, unlimited emails, great support.
**Cons**: Lacks robust e-commerce analytics.

**Best for**: Bloggers/content creators. Advice: Tag subscribers by behavior (e.g., opened email 3x) for targeted sequences—boosts open rates by 20-40%.

## 4. Klaviyo

### Key Features
E-commerce powerhouse: Revenue tracking, dynamic product feeds, A/B testing, and Facebook/Google flow builders.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails. Paid from $20/month based on contacts.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Shopify integration gold standard, predictive analytics.
**Cons**: E-com only shines; basic for non-stores.

**Best for**: Online stores. Pro tip: Enable flow analytics to optimize welcome series—Klaviyo users see 6x ROI on average.

## 5. GetResponse

### Key Features
Email, webinars, funnels, landing pages, and AI email generator.

### Pricing
Free trial; starts $19/month (1,000 contacts).

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: All-in-one, conversion funnels, high deliverability.
**Cons**: Webinars extra on basic plans.

**Best for**: Agencies scaling campaigns. Advice: Use autoresponders for lead nurturing; test subject lines with their tool for 15%+ open lifts.

## 6. AWeber

### Key Features
Reliable sending, tagging, RSS-to-email, and e-commerce integrations.

### Pricing
Free up to 500 subs. $19/month after.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: 99% deliverability, easy migrations, 24/7 support.
**Cons**: Dated interface, fewer automations.

**Best for**: Small businesses prioritizing reliability. Tip: Schedule RSS campaigns for consistent newsletters without daily effort.

## 7. Constant Contact

### Key Features
Event management, social ads, AI recommendations, mobile app.

### Pricing
$12/month (500 contacts). No free plan.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Beginner-friendly, robust templates, local business tools.
**Cons**: Pricey for large lists.

**Best for**: Non-profits/retail. Advice: Leverage contact management for segmented lists—reduces unsubscribes by 25%.

## 8. Moosend

### Key Features
Unlimited emails, automation, A/B testing, page builder, API.

### Pricing
Free up to 1,000 subs. $9/month pro.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Budget-friendly, fast setup, e-com focus.
**Cons**: Newer player, fewer integrations.

**Best for**: Startups. Tip: Use behavioral triggers for hyper-personalization.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess needs**: List size? E-com? Automation depth?
2. **Trial platforms**: Most offer 14-30 days free.
3. **Check integrations**: Zapier bridges gaps.
4. **Migrate**: Export CSV from Mailchimp > Import > Map tags. Test sends.
5. **Warm IP**: Start small to build sender reputation.

Budget tip: Under 5k subs? Brevo/Moosend save 50%+ vs. Mailchimp.

## Conclusion

The best **Mailchimp alternative** depends on your goals—Klaviyo for shops, ActiveCampaign for pros, Brevo for value. All outperform Mailchimp in key areas like cost or features for scaling users. Start with a free trial, import your list, and track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%). Switching unlocks better ROI—many report 2-3x engagement post-move.
