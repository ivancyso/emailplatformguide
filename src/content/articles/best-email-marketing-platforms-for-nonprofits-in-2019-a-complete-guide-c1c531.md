# Best Email Marketing Platforms for Nonprofits in 2019: A Complete Guide

Email marketing remains one of the most cost-effective ways for nonprofits to connect with donors, volunteers, and supporters. In 2019, with tightening budgets and increasing competition for donations, choosing the right email marketing platform is crucial. This guide focuses on **email marketing for nonprofits**, highlighting platforms that offer affordable pricing, nonprofit discounts, and features tailored to mission-driven organizations.

We'll cover key features to prioritize, top platforms with real-world pros and cons, a comparison table, practical setup advice, and compliance tips. Whether you're a small community group or a larger charity, these insights will help you select a tool that maximizes ROI without breaking the bank.

## Why Email Marketing Matters for Nonprofits in 2019

Nonprofits rely on email to drive 80% of online donations, according to industry benchmarks from sources like the Nonprofit Tech for Good report. It's direct, personal, and measurable. In 2019, amid economic shifts and digital fatigue, effective email campaigns can increase open rates by 20-30% through segmentation and automation.

Key benefits include:
- **Low cost per engagement**: Often under $0.01 per email sent.
- **High ROI**: For every $1 spent, nonprofits see $42 in return (DMA data).
- **Donor retention**: Personalized emails boost repeat giving by 25%.

However, nonprofits face unique challenges: limited funds, strict compliance (e.g., CAN-SPAM Act), and the need for donor-centric features like suppression lists for unsubscribes.

## Essential Features for Nonprofit Email Marketing Platforms

When evaluating **email marketing for nonprofits**, prioritize these features:

### 1. Nonprofit Discounts and Free Tiers
Many platforms verify nonprofit status via GuideStar or similar to offer 10-20% discounts or free plans for small lists (under 2,000 subscribers).

### 2. Donor Segmentation and Personalization
Tag contacts as 'major donors,' 'volunteers,' or 'lapsed supporters' for targeted campaigns. Dynamic content blocks adapt based on donor history.

### 3. Automation Workflows
Set up welcome series for new subscribers, re-engagement for inactive donors, or year-end appeals.

### 4. Integrations
Connect with donation platforms like Donorbox, Classy, or CRMs like Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud.

### 5. Reporting and Analytics
Track open rates, click-throughs, and donation attribution. Look for A/B testing to optimize subject lines.

### 6. Deliverability and Compliance Tools
Built-in CAN-SPAM footers, GDPR consent forms, and spam testers ensure emails land in inboxes.

## Top Email Marketing Platforms for Nonprofits in 2019

Here are five standout platforms based on affordability, ease of use, and nonprofit-friendly features. All offer free trials.

### 1. Mailchimp
Mailchimp is the go-to for 2019 nonprofits due to its free plan (up to 2,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month) and 15% discount for verified nonprofits.

**Pros**:
- Intuitive drag-and-drop editor with nonprofit templates (e.g., fundraising thermometers).
- Automation for donor nurturing.
- Integrates with Zapier, Google Analytics, and donation tools.

**Cons**:
- Advanced segmentation requires paid plans ($10+/month).
- Customer support is email-only on free tier.

Ideal for startups like local food banks.

### 2. Constant Contact
Known for user-friendliness, Constant Contact offers nonprofit pricing starting at $5/month for small lists with discounts up to 30%.

**Pros**:
- 100+ mobile-responsive templates optimized for events and appeals.
- Event management integration for volunteer sign-ups.
- Robust reporting with donation tracking links.

**Cons**:
- No free plan; minimum $20/month without discount.
- Limited automation compared to competitors.

Great for mid-sized orgs hosting galas.

### 3. Sendinblue (Now Brevo)
Affordable at $25/month for 20,000 emails, with a free plan for 300 emails/day. Nonprofits get transactional email perks.

**Pros**:
- Excellent deliverability (99%+ rates).
- Advanced automation and SMS add-ons for multi-channel campaigns.
- CRM integration for donor profiles free.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve for beginners.
- Design templates are basic.

Suits data-driven nonprofits like environmental groups.

### 4. Benchmark Email
Offers 33% nonprofit discounts, free up to 250 subscribers.

**Pros**:
- Simple editor with RSS-to-email for newsletters.
- A/B testing and polls for engagement.
- One-click Google Analytics integration.

**Cons**:
- Fewer integrations than Mailchimp.
- Support can be slow.

Perfect for volunteer newsletters.

### 5. ActiveCampaign
Starts at $9/month, with nonprofit discounts via application. Focuses on automation.

**Pros**:
- Powerful site tracking and lead scoring for donor behavior.
- Deep CRM features for long-term relationships.
- Conditional content for personalized appeals.

**Cons**:
- Overkill for very small teams.
- Higher cost for large lists ($49+/month).

Best for growing orgs with complex donor journeys.

## Practical Advice: Implementing Email Marketing for Nonprofits

### Getting Started
1. **Verify Nonprofit Status**: Use TechSoup or GuideStar for discounts.
2. **Build Your List Legally**: Use double opt-in forms on your website. Offer lead magnets like impact reports.
3. **Segment Early**: Categorize by donation amount, engagement level, and location.

### Campaign Best Practices
- **Subject Lines**: Keep under 50 chars, e.g., "Your $50 Helped 10 Families – Thank You!"
- **Content Tips**: 60/40 story-to-ask ratio. Use images sparingly (under 100KB total).
- **Timing**: Send Tuesdays-Thursdays, 8-10 AM donor time zones.
- **A/B Test**: Rotate two versions on 20% of list.

### Measuring Success
Track metrics: Open rate >25%, CTR >3%, donation conversion >1%. Use UTM tags for Google Analytics.

### Compliance Checklist
- Include physical address and unsubscribe link.
- Honor unsubscribes within 10 days.
- For EU donors, add GDPR consent checkboxes.

### Scaling Up
Integrate with tools like Google for Nonprofits or DonorPerfect. Budget $50-200/month for 5,000-20,000 subs.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Sending to cold lists: Results in <10% opens.
- Ignoring mobile: 50%+ opens on phones.
- No follow-ups: Single emails convert 2x less than series.

## Conclusion
In 2019, **email marketing for nonprofits** empowers organizations to thrive on limited budgets. Mailchimp leads for beginners, while ActiveCampaign suits advanced needs. Start with a free trial, focus on segmentation and automation, and always prioritize donor trust.

Test two platforms, track your first campaign's ROI, and scale what works. Your mission deserves tools that amplify impact – choose wisely to turn emails into enduring support.
