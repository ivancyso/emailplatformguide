# Omnisend Review 2024: Features, Pricing, and Is It Right for Your Ecommerce Store?

If you're shopping for an email marketing platform tailored to ecommerce, you've likely come across Omnisend. In this comprehensive Omnisend review, we'll dive deep into its features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance. Designed for online stores, especially those on Shopify and other platforms, Omnisend stands out with its omnichannel approach, blending email, SMS, and push notifications.

Whether you're a small business owner scaling your store or a marketer evaluating tools, this review provides practical insights to help you decide if Omnisend fits your needs. We'll cover setup tips, automation strategies, and comparisons without hype or unsubstantiated claims.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an ecommerce-focused marketing automation platform launched in 2014. It specializes in helping online retailers nurture customer relationships through targeted campaigns. Unlike generalist tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend pulls data directly from your store (e.g., Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce) to create hyper-personalized messages.

Key differentiator: Omnichannel support. You can send emails, SMS, web push notifications, and popups from one dashboard. It serves over 70,000 brands, with a free plan for startups.

**Practical Advice:** Start by connecting your ecommerce platform during signup. Omnisend auto-syncs customer data, products, and orders, saving hours of manual import.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs robust features for ecommerce marketers. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Campaigns and Templates

Create drag-and-drop emails with 250+ responsive templates optimized for product blocks, carousels, and dynamic content. Use product feeds to insert real-time inventory.

**Pro Tip:** Segment by purchase behavior (e.g., abandoned carts) for 20-30% higher open rates. Test subject lines A/B style—Omnisend's editor makes it simple.

### Automations

Pre-built workflows for welcome series, abandoned carts, back-in-stock alerts, and post-purchase upsells. Custom automations trigger on events like page views or tags.

**Practical Setup Guide:**
1. Go to Automations > Create Workflow.
2. Choose a trigger (e.g., "Cart abandoned >1 hour").
3. Add branches for SMS fallback if email fails.
4. Set delays and personalize with {{first_name}} and {{product_name}} variables.

Recovery rates for carts average 10-15% with these, per Omnisend's benchmarks.

### SMS and Push Notifications

SMS campaigns deliver high open rates (98%). Push notifications work for web/app without downloads.

**Advice:** Use SMS sparingly for high-urgency (e.g., flash sales) to avoid opt-out fatigue. Comply with TCPA by getting consent.

### Popups and Forms

Wheel of fortune, spin-to-win, or exit-intent popups capture emails. List-building forms embed anywhere.

**Optimization Tip:** Target new visitors only; set popup delay to 5-10 seconds for 5-10% conversion boosts.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Hyper-segment by behavior, RFM (recency, frequency, monetary), or custom tags. Reports track revenue per email, ROI, and unsubscribe rates.

**Insight:** Use the Revenue Chart to attribute sales—expect 40x ROI on automations for engaged lists.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

**Pros:**
- Ecommerce-native integrations (Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives).
- Intuitive interface; no coding needed.
- Strong automation ROI for DTC brands.
- 24/7 support via chat/email.

**Cons:**

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for:
- Shopify/WooCommerce stores under 50k contacts.
- Brands prioritizing automations over complex journeys.
- Marketers wanting email + SMS without multiple tools.

Not for: Agencies needing white-label or non-ecom businesses.

**Case Study Tip:** Brands like Steve Madden report 300% revenue lifts from cart recovery.
