# Top Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: 10 Best Email Marketing Platforms for Growing Businesses

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its rising prices, limited automation for free users, and recent pricing changes have many businesses seeking Mailchimp alternatives. If you're frustrated with deliverability issues, complex interfaces, or costs that scale too quickly, it's time to explore options that offer better value, advanced features, and scalability.

This roundup focuses on proven Mailchimp alternatives tailored for small to medium businesses, e-commerce stores, and creators. We'll cover key features, pricing, pros, cons, and practical advice for switching. All data is based on official sites and user reviews as of 2024.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Before diving into alternatives, consider these common pain points:

- **Pricing**: Mailchimp's free plan limits you to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month. Paid plans start at $13/month but jump to $75+ for 5,000 contacts.
- **Automation**: Basic workflows lack depth compared to competitors.
- **Deliverability**: Recent reports show dips below 85% in some cases.
- **E-commerce Integration**: Not as robust for Shopify or WooCommerce users.

Switching can save 30-50% on costs while unlocking better segmentation, A/B testing, and analytics. Practical tip: Export your Mailchimp list via CSV before migrating—most tools import seamlessly.

## 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo stands out for its all-in-one marketing suite with transactional emails baked in.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts on free plan (300 emails/day).
- Advanced automation, SMS, chat, and CRM.
- Drag-and-drop editor with 40+ templates.

**Pricing**: Free; paid from $25/month (20,000 emails).

**Pros**: Affordable scaling, high deliverability (95%+), multi-channel.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve for beginners.

**Best For**: E-commerce with transactional needs. Migrate by connecting your domain for authentication—improves deliverability instantly.

## 2. ActiveCampaign

A powerhouse for automation, ActiveCampaign excels in behavioral triggers.

**Key Features**:
- 870+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress).
- Machine learning for send times.
- Site tracking and split testing.

**Pricing**: Starts at $29/month (500 contacts).

**Pros**: Deep personalization, CRM included.
**Cons**: Overwhelming for simple newsletters.

**Best For**: Agencies and SaaS. Tip: Use conditional content blocks for dynamic emails based on user behavior.

## 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Ideal for creators, ConvertKit focuses on simplicity and subscriptions.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automations.
- Tagging and segments.
- Native landing pages and forms.

**Pricing**: Free up to 300 subscribers; $29/month for 1,000.

**Pros**: Creator-friendly, one-click upsells.
**Cons**: Limited reporting.

**Best For**: Bloggers/podcasters. Advice: Tag subscribers by content opens to nurture leads effectively.

## 4. Klaviyo

E-commerce king with data-driven flows.

**Key Features**:
- Predictive analytics.
- 350+ integrations.
- A/B testing on subject lines.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts; scales by sends ($20+).

**Pros**: 92%+ deliverability, revenue tracking.
**Cons**: E-com focused, pricey for non-shops.

**Best For**: Shopify stores. Pro tip: Set up abandoned cart flows—recovers 10-15% lost sales.

## 5. GetResponse

Balanced option with webinars and funnels.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

### Step 1: Assess Your Needs
- List size? Automation depth? Integrations?
- Budget: Aim for $10-50/month for starters.

### Step 2: Compare Deliverability and Compliance
Check tools' sender scores on sites like Mail-Tester. Ensure GDPR/CCPA support.

### Step 3: Test with Trials
Most offer 14-30 day trials. Send test campaigns to your list.

### Step 4: Migration Best Practices
1. Export CSV from Mailchimp (Contacts > Export).
2. Import to new tool; map fields.
3. Update DNS for DKIM/SPF (reduces spam flags).
4. Run a welcome series to re-engage.
5. Monitor analytics for 2 weeks.

Practical advice: Start with 10% of your list to test. Tools like Brevo or MailerLite have one-click imports.

Common pitfalls: Forgetting unsubscribes—always import suppression lists.
