# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which One Wins for Your Email Strategy?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can feel overwhelming when you're building your email list. Both are popular platforms designed for creators, bloggers, and small businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, user experience, and more based on their current offerings as of 2023. Our goal is to give you practical advice to decide which tool aligns with your goals—whether you're a solo creator or scaling a team.

## Pricing: Affordability vs Value

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. MailerLite keeps it simple and budget-friendly, while ConvertKit emphasizes value for serious creators.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite offers a generous free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Paid plans start at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20 for 1,000 subs, and so on. Features like automation and landing pages are included even on the free tier. No contracts, and you can upgrade/downgrade anytime. It's ideal if you're testing the waters or have a tight budget.

**Practical Tip:** Start with the free plan to import contacts and send your first campaign. Monitor open rates before committing.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) has a free plan up to 300 subscribers with basic broadcasts. Creator plan starts at $9/month for up to 300 subs, jumping to $29 for 1,000. The Creator Pro plan adds advanced commerce at $29/month base. Annual billing saves 20%. It's pricier but includes unlimited emails and seats.

**Practical Tip:** If your list exceeds 1,000 quickly, calculate long-term costs—ConvertKit's unlimited sends prevent overage fees common in other tools.

**Winner:** MailerLite for bootstrappers; ConvertKit for growing creators who need unlimited sends.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop vs Intuitive Flows

Both platforms prioritize simplicity, but their interfaces differ.

### MailerLite's User-Friendly Editor
MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly with photo blocks, columns, and dynamic content. The dashboard is clean, with quick access to campaigns, automations, and popups. Setup takes minutes—no coding required.

**Practical Advice:** Use their template library (100+ responsive designs) and A/B testing for subject lines to boost opens by 20-30% right away.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Design
ConvertKit shines with its visual automation builder (formerly sequences). Tagging system segments subscribers automatically. The editor is block-based but more flexible for storytelling emails. Slight learning curve for tags, but tutorials help.

**Practical Advice:** Leverage subscriber tags for personalized follow-ups, like "purchased" or "engaged," to increase conversions without manual lists.

**Winner:** MailerLite for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for users wanting smart segmentation.

## Core Features: Automation, Templates, and Integrations

### Email Automation
- **MailerLite:** Workflow automation with triggers like sign-ups or clicks. Includes abandoned cart for e-commerce. Unlimited workflows on all plans.
- **ConvertKit:** Advanced sequences and automations with if/then logic. Commerce features like one-click upsells native to Pro plan.

**Tip:** For bloggers, ConvertKit's sequences nurture leads through content funnels; MailerLite suits simple welcome series.

### Landing Pages and Forms
Both offer popups, forms, and pages. MailerLite has unlimited pages on paid plans with A/B testing. ConvertKit's forms are embeddable with tagging rules.

### Analytics and Deliverability
MailerLite reports opens, clicks, and revenue tracking. ConvertKit adds revenue per subscriber and link tracking. Both have strong deliverability (MailerLite ~99%, ConvertKit similar via best practices).

**Practical Advice:** Always warm up your domain with low-volume sends first. Use double opt-ins to maintain list health.

### Integrations
- MailerLite: 250+ via Zapier, WordPress, Shopify.
- ConvertKit: Native with Gumroad, Teachable; strong Zapier support.

**Winner:** Tie—choose based on your stack (e.g., ConvertKit for creator tools).

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite offers 24/7 live chat, email support, and a knowledge base. Response times are fast (under 1 hour).

ConvertKit provides email support (business hours), extensive Creator Network (podcasts, courses), and priority for Pro users.

**Practical Tip:** Join both free trials and test support—ask about API limits or custom domains.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Budget-conscious solopreneurs.
- Need popups/landing pages immediately.
- Simple newsletters or e-commerce basics.

**Real-World Example:** A blogger with 500 subs can run automations and popups for under $10/month, focusing on growth without complexity.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators selling digital products.
- Advanced tagging and revenue tracking.
- Scaling to 10k+ subs with unlimited sends.

**Real-World Example:** Podcasters use sequences for episode launches, tracking which listeners upgrade to paid tiers seamlessly.

## Migration and Getting Started

Switching? Both import CSVs easily. MailerLite has a migration service on higher plans; ConvertKit guides manual transfers.

**Step-by-Step Advice:** 1. Export from current tool. 2. Sign up for free plan. 3. Import and verify. 4. Replicate top campaigns. 5. Test sends to yourself.

## Conclusion: Your Best Pick Depends on Goals

MailerLite edges out for affordability and simplicity, perfect for starters under 5,000 subs. ConvertKit dominates for creators needing robust automations and monetization—worth the extra cost if revenue is key.

Test both free plans for a week. Track metrics like open rates (aim >25%) and clicks (>3%). Whichever feels intuitive and scales with you wins.
