# Best Email Marketing Software in 2023: Top 10 Picks for Businesses and Creators

Choosing the **best email marketing software** can transform your business outreach. With email delivering an average ROI of $42 for every $1 spent (according to DMA data), the right platform is essential for small businesses, e-commerce stores, and content creators. This roundup reviews the top 10 options based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, focusing on features like automation, deliverability, and integrations.

We'll cover key criteria, detailed breakdowns of each tool, a comparison, and practical tips to help you select the perfect fit for your audience—whether you're just starting or scaling up.

## What Makes the Best Email Marketing Software?

Before diving into the list, understand the essentials. The **best email marketing software** balances usability, power, and cost.

### Key Features to Prioritize

- **List Management and Segmentation**: Segment audiences by behavior, location, or purchase history for targeted campaigns.
- **Automation Workflows**: Drip sequences, abandoned cart emails, and welcome series save time.
- **Templates and Design Tools**: Drag-and-drop editors for responsive emails without coding.
- **Analytics and Reporting**: Track open rates (industry avg. 21%), clicks (2.3%), and conversions.
- **Deliverability**: High inbox placement rates (aim for 95%+).
- **Integrations**: Connect with Shopify, WordPress, Zapier, etc.
- **Compliance**: Built-in GDPR, CAN-SPAM tools like double opt-in.

### Pricing Considerations

Most offer freemium tiers. Expect $10–$50/month for 1,000 subscribers, scaling up. Factor in contacts and sends—pay-per-use can add up.

Practical tip: Start with free trials (most 14–30 days) and import a test list to evaluate.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Picks

Here's our curated list, ranked by versatility, user ratings (4.5+ on G2), and value.

### 1. Mailchimp

The gold standard for beginners. Free for up to 500 contacts/1,000 sends/month.

**Pros**:
- Intuitive drag-and-drop builder.
- 250+ templates.
- Basic automation and A/B testing.

**Cons**:
- Advanced features locked behind pricier plans ($13+/month).
- Customer service slower for free users.

Best for: Small businesses. Integrates with Facebook, Google Analytics. Practical advice: Use its audience dashboard to segment by engagement—boost opens by 14% per their studies.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

Powerhouse for automation (4.6/5 G2 rating).

**Pros**:
- Advanced machine learning for predictive sending.
- CRM integration, site tracking.
- Deep segmentation (e.g., lead scoring).

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve.
- Starts at $29/month.

Best for: Mid-sized teams. Tip: Set up "if/then" automations for cart recovery—increases conversions by 20–30%.

### 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Creator-focused, simple pricing from $9/month.

**Pros**:
- Visual automations.
- Tagging over lists for precision.
- Native landing pages/forms.

**Cons**:
- Fewer templates.
- E-commerce features basic.

Best for: Bloggers/influencers. Advice: Use subscriber scoring to nurture high-value fans—great for course launches.

### 4. Klaviyo

E-commerce king (Shopify integration).

**Pros**:
- Revenue tracking per email.
- Dynamic product recommendations.
- Free up to 250 contacts.

**Cons**:
- Geared toward stores; less for B2B.
- $20+/month after free.

Best for: Online shops. Tip: Enable flow analytics to optimize flows—users report 25% revenue lift.

### 5. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars ($19/month starter).

**Pros**:
- Conversion funnel builder.
- Autoresponders, landing pages.
- High deliverability (99%).

**Cons**:
- Interface dated.

Best for: Agencies. Advice: A/B test subject lines weekly—improves opens by 10–20%.

### 6. Constant Contact

User-friendly for non-profits/small biz ($12/month).

**Pros**:
- Event management.
- Social ads integration.
- AI subject line generator.

**Cons**:
- Limited automation.

Tip: Leverage contact management for seasonal campaigns.

### 7. AWeber

Reliable veteran ($19/month, free trial).

**Pros**:
- Strong tagging/segmentation.
- RSS-to-email.

**Cons**:
- Design tools basic.

Best for: Newsletters. Advice: Use split testing for sends.

### 8. Sendinblue (Brevo)

Affordable transactional + marketing ($25/month).

**Pros**:
- Unlimited contacts on some plans.
- SMS/chat integration.

**Cons**:
- Automation limits on basic.

Tip: Combine email/SMS for 6x engagement.

### 9. Drip

E-commerce automation ($39/month).

**Pros**:
- Customer journey mapping.
- Personalization at scale.

**Cons**:
- Pricey for starters.

### 10. Omnisend

Omnichannel for e-com (free plan).

**Pros**:
- Pop-ups, SMS, push.
- Pre-built workflows.

**Cons**:
- Analytics overwhelming.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing Software for You

1. **Assess Needs**: Solo? Mailchimp. Complex automations? ActiveCampaign.
2. **Budget Test**: Calculate cost per 1,000 subscribers.
3. **Trial Run**: Import lists, build a campaign, check mobile preview.
4. **Scalability**: Ensure it grows with you (e.g., API access).

**Practical Advice**:
- Always use double opt-in to maintain list health (reduces spam complaints).
- Aim for 40–50 char subject lines.
- Personalize with [First Name]—lifts opens 20%.
- Monitor spam scores via tools like Mail-Tester.
- Integrate with Google Analytics for full-funnel tracking.
- Comply: Add unsubscribe links, honor requests within 10 days.

For e-com: Prioritize Klaviyo/Omnisend for cart abandonment (recovers 10–15% lost sales).

B2B? ActiveCampaign's CRM shines.

## Conclusion

The **best email marketing software** depends on your goals, but Mailchimp suits most starters, while ActiveCampaign powers growth. Test 2–3 via trials to match your workflow. With the right tool, you'll boost engagement and revenue—start today and watch your list thrive.
