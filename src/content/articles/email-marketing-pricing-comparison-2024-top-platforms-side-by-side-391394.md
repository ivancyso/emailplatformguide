# Email Marketing Pricing Comparison 2024: Top Platforms Side-by-Side

Choosing the right email marketing platform can make or break your campaigns. With dozens of tools vying for your attention, pricing is often the deciding factor. In this **email marketing pricing comparison**, we'll break down costs for popular platforms like Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, GetResponse, Brevo, and Constant Contact. We'll focus on real plans, subscriber-based pricing (the most common model), and value for money. All prices are monthly (billed annually where applicable) as of mid-2024—always verify on official sites as they change.

This guide targets startups, small businesses, and growing teams looking to scale without overspending. Expect practical advice on hidden fees, feature trade-offs, and how to pick the best fit.

## Why Pricing Matters More Than You Think

Email marketing ROI hinges on cost efficiency. Platforms charge based on contacts (subscribers), emails sent, or features. A cheap tool with limits can cost more long-term if you outgrow it.

**Key pricing models:**
- Subscriber tiers (e.g., $X for up to 1,000 subs)
- Send limits (e.g., 300 emails/day free)
- Feature unlocks (automation, A/B testing)

Practical tip: Calculate your total cost of ownership (TCO). Estimate monthly sends (aim for 4-6 per sub), add-ons like landing pages, and support needs. Tools with forever-free tiers suit bootstrappers; enterprise plans fit high-volume senders.

## Top Email Marketing Platforms: Detailed Pricing Breakdown

We'll cover six leaders, highlighting starter, mid-tier, and pro plans. Focus is on core features like automation, segmentation, and analytics.

### Mailchimp

Mailchimp's freemium model appeals to beginners.
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic templates.
- **Essentials**: $13/mo (500 contacts), unlimited sends, basic automation.
- **Standard**: $20/mo (500 contacts), advanced automation, A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350/mo (10,000 contacts), priority support, multivariate testing.

**Pros for price**: Generous free tier. **Cons**: Automation limited below Standard; pricey at scale (e.g., $299/mo for 10k subs on Premium). Ideal for solopreneurs testing waters.

### ActiveCampaign

Automation powerhouse for mid-sized teams.
- **Lite**: $29/mo (1,000 contacts), email automation, basic CRM.
- **Plus**: $49/mo (1,000), site tracking, machine learning.
- **Pro**: $149/mo (1,000), custom reporting, dedicated support.
- **Enterprise**: Custom (50k+ contacts).

**Value note**: Scales well; automation saves time on complex workflows. No free plan, but 14-day trial. Great for e-commerce with behavioral triggers.

### ConvertKit (now Kit)

Creator-focused, simple pricing.
- **Free**: Up to 300 subs, unlimited sends (with branding).
- **Creator**: $29/mo (500 subs), landing pages, subscriber tagging.
- **Creator Pro**: $49/mo (500), advanced broadcasts, one-click upsells.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

**Practical advice**: Perfect for bloggers/newsletters. Visual automations are intuitive—no coding needed. Grows affordably until 10k subs (~$125/mo).

### GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars.
- **Email Marketing**: $19/mo (1,000 contacts), autoresponders.
- **Marketing Automation**: $59/mo (1,000), abandoned cart.
- **Ecommerce Marketing**: $119/mo (1,000), full funnel.

**Standout**: 30-day free trial, cheap entry. Includes funnels and hosting. Watch for add-ons like paid ads integration (~$10+).

### Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Transactional + marketing emails.
- **Free**: 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts.
- **Starter**: $25/mo (20k emails), no daily cap.
- **Business**: $65/mo (20k), automation, sales CRM.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

**Budget hack**: Unlimited contacts make it scale-friendly for low-volume lists. API for devs. Excellent for transactional emails (e.g., receipts).

### Constant Contact

User-friendly for non-techies.

- **Lite**: $12/mo (500 contacts), basic list building.
- **Standard**: $35/mo (500), automation, retargeting.
- **Premium**: $80/mo (500), advanced insights.

**Pro tip**: 60-day free trial. Strong templates, but automation lags behind ActiveCampaign. Good for nonprofits/events.

## Factors to Consider Beyond Base Pricing

Don't chase the lowest price—evaluate TCO.

### 1. Subscriber Growth Projections
If your list grows 20%/month, pick scalable pricing. Brevo wins for unlimited contacts; Mailchimp jumps fast post-2k subs.

**Advice**: Use calculators on sites (e.g., ActiveCampaign's). Budget 10-20% buffer.

### 2. Feature Value per Dollar
- Automation: ActiveCampaign/ConvertKit excel.
- Ecommerce: Klaviyo (starts $20 for 250 contacts) if Shopify-integrated (not in table).
- Deliverability: All >95%, but test with GlockApps.

### 3. Hidden Costs
- Overages: Mailchimp charges extra subs.
- Add-ons: SMS ($0.01/message), domains ($10-20/yr).
- Support: Free chat vs. phone (Premium tiers).
- Migration: Free imports usually, but custom setups cost $500+.

**Pro tip**: Start with trials. Export lists easily (CSV/Zapier).

### 4. Sending Limits & Compliance
GDPR/CCPA tools built-in. ESPs throttle spammers—focus on <1% complaint rates.

## Best Picks by Budget and Use Case

- **Under $20/mo (Starters)**: Constant Contact Lite or GetResponse. Build lists without commitment.
- **$20-50/mo (Small Biz)**: Brevo Starter or ConvertKit. Unlimited sends + basics.
- **$50+ (Growth)**: ActiveCampaign Plus. ROI from automations pays back in 1-2 months.
- **High-Volume**: Brevo Business—cost per 1k sends drops to pennies.

**Real-world example**: A 2k-sub newsletter saves $100/mo switching from Mailchimp Standard ($75) to ConvertKit Pro ($49) for better tagging.

## Practical Steps to Choose and Switch

1. Audit current needs: Subs? Sends/mo? Automations?
2. Trial 2-3 tools simultaneously.
3. Compare TCO for 6/12 months.
4. Migrate: Use built-in importers; test sends.
5. Monitor: Track open rates (aim 20-30%), unsubscribes (<0.5%).

Tools like Zapier bridge gaps during transition.
