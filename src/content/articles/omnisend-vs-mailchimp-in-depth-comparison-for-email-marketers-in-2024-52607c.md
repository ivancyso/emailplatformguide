# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With over 4 billion email users worldwide, email remains a top channel for customer engagement, boasting average ROI of $36 for every $1 spent. For businesses evaluating options, **Omnisend vs Mailchimp** is a common debate. Mailchimp has long been a household name for beginners, while Omnisend shines in ecommerce automation.

This comparison breaks down key aspects like pricing, features, integrations, and usability. Whether you're a small shop owner or scaling marketer, we'll help you decide based on real-world use cases. Both platforms offer free trials, so test them yourself.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both tools have free plans, but they differ in scalability.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Basic automation and templates included.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/month (billed annually) for 500 contacts, unlimited sends.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, with advanced automation and A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for high-volume users, adding personalization and support.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts and features. Costs rise quickly beyond 5,000 contacts—expect $100+/month.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Unlimited contacts, 500 monthly emails, basic automation.
- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts), unlimited emails, SMS add-ons.
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts), advanced segmentation, A/B testing, 24/7 support.

Omnisend bills by contacts but offers fairer scaling for ecommerce. SMS and push notifications incur extra fees, but bundles save money.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your contact list size and send volume. For under 1,000 contacts, both free plans suffice. Ecommerce stores benefit from Omnisend's cost-effective SMS; general marketers may prefer Mailchimp's ad integrations.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp offers 100+ drag-and-drop templates, mobile-responsive out-of-the-box. Customization is intuitive for non-designers.

Omnisend provides ecommerce-focused templates (product blocks, dynamic content). Its editor supports JSON for advanced tweaks, ideal for Shopify users.

**Tip**: Preview on multiple devices. Use Mailchimp for quick newsletters; Omnisend for personalized product recommendations.

### Automation Workflows
Omnisend leads here with pre-built ecommerce flows: abandoned cart (recovery rates up to 20% industry average), welcome series, back-in-stock alerts.

Mailchimp's automations are customer journey-based but less specialized. Single-step triggers work well for beginners.

**Practical Advice**: Map your customer lifecycle. Set up Omnisend's cart recovery if you sell online—integrate with Shopify for real-time data. Test Mailchimp for drip campaigns in services.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both segment by behavior, location, tags. Omnisend excels with product-based segmentation (e.g., "viewed high-price items"). Mailchimp uses merge tags effectively.

**Pro Tip**: Start with open/click behavior segments. Aim for 20-30% open rates; personalize subject lines to boost by 26%.

### SMS and Omnichannel
Omnisend's strength: Native SMS, web push, popups. Combine channels for 6x higher engagement.

Mailchimp added SMS recently but as an add-on; focuses on email + social ads.

## Integrations: Connecting Your Tech Stack

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps: Google Analytics, Facebook, WordPress, Zapier.

Omnisend targets ecommerce: Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce (one-click sync). Also Zapier, Google.

**Advice**: List your tools. Ecommerce? Omnisend auto-syncs orders. Blogs/CRMs? Mailchimp's depth wins. Use Zapier as a bridge.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is beginner-friendly, with guided tours. Dashboard feels familiar like Gmail.

Omnisend is clean but ecommerce-oriented—steeper for non-shop users. Excellent onboarding videos.

**Tip**: Spend 30 mins in each demo account. Mailchimp for solo users; Omnisend if you have a VA handling flows.

## Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp: Revenue tracking, unsubscribe trends, A/B insights.

Omnisend: Ecommerce ROI calculator, cross-channel attribution.

**Practical Advice**: Track beyond opens—focus on revenue per email. Set goals in Google Analytics for UTM links.

## Customer Support Options

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for paid; self-help vast.

Omnisend: 24/7 chat for Pro, responsive email, knowledge base.

Prioritize chat if you're time-poor.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Omnisend** if: Ecommerce store (Shopify/Woo), need cart recovery/SMS, under 10k contacts.
- **Pick Mailchimp** if: Beginner, content marketing, large audiences, need ads/landing pages.

**Decision Framework**:
1. Business type? Ecomm → Omnisend.
2. Budget? Under $50/mo → Compare free trials.
3. Needs SMS? Omnisend.
4. Scale? Test migration paths.

Both score 4.5+ on G2/Capterra. Trial for 14-30 days.

## Conclusion

**Omnisend vs Mailchimp** boils down to your niche. Omnisend edges for ecommerce with superior automation and omnichannel, while Mailchimp remains versatile for all-around marketing. Neither is "best" universally—align with goals.

Start with free plans, build one automation, analyze results. For growing businesses, Omnisend's ROI focus often pays off faster. Whichever you choose, consistent sending and segmentation drive success.
