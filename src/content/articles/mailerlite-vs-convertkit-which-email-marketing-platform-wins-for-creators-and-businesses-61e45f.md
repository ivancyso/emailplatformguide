# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: In-Depth Comparison for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your audience engagement and revenue. In this MailerLite vs ConvertKit showdown, we'll break down key aspects like pricing, features, automation, and usability to help you decide. Both tools cater to creators, bloggers, and small businesses, but they shine in different areas. Whether you're starting free or scaling up, this guide provides practical insights based on real user experiences and official specs.

## Pricing: Affordability and Value Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when picking an email marketing tool. MailerLite offers a generous free plan, making it ideal for bootstrapped users.

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers (billed annually), scaling to $20 for 1,000 subs.
- **Advanced**: From $21/month for 500 subs, adding A/B testing and web push notifications.

MailerLite's model is subscriber-based, with no caps on email sends beyond the plan limits. It's transparent and scales predictably.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free Trial**: 14 days, no credit card required.
- **Creator**: Starts at $9/month for up to 300 subscribers (annual billing), jumping to $29 for 1,000.
- **Creator Pro**: From $29/month for 300 subs, unlocking advanced automation and monetization features like paid newsletters.

ConvertKit charges based on subscribers, with unlimited emails. However, it lacks a perpetual free plan, which can deter beginners.

**Practical Advice**: If you're under 1,000 subscribers, start with MailerLite's free tier to test waters without commitment. For larger lists needing creator-specific tools like subscriptions, ConvertKit's Pro plan offers better ROI. Always calculate based on your list size—use their pricing calculators for accuracy.

## Ease of Use: Interfaces and Onboarding

Both platforms prioritize simplicity, but their designs target slightly different users.

### MailerLite's Intuitive Drag-and-Drop
MailerLite's editor is beginner-friendly with a clean dashboard. Drag-and-drop blocks make campaign creation fast—no coding needed. Onboarding includes tutorials and a setup wizard.

**Tip**: Customize templates in minutes. Use the preview feature across devices to ensure mobile responsiveness, crucial since 50%+ of emails are opened on phones.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Dashboard
ConvertKit feels more like a CRM for creators. Its visual automation builder uses a flowchart style, which is powerful but has a slight learning curve. The inbox preview and tagging system streamline segmentation.

**Tip**: Leverage ConvertKit's sequences for welcome series. Tag subscribers based on behavior (e.g., "opened ebook") for hyper-personalized sends.

MailerLite edges out for absolute beginners; ConvertKit suits those comfortable with tech.

## Core Features: Templates, Landing Pages, and Integrations

### Email Templates and Design
- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates, photo editor, and RSS-to-email. Strong for newsletters and promos.
- **ConvertKit**: Commerce-focused templates, digital product integration. Excels in clean, minimalist designs for creators.

Both support custom HTML/CSS for pros.

### Landing Pages and Forms
- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, popups, and forms on all plans. Embeddable anywhere with analytics.
- **ConvertKit**: Native landing pages, opt-in forms, and checkout pages (Pro). Great for selling courses.

**Practical Advice**: Build a lead magnet funnel. With MailerLite, A/B test popup timing (e.g., 30s delay converts 20% better). In ConvertKit, use subscriber scores to prioritize hot leads.

### Integrations
Both integrate with 1,000+ apps via Zapier. MailerLite connects natively to WordPress, Google Analytics; ConvertKit to Gumroad, Teachable for creators.

## Automation and Segmentation: Power Under the Hood

Automation is where these tools differentiate.

### MailerLite Automation
Trigger-based workflows: welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement. Time-based delays and if/then logic. Free plan includes basics.

**Example Workflow**: If subscriber clicks link → tag as "interested" → send upsell 3 days later.

### ConvertKit Automation
Advanced tagging and visual automations. Rules like "if subscribed to sequence X and opens Y% → action." Pro unlocks subscriber scoring.

**Practical Advice**: Audit your list quarterly. Use MailerLite's win-back campaign for inactive subs (segment >90 days no open). ConvertKit's commerce automations shine for one-click upsells—test with a 10% discount trigger.

ConvertKit wins for complex creator funnels; MailerLite for straightforward needs.

## Deliverability and Analytics

Both boast high deliverability (99%+ claimed). MailerLite uses dedicated IPs on higher plans; ConvertKit emphasizes authentication setup (DKIM/SPF).

Analytics: Open/click rates, bounce handling, revenue tracking (ConvertKit Pro).

**Tip**: Warm up new domains gradually—start with 50 sends/day. Monitor spam complaints; aim <0.1%.

## Customer Support and Community

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive knowledge base. High satisfaction (4.9/5 on Capterra).
- **ConvertKit**: Email/ticket support (response <24h), creator community, webinars. Also highly rated (4.6/5).

MailerLite's live chat is a game-changer for urgent issues.

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Small business/blogger on budget, need free start, simple automations, popups for leads.
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Selling digital products, complex sequences, creator economy focus (podcasts, courses).

**Migration Tip**: Export CSV from old tool. MailerLite importer handles segments; ConvertKit preserves tags. Test with 10% list first.

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage and needs. MailerLite's free access and ease make it perfect for testing and growth under 5k subs.
