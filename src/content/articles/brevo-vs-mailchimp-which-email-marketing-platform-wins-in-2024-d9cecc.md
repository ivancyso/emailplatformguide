# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp—the industry giant—helps narrow it down. This Brevo vs Mailchimp comparison focuses on key factors like pricing, features, ease of use, and scalability for small businesses, solopreneurs, and growing teams.

Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling to enterprise levels, we'll break down real-world differences based on current offerings. By the end, you'll have practical advice to decide which tool fits your needs.

## Pricing: Where Value Meets Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer free tiers, but their structures differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. It's great for testing but limits automation and advanced segmentation. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for Premium (10,000+ contacts). Features like unlimited sends come at higher tiers, and overages add costs. For e-commerce users, their Standard plan ($20+/month) unlocks abandoned cart emails.

**Practical Tip:** If your list is under 500, Mailchimp's free plan includes basic templates and A/B testing—ideal for newsletters without complex automations.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo shines with a free plan allowing unlimited contacts but capped at 300 emails/day. No subscriber limits mean it's perfect for growing lists without immediate upgrades. Paid plans begin at $25/month (Starter) for 20,000 emails/month, up to $65/month (Business) for 100,000 emails with advanced automation and SMS. Transactional emails are pay-as-you-go, starting at $0.001/email.

**Practical Tip:** For transactional-heavy sites (e.g., order confirmations), Brevo's model saves money—integrate via API for high-volume sends without marketing plan costs.

**Winner:** Brevo for cost-conscious users with large lists; Mailchimp for simple, low-volume needs.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop vs Intuitive Builders

Both platforms prioritize user-friendliness, but interfaces vary.

Mailchimp's editor is legendary for its drag-and-drop simplicity. New users can build campaigns in minutes with 100+ responsive templates. The dashboard feels familiar, like a content management system.

Brevo's builder is equally intuitive, with a block-based editor and AI-assisted content generation. It integrates chat, SMS, and WhatsApp seamlessly, reducing tool-switching.

**Practical Advice:** Test both free plans. Mailchimp suits visual designers; Brevo appeals to multi-channel marketers. Onboarding tutorials in both take under 10 minutes.

## Core Features: Automation, Segmentation, and Analytics

### Email Automation

Mailchimp offers customer journeys with triggers like purchases or sign-ups. Classic automations are free; advanced ones require paid plans.

Brevo provides unlimited automations on all plans, including multi-step workflows, webhooks, and sales CRM integration. It's stronger for e-commerce with cart abandonment and re-engagement flows.

**Tip:** Use Brevo for drip campaigns in sales funnels; Mailchimp for behavioral triggers like page visits.

### List Segmentation and Personalization

Mailchimp excels with tags, groups, and behavioral segmentation (e.g., clicked links). Merge fields enable dynamic content.

Brevo matches with custom fields, predictive segmentation, and real-time list syncing. Its CRM auto-enriches contacts.

### Templates and Design

Mailchimp: 200+ industry-specific templates, theme builder.

Brevo: 50+ templates, but strong customization and multipurpose blocks.

### Analytics and Reporting

Both track opens, clicks, bounces. Mailchimp's heatmaps and revenue tracking shine for e-com. Brevo adds SMS/WhatsApp metrics and deliverability scores.

**Practical Advice:** For ROI-focused campaigns, Mailchimp's e-commerce reports help attribute sales. Brevo's real-time dashboard aids quick optimizations.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Deliverability rates hover around 95% for both, per industry benchmarks. Mailchimp's domain authentication (DKIM/SPF) is straightforward. Brevo emphasizes IP warming and dedicated IPs on higher plans.

Both comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM. Brevo's EU base aids European users.

**Tip:** Always verify domains and clean lists monthly to maintain rates above 90%.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics) via native or Zapier.

Brevo supports 300+ too, with strengths in e-com (WooCommerce, Magento) and its native CRM.

For scaling, Mailchimp handles millions of subscribers enterprise-wide. Brevo scales via API for developers.

**Practical Advice:** If you're on Shopify, both integrate well—test API limits for high-traffic sites.

## Customer Support Options

Mailchimp: Free users get docs/Academy; paid get email/chat (Priority for Premium).

Brevo: 24/7 chat/email on all plans, phone for Business+.

**Winner:** Brevo for responsive help without upgrades.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros:
- Iconic brand, vast resources.
- Excellent for beginners.
- Robust e-com features.

Cons:
- Free plan limitations.
- Pricing jumps quickly.

### Brevo Pros:
- Unlimited contacts free.
- Multi-channel (email/SMS/chat).
- Affordable scaling.

Cons:
- Daily send limits on free.
- Fewer templates.

## Who Should Choose What?

- **Pick Mailchimp if:** You're a beginner, need polished templates, or focus on U.S. e-com with behavioral automations.
- **Pick Brevo if:** Building large lists, need SMS/transactionals, or want all-in-one marketing/CRM on a budget.

**Practical Advice:** Start with free trials (Brevo: 3 days full access; Mailchimp: always free tier). Import a test list, run a campaign, and compare deliverability.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp isn't a clear knockout—it's about fit. Brevo edges for affordability and multi-channel versatility, ideal for growing SMBs. Mailchimp wins for ease and e-com depth, suiting content-heavy marketers.
