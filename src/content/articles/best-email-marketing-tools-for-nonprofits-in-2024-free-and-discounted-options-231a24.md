# Best Email Marketing Tools for Nonprofits in 2024: Free and Discounted Options

Email marketing remains a cornerstone for nonprofits looking to connect with donors, volunteers, and supporters. With tight budgets, nonprofits need platforms that offer robust features without breaking the bank. This roundup focuses on tools with free plans, affordable pricing, or dedicated nonprofit discounts. We'll cover key features, real-world pricing (as of my last update), pros/cons, and practical advice to help you choose the right one.

Whether you're sending newsletters, fundraising appeals, or event invites, these platforms prioritize ease of use, high deliverability, and compliance with regulations like CAN-SPAM and GDPR.

## Why Email Marketing Matters for Nonprofits

Nonprofits rely on email to nurture relationships and drive action. According to industry benchmarks from sources like Nonprofit Tech for Good, email yields an average ROI of $42 for every $1 spent—far higher than social media or direct mail. Key benefits include:

- **Personalized donor engagement**: Segment lists by giving history or interests.
- **Cost-effective fundraising**: Automated campaigns increase repeat donations.
- **Measurable impact**: Track opens, clicks, and conversions.

However, choosing the wrong tool can lead to low deliverability or overwhelming costs. Look for integrations with CRM systems like Salesforce or DonorPerfect, mobile-responsive templates, and A/B testing.

## Key Features to Prioritize

When selecting an email marketing platform for your nonprofit:

### 1. Pricing and Nonprofit Discounts
Free tiers or discounts are essential. Avoid platforms that charge per contact without caps.

### 2. List Management and Segmentation
Handle large lists efficiently. Features like tags, custom fields, and automation workflows save time.

### 3. Deliverability and Compliance
High inbox placement rates (above 95%) and built-in unsubscribe/DNC tools are non-negotiable.

### 4. Templates and Design
Drag-and-drop editors with nonprofit-specific templates (e.g., donation forms).

### 5. Analytics and Reporting
Track campaign performance, donor retention, and revenue attribution.

### 6. Integrations
Connect with tools like Google Analytics, Zapier, or fundraising platforms.

## Top Email Marketing Tools for Nonprofits

Here are seven standout platforms, selected based on user reviews from G2, Capterra, and nonprofit forums. All offer free trials or plans.

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp is a nonprofit favorite due to its user-friendly interface and generous free plan.

- **Pricing**: Free for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month. Paid plans start at $13/month; nonprofits get 15-20% off via their nonprofit program (apply at mailchimp.com/nonprofits).
- **Pros**: Intuitive drag-and-drop builder, 250+ templates, automation, A/B testing, detailed analytics.
- **Cons**: Free plan limits advanced automations; higher tiers get pricey for large lists.
- **Best for**: Small nonprofits starting out.

Practical tip: Use behavioral triggers to send thank-you emails post-donation, boosting retention by 20-30% per benchmarks.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo excels in affordability and SMS integration.

- **Pricing**: Free plan: 300 emails/day forever. Paid from $25/month; volume discounts for nonprofits.
- **Pros**: Unlimited contacts on free plan, transactional emails, CRM included, excellent deliverability (99%).
- **Cons**: Template library is basic; learning curve for automations.
- **Best for**: Growing orgs needing multi-channel (email + SMS).

Practical tip: Segment by engagement—re-engage lapsed donors with win-back sequences to recover 10-15% of lost revenue.

### 3. MailerLite

A lightweight, budget-friendly option with strong automation.

- **Pricing**: Free up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Paid from $10/month; nonprofit discounts available on request.
- **Pros**: Pop-up forms, landing pages, A/B testing, website builder integration.
- **Cons**: No phone support on free plan.
- **Best for**: Content-heavy newsletters.

Practical tip: Leverage their survey tools to gather feedback, improving open rates over time.

### 4. Constant Contact

Tailored for small businesses and nonprofits with event management.

- **Pricing**: Starts at $12/month for 500 contacts; up to 30% nonprofit discount (constantcontact.com/nonprofit).
- **Pros**: Event registration, donation forms, social media ads integration, 24/7 support.
- **Cons**: No free plan; per-contact pricing scales up.
- **Best for**: Event-driven campaigns.

Practical tip: Use AI-powered subject line suggestions to hit 25%+ open rates.

### 5. Benchmark Email

Underrated gem with nonprofit focus.

- **Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts/3,500 sends/month. Paid from $13/month; 25% nonprofit discount.
- **Pros**: Image editor, RSS-to-email, high customization, polls.
- **Cons**: Automation is basic compared to leaders.
- **Best for**: Visual storytellers.

Practical tip: Automate welcome series for new subscribers to convert 15% to donors.

### 6. Sender

Modern platform with free forever plan.

- **Pricing**: Free up to 2,500 subscribers/15,000 emails/month. Paid from $8/month.
- **Pros**: Chat support, multi-language, e-commerce tools adaptable for donations.
- **Cons**: Fewer integrations.
- **Best for**: International nonprofits.

Practical tip: A/B test send times based on audience timezone data.

### 7. ActiveCampaign

Advanced automation for mid-sized nonprofits.

- **Pricing**: Starts at $29/month; nonprofit discounts up to 30% (activecampaign.com/nonprofit).
- **Pros**: Powerful CRM, machine learning scoring, site tracking.
- **Cons**: Steeper learning curve.
- **Best for**: Data-driven orgs.

Practical tip: Score leads by engagement to prioritize high-value donors.

## Practical Advice for Implementation

1. **Start Small**: Test free plans with a 500-person segment. Track metrics like open rate (aim 20-30%) and click-through (2-5%).

2. **Build Clean Lists**: Use double opt-in to comply with laws and improve deliverability. Import from events or websites ethically.

3. **Craft Compelling Content**: Subject lines under 50 chars, mobile-first design, clear CTAs like "Donate Now." Personalize with [First Name].

4. **Automate Wisely**: Set up drip campaigns: Welcome > Impact update > Ask.

5. **Analyze and Iterate**: Use UTM tags for Google Analytics. Tools like Litmus for testing.

6. **Budget Tip**: Apply for discounts early—most require 501(c)(3) proof.

7. **Scale Up**: Migrate data easily; most offer CSV exports.

Common pitfalls: Over-sending (limit 1-2/week), ignoring mobile (60% opens), neglecting segmentation.

Evaluate based on your list size, tech comfort, and needs—most offer 14-30 day trials. Prioritize deliverability and automation to maximize impact.
