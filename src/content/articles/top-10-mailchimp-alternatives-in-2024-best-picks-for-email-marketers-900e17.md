# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Picks for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but with recent pricing changes, limited automation in lower tiers, and growing competition, many users are exploring alternatives. If you're facing high costs, scalability issues, or need advanced features like better automations or CRM integration, it's time to consider switches.

This roundup covers the top Mailchimp alternatives based on user reviews from sites like G2 and Capterra, real-world features, and pricing as of 2024. We'll break down pros, cons, pricing, and ideal use cases with practical advice to help you choose.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month (down from previous limits), and paid plans start at $13/month for essentials but jump quickly. Automation is basic unless you're on premium ($350+/month). Deliverability is solid at ~90%, but alternatives often match or exceed this with more flexibility.

Common pain points:
- **Pricing opacity**: Billed by contacts, not sends.
- **Limited free tier**: Not viable for growth.
- **Steep learning curve for advanced features**.

Switching saves money (e.g., unlimited sends on some plans) and unlocks tools like AI personalization or ecommerce integrations. Always export your list via CSV before migrating—most platforms offer import tools.

## 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
Brevo excels in multi-channel marketing: email, SMS, chat, and WhatsApp. Unlimited contacts on free plan (300 emails/day). Drag-and-drop editor, 40,000+ integrations via Zapier.

### Pricing
Free: 300 emails/day. Starter: $25/month (20k emails). Business: $65/month.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Affordable scaling, high deliverability (99% claimed), transactional emails free.
**Cons**: Automation limits on free/starter.

**Best for**: SMBs needing SMS alongside email. **Practical tip**: Use their API for WooCommerce sync—test with a small segment first to monitor bounce rates.

## 2. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
Advanced automation with machine learning (predictive sending), built-in CRM, site tracking, and split testing. 900+ integrations.

### Pricing
Lite: $29/month (500 contacts). Plus: $49 (automation + CRM). Pro: $149.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Deep personalization, excellent support (chat/phone).
**Cons**: Overkill for simple newsletters; steeper learning curve.

**Best for**: Ecommerce and lead nurturing. **Practical tip**: Set up 'if/then' automations for cart abandonment—track ROI via their reporting dashboard, aiming for 20-30% open rates initially.

## 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

### Key Features
Creator-focused: visual automations, landing pages, subscriber scoring. Native forms and tagging.

### Pricing
Free: Up to 300 subs. Creator: $29/month (500 subs). Creator Pro: $49.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Simple UI, unlimited emails, strong for newsletters.
**Cons**: Lacks robust ecommerce tools.

**Best for**: Bloggers and creators. **Practical tip**: Use subscriber tags for segmentation (e.g., 'engaged' vs. 'inactive') and send re-engagement sequences—expect 40%+ open rates with personalized subject lines.

## 4. Klaviyo

### Key Features
Ecommerce powerhouse: revenue tracking, dynamic product feeds, A/B testing, and AI flow optimization. Shopify/ BigCommerce native.

### Pricing
Free: Up to 250 contacts/500 emails. Scales with contacts (~$20/1k).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Best-in-class analytics, 1.2B+ emails/month deliverability.
**Cons**: Ecommerce-only focus; pricey at scale.

**Best for**: Online stores. **Practical tip**: Build flows for post-purchase upsells—integrate with Google Analytics for attribution, targeting 15% revenue lift.

## 5. MailerLite

### Key Features
Drag-and-drop builder, popups, automation, A/B testing. Website builder included.

### Pricing
Free: 1,000 subs/12k emails. Growing: $10/month (500 subs unlimited).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Cheapest paid plans, easy migration, high deliverability.
**Cons**: Fewer advanced CRM features.

**Best for**: Budget-conscious startups. **Practical tip**: Leverage popups for list growth (aim for 5-10% conversion)—A/B test placements on high-traffic pages.

## 6. GetResponse

### Key Features
Webinars, funnels, AI email generator, ecommerce tools. 30+ languages.

### Pricing
Free: Basic trial. Email Marketing: $19/month (1k contacts). Marketing Automation: $59.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: All-in-one (autoresponders, landing pages).
**Cons**: Interface feels dated.

**Best for**: Webinar marketers. **Practical tip**: Host free webinars to nurture leads—follow up with 3-email sequences for 25% attendance-to-sale conversion.

## 7. AWeber

### Key Features
Reliable delivery, tagging, canned responses. Strong support.

### Pricing
Lite: $19/month (500 subs). Unlimited: $149.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: 25+ years experience, easy for beginners.
**Cons**: Basic automations.

**Best for**: Non-profits/small biz. **Practical tip**: Use RSS-to-email for blogs—schedule consistently to maintain 25%+ opens.

## 8. Constant Contact

### Key Features
Event management, social ads, AI subject lines. Mobile app.

### Pricing
Lite: $12/month (500 contacts). Standard: $37.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Beginner-friendly, list-building tools.
**Cons**: No free plan, contact-based pricing.

**Best for**: Local businesses. **Practical tip**: Integrate with Facebook for ads-to-email—track engagement with UTM parameters.

## 9. Omnisend

### Key Features
Omnichannel (email/SMS/push), pre-built workflows for ecommerce.

### Pricing
Free: 250 contacts/500 emails. Standard: $16/month.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Free SMS credits, popup builder.
**Cons**: Ecommerce slant.

**Best for**: Shopify users. **Practical tip**: Automate welcome series with product recs—monitor cart recovery rates (target 10-20%).

## 10. Zoho Campaigns

### Key Features
CRM integration (Zoho suite), journey builder, RSS campaigns.

### Pricing
Free: 2k subs/6k emails. Standard: $4/month (extend trial).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Integrates with Zoho CRM/Desk.
**Cons**: Best in Zoho ecosystem.

**Best for**: Zoho users. **Practical tip**: Sync leads from CRM—use heatmaps for personalization.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: Newsletters? ConvertKit/MailerLite. Ecommerce? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Automation? ActiveCampaign.
2. **Budget Check**: Start free where possible (Brevo, MailerLite). Calculate TCO: contacts x monthly fee.
3. **Migration Plan**: Export Mailchimp list > Clean data (remove bounces) > Import > Test send.
4. **Test Deliverability**: Use tools like Mail-Tester.com post-switch.
5. **Key Metrics**: Aim for 20-30% opens, 2-3% clicks, <0.5% complaints.

Compare via free trials—most offer 14-30 days.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo for affordability, ActiveCampaign for power users, or Klaviyo for shops offer better value without sacrificing features. The best pick depends on your audience size, goals, and tech stack. Start with a trial, migrate a test list, and measure engagement. Switching can cut costs by 50%+ while boosting ROI—many report 2x revenue from advanced automations.

Ready to upgrade? Pick 2-3 from this list, trial them, and optimize. Your email strategy deserves better than Mailchimp's limits.
