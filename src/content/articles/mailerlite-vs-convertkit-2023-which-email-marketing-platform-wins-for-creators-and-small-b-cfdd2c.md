# MailerLite vs ConvertKit 2023: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins for Creators and Small Businesses?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you connect with your audience. For bloggers, creators, and small business owners, MailerLite and ConvertKit stand out as popular choices. Both offer powerful tools for building lists, sending newsletters, and automating campaigns. But which one is better for your needs?

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur on a budget or a growing creator needing advanced segmentation, this guide provides practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email tool. Both platforms use subscriber-based tiers, but they differ significantly in affordability and features included.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite is known for its budget-friendly plans:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups. Ideal for beginners testing the waters.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers (scales up).
- **Advanced**: Starts at $20/month with extra features like A/B testing and web notifications.

Practical tip: If you're under 1,000 subscribers, stick with the free plan to experiment without commitment. Upgrading is seamless, and there's no transaction fee on paid plans for e-commerce integrations.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (rebranded as Kit) targets creators with these tiers:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (recently expanded), with forms and basic broadcasts.
- **Creator**: Starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers.
- **Creator Pro**: Starts at $29/month, adding advanced reporting and one-click upsells.

ConvertKit charges more for similar subscriber counts, but justifies it with creator-specific tools like paid newsletters. Note: They offer a 30-day money-back guarantee.

**Winner on Price**: MailerLite for most users, especially startups. ConvertKit suits those monetizing content directly.

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools Head-to-Head

Both platforms excel in email marketing essentials, but their strengths vary.

### Email Editor and Templates

- **MailerLite**: Intuitive drag-and-drop editor with 80+ responsive templates. Easy customization without coding. Supports HTML for advanced users.
- **ConvertKit**: Block-based visual editor focused on simplicity. Fewer templates (around 50), but optimized for newsletters and sales pages.

Advice: MailerLite's editor is faster for non-designers. Test templates by importing your branding—both allow previews across devices.

### Automation and Workflows

- **MailerLite**: Robust automation builder with triggers like sign-ups or purchases. Includes RSS-to-email and multi-step sequences.
- **ConvertKit**: Tag-based automation shines for creators. Visual sequences for welcome series, abandoned carts, and broadcasts.

Practical advice: Use ConvertKit if you segment by behavior (e.g., tagging engaged readers). MailerLite works well for list-based automations like birthday emails.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, popups, and forms on all plans. High-converting templates and A/B testing.
- **ConvertKit**: Forms and landing pages included, with checkout pages for paid subscriptions. Popups via integrations.

Tip: MailerLite's popups have exit-intent tech—deploy them on your site to boost sign-ups by 20-30% without annoying visitors.

### Integrations and E-commerce

Both integrate with Stripe, Shopify, WordPress, etc. ConvertKit edges out with native commerce for digital products. MailerLite supports Zapier for 1,000+ apps.

### Analytics and Deliverability

- **MailerLite**: Open rates, clicks, bounces. Strong deliverability (99%+ claimed).
- **ConvertKit**: Detailed subscriber activity, revenue tracking. Excellent inbox placement.

Track metrics weekly: Aim for 20-30% open rates. Both provide spam score checks.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Interface

MailerLite's clean dashboard feels beginner-friendly, with quick-start wizards. ConvertKit's interface prioritizes sequences and tags, which has a learning curve but rewards with power-user features.

**Practical Advice**: Start with MailerLite if you're new—import contacts in minutes. ConvertKit users report 1-2 hours to master tagging. Both have mobile apps for on-the-go management.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive help center, and Facebook group. Fast responses (under 1 hour).
- **ConvertKit**: Email support (business hours), podcasts, webinars, and creator community. No live chat on free plan.

Winner: MailerLite for responsive help. Join their groups for templates and tips.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs ConvertKit?

- **Choose MailerLite** if: You're a small business, blogger, or on a budget. Perfect for lead gen, newsletters, and simple automations. Scale without breaking the bank.
- **Choose ConvertKit** if: You're a content creator selling courses/ebooks. Tag-based segmentation and one-click upsells streamline sales funnels.

Test both free plans: Export/import lists easily. Consider your growth—MailerLite for 1K-10K subs, ConvertKit for revenue-focused scaling.

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to priorities: affordability and simplicity (MailerLite) or creator monetization (ConvertKit). For most choosing platforms, MailerLite offers unbeatable value with comprehensive features. Start there, migrate if needed.
