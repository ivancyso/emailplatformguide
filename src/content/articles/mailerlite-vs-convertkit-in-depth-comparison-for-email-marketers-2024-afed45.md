# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can be tough when you're building your email list. Both are popular for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. This comparison dives into pricing, features, usability, and real-world advice to help you decide.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. MailerLite offers a generous free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—perfect for testing without commitment. Paid plans start at $10/month for 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20 for 1,000, with unlimited emails. Higher tiers like Advanced ($21+) add features like A/B testing and web push notifications.

ConvertKit, rebranded from ConvertKit to Kit (but still widely known as ConvertKit), has no free plan but a 14-day trial. Creator plan starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers and 10,000 emails, jumping to $29 for 500-1,000. The Creator Pro at $29+ includes advanced automations and revenue tracking. Enterprise is custom.

**Practical tip:** If you're under 1,000 subs, start with MailerLite's free tier. For creators monetizing via products, ConvertKit's commerce tools justify the cost. Calculate your subscriber growth: MailerLite saves money early, but ConvertKit scales better for high-volume senders.

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools Side-by-Side

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 80+ responsive templates. It supports custom HTML and dynamic content blocks. ConvertKit uses a simple visual builder focused on clean, mobile-first designs, with fewer templates (around 50) but optimized for creators.

**Advice:** MailerLite suits e-commerce with product blocks; ConvertKit excels for newsletters with broadcast simplicity.

### Automation and Sequences

ConvertKit shines here with its sequence builder—drip campaigns that feel like storytelling. Tagging system (vs. lists) allows sophisticated subscriber journeys. MailerLite offers workflows with triggers like opens/clicks, but it's more linear. Both integrate with Zapier.

**Pro tip:** Map your customer journey first. Use ConvertKit for nurture sequences (e.g., welcome series); MailerLite for event-based automations like cart abandonment.

### Segmentation and List Management

ConvertKit's tagging is powerful—no list limits, segment by behavior/tags. MailerLite uses groups and segments based on activity/location. Both handle large lists well.

### Landing Pages and Forms

MailerLite includes unlimited landing pages, popups, and forms in all plans. ConvertKit offers forms, landing pages (Pro+), and checkouts for selling digital products.

**Practical advice:** Test opt-in rates. MailerLite's popups convert 20-30% higher for beginners per user reports; ConvertKit's native commerce boosts sales funnels.

### Integrations and Analytics

Both connect to 1,000+ apps via native/Zapier. MailerLite has better WooCommerce/Google Analytics ties. ConvertKit tracks subscriber value and revenue per subscriber uniquely.

Analytics: MailerLite shows opens/clicks/geolocation; ConvertKit adds predicted revenue.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

MailerLite wins for beginners—setup in minutes, no learning curve. Interface is clean, with tutorials. ConvertKit is straightforward but tag-based system needs practice.

Support: MailerLite offers 24/7 chat/email (paid), priority for Advanced. ConvertKit has email support (faster on higher plans), extensive Creator Network community, and podcast/resources.

**Tip for switching:** Export lists as CSV from one to import. MailerLite migration is seamless; ConvertKit requires tag cleanup.

**Real advice:** If 70%+ revenue from email, ConvertKit's tools pay off. Track lifetime value pre-purchase.

## Migration and Testing Advice

1. **Audit current setup:** List automations/integrations.
2. **Trial both:** MailerLite free, ConvertKit 14 days.
3. **A/B test campaigns:** Send identical emails.
4. **Check deliverability:** Use GlockApps or Mail-Tester.
5. **Scale test:** Simulate 10k subs.

Common pitfalls: Ignoring tag vs. list differences—ConvertKit users report 15% better engagement post-switch.

## Conclusion

MailerLite edges for affordability and ease (ideal under 5k subs), while ConvertKit dominates for creators scaling revenue. Neither is 'best'—pick by goals. Start with MailerLite if budget-tight; ConvertKit if monetizing content. Whichever, focus on list-building: aim for 1-5% monthly growth via lead magnets.
