# MailerLite vs ConvertKit 2024: Which Email Platform Wins?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. MailerLite and ConvertKit are popular choices for creators, bloggers, small businesses, and e-commerce stores. Both offer powerful features like automations, segmentation, and landing pages, but they cater to slightly different needs.

MailerLite shines with its affordability and beginner-friendly interface, including a free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers. ConvertKit, rebranded from its creator-focused roots, excels in advanced tagging and sequences for selling digital products.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and real-world use cases. By the end, you'll know which tool fits your workflow. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown

Pricing is a top concern for most users selecting an email platform. Both MailerLite and ConvertKit charge based on subscriber count, with unlimited email sends on paid plans.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite offers one of the most generous free plans:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes basic automations, forms, and landing pages.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $9/month for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $69/month for 25,000 subs.
- **Advanced**: $20/month base for 500 subs, with extras like A/B testing and web push notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume users.

Practical advice: Start with the free plan to test campaigns. Upgrade only when you hit limits—ideal for bootstrapped solopreneurs.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now part of Kit by ConvertKit) emphasizes unlimited sends and focuses on creators:
- **Free Trial**: 14 days, no credit card required.
- **Creator Plan**: Starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, $29/month for 1,000 subs, up to $779 for 100,000+.
- **Creator Pro**: From $25/month (300 subs), adding advanced reporting, subscriber scoring, and one-click upsells.

No perpetual free plan, but unlimited emails make it cost-effective for frequent senders. Advice: If you send weekly newsletters, ConvertKit's flat-rate sending saves money long-term compared to per-email fees elsewhere.

**Winner**: MailerLite for budget-conscious starters; ConvertKit for scaling creators with consistent sending.

## Feature Comparison

Both platforms pack robust features, but differences emerge in depth and focus.

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 80+ responsive templates. Photo blocks and plain-text options ensure high deliverability. Advice: Use the classic editor for complex custom HTML to avoid design glitches.

ConvertKit's visual builder is block-based, optimized for newsletters and broadcasts. It auto-saves and previews across devices. Pro tip: Leverage commerce blocks for in-email product sales without leaving the inbox.

### Automations and Sequences

MailerLite automations use a visual workflow builder for triggers like sign-ups or purchases. Supports time delays and A/B splits on paid plans.

ConvertKit's sequences are its standout: Drip campaigns based on tags and actions. Re-engagement automations reactivate dormant subscribers automatically. Advice: For course launches, ConvertKit's tagging system segments users by behavior (e.g., "watched video 1") for personalized follow-ups.

### Segmentation and Tagging

MailerLite: Groups, segments by custom fields, clicks, or opens. Easy for basic lists.

ConvertKit: Advanced tagging (manual/auto) and scoring. Segment by purchase history or engagement score. Practical tip: Tag subscribers as "VIP" after three opens to prioritize them in sends.

### Forms, Landing Pages, and Popups

Both offer embeddable forms and popups. MailerLite includes popups, scroll boxes, and fixed bars with exit-intent triggers. ConvertKit focuses on subscription forms and simple landing pages (Pro adds more).

Advice: MailerLite wins for multi-channel lead gen; use its website builder for quick opt-in pages.

### Integrations and Analytics

Integrations: Both connect to WordPress, Shopify, Zapier (1,000+ apps). MailerLite has native Facebook/Instagram leads; ConvertKit excels with Gumroad/Stripe for creators.

Analytics: Open/click rates, revenue tracking. ConvertKit's Pro dashboard shows subscriber value and predicted revenue.

**Feature Verdict**: ConvertKit for creator automations; MailerLite for versatile lead capture.

## Ease of Use and User Interface

MailerLite's dashboard is clean and fast-loading, with a shallow learning curve. New users can send their first email in minutes. Mobile app for on-the-go management.

ConvertKit's interface is streamlined for newsletters, but tagging takes practice. Advice: Watch ConvertKit's tutorials for sequences—worth the 30-minute investment.

For beginners: MailerLite. For power users: ConvertKit's depth pays off.

## Customer Support and Resources

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive help center, and Facebook group. Response times under 2 hours.

ConvertKit: Email support (business hours), chat for Pro users, creator-focused courses and podcasts.

Practical advice: Join MailerLite's community for peer tips; ConvertKit's blog for strategy.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Small businesses or bloggers under 1,000 subs
- Users needing popups and websites on a budget
- Agencies managing multiple low-volume clients

Example: A local bakery building a list via in-store QR codes—free plan handles it perfectly.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators selling courses/ebooks
- Podcasters with tag-based funnels
- Teams needing subscriber lifetime value tracking

Example: A blogger launching a membership—sequences nurture leads to sales automatically.

## Final Verdict and Recommendation

Neither is "best" universally—it depends on your stage and style.

**Pick MailerLite if**: You're starting out, value free tools, or prioritize lead gen variety. It's practical for 80% of small users.

**Pick ConvertKit if**: You create content, sell digitally, and want automation depth. ROI comes from higher conversions.

Test both: MailerLite's free plan and ConvertKit's trial let you migrate lists easily via CSV. Track your first campaign's open rates and clicks to decide.

Whichever you choose, focus on value-driven emails: Segment ruthlessly, automate welcomes, and A/B test subjects. Consistent sending builds trust and revenue.
