# Email Marketing Pricing Comparison 2024: Top Platforms Side-by-Side

Choosing the right email marketing platform isn't just about features—it's about fitting your budget as your list grows. In this email marketing pricing comparison, we'll break down costs for popular tools like Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, Klaviyo, GetResponse, and Brevo (formerly Sendinblue). Prices are based on current official data as of late 2024 (always verify on their sites as they can change). We'll focus on monthly billing for fairness, highlight free tiers, and provide practical advice for solopreneurs, small businesses, and scaling teams.

This guide helps you calculate true costs based on subscribers, email volume, and needed features like automations or analytics. Expect to pay $0–$500+/mo depending on your scale.

## Key Factors Influencing Email Marketing Pricing

Before diving into numbers, understand what drives costs:

- **Subscriber Count**: Most platforms charge based on contacts (subscribers). Overages add fees.
- **Email Volume**: Some limit sends per month; extras cost more.
- **Features**: Basic sending vs. advanced automations, A/B testing, or e-commerce integrations.
- **Billing Cycle**: Annual plans save 15–20%.
- **Add-Ons**: SMS, landing pages, or support can add $10–100/mo.

**Practical Tip**: Estimate your needs. If you have 1,000 subscribers and send weekly newsletters (4x/mo, 40% open rate = ~400 opens/email), you'll need at least 1,600 sends/mo. Factor in 20–50% growth yearly to avoid surprise upgrades.

## Pricing Models Explained

Platforms use these structures:

- **Freemium**: Free forever up to a limit (e.g., Mailchimp: 500 subs, 1,000 sends/mo).
- **Tiered per Contacts**: Scales with list size (e.g., ActiveCampaign: $29/mo for 500 contacts).
- **Send-Based**: Pay per email volume (e.g., Brevo: €25/mo for 20,000 emails).
- **Enterprise/Custom**: For 100k+ subs, negotiated pricing.

**Pro Advice**: Start free, but migrate early if limits cramp growth. Calculate ROI: If emails drive $1,000/mo sales, a $50 plan pays for itself.

## Mailchimp Pricing Deep Dive

Mailchimp's freemium draws beginners, but costs rise fast.

### Free Plan
Ideal for <500 subs. Unlimited emails? No—1,000/mo cap. No automations.

### Paid Tiers
- **Essentials** ($13–68 for 500–5k): Basic automations, A/B tests.
- **Standard** ($20–300+): Behavioral targeting.
- **Premium** ($350+): Advanced reporting.

**Practical Advice**: Great for e-commerce starters. Watch send limits (12x subs/mo). Upgrade at 2,500 subs to avoid $0.03/extra email.

## ActiveCampaign Pricing Breakdown

Automation powerhouse for mid-sized lists.

- **Lite** ($29+): Email + basic site tracking.
- **Plus** ($49+): Custom objects, machine learning.
- **Professional** ($149+): Full CRM.

**Tip**: If you need sales funnels, this beats Mailchimp. Pause billing during low seasons to save.

## ConvertKit and Klaviyo for Niches

**ConvertKit**: Creators love unlimited sends. $29 gets 1,000 subs + broadcasts/sequences. Scales predictably—no send caps.

**Klaviyo**: Shopify users: Free tier generous, but $60/mo for 1k gets flows/revenue tracking. Best if emails drive >$10k/mo sales.

**Advice**: Test imports—ConvertKit's tagging shines for segments.

## Budget Picks: Under $50/Mo

- **Brevo**: Unlimited contacts, pay per send. Perfect high-volume newsletters.
- **GetResponse**: All-in-one with landing pages.
- **Mailchimp Essentials**: If you stay under sends.

**Scaling Tip**: Track churn (aim <2%/mo). Use formulas: Cost = (Subs / Tier Max) * Base Price + Overages.

## Value for Growing Businesses ($50–200/Mo)

- **ActiveCampaign Plus**: ROI king for leads.
- **ConvertKit Pro**: Visual automations.
- **GetResponse Plus**: Webinars boost engagement.

**Pro Tip**: Negotiate enterprise at 50k+ subs. Integrate with Zapier to cut tool costs.

## Hidden Costs and Savings Strategies

- **Overages**: Mailchimp 3¢/contact; Klaviyo scales auto.
- **Support**: Free chat? Rare—pay for priority.
- **Migration**: Free tools like Zapier, but time = money.

**Savings Hacks**:
1. Annual prepay.
2. Clean lists quarterly (remove inactives).
3. Start with volume-based if irregular sends (Brevo).
4. Trial all—most 14–30 days free.
5. Monitor deliverability (all have tools).

## When to Upgrade: Real Scenarios

- **Solopreneur (0–1k subs)**: Free Mailchimp/ConvertKit.
- **Small Biz (1–5k)**: GetResponse $49.
- **E-com (5–20k)**: Klaviyo $100+.
- **Agency (10k+)**: ActiveCampaign Pro.

**Case Study Tip**: A blog with 2k subs switched from Mailchimp ($60) to ConvertKit ($50)—saved $120/yr, gained 20% opens via tagging.

## Conclusion: Pick Your Winner

No one-size-fits-all. For pure affordability: Brevo or GetResponse. Automations: ActiveCampaign. Creators: ConvertKit. E-com: Klaviyo. Mailchimp for beginners.

**Action Steps**:
1. List your subs/sends/features.
2. Use calculators on sites (e.g., ActiveCampaign's).
3. Start a free trial.
4. Track metrics post-launch.
