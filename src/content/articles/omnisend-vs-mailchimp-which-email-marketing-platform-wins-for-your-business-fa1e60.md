# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's customer engagement and revenue. With so many options, pitting Omnisend against Mailchimp makes sense for many users. Both are popular, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a generalist powerhouse known for its beginner-friendly interface, while Omnisend shines in ecommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

This comparison dives into pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more to help you decide. Whether you're a small business owner, ecommerce entrepreneur, or marketer scaling up, we'll provide practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer tiered plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends—ideal for startups testing the waters. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for enterprise needs. Key features like advanced automations and A/B testing unlock at higher tiers (Standard: $20/month; Premium: $350/month).

**Practical Tip:** If you're under 2,000 contacts and need basic campaigns, stick to the free tier. But watch out—extra sends or contacts quickly inflate costs.

### Omnisend Pricing
Omnisend skips a traditional free plan but offers a generous free tier for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month. Paid plans begin at $16/month (Standard) for unlimited emails to 500 contacts, with Pro at $59/month adding advanced segmentation and unlimited SMS. No contact-based overages; you pay per plan limits.

**Practical Tip:** Ecommerce stores benefit from Omnisend's unlimited sends on paid plans, making it cost-effective for high-volume lists without surprise fees.

**Winner:** Omnisend for growing ecommerce lists; Mailchimp for absolute beginners on a zero budget.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities

Both platforms excel in email automation, but Omnisend's omnichannel edge sets it apart.

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with hundreds of customizable templates. It's great for non-designers creating newsletters or promos.

Omnisend matches with a responsive builder and ecommerce-specific templates (e.g., cart recovery, upsell flows). Its popup builder for list growth is more advanced.

**Practical Advice:** Use Mailchimp for content-heavy emails like blogs; Omnisend for product-focused campaigns.

### Automation Workflows

#### Mailchimp Automations
Single-step automations on lower plans; customer journeys (multi-step) require Standard or higher. Good for welcome series or re-engagement.

#### Omnisend Automations
Pre-built workflows for abandoned carts, win-back, and cross-sells. Omnichannel triggers (e.g., SMS after email) make it ecommerce gold.

**Practical Tip:** Set up Omnisend's cart recovery first—users report 15-30% recovery rates with minimal setup.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp offers tag-based segmentation; behavioral data needs Premium.

Omnisend's 360° customer profiles track purchase history across channels for hyper-personalized sends.

**Practical Advice:** Segment by RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) in both, but Omnisend automates it better for sales boosts.

### SMS and Push Notifications
Mailchimp added SMS recently (via integrations), but it's not native.

Omnisend bundles SMS, web push, and popups seamlessly, with credits included in Pro plans.

**Winner:** Omnisend for multichannel marketing.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface feels polished for beginners—quick onboarding with tutorials.

Omnisend has a steeper learning curve due to advanced features but excels with ecommerce integrations.

**Support:** Mailchimp offers 24/7 email/chat on paid plans; priority for Premium.
Omnisend provides live chat, email, and a knowledge base; highly rated for responsiveness.

**Practical Tip:** Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing; migrate to Omnisend as you scale.

## Integrations and Ecommerce Fit

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, WordPress, Shopify).

Omnisend natively supports Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce—auto-syncing orders/inventory. Plus Zapier for more.

**Practical Advice:** For Shopify stores, Omnisend's one-click sync saves hours vs. Mailchimp's manual imports.

## Analytics and Reporting

Both provide open rates, clicks, revenue tracking. Mailchimp's dashboards are comparative; Omnisend ties metrics to revenue per channel.

**Tip:** Track ROI with UTM parameters in both—focus on attributed revenue, not just opens.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros:** Free plan, vast template library, strong community resources.
**Cons:** Pricing jumps with contacts/sends, limited omnichannel.

### Omnisend
**Pros:** Ecommerce automations, unlimited emails, multichannel.
**Cons:** No robust free plan beyond basics, less ideal for non-ecom.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp if:** You're a blogger, service business, or solopreneur starting free. Great for content marketing.
- **Choose Omnisend if:** You run an online store (Shopify/Woo) needing cart recovery, SMS, and sales automations.

**Migration Tip:** Export lists via CSV from Mailchimp to Omnisend easily. Test with a small segment first.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce due to its tailored automations and omnichannel power, while Mailchimp remains the go-to for beginners and general marketing. Evaluate your business type, list size, and channels. Most users find Omnisend delivers higher ROI for sales-driven campaigns—start with their 14-day trial.
