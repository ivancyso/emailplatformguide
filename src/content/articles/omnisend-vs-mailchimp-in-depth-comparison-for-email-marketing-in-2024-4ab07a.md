# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for Email Marketing in 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Omnisend against Mailchimp is a common debate, especially for e-commerce stores and small businesses. Both tools offer robust features for building lists, automating campaigns, and analyzing performance, but they cater to slightly different needs.

In this comparison, we'll break down key aspects like pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and support. Whether you're a beginner scaling up or an e-commerce pro needing omnichannel marketing, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing since 2001. It's renowned for its user-friendly interface and free plan, making it accessible for solopreneurs and small teams. Key strengths include drag-and-drop email builders, audience segmentation, and basic automation.

**Practical Advice:** Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing. Use its free tier (up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month) to test campaigns without commitment. Focus on its pre-built templates for quick newsletters.

However, as your list grows, costs can rise, and advanced e-commerce features like product recommendations are limited compared to specialists.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend is designed for e-commerce, launched in 2014, and excels in omnichannel marketing (email, SMS, push notifications). It integrates seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce, offering workflows tailored for abandoned carts, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells.

**Practical Advice:** Opt for Omnisend if you run an online store. Set up its pre-built automation workflows right away—these can recover 15-30% of lost sales through targeted reminders, based on industry benchmarks from similar tools.

It's more automation-focused but has a steeper learning curve for non-e-commerce users.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major factor when choosing between Omnisend vs Mailchimp.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month.
- **Essentials:** $13/month (50k sends, 5k contacts).
- **Standard:** $20/month (basic automations).
- **Premium:** $350+/month (advanced features, A/B testing).

Mailchimp charges based on contacts and sends, with overages adding up quickly.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month.
- **Standard:** $16/month (unlimited emails for 500 contacts).
- **Pro:** $59/month (advanced segmentation, A/B testing).
- **Enterprise:** Custom.

Omnisend offers unlimited emails in paid plans, making it cost-effective for high-volume senders.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your monthly sends and contact growth. For 2,500 contacts sending weekly newsletters (4k sends/month), Mailchimp Essentials costs $13 but limits features, while Omnisend Standard at $16 gives unlimited sends and better automations. Use their pricing calculators for precise quotes.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates
Both have drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp offers 100+ templates with strong mobile optimization. Omnisend shines with dynamic content blocks for product feeds.

**Practical Tip:** In Mailchimp, use 'Classic Builder' for simplicity. In Omnisend, enable 'Product Picker' to auto-populate emails with real inventory.

### Automation Workflows
Omnisend leads with 200+ pre-built workflows for e-commerce events. Mailchimp's automations are solid for basics like birthdays but lack depth in cart recovery.

**Practical Tip:** Test Omnisend's abandoned cart flow: Trigger emails/SMS at 1hr, 24hr, 72hr intervals with escalating discounts to boost recovery rates.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp uses tags and groups. Omnisend leverages behavioral data (browsed products, order history) for hyper-personalization.

### Analytics and Reporting
Both provide open rates, clicks, revenue tracking. Omnisend's e-commerce dashboard shows ROI per campaign.

**Practical Tip:** Track 'Revenue per Recipient' in Omnisend to justify ad spend; Mailchimp's reports suit content-focused lists.

### Additional Channels
Omnisend includes SMS and web push (Pro plan+). Mailchimp focuses on email with add-ons.

## Ease of Use

Mailchimp wins for beginners—intuitive dashboard, no coding needed. Omnisend requires setup for integrations but offers guided onboarding.

**Practical Advice:** Spend 30 minutes on Mailchimp's tutorials for quick wins. For Omnisend, connect your store first via 1-click integrations to unlock power features.

## Integrations

Mailchimp: 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).
Omnisend: E-commerce heavy (Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives), plus Zapier.

**Practical Tip:** If using Shopify, Omnisend syncs customer data automatically. Mailchimp needs manual imports for optimal results.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/chat for paid plans; vast knowledge base.
Omnisend: 24/7 live chat, dedicated managers for Pro+.

**Practical Advice:** Use community forums for both, but escalate to Omnisend chat for workflow issues—they respond in minutes.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros:** Free plan, easy, creative templates.
**Cons:** Expensive scaling, limited e-commerce automations.

### Omnisend
**Pros:** E-commerce focus, unlimited sends, omnichannel.
**Cons:** Smaller free plan, learning curve.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp if:** You're a blogger, service business, or beginner with <5k contacts needing simple emails.
- **Choose Omnisend if:** E-commerce store owner prioritizing sales automations and multi-channel.

**Practical Advice:** Audit your needs: High cart abandonment? Omnisend. Content newsletters? Mailchimp. Trial both free plans for 14-30 days.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for e-commerce with superior automations and value on sends, while Mailchimp remains the go-to for ease and free entry. Neither is 'best' universally—align with your goals. For growing stores, Omnisend's ROI potential shines; for casual users, Mailchimp suffices.
