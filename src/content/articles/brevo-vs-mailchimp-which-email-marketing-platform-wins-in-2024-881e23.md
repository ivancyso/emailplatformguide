# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can make or break your email marketing strategy. Both are popular platforms designed for businesses of all sizes, but they cater to different needs. Brevo emphasizes affordability and multichannel marketing, while Mailchimp shines in user-friendly design and robust automation.

This guide breaks down key aspects like pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, deliverability, and support. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or scaling enterprise, we'll help you decide which tool aligns with your goals. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing platform. Both Brevo and Mailchimp offer free plans, but their paid tiers differ significantly.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo's model is contact-based with daily email limits on lower plans:
- **Free Plan**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails per day (about 9,000/month).
- **Starter**: Starts at $25/month for 20,000 emails/month, unlimited contacts.
- **Business**: From $65/month, adds features like A/B testing and landing pages.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume senders.

Brevo excels for transactional emails (e.g., order confirmations) with no extra fees for sends beyond contacts. It's cost-effective for growing lists without punishing you for high engagement.

**Practical Advice**: If your business sends frequent transactional emails or SMS, Brevo's structure saves money. Calculate your monthly sends: if under 20,000, Starter keeps costs low.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp uses a tiered, contact-based model:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts (scales up).
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, adds automation.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced segmentation.

Costs rise quickly with audience size—e.g., 10,000 contacts on Standard is ~$100/month. Overages for extra sends add up.

**Practical Advice**: Mailchimp suits low-volume newsletters. Audit your list: if growing past 5,000 contacts, budget for steep increases. Use their pricing calculator for accuracy.

**Winner on Pricing**: Brevo for most users due to predictable costs and higher free limits.

## Features: Core Capabilities Compared

Both platforms pack email essentials, but differences emerge in automation, templates, and extras.

### Email Builder and Templates

- **Brevo**: Drag-and-drop editor with 48 responsive templates. Strong customization via HTML. Includes SMS and WhatsApp templates.
- **Mailchimp**: Intuitive drag-and-drop with 100+ free templates. Excellent for beginners with content blocks and AI-assisted design.

**Practical Advice**: Test both editors. Brevo for custom HTML pros; Mailchimp for quick, polished newsletters.

### Automation and Workflows

- **Brevo**: Automation builder for drip campaigns, abandoned carts, and re-engagement. Multichannel (email + SMS). Classic and updated workflows.
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journey Builder for complex automations. Triggers like purchases or site visits. Behavioral targeting shines.

**Practical Advice**: For e-commerce, set up Brevo's cart abandonment (SMS boosts recovery 20-30%). Mailchimp's journeys suit nurture sequences.

### Segmentation and Analytics

- **Brevo**: Tags, custom fields, and real-time reporting. Heatmaps for engagement.
- **Mailchimp**: Advanced segmentation (e.g., by behavior). Detailed reports with AI insights.

**Practical Advice**: Segment lists weekly—e.g., engaged vs. inactive. Brevo's CRM integration tracks leads seamlessly.

### Additional Features

Brevo: Built-in CRM, chat, ads, landing pages, forms. Mailchimp: E-commerce integrations, postcards, social ads.

**Winner on Features**: Tie—Brevo for all-in-one, Mailchimp for marketing depth.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface feels like a creative studio—intuitive for non-techies. Onboarding includes tutorials and templates.

Brevo is straightforward but leans functional. Dashboard shows sends, opens, and revenue. Quick setup for transactional emails.

**Practical Advice**: Start with free trials. Mailchimp if visual design matters; Brevo if you need CRM from day one. Import lists via CSV—clean data first to avoid bounces.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for beginners.

## Integrations and Scalability

- **Brevo**: 300+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress, Zapier). API for developers.
- **Mailchimp**: 250+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook). Strong WooCommerce support.

Both scale well, but Brevo handles millions of emails daily with dedicated IPs on Enterprise.

**Practical Advice**: Check Zapier compatibility. For Shopify stores, test webhook syncs.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Deliverability impacts ROI—aim for 95%+ inbox rates.

- **Brevo**: 99% deliverability, strong authentication (DKIM/SPF). EU-based, GDPR compliant.
- **Mailchimp**: 98-99%, with domain authentication tools. Excellent blacklist monitoring.

**Practical Advice**: Warm up IPs gradually (start 50-100/day). Use double opt-in. Monitor spam scores via GlockApps.

**Winner**: Brevo edges out for transactional reliability.

## Customer Support

- **Brevo**: Email/chat 24/5 (paid), knowledge base, academy.
- **Mailchimp**: 24/7 email/phone (higher tiers), extensive help center.

**Practical Advice**: Paid plans unlock priority help. Communities like Reddit's r/Emailmarketing offer peer tips.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- Transactional-heavy businesses (e.g., SaaS, e-com).
- Budget-conscious teams needing SMS/CRM.
- International senders (multilingual support).

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Content creators and agencies.
- Beginners prioritizing templates/automation.
- Brands with established audiences.

## Conclusion: Pick the Right Tool for Your Needs

Brevo vs Mailchimp? Brevo wins for affordability, multichannel, and scalability—ideal if you're cost-sensitive or transactional-focused. Mailchimp excels in ease and creative automation for marketers.

**Recommendation**: Try both free plans. Track metrics like open rates (target 20-30%) and ROI after 3 months. Migrate via CSV exports if needed.

Ultimately, the best platform drives results. Focus on list building, value-driven content, and testing. Your audience decides the winner.
