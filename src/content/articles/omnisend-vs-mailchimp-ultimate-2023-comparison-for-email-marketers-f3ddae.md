# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With over 4 billion email users worldwide, email remains a top channel for customer engagement, boasting average ROI of $36 for every $1 spent. But with platforms like Omnisend and Mailchimp dominating the market, how do you decide?

This head-to-head comparison dives into Omnisend vs Mailchimp based on real user experiences, official documentation, and key metrics. We'll cover pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you—whether you're a small business owner, e-commerce store manager, or marketer—make an informed choice. Let's break it down.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, launched in 2001, is one of the most recognized names in email marketing. It serves over 12 million users and powers campaigns for brands like Spotify and IKEA. Its strengths lie in user-friendly design and robust free plan.

**Key Features:**
- Drag-and-drop email builder with 100+ templates.
- Basic automation like welcome series and abandoned cart (in paid plans).
- Audience segmentation by tags, groups, and behaviors.
- Integrations with 300+ apps, including Shopify, WordPress, and Google Analytics.
- Reporting on open rates, clicks, and revenue.

Mailchimp excels for beginners and content creators needing simple newsletters. However, advanced e-commerce features require higher tiers.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend, founded in 2014, targets e-commerce businesses with omnichannel marketing. It supports email, SMS, and web push notifications, integrating seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce.

**Key Features:**n- Advanced email builder with dynamic content blocks.
- Pre-built workflows for cart recovery, win-back, and cross-sells.
- Omnichannel campaigns combining email + SMS.
- Deep segmentation using purchase history and product data.
- Pop-up forms and landing pages for list building.
- Comprehensive analytics with revenue tracking.

Omnisend shines for online stores aiming to boost sales through personalized, multi-channel messaging.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major factor. Both offer free plans, but scales differ based on contacts and features.

| Feature/Plan | Mailchimp | Omnisend |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| **Free** | Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/mo, basic features | Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/mo, full automation & SMS trials |
| **Essentials/Standard** | $13/mo (500 contacts) to $350+/mo (50k) | $16/mo (500 contacts) to $59/mo (10k), unlimited emails |
| **Premium/Pro** | $350+/mo for advanced automation | $59+/mo (10k+), adds A/B testing, dedicated IP |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Custom |

**Practical Advice:** Start with Mailchimp's free plan for testing newsletters. Switch to Omnisend if your list exceeds 250 and you sell online—its pay-per-contact model with unlimited sends saves money on high-volume campaigns. Always calculate based on your contact list size using their pricing calculators.

## Feature Face-Off: Key Differences

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's builder is intuitive with mobile-responsive templates. Customize via drag-and-drop; add merge tags easily.

Omnisend offers more dynamic options, like product blocks that pull live inventory from your store. **Tip:** Use Omnisend for product-focused emails to increase click-through rates by 20-30% via personalization.

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp provides simple automations in Standard plans ($75+/mo). Omnisend's free plan includes unlimited workflows, including omnichannel sequences.

**Practical Advice:** Set up a 3-email abandoned cart flow: Email 1 (1hr), SMS 2 (24hr), Email 3 (72hr). Omnisend users report 15-25% recovery rates; test splits to optimize timing.

### Integrations and E-commerce Sync

Both integrate with major platforms. Mailchimp syncs basic data; Omnisend pulls real-time product catalogs, customer data, and orders.

**Pro Tip:** For Shopify users, Omnisend auto-triggers post-purchase upsells. Connect via native apps—no Zapier needed for core functions.

### SMS and Omnichannel

Mailchimp added SMS recently (Premium plans). Omnisend bundles it affordably, with 98% delivery rates.

**Advice:** SMS open rates hit 98% vs email's 20-30%. Use for urgent promos like flash sales, but comply with TCPA opt-ins.

### Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp tracks basics + revenue per email. Omnisend adds cohort analysis and SMS ROI.

**Tip:** Focus on metrics like attributed revenue. Aim for 25%+ open rates; A/B test subjects weekly.

## Ease of Use and User Interface

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners—clean dashboard, quick setup (under 10 mins).

Omnisend has a slight learning curve for omnichannel but offers guided tours and templates. **Advice:** Spend 30 mins on onboarding videos. Both score 4.5+ on G2/Capterra.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for paid; chat 24/7 on higher plans.

Omnisend: Priority chat/email, free migration help, and dedicated managers on Pro.

**Practical Tip:** Join communities—Mailchimp's forums are vast; Omnisend's Slack for e-com tips.

## Pros and Cons

**Mailchimp Pros:**
- Generous free tier.
- Brand recognition.
- Great for non-profits/blogs.

**Cons:**
- Expensive scaling.
- Limited e-com depth.

**Omnisend Pros:**
- E-com focused.
- Unlimited emails.
- Multi-channel.

**Cons:**
- Smaller free plan.
- Less ideal for non-stores.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick Mailchimp if:** General marketing, small lists (<5k), budget newsletters.
- **Pick Omnisend if:** E-commerce, need SMS/push, revenue tracking.

**Migration Advice:** Export CSV from one, import to the other. Test with 10% list first.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for e-commerce with superior automation and omnichannel at better value. Mailchimp suits broad, simple needs. Evaluate your goals: revenue-driven? Omnisend. Brand awareness? Mailchimp.
