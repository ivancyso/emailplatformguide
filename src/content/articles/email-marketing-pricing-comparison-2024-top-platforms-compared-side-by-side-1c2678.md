# Email Marketing Pricing Comparison 2024: Top Platforms Compared Side-by-Side

Choosing the right email marketing platform isn't just about features—it's about finding the best fit for your budget. In this comprehensive email marketing pricing comparison, we'll break down costs for popular tools like Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, Brevo (formerly Sendinblue), ConvertKit, and GetResponse. We'll focus on real pricing tiers, what you get, and practical tips for small businesses, e-commerce stores, and growing teams. All data is based on official pricing pages as of mid-2024; always check for updates or promotions.

Whether you're starting with 500 subscribers or scaling to 10,000+, understanding per-subscriber costs and feature gates is key to avoiding surprises. Let's dive in.

## Why Pricing Matters More Than Ever in Email Marketing

Email marketing delivers an average ROI of $36 for every $1 spent, but overpaying for unused features can eat into profits. Platforms charge based on:
- **Contact list size**: Most scale monthly fees with subscribers.
- **Email sends**: Some limit daily/monthly volumes.
- **Features**: Automation, A/B testing, or analytics often unlock at higher tiers.

**Practical tip**: Calculate your cost per 1,000 subscribers (CP1K). For example, if a plan costs $50 for 5,000 contacts, that's $10 CP1K. Track your growth to predict expenses.

Free plans exist but cap sends or features—great for testing, not scaling. Paid plans start at $10-30/month for basics.

## Key Factors to Consider Beyond Price

Don't chase the cheapest option. Evaluate:
1. **Deliverability**: Higher rates mean more opens/revenue.
2. **Integrations**: With Shopify, WordPress, etc.
3. **Ease of use**: Drag-and-drop vs. code-heavy.
4. **Support**: Live chat vs. email tickets.
5. **Scalability**: Linear pricing vs. jumps at thresholds.

**Pro advice**: Start with free trials (most offer 14-30 days). Import a test list and send campaigns to gauge value.

## Detailed Pricing Breakdown: Top Email Marketing Platforms

Here's a head-to-head of five leading tools, focusing on starter to mid-tier plans. Prices are monthly, billed annually where applicable (monthly is 15-20% more).

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's freemium model shines for beginners:
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month. Basic templates, no automation.
- **Essentials**: $13/mo (500 contacts), scales to $75 (5,000). Single-step automations, A/B testing.
- **Standard**: $20/mo (500), up to $150 (5,000). Multi-step automations, custom landing pages.
- **Premium**: $350/mo (500+). Advanced segmentation, unlimited sends.

**Best for**: Non-profits or tiny lists. CP1K around $15-20 for mid-plans. Drawback: Pricing jumps sharply post-10k contacts.

### ActiveCampaign Pricing

Automation powerhouse with strong CRM:
- **Starter**: $29/mo (500 contacts), $149 (5,000). Basic automation, site tracking.
- **Plus**: $49/mo (500), $259 (5,000). Custom reporting, lead scoring.
- **Professional**: $149/mo (500), $499 (5,000). Predictive sending, machine learning.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

**Best for**: E-commerce with complex funnels. Excellent deliverability (95%+). CP1K ~$30 starter, but feature-rich.

### Brevo (Sendinblue) Pricing

Send-focused with transactional emails:
- **Free**: 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts. Basic automation.
- **Starter**: $25/mo (20k emails), scales with volume not contacts.
- **Business**: $65/mo (20k), adds retargeting.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

**Best for**: High-volume senders on a budget. Unique volume-based pricing avoids contact penalties. CP1K effectively low.

### ConvertKit Pricing

Creator-friendly, simple scaling:
- **Free**: Up to 300 subscribers (formerly 1k sends).
- **Creator**: $15/mo (300 subs), $29 (1k), $79 (4k). Visual automations, landing pages.
- **Creator Pro**: $25/mo (300), adds revenue tracking.

**Best for**: Bloggers/influencers. Transparent, no send limits. CP1K ~$20.

### GetResponse Pricing

All-in-one with webinars:
- **Email Marketing**: $19/mo (1k contacts), $59 (5k). Autoresponders, funnels.
- **Marketing Automation**: $59/mo (1k), $199 (5k). Abandoned cart, webinars.
- **Ecommerce Marketing**: $119/mo (1k).

**Best for**: Teams needing webinars. CP1K competitive at $12-40.

## Best Value Picks by Use Case

- **Budget starter (<1k subs)**: Brevo or ConvertKit—under $25/mo with generous limits.
- **Growing SMB (1-10k)**: Mailchimp Standard or GetResponse—balanced features.
- **E-commerce heavy**: ActiveCampaign—ROI from automations justifies cost.

**Practical advice**: Use calculators on sites (e.g., Mailchimp's). Factor add-ons like SMS ($0.01/message) or dedicated IPs ($10-300/mo).

## Practical Tips for Choosing and Optimizing Costs

1. **Audit your list**: Remove inactives quarterly to stay in lower tiers.
2. **Test deliverability**: Tools like Mail-Tester.com; poor rates waste money.
3. **Bundle features**: Skip if not using (e.g., don't pay for webinars).
4. **Negotiate enterprise**: Over 50k contacts? Email sales for discounts.
5. **Monitor usage**: Most dashboards show overages; set alerts.
6. **Migrate wisely**: Export/import tools like NeverBounce for cleaning.

**Real-world example**: A 2k-sub list on Mailchimp Essentials (~$30/mo) vs. ActiveCampaign Starter (~$50/mo). If you need deep automations, invest; else save.

Switching? Most allow easy CSV exports. Time it post-trial.

## Conclusion: Pick the Plan That Scales with You

This email marketing pricing comparison shows no one-size-fits-all—Brevo wins on volume, ActiveCampaign on power, Mailchimp on simplicity. Start with your subscriber count, must-have features, and growth projections. Trial 2-3 platforms, run real campaigns, and commit annually for savings.
