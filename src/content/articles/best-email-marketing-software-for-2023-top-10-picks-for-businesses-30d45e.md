# Best Email Marketing Software for 2023: Top 10 Picks for Businesses

Choosing the right email marketing software is crucial for businesses looking to nurture leads, boost engagement, and drive sales. With so many options available, finding the best email marketing software can feel overwhelming. This roundup focuses on proven platforms based on user reviews, features, and real-world performance from sources like G2, Capterra, and official sites. We'll cover key factors to consider, detailed overviews of the top 10, a comparison table, and practical tips to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or marketing manager, these tools offer drag-and-drop editors, automation, analytics, and integrations to scale your email efforts.

## What Makes the Best Email Marketing Software?

Before diving into the list, understand the essentials. The best email marketing software balances ease of use, deliverability, automation, and value for money.

### Key Features to Prioritize

- **Drag-and-Drop Builders**: Intuitive editors for creating professional emails without coding.
- **Automation Workflows**: Trigger emails based on user actions, like welcome series or abandoned cart reminders.
- **List Segmentation**: Divide subscribers by behavior, location, or purchase history for targeted campaigns.
- **Analytics & Reporting**: Track open rates, clicks, conversions, and revenue per email.
- **Integrations**: Connect with CRM (e.g., HubSpot), e-commerce (Shopify), or Zapier.
- **Deliverability**: High inbox placement rates to avoid spam folders.
- **Compliance Tools**: Built-in GDPR, CAN-SPAM support, and double opt-in.

### Pricing Considerations

Most offer free tiers for small lists (under 1,000-2,000 subscribers). Paid plans scale from $10-$50/month for starters, up to enterprise levels. Factor in contacts, sends, and add-ons like SMS.

### Deliverability and Support

Look for 95%+ deliverability. Responsive support via chat, email, or phone is vital for beginners.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software

Here's our curated list of the best email marketing software, ranked by versatility, user satisfaction (4.5+ stars on review sites), and features.

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp remains a leader for its free plan (up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month) and user-friendly interface. Ideal for beginners.

**Pros**:
- Excellent templates and A/B testing.
- Audience insights and behavioral targeting.
- Integrates with 250+ apps.

**Cons**:
- Automation limited on free plan.
- Pricing jumps after 500 contacts ($13/month for 500).

**Best For**: Small businesses starting out. Practical tip: Use their content optimizer to improve subject lines—tests show 20-30% open rate boosts.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

Advanced automation powerhouse with CRM features. Starts at $29/month for 500 contacts.

**Pros**:
- Deep segmentation and machine learning scoring.
- Site tracking and split automation.
- Strong reporting on ROI.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve.
- No free plan beyond trial.

**Best For**: E-commerce and lead nurturing. Tip: Set up 'if/then' automations for cart abandonment—recover 10-15% lost sales.

### 3. ConvertKit (now Kit)

Creator-focused with unlimited sends on paid plans ($29/month).

**Pros**:
- Visual automations and tagging.
- Landing pages and forms included.
- Subscriber value tracking.

**Cons**:
- Basic templates.
- Geared toward solopreneurs.

**Best For**: Bloggers and course creators. Tip: Use broadcasts for newsletters; segment by tag for 40% higher engagement.

### 4. Constant Contact

All-in-one with event management. $12/month for 500 contacts.

**Pros**:
- AI-powered subject lines.
- Social media ads integration.
- 60-day free trial.

**Cons**:
- Limited automation depth.
- Higher pricing for growth.

**Best For**: Non-profits and local businesses. Tip: Leverage list-building tools like pop-ups to grow lists 2x faster.

### 5. GetResponse

Conversion-focused with webinars. $19/month for 1,000 contacts.

**Pros**:
- Funnel builder and sales funnels.
- High deliverability (99%).
- Autoresponders included.

**Cons**:
- Interface feels dated.

**Best For**: E-commerce funnels. Tip: A/B test landing pages within emails for 25% conversion lifts.

### 6. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Multichannel (email + SMS). Free up to 300 emails/day.

**Pros**:
- Transactional emails.
- Chat and ads.
- Affordable scaling ($25/month).

**Cons**:
- Daily send limits on free.

**Best For**: SMBs needing SMS. Tip: Combine email/SMS for 6x open rates.

### 7. AWeber

Reliable since 1998. $19/month for 500 subscribers.

**Pros**:
- SparkPost integration for deliverability.
- Tag-based automation.
- 24/7 support.

**Cons**:
- Fewer modern templates.

**Best For**: Long-term users. Tip: Use RSS-to-email for consistent newsletters.

### 8. Klaviyo

E-commerce specialist. Free up to 250 contacts.

**Pros**:
- Predictive analytics.
- Shopify integration.
- Flow builder.

**Cons**:
- E-com focused only.

**Best For**: Online stores. Tip: Post-purchase flows increase LTV by 30%.

### 9. Drip

Behavioral email for e-com. $39/month.

**Pros**:
- Customer journey mapping.
- Revenue tracking.

**Cons**:
- Pricey for small lists.

**Best For**: Advanced marketers.

### 10. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free with CRM. Paid from $20/month.

**Pros**:
- Full inbound suite.
- Smart content.

**Cons**:
- Best with HubSpot ecosystem.

**Best For**: Growing teams.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing Software for You

1. **Assess Your Needs**: Small list? Go free like Mailchimp. Complex automations? ActiveCampaign.
2. **Test with Trials**: Sign up for 14-30 day trials; import a test list and build a campaign.
3. **Check Integrations**: Ensure it works with your stack (e.g., WordPress, Shopify).
4. **Review Deliverability**: Use tools like Mail-Tester pre-launch.
5. **Scale Planning**: Calculate costs at 2x-5x current list size.
6. **Practical Setup Advice**:
   - Start with double opt-in to build clean lists.
   - Aim for 20-30% open rates initially; optimize with personalization.
   - Segment ruthlessly: Engaged vs. inactive.
   - A/B test everything: subjects, send times (Tues-Thurs 10AM best).
   - Monitor unsubscribes (<0.5% ideal).
