# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate Comparison Guide

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and grow your business. For creators, bloggers, and small business owners, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both tools offer powerful features for building email lists, sending newsletters, and automating workflows, but they cater to slightly different needs.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, customer support, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur starting out or scaling your operations, you'll get practical advice to decide which platform fits your goals. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Affordability vs Value

Pricing is often the first hurdle when evaluating email marketing tools. Both MailerLite and ConvertKit use tiered plans based on subscriber count, but their structures differ significantly.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite is known for its budget-friendly approach:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 2,500. Unlimited emails.
- **Advanced**: From $21/month, adds A/B testing and web personalization.

Practical tip: If you're under 1,000 subscribers, start free and upgrade seamlessly. No credit card required, making it low-risk for testing.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) focuses on creators with higher starting prices:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers (limited to 10,000 emails/year—much stricter than MailerLite).
- **Creator**: Starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, $29 for 1,000. Unlimited emails, broadcasts, and sequences.
- **Creator Pro**: From $25/month, includes advanced reporting, subscriber scoring, and one-click upsells.

Practical tip: ConvertKit's pricing jumps quickly with growth. Calculate your projected subscriber growth; it's costlier for lists over 5,000 (e.g., $79/month vs MailerLite's $50).

**Winner**: MailerLite for affordability, especially startups. ConvertKit justifies costs for revenue-focused creators.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop Simplicity vs Intuitive Creator Tools

User interface matters for non-technical users. Both platforms prioritize simplicity, but approaches vary.

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Editor
MailerLite shines with its intuitive drag-and-drop builder. Customize emails, popups, and landing pages without coding. The dashboard is clean, with quick access to campaigns, automations, and analytics.

**Pro Tip**: Use the template library (100+ responsive designs) to launch your first campaign in under 30 minutes. Mobile previews ensure flawless delivery across devices.

### ConvertKit's Visual Automations
ConvertKit uses a visual canvas for sequences (their automation term). Tag-based subscriber management is powerful—no segments needed. The editor is block-based, similar to WordPress Gutenberg.

**Pro Tip**: Set up a welcome sequence by dragging blocks: tag subscriber > send email > wait > tag again. Ideal for evergreen funnels like lead magnets.

**Winner**: Tie—MailerLite for beginners, ConvertKit for automation pros.

## Key Features Breakdown

### Automation and Workflows
- **MailerLite**: Workflow builder with triggers (e.g., signup, purchase). Supports A/B testing on Advanced plans. Time-based delays and multi-step paths.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences are king here. Unlimited automations with tagging logic. Re-engagement campaigns auto-reactivate dormant subscribers.

Practical advice: For simple abandoned cart flows, MailerLite suffices. ConvertKit excels in content upgrades and one-to-many selling.

### Forms, Popups, and Landing Pages
Both offer embeddable forms and popups:
- MailerLite: Exit-intent popups, scroll boxes, and 1-click signup forms. Unlimited landing pages on all plans.
- ConvertKit: Inline, embedded, and flyout forms. Native landing pages with commerce integrations.

**Tip**: Test popups on your site—MailerLite's analytics show conversion rates per form.

### Templates and Design
MailerLite: 80+ free templates, highly customizable.
ConvertKit: Fewer but creator-optimized (e.g., newsletter-focused).

### Analytics and Reporting
- MailerLite: Open rates, clicks, bounces, revenue tracking.
- ConvertKit: Detailed subscriber activity, revenue per subscriber, churn prediction (Pro plan).

**Winner**: ConvertKit for revenue insights.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

No platform works in isolation. Both integrate with 1,000+ apps via Zapier.

- **MailerLite**: Native with WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics. Facebook lead ads sync.
- **ConvertKit**: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable, Patreon. Commerce tools for digital sales.

Practical advice: If you're on Shopify, MailerLite's direct integration saves time. ConvertKit pairs best with creator stacks like Memberful.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive help center, video tutorials. Free migration service.
- **ConvertKit**: Email support (response within 24h), podcast, creator community. Priority for Pro users.

**Tip**: MailerLite's chat resolves issues instantly—great for urgent campaigns.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
Small businesses, bloggers, and nonprofits with budgets under $50/month. Perfect if you need popups, landing pages, and basic automations without complexity.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Content creators, course sellers, and podcasters prioritizing sequences and revenue tracking. Worth it if your email list drives $1K+ monthly sales.

Practical decision framework:
1. Subscribers <2,500? Start MailerLite free.
2. Heavy automation needs? Trial ConvertKit.
3. Migrate easily—both offer CSV imports.

## Conclusion

**MailerLite vs ConvertKit** boils down to your stage and style. MailerLite wins for cost-effective, all-in-one marketing, ideal for beginners growing lists affordably. ConvertKit edges out for sophisticated creators monetizing audiences with precision automations.

Test both free plans side-by-side. Track your first campaign's open rates (aim for 20-40%) and decide based on real data. Whichever you pick, consistent value-driven emails will build your list faster than any tool.
