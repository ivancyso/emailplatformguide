# Mailchimp vs ConvertKit 2023: Which Email Platform Wins?

Choosing between Mailchimp and ConvertKit can feel overwhelming when you're building your email list. Both are popular email marketing tools, but they cater to slightly different audiences. Mailchimp is a go-to for small businesses and beginners with its user-friendly interface and free plan. ConvertKit, on the other hand, targets creators, bloggers, and course sellers with powerful automation and tagging systems.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, and more based on real user experiences and official specs as of 2023. Whether you're a solopreneur or scaling a team, you'll get practical advice to decide. Let's dive in.

## Mailchimp Overview

Mailchimp launched in 2002 and has grown into a marketing powerhouse with over 12 million users. It's known for its all-in-one platform that includes email campaigns, automations, landing pages, and even ads.

**Key Strengths:**
- Intuitive drag-and-drop editor.
- Free plan for up to 500 subscribers and 1,000 emails/month.
- Built-in audience insights and A/B testing.

Practical tip: Start with Mailchimp's free tier if you're testing email marketing. Use their templates to launch your first campaign in under 30 minutes. Connect it to your website via plugins like WordPress or Shopify for seamless list growth.

However, as your list grows beyond 2,000 subscribers, costs rise quickly, and advanced automations require higher plans.

## ConvertKit Overview

ConvertKit (formerly Landing Page Monkey) rebranded in 2016 and focuses on creators. It emphasizes sequences (automated email series), tagging for segmentation, and commerce tools like digital product sales.

**Key Strengths:**
- Tag-based subscriber management over traditional lists.
- Native support for selling courses, ebooks, and memberships.
- Visual automation builder.

Practical tip: If you run a newsletter or sell online courses, use ConvertKit's forms and landing pages optimized for conversions. Their subscriber score feature helps prioritize engaged readers, improving open rates by focusing on high-value contacts.

No free plan, but a 14-day trial lets you test without commitment. Plans scale based on subscribers, starting affordably.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major decider. Here's a side-by-side as of 2023:

| Subscribers | Mailchimp | ConvertKit |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| Up to 500   | Free      | $9/mo (Creator) |
| 1,000       | $13/mo    | $16/mo     |
| 5,000       | $75/mo    | $50/mo     |
| 10,000      | $110/mo   | $85/mo     |

Mailchimp wins for tiny lists with its free option. ConvertKit becomes cheaper at 5,000+ subscribers and includes unlimited emails. Both offer annual discounts (15-20% off).

**Advice:** Calculate your projected growth. For 1,000 subs sending weekly, Mailchimp's Essentials ($13) limits sends; upgrade to Standard ($75+). ConvertKit's Creator plan handles unlimited sends from day one.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly with 100+ responsive templates. Customize with dynamic content blocks.

ConvertKit offers a simpler builder with 100+ templates focused on clean, mobile-first designs. It's less flashy but faster for creators.

**Tip:** Use Mailchimp for branded, image-heavy emails. Opt for ConvertKit if speed matters—build and send in 10 minutes.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp's automations include customer journeys, abandoned cart, and RSS-to-email. Classic automations are free; advanced ones need paid plans.

ConvertKit shines with sequences (drip campaigns) and a visual automation map using triggers like tag adds or purchases. Unlimited automations on all plans.

**Practical Advice:** Map your buyer journey first. In ConvertKit, tag subscribers as 'lead' → 'trial user' → 'buyer' for personalized nurtures. Mailchimp users: Leverage behavioral triggers to re-engage inactive subs.

### Segmentation and Tagging

Mailchimp uses groups and tags but relies on lists. Merge fields enable personalization.

ConvertKit's tag system is superior—no list limits, one-click broadcasts to tagged segments.

**Tip:** For e-commerce, ConvertKit's purchase-based tags automate upsells. Mailchimp suits simple list splits.

### Integrations and Analytics

Both integrate with 100+ tools (Zapier, Google Analytics, Stripe). Mailchimp has deeper ecommerce ties (Shopify, WooCommerce). ConvertKit excels with creator platforms (Teachable, Gumroad).

Analytics: Mailchimp tracks opens, clicks, revenue. ConvertKit adds subscriber growth reports and revenue per subscriber.

**Advice:** Review reports weekly. Aim for 20-30% open rates; tweak subjects based on data.

### Deliverability and Support

Both maintain high deliverability (Mailchimp ~99%, ConvertKit ~98%). Email authentication (DKIM/SPF) is automatic.

Support: Mailchimp offers 24/7 chat on paid plans; ConvertKit provides priority email support and a knowledge base.

## Pros and Cons

**Mailchimp Pros:** Free start, robust templates, all-in-one marketing.
**Cons:** Expensive scaling, automation limits on basics.

**ConvertKit Pros:** Creator-focused, unlimited sends, powerful tagging.
**Cons:** No free plan, steeper learning for non-creators.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Beginners/small businesses under 2,000 subs.
- Teams needing ads/social alongside email.
- Ecommerce stores (abandoned cart flows).

**Migration Tip:** Export CSV from old tools; import to Mailchimp in minutes.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers, podcasters, course creators.
- Lists over 5,000 needing cost-efficiency.
- Heavy automation users.

**Setup Tip:** Start with a welcome sequence: Email 1 (intro), 2 (value), 3 (offer). Track with tags.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage and style. Beginners love Mailchimp's free entry and ease. Creators thrive on ConvertKit's depth and value.

Test both—Mailchimp free, ConvertKit trial. Track metrics like open rates (target 25%) and growth (10% monthly). Whichever you pick, consistent value-driven emails build loyal audiences.
