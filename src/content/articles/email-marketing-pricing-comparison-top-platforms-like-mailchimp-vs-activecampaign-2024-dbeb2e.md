# Email Marketing Pricing Comparison: Choosing the Right Platform for Your Budget

Selecting the best email marketing platform isn't just about features—pricing plays a pivotal role, especially for businesses scaling their subscriber lists. In this comprehensive email marketing pricing comparison, we'll break down costs for popular tools like Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, Brevo (formerly Sendinblue), GetResponse, Constant Contact, and HubSpot. We'll focus on real-world pricing structures as of 2024, considering subscriber limits, send volumes, and essential features. Whether you're a solopreneur with 1,000 subscribers or a growing team with 50,000+, this guide helps you identify the best value without hidden fees or overpaying for unused features.

Pricing in email marketing typically scales with your contact list size, email volume, and add-ons like automation or A/B testing. Free tiers are great for testing, but paid plans unlock higher limits and advanced tools. Always check official sites for the latest rates, as they can change.

## Key Factors to Consider in Email Marketing Pricing

Before diving into specifics, understand what drives costs. This ensures you're comparing apples to apples.

### Subscriber Count and Billing Increments
Most platforms charge based on the total number of active subscribers, not just sends. For example:
- Mailchimp and ConvertKit bill monthly for contact tiers (e.g., up to 500, 1,000, 5,000).
- Brevo emphasizes email volume (e.g., 20,000 emails/month) alongside contacts.

**Practical Advice:** Audit your list regularly to avoid paying for inactive subscribers. Tools like zero-bounce verification can reduce your billable count by 20-30%.

### Feature Tiers and Add-Ons
Entry-level plans cover basics (templates, segmentation), but automation, CRM integration, or SMS often require upgrades.
- Starter plans: ~$10-30/month for 500-1,000 subs.
- Pro plans: $50+ for advanced analytics and workflows.

**Tip:** Start with a free trial (most offer 14-30 days) to test features before committing. Calculate annual costs—some offer discounts for yearly billing (10-20% savings).

### Deliverability and Hidden Costs
Cheap plans might skimp on dedicated IPs or support, leading to higher bounce rates and lost revenue. Factor in support response times and migration fees.

### Send Volume vs. Contacts
Brevo and MailerLite prioritize sends, ideal for low-engagement lists.

## Detailed Pricing Breakdown of Top Platforms

Here's an honest look at pricing (monthly, billed annually where applicable; excludes taxes/VAT). Data sourced from official sites as of mid-2024.

### Mailchimp
- **Free:** Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month, basic templates.
- **Essentials:** Starts at $13 (500 contacts), $60 (5,000), scales to $350+ (100,000). Includes automation, A/B testing.
- **Standard:** $20 (500), $75 (5k), up to $600+ (100k). Adds behavioral targeting.
- **Premium:** $350+ (10k+). Enterprise features.

Best for: Beginners with growing lists. Note: Recent pricing hikes make it pricier for mid-tier.

### ActiveCampaign
- **Starter:** $29 (500 contacts), $149 (5,000), $259 (25k). Email automation, site tracking.
- **Plus:** $49 (500), $229 (5k). Adds team access, custom reporting.
- **Pro:** $149 (500), $499 (5k). Deep CRM, machine learning.
- **Enterprise:** Custom.

Best for: E-commerce with automation needs. Excellent ROI for sales funnels.

### ConvertKit (now Kit)
- **Free:** Up to 300 subscribers? Wait, up to 1,000 sends/month for ~300 subs.
- **Creator:** $15 (300 subs), $29 (1,000), $79 (5k). Visual automations, landing pages.
- **Creator Pro:** $25 (300), $49 (1k), $129 (5k). Advanced broadcasts, subscriber scoring.

Best for: Creators/bloggers. Transparent, creator-focused pricing.

### Brevo
- **Free:** 300 emails/day (~9,000/month), unlimited contacts.
- **Starter:** $25 (20k emails/month, 5k contacts), $65 (50k emails).
- **Business:** $65 (20k emails), scales up. Sales CRM, ads.
- **Enterprise:** Custom.

Best for: High-volume senders on a budget. Volume-based shines for sporadic campaigns.

### GetResponse
- **Email Marketing:** $19 (1k contacts), $59 (5k), $159 (25k). Autoresponders, webinars.
- **Marketing Automation:** $59 (1k), $119 (5k).
- **Ecommerce Marketing:** $119 (1k).

Best for: All-in-one with webinars. Aggressive discounts for long-term.

### Constant Contact
- **Lite:** $12 (500 contacts), $45 (5k). Basic emails, list growth tools.
- **Standard:** $35 (500), $80 (5k). Automation, surveys.
- **Premium:** $80 (500), $195 (5k). Advanced retargeting.

Best for: Non-profits/small biz. Strong support, easy setup.

### HubSpot
- **Free:** 2,000 emails/month, basic CRM.
- **Starter:** $20 (1k contacts/marketing contacts), $45 (2k? Scales).
- **Professional:** $890 (2k marketing contacts). Full inbound suite.

Best for: Teams needing CRM integration. Free tier is robust.

## Best Plans by Budget and Use Case

- **Under $20/month (Starters, <1k subs):** Brevo Free/Starter, HubSpot Free, ConvertKit Free.
- **$20-100 (5k subs, growing):** Mailchimp Essentials, GetResponse, Constant Contact.
- **$100+ (Advanced, 10k+):** ActiveCampaign Plus/Pro for ROI-focused teams.

**Practical Advice:** Use pricing calculators on sites (e.g., ActiveCampaign's). For 10k subs, ActiveCampaign might cost $259 vs. Mailchimp's $200+, but superior automation justifies it for e-com.

## Practical Tips for Choosing and Optimizing Costs

1. **Trial Stack:** Sign up for 14-day trials across 3 platforms. Import a test list, run campaigns, measure deliverability (aim >95%).
2. **Total Cost Calculation:** (Monthly fee x 12) + add-ons - discounts. Factor churn: If list grows 20%/year, project 2-year costs.
3. **Negotiate Enterprise:** For 50k+ subs, contact sales for custom deals (10-30% off).
4. **Migration Strategy:** Export CSV from old tool; most import free. Budget $100-500 for pro help if complex.
5. **Compliance Savings:** GDPR/CCPA tools prevent fines; built-in for all listed.
6. **Benchmark ROI:** Track open rates (20-30% avg), clicks (2-5%). Platforms with analytics pay for themselves via better conversions.

Avoid: Overbuying features (e.g., SMS if not needed). Re-engage lists quarterly to trim costs.

## Conclusion

This email marketing pricing comparison reveals no one-size-fits-all—Mailchimp suits simple needs, ActiveCampaign excels in automation, Brevo wins on volume. For most choosing email marketing platforms, start with your subscriber count and must-have features, then trial top picks. Prioritize value over cheapest: A $50 plan boosting revenue 10% trumps a $20 dud. Visit official pricing pages, run the numbers, and scale smartly. Your inbox empire awaits—pick wisely!
