# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. For solopreneurs, bloggers, and small businesses, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both tools offer powerful features for building email lists, sending newsletters, and automating workflows without the complexity of enterprise solutions like Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, core features, user experience, support, and real-world use cases. Our analysis draws from official documentation, user reviews on sites like G2 and Capterra, and hands-on testing insights. By the end, you'll know which platform suits your needs—whether you're a beginner on a budget or a creator needing advanced automations.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both MailerLite and ConvertKit use subscriber-based tiers, scaling with your list size.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite stands out for affordability:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automations, landing pages, and popups.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $9/month (500 subs), up to $310/month (150k subs). All features unlocked.
- **Advanced**: $20/month base, adds A/B testing, web push notifications, and priority support.

No contracts, monthly billing, and a 30-day money-back guarantee. Annual plans save 30%. It's ideal for bootstrapped users—many report staying under $20/month even with thousands of subscribers.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit targets creators with these tiers:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers (recently expanded), unlimited emails, but limited to basic broadcasts and forms.
- **Creator**: $15/month (300 subs) to $29/month (1k subs), scaling to $779/month (50k subs). Includes unlimited emails, automations, landing pages, and subscriber segments.
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month base, adds advanced reporting, one-click upsells, and visual automations.

Annual discounts apply (20% off). ConvertKit charges extra for add-ons like paid newsletters via Commerce (2.9% + $0.30/transaction). Users praise the unlimited sends, but costs rise quickly past 5k subscribers.

**Winner**: MailerLite for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit for unlimited emails on paid plans.

Practical tip: Calculate your TCO using their pricing calculators. Factor in list growth—MailerLite's flat fees keep costs predictable.

## Ease of Use: Interfaces and Onboarding

Both platforms prioritize simplicity, but differences emerge in setup and daily workflows.

### MailerLite User Experience
Drag-and-drop editor is intuitive for non-designers. Onboarding includes video tutorials and templates for newsletters, popups, and sites. Navigation is clean: dashboard shows key metrics like open rates (industry avg. 20-30%) at a glance.

**Pros**:
- Mobile-responsive editor.
- One-click imports from CSV/Google Sheets.
- Built-in photo editor.

New users onboard in under 10 minutes. Review scores: 4.7/5 on G2 for ease of use.

### ConvertKit User Experience
Creator-focused with a tagging system over traditional segments. Visual automation builder uses flowcharts—great for sequences like welcome series (boost engagement by 50% per studies).

**Pros**:
- Clean subscriber dashboard with tags/behaviors.
- Native commerce for paid subs.
- Integrates seamlessly with WordPress/ghost.

Slight learning curve for tags, but tutorials help. G2 score: 4.5/5.

**Winner**: Tie—MailerLite for beginners, ConvertKit for automation pros.

Tip: Test free plans. MailerLite suits quick campaigns; ConvertKit excels in subscriber journeys.

## Core Features: Head-to-Head

### Email Editor and Templates
Both offer responsive HTML editors with 100+ templates.
- MailerLite: Advanced blocks (RSS feeds, countdowns), plain-text option.
- ConvertKit: Simple, focused on content blocks; strong for newsletters.

### Automations
- **MailerLite**: Trigger-based (e.g., birthday emails), workflows with if/else logic. Unlimited on paid plans.
- **ConvertKit**: Visual automations shine—tag triggers, broadcasts to segments. Pro: subscriber scoring.

ConvertKit edges for complex funnels (e.g., course launches).

### Landing Pages and Forms
- MailerLite: Unlimited pages/forms/popups, A/B testing, custom domains.
- ConvertKit: Forms, pages, ephemerals; Pro upsells on pages.

### Analytics and Segmentation
Both track opens/clicks. MailerLite adds heatmaps; ConvertKit uses tags for hyper-targeted sends (e.g., "purchased ebook" tag → upsell sequence).

**Integrations**: 100+ each (Zapier, Google Analytics). ConvertKit native with Gumroad, Teachable.

**Winner**: ConvertKit for creators; MailerLite for all-in-one marketing.

## Customer Support and Community

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 email/chat, extensive help center, Facebook group. Response <2 hours.
- **ConvertKit**: Email support (business hours), podcast/webinars, creator network. Highly rated for helpfulness.

Both score 4.6+ on support.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Budget < $20/mo, need popups/websites, simple automations. Perfect for ecom stores, agencies.
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Blogger/creator, complex sequences, monetize newsletters. Scales with audience growth.

Real-user data: MailerLite powers 1M+ sites; ConvertKit 200k+ creators.

## Conclusion

**MailerLite vs ConvertKit** boils down to priorities: affordability and simplicity (MailerLite) vs creator tools and unlimited scale (ConvertKit). Start with free trials—import a test list, build a workflow, send a campaign. Track ROI via open rates (>25% goal) and conversions.

For most small teams, MailerLite offers better value today. But if automations drive your business, ConvertKit's visual builder pays off. Whichever you choose, consistent sending (1-2x/week) yields 4x revenue per EmailOctane stats.
