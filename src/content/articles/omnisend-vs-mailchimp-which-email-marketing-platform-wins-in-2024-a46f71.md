# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's customer engagement and revenue. Omnisend and Mailchimp are two leading platforms, but they cater to slightly different needs. Omnisend shines for ecommerce stores with its omnichannel features, while Mailchimp offers broad appeal for beginners and general marketers.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and support. Whether you're scaling an online store or nurturing leads, get practical advice to decide. Both offer free trials, so test them yourself.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is a top concern when selecting an email platform. Both have free plans, but costs scale with subscribers and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends, with basic features like templates and automation. Paid plans start at:
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts (unlimited sends, but no advanced segmentation).
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts (behavioral targeting, automation).
- **Premium**: $350+/month for 500 contacts (A/B testing, multivariate, priority support).

Prices rise quickly with list size—e.g., 10,000 contacts on Standard is $100+/month. No SMS included; add-ons extra.

### Omnisend Pricing
Omnisend's free plan covers 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, including popups and basic automation. Paid tiers:
- **Standard**: $16/month for 500 contacts (unlimited emails, SMS at extra cost, segments).
- **Pro**: $59/month for 500 contacts (advanced reporting, A/B testing, high-volume SMS).
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing.

Omnisend is often cheaper for ecommerce users due to bundled features like SMS credits. Practical tip: Calculate based on your list size using their pricing calculators. For 5,000 contacts, Mailchimp Standard ~$75/month vs. Omnisend Pro ~$138/month—but Omnisend includes more channels.

**Winner?** Omnisend for growing ecommerce lists; Mailchimp for small free-tier users.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities

Both platforms deliver email essentials, but differences emerge in automation and multichannel support.

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp boasts 100+ drag-and-drop templates, customizable with its editor. Great for non-designers; includes stock images and content blocks.

Omnisend offers 250+ ecommerce-optimized templates, dynamic product blocks that pull from your store (e.g., Shopify). Its editor supports conditional content.

**Advice**: If visual appeal matters, preview templates on both. Mailchimp for quick newsletters; Omnisend for personalized product recommendations.

### Automation Workflows
Automation is where Omnisend pulls ahead for ecommerce:
- **Omnisend**: Pre-built workflows for cart abandonment, welcome series, post-purchase upsells. Omnichannel triggers (email + SMS + push). High recovery rates reported (up to 30% for carts).
- **Mailchimp**: Single, customer, or classic automations. Triggers like tag added or purchase, but less ecommerce-specific.

Practical tip: Map your customer journey first. Use Omnisend for win-back flows; Mailchimp for simple drip campaigns.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp: Tags, groups, behavioral data for segments.
Omnisend: Advanced segments by purchase history, browsing behavior. Dynamic segments update in real-time.

**Pro Tip**: Segment by RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) value on both—Omnisend automates this better.

### Additional Channels
Omnisend: SMS (pay-per-use, ~$0.015/message), web push, popups/forms. True omnichannel.
Mailchimp: Primarily email; SMS via Intuit (extra cost), no native push.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is intuitive for beginners—familiar dashboard, guided setup. Onboarding includes tutorials and templates.

Omnisend targets ecommerce users: Quick Shopify/WooCommerce integration, visual workflow builder. Slightly steeper for non-ecom, but clean UI.

**Advice for New Users**: Start with Mailchimp if email-only; Omnisend if integrating with your store. Both have 14-day trials—import a test list to compare.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp: 300+ integrations (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier, CRMs like Salesforce). Excellent for agencies.

Omnisend: 160+ apps, strong in ecommerce (Shopify, BigCommerce, Klaviyo alternatives). Native with Google Tag Manager.

**Scalability Tip**: For solopreneurs, both scale to 100k+ lists. Enterprises prefer Mailchimp's API robustness; Omnisend for high-volume transactional emails.

## Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp: Revenue tracking, click maps, comparative reports.
Omnisend: Ecommerce-focused ROI calculator, cohort analysis, cross-channel attribution.

**Practical Use**: Track open rates (>20% good), clicks (>2%), revenue per recipient. Omnisend's dashboard highlights upsell opportunities.

## Customer Support Options

Mailchimp: 24/7 email/chat for paid; extensive knowledge base.
Omnisend: Live chat (business hours), email, dedicated manager for Pro+. Responsive for ecommerce queries.

**Tip**: Paid plans unlock better support—don't rely on free tier alone.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros
- Generous free plan
- Beginner-friendly
- Vast template library

Cons
- Expensive scaling
- Limited multichannel

### Omnisend Pros
- Ecommerce automations
- Omnichannel (SMS/push)
- Better ROI tracking

Cons
- Smaller free plan
- Less general marketing focus

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick Mailchimp** if: Small business, non-ecommerce, budget-conscious starter, need broad integrations.
- **Pick Omnisend** if: Online store, want SMS/push, advanced automations, focus on revenue recovery.

**Practical Advice**: Audit your needs—list size, channels, integrations. Run A/B tests on trials: Send a campaign to 100 contacts, track engagement. Consider churn: Mailchimp users often migrate for costs; Omnisend retains ecommerce loyalists.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce-driven businesses with superior automation and multichannel, while Mailchimp remains the go-to for versatile, easy email marketing. Neither is 'best' universally—align with your goals. Start trials today, measure results, and scale confidently. For most growing stores, Omnisend's features justify the switch from Mailchimp.
