AWeber has been in the email marketing business since 1998 — making it one of the oldest platforms in the space. But does longevity translate to quality in 2026? We tested it to find out.

## What Is AWeber?

AWeber is a list-based email marketing platform that emphasizes reliability, templates, and customer support. It's popular among small businesses, solopreneurs, and affiliate marketers.

Unlike newer tag-based platforms like [Kit](/convertkit-review), AWeber uses a traditional list model where subscribers can belong to one or more mailing lists. For a head-to-head breakdown, see our [ConvertKit vs AWeber](/convertkit-vs-aweber) comparison.

## Key Features

### Email Templates
AWeber's biggest strength is its template library — over 700 designs covering every industry and use case. The Smart Designer feature analyzes your website and auto-generates branded email templates, which is a nice time-saver.

### Deliverability
With 25+ years of experience, AWeber has established strong relationships with ISPs and email providers. Their deliverability rates are consistently among the best in the industry.

### Automation
AWeber offers tag-based automation with visual workflows. It's functional and handles common scenarios well (welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement). However, it lacks the sophistication of [ActiveCampaign](/activecampaign-vs-mailchimp) or Kit's automation builders.

### Landing Pages
AWeber includes a landing page builder, though it's more basic than dedicated tools. It works for simple opt-in pages but won't replace a tool like Leadpages for complex marketing pages.

### Affiliate Marketing Support
Unlike many competitors, AWeber explicitly allows affiliate marketing content in emails (with consent and guidelines adherence). This makes it a top choice for affiliate marketers who've been burned by stricter platforms.

## Who Should Use AWeber?

**Great for:**
- Small businesses wanting proven reliability
- Affiliate marketers (explicitly allowed)
- Users who want extensive template options
- Anyone who values phone support

**Not ideal for:**
- Users who want cutting-edge automation
- Budget-conscious users (MailerLite is cheaper)
- Creators focused on digital products (Kit is better)
- Those who prefer a modern, minimal interface

## The Verdict

AWeber is a solid, reliable email marketing platform that does the fundamentals well. Its template library is unmatched, deliverability is excellent, and customer support is responsive.

However, it's starting to show its age. The interface feels dated, automation lags behind newer competitors, and pricing isn't the most competitive. If reliability and templates are your priorities, AWeber delivers. If you want cutting-edge features and a modern experience, look at Kit or [MailerLite](/mailerlite-review) instead.
