# MailerLite vs ConvertKit 2024: In-Depth Comparison

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. For bloggers, creators, small businesses, and solopreneurs, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both tools excel in user-friendly interfaces and powerful automations, but they cater to slightly different needs.

MailerLite shines with its affordability and simplicity, making it ideal for beginners and budget-conscious users. ConvertKit, on the other hand, targets content creators with advanced tagging and monetization features.

In this guide, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more to help you decide. Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling to thousands of subscribers, read on for practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities.

## Quick Overview

| Feature | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Best For** | Beginners, small businesses, e-commerce | Bloggers, creators, course sellers |
| **Free Plan** | Up to 1,000 subs, 12,000 emails/mo | Up to 10,000 subs (limited features) |
| **Starting Paid** | $10/mo (500 subs) | $15/mo (300 subs, Creator plan) |
| **Key Strength** | Drag-and-drop editor, popups | Sequences, tagging system |

This table gives a snapshot—let's dive deeper.

## Pricing Breakdown: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

Pricing is often the first deciding factor. Both offer transparent, subscriber-based plans without hidden fees.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite's free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—perfect for testing. Paid plans start at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20 for 1,000 subs, $50 for 2,500, and so on. Advanced features like A/B testing and web push notifications are included in all paid tiers.

**Practical Tip**: If you're under 1,000 subs, stick to free. Upgrade only when you hit limits to avoid early costs. Annual billing saves 30%.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit recently expanded its free plan to support up to 10,000 subscribers with core features like broadcasts and forms. Paid Creator plans start at $15/month for 300 subs, $29 for 1,000, $79 for 3,000, up to enterprise options. The Creator Pro plan ($25+/mo) unlocks advanced automations, one-click upsells, and subscriber scoring.

**Practical Tip**: Creators with growing lists benefit from the free tier's generosity. Compare total costs: For 5,000 subs, ConvertKit is ~$79/mo vs MailerLite's ~$75/mo.

**Winner**: MailerLite for tiny budgets; ConvertKit for high-volume free use.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Editor and Templates
MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 80+ responsive templates. You can add custom HTML blocks easily. Previews across devices are seamless.

ConvertKit uses a block-based visual editor focused on clean, text-heavy designs—great for newsletters. Fewer templates (around 50), but they emphasize personalization.

**Advice**: Test both editors with your content. MailerLite suits image-heavy campaigns; ConvertKit for storytelling emails.

### Automation and Sequences
ConvertKit's strength is its "Sequences"—drip campaigns triggered by tags or actions. Tagging is powerful: segment users by behavior (e.g., "opened email X").

MailerLite offers workflow automations with if/else branches, RSS-to-email, and abandoned cart triggers. It's visual and beginner-friendly.

**Practical Example**: For a course launch, ConvertKit's tagging auto-enrolls buyers into upsell sequences. In MailerLite, build a similar flow with tags and triggers in 10 minutes.

### Segmentation and Forms
Both segment by custom fields, tags, and activity. MailerLite adds popups, embedded forms, and surveys. ConvertKit excels in subscriber commerce: sell digital products directly.

**Tip**: Use MailerLite popups for list growth (e.g., exit-intent). ConvertKit forms integrate with landing pages for seamless funnels.

### Analytics and Reporting
MailerLite provides open/click rates, bounce handling, and revenue tracking per campaign. ConvertKit adds subscriber value reports and revenue per subscriber.

**Advice**: Review analytics weekly. Track unsubscribe rates—aim under 0.5%.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

MailerLite's dashboard is clean, with quick-start wizards. No steep learning curve; import lists in minutes.

ConvertKit feels creator-native, with tutorials for bloggers. Onboarding emails guide you through setup.

**User Feedback**: MailerLite scores 4.7/5 on ease (Capterra); ConvertKit 4.6/5. Beginners pick MailerLite; experienced users love ConvertKit's depth.

**Tip**: Spend 30 mins migrating a test list. Both support CSV imports and Zapier for seamless switches.

## Integrations and Customer Support

### Integrations
MailerLite: 1,000+ via native/Zapier, including WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics.
ConvertKit: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable, WordPress plugins. Native commerce integrations shine.

**Advice**: Check Zapier compatibility. For WooCommerce, MailerLite edges out.

### Support
MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Fast responses (under 1 hour).
ConvertKit: Email support (paid plans), chat for Pro, extensive help docs/videos.

**Tip**: Use free trials to test support. Ask about specific integrations.

## Migration Tips

Switching? Export CSV from old tool, import to new. Rebuild automations first. Test sends to 10% of list.

**Pro Advice**: Run parallel campaigns for 2 weeks to compare deliverability (both ~99%).

## Conclusion

**MailerLite vs ConvertKit** boils down to your goals. Choose **MailerLite** if you're budget-focused, need quick wins with forms/popups, or run a small business—its free plan and ease make it unbeatable for starters.

Opt for **ConvertKit** if you're a creator monetizing content; its tagging, sequences, and commerce tools drive higher lifetime value.

Both deliver high deliverability (above 95%) and comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM. Start with free trials: MailerLite for broad appeal, ConvertKit for depth.

Ultimately, test both with your audience. Track metrics like open rates (aim 30%+) and conversions. Whichever you pick, consistent value-driven emails will grow your list.
