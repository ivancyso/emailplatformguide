# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Tool Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business. With so many options, pitting **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** head-to-head helps creators, bloggers, and small business owners decide based on real needs. Both are popular for their simplicity and power, but they cater to slightly different audiences. MailerLite shines with affordability and ease, while ConvertKit targets creators with advanced automation.

In this comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, usability, and more with practical advice. No hype—just facts to guide your choice. Let's dive in.

## Overview of MailerLite

MailerLite is a Lithuania-based platform launched in 2010, known for its budget-friendly plans and intuitive interface. It's ideal for beginners and small teams handling up to thousands of subscribers.

**Key Strengths:**
- Free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month.
- Drag-and-drop editor for emails, popups, and landing pages.
- Basic automation workflows.

MailerLite powers over 2 million users worldwide. Practical tip: Start with the free plan to test list-building popups on your site—they convert well without coding.

## Overview of ConvertKit

ConvertKit, founded in 2013 by Nathan Barry, is creator-focused (think bloggers, podcasters, YouTubers). It emphasizes sequences and tagging over traditional lists.

**Key Strengths:**
- Visual automation builder for complex sequences.
- Native commerce tools like paid newsletters.
- Strong tagging for segmentation.

It serves over 25,000 creators. Advice: If you're selling digital products, ConvertKit's one-click upsells streamline revenue without extra plugins.

## Pricing Comparison: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

Pricing is a major decider. Here's a side-by-side (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates):

| Feature | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Free Plan | Yes (1,000 subs, 12k emails/mo) | No (14-day trial) |
| Entry Paid | Growing Business: $10/mo (500 subs) | Creator: $15/mo (300 subs, annual) |
| Mid-Tier | Advanced: $20/mo (2,500 subs) | Creator: $29/mo (500 subs) |
| High Volume | Custom | Enterprise: Custom |

**MailerLite** wins on cost—scales affordably up to 10,000 subs under $50/mo. **ConvertKit** starts higher but includes unlimited emails and advanced features.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your subscriber growth. For <1,000 subs, MailerLite saves money. Over 5,000? Weigh ConvertKit's automation value. Both offer annual discounts (20% off).

## Ease of Use: User Interface Breakdown

**MailerLite** feels like a modern dashboard—clean, fast, no clutter. The editor is block-based, perfect for non-designers. Onboarding takes minutes.

**ConvertKit** has a steeper curve due to its tagging system (subscribers as "people" with tags). But once learned, it's efficient for personalization.

**Winner:** MailerLite for beginners. Test both trials: Import a CSV list and send a test campaign to feel the flow.

### Drag-and-Drop Editors

MailerLite's editor offers 80+ responsive templates. ConvertKit's is simpler, focused on text-heavy newsletters. Tip: MailerLite for visually rich emails; ConvertKit for storytelling.

## Features Face-Off

### Automation and Workflows

ConvertKit excels with a visual canvas for sequences (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart). MailerLite has triggers but fewer branches—solid for basics.

**Advice:** Map your customer journey first. Need if/then logic? ConvertKit. Simple drip campaigns? MailerLite suffices.

### Segmentation and Tagging

ConvertKit's tags beat MailerLite's segments for granularity (e.g., tag "podcast listener"). MailerLite uses groups and custom fields effectively.

### Forms, Popups, and Landing Pages

Both offer embeddable forms. MailerLite includes popups/websites builder free. ConvertKit's forms are tag-based, with better mobile optimization.

**Pro Tip:** A/B test popups. MailerLite's exit-intent works great for ecom sites.

### Templates and Design

MailerLite: 100+ templates, RSS-to-email. ConvertKit: Minimalist, creator-optimized.

### Integrations

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress/Google Analytics. ConvertKit: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable; 50+ natives.

**Advice:** List your tools (e.g., Shopify?). ConvertKit edges for creator stacks.

### Analytics and Reporting

Both track opens/clicks. ConvertKit adds revenue tracking; MailerLite has heatmaps.

## Customer Support and Resources

MailerLite: 24/7 chat, email, knowledge base. ConvertKit: Email/ticket (priority for paid), Creator Network community.

**Practical:** MailerLite for instant help; ConvertKit for peer learning.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite Pros:
- Cheaper, free tier.
- All-in-one (sites/popups).
- Fast support.

**Cons:**
- Limited advanced automation.
- Fewer creator-specific tools.

### ConvertKit Pros:
- Powerful sequences/tagging.
- Monetization features.
- Unlimited sends.

**Cons:**
- Pricier.
- No free plan.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Budget-conscious solopreneurs.
- Beginners building lists.
- Businesses needing websites/popups.

**Migration Tip:** Export from other tools easily; their importer handles Mailchimp seamlessly.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators with audiences.
- Those selling courses/products.
- Advanced automation users.

**Advice:** Use tags religiously—segment by behavior for 20-30% higher opens.

## Final Verdict: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

**MailerLite** for affordability and simplicity—perfect if you're starting or scaling economically. **ConvertKit** for creators needing depth in automation and sales.

Test both: MailerLite's free plan + ConvertKit's trial. Track metrics like deliverability (both >95%) and growth.

Ultimately, align with your goals. For most small teams, MailerLite's value shines; power users love ConvertKit.
