[Kit (ConvertKit)](/convertkit-review) and [MailerLite](/mailerlite-review) are two of the most recommended email marketing platforms for creators, bloggers, and small businesses. Both offer free plans, clean interfaces, and solid automation — but they differ significantly on pricing, design philosophy, and feature focus.

Here is how they compare.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Kit (ConvertKit) | MailerLite |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Best for** | Creators, course sellers | Budget-conscious businesses |
| **Free plan** | 1,000 subscribers | 1,000 subscribers, 12K emails/mo |
| **Subscriber model** | Tag-based (no duplicates) | List + tag hybrid |
| **Email templates** | 9 (plain-text focus) | Many (modern, visual) |
| **Automation** | Excellent | Good |
| **Website builder** | No | Yes |
| **Digital product sales** | Yes (built-in commerce) | Basic (via Stripe) |
| **Starting price** | $29/mo | $9/mo |
| **Creator Network** | Yes | No |

## Pricing: MailerLite Wins Decisively

This is the biggest differentiator. MailerLite costs a fraction of Kit at every subscriber tier.

| Subscribers | Kit (Creator) | MailerLite (Growing Business) |
|-------------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| 1,000 | $29/mo | $9/mo |
| 2,500 | $49/mo | $15/mo |
| 5,000 | $79/mo | $29/mo |
| 10,000 | $119/mo | $47/mo |
| 25,000 | $199/mo | $139/mo |
| 50,000 | $379/mo | $239/mo |

At 10,000 subscribers, MailerLite saves $864/year. At 50,000, the difference grows to $1,680/year. That money could fund paid ads, freelance designers, or other marketing tools.

Both offer free plans at 1,000 subscribers, but MailerLite's free plan includes automation and landing pages, while Kit's free plan excludes automation.

**Winner:** MailerLite.

## Email Design: MailerLite Wins

Kit intentionally limits templates — just 9 options, all leaning toward plain-text styled emails. The philosophy is that simpler emails feel more personal and get higher open rates. This is true for some creator audiences, but limiting for businesses that need branded, visual emails.

MailerLite offers a large template library with modern, professionally designed options. The drag-and-drop editor includes content blocks for countdown timers, product listings, surveys, and RSS feeds. It loads fast and is easy to use.

If you prefer minimal, text-first emails, Kit works well. If you want design flexibility, MailerLite is clearly better.

**Winner:** MailerLite.

## Automation: Kit Wins

Kit's visual automation builder is one of the best in the industry. It handles complex, multi-path workflows with ease:
- Event-based triggers (tag added, product purchased, link clicked)
- Conditional logic with if/else branching
- Integration triggers (Teachable, Gumroad, Zapier)
- Visual sequences within automations
- Tag-based routing for personalized content paths

MailerLite's automation covers the same basics — welcome sequences, triggers, conditions — but the builder is less flexible for complex, multi-step workflows. For standard automations (welcome series, date-based sends), MailerLite is sufficient. For sophisticated creator funnels (course launches, segmented content delivery), Kit is stronger.

**Winner:** Kit.

## Subscriber Management: Kit Wins

Kit uses a subscriber-centric model where each person exists once, organized by tags and custom fields. You never pay for duplicates, and segmentation is clean.

MailerLite uses a hybrid model with groups (similar to lists) and tags. Subscribers can exist in multiple groups, which can lead to overlap — though MailerLite handles deduplication better than traditional list-based platforms.

**Winner:** Kit.

## Commerce and Monetization: Kit Wins

Kit includes built-in commerce for creators:
- Sell digital products (ebooks, templates, presets)
- Paid newsletter subscriptions
- Tip jars
- No transaction fees on the Creator Pro plan
- Creator Network for cross-promotion and growth

MailerLite supports selling digital products via Stripe integration and its website builder, but the commerce features are more basic. There is no creator network or paid newsletter feature.

**Winner:** Kit.

## Website and Landing Pages: MailerLite Wins

MailerLite includes a full website builder with blog, landing pages, and custom domains — all on the free plan. You can build a functional small business website without a separate platform.

Kit offers landing pages but no website builder. If you need a website, you will need WordPress, Squarespace, or another tool alongside Kit.

**Winner:** MailerLite.

## The Verdict

**Kit (ConvertKit)** is the better choice for serious creators who need powerful automation, subscriber-centric management, and built-in commerce. The premium pricing is justified if you monetize your audience through digital products, courses, or paid newsletters.

**MailerLite** is the better choice for budget-conscious businesses and creators who want solid email marketing features at a fraction of the cost. If you do not need Kit's creator-specific tools, MailerLite delivers 80% of the functionality at 30-40% of the price.
