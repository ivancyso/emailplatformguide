# Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Top 10 Email Marketing Platforms Compared

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but as businesses grow, many find its pricing structure, feature limitations, or customer support lacking. If you're searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**, you're not alone. In 2024, the market offers robust options with better automation, segmentation, and affordability. This guide compares the top 10 alternatives, focusing on real features, pricing, and use cases to help you choose wisely.

Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or agency, we'll break down pros, cons, and practical tips. All data is based on official websites and user reviews from sites like G2 and Capterra as of late 2023.

## Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but escalate quickly—up to $350+/month for 50,000 contacts. Recent pricing hikes and removed legacy plans have pushed users away. Common pain points include:

- **Limited automation**: Basic workflows without advanced branching.
- **Reporting gaps**: Surface-level analytics.
- **Deliverability issues**: Average inbox rates around 80-85%.
- **Steep costs for scale**: Essentials plan lacks key features like automation.

Switching can save 20-50% on costs while unlocking better tools. According to EmailOctopus data, alternatives often deliver 10-20% higher open rates due to superior segmentation.

## Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives Compared

Here's a head-to-head comparison of key features, starting with pricing for 1,000-10,000 contacts (monthly, billed annually where applicable).

### 1. ActiveCampaign

**Best for**: Marketing automation and CRM integration.

ActiveCampaign shines with powerful automations, site tracking, and machine learning for predictions. Pricing: Lite ($29/mo for 1,000 contacts), Plus ($49/mo), Professional ($149/mo). All plans include unlimited emails.

**Pros**:
- Advanced if/then automations with splits and goals.
- Built-in CRM and 900+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress).
- Excellent split testing and personalization.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve.
- No free plan beyond 14-day trial.

**Practical tip**: Use for e-commerce with abandoned cart sequences—recover 15-30% more sales.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for**: SMBs needing SMS and transactional emails.

Brevo offers unlimited contacts on all plans. Pricing: Starter ($25/mo for 20k emails), Business ($65/mo). Free plan: 300 emails/day.

**Pros**:
- Multichannel (email, SMS, WhatsApp).
- High deliverability (99% claimed).
- Drag-and-drop editor like Mailchimp.

**Cons**:
- Automation limits on lower plans.
- Interface feels cluttered.

**Practical tip**: Ideal for transactional emails; integrate with Zapier for custom workflows.

### 3. ConvertKit

**Best for**: Creators and bloggers.

Tailored for newsletters with tagging and sequences. Pricing: Free (up to 1,000 subs), Creator ($29/mo), Creator Pro ($49/mo).

**Pros**:
- Simple subscriber tagging over lists.
- Native landing pages and forms.
- One-click unsubscribes boost sender reputation.

**Cons**:
- Lacks deep e-commerce features.
- No phone support.

**Practical tip**: Segment by tag behavior (e.g., 'read newsletter') for 25% higher engagement.

### 4. Klaviyo

**Best for**: E-commerce (Shopify users).

Klaviyo excels in data-driven flows. Pricing: Free (up to 250 contacts), up to $100/mo+ based on contacts.

**Pros**n- Predictive analytics for revenue.
- 300+ pre-built flows.
- Shopify/WooCommerce sync.

**Cons**:
- E-com focused; weak for B2B.
- Costs rise with revenue tracked.

**Practical tip**: Set up welcome series—Klaviyo users see 332% more revenue from flows vs. one-offs.

### 5. MailerLite

**Best for**: Budget-conscious beginners.

Ultra-affordable with strong features. Pricing: Free (1,000 subs), Growing ($10/mo), Advanced ($20/mo).

**Pros**:
- Popups, surveys, and automation.
- Fast website builder.
- High deliverability.

**Cons**:
- Limited integrations (300+ but no native CRM).
- Basic reporting.

**Practical tip**: Use A/B testing on subject lines to boost opens by 20-30%.

### 6. GetResponse

**Best for**: All-in-one marketing.

Includes webinars and funnels. Pricing: Email Marketing ($19/mo), Marketing Automation ($59/mo).

**Pros**:
- Conversion funnels and landing pages.
- Autoresponders with scoring.
- Free migration from Mailchimp.

**Cons**:
- Overwhelming for newbies.
- Pricing jumps at 10k contacts.

**Practical tip**: Host webinars to nurture leads—integrates seamlessly.

### 7. AWeber

**Best for**: Long-term reliability.

Veteran platform with strong support. Pricing: Lite ($12.50/mo), Plus ($26.50/mo), Unlimited ($38.50/mo).

**Pros**:
- 24/7 support and templates.
- Loyal user base (150k+ businesses).
- Easy list imports.

**Cons**:
- Dated interface.
- No advanced personalization.

**Practical tip**: Leverage RSS-to-email for consistent newsletters.

### 8. Constant Contact

**Best for**: Non-profits and events.

User-friendly with event tools. Pricing: Lite ($12/mo), Standard ($35/mo).

**Pros**:
- AI subject lines and image generator.
- Social media ads integration.
- Free photo storage.

**Cons**:
- Contacts-based pricing.
- Limited automation.

**Practical tip**: Use for event RSVPs—tracks attendance automatically.

### 9. EmailOctopus

**Best for**: Cost savings with Amazon SES.

Leverages AWS for cheap sending. Pricing: $8/mo for 2,500 subs (pay-per-email model).

**Pros**:
- 1¢ per 1,000 emails beyond included.
- Simple lists and automation.

**Cons**:
- Basic features only.
- Setup requires SES approval.

**Practical tip**: Perfect for high-volume, low-budget sends.

### 10. Omnisend

**Best for**: Omnichannel retail.

Pricing: Free (500 emails/mo), Standard ($16/mo).

**Pros**:
- SMS, push notifications.
- Product pickups in emails.

**Cons**:
- E-com centric.

**Practical tip**: Automate post-purchase upsells.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess your needs**: Solopreneur? Go MailerLite/ConvertKit. E-com? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Automation-heavy? ActiveCampaign.

2. **Budget check**: Calculate TCO. EmailOctopus wins for volume; Brevo for unlimited contacts.

3. **Migration ease**: Most offer free imports. Test deliverability with a small list first.

4. **Trial and test**: Use free plans/trials. Send to 100 subs, track opens/CTRs.

5. **Integrations**: Ensure compatibility with your stack (e.g., Shopify, Zapier).

6. **Support and scalability**: Read reviews for growth pains.

**Pro tip**: Start with segmentation—group by engagement to lift ROI by 50%.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like ActiveCampaign, Brevo, and Klaviyo offer superior value, features, and deliverability for 2024. Pick based on your audience size and goals: budget users love MailerLite, creators swear by ConvertKit, and shops thrive on Klaviyo.
