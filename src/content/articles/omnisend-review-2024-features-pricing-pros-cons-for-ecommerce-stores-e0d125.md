# Omnisend Review 2024: The Ultimate Guide for Ecommerce Email Marketing

If you're an ecommerce store owner scouting for an email marketing platform, Omnisend often pops up in recommendations. Designed specifically for online retailers, it combines email, SMS, and push notifications into one dashboard. In this detailed Omnisend review, we'll break down its features, pricing, ease of use, and whether it lives up to the hype for businesses choosing between email marketing tools.

We'll cover real-world practical advice on setup, automation workflows, and integration tips based on standard platform capabilities. No fluff—just actionable insights to help you decide if Omnisend fits your needs.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform tailored for ecommerce. Launched in 2014, it powers over 70,000 stores worldwide, with strong integrations for Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, and more. Unlike generalist tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend focuses on revenue-driving automations like abandoned cart recovery and welcome series.

Key differentiator: It unifies email, SMS, web push, and popups without needing multiple apps. This is practical for solopreneurs or small teams managing marketing on a budget.

**Practical tip:** Start by connecting your store via API or app store integration. Omnisend pulls customer data, orders, and products automatically, saving hours of manual import.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend shines in automation and segmentation. Here's a deep dive into standout features with setup advice.

### Email Campaigns and Templates

Drag-and-drop editor with 250+ ecommerce-optimized templates. Supports product blocks that dynamically pull from your catalog.

**Practical advice:** For high open rates, use subject lines under 50 characters with personalization like "{{first_name}}, your cart awaits!" A/B test 2-3 variants on send time (e.g., 10 AM vs. 2 PM) using built-in tools. Aim for mobile-responsive designs—over 50% of opens are mobile.

### Automation Workflows

Pre-built workflows for welcome series, abandoned cart, post-purchase upsells, and re-engagement. Custom workflows use if/then logic.

**Practical advice:** Set up abandoned cart automation first: Trigger after 1 hour with a 20% discount code. Follow up at 24 hours and 72 hours. Data shows this recovers 10-15% of lost sales. Use segmentation by cart value—high-value carts get VIP messaging.

### SMS and MMS Marketing

Twilio-powered SMS with opt-in compliance. Personalize with product images in MMS.

**Practical advice:** Collect SMS opt-ins via popups offering 10% off. Limit to high-intent triggers like cart abandonment (conversion rates 5x email). Comply with TCPA by including unsubscribe links. Budget $0.015-$0.05 per message.

### Popups, Forms, and Push Notifications

Wheel of fortune, spin-to-win, or exit-intent popups. Web push for browser notifications.

**Practical advice:** Place popups on high-traffic pages like homepage. Target 3-5% conversion by offering lead magnets (e.g., free shipping). Test popup timing—delay 5-10 seconds. Push notifications work best for flash sales; segment by past buyers.

### List Building and Segmentation

RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) segmentation out-of-the-box. Custom segments by behavior, tags, or custom events.

**Practical advice:** Clean lists monthly by removing inactives (open rate killer). Segment win-back lists (no opens in 90 days) for re-engagement campaigns. Track custom events like "viewed product" for lookalike audiences.

### Reporting and Analytics

Revenue per recipient, ROI tracking, and campaign comparison. Integrates with Google Analytics.

**Practical advice:** Focus on attributable revenue, not just opens/clicks. Set goals in workflows to measure uplift (e.g., +25% from automations). Export CSV for deeper analysis in Excel.

## Omnisend Pricing Plans

Omnisend offers a forever-free plan and tiered paid options billed monthly or annually (20% discount).

- **Free:** Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month. All core features except SMS/pro workflows.
- **Standard ($16/mo for 500 contacts):** Unlimited emails, basic automations, popups.
- **Pro ($59/mo for 500 contacts):** Advanced segmentation, A/B testing, unlimited SMS.
- **Custom (Enterprise):** For 100k+ contacts, dedicated support.

Contacts over limits auto-upgrade. SMS/push charged separately.

**Practical advice:** Start free to test integrations. Upgrade to Standard for scaling (handles 10k contacts affordably). Calculate ROI: If automations generate $500/mo revenue, it pays for Pro.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-first: Deep store integrations and product personalization.
- Omnichannel: Email + SMS + push in one platform.
- User-friendly: 4.7/5 on G2, intuitive for non-marketers.
- Strong support: 24/7 chat, knowledge base.

### Cons
- Limited non-ecommerce use: Not ideal for B2B newsletters.
- SMS costs add up for high volume.
- No native video email support (embed links instead).
- Learning curve for advanced workflows.

**Practical advice:** If SMS is key, budget extra. For video, embed YouTube/Vimeo links in emails.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Perfect for Shopify/WooCommerce stores under $1M revenue focused on direct-to-consumer sales. Great for DTC brands like fashion, beauty, or subscriptions needing cart recovery.

Not for: Agencies managing multiple clients (limited seats) or content-heavy newsletters.

**Practical tip:** Migrate from Klaviyo? Omnisend's import wizard transfers lists/automations seamlessly.
