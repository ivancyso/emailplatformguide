# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. In this MailerLite vs ConvertKit comparison, we'll break down everything you need to know—from pricing and features to automation capabilities and user experience. Whether you're a solo creator, small business owner, or growing team, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide.

Both platforms are popular for their user-friendly interfaces and powerful features, but they cater to slightly different audiences. MailerLite excels in affordability and simplicity for beginners, while ConvertKit shines for content creators with advanced tagging and sequencing.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing platform. Here's how MailerLite and ConvertKit stack up.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite offers a **free plan** for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—perfect for testing without commitment. Paid plans start at **$10/month** for 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to **$20/month** for 1,000. Higher tiers go up to **$270/month** for 150,000 subscribers.

Key perks:
- No contracts; monthly or annual billing (20% discount on annual).
- Unlimited emails on all plans (send frequency limited by subscriber count).
- Includes landing pages, popups, and forms.

**Practical advice**: If you're bootstrapping or have under 1,000 subscribers, start free. Upgrade only when you hit limits—it's cost-effective for small lists.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) has **no free plan** but a **14-day free trial**. The Creator plan starts at **$15/month** for up to 300 subscribers, jumping to **$29/month** for 1,000. The Creator Pro plan (with advanced commerce features) begins at **$25/month** for 300 subscribers.

For 10,000+ subscribers, expect **$119–$379/month** depending on the plan.

Key perks:
- Annual billing saves 20%.
- Unlimited emails and sends.

**Practical advice**: ConvertKit's pricing suits those investing in creator tools like paid newsletters. Budget for growth, as costs rise quickly with list size compared to MailerLite.

**Winner**: MailerLite for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit for premium creator features.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Interface

Both platforms prioritize simplicity, but differences emerge in workflows.

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

MailerLite's editor is intuitive with a **drag-and-drop builder** mimicking popular page builders. Onboarding includes video tutorials and a setup wizard. Navigation is clean, with a dashboard showing key metrics like open rates.

**Pros**:
- Quick campaign setup (under 10 minutes for basics).
- Mobile-responsive templates.

**Cons**: Fewer advanced customization options.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Dashboard

ConvertKit uses a **visual automation builder** and tagging system that's powerful once learned. The interface focuses on sequences and broadcasts, with a steeper curve for beginners.

**Pros**:
- Tag-based segmentation feels natural for creators.
- Clean subscriber profiles.

**Cons**: Less visual for non-creators; editor is plain.

**Practical advice**: Test both trials. If you prefer Canva-like ease, go MailerLite. For tagging enthusiasts (e.g., segmenting by blog post opens), ConvertKit wins.

## Core Features: Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates

- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates, photo editor integration, and RSS-to-email. Strong for newsletters and promos.
- **ConvertKit**: Simpler builder with 100+ templates optimized for creators. Excels in plain-text emails that perform well.

**Tip**: Use MailerLite for visually rich emails; ConvertKit for high-engagement text-based ones.

### Automation and Workflows

- **MailerLite**: Automation builder with triggers like sign-ups or clicks. Supports A/B testing and multi-step flows. Free plan includes basics.

- **ConvertKit**: Superior **visual automation canvas** with tags for dynamic segmentation (e.g., auto-tag openers). Sequences are a standout for drip campaigns.

**Practical advice**: Map your customer journey first. For simple welcome series, MailerLite suffices. Complex funnels? ConvertKit's tagging reduces manual work.

### Segmentation and List Management

Both segment by behavior, but:
- MailerLite: Groups, tags, and custom fields.
- ConvertKit: Tag-first approach (e.g., "lead-magnet-downloader") for precise targeting.

**Tip**: Export CSV lists to test segmentation logic before committing.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, popups, and forms on all plans. High-converting templates.
- **ConvertKit**: Forms and landing pages included; Commerce add-on for paid subscriptions.

**Winner**: MailerLite for lead gen tools.

### Integrations and Deliverability

Both integrate with Zapier, WordPress, etc. Deliverability hovers around 99% (industry standard; check yourdomain tests).

- MailerLite: 1,000+ integrations; EU-based for GDPR.
- ConvertKit: Strong with creator tools like Gumroad.

**Practical advice**: Verify SPF/DKIM setup during trials to ensure inbox placement.

## Support and Resources

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive help center, and Facebook group.
- **ConvertKit**: Email support (priority for Pro), Creator Network community, podcasts.

**Tip**: Join communities early—ConvertKit's for inspiration, MailerLite's for quick fixes.

## Pros and Cons Summary

### MailerLite
**Pros**:

- All-in-one (sites, popups).
- Beginner-friendly.

**Cons**:
- Limited advanced automations.
- Fewer creator-specific tools.

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:
- Powerful tagging/sequences.
- Creator economy focus (subscriptions).
- High customization.

**Cons**:
- Higher price, no free plan.
- Steeper learning curve.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Small businesses, bloggers starting out.
- Those needing free tools and lead magnets.
- Budget under $20/month.

**Example**: A local shop owner sending weekly promos.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators, authors, podcasters.
- Need for subscriber monetization.
- Complex audience segmentation.

**Example**: Newsletter writer with paid tiers.

**MailerLite** is the practical choice for most beginners and cost-sensitive users due to its free tier, ease, and value. **ConvertKit** edges out for serious creators prioritizing automations and revenue tools.
