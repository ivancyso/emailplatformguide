# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business outreach. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp are two leading tools, each with strengths for different users. This guide compares them head-to-head on pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur starting with a free plan or a growing team needing advanced automation, we'll break it down with practical insights.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free tiers, but they differ in scaling.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. It's great for testing but limits growth.

Paid plans (Essentials, Standard, Premium) start at $13/month for 500 contacts (billed annually). Costs rise quickly:
- 5,000 contacts: ~$75/month (Standard)
- 10,000 contacts: ~$100+/month

Higher tiers add features like automation and A/B testing. Watch for contact-based billing, which can inflate costs as your list grows.

**Practical Tip:** If your list exceeds 5,000, calculate total costs including add-ons like landing pages ($13+/month extra).

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts but caps at 300 emails/day. Ideal for low-volume senders.

Paid plans are email-volume based (Starter, Business, Enterprise):
- Starter: 5,000 emails/month for $25/month
- Business: 20,000 emails for $65/month

Unlimited contacts make it scalable without per-subscriber fees. SMS and advanced automation are add-ons.

**Practical Tip:** For high-contact lists with moderate sends (e.g., 10k contacts, 10k emails/month), Brevo saves money long-term. Use their pricing calculator for custom quotes.

**Winner:** Brevo for growing lists; Mailchimp for very small starters.

## Features: Core Capabilities Compared

Both platforms excel in email marketing, but nuances matter.

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 100+ responsive templates. Customization is beginner-friendly.

Brevo offers a block-based editor with 40+ templates, plus transactional email focus. It's flexible for code tweaks.

**Advice:** Test both free editors—Mailchimp feels more polished for non-tech users.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp's automation is robust in Standard+ plans: welcome series, abandoned cart, behavioral triggers.

Brevo shines with multi-channel automation (email + SMS + WhatsApp) from Business plan. Classic and advanced workflows handle complex journeys.

**Practical Tip:** For e-commerce, Brevo's cart abandonment flows integrate natively with Shopify/WooCommerce.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Mailchimp uses tags, groups, and behavioral data for segmentation. Reports cover opens, clicks, revenue.

Brevo's segmentation is contact-based with real-time updates. Analytics include heatmaps and deliverability stats.

**Tip:** Export segments to CSV in both for deeper analysis in Google Sheets.

### Integrations and E-commerce

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Zapier, Shopify).

Brevo supports 300+ via Zapier, plus native CRM, WordPress, and transactional APIs.

**Advice:** If you use custom APIs, Brevo's developer-friendly docs edge out.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp wins for beginners: guided setup, tutorials, and a fun interface. Dashboard is clean.

Brevo is straightforward but has a steeper curve for automation setup. Modern UI with dark mode.

**Practical Tip:** Start with Mailchimp's free forever plan if you're new; switch to Brevo once comfortable with basics.

## Deliverability and Support

Both prioritize deliverability (Mailchimp: 99%+; Brevo: strong IP reputation).

Support: Mailchimp offers email/chat (paid plans); Brevo provides 24/7 chat/email for all, phone for higher tiers.

**Tip:** Warm up domains gradually in either to boost inbox rates—aim for 20% list growth/month max.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros:
- User-friendly interface
- Extensive template library
- Strong e-commerce integrations

Cons:
- Expensive for large lists
- Free plan limits

### Brevo Pros:
- Affordable scaling
- Multi-channel (SMS)
- Unlimited contacts free

Cons:
- Fewer templates
- Automation paywalled higher

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- Businesses with large lists/low sends (agencies, newsletters)
- Multi-channel needs (email + SMS)
- Cost-conscious scalers

**Migration Tip:** Export Mailchimp lists via CSV, import to Brevo, remap automations.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Beginners/solopreneurs
- Design-focused marketers
- Heavy e-commerce users

**Migration Tip:** Use Zapier for seamless data transfer from Brevo.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

Brevo edges out for value and scalability, especially post-rebrand improvements. Mailchimp remains king for ease and design.

Test both free plans: Build a sample campaign, segment 100 contacts, run an automation.

**Actionable Steps:** 1. Audit your list size/sends. 2. Sign up free. 3. Compare one feature weekly. 4. Check ROI after 30 days.

In 2024, the 'best' depends on your stage—Brevo for growth, Mailchimp for polish. Whichever you pick, consistent testing drives 20-30% better engagement.
