# Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Top 10 Email Marketing Platforms Compared

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many businesses searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're dealing with rising costs or need more advanced features like robust automation, CRM integration, or better deliverability, it's time to explore other options.

In this guide, we'll compare the top 10 Mailchimp alternatives based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, pricing transparency from official sites, and key features for small to medium businesses. Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or agency, these platforms offer scalable solutions. We'll break down pros, cons, pricing, and practical tips to help you switch seamlessly.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but jump quickly—e.g., 5,000 contacts can cost $75+/month. Users often complain about poor automation depth, sunsetted features like landing pages, and declining deliverability rates (around 85-90% per Mailtrap data).

Alternatives shine in areas like:
- **Affordable scaling**: Flat-rate or contact-based pricing without legacy penalties.
- **Advanced automation**: Behavioral triggers, A/B testing, and CRM sync.
- **E-commerce focus**: Abandoned cart flows and product recommendations.
- **Ease of migration**: CSV imports and template transfers.

Practical tip: Before switching, export your Mailchimp audience as CSV, clean duplicates with tools like NeverBounce, and test a small campaign on the new platform.

## 1. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign is a powerhouse for marketing automation, ideal for businesses needing CRM and email in one.

### Key Features
- 750+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress).
- Advanced automations with if/then logic.
- Site tracking and split testing.

### Pricing
Starts at $29/month (1,000 contacts, unlimited emails). Free trial: 14 days.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Deep personalization, excellent support (phone/chat). High deliverability (95%+).
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve; no free plan.

**Best for**: Agencies and e-commerce with 1K+ contacts. Migrate by mapping custom fields—takes ~2 hours.

## 2. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

ConvertKit targets creators and bloggers with simple, creator-focused tools.

### Key Features
- Visual automations (sequences).
- Native landing pages and forms.
- Subscriber scoring.

### Pricing
Free up to 300 subscribers; Creator plan $29/month (1,000 subs).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Intuitive interface, unlimited emails, strong tagging.
**Cons**: Limited reporting; no phone support.

**Best for**: Solopreneurs. Tip: Use their import wizard for Mailchimp lists—supports segments.

## 3. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo offers transactional + marketing email with SMS/WhatsApp.

### Key Features
- Unlimited contacts on free plan.
- Multichannel (email, SMS, chat).
- Automation workflows.

### Pricing
Free: 300 emails/day; Starter $25/month (20K emails).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Cost-effective for high volume; EU-based servers for GDPR.
**Cons**: Design templates basic.

**Best for**: International teams. Practical: Set up double opt-in to boost engagement post-migration.

## 4. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels.

### Key Features
- Conversion funnels, webinars (up to 1K attendees).
- AI email generator.
- E-commerce integrations.

### Pricing
$19/month (1,000 contacts). 30-day free trial.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Autoresponders shine; good mobile app.
**Cons**: Overwhelming dashboard.

**Best for**: Webinar users. Tip: A/B test subject lines—GetResponse's tool outperforms Mailchimp's.

## 5. Klaviyo

E-commerce specialist with data-driven personalization.

### Key Features

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts; scales with spend (e.g., $20/month for 500).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Shopify native; ROI-focused metrics.
**Cons**: Marketing emails limited on free.

**Best for**: Online stores. Integrate in minutes via app store.

## 6. AWeber

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: List size? Automation depth? Integrations?
2. **Trial Platforms**: Most offer 14-30 days free—import 10% of list to test.
3. **Pricing Calc**: Use calculators (e.g., ActiveCampaign's) for your volume.
4. **Migration Steps**:
   - Export CSV from Mailchimp (Audience > Export).
   - Sign up, import, map tags/fields.
   - Recreate top automations.
   - Update website forms (plugins like FluentCRM for WP).
   - Monitor first sends for deliverability.
5. **Practical Advice**: Segment lists pre-migration (e.g., engaged vs. inactive). Aim for 20-30% open rates benchmark. Use Google Postmaster for domain warmup.

Switching typically takes 1-3 days; many report 20-50% cost savings and higher engagement.
