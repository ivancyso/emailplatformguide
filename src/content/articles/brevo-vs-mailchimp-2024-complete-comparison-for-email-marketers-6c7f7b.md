# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are popular platforms designed for businesses of all sizes, offering automation, templates, and analytics. This in-depth comparison breaks down their pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide based on your needs.

Whether you're a solopreneur with a small list or scaling a team, we'll provide practical advice on setup, migration, and optimization.

## Pricing Comparison: Brevo vs Mailchimp

Pricing is often the first deciding factor. Both offer free plans, but they differ in limits and scaling costs.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts and 300 emails per day—ideal for testing without commitment. Paid plans start at $25/month for 20,000 emails (Starter), scaling to $65/month for 20,000 emails with advanced automation (Business). Enterprise plans are custom-priced for high-volume senders.

Practical tip: If your list grows beyond 500 contacts but sends are low-volume daily, Brevo's free tier shines. Upgrade only when needing SMS or more automations.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Essentials starts at $13/month for 500 contacts (unlimited sends within fair use), Standard at $20/month for behavioral automations, and Premium at $350/month for 10,000+ contacts.

Note: Mailchimp shifted to contact-based pricing, which can get expensive quickly as lists grow. For example, 5,000 contacts on Essentials costs around $75/month.

### Verdict on Pricing
Brevo wins for high-volume, low-frequency senders (e.g., newsletters). Mailchimp suits frequent campaigns with small lists. Calculate your monthly sends x contacts to compare—Brevo often costs 30-50% less for mid-sized lists.

## Ease of Use: User Interface and Onboarding

### Brevo's Interface
Brevo features a clean, intuitive dashboard with drag-and-drop email builder. Onboarding includes guided tours for campaigns and automations. It's beginner-friendly, especially for transactional emails.

Practical advice: Start with Brevo's template library (100+ free designs). Customize via the editor without coding—add dynamic blocks for personalization in minutes.

### Mailchimp's Interface
Mailchimp's UI is polished with a creative studio vibe. The email builder is robust, supporting complex layouts. However, navigation can feel cluttered for new users due to extensive menus.

Practical tip: Use Mailchimp's content optimizer during setup—it scores your emails for engagement potential. Great for A/B testing subject lines right from onboarding.

### Who Wins?
Brevo for simplicity (faster setup under 10 minutes), Mailchimp for power users comfortable with more options.

## Features Breakdown

### Email Automation
Brevo offers multi-step workflows, including webhooks and SMS triggers. Classic automation is free; advanced requires Business plan.

Mailchimp excels with customer journey builder on Standard+, including behavioral triggers like site abandons.

Practical advice: For e-commerce, set up Brevo's cart abandonment flow (integrates natively with WooCommerce). In Mailchimp, tag subscribers by behavior for segmented re-engagement—boost open rates by 20% typically.

### Templates and Design
Both have responsive templates. Brevo: 50+ industry-specific. Mailchimp: 100+ with theme builder.

### Analytics and Reporting
Brevo provides real-time tracking, heatmaps, and split testing. Mailchimp offers detailed reports with predictive analytics on Premium.

### Additional Features

#### h3: SMS and Chat
Brevo bundles SMS and live chat natively—send transactional texts from $0.01/message.

Mailchimp lacks built-in SMS (third-party via integrations).

#### h3: Landing Pages and Forms
Brevo: Unlimited forms/pages on paid plans. Mailchimp: Basic on free, advanced popups on higher tiers.

#### h3: E-commerce Tools
Both support product recommendations. Brevo integrates with Shopify for orders; Mailchimp has robust abandoned cart recovery.

## Integrations and Scalability

Brevo connects to 300+ apps (Zapier, WordPress, Google Analytics). Strong for transactional APIs.

Mailchimp boasts 6,000+ integrations, including deep CRM ties (HubSpot, Salesforce).

Practical advice: Audit your stack— if heavy on e-com (BigCommerce), Brevo scales better. For agencies with CRMs, Mailchimp's API is more mature.

Scalability: Brevo handles 100M+ emails/month reliably; Mailchimp suits enterprises but pricing scales steeply.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

Brevo: Email/chat support (paid plans get priority, 24/7 phone). Deliverability ~95% (IP warming tools included).

Mailchimp: Extensive knowledge base, email support (priority on Premium). Industry-leading 98%+ deliverability with dedicated IPs.

Tip: Warm up domains gradually on either—start with 50 sends/day to avoid spam filters. Monitor bounce rates under 2%.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros
- Affordable scaling
- All-in-one (email + SMS + chat)
- Unlimited contacts free

Cons: Fewer templates, basic reporting on free.

### Mailchimp Pros
- Superior automation/design
- Vast integrations
- Strong community resources

Cons: Expensive for large lists, no native SMS.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?
Small businesses, e-com stores, or transactional-heavy users (e.g., devs needing APIs). Best if budget < $50/month and sends are daily bursts.

Practical migration tip: Export CSV from Mailchimp, import to Brevo (maps fields automatically). Remap automations in 1-2 hours.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
Content creators, agencies, or growth-focused teams needing advanced segmentation. Ideal for 1k-10k lists with frequent campaigns.

Tip: Leverage audience insights for hyper-personalization—group by purchase history for 15-25% uplift in conversions.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your priorities: Brevo for cost-effective, multi-channel marketing; Mailchimp for feature-rich campaigns. Test both free plans—send a sample newsletter to 100 contacts and track metrics.

For most small-mid businesses, **Brevo edges out as the smarter pick in 2024** due to pricing and SMS inclusion, unless you need Mailchimp's ecosystem depth.
