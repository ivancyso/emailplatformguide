# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Picks for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a staple in email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for alternatives. If you're facing similar issues—high fees after 500 subscribers, clunky editor, or inadequate support—this guide covers the top Mailchimp alternatives. These platforms offer better value, advanced features, and scalability for solopreneurs, small businesses, and e-commerce stores.

We'll break down each option with key features, pricing highlights, pros/cons, and ideal users. All info is based on official sites and user feedback as of 2024. Let's dive in.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Before exploring alternatives, consider these common pain points:

- **Pricing jumps**: Mailchimp's Essentials plan starts at $13/month for 500 contacts but scales quickly to $350+/month for 50k contacts.
- **Automation limits**: Basic automations on lower tiers; advanced ones require Premium ($350+/month).
- **Editor issues**: Drag-and-drop is functional but lacks mobile-first precision.
- **Deliverability**: Solid but not always top-tier for high-volume senders.

Alternatives often provide unlimited contacts on lower plans, superior automations, and better integrations. Practical tip: Audit your current usage (list size, send frequency, automations) before migrating to match needs accurately.

## 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
Brevo combines transactional emails, SMS, and marketing automation in one platform. Standout: Unlimited contacts on paid plans, excellent deliverability (99%+ claimed), and a modern editor.

### Pricing
Free plan: 300 emails/day. Paid from €25/month (20k emails).

### Pros
- Affordable scaling.
- Multi-channel (email + SMS + chat).
- API for developers.

### Cons
- Learning curve for advanced automations.

**Best for**: SMBs needing transactional + marketing emails. Migrate tip: Use their importer for seamless CSV transfer from Mailchimp.

## 2. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
Automation king with machine learning for split testing, site tracking, and CRM integration. Deep segmentation based on behavior.

### Pricing
Starts at $29/month (1k contacts). Lite plan suits beginners.

### Pros
- Powerful automations (e.g., if/then branches).
- 700+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress).
- High deliverability.

### Cons
- Steeper learning curve.
- No free plan beyond trial.

**Best for**: E-commerce and lead nurturing. Advice: Start with their automation blueprints to replicate Mailchimp journeys quickly.

## 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

### Key Features
Creator-focused: Visual automations, tagging system, landing pages, and subscriber scoring.

### Pricing
Free up to 1k subscribers. Creator plan $29/month.

### Pros
- Intuitive for newsletters.
- Broadcasts and sequences shine.
- One-click upsells.

### Cons
- Fewer templates.
- E-com integrations extra.

**Best for**: Bloggers and creators. Tip: Use commerce tools for paid newsletters—export segments easily from Mailchimp.

## 4. Klaviyo

### Key Features
E-commerce powerhouse with revenue tracking, dynamic product recommendations, and A/B testing.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails. Scales with revenue (e.g., $60/month for 1k).

### Pros
- Shopify/WooCommerce native.
- Predictive analytics.
- Flow builder for abandoned carts.

### Cons
- E-com focused; less for general lists.

**Best for**: Online stores. Practical: Integrate with your store first, then import historical data.

## 5. MailerLite

### Key Features
Drag-and-drop editor, pop-ups, landing pages, and basic automations. High deliverability.

### Pricing
Free up to 1k subscribers/12k emails. Growing Business $10/month.

### Pros
- Super affordable.
- Website builder included.
- A/B testing free.

### Cons
- Automations less advanced.

**Best for**: Budget-conscious startups. Tip: Their migration tool handles Mailchimp lists in minutes.

## 6. GetResponse

### Key Features
All-in-one: Email, webinars, funnels, and e-com store builder.

### Pricing
Free up to 500 contacts. Email Marketing $19/month.

### Pros
- Conversion funnel tools.
- Autoresponders.
- 150+ templates.

### Cons
- Interface feels dated.

**Best for**: Marketers wanting webinars. Advice: Test funnels with small segments post-migration.

## 7. AWeber

### Key Features
Reliable automation, landing pages, and strong support. Tag-based segmentation.

### Pricing
$12.50/month (500 subscribers).

### Pros
- 24/7 support.
- High deliverability.
- Easy editor.

### Cons
- Fewer advanced features.

**Best for**: Non-profits/small biz. Tip: Use their free migration service.

## 8. Constant Contact

### Key Features
Event management, social ads, and AI content generator.

### Pricing
$12/month (500 contacts).

### Pros
- Beginner-friendly.
- Local business tools.

### Cons
- Limited automations.

**Best for**: Offline businesses. Advice: Leverage AI for quick campaigns.

## 9. Omnisend

### Key Features
Omnichannel for e-com: Email, SMS, push. Pre-built workflows.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails. Standard $16/month.

### Pros
- E-com automations.
- Unified inbox.

### Cons
- SMS costs extra.

**Best for**: Retailers. Tip: Sync with Shopify for real-time data.

## 10. Drip

### Key Features
Advanced workflows, revenue tracking, and personalization.

### Pricing
$39/month (500 contacts).

### Pros
- Deep e-com insights.
- Custom events.

### Cons
- Pricey for small lists.

**Best for**: Agencies/scaling stores.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

### Step-by-Step Guide
1. **Assess Needs**: List size? Automations? Integrations (e.g., Zapier, Shopify)? Budget?
2. **Compare Pricing**: Use calculators on sites—Brevo/MailerLite win for volume.
3. **Trial Platforms**: Most offer 14-30 day trials. Import a test list.
4. **Check Deliverability**: Review InboxRow or Mail-Tester scores.
5. **Migrate Data**: Export CSV from Mailchimp (Audience > Export). Most alternatives have one-click importers. Notify subscribers of change.
6. **Test Campaigns**: Send to small groups first.

**Practical Advice**:
- Prioritize automation depth (ActiveCampaign/Klaviyo) for growth.
- For cost-savings: MailerLite/Brevo.
- E-com: Klaviyo/Omnisend.
- Always enable double opt-in post-migration to maintain list health.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ActiveCampaign, and MailerLite offer better pricing, features, and flexibility for 2024. The right choice depends on your niche—e-com leans Klaviyo, creators prefer ConvertKit. Start with a free trial, migrate strategically, and watch engagement soar. Ready to switch? Pick one, test it, and optimize. Your inbox (and wallet) will thank you.
