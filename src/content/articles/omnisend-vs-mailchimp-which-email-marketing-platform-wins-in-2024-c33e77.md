# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your customer engagement and drive revenue. In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down Omnisend vs Mailchimp across key factors like pricing, features, ease of use, and more. Both platforms cater to businesses looking to scale email campaigns, but they shine in different areas: Mailchimp for general small businesses and beginners, Omnisend for ecommerce stores with omnichannel needs.

Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or ecommerce manager, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing platform. Both offer free plans, but their structures differ based on audience size and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends, ideal for testing. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for larger lists (Premium). Key tiers:
- **Essentials**: Basic automation, A/B testing.
- **Standard**: Advanced automation, behavioral targeting.
- **Premium**: Multivariate testing, optimization tools.

Overage fees apply if you exceed contact limits. Mailchimp recently shifted to performance-based pricing, charging more for engaged audiences, which can increase costs for active lists.

### Omnisend Pricing

Omnisend's free plan allows 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, with full access to core features like automation. Paid plans begin at $16/month (Standard) for 500 contacts, up to $59/month (Pro) for advanced segmentation. No overage fees—instead, plans scale seamlessly.
- **Standard**: Email + SMS, popups, automation.
- **Pro**: Custom events, advanced reporting.

Omnisend includes SMS credits in higher plans, making it cost-effective for multichannel campaigns. For ecommerce, it often proves cheaper long-term due to built-in recovery tools.

**Practical Tip**: Calculate your monthly sends and contacts using each tool's pricing calculator. If you're under 500 contacts, start free on both. Ecommerce stores benefit from Omnisend's SMS inclusion.

## Features Breakdown

Both platforms offer robust features, but their strengths vary.

### Email Templates and Design

Mailchimp excels with its drag-and-drop editor and 100+ free templates optimized for various industries. High customization, including code blocks for advanced users.

Omnisend provides ecommerce-focused templates (e.g., cart abandonment, upsell flows) with a user-friendly builder. It supports dynamic product blocks that pull live data from your store.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for design flexibility; Omnisend for ecommerce-specific blocks.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp's automation is solid for beginners: welcome series, re-engagement, basic triggers. Customer Journey Builder (in higher plans) allows branching logic.

Omnisend shines here with pre-built ecommerce workflows like abandoned cart, back-in-stock, and cross-sell. Visual builder supports unlimited workflows, omnichannel triggers (email + SMS + push).

**Practical Advice**: Map your customer journey first. Use Omnisend if automations involve post-purchase behaviors; Mailchimp for simple newsletters.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Mailchimp offers tag-based segmentation, behavioral triggers, and predictive segmentation (Premium).

Omnisend's advanced segmentation uses custom events, product data, and RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis out-of-the-box. Personalization pulls dynamic content like recommended products.

**Tip**: Test segments with small sends. Omnisend's ecommerce integrations make hyper-personalization easier.

### Integrations and Omnichannel

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Shopify, WordPress) via native or Zapier.

Omnisend focuses on ecommerce: native Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce apps, plus SMS/Web Push. Fewer general integrations but deeper ecommerce ones.

**Pro Tip**: Check your stack. Shopify users save time with Omnisend's one-click sync.

### Analytics and Reporting

Both provide open rates, clicks, revenue tracking. Mailchimp's dashboards are intuitive with comparative reports. Omnisend emphasizes ROI with attributed revenue per channel.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is beginner-friendly, with extensive tutorials and a vast help center. Support: email/chat (paid plans), no phone.

Omnisend's dashboard is clean, ecommerce-oriented. 24/7 live chat, email support, and a knowledge base with video guides.

**User Feedback Insight**: New users prefer Mailchimp's familiarity; ecommerce pros rave about Omnisend's speed.

**Practical Advice**: Sign up for free trials. Spend 30 minutes building a workflow to gauge intuitiveness.

## Deliverability and Performance

Mailchimp boasts high deliverability (98%+) due to its scale and authentication tools (DKIM, SPF).

Omnisend maintains strong rates (95%+) with dedicated IPs on Pro plans and ecommerce warmup advice.

**Best Practice**: Always warm up new domains, use double opt-in, and clean lists regularly on either platform.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- Generous free plan.
- Extensive templates and integrations.
- Beginner-friendly.

**Cons**:
- Pricing jumps with engagement.
- Limited omnichannel.
- Weaker ecommerce automations.

### Omnisend
**Pros**:
- Ecommerce-focused automations.
- SMS/push included.
- Predictable pricing.

**Cons**:
- Smaller template library.
- Fewer non-ecommerce integrations.
- Free plan limits.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp** if: You're a service-based business, blogger, or beginner needing simple newsletters and broad integrations. Great for lists under 5,000 with low SMS needs.

- **Choose Omnisend** if: You run an online store (Shopify/Woo) and want cart recovery, upsells, SMS. Ideal for revenue-focused campaigns.

**Decision Framework**:
1. List size and budget?
2. Ecommerce or general marketing?
3. Need SMS/push?
4. Trial both and track setup time.

Many switch from Mailchimp to Omnisend for ecommerce growth, citing 30%+ revenue lifts from automations.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce due to specialized features and multichannel support, while Mailchimp remains a safe, versatile choice for beginners. Neither is 'best' universally—align with your goals.
