# 10 Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Top Email Marketing Platforms Compared

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, offering a free tier and user-friendly interface. However, as businesses grow, many users seek Mailchimp alternatives due to rising costs, limited automation, or better integrations. If you're experiencing audience limits on the free plan (now capped at 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month), complex pricing, or need advanced features like deeper personalization, it's time to explore options.

This roundup compares 10 proven Mailchimp alternatives based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, focusing on small businesses, e-commerce stores, and creators. We'll cover key features, pricing (as of late 2024; always check official sites), pros/cons, and practical tips for switching. Whether you need robust automation or budget-friendly plans, there's a fit here.

## Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp's pricing starts free but jumps to $13/month for 500 contacts (Essentials plan), scaling quickly—up to $350+/month for larger lists. Automation is basic compared to competitors, and recent changes like sunsetted features have frustrated users. Common pain points:

- **High costs for growing lists**: Pay-per-contact model.
- **Limited advanced segmentation**: Struggles with complex behaviors.
- **E-commerce gaps**: Better options exist for Shopify/ WooCommerce.

Switching can save 30-50% on costs while unlocking better ROI through higher deliverability and engagement. Pro tip: Export your Mailchimp list as CSV before migrating—most alternatives offer free importers.

## 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo stands out for its unlimited contacts on paid plans and seamless transactional email support. Pricing: Free for 300 emails/day; paid from $25/month for 20k emails.

**Pros**: Excellent deliverability (98%+), SMS/WhatsApp integration, detailed analytics. **Cons**: Automation builder less visual than Mailchimp.

**Practical Advice**: Ideal if you send transactional emails (invoices, resets). Migrate via their importer; test with a small segment first to monitor bounce rates. Users report 20% higher open rates post-switch.

## 2. ActiveCampaign

The automation king, ActiveCampaign excels in customer journey mapping with if/then logic.

**Pros**: 870+ integrations (including CRMs like Salesforce), site tracking, machine learning for send times. Starts at $29/month (1k contacts). **Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

**Practical Advice**: For sales teams, use conditional content for personalized nurtures. Export Mailchimp automations as JSON if possible; their support offers free migration help. ROI tip: A/B test win-back campaigns to recover 15% of lost revenue.

## 3. ConvertKit

Tailored for creators, ConvertKit focuses on subscriber value with forms, landing pages, and broadcasts.

**Pros**: Generous free plan (up to 10k subscribers), visual automations, subscriber scoring. Paid from $15/month. **Cons**: Fewer templates.

**Practical Advice**: Perfect for newsletters—use tags for segmentation (e.g., 'podcast listener'). Import CSV directly; set up welcome sequences immediately to maintain engagement. Creators see 25% list growth via incentives.

## 4. Klaviyo

E-commerce powerhouse integrated with Shopify, BigCommerce.

**Pros**: Predictive analytics, revenue per recipient metric, A/B testing on flows. Free to 250 contacts; $20/month after. **Cons**: E-com focused, less for general lists.

**Practical Advice**: Track abandoned carts automatically. Sync Mailchimp data via API; prioritize flows like post-purchase upsells. Stores report 30% revenue lift.

## 5. MailerLite

Affordable and intuitive, with strong drag-and-drop.

**Pros**: Free to 1k subscribers/12k emails, pop-ups, surveys. Paid $10/month. **Cons**: Basic reporting.

**Practical Advice**: Great starter alt—clone Mailchimp campaigns. Use feature voting board for requests. Migrate in under an hour; focus on mobile-responsive designs for 50%+ opens.

## 8. AWeber

Veteran platform with reliable delivery.

**Pros**: 150+ templates, RSS campaigns. $12.50/month. **Cons**: Dated interface.

**Practical Advice**: For blogs—auto-send new posts. Tag-based lists; their concierge onboarding eases switches.

## 9. Omnisend

Omnichannel for e-com (email + SMS + push).

**Pros**: Free to 500/500 SMS, popup builder. $16/month. **Cons**: SMS costs extra.

**Practical Advice**: Layer SMS on emails for 6x opens. Pre-built workflows; test cross-channel for cart recovery.

## 10. Drip

Advanced for behavioral e-com marketing.

**Pros**: Customer lifecycle emails, workflows. $39/month trial. **Cons**: Pricier.

**Practical Advice**: Score leads by actions. API migration; personalize with dynamic blocks.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: List size >5k? Prioritize unlimited contacts (Brevo). Heavy automation? ActiveCampaign/Klaviyo.
2. **Budget Check**: Calculate TCO—factor sends, contacts, add-ons.
3. **Test Deliverability**: Use tools like Mail-Tester post-migration.
4. **Migration Steps**:
   - Backup/export lists.
   - Sign up for trial/import.
   - Recreate top campaigns.
   - Update website forms.
   - Monitor first sends.
5. **Integrations**: Ensure CRM/e-com compatibility.
6. **Support**: Chat vs. email—ActiveCampaign wins here.

Pro Tip: Run parallel campaigns for 2 weeks to compare metrics. Tools like EmailOctopus can bridge gaps.
