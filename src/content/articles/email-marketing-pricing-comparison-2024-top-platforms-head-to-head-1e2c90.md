# Email Marketing Pricing Comparison 2024: Top Platforms Head-to-Head

Choosing the right email marketing platform can make or break your campaigns. With so many options, pricing is a key factor—especially as your subscriber list grows. This email marketing pricing comparison dives into the costs of popular tools like Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, Brevo (formerly Sendinblue), MailerLite, ConvertKit, and GetResponse. We'll break down starter plans, mid-tier options, and enterprise pricing based on subscriber counts (500, 2,500, 10,000, and 50,000). All data is current as of mid-2024; always check official sites for updates.

We'll cover factors to consider, a handy comparison table, detailed breakdowns, and practical tips to help you decide without overpaying.

## Key Factors to Consider in Email Marketing Pricing

Pricing isn't just about the monthly fee. Here's what to evaluate:

### 1. Subscriber Count and Billing Structure
Most platforms charge based on contacts (subscribers), not sends. As your list grows, costs scale. Look for:
- **Autorenewal discounts**: Annual billing often saves 15-20%.
- **Overage fees**: Some charge extra for exceeding limits.
- **Free tiers**: Great for testing, but limited features.

**Practical tip**: Estimate your growth. If starting small (<1,000 subs), prioritize free/cheap plans. For scaling businesses, choose unlimited sends.

### 2. Features per Price Tier
Basic plans handle newsletters; advanced ones offer automations, A/B testing, and segmentation.
- Entry-level: Drag-and-drop builders, basic templates.
- Mid-tier: Automations, landing pages.
- Premium: CRM integration, advanced analytics.

**Practical tip**: List must-haves (e.g., e-commerce integrations for Shopify users) and match to plans. Avoid paying for unused features.

### 3. Deliverability, Support, and Hidden Costs
High deliverability (95%+) prevents spam flags. Support varies:
- Email/chat for paid plans.
- Add-ons like SMS or premium templates cost extra.

**Practical tip**: Test with a free trial. Check reviews on G2 or Capterra for real deliverability stats.

### 4. Trial Periods and Money-Back Guarantees
Most offer 14-30 day trials. Some have 30-day refunds.

**Practical tip**: Migrate a small list during trial to test imports and sends.

## Detailed Pricing Breakdowns

### Mailchimp: Best for Beginners
Mailchimp's free plan suits solopreneurs (500 subs, 1,000 sends/month, basic automations). Paid tiers:
- Essentials ($13/mo for 500 subs): Unlimited sends, templates.
- Standard ($20/mo for 500-5k): Behavioral targeting.
- Premium ($350+ for 10k+): Advanced segmentation.

**Value check**: Great for <10k lists, but pricey at scale. Practical for e-commerce with integrations.

### ActiveCampaign: Automation Powerhouse
No free plan, but 14-day trial. Starts at Lite ($29/mo, 500 contacts): Email automation, site tracking.
- Plus ($49): Custom reporting.
- Pro ($149): Machine learning predictions.

**Value check**: Ideal for marketers needing CRM-like features. Unlimited sends justify cost for complex workflows.

### Brevo: Unlimited Sends Winner
Free forever (300 contacts/day). Paid from $25/mo (unlimited contacts/emails on higher tiers):
- Starter: Basic automation.
- Business ($65 for 10k): Sales CRM.

**Value check**: Budget-friendly for high-volume senders. Add-ons for SMS/chat.

### MailerLite: Free Tier Champion
Best free plan (1,000 subs, 12,000 sends). Paid ($10/mo starter):
All features (popups, surveys) unlocked early. Scales affordably.

**Value check**: Perfect for bootstrappers. No feature gates.

### ConvertKit: Creator-Friendly
$15/mo Creator (300 subs, unlimited sends/emails). Grows to $399 for 50k.
Focus: Visual automations, tagging.

**Value check**: Suits bloggers/podcasters. Simple scaling.

### GetResponse: All-in-One Suite
$19/mo Email Marketing (1,000 subs). Includes funnels, webinars.
Higher tiers add conversion funnels.

**Value check**: Good for agencies needing extras.

## Best Picks by Budget and Use Case

- **Under $20/mo (<2,500 subs)**: MailerLite or Mailchimp free/paid.
- **$20-50/mo (Scaling lists)**: Brevo or ConvertKit.
- **$50+ (Advanced needs)**: ActiveCampaign or GetResponse.
- **High-volume senders**: Brevo.
- **E-commerce**: Klaviyo (starts $20, but not in table; scales with revenue).

**Practical advice**:
1. Calculate TCO: (Monthly fee x 12) + add-ons - annual discount.
2. Use calculators on sites (e.g., Mailchimp's).
3. Start free/trial, upgrade as needed.
4. Watch for sunset free plans (Mailchimp reduced limits recently).
5. Negotiate enterprise deals.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Ignoring contact churn: Pay only for engaged subs.
- Feature overload: Stick to 80/20 rule.
- Forgetting compliance: GDPR/CCPA tools add value.

## Conclusion
In this email marketing pricing comparison, no one-size-fits-all winner exists. MailerLite shines for free/low-cost, ActiveCampaign for power users, and Brevo for unlimited value. Assess your subs, features, and growth—then trial 2-3 options.

For most small businesses, start with MailerLite or Brevo under $25/mo. Scaling? ActiveCampaign's automations pay off long-term. Bookmark this, check official pricing, and launch smarter campaigns today.
