# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's customer engagement and revenue. In this head-to-head matchup of **Omnisend vs Mailchimp**, we'll break down key factors like pricing, features, ease of use, and scalability. Whether you're a small ecommerce store or scaling up, this guide helps you decide based on real-world use cases.

## Overview of Omnisend and Mailchimp

**Mailchimp** has been a staple since 2001, known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and free plan for beginners. It serves over 12 million users with email campaigns, automations, and basic CRM features. Ideal for solopreneurs and small teams.

**Omnisend**, launched in 2014, targets ecommerce brands with omnichannel marketing (email, SMS, web push). It integrates seamlessly with Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce, focusing on recovery flows like abandoned carts. It's praised for advanced segmentation and automation without needing coding.

Both platforms emphasize deliverability, but Omnisend edges out in ecommerce-specific tools while Mailchimp shines in general marketing versatility.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is a top decider. Here's a practical breakdown (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates):

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic features.
- **Essentials**: $13/mo (5,000 contacts), adds automation.
- **Standard**: $20/mo (5,000), behavioral targeting.
- **Premium**: $350/mo (10,000+), advanced reporting.

Mailchimp charges per contact, which scales costs quickly. Overages apply for extra sends.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, full features.
- **Standard**: $16/mo (500 contacts), unlimited emails.
- **Pro**: $59/mo (500), A/B testing, custom events.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

Omnisend offers **unlimited emails** in paid plans, a huge win for frequent senders. SMS and push add-ons start at $0.02/message.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your TCO. For 5,000 contacts sending weekly newsletters + automations:
- Mailchimp Standard: ~$20-50/mo.
- Omnisend Standard: ~$59/mo but unlimited sends save on high-volume.

Test free trials: Mailchimp's is generous; Omnisend's lets you import lists immediately.

## Features Breakdown

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp's is beginner-friendly with 100+ templates. Omnisend provides ecommerce-optimized ones (product blocks, dynamic content) and a popup builder for list growth.

**Winner**: Omnisend for dynamic product feeds.

### Automation Workflows

#### Mailchimp Automations
- Customer journeys, tag-based triggers.
- Single-step or classic automations.
- Practical tip: Use for welcome series or re-engagement.

#### Omnisend Automations
- Pre-built workflows: abandoned cart ( recovers 15-30% avg), cart recovery, back-in-stock.
- Multi-channel: email → SMS escalation.
- Custom events via API.

**Practical Advice**: If ecommerce, set up Omnisend's cart abandonment: Trigger on cart value >$50, send SMS Day 2. Boosts revenue 10-20% typically.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp: Groups, tags, basic behavioral.
Omnisend: 360° profiles, predictive segmentation (e.g., VIP buyers), product recommendations.

**Tip**: Segment by purchase history in both, but Omnisend auto-tags based on behavior.

### Integrations
Mailchimp: 300+ (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).
Omnisend: 140+, deep ecommerce (Shopify native app, Klaviyo-like flows).

**Advice**: For Shopify users, Omnisend syncs orders real-time—no manual CSV imports.

### Analytics and Reporting
Both track opens, clicks, revenue. Omnisend adds ROI per campaign, SMS attribution. Mailchimp excels in A/B testing reports.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp: 4.5/5 intuitiveness, vast tutorials.
Omnisend: Steeper for non-ecom, but guided onboarding.

Support:
- Mailchimp: Email/ticket (paid), huge knowledge base.
- Omnisend: 24/7 chat/email, dedicated manager (Pro+).

**Practical Tip**: Start with Mailchimp if new to email; switch to Omnisend for growth.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- Free plan.
- All-in-one (landing pages, ads).
- Creative tools.

**Cons**:
- Expensive scaling.
- Limited ecommerce automations.
- Send limits.

### Omnisend
**Pros**:
- Omnichannel.
- Unlimited emails.
- Ecommerce focus.

**Cons**:
- No free ad tools.
- SMS costs extra.
- Less for non-ecom.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Choose Mailchimp if**:
- Beginner/small list (<5k).
- General marketing (blogs, services).
- Budget-tight start.

**Choose Omnisend if**:
- Ecommerce store (Shopify/Woo).
- Need SMS/push.
- High-volume sends.

**Migration Advice**:
1. Export CSV from old tool.
2. Map fields (ensure tags transfer).
3. Recreate top automations first.
4. A/B test campaigns post-switch.

Many users report 20-50% better ROI with Omnisend for stores due to recovery flows.

## Conclusion

**Omnisend vs Mailchimp** boils down to your business: Mailchimp for versatile, easy entry; Omnisend for ecommerce power. Both deliver 95%+ rates, but Omnisend's automations often yield higher revenue per subscriber.

Start with free trials. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%), CTOR (3-5%), revenue attribution. Whichever fits, consistent testing wins.
