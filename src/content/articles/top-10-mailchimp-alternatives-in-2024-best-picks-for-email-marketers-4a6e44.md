# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Picks for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but as businesses grow, many seek alternatives due to pricing changes, limited automation, or specific feature gaps. If you're evaluating options, this roundup covers the top Mailchimp alternatives based on real user feedback, features, and value. We'll compare key aspects like pricing, automation, deliverability, and integrations to help you choose wisely.

Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or agency, these platforms offer scalable solutions. All data is drawn from official sites and platforms like G2 and Capterra as of late 2023.

## Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but scale quickly with audience size. Recent deliverability concerns and a shift to a more sales-focused model have pushed users elsewhere. Alternatives often provide:

- **Better pricing for larger lists**: Flat or contact-based tiers without exponential jumps.
- **Advanced automation**: Without needing premium add-ons.
- **E-commerce focus**: Native Shopify/WooCommerce integrations.
- **Superior support**: Live chat or dedicated managers.

Practical tip: Audit your current workflow. Need strong automations? Go for ActiveCampaign. Creator-focused? Try ConvertKit.

## 1. ActiveCampaign

**Best for advanced automation.**

ActiveCampaign shines with its deep automation builder, site tracking, and CRM integration. Pricing starts at $29/month for 1,000 contacts (Lite plan), up to $149 for Plus.

### Key Features
- Drag-and-drop editor with conditional content.
- Machine learning for send-time optimization.
- 900+ integrations via Zapier.

### Pros & Cons
| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Powerful automations | Steeper learning curve |
| Excellent deliverability (99%+) | No free plan |

**Who it's for**: Marketers needing sales funnels. Start with their 14-day trial to test automations.

## 2. ConvertKit (now Kit)

**Best for creators and bloggers.**

ConvertKit focuses on simplicity with unlimited emails and subscriber rewards. Free for up to 300 subscribers; Creator plan at $29/month for 1,000.

### Key Features
- Visual automation sequences.
- Native landing pages and forms.
- Tagging over segments for personalization.

### Practical Advice
Use subscriber tags to segment by behavior (e.g., "opened last email"). Integrates seamlessly with WordPress.

**Pros & Cons**
| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Generous free tier | Limited reporting |
| Creator-friendly UI | E-com light |

## 3. Klaviyo

**Best for e-commerce (Shopify users).**

Klaviyo excels in data-driven flows like abandoned cart recovery. Free up to 250 contacts; scales to $20+ based on contacts/emails.

### Key Features
- Predictive analytics for revenue.
- One-click Shopify sync.
- A/B testing on subject lines.

### Pro Tip
Set up post-purchase flows: Thank you > Upsell > Review request. Boosts LTV by 20-30% per their case studies.

**Pros & Cons**
| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| E-com powerhouse | Complex for beginners |
| Free tier generous | Pricing spikes with volume |

## 4. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

**Best budget all-rounder with SMS.**

Brevo offers transactional emails + marketing at $25/month for 20k emails (Starter). Free: 300 emails/day.

### Key Features
- Unlimited contacts.
- Chat, SMS, WhatsApp.
- Automation with wait nodes.

**Advice**: Ideal if you outgrew Mailchimp's limits but want affordability. Use for multi-channel campaigns.

**Pros & Cons**
| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Multi-channel | Editor less intuitive |
| Flat pricing | EU-focused support |

## 5. MailerLite

**Best free plan alternative.**

Unlimited emails on free tier (1,000 subs). Growing Business at $10/month for 1,000 subs.

### Key Features
- Pop-ups and landing pages.
- Basic automations.
- High deliverability.

**Practical**: A/B test pop-up placements for 15-20% opt-in boosts.

**Pros & Cons**
| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Affordable scaling | Fewer advanced features |
| Easy migration | No phone support |

## 6. GetResponse

**Best for webinars and funnels.**

Starts at $19/month (Email Marketing). Includes webinars, funnels.

### Key Features
- Conversion funnels builder.
- Autoresponders.

**Tip**: Host webinars to nurture leads—proven 3x engagement.

**Pros & Cons**
| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| All-in-one | Overwhelming dashboard |

## 7. Constant Contact

**Best for small businesses.**

$12/month for 500 contacts. AI-powered tools.

### Key Features
- Event management.
- Social ads.

**Advice**: Great for nonprofits with list-building tools.

## 8. AWeber

**Reliable veteran.** $19/month starter.

Strong deliverability, RSS campaigns.

## 9. Omnisend

**Omnichannel e-com.** Free to 250 contacts; $16/month Standard.

SMS + push notifications.

## 10. Drip

**E-com automation.** $39/month.

Workflows with customer data platform.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **List Size & Budget**: Under 1k subs? MailerLite/ConvertKit. Larger? Brevo.
2. **Features Needed**: E-com? Klaviyo. Automations? ActiveCampaign.
3. **Migration**: Most offer CSV imports; test deliverability post-switch.
4. **Trials**: All have 14-30 day trials—import a segment and run a test campaign.
5. **Support**: Check G2 ratings (ActiveCampaign 4.5+).

**Practical Steps**:
- Export Mailchimp list.
- Sign up for 2-3 trials.
- Build one automation.
- Review analytics after a week.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like ActiveCampaign, Klaviyo, and Brevo offer better value for growing businesses. Prioritize based on your niche—e-com leans Klaviyo, creators to ConvertKit. No one-size-fits-all, but testing reveals the fit. Switch wisely to improve deliverability and ROI. Ready to migrate? Start with a free trial today and reclaim your email strategy.
