# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business outreach. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp are two giants in the space, each catering to different needs. This guide breaks down their features, pricing, and performance to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or scaling marketer, understanding these tools' strengths is key. We'll cover practical advice on setup, automation, and optimization based on real user experiences and official docs.

## Pricing: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but they differ in scalability.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for larger lists (Premium). Key factors:
- Billed by contacts, not sends.
- Overage fees apply if you exceed limits.
- Annual discounts available (up to 20%).

**Practical Advice:** Ideal for beginners testing waters. Monitor contact growth closely to avoid surprise costs. Use their pricing calculator for estimates.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts but caps at 300 emails/day. Paid plans begin at $25/month (Starter) for 20,000 emails/month, up to Enterprise (custom). Highlights:
- Unlimited contacts across plans.
- Pay per email volume, not contacts.
- No overage fees; upgrade as needed.

**Practical Advice:** Great for growing lists without contact-based penalties. If you send sporadically, the free plan's daily limit works well. Track send volumes monthly to pick the right tier.

**Winner:** Brevo for cost-effectiveness with large lists; Mailchimp for very small, low-volume users.

## Features Head-to-Head

Both platforms excel in core email marketing, but nuances matter.

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp offers a drag-and-drop builder with 100+ responsive templates. Customization is intuitive, with content blocks for images, text, and products.

Brevo's builder is similar, with 40+ templates focused on e-commerce and newsletters. It includes dynamic content blocks and A/B testing natively.

**Practical Advice:** Start with templates to save time. Test mobile previews always—80% of emails are opened on phones. Personalize subject lines using merge tags like {first_name}.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp's automations include welcome series, abandoned cart, and RSS campaigns. Classic Automations are free; Customer Journey (advanced) requires Premium.

Brevo shines with multi-channel automations (email + SMS), triggers like birthdays, and resale campaigns. All plans include automation.

**Practical Advice:** Map customer journeys first: awareness > nurture > purchase. Use Brevo for SMS integration if your audience prefers texts; Mailchimp for behavioral triggers in e-com.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Mailchimp segments by tags, groups, or behavior. Reports cover opens, clicks, revenue.

Brevo uses contact attributes and lists for segmentation. Analytics include heatmaps, bounce tracking, and ROI calculators.

**Practical Advice:** Segment lists weekly (e.g., engaged vs. inactive). Aim for 20-30% open rates initially; A/B test to improve. Export data to Google Analytics for deeper insights.

### Integrations and Deliverability

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Shopify, WordPress, Google). Deliverability: 80-90% inbox rates reported.

Brevo connects to 300+ (Zapier, WooCommerce) and handles transactional emails seamlessly. Known for high deliverability (95%+ via dedicated IPs on paid plans).

**Practical Advice:** Verify domain authentication (DKIM/SPF) on day one to boost deliverability. Integrate with CRM like HubSpot for unified data.

## Ease of Use and Support

Mailchimp's interface is beginner-friendly, with tutorials and 24/7 chat/email support (paid plans).

Brevo is clean but has a steeper learning curve for advanced features. Support via chat/email (business hours free; 24/7 paid), knowledge base, and academy.

**Practical Advice:** Spend 30 mins in free trials. Mailchimp suits non-tech users; Brevo rewards automation pros. Join communities like Reddit's r/Emailmarketing for tips.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros:
- User-friendly
- Robust templates
- E-commerce focus

Cons:
- Expensive for large lists
- Limited free automation

### Brevo Pros:
- Affordable scaling
- Multi-channel
- Unlimited contacts

Cons:
- Fewer templates
- Daily free limit

## Who Should Choose What?

- **Pick Mailchimp if:** You're new, need polished designs, or run simple e-com stores.
- **Pick Brevo if:** You have growing lists, want SMS, or prioritize cost/deliverability.

**Practical Advice:** Audit your needs: list size, send frequency, channels. Trial both for 14-30 days. Migrate via CSV exports if switching.

## Conclusion

Brevo edges out for value and scalability, especially post-rebrand improvements. Mailchimp remains king for ease and branding. Test based on your volume—neither is 'one-size-fits-all.'

For 2024, factor AI trends: Both add predictive sends. Start small, measure ROI (aim 40:1 return), and scale. Your choice depends on budget and goals—happy emailing!
