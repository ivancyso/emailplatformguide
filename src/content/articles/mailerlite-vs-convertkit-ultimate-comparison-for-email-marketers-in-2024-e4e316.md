# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. MailerLite and ConvertKit are two popular platforms favored by small businesses, bloggers, and creators. Both offer automation, beautiful templates, and segmentation, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, support, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling to thousands of subscribers, we'll provide practical advice based on real user scenarios.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is often the first deciding factor. Both platforms use subscriber-based tiers, but MailerLite emphasizes affordability, while ConvertKit targets creators with value-packed plans.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite offers a generous **free plan** for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. This includes core features like automation and landing pages—ideal for bootstrapped solopreneurs testing the waters.

Paid plans:
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month (500 subs), scales to $20 for 2,500 subs.
- **Advanced**: $21/month (500 subs) with extras like A/B testing and web push notifications.
- No contracts; monthly or annual billing (20% discount on annual).

Practical tip: If you're under 1,000 subs, stick to free and upgrade seamlessly as you grow. Annual billing saves money for consistent senders.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (rebranded as Kit but still widely known as ConvertKit) dropped its free plan but offers a **14-day free trial**. Plans start higher:
- **Creator**: $15/month (300 subs, annual) or $29/monthly.
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month (500 subs, annual) with advanced reporting.
- **Enterprise**: Custom for large lists.

Higher tiers unlock commerce tools, subscriber scoring, and predictive analytics.

Practical tip: ConvertKit suits those willing to invest early for creator-specific tools like one-click upsells. Calculate costs: for 1,000 subs, expect ~$50/month annually.

| Subscribers | MailerLite (Monthly) | ConvertKit (Monthly) |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Up to 1,000 | Free                | ~$50 (annual equiv) |
| Up to 5,000 | $30                 | ~$100               |

MailerLite wins on entry-level pricing; ConvertKit justifies costs for advanced users.

## Ease of Use and Interface

### MailerLite: Beginner-Friendly

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with a clean dashboard. No coding needed for popups, forms, or sites. Setup takes minutes: import contacts, design a campaign, and launch.

Pros:
- Visual automation builder (if-then flows).
- Mobile app for on-the-go management.

Cons: Fewer advanced customization options.

### ConvertKit: Powerful but Steeper Curve

ConvertKit uses a tagging system over traditional lists, which is powerful for segmentation but requires learning. The visual builder is modern, with sequence automations shining for nurture campaigns.

Pros:
- Tag-based subscriber management for hyper-personalization.
- Clean analytics dashboard.

Cons: Interface can overwhelm absolute beginners.

**Practical advice**: Test both trials. If you're non-technical, MailerLite's simplicity saves hours. Creators comfortable with Zapier-like logic prefer ConvertKit.

## Feature Breakdown

### Email Builder and Templates

Both have responsive, drag-and-drop editors.
- **MailerLite**: 80+ templates, blocks for images/videos. Strong on popups and embedded forms.
- **ConvertKit**: Creator-optimized templates, commerce blocks for digital products.

Winner: Tie, but ConvertKit edges for product sellers.

### Automation and Segmentation

- **MailerLite**: Drag-and-drop automations (welcome series, abandoned carts). Basic segmentation by tags/behavior.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences (drip campaigns) and advanced tagging/actions. Subscriber scoring predicts engagement.

Practical tip: For simple autoresponders, MailerLite suffices. Use ConvertKit for complex funnels like course launches.

### Landing Pages and Forms

- MailerLite: Unlimited landing pages/websites on all plans, plus popups/quizzes.
- ConvertKit: Forms, landing pages, checkouts integrated.

MailerLite excels for lead gen on a budget.

### Analytics and A/B Testing

- MailerLite: Open/click rates, revenue tracking, A/B on paid plans.
- ConvertKit: Detailed reports, churn prediction on Pro.

## Integrations

Both integrate with 1,000+ apps via Zapier.

- **MailerLite**: Native WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics.
- **ConvertKit**: Deep WooCommerce, Teachable, Gumroad for creators.

Practical advice: E-commerce users pick MailerLite/Shopify; course creators choose ConvertKit.

## Customer Support

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email, extensive academy/videos. Highly rated (4.9/5 on Capterra).
- **ConvertKit**: Email support (response <24h), help docs, creator community. Solid but less instant.

MailerLite wins for responsive help.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros**:
- Free plan.
- Affordable scaling.
- Easy popups/landing pages.

**Cons**:
- Limited advanced segmentation.
- Fewer creator-specific tools.

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:
- Superior tagging/automations.
- Built for monetization.
- Excellent for bloggers.

**Cons**:
- Higher cost.
- No perpetual free tier.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Beginners/small businesses (<2,500 subs).
- Budget-conscious marketers needing quick wins.
- Agencies building lead magnets (popups shine).

Example: A local bakery automating newsletters—free plan handles 500 subs effortlessly.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators/bloggers with products.
- Those prioritizing personalization/scaling.
- Users okay with $30+/month investment.

Example: Podcaster nurturing leads into paid memberships—tagging automates perfectly.

**Practical advice**: Audit your list size, tech comfort, and goals. Start with MailerLite free; switch to ConvertKit if tagging becomes essential. Track ROI: aim for 20-30% open rates baseline.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to **cost vs power**. MailerLite is the affordable, user-friendly choice for most startups, delivering 80% of features at a fraction of the price. ConvertKit pulls ahead for serious creators needing sophisticated automations and monetization.

No one-size-fits-all—trial both. For 2024, if affordability rules, go MailerLite. For growth-focused creators, ConvertKit scales better long-term.
