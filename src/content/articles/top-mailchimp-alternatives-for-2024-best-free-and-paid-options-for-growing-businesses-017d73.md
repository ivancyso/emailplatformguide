# Top Mailchimp Alternatives for 2024: Best Free and Paid Options for Growing Businesses

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation in lower tiers, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for alternatives. If you're dealing with high costs, need advanced automations, or want better deliverability without complexity, it's time to explore options.

This roundup covers the top Mailchimp alternatives based on user reviews from G2, Capterra, and real-world features. We'll compare pricing, key strengths, and who each is best for. All data is current as of 2024—always check official sites for updates.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month, with paid plans starting at $13/month but jumping quickly (e.g., $75+/month for 5,000 contacts). Advanced features like behavioral automations require higher tiers.

Common pain points:
- **Pricing opacity**: Bills can surprise as lists grow.
- **Automation limits**: Basic journeys in free/essentials plans.
- **Editor quirks**: Drag-and-drop can feel clunky for complex designs.
- **Deliverability**: Averages 80-90% but lags behind specialists.

Alternatives often offer better value, unlimited sends, or niche strengths like e-commerce focus.

## Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)
Brevo stands out for transactional emails alongside marketing. Free plan: unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day. Paid starts at $25/month for 20,000 emails.

**Key features**:
- Multi-channel (email, SMS, WhatsApp).
- Automation workflows with triggers.
- Drag-and-drop editor similar to Mailchimp but faster.

**Best for**: SMBs needing transactional + marketing emails. Integrates with Shopify, WordPress.

**Pricing edge over Mailchimp**: Unlimited contacts on all plans.

### 2. ActiveCampaign
Powerhouse for automations. Starts at $29/month (1,000 contacts).

**Key features**:
- Advanced machine learning scoring.
- 700+ integrations (CRM like Salesforce).
- Site tracking for personalized emails.

**Practical advice**: Use conditional content blocks for dynamic emails. Ideal if you sell courses or services—set up win-back sequences easily.

**Drawback**: Steeper learning curve; start with their free trial and templates.

### 3. ConvertKit (now Kit)
Creator-focused, simple interface. Free up to 300 subscribers; $15/month for 500.

**Key features**:
- Visual automations (no coding).
- Tagging for segmentation.
- Native landing pages/forms.

**Best for**: Bloggers, podcasters. One-click upsells in emails boost conversions by segmenting by opens/clicks.

**Pro tip**: Tag subscribers by content downloads for targeted nurtures—simpler than Mailchimp's segments.

### 4. GetResponse
All-in-one with webinars. Free trial; $19/month (1,000 contacts).

**Key features**:
- Autoresponders + abandoned cart.
- Conversion funnels.
- E-commerce tools.

**Practical advice**: Build funnels like email > webinar > upsell. A/B test subject lines directly in campaigns.

### 5. Klaviyo
E-commerce king for Shopify. Free up to 250 contacts/500 sends; scales with revenue.

**Key features**:
- Predictive analytics.
- Flow builder for post-purchase.

**Best for**: Online stores. Flows recover 10-30% more revenue via SMS/email combos.

**Advice**: Integrate with Shopify first—auto-syncs data for hyper-personalization.

### 6. Constant Contact
User-friendly for beginners. $12/month (500 contacts).

**Key features**:
- Event management.
- Social ads integration.

**Best for**: Non-profits, local businesses. Mobile app for on-the-go sends.

### 7. AWeber
Reliable since 1998. Free up to 500 subs; $19/month.

**Key features**:
- RSS-to-email.
- Strong tagging.

**Advice**: Use for newsletters—auto-pulls blog posts.

### 8. Moosend
Affordable automation. Free up to 1,000 subs; $9/month.

**Key features**:
- Unlimited emails.
- Page builder.

**Best for**: Agencies—white-label option.

### 9. Omnisend
Omnichannel for e-com. Free up to 250 contacts; $16/month.

**Key features**:
- SMS/push notifications.

### 10. MailerLite
Budget-friendly. Free up to 1,000 subs/12,000 emails; $10/month.

**Key features**:
- Popups/surveys.
- Simple automations.

**Pro tip**: Embed forms easily on sites for quick list growth.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess your needs**: E-com? Klaviyo. Creators? ConvertKit. Volume sends? Brevo.
2. **Budget check**: Calculate based on list size. Use tools' calculators.
3. **Test deliverability**: Send test campaigns; check spam scores via Mail-Tester.
4. **Migration ease**: Export Mailchimp CSV, import directly—most support it.
5. **Trial everything**: All offer 14-30 day trials. Migrate a segment first.

**Practical migration steps**:
- Export audience from Mailchimp (CSV).
- Map tags/fields.
- Recreate top campaigns.
- Update website forms/integrations (Zapier helps).

Prioritize 95%+ deliverability and mobile-responsive templates.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ActiveCampaign, and Klaviyo offer better pricing, features, and scalability for 2024. For most users, Brevo's free tier or ConvertKit's simplicity wins; e-com picks Klaviyo.

Pick 2-3 to trial based on your niche. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and revenue per email post-switch. Your inbox (and wallet) will thank you.
