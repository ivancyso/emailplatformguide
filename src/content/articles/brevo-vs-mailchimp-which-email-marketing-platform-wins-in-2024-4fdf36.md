# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp—the two giants in the space—helps narrow it down. This Brevo vs Mailchimp guide targets businesses, freelancers, and marketers deciding between these tools based on real-world needs like pricing, features, and scalability.

We'll break down key aspects with practical advice to help you decide. Whether you're a startup with a tight budget or an e-commerce store needing automation, read on for unbiased insights.

## Pricing: Where Value Meets Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer free tiers, but they differ significantly in limits and scaling costs.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. It's great for testing but restrictive for growth. Paid plans start at the Essentials tier ($13/month for 500 contacts, 5,000 sends), scaling to Standard ($20/month) and Premium ($350+/month) with advanced automation and support.

**Practical Advice:** If your list exceeds 1,000 contacts quickly, expect costs to rise. For example, 5,000 contacts on Essentials costs $59/month. Use their pricing calculator to forecast—ideal for solopreneurs starting small.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo shines with a generous free plan: unlimited contacts but 300 daily emails (about 9,000/month). Paid plans begin at Starter ($25/month for 20,000 emails), Pro ($65/month for 20,000 emails with automation), and Enterprise (custom).

Transactional emails are free up to 300/day, a boon for e-commerce. No contact-based pricing means costs stay predictable as your list grows.

**Practical Advice:** Choose Brevo if you prioritize unlimited contacts. For a business sending 50,000 emails/month to 10,000 contacts, Brevo's Pro plan at $65 beats Mailchimp's $350+ Premium. Always factor in SMS add-ons (Brevo: $10/1,000 credits).

**Winner: Brevo** for most SMBs due to scalability without contact penalties.

## Features: Automation, Templates, and Integrations

Both excel in core email marketing, but nuances matter.

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 100+ mobile-responsive templates. Customization is beginner-friendly.

Brevo offers a similar block-based editor with 40+ templates, plus dynamic content blocks for personalization.

**Practical Advice:** Test both free editors. Mailchimp suits design novices; Brevo for those needing transactional + marketing emails in one dashboard.

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp's automation includes welcome series, abandoned cart, and behavioral triggers (Standard+ plans).

Brevo provides multi-step journeys, A/B testing, and RSS-to-email campaigns from day one on paid plans.

**Practical Advice:** For e-commerce, Brevo's built-in cart abandonment flows integrate seamlessly with WooCommerce/Shopify. Set up a simple drip: Day 1 welcome, Day 3 tip, Day 7 offer—track opens to refine.

### Integrations and Deliverability

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Shopify, Google Analytics, Zapier). Deliverability hovers around 90-95% with authentication tools.

Brevo connects to 300+ (including Facebook Ads, WordPress), boasting 99% deliverability via dedicated IPs on Pro plans.

**Practical Advice:** Verify domain SPF/DKIM/MX records on both for inbox placement. Brevo's edge in transactional deliverability helps high-volume senders avoid spam folders.

**Winner: Tie**—Mailchimp for ecosystem depth, Brevo for deliverability.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

### User Interface

Mailchimp's colorful dashboard feels playful, with guided onboarding.

Brevo's clean, CRM-like interface scales better for teams.

**Practical Advice:** Import a CSV list and send a test campaign. Mailchimp's analytics are visually engaging; Brevo's heatmaps reveal click patterns for optimization.

### Support Options

Mailchimp: Email/chat for paid users; free users get docs/forums.

Brevo: 24/7 chat/email, phone for Pro+, plus a knowledge base.

**Practical Advice:** Time a support query during your trial. Brevo's faster responses suit urgent issues like bounce handling.

**Winner: Brevo** for responsive support.

## Advanced Tools: A/B Testing, Analytics, and Segmentation

### Segmentation and Personalization

Both allow list segmentation by behavior/location. Mailchimp tags merge fields; Brevo uses custom fields with real-time updates.

**Practical Advice:** Segment engaged subscribers (opens >3%) for nurture campaigns. Brevo's automation scores leads automatically—send high-scorers premium offers.

### A/B Testing and Reporting

Mailchimp tests subjects/content (paid plans). Reports cover opens, clicks, revenue.

Brevo includes subject/line testing free on Pro, with revenue tracking and unsubscribe analysis.

**Practical Advice:** A/B test two subject lines: "50% Off" vs. "Exclusive Deal." Aim for 20%+ open lift, then scale winners.

**Winner: Brevo** for accessible testing.

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Mailchimp if:** You're a beginner needing polished templates and vast integrations. Best for content creators/blogs under 5,000 contacts.

- **Pick Brevo if:** You want affordable scaling, transactional emails, or SMS. Ideal for e-commerce/SMBs with growing lists.

**Practical Advice:** Start with 14-day trials. Migrate via CSV export/import—clean data first to avoid deliverability hits. Track ROI: (Revenue from emails - platform cost) / cost.

## Conclusion: Your Next Steps

In the Brevo vs Mailchimp showdown, Brevo edges out for cost-effectiveness, deliverability, and unlimited contacts, making it the smarter pick for most growing businesses in 2024. Mailchimp remains strong for ease and branding.
