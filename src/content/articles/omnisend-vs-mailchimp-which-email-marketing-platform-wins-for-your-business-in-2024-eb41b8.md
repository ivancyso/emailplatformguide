# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your customer engagement and sales. With Omnisend and Mailchimp as two leading contenders, this comparison breaks down their strengths, weaknesses, and ideal use cases. Whether you're running an online store or a service-based business, we'll help you decide based on real features and practical insights.

## Quick Overview of Omnisend and Mailchimp

**Mailchimp** has been a staple since 2001, known for its user-friendly interface and broad marketing tools. It's popular among small businesses, bloggers, and nonprofits. Mailchimp offers email campaigns, automations, landing pages, and social media ads in one dashboard.

**Omnisend**, launched in 2014, specializes in ecommerce marketing. It excels in omnichannel campaigns combining email, SMS, and web push notifications. It's particularly strong for Shopify and WooCommerce users, focusing on cart recovery and personalized customer journeys.

Both platforms offer free plans, but they cater to different audiences: Mailchimp for general marketing, Omnisend for sales-driven ecommerce.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is a key decider. Here's a breakdown based on current plans (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates).

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic features.
- **Essentials**: $13/month (5,000 contacts), unlimited sends, basic automations.
- **Standard**: $20/month (5,000 contacts), advanced automations, A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350+/month (10,000+ contacts), full features including multivariate testing.

Mailchimp bills by contacts and sends emails based on volume. Overages can add up quickly.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, core features like automations.
- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts), SMS/push included, unlimited emails.
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts), advanced segmentation, A/B testing.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume needs.

Omnisend charges per contact but offers unlimited emails in paid plans, making it cost-effective for frequent senders. SMS credits are extra but bundled in higher tiers.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your monthly sends and contact growth. For ecommerce with high traffic, Omnisend's unlimited emails save money. Test free plans first—export lists easily if switching.

## Feature Breakdown

### Email Builder and Templates
Both have drag-and-drop builders.
- **Mailchimp**: 100+ templates, highly customizable with content blocks. Great for newsletters.
- **Omnisend**: Ecommerce-focused templates with dynamic product blocks that pull from your store. Pre-built flows for welcome series, abandoned carts.

**Winner**: Omnisend for shops; Mailchimp for content-heavy emails.

### Automations and Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Behavioral automations, customer journeys, RSS-to-email. Standard plan unlocks branching.
- **Omnisend**: Superior for ecommerce—pre-built workflows for cart abandonment (up to 7 emails), back-in-stock, cross-sells. Omnichannel triggers (SMS fallback if email opens fail).

**Practical Tip**: Use Omnisend's cart recovery automation, which recovers 15-30% of lost sales on average (industry benchmark). In Mailchimp, build custom journeys but test thoroughly.

### Segmentation and Personalization
- **Mailchimp**: Tags, groups, behavioral segments. Predictive segmentation in Premium.
- **Omnisend**: Advanced ecommerce segments (browsed products, order value). Hyper-personalization with product recommendations via AI.

**Pro Tip**: Segment by purchase history in both, but Omnisend auto-populates dynamic content blocks for better relevance.

### Integrations
- **Mailchimp**: 300+ including Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier. Native Shopify but less deep.
- **Omnisend**: 100+ with heavy focus on ecommerce (Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce). Native SMS via Twilio.

Both integrate with Zapier for custom workflows.

### Analytics and Reporting
- **Mailchimp**: Detailed reports on opens, clicks, revenue. Ecommerce tracking add-on.
- **Omnisend**: Revenue tracking out-of-box, ROI per campaign, cross-channel attribution.

**Advice**: Track revenue per recipient (RPR) in Omnisend for ecommerce ROI clarity.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

**Mailchimp** wins for beginners—intuitive dashboard, vast tutorials, 24/7 chat/email support (paid plans).

**Omnisend** has a clean interface tailored to stores, with onboarding wizards. Support via chat/email, priority for Pro+.

**Practical Advice**: If non-techy, start with Mailchimp. Ecommerce owners appreciate Omnisend's store-sync setup (5 minutes via app).

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Choose Mailchimp if**:
- You're a solopreneur, blogger, or service business.
- Need social ads or landing pages.
- Prioritize ease over ecommerce specifics.

**Choose Omnisend if**:
- Running an online store (Shopify/Woo).
- Want SMS/push for higher engagement (SMS open rates ~98%).
- Focus on revenue automation.

**Migration Tip**: Both allow CSV imports. Use Zapier for ongoing sync. Test campaigns on small segments first.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce with omnichannel and revenue tools, while Mailchimp shines for versatile, easy marketing. Assess your business: high-volume store? Go Omnisend. General campaigns? Mailchimp.

Start with free trials—sign up via their sites. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and conversions post-launch. Whichever you pick, consistent testing and segmentation drive success.
