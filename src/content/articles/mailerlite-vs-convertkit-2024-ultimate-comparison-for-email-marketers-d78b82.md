# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. For creators, bloggers, and small business owners, MailerLite and ConvertKit stand out as popular, creator-friendly platforms. Both offer drag-and-drop editors, automation, and segmentation, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're migrating from another tool or starting fresh, focus on your subscriber count, automation complexity, and budget. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both platforms scale with subscribers, but their structures differ.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite shines with affordability. It offers a **free plan** for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—perfect for testing. Paid plans start at $10/month (billed annually) for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $20/month for 2,500 subs, $50 for 10,000, and so on. No contracts, cancel anytime. Add-ons like premium templates cost extra ($10/month).

**Practical tip**: If you're under 1,000 subs, stick to free and upgrade seamlessly. Annual billing saves 20-30%.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) starts with a **free plan** for up to 1,000 subscribers (limited broadcasts, no advanced automations). Paid Creator plan begins at $15/month for 300 subs, $29 for 1,000, $79 for 3,000, up to enterprise levels. Commerce features (subscriptions, one-click upsells) are included in higher tiers.

**Practical tip**: ConvertKit's pricing jumps faster for growing lists. Use the 14-day trial to test commerce tools if selling digital products.

**Winner**: MailerLite for budgets under 10k subs; ConvertKit for revenue-focused creators.

## User Interface and Ease of Use

Both have intuitive drag-and-drop builders, but MailerLite feels more beginner-friendly.

MailerLite's dashboard is clean, with quick access to campaigns, automations, and sites. The editor supports responsive designs without coding.

ConvertKit emphasizes sequences and tags, which can overwhelm new users but rewards with powerful segmentation. Its visual automation builder is flowchart-style, great for complex flows.

**Practical advice**: Spend 30 minutes building a sample newsletter in each. MailerLite suits quick setups; ConvertKit for tag-based personalization (e.g., "podcast listener" tags trigger custom emails).

## Feature Comparison

### Email Templates and Design

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, plus a drag-and-drop editor with JSON exports for custom HTML. High customization, A/B testing built-in.

ConvertKit: 100+ templates optimized for creators. Strong mobile previews and plain-text options for authenticity.

Both deliver 99%+ inbox rates, but test your domain's SPF/DKIM setup first.

### Automation and Workflows

MailerLite: Unlimited automations on all plans. Triggers like "subscribed to form" or "opened email." Multi-step workflows with delays and conditions.

ConvertKit: Excels here—visual automations with tags/segments. Sequences auto-send series; commerce automations handle payments.

**Practical tip**: Start simple: Welcome series (email 1: intro, email 2: freebie, email 3: upsell). ConvertKit edges for e-commerce; MailerLite for general use.

### Segmentation and Tagging

MailerLite: Groups, tags, custom fields. Dynamic segments based on behavior (e.g., clicked link).

ConvertKit: Tag-based system is superior for creators. Auto-tag on form submits, purchases. Re-engagement campaigns target "inactive" tags.

**Pro advice**: Segment by engagement: Top 20% openers get exclusive content. ConvertKit's tags make this effortless.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

MailerLite: Unlimited landing pages, popups, forms on paid plans. Website builder included (basic sites).

ConvertKit: Forms and landing pages optimized for lead gen. Native checkout pages for products.

**Tip**: Embed popups on exit-intent. MailerLite's A/B popup testing boosts conversions 20-30%.

### Analytics and Reporting

Both track opens, clicks, unsubscribes. MailerLite adds revenue tracking per campaign; ConvertKit shines in subscriber revenue attribution.

**Advice**: Review weekly reports. Aim for 30%+ open rates; tweak subjects if below.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics.

ConvertKit: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable, Patreon. Zapier too, plus Kit's commerce stack.

**Practical**: If on WordPress, both integrate seamlessly. Test Zapier for CRM syncs.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

MailerLite: Email/chat support (paid), extensive help docs, Facebook group. Excellent deliverability (uses Amazon SES).

ConvertKit: Priority email support, podcasts/tutorials. Top-tier deliverability with dedicated IPs on high plans.

Both GDPR/CCPA compliant. **Tip**: Warm up new domains with 50-100 daily sends.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite Pros and Cons

**Pros**:
- Cheaper/free tier
- All features unlocked early
- Great for non-profits/small biz

**Cons**:
- Less advanced commerce
- Templates less creator-focused

### ConvertKit Pros and Cons

**Pros**:
- Creator-centric automations
- Built-in selling tools
- Superior tagging

**Cons**:
- Higher cost
- Steeper learning curve

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs ConvertKit?

**Pick MailerLite if**:
- Budget < $20/mo
- Need landing pages/popups
- Simple automations suffice
- Solo blogger/small team

**Pick ConvertKit if**:
- Sell courses/memberships
- Heavy tagging/segmentation
- 1k+ engaged subs
- Creator economy focus

**Migration tip**: Export CSV from old tool, import subscribers. Clean lists first (remove inactives). Both offer import wizards.

## Conclusion

MailerLite wins for affordability and simplicity—ideal for startups testing email waters. ConvertKit dominates for serious creators monetizing audiences with tags and commerce.

Test both free/trial versions with your list. Track metrics for 30 days: opens, clicks, revenue. No tool is perfect; pair with strong content.

Ultimately, the "best" depends on your goals. For most under 5k subs, start with MailerLite. Scaling creators? ConvertKit scales revenue.
