# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate 2024 Comparison Guide

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. For creators, small businesses, and marketers, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both tools offer robust features for building audiences, sending newsletters, and automating campaigns, but they cater to slightly different needs.

MailerLite emphasizes affordability and simplicity, making it ideal for beginners and budget-conscious users. ConvertKit, on the other hand, targets content creators with advanced automation and monetization tools. In this in-depth **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more to help you decide.

## Pricing: Which Offers Better Value?

Pricing is often the first factor when pitting **MailerLite vs ConvertKit**. Both have free plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite's pricing is straightforward and competitive:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation and landing pages.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers (scales up).
- **Advanced**: $20/month for 500 subscribers, adds A/B testing and web push notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for large lists.

No contracts, and you can downgrade anytime. MailerLite also offers monthly or annual billing (20% discount on annual).

### ConvertKit Pricing (Now Kit)

ConvertKit rebranded aspects to Kit but retains core pricing:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (recently reduced from 1,000), unlimited emails, basic forms/landing pages.
- **Creator**: Starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, adds subscriber tagging and broadcasts.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month for 300 subscribers, unlocks advanced automations, segments, and commerce.
- Higher tiers scale by subscriber count, up to Enterprise.

Annual billing saves 20%. ConvertKit lacks a forever-free tier beyond basics, and costs rise quickly with growth.

**Practical Advice**: For startups under 1,000 subs, MailerLite's free plan is more generous. ConvertKit suits those willing to invest in creator-specific tools from day one. Calculate your projected list size: MailerLite is cheaper for lists under 10,000 subs.

## Features Head-to-Head

Both platforms excel in core email marketing, but differences shine in automation and extras.

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 80+ responsive templates. Customize blocks easily—no coding needed. ConvertKit's visual builder is block-based too, optimized for newsletters with clean, mobile-first designs. It shines for long-form content.

**Winner**: Tie for beginners; ConvertKit for creators needing visual storytelling.

### Automation and Workflows

- **MailerLite**: Trigger-based automations (welcome series, abandoned cart). Supports RSS-to-email and simple if/then logic. Advanced plan adds multi-step workflows.
- **ConvertKit**: Superior with "Sequences" (drip campaigns) and tagging system. Automate based on opens/clicks/tags. Pro plan enables visual automation builder.

**Practical Advice**: Use MailerLite for basic nurtures like welcome emails. Switch to ConvertKit if you sell digital products—its commerce automation handles upsells seamlessly.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

MailerLite bundles unlimited landing pages, popups, and forms (even on free plan). High customization with A/B testing on paid plans.

ConvertKit offers landing pages, opt-in forms, and link pages (great for product launches). Popups are basic; third-party integrations needed for advanced.

**Tip**: Test MailerLite popups for lead gen on blogs—conversion rates often hit 5-10% with exit-intent triggers.

### Analytics and Reporting

Both provide open/click rates, bounce handling, and unsubscribes. MailerLite adds revenue tracking per campaign. ConvertKit's reports focus on subscriber value and revenue per subscriber—creator gold.

### Integrations

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics.
ConvertKit: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable, Stronger for creator tools like Patreon.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly or Power User?

MailerLite wins for simplicity—setup in minutes, no steep curve. Dashboard is clean, tutorials abound.

ConvertKit has a learning curve for tagging/segments but pays off for complex audiences. Visual automations reduce errors.

**Practical Advice**: Start with MailerLite if you're new. Migrate to ConvertKit once you hit 5,000 subs and need segmentation. Import subscribers easily via CSV.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

### Support

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Fast responses (under 1 hour).
ConvertKit: Email/ticket only (no live chat), priority for Pro+. Extensive Creator Academy videos.

### Deliverability

Both boast 99%+ rates. MailerLite: Dedicated IPs on higher plans. ConvertKit: Strong inbox placement via authentication best practices.

**Tip**: Always warm up IPs, segment lists, and clean bounces to maintain 95%+ delivery.
