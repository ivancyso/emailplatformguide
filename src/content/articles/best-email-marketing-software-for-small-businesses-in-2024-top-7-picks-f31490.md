# Best Email Marketing Software for Small Businesses in 2024: Top 7 Picks

Choosing the right email marketing software is crucial for small businesses looking to nurture leads, boost sales, and build customer loyalty. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to pick the best one. In this roundup, we focus on the best email marketing software tailored for small businesses—platforms that offer ease of use, affordable pricing, and powerful features without unnecessary complexity. Our selections are based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, key features like automation and segmentation, and value for money as of 2024.

We'll cover what to look for, detailed reviews of our top 7 picks, and practical tips to help you decide.

## What to Look for in the Best Email Marketing Software

Before diving into the list, understand the essential criteria for small businesses:

### Key Features

- **Drag-and-Drop Editors**: Intuitive builders for creating professional emails without coding.
- **Automation Workflows**: Trigger emails based on user actions, like welcome series or abandoned cart reminders.
- **List Segmentation**: Divide contacts by behavior, location, or purchase history for targeted campaigns.
- **Integrations**: Connect with tools like Shopify, WordPress, or Google Analytics.
- **Analytics**: Track open rates, clicks, and ROI to refine strategies.
- **Deliverability**: High inbox placement rates to avoid spam folders.

### Pricing and Scalability

Small businesses need starter plans under $50/month with free tiers or trials. Look for pay-as-you-grow models based on contacts, not sends.

### Ease of Use and Support

Prioritize platforms with quick onboarding, templates, and 24/7 chat support.

## Top 7 Best Email Marketing Software for Small Businesses

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp remains a top choice for its free plan (up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month) and user-friendly interface. Ideal for beginners.

**Pros**:
- Extensive template library.
- Built-in CRM and landing pages.
- Strong automation for journeys.

**Cons**:
- Advanced features locked behind higher tiers.
- Pricing jumps after 2,000 contacts.

**Pricing**: Free; Essentials from $13/month (500 contacts).

**Best For**: Solopreneurs starting out. Practical tip: Use their audience dashboard to segment by engagement for 20-30% higher open rates.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

For growing small businesses, ActiveCampaign excels in advanced automation and CRM integration.

**Pros**:
- Deep personalization with conditional content.
- Site tracking for behavior-based emails.
- Excellent split testing.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve.
- No free plan beyond trial.

**Pricing**: Lite from $29/month (1,000 contacts).

**Best For**: Ecommerce stores. Tip: Set up "if/then" automations for cart recovery—users report up to 15% sales lift.

### 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Creator-focused but great for small service businesses with its visual automations.

**Pros**:
- Unlimited emails on all plans.
- Native landing pages and forms.
- Subscriber scoring.

**Cons**:
- Limited reporting compared to enterprise tools.

**Pricing**: Free (up to 300 subscribers); Creator from $29/month.

**Best For**: Bloggers and coaches. Advice: Leverage tagging for nurture sequences to convert 10x better than broadcasts.

### 4. Klaviyo

Ecommerce powerhouse with hyper-targeted flows.

**Pros**n- AI-powered recommendations.
- Deep Shopify/WooCommerce integration.
- Predictive analytics.

**Cons**:
- Geared toward stores; less for general use.
- Costs rise with revenue.

**Pricing**: Free (250 contacts); scales with sends/revenue.

**Best For**: Online shops. Tip: Install their tracking pixel for cross-device personalization, boosting revenue per recipient.

### 5. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels.

**Pros**:
- Conversion funnels.
- Autoresponders included.
- A/B testing on subject lines.

**Cons**:
- Interface feels dated.

**Pricing**: Email Marketing from $19/month (1,000 contacts).

**Best For**: Businesses needing webinars. Practical: Use ready-made funnels to launch campaigns in under an hour.

### 6. AWeber

Reliable veteran with strong deliverability.

**Pros**:
- Free lite version.
- Tag-based automation.
- 24/7 support.

**Cons**:
- Fewer modern integrations.

**Pricing**: Lite free; Pro from $19.99/month.

**Best For**: Non-profits. Tip: Their spam checker tool ensures 95%+ deliverability—test every campaign.

### 7. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Transactional + marketing emails at low cost.

**Pros**:
- Pay-per-send model.
- SMS and chat integration.
- Unlimited contacts on free plan.

**Cons**:
- Automation limits on basic plans.

**Pricing**: Free (300 emails/day); Starter $25/month.

**Best For**: Multi-channel. Advice: Combine email/SMS for 6x engagement rates.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing Software for Your Business

1. **Assess Your Needs**: Ecommerce? Go Klaviyo. Beginners? Mailchimp.
2. **Start with Trials**: Test 2-3 platforms for a week.
3. **Calculate Costs**: Factor contacts growth; avoid per-send traps.
4. **Check Integrations**: Ensure compatibility with your stack (e.g., Zapier for most).
5. **Prioritize Compliance**: GDPR/CAN-SPAM tools built-in.

Practical Advice:
- Clean lists regularly (remove inactives quarterly) to maintain sender reputation.
- Aim for 20-30% open rates initially; segment to improve.
- A/B test one variable per campaign (subject, send time).
- Use mobile-responsive templates—50%+ opens are mobile.
