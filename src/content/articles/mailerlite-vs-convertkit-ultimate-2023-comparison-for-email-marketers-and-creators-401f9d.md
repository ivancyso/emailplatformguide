# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. For bloggers, creators, and small business owners, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both platforms excel in automation and user-friendly designs, but they cater to slightly different needs.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, deliverability, support, and more. We'll provide practical advice to help you decide based on your list size, budget, and goals. Data is based on official sites and user reviews from G2 and Capterra as of late 2023.

## Pricing: Affordability vs Premium Value

Pricing is often the first hurdle. MailerLite shines for budget-conscious users, while ConvertKit targets creators willing to invest.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite offers a **free plan** for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month—perfect for starters. Paid plans start at $10/month (Growing Business, up to 500 subs) and scale to $20 for 1,000 subs. Features like automation and landing pages are included across plans. No contracts; monthly or annual billing with discounts.

**Practical tip**: If you're under 1,000 subs, start free and upgrade seamlessly. Annual plans save 30%.

### ConvertKit Pricing

No free plan, but a **14-day trial**. Creator plan starts at $9/month (up to 300 subs), Free plan (waitlisted) for basics. Creator scales to $29 for 1,000 subs; Creator Pro at $29+ adds advanced reporting and revenue optimizations. Annual billing saves 20%.

**Practical tip**: ConvertKit's pricing jumps at higher tiers (e.g., $79 for 4,000 subs), so calculate based on growth projections.

**Winner**: MailerLite for affordability, especially startups. ConvertKit if you need pro features early.

## Ease of Use: Intuitive Editors and Onboarding

Both are beginner-friendly, but interfaces differ.

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

Clean dashboard with a responsive drag-and-drop editor. Pre-built templates for newsletters, popups, and forms. Onboarding includes video tutorials and a setup wizard.

**Pro advice**: Use the A/B testing for subject lines—set it up in minutes to boost open rates by 20-30%.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Design

Visual automations builder (no code needed). Tag-based subscriber management feels natural for content creators. Editor is block-based, similar to WordPress Gutenberg.

**Pro advice**: Leverage the 'Sequences' for drip campaigns—ideal for course launches, with built-in checkout integrations.

**Winner**: Tie—MailerLite for sheer simplicity, ConvertKit for creators familiar with tagging.

## Core Features: Automation, Segmentation, and Integrations

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, HTML editor, RSS-to-email. ConvertKit: Commerce-focused templates, subscriber previews.

### Automation and Workflows

MailerLite: Unlimited automations with triggers like birthdays or cart abandonment. ConvertKit: Advanced sequences, visual editor, one-click upsells.

**Practical advice**: For e-commerce, ConvertKit's commerce tools (subscriptions, one-time sales) integrate with Gumroad/Stripe seamlessly. MailerLite suits service-based automations better.

### Segmentation and Tagging

MailerLite: Groups, segments by behavior/custom fields. ConvertKit: Tag-based (e.g., 'lead-magnet-downloader')—more flexible for personalization.

**Pro tip**: In ConvertKit, automate tag addition on form submit to segment without lists—reduces churn by targeting interests.

### Landing Pages and Forms

Both offer unlimited pages/forms. MailerLite includes popups and countdown timers. ConvertKit has digital product delivery.

### Integrations

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, WordPress, Shopify. ConvertKit: Native with Teachable, Patreon, strong creator ecosystem.

**Winner**: ConvertKit for creators selling products; MailerLite for general marketing.

## Deliverability and Analytics

Deliverability: Both score high (MailerLite ~99%, ConvertKit ~98% per user reports). Authentication (DKIM/SPF) is automatic.

Analytics: MailerLite tracks opens, clicks, revenue. ConvertKit adds subscriber value scoring and revenue per subscriber.

**Practical advice**: Monitor spam complaints—use both's suppression lists. Test sends to personal inboxes first.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive help center. ConvertKit: Email/ticket (business hours), creator-focused resources, podcast.

**Pro tip**: MailerLite's chat resolves issues in <5 mins; great for non-tech users.

**Winner**: MailerLite for responsive support.

## Pros and Cons Summary

### MailerLite Pros
- Free plan
- Cheaper scaling
- Popups and websites included

### MailerLite Cons
- Less advanced commerce
- Basic tagging

### ConvertKit Pros
- Creator tools (sequences, commerce)
- Tag automation
- Growth-focused analytics

### ConvertKit Cons
- Higher cost
- No free plan

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Bootstrapped bloggers/small businesses
- Lists under 5,000
- Need for popups/landing pages
- Budget priority

**Migration tip**: Import CSV easily; remap fields to groups.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Course creators/influencers
- Selling digital products
- Complex automations
- Willing to pay for premium

**Migration tip**: Export tags from old tools; use their importer for sequences.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

**MailerLite** wins for most users due to cost and simplicity—ideal if you're testing email marketing. **ConvertKit** edges out for serious creators monetizing audiences.

Test both: MailerLite's free plan and ConvertKit's trial. Track metrics like open rates (aim >25%) and growth.

**Actionable plan**:
1. Sign up for trials/free.
2. Import 100 subs, send a campaign.
3. Build one automation.
4. Compare costs for your projected list size.
