Mailchimp and Kit (ConvertKit) are two of the most popular email marketing platforms — but they serve different audiences. This comparison breaks down exactly where each excels and which one is right for you.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Mailchimp | Kit (ConvertKit) |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Best for** | Small businesses, ecommerce | Creators, bloggers |
| **Free plan** | 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/mo | 1,000 subscribers, unlimited emails |
| **Email templates** | 100+ | 9 |
| **Landing pages** | Yes (limited on free) | 50+ templates |
| **Automation** | Good | Excellent |
| **Subscriber model** | List-based | Tag-based |
| **Commerce** | Ecommerce integrations | Built-in digital sales |
| **Starting price** | $13/mo (500 contacts) | $29/mo (1,000 subs) |

## Interface and Ease of Use

**Mailchimp** has a polished interface with a lot of features visible upfront. This can feel overwhelming for beginners, but experienced marketers appreciate having everything accessible. The drag-and-drop email builder is one of the best in the industry.

**Kit** takes a minimalist approach. The interface is clean and focused, which makes it faster to learn (see our full [Kit review](/convertkit-review) for details). You can send your first email within minutes of signing up. The trade-off is fewer design options for individual emails.

**Winner:** Kit for beginners and simplicity. Mailchimp for design flexibility.

## Email Templates and Design

This is where the platforms diverge significantly.

**Mailchimp** offers 100+ professionally designed templates with extensive customization. You can create visually rich emails with multiple columns, images, and branded elements.

**Kit** offers only 9 basic templates. The philosophy is intentional — Kit believes text-based emails perform better for creators (and the data generally supports this). But if you need visually rich emails, Kit will disappoint.

**Winner:** Mailchimp, by a wide margin.

## Automation

**Kit's** visual automation builder is exceptional. Creating complex sequences with conditional branching, delays, and tag-based triggers is intuitive. For creators running launch sequences or course funnels, Kit's automation is hard to beat.

**Mailchimp** has improved its automation significantly but still feels more rigid. Customer Journey Builder works well for standard workflows but lacks the flexibility of Kit's approach.

**Winner:** Kit, especially for content-driven automation.

## Pricing Comparison

| Subscribers | Mailchimp (Standard) | Kit (Creator) |
|-------------|---------------------|---------------|
| 500 | $13/mo | Free |
| 1,000 | $26/mo | $29/mo |
| 2,500 | $46/mo | $49/mo |
| 5,000 | $75/mo | $79/mo |
| 10,000 | $110/mo | $119/mo |
| 25,000 | $230/mo | $199/mo |
| 50,000 | $350/mo | $379/mo |

At lower subscriber counts, Mailchimp is slightly cheaper (but Kit's free plan is more generous). At scale, the pricing is comparable, with Kit becoming more competitive above 25,000 subscribers.

**Winner:** Tie — depends on your subscriber count and plan needs.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Small businesses that need visually rich emails
- Ecommerce stores (strong Shopify/WooCommerce integrations)
- Teams that need multi-user collaboration
- Anyone who values email design flexibility

## Who Should Choose Kit?

- Bloggers, YouTubers, and content creators
- Anyone selling digital products (courses, ebooks)
- Newsletter operators focused on text content
- Users who want simple, powerful automation

## The Verdict

There's no universal winner here. **Mailchimp** is better for businesses that need visual emails and ecommerce integration. **Kit** is better for creators who want clean automation and a subscriber-centric approach.

If you're a creator or blogger, go with Kit. If you're running a small business or ecommerce store, Mailchimp is the safer choice.
