# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate 2024 Showdown for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your audience engagement and revenue. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for creators, bloggers, and small businesses. Both are popular for their simplicity and power, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer transparent, subscriber-based plans without hidden fees.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite stands out with its generous free plan: up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. No credit card required, making it ideal for testing.

Paid plans start at $10/month (billed annually) for up to 500 subscribers under the 'Growing Business' tier. It scales to $20/month for 1,000-2,500 subs, $50 for 10,000, and so on. Enterprise options are custom. All plans include unlimited emails (within fair use), automation, and websites/landing pages. Annual billing saves 30%.

**Practical Tip:** Start free to build your list, then upgrade seamlessly. Perfect if you're bootstrapping.

### ConvertKit Pricing (Now Kit)

ConvertKit dropped its free plan in recent updates but offers a 14-day free trial. The 'Creator' plan starts at $15/month (annual) for up to 300 subscribers, jumping to $29 for 500-1,000. 'Creator Pro' begins at $25/month for advanced features like subscriber scoring and commerce tools, scaling similarly.

No subscriber limits on sends once paid—unlimited emails. Annual discounts apply.

**Practical Tip:** Use the trial to import contacts and test sequences. Budget for growth, as costs rise quickly past 1,000 subs.

**Winner:** MailerLite for affordability, especially starters. ConvertKit if you need pro features early.

## Ease of Use and Interface

Both have intuitive dashboards, but differences shine in onboarding.

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

Clean, modern interface with a responsive editor. Beginners love the block-based builder—no coding needed. Quick setup: import CSV, design campaign in minutes.

Mobile app for managing on-the-go. A/B testing and RSS campaigns are one-click.

**Practical Advice:** Customize templates with your branding. Use the preview feature across devices to avoid formatting issues.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Focused Design

Visual automations via 'Sequences' and 'Visual Automations' builder. Tagging system simplifies segmentation over lists. Editor is straightforward but less visual than MailerLite's.

Strong for content creators with forms optimized for lead magnets.

**Practical Advice:** Set up tags like 'lead-magnet-download' early. Use the automation audit to optimize flows.

**Winner:** MailerLite for sheer simplicity; ConvertKit for automation pros.

## Core Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, drag-and-drop with JSON exports for devs. Advanced personalization (dynamic content blocks).

ConvertKit: Simpler builder, 100+ templates focused on newsletters. Strong plain-text option for authenticity.

### Automation and Workflows

MailerLite: Drag-and-drop automation builder with triggers like birthdays, abandons. Multi-step with delays, A/B paths.

ConvertKit: Excels here—Sequences for drip campaigns, Visual Automator for complex if/then logic. Tag-based triggers shine for creators (e.g., auto-tag podcast listeners).

**Tip:** In MailerLite, chain automations for welcome series. In ConvertKit, use subscriber segments for personalized broadcasts.

### Segmentation and Analytics

MailerLite: Groups, segments by behavior/activity. Detailed reports: opens, clicks, revenue tracking.

ConvertKit: Tag/subscriber score system over traditional segments. Robust analytics with revenue per subscriber.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

Both offer these. MailerLite: Unlimited websites/forms/popups on all plans, with A/B testing. ConvertKit: Forms and landing pages optimized for conversions, plus commerce (sell digital products directly).

### Integrations

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress, Google Analytics.

ConvertKit: Deep with creator tools (Teachable, Gumroad), Zapier, plus Kit Commerce.

**Practical Advice:** Test integrations pre-launch. For WooCommerce users, MailerLite has direct plugins; ConvertKit pairs well with MemberPress.

**Winner:** ConvertKit for creators selling courses; MailerLite for all-in-one websites.

## Deliverability and Support

Deliverability: Both score high (MailerLite ~99%, ConvertKit ~98% per industry benchmarks). Authentication (DKIM/SPF) is automatic.

Support:

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, extensive academy/videos. Free plan gets priority.

ConvertKit: Email support (faster on higher plans), huge knowledge base, creator community. Live chat on Pro.

**Tip:** Warm up your domain gradually—send to engaged lists first to boost reputation.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite

**Pros:** Free plan, cheap scaling, unlimited everything, great editor.

**Cons:** Fewer creator-specific tools, basic commerce.

### ConvertKit

**Pros:** Superior automations, tagging, monetization features.

**Cons:** Pricier, no free tier, steeper for non-creators.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Bootstrappers and small businesses under 5k subs.
- Need landing pages/popups on a budget.
- Prefer visual builders.

**Migration Tip:** Export from competitors via CSV; use their importer tool.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers, podcasters, course creators.
- Heavy automation users.
- Willing to invest in growth tools.

**Migration Tip:** Map old lists to tags; recreate sequences during trial.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

MailerLite wins for cost-conscious users wanting quick wins. ConvertKit edges out for serious creators prioritizing automations and sales.

Test both: MailerLite free, ConvertKit trial. Track metrics like open rates (aim 30%+), click-through (3%+), and unsubscribes (<0.5%).

Whichever you pick, focus on value-driven content. Build relationships, not lists. Your audience will thank you.
