# ConvertKit Review 2023: Pricing, Features & Is It Right for Creators?

If you're a blogger, podcaster, or content creator searching for an email marketing platform, you've likely come across ConvertKit. In this comprehensive ConvertKit review, we'll dive deep into its features, pricing, ease of use, and whether it's the best choice for your audience-building needs. ## What is ConvertKit?

ConvertKit, formerly known as ConvertKit, is an email marketing tool designed specifically for creators. Launched in 2013 by Nathan Barry, it emphasizes simplicity and powerful automation without the bloat of enterprise-level platforms. Unlike broad tools, ConvertKit prioritizes subscriber relationships through tagging, sequences, and commerce features.

Key differentiators include:
- **Creator Network**: Connect with other creators for cross-promotions.
- **Visual Automations**: Drag-and-drop builder for complex workflows.
- **Commerce**: Sell digital products directly via email.

It's not ideal for massive e-commerce stores but shines for solopreneurs and small teams growing newsletters or courses.

## ConvertKit Pricing: Transparent Tiers for Growth

ConvertKit uses subscriber-based pricing, which scales predictably. As of 2023:

- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers and 1,000 emails/month. Includes forms, landing pages, and basic automations. Perfect for testing.
- **Creator Plan**: Starts at $9/month (up to 300 subs), up to $29/month (1,000 subs). Unlimited emails, sends, and full features.
- **Creator Pro Plan**: From $29/month (300 subs) to higher tiers. Adds advanced reporting, one-click upsells, and subscriber scoring.

Practical tip: Start free to import contacts and build one automation. Upgrade when you hit limits—billing is monthly, with annual discounts (20% off).

No hidden fees, but third-party integrations (e.g., Zapier) may add costs. Compared to Mailchimp's free tier (2,000 subs but ads), ConvertKit's is ad-free and creator-focused.

## Key Features of ConvertKit

### Forms and Landing Pages

ConvertKit's forms are embeddable and customizable—no coding needed. Use inline, pop-up, or fixed-bar forms with incentives like lead magnets.

**Practical Advice**:
1. Create a form: Go to "Grow" > "Forms". Choose template, add fields (name/email), set trigger (e.g., exit-intent).
2. A/B test headlines: Duplicate forms and track conversions in reports.
3. Embed on WordPress: Copy-paste code; integrates seamlessly with most themes.

Landing pages are one-page sites for lead capture, with mobile optimization. Tip: Link from social bios for quick subscriber growth.

### Email Sequences and Broadcasts

- **Broadcasts**: One-off newsletters. Rich editor with images, buttons, and personalization ({First Name}).
- **Sequences**: Automated drip campaigns. Send 5-10 emails post-signup to nurture leads.

**Setup Tip**: Build a welcome sequence:
1. Subscribers > Automations > New Sequence.
2. Add emails with delays (e.g., Day 1: Intro; Day 3: Freebie).
3. Tag subscribers on open/click for segmentation.

Open rates average 40-50% for creators using personalization.

### Automations and Tagging

ConvertKit's visual automation builder is a standout—no linear limits like some tools.

**Pro Tip**: Use tags over segments. Tag for behavior (e.g., "opened-podcast-ep1"). Trigger actions like:
- If "purchased-course", send upsell.
- If inactive 30 days, re-engagement sequence.

Example Workflow:
1. New subscriber → Tag "new-lead" → Send welcome.
2. Clicks link → Tag "interested" → Advanced content.

Integrates with 100+ apps via native or Zapier.

### Commerce and Monetization

Sell ebooks, courses, or memberships directly. One-click upsells in Pro plan increase revenue by 20-30% per reports from users.

**Advice**: Set up a product: Commerce > New Product. Link in sequences. Track revenue in dashboard.

## Pros and Cons of ConvertKit

### Pros
- Intuitive for non-tech users.
- Unlimited sends/emails on paid plans.
- Strong creator community and support (email/chat).
- GDPR-compliant with easy unsubscribe.

### Cons
- No built-in drag-and-drop email templates (use editor).
- Limited CRM beyond email.
- Higher cost for large lists vs. budget options.

## Who Should Use ConvertKit?

Ideal for:
- Bloggers building newsletters (e.g., 1K-10K subs).
- Podcasters automating episode promos.
- Course creators selling digital products.

Not for: High-volume transactional emails or complex e-com.

**Migration Tip**: Export CSV from old tool (Mailchimp), import to ConvertKit. Map tags during setup to retain data.
