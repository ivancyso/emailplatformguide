# 10 Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Top Email Marketing Platforms Reviewed

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many businesses searching for alternatives. If you're facing high fees, poor deliverability, or lack of advanced features, it's time to explore options that offer better value, superior automation, and scalability.

In this roundup, we evaluate 10 proven Mailchimp alternatives based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, official pricing pages, and key features like automation, segmentation, and integrations. Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or agency, these tools can help you boost open rates and ROI without breaking the bank. We'll cover pros, cons, pricing, and practical tips for switching.

## Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but escalate quickly—up to $350/month for 50,000 contacts with advanced automation. Common pain points include:

- **Limited free tier**: No advanced automations or A/B testing.
- **Pricing opacity**: Billed by contacts, not sends.
- **Deliverability issues**: Some users report higher spam rates.
- **Steep learning curve for automations**.

Switching can save 30-50% on costs while unlocking better tools. Before migrating, export your Mailchimp list (CSV format), test deliverability with a small segment, and check integrations like Shopify or WordPress.

## Top Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign excels in advanced automation and CRM integration, making it ideal for marketing teams.

**Key Features**:
- Deep automation workflows (if/then branches, site tracking).
- Built-in CRM and machine learning for predictions.
- 900+ integrations (e.g., Shopify, Salesforce).
- Split testing and personalization.

**Pricing**: Free for 25 contacts; Lite starts at $29/month (1,000 contacts).

**Pros**: Powerful automations save time; excellent support.
**Cons**: Overwhelming for beginners; higher cost for large lists.

**Best For**: E-commerce and B2B with complex funnels. Tip: Use their 14-day trial to map your Mailchimp automations.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo offers transactional emails alongside marketing, with strong EU compliance.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts on free plan.
- SMS, chat, and WhatsApp multichannel.
- Automation builder and sales CRM.
- High deliverability (99% claimed).

**Pricing**: Free forever (300 emails/day); Starter at $25/month (20k emails).

**Pros**: Affordable scaling; easy drag-and-drop editor.
**Cons**: Limited templates; slower support on free.

**Best For**: SMBs needing SMS. Migrate by importing CSV and remapping automations—takes under an hour.

### 3. Klaviyo

Klaviyo is e-commerce focused, powering brands like Gymshark.

**Key Features**:
- Revenue tracking and product recommendations.
- Advanced segmentation (behavior-based).
- Flows for abandoned carts (95%+ open rates possible).
- Shopify/Native integrations.

**Pricing**: Free to $20/month (250 contacts); scales with revenue.

**Pros**: E-com gold standard; predictive analytics.
**Cons**: Marketing emails secondary to flows.

**Best For**: Online stores. Practical advice: Connect your store first, then import lists for personalized sends.

### 4. ConvertKit (now Kit)

Creator-friendly with simple automations.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automations and tagging.
- Landing pages and forms.
- Subscriber scoring.

**Pricing**: Free to 300 subscribers; Creator at $29/month.

**Pros**: Intuitive for non-tech users; great for newsletters.
**Cons**: Fewer enterprise features.

**Best For**: Bloggers/influencers. Tip: Use tagging to segment like Mailchimp lists.

### 5. MailerLite

Budget pick with strong visuals.

**Key Features**:
- Drag-and-drop editor, popups.
- Automation and A/B testing.
- Website builder.

**Pricing**: Free to 1,000 subscribers; Growing Business $10/month.

**Pros**: Cheap; high deliverability.
**Cons**: Basic reporting.

**Best For**: Startups. Export Mailchimp JSON for seamless import.

### 6. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars.

**Key Features**:
- Funnel builder, webinars.
- E-com tools, AI optimizer.

**Pricing**: Free to 500 contacts; Email Marketing $19/month.

**Pros**: Conversion funnels.
**Cons**: Clunky interface.

**Best For**: Lead gen. Test webinars post-migration.

### 7. AWeber

Veteran tool with reliability.

**Key Features**:
- Landing pages, tagging.
- Strong support.

**Pricing**: Lite $12.50/month (500 subscribers).

**Pros**: Proven deliverability.
**Cons**: Dated design.

**Best For**: Non-profits.

### 8. Constant Contact

User-friendly for beginners.

**Key Features**:
- Event management, social ads.

**Pricing**: $12/month (500 contacts).

**Pros**: Easy setup.
**Cons**: Expensive add-ons.

**Best For**: Local businesses.

### 9. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free CRM-integrated.

**Key Features**:
- Free tools with paid CRM.
- Personalization.

**Pricing**: Free; Marketing Hub $20/month.

**Pros**: No limits on sends.
**Cons**: Upsell-heavy.

**Best For**: Growing teams.

### 10. Moosend

Affordable automation powerhouse.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited emails, AI segmentation.

**Pricing**: Free to 1,000; Pro $9/month.

**Pros**: Value-packed.
**Cons**: Newer player.

**Best For**: Budget-conscious.

## How to Choose and Switch to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: Prioritize automation (ActiveCampaign), e-com (Klaviyo), or budget (MailerLite).
2. **Check Deliverability**: Use tools like Mail-Tester; aim for 9/10+ scores.
3. **Trial Run**: Import 10% of list, send test campaign.
4. **Migrate Data**: Use built-in importers; clean lists first (remove inactives).
5. **Train Team**: Most have academies—spend 2-4 hours.
6. **Monitor Metrics**: Track open rates (aim 20-30%), clicks (2-5%).

Expect 1-2 weeks for full switch. Cancel Mailchimp after verifying.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like ActiveCampaign, Brevo, and Klaviyo offer superior features and pricing for most users. For e-commerce, go Klaviyo; for automation pros, ActiveCampaign; for free scaling, Brevo or MailerLite. Start with a free trial, migrate strategically, and watch engagement soar. Which one fits your business? Test two today and compare results. Your inbox (and wallet) will thank you.
