# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your customer engagement and drive real revenue. For businesses evaluating options, the Omnisend vs Mailchimp debate is common. Both platforms offer robust features for email campaigns, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a long-standing favorite for general marketers with its user-friendly interface and free tier. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines in ecommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're a small business owner, ecommerce store manager, or marketing team lead, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. We'll focus on real-world use cases, avoiding hype and sticking to verifiable platform capabilities as of 2024.

## Pricing: Free Plans, Tiers, and Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email tool. Both Omnisend and Mailchimp offer free plans, but they differ in scale and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Paid plans start at $13/month for Essentials (up to 500 contacts, 5,000 sends), scaling to Premium at $350+/month for high-volume users. Key factors:
- **Contact-based billing**: Costs rise with audience size.
- **Send limits**: Tied to plan level; overages incur fees.
- **Add-ons**: SMS and automation extras cost more.

Practical advice: Ideal for startups testing waters. Monitor contact growth closely, as jumping tiers can double costs quickly. For example, 5,000 contacts on Standard plan runs about $75/month.

### Omnisend Pricing
Omnisend's free plan covers 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, with full automation access. Standard plan starts at $16/month (500 contacts), up to Pro at $59+/month. Unique perks:
- **No send limits** on most plans—just contact-based.
- **SMS included** in bundles without steep add-ons.
- **Ecommerce discounts**: Lower rates for Shopify users.

Practical tip: Ecommerce brands benefit from unlimited sends. A store with 2,500 contacts pays around $32/month on Standard, often cheaper than Mailchimp equivalents for transactional emails.

**Winner?** Omnisend for ecommerce scaling; Mailchimp for absolute beginners.

## Ease of Use: Interfaces and Onboarding

User experience matters for non-technical teams.

### Mailchimp's Drag-and-Drop Simplicity
Mailchimp's editor is intuitive, with pre-built templates and a classic dashboard. Onboarding includes tutorials and a mobile app. However, advanced automations require workflow building, which can feel clunky for complex journeys.

Pro tip: Use the content optimizer for A/B testing emails quickly. New users set up a campaign in under 10 minutes.

### Omnisend's Ecommerce-First Design
Omnisend emphasizes visual workflow builders for carts, welcomes, and post-purchase flows. The dashboard prioritizes revenue metrics. It's slightly steeper for pure email users but excels in omnichannel setup.

Advice: Connect your store (e.g., Shopify) first—auto-syncs data, saving hours. Drag-and-drop is responsive, but test SMS previews to avoid formatting issues.

**Verdict**: Mailchimp edges for beginners; Omnisend for store owners.

## Key Features Comparison

### Email Templates and Design
Both offer 100+ responsive templates. Mailchimp has more creative options; Omnisend focuses on product-focused blocks (dynamic product feeds).

Practical: Customize with custom HTML in both, but Omnisend's product sync auto-populates carts.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journeys for behavioral triggers. Basic abandoned cart via integrations.
- **Omnisend**: Superior pre-built workflows (e.g., 5-step cart recovery averaging 20-30% recovery rates in ecommerce benchmarks). Includes SMS/push for multi-touch.

Tip: In Omnisend, segment by behavior (browsed but no purchase) for hyper-targeted flows. Mailchimp suits simple drip campaigns.

### Segmentation and Analytics
Mailchimp uses tags/groups; Omnisend leverages predictive segmentation (e.g., likely buyers). Both track opens/clicks/revenue.

Advice: Omnisend's revenue per recipient metric guides optimization—aim for $0.50+ per email.

### Omnichannel Capabilities
Omnisend wins with native SMS, web push, and popups. Mailchimp adds SMS as paid extras.

## Integrations: Connecting Your Tech Stack

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Omnisend targets ecommerce: Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce (one-click), plus Klaviyo-like depth.

Practical advice: For Shopify users, Omnisend's native app installs in minutes, syncing orders/inventory. Mailchimp requires more setup for real-time data.

## Customer Support and Resources

Mailchimp: 24/7 email/chat on paid plans; extensive help center.
Omnisend: Priority chat/email; dedicated ecommerce guides and migration support.

Tip: Both have academies—complete Omnisend's for workflow certification to maximize ROI.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Omnisend if**: Running an online store (Shopify/Woo). Need SMS/cart recovery. Budget for growth-focused tools.
- **Pick Mailchimp if**: General newsletters, blogs, services. Starting free. Prefer all-in-one branding.

Test both: Mailchimp's free tier for broad appeal; Omnisend trial for store sync.

## Conclusion

Omnisend vs Mailchimp boils down to your business type. Omnisend dominates ecommerce with automation and omnichannel, often delivering higher ROI through recovery flows. Mailchimp remains versatile for diverse marketing. Evaluate based on contacts, channels, and goals—many switch after outgrowing free plans.
