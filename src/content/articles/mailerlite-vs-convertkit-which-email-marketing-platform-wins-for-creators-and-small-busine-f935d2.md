# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: In-Depth Comparison for 2023

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive sales. For creators, bloggers, and small business owners, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both tools offer powerful automation, beautiful templates, and subscriber management, but they cater to slightly different needs.

In this guide, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and customer support. Whether you're on a tight budget or need advanced creator tools, you'll get practical advice to decide. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms use tiered plans based on subscriber count, but their structures differ.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite shines with affordability:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, forms, and landing pages—perfect for testing.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 2,500. Unlimited emails.
- **Advanced**: From $21/month, adds A/B testing, web push notifications, and priority support.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume users.

No contracts, monthly billing, and a 30-day money-back guarantee. It's ideal for bootstrapped creators starting small.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit targets serious creators:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (recently expanded), with forms and basic broadcasts. No automation on free tier.
- **Creator**: Starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, up to $29 for 1,000. Includes sequences, tagging, and commerce.
- **Creator Pro**: From $25/month (500 subs), adds advanced automations, subscriber scoring, and visual automations.
- **Higher Tiers**: Scale to $1,579/month for 200k+ subs.

Annual discounts (20% off) and a 30-day trial. More expensive long-term, but packed with creator-specific value.

**Practical Tip**: If under 1,000 subs, MailerLite's free plan lets you grow without cost. ConvertKit suits those ready to invest in sequences from day one.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities

Both excel in email marketing essentials, but nuances matter.

### Email Builder and Templates

- **MailerLite**: Drag-and-drop editor with 80+ responsive templates. Classic and photo blocks for quick designs. Time-saving features like repeaters for product lists.
- **ConvertKit**: Visual builder with blocks for text, images, buttons. 100+ templates optimized for creators. Strong on personalization with merge tags.

**Winner for Beginners**: MailerLite—simpler interface.

### Automation and Sequences

- **MailerLite**: Workflow builder for triggers like sign-ups or purchases. Drip campaigns, tags, and segments. RSS-to-email for bloggers.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences (automated email series) are legendary. Tagging system for behavior-based automation. Visual automation builder in Pro plans.

**Practical Advice**: Use ConvertKit sequences for course launches (e.g., welcome series). MailerLite for simple abandoned cart flows.

### Landing Pages and Forms

Both offer popups, forms, and pages:
- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, popups, and surveys on paid plans. A/B testing included.
- **ConvertKit**: Native landing pages, opt-in forms, and link pages (great for product funnels). Integrates with checkout tools.

**Tip**: MailerLite for multi-page campaigns; ConvertKit for one-click upsells.

### Segmentation and Analytics

- **MailerLite**: Tags, groups, and custom fields. Click maps, open rates, revenue tracking.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tagging (actions auto-tag subscribers). Reports on opens, clicks, revenue per subscriber.

Segment lists by engagement to boost deliverability—do this weekly on either platform.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

MailerLite feels more intuitive for non-techies. Clean dashboard, quick setup (under 10 minutes). Extensive video tutorials and 24/7 live chat.

ConvertKit has a steeper curve due to tagging/sequences but excellent Creator Network resources (podcasts, courses). Email support, no live chat on basic plans.

**Pro Tip**: Start with MailerLite if you're new; migrate to ConvertKit as your list grows and needs complex funnels.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

- **MailerLite**: 1,000+ via Zapier. Native WordPress plugin, Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel. E-commerce: WooCommerce, Shopify.
- **ConvertKit**: Strong with creator tools—Gumroad, Teachable, Patreon. Zapier, plus commerce integrations for subscriptions.

Both handle Stripe/PayPal for payments. **Advice**: Check Zapier limits on free plans.

## Customer Support and Community

- **MailerLite**: Live chat (10-20 min response), email, knowledge base. High ratings (4.9/5 on G2).
- **ConvertKit**: Email/ticket support, help docs, free migrations. Creator-focused community forums.

MailerLite edges out for speed; ConvertKit for depth.

## Pros and Cons Summary

### MailerLite
**Pros**:
- Cheaper, generous free plan.
- Easy editor, unlimited everything on paid.
- Great for small teams.

**Cons**:
- Fewer creator-specific tools.
- Basic reporting vs. competitors.

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:
- Powerful sequences and tagging.
- Built for monetization (subscriptions, products).
- Excellent for bloggers/podcasters.

**Cons**:
- Higher cost, no unlimited emails on low tiers.
- Less beginner-friendly.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Budget-conscious, need free tier, simple automations, or high-volume emails affordably. Ideal for e-commerce stores or agencies.
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Creator selling digital products, need tagging/sequences, or value community resources. Best for course creators.

**Migration Tip**: Both offer free imports from other tools. Test with a small list first.

## Conclusion: Your Next Steps

**MailerLite vs ConvertKit** boils down to priorities—affordability and simplicity (MailerLite) or creator power (ConvertKit). For most starting out, begin with MailerLite's free plan to build habits, then scale to ConvertKit if sequences become key.

Sign up for trials today: [MailerLite](https://www.mailerlite.com) or [ConvertKit](https://convertkit.com). Focus on consistent sending (1-2/week) and value-driven content to grow your list 20-30% monthly.
