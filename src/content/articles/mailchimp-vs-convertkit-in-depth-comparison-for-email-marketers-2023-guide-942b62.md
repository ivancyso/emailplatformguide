# Mailchimp vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business. With Mailchimp and ConvertKit leading the pack, this head-to-head comparison breaks down everything you need to know. Whether you're a small business owner, blogger, or e-commerce seller, we'll cover pricing, features, automation, deliverability, and more to help you decide.

Both platforms excel in different areas: Mailchimp offers a robust free plan and beginner-friendly tools, while ConvertKit shines for creators with advanced subscriber management. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Comparison: Free Plans vs Scalable Subscriptions

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Here's a practical breakdown based on current plans (as of late 2023—always check official sites for updates).

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Includes basic templates, automation, and landing pages. Ideal for startups testing the waters.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/mo for 500 contacts (up to 50,000+ at higher tiers). Adds A/B testing and better segmentation.
- **Standard**: $20/mo base, includes advanced automation and behavioral targeting.
- **Premium**: $350+/mo for large lists with priority support.

**Practical Advice**: Start free if your list is under 500. Upgrade as you grow—billed monthly or annually for 15-20% savings. Watch for send limits; exceeding them pauses campaigns.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- No free plan, but a 14-day trial.
- **Creator**: $15/mo for up to 300 subscribers (unlimited emails). Scales to $29/mo for 1,000 subs.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/mo base, adds advanced automation, subscriber scoring, and integrations.

**Practical Advice**: ConvertKit's pricing is subscriber-based, not send-volume, perfect for frequent emailers like newsletters. Annual billing saves 20%. Export your list anytime—no lock-in.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for beginners (free tier); ConvertKit for unlimited sends without volume caps.

## Features Face-Off: Templates, Automation, and Integrations

Both offer drag-and-drop editors, but their strengths differ.

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's editor is intuitive with 100+ responsive templates. Customize with dynamic content blocks for products or events.

ConvertKit focuses on simplicity: Clean templates optimized for creators. Visual builder for sequences and broadcasts.

**Practical Tip**: Import your branding (logos, colors) in both. Test mobile previews—80% of emails are opened on phones.

### Automation and Workflows

#### Mailchimp Automations
- Customer journey builder: Trigger emails on sign-ups, purchases, or inactivity.
- Single, classic, and multi-step automations.

**Pro Tip**: Use tag-based triggers for abandoned carts. Integrate with Shopify or WooCommerce seamlessly.

#### ConvertKit Automations
- Tag-based subscriber journeys: Actions like "if subscribed to X, send Y."
- Visual automation builder in Pro plan.

**Pro Tip**: Excel for lead magnets—automate welcome series post-opt-in. Rules engine handles complex funnels without coding.

### Integrations

Mailchimp: 300+ including Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier.
ConvertKit: Strong with WordPress, Teachable, Gumroad—creator favorites. Zapier support too.

**Advice**: List your must-haves (e.g., CRM like HubSpot). Both handle Stripe for payments.

**Winner**: Tie—Mailchimp for e-commerce; ConvertKit for content creators.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's dashboard feels like a marketing suite—overwhelming at first but packed with tutorials.

ConvertKit is minimalist: Focus on emails without bloat. Onboarding emails guide you step-by-step.

### Support Options
- Mailchimp: 24/7 email/chat for paid plans; extensive help center.
- ConvertKit: Email support (response <24h), live chat for Pro, creator-focused resources.

**Practical Advice**: Use free trials. Mailchimp suits teams; ConvertKit solo users. Join communities: Mailchimp's Facebook group or ConvertKit's Facebook creator group for peer tips.

## Deliverability and Analytics

High deliverability keeps you out of spam folders.

Mailchimp: 99%+ rates, domain authentication tools.
ConvertKit: Creator-optimized (98-99%), dedicated IP option.

Analytics:
- Both track opens, clicks, bounces.
- Mailchimp: Revenue tracking, heatmaps.
- ConvertKit: Subscriber value, revenue per subscriber.

**Tip**: Warm up new domains gradually (start 50 sends/day). Authenticate SPF/DKIM in settings.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Beginners/small businesses.
- E-commerce stores needing transactional emails.
- Budget-conscious with <500 subs.

**Migration Tip**: Export CSV from old tool, import to Mailchimp. Clean list first (remove inactives >6mo).

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers/podcasters/newsletter creators.
- Those sending daily/weekly without limits.
- Advanced segmentation needs.

**Migration Tip**: Use tags over lists. Map old segments during import.

## Real-User Insights and Case Studies

From G2/Capterra (4.5+ stars both):
- Mailchimp: Praised for ease (4.4/5), criticized for pricing jumps.
- ConvertKit: Loved for support (4.7/5), some miss free plan.

Case: Pat Flynn switched to ConvertKit for podcast growth—doubled revenue via automations.

## Conclusion: Your Best Pick

**Mailchimp** if you're starting out, need free tools, or run an online store. It's versatile and scalable.

**ConvertKit** for creators prioritizing clean automations and unlimited sends.

Test both trials. Consider your list size, send frequency, and goals. Track ROI: Aim for 20-30% open rates initially.

Ultimately, the "best" is what fits your workflow. Switch if needed—data portability is easy. Start building your list today!
