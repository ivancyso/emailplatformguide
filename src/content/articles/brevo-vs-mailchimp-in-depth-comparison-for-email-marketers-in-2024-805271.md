# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp makes sense for many users. Both platforms offer robust features for email campaigns, automations, and audience management, but they cater to slightly different needs.

This Brevo vs Mailchimp comparison dives deep into pricing, features, usability, support, and real-world use cases. Whether you're a small business owner, freelancer, or marketing team lead, you'll get practical advice to decide which tool aligns with your goals. Let's break it down step by step.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing platform. Both Brevo and Mailchimp offer free plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo uses a contact-based pricing model with daily email limits on lower tiers. The free plan allows unlimited contacts but caps at 300 emails per day. Paid plans start at $25/month for 20,000 emails/month (Starter), scaling to $65/month for Pro with advanced automation and 20,000 emails. Enterprise plans are custom-priced.

Practical tip: If your volume is low and sporadic, Brevo's free plan is generous for daily sends without subscriber limits. Upgrade only when you hit the daily cap.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp charges based on contacts and features. The free Essentials plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Standard starts at $13/month for 500 contacts (unlimited sends post-send limits), and Premium at $350+/month for larger lists with advanced analytics.

Practical tip: Mailchimp's free tier suits beginners with small lists, but costs rise quickly as your audience grows. Calculate based on your contact count using their pricing calculator.

**Winner: Brevo** for high-volume senders on a budget; Mailchimp for unlimited sends on paid plans with smaller lists.

## Core Features: Email Campaigns, Automations, and More

Both tools excel in email marketing, but their feature sets vary.

### Email Builder and Templates
Brevo offers a drag-and-drop editor with responsive templates and AI-powered content suggestions. Mailchimp's editor is intuitive, with a vast library of industry-specific templates and creative assets.

H3: Practical Advice
Test both editors with a sample campaign. Brevo shines for transactional emails (e.g., order confirmations), while Mailchimp's is better for visually rich newsletters.

### Automations and Workflows
Brevo includes multi-channel automations (email, SMS, WhatsApp) from the free plan, with triggers like cart abandonment. Mailchimp's automations require Standard or higher, focusing on customer journeys with A/B testing.

H3: Practical Advice
For e-commerce, Brevo's SMS integration recovers more abandoned carts. Use Mailchimp if you need behavioral triggers like 'opened email X times.'

### CRM and Segmentation
Brevo has built-in CRM for lead tracking and advanced segmentation (e.g., by engagement score). Mailchimp offers audience tags and behavioral segmentation but lacks native CRM depth.

H3: Practical Advice
Segment lists weekly: Brevo for scoring leads automatically; Mailchimp for simple tags. Integrate with tools like Zapier for both.

### Additional Tools
Brevo: SMS, chat, landing pages, ads. Mailchimp: Social ads, postcards, ecommerce integrations (Shopify, etc.).

**Winner: Tie** – Brevo for omnichannel; Mailchimp for ecommerce depth.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp wins for beginners with its polished interface and extensive tutorials. Brevo's dashboard is clean but has a steeper learning curve for advanced features like API usage.

H3: Practical Advice
Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing. Switch to Brevo after gaining experience for cost savings. Both offer 14-day trials (Brevo) or free tiers to test.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

Brevo provides 24/7 email support on paid plans, phone for higher tiers, and a knowledge base. Mailchimp offers email support (priority on Premium) and chatbots.

Deliverability: Both maintain high rates (Brevo ~99%, Mailchimp ~98% per industry benchmarks), but warm up domains gradually.

H3: Practical Advice
Authenticate SPF/DKIM on signup. Monitor bounce rates; Brevo's transactional focus aids deliverability for sales emails.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros
- Affordable scaling
- Multi-channel (SMS/chat)
- Unlimited contacts free

Cons:
- Daily send limits
- Fewer templates

### Mailchimp Pros
- User-friendly
- Strong analytics
- Ecommerce focus

Cons:
- Expensive for large lists
- Limited free automations

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

**Choose Brevo if:** You're a growing business needing SMS/CRM integration on a budget, or send high volumes transactionally.

**Choose Mailchimp if:** You prioritize ease, creative designs, and ecommerce with moderate lists.

Practical decision framework:
1. List size <1k? Mailchimp free.
2. Need SMS? Brevo.
3. Budget < $30/mo high volume? Brevo.
4. Advanced reporting? Mailchimp Premium.

Test both: Export a CSV list and run a campaign.

## Conclusion: Make the Right Choice for Your Email Strategy

Brevo vs Mailchimp isn't a one-size-fits-all battle. Brevo edges out for affordability and versatility, ideal for scaling teams. Mailchimp remains king for intuitive, design-focused marketing.

Assess your needs: volume, channels, budget. Start with free plans, track metrics like open rates (aim >20%), and iterate. Whichever you pick, consistent testing and segmentation will boost ROI.
