# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses scaling their email marketing. Both are popular platforms with robust features, but they cater to slightly different needs. Brevo excels in multichannel marketing like SMS and WhatsApp, while Mailchimp is renowned for its user-friendly drag-and-drop editor and extensive templates.

In this comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and deliverability based on real user experiences and official specs. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business, or growing team, this guide helps you decide. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but limits differ significantly.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo's model is contact-based with daily email limits on free tiers:
- **Free Plan**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day. Ideal for low-volume senders.
- **Starter**: $25/month (300 emails/day, 20k/month total), removes Brevo branding.
- **Business**: $65/month (unlimited emails, advanced automation, A/B testing).
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume needs.

Brevo shines for transactional emails (unlimited on paid plans) and includes SMS/chat credits. No overage fees—great for unpredictable campaigns.

**Practical Advice**: If you send sporadic bursts (e.g., newsletters weekly), Brevo's daily cap fits without monthly overages. Calculate your needs: 300/day = ~9k/month free.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp uses audience size tiers:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month, 1 audience.
- **Essentials**: $13/month (5k contacts, 50k emails/month), basic automation.
- **Standard**: $20/month (5k contacts, 150k emails/month), dynamic content.
- **Premium**: $350+/month (high-volume, advanced reporting).

Costs rise quickly with contacts. Automation contacts count separately on lower plans.

**Practical Advice**: For growing lists (>10k contacts), Mailchimp gets pricey fast. Use their pricing calculator; switch if contacts exceed 10k without high engagement needs.

**Winner**: Brevo for unlimited contacts/free high-volume transactional; Mailchimp for small, steady lists under 5k.

## Core Features: Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 100+ mobile-responsive templates. Customization is beginner-friendly—no code needed.

Brevo's builder is block-based, similar but with more transactional focus. Fewer templates (~50), but strong for e-commerce (product blocks).

**Practical Tip**: Test both free editors. Mailchimp suits creative marketers; Brevo for sales-driven emails.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Brevo**: Visual builder for drip campaigns, abandoned cart, re-engagement. Multichannel (email + SMS). Wait nodes, splits, goals.
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journey Builder (paid plans). Single-step automations free; complex on Standard+.

Brevo edges out for omnichannel; integrate SMS for 20-30% higher open rates per industry benchmarks.

**Advice**: Map your funnel. For cart recovery + SMS, Brevo. For behavioral emails (page visits), both work—start simple.

### Landing Pages and Forms
Both offer builders:
- Brevo: Unlimited pages/forms on paid, pop-ups, A/B tests.
- Mailchimp: Forms free, pages on Standard+ (limited free).

**Tip**: Use Brevo for lead gen with SMS follow-up.

### SMS and Other Channels
Brevo includes SMS/WhatsApp (pay-per-use, ~$0.08/message). Mailchimp adds SMS on Premium ($350+).

**Pro Tip**: For US/EU audiences, SMS boosts engagement 6x over email (per Twilio data). Budget $50/month for 500 SMS.

### Integrations
- Mailchimp: 300+ (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics).
- Brevo: 250+ (similar, plus Zapier native).

Both solid; Mailchimp wins for creative tools (Canva-like).

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp feels like a creative suite—perfect for non-techies. Dashboard is colorful, tutorials abundant.

Brevo is straightforward, business-oriented. Steeper for multichannel but faster setup for transactional.

**User Feedback**: G2 rates Mailchimp 4.5/5 ease (simpler UI); Brevo 4.4/5 (better for pros).

**Advice**: Free trial both. Import CSV lists to test segmentation (Brevo auto-segments by engagement).

## Deliverability and Analytics

Both boast 95%+ rates:
- Brevo: Strong IP reputation, dedicated IPs on Enterprise.
- Mailchimp: Proven (billions sent), detailed reports.

Analytics: Mailchimp's heatmaps/track clicks; Brevo's revenue tracking.

**Tip**: Warm up domains gradually (50-100/day first week). Monitor bounce <2%, spam <0.3%.

## Customer Support

- **Mailchimp**: Email/chat (paid), vast knowledge base.
- **Brevo**: 24/7 chat/email (all plans), phone for Business+.

Brevo responds faster (avg 1hr vs Mailchimp's 24hr).

**Advice**: Prioritize live chat if solo.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros
- Unlimited contacts
- Multichannel
- Affordable scaling

Cons: Daily limits, fewer templates.

### Mailchimp Pros
- Intuitive editor
- Free resources
- Brand recognition

Cons: Expensive growth, limited free.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?
E-commerce stores, agencies needing SMS, high-volume transactional senders. Save 50%+ vs Mailchimp at 10k+ contacts.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
Content creators, nonprofits, beginners prioritizing design. Stick if under 5k contacts.

**Migration Tip**: Export CSV from one, import to other. Test sends first.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp? Brevo wins for cost-effective, multichannel growth (especially >5k contacts). Mailchimp for ease and creativity on small scales.

Assess your volume, channels, budget. Start free trials—most users switch within 14 days. Track ROI: aim 30-40x spend (e.g., $1k revenue/$25 platform).
