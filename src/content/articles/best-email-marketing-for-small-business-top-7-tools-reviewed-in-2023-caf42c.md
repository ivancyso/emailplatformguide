# Best Email Marketing for Small Business: Top 7 Tools in 2023

Email marketing remains one of the most effective ways for small businesses to nurture leads, drive sales, and build customer loyalty. With ROI often exceeding 40:1, it's a no-brainer. But choosing the **best email marketing for small business** can be overwhelming amid dozens of platforms. This roundup focuses on tools tailored for small teams—affordable, user-friendly, with essential features like automation, templates, and analytics.

We evaluated based on pricing (under $50/month for starters), ease of use, deliverability, integrations (e.g., Shopify, WordPress), and small business reviews from G2 and Capterra. No fluff—just practical picks for solopreneurs and teams under 10.

## Why Email Marketing is Essential for Small Businesses

Small businesses thrive on personal connections. Email lets you reach customers directly, bypassing algorithm changes on social media. Key benefits:
- **High open rates**: 20-30% vs. 1-5% on social.
- **Personalization**: Segment lists for targeted sends.
- **Automation**: Welcome series, abandoned cart emails save time.

Practical tip: Start with a clean list. Use double opt-in to comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM and boost engagement.

## Key Criteria for the Best Email Marketing Tools

Look for:
1. **Free/starter plans** for testing.
2. **Drag-and-drop builders**—no coding needed.
3. **Automation workflows** for birthdays, re-engagement.
4. **Mobile-responsive templates**.
5. **Analytics**: Open/click rates, A/B testing.
6. **Integrations**: CRM, e-commerce.
7. **Deliverability**: 95%+ to avoid spam folders.

Budget: Aim for $0-30/month initially.

## Top 7 Best Email Marketing Tools for Small Business

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp dominates with its free plan (up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month). Ideal for beginners.

**Key Features**:
- Intuitive drag-and-drop editor.
- 200+ templates.
- Basic automations (e.g., welcome emails).
- Audience insights and A/B testing.
- Integrates with 300+ apps like Google Analytics.

**Pricing**: Free; Essentials $13/month (500 contacts).

**Pros**:
- Free tier scales well.
- Excellent templates.

**Cons**:
- Advanced automations on higher plans ($20+).

**Practical Advice**: Use the free audience dashboard to segment by purchase history. Test subject lines—A/B can lift opens by 20%.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo shines with unlimited contacts on paid plans and transactional emails.

**Key Features**:
- Free plan: 300 emails/day.
- Automation workflows.
- SMS integration.
- Detailed reporting.
- Shopify/WooCommerce sync.

**Pricing**: Free; Starter $25/month (20k emails).

**Pros**:
- No contact limits.
- Multichannel (email + SMS).

**Cons**:
- Editor less polished than Mailchimp.

**Practical Advice**: Set up transactional emails for order confirmations—boosts trust. Use their CRM for lead tracking without extra cost.

### 3. Constant Contact

Beginner-focused with stellar support.

**Key Features**:
- 100+ mobile-optimized templates.
- Event management tools.
- Social media ads integration.
- List-building tools (pop-ups).

**Pricing**: $12/month (500 contacts).

**Pros**:
- 60-day free trial.
- Live chat support.

**Cons**:
- No free plan.
- Limited automations on basic.

**Practical Advice**: Leverage their AI subject line generator. Pair with pop-ups on your site to grow lists organically.

### 4. ConvertKit

Creator-friendly for newsletters and courses.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automations.
- Tagging/segmentation.
- Landing pages/forms.
- Subscriber scoring.

**Pricing**: Free (300 subs); Creator $29/month.

**Pros**:
- Simple for solopreneurs.
- Great for digital products.

**Cons**:
- E-commerce integrations extra.

**Practical Advice**: Use sequences for lead magnets. Tag based on behavior (e.g., 'clicked pricing') for personalized follows-ups.

### 5. ActiveCampaign

Powerful for growing businesses.

**Key Features**:
- Advanced automations (if/then logic).
- Site tracking.
- CRM built-in.
- Machine learning for send times.

**Pricing**: $29/month (500 contacts).

**Pros**:
- Deep personalization.
- High deliverability.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve.

**Practical Advice**: Build 'win-back' campaigns for inactive subs. Integrate with your site for behavior-based emails.

### 6. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars.

**Key Features**:
- Conversion funnels.
- Webinars (up to 100 attendees free).
- E-commerce tools.
- Autoresponders.

**Pricing**: Free (500 contacts); $19/month.

**Pros**:
- Bonus features like landing pages.

**Cons**:
- Interface feels dated.

**Practical Advice**: Use funnel builder for sales sequences. Host free webinars to warm leads.

### 7. AWeber

Reliable veteran with strong deliverability.

**Key Features**:
- 150+ templates.
- Smart send (optimal timing).
- E-commerce integrations.
- Tag-based automation.

**Pricing**: $12.50/month (500 subs).

**Pros**:
- Proven 99% deliverability.
- Free migration.

**Cons**:
- Fewer modern templates.

**Practical Advice**: Enable smart send to hit inboxes at peak times. Use tags for dynamic content blocks.

## How to Choose and Get Started

1. **Assess needs**: Newsletters? E-commerce? Pick accordingly (Mailchimp for simple, ActiveCampaign for complex).
2. **Test free trials**: Import a small list, send a campaign.
3. **Focus on compliance**: Add unsubscribe links, monitor bounce rates (<2%).
4. **Grow smart**: Aim for 1-2 sends/week. Personalize with first names.
5. **Measure success**: Track revenue per email (RPE). Tools like Google Analytics help.

Pro tip: Start with 3 segments—new, active, lapsed—for 30% better engagement.

## Conclusion

The **best email marketing for small business** balances affordability and power. Mailchimp or Brevo suit most starters; scale to ActiveCampaign as you grow.
