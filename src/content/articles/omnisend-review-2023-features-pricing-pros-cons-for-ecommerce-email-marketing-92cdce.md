# Omnisend Review 2023: Is It the Best Email Marketing Platform for Ecommerce?

If you're running an ecommerce store and looking to boost sales through email marketing, you've likely come across Omnisend. This all-in-one platform specializes in email, SMS, and push notifications tailored for online retailers. In this comprehensive Omnisend review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, ease of use, and whether it's worth your investment. Whether you're on Shopify, WooCommerce, or another platform, we'll provide practical advice to help you decide.

Omnisend stands out by focusing on ecommerce-specific needs like abandoned cart recovery, welcome series, and product recommendations. It's not a general-purpose tool like Mailchimp; it's built for revenue growth. Let's break it down.

## What is Omnisend and Who Should Use It?

Omnisend is an ecommerce marketing automation platform launched in 2014. It helps stores send targeted emails, SMS, and web push notifications to drive conversions. With over 70,000 users, it's popular among small to medium-sized online businesses.

**Ideal for:**
- Ecommerce owners on Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce.
- Brands with 1,000+ subscribers needing automation.
- Marketers wanting omnichannel campaigns (email + SMS).

**Not ideal for:** B2B lead gen or non-ecommerce newsletters.

Practical tip: Start with Omnisend's free plan to test integrations. Connect your store in minutes via native apps—no coding required.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs robust features without overwhelming beginners. Here's what you get:

### Email and SMS Automation Workflows

Omnisend's drag-and-drop workflow builder lets you create sequences like:
- **Abandoned cart emails:** Recover 10-30% of lost sales by sending reminders 1-24 hours after abandonment.
- **Welcome series:** Nurture new subscribers with 3-5 emails introducing your brand.
- **Post-purchase flows:** Upsell with thank-you emails and cross-sells.

**Practical advice:** Use pre-built templates but customize triggers (e.g., cart value > $50). A/B test subject lines—Omnisend reports average open rates of 25-40% for ecommerce automations.

### List Segmentation and Personalization

Segment contacts by purchase history, behavior, or custom tags. Dynamic product blocks pull real-time inventory.

**How to segment effectively:**
1. Go to Contacts > Segments.
2. Create rules like "VIP customers: spent > $100 last 30 days."
3. Send personalized recommendations—boost click rates by 20-50%.

### Popups, Forms, and Landing Pages

Capture leads with exit-intent popups, spin-to-win wheels, and signup forms. All no-code.

**Pro tip:** Place popups on high-traffic pages like homepage. Offer 10-15% discounts for emails—conversion rates often hit 5-10%.

### Omnichannel: SMS and Push Notifications

SMS open rates exceed 90%. Send promotional texts or cart reminders.

**Setup guide:**
- Verify your number (US/Canada focus, international expanding).
- Integrate with workflows (e.g., SMS after email if no open).

Push notifications work on mobile browsers—great for flash sales.

### Analytics and Reporting

Track revenue per email, ROI, and unsubscribe rates. Ecommerce tracking pixels measure actual sales.

**Advice:** Focus on "Attributed Revenue" dashboard. Aim for $42 ROI per $1 spent (Omnisend benchmark).

### Integrations

Seamless with Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Klaviyo alternatives, Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel. 140+ apps via Zapier.

## Omnisend Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

No contracts—monthly or annual billing (20% off annually).

| Plan | Price | Contacts | Key Features |
|------|-------|----------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | Up to 250 | 500 emails/mo, basic automations |
| Standard | $16/mo (annual) | Up to 500 | Unlimited emails, SMS, popups |
| Pro | $59/mo (annual) | Up to 500 | Advanced segmentation, A/B testing |

**Contacts scale pricing:** +$0.80/100 extra contacts on Standard.

**Practical choice:** Start free, upgrade to Standard for most stores (under 10k contacts). SMS costs ~$0.015/message—budget accordingly.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-focused: Deep product data sync.
- User-friendly interface: 4.7/5 on G2.
- High deliverability: 99% inbox rate.
- Generous free plan.
- 24/7 support on paid plans.

### Cons
- SMS limited to certain countries.
- No native phone support.
- Advanced reporting locked behind Pro.
- Learning curve for complex workflows.

Real user feedback (from G2/Capterra): "Doubled revenue from automations" but "SMS setup tricky for EU."

## How to Get Started with Omnisend: Step-by-Step

1. **Sign up:** Free account at omnisend.com.
2. **Connect store:** Auto-sync products/customers.
3. **Build audience:** Add signup forms/popups.
4. **Set automations:** Clone pre-builts (e.g., cart recovery).
5. **Launch campaign:** Design with 250+ templates.
6. **Analyze:** Weekly reviews of top performers.

**Tip:** Import existing lists carefully to avoid spam flags. Use double opt-in.

## Omnisend vs Competitors

- **Vs Mailchimp:** Omnisend better for ecommerce automations; Mailchimp cheaper for basics.

- **Vs ActiveCampaign:** Omnisend ecommerce-only; AC more CRM-focused.

Choose Omnisend if revenue tracking is key.

Omnisend earns its spot as a top email marketing tool for ecommerce. With powerful automations, omnichannel support, and ecommerce integrations, it delivers real ROI—users report 2-5x revenue lifts. The free plan and intuitive editor make it accessible, while scaling pricing suits growing stores.

**Final verdict:** 4.8/5. Perfect if you're ecommerce-focused and ready to automate. Sign up free today and set up your first cart recovery flow. For non-ecom, consider alternatives.
