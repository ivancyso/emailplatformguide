# MailerLite vs ConvertKit 2024: The Ultimate Head-to-Head Comparison

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business or content creation efforts. With so many options available, narrowing down to MailerLite vs ConvertKit is a common dilemma for bloggers, solopreneurs, and small teams. Both tools excel in user-friendliness and powerful automation, but they cater to slightly different audiences.

MailerLite is known for its affordability and simplicity, making it ideal for beginners and budget-conscious users. ConvertKit, on the other hand, targets creators with advanced tagging, sequences, and commerce features. In this detailed comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're just starting with email lists or scaling up, this guide provides practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities.

## Overview of MailerLite

MailerLite has built a reputation as one of the most affordable and intuitive email marketing tools. Launched in 2010, it serves over 2 million users worldwide. Key strengths include:

- Drag-and-drop email builder
- Automation workflows
- Landing pages and popups
- High deliverability rates

It's particularly popular among small businesses, bloggers, and e-commerce starters due to its free plan and low-cost upgrades.

### Who Should Use MailerLite?

- Beginners needing quick setup
- Budget-limited solopreneurs
- Users focused on newsletters and basic automations

## Overview of ConvertKit

ConvertKit, formerly known as ConvertKit, is creator-centric software designed for bloggers, podcasters, and online course sellers. It emphasizes subscriber tagging, visual automations, and monetization tools. Highlights include:

- Advanced tagging and segmentation
- Email sequences for nurturing
- Native commerce and subscriptions
- Strong integrations with creator tools

ConvertKit shines for those building audiences around content and products.

### Who Should Use ConvertKit?

- Content creators with growing lists
- Users selling digital products
- Teams needing sophisticated subscriber journeys

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is a major factor when pitting MailerLite vs ConvertKit. Both offer free plans, but scales differently as your list grows.

### MailerLite Pricing

- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes basic automation, forms, and landing pages.
- **Growing Business (Standard)**: Starts at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $270/month for 150,000+.
- **Advanced Plan**: From $21/month for 500 subscribers, adding A/B testing, web notifications, and priority support.

MailerLite bills monthly or annually (20% discount). No contracts, cancel anytime. Practical tip: Start free and upgrade seamlessly as you hit limits—no data loss.

### ConvertKit Pricing

- **Free Plan**: Up to 10,000 subscribers (recently expanded), with forms, landing pages, and basic automations.
- **Creator Plan**: $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, up to $2,500+/month for 200,000+.
- **Creator Pro Plan**: $29/month base, adding advanced reporting, one-click upsells, and subscriber scoring.

Annual billing saves 20%. ConvertKit's free tier is generous for testing, but paid plans ramp up faster for larger lists.

**Verdict**: MailerLite wins for sheer affordability, especially under 5,000 subscribers. ConvertKit justifies higher costs with creator-specific features.

## Ease of Use: Setup and Navigation

Both platforms prioritize simplicity, but differences emerge.

### MailerLite Interface

Clean, minimalist dashboard. Drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly with 80+ templates. Onboarding wizard guides new users in minutes. Mobile app for on-the-go management.

**Practical Advice**: Import contacts via CSV in under 5 minutes. Use the template library to launch your first campaign fast.

### ConvertKit Interface

Intuitive for creators, with a focus on 'sequences' and tags. Visual automation builder uses flowcharts. Slightly steeper learning curve for tagging but powerful once mastered.

**Practical Advice**: Tag subscribers by behavior (e.g., 'opened email X') during onboarding to segment early.

**Verdict**: MailerLite edges out for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for those comfortable with logic-based tools.

## Email Builder and Design Features

### MailerLite Email Editor

Responsive drag-and-drop with blocks for images, text, buttons. Photo editor built-in. Ensures mobile optimization automatically.

### ConvertKit Email Editor

Similar drag-and-drop, but excels in personalization with dynamic content blocks. Supports custom HTML for pros.

**Tip**: Test both editors with a sample newsletter. MailerLite for speed, ConvertKit for tailored experiences.

## Automation and Workflows

Automation is where both shine.

### MailerLite Automations

Workflow builder with triggers like sign-ups, opens, clicks. Includes RSS-to-email for bloggers. Unlimited steps in paid plans.

**Example**: Welcome series + abandoned cart recovery.

### ConvertKit Automations

Visual sequences for drip campaigns. Rule-based tagging (e.g., if/then logic). Integrates with Zapier deeply.

**Example**: Tag 'lead' → send sequence → offer product.

**Verdict**: ConvertKit for complex creator funnels; MailerLite for straightforward automations.

## Segmentation and Tagging

- **MailerLite**: Groups and segments by custom fields, behavior. Simple but effective.
- **ConvertKit**: Tag-based system is superior—auto-apply tags, purchase history tracking.

**Advice**: If audience <1k, MailerLite suffices. For nuanced journeys, ConvertKit's tags save time long-term.

## Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

Both offer these without extra cost.

- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, popups, forms. HTML customization.
- **ConvertKit**: Native pages, opt-in incentives, checkout pages in Pro.

**Tip**: Use MailerLite popups for exit-intent; ConvertKit incentives for high-converting forms.

## Integrations and E-commerce

- **MailerLite**: 250+ via Zapier, native WooCommerce, WordPress plugin.
- **ConvertKit**: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable, StrongDM. Commerce for subscriptions.

ConvertKit leads for creator stacks.

## Analytics and Reporting

- **MailerLite**: Open/click rates, bounce, unsubscribes. Heatmaps in Advanced.
- **ConvertKit**: Revenue tracking, subscriber value, A/B testing.

**Pro Tip**: Track ROI with UTM links in both.

## Customer Support

- **MailerLite**: Email, chat (paid), extensive help center, academy.
- **ConvertKit**: Email, chat, migration service, creator community.

Both responsive; ConvertKit offers live onboarding.

## Who Wins? Recommendations

- **Choose MailerLite if**: You're a beginner, on a tight budget, or need basic newsletters/popups. Perfect for small blogs/e-shops.
- **Choose ConvertKit if**: You're a creator selling courses/products, need deep segmentation, or plan to scale audiences.

**Migration Tip**: Both import CSVs easily. Test free plans side-by-side with your list.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your goals and budget. MailerLite offers unbeatable value for starters, while ConvertKit empowers creators with pro-level tools. In 2024, try both free tiers—export your data anytime.

For most small users, start with MailerLite and switch if tagging/sequences become bottlenecks. Whichever you pick, consistent sending and value-driven content will drive results.
