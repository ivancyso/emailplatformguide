# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can make or break your email marketing strategy. Both are popular platforms for small to medium businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this guide, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, deliverability, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling up, understanding these tools' strengths is key. We'll provide practical advice on testing both and migrating if needed.

## Pricing: Which Offers Better Value?

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms have free tiers, but their structures differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails per month. Paid plans start at $13/month for Essentials (up to 500 contacts, 5,000 emails). Higher tiers like Standard ($20+/month) add automation and A/B testing. Premium ($350+/month) is for enterprises with advanced reporting.

**Practical Tip:** If your list is under 500, start free. Watch for overage fees if you exceed sends—Mailchimp charges extra.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo shines with a free plan allowing unlimited contacts but only 300 emails/day. Paid plans begin at $25/month for 20,000 emails (Starter), scaling to $65/month for 20,000 emails with sales features (Business). No contact limits on paid plans, which is ideal for growing lists.

**Practical Tip:** Brevo suits high-volume senders on a budget. Calculate your monthly sends first—use their pricing calculator to avoid surprises.

**Winner:** Brevo for cost-effectiveness on larger lists; Mailchimp for beginners with small audiences.

## Features Breakdown

Both offer core email marketing tools, but let's dive deeper.

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop builder is intuitive, with hundreds of mobile-responsive templates. Customization is straightforward, even for non-designers.

Brevo's builder is similar but includes more transactional email options. Templates are professional, with strong e-commerce focus.

**Practical Advice:** Test both builders with a sample campaign. Mailchimp edges out for creative freedom; Brevo for quick setups.

### Automation and Workflows
Mailchimp's automation is robust in Standard+ plans: welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement. Classic Automations are user-friendly.

Brevo offers automation from free plan, including drip campaigns and behavioral triggers. It integrates SMS and WhatsApp for multichannel.

**Practical Tip:** Map your customer journey first. Use Brevo if multichannel is key; Mailchimp for e-commerce automations like Shopify integrations.

### Segmentation and Analytics
Mailchimp excels in behavioral segmentation (opens, clicks) and detailed reports with revenue tracking.

Brevo provides tag-based segmentation, real-time analytics, and heatmaps for engagement.

**Practical Advice:** Export your current list to test segmentation. Prioritize deliverability stats—both track bounces/SPF/DKIM setup.

### Integrations and E-commerce
Mailchimp integrates seamlessly with Shopify, WooCommerce, Google Analytics.

Brevo supports 300+ apps via Zapier, plus native CRM and SMS.

**Practical Tip:** List your tools (e.g., WordPress, Stripe). Check native vs. Zapier to minimize costs.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is polished and beginner-friendly, with extensive tutorials and 24/7 chat/email support on paid plans.

Brevo is clean but has a steeper learning curve for advanced features. Support includes live chat, email, and a knowledge base; phone for higher tiers.

**Practical Advice:** Sign up for free trials. Spend 30 minutes building a campaign in each. Mailchimp wins for non-tech users.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Deliverability matters—poor rates hurt ROI.

Mailchimp boasts high rates (95%+) due to its reputation and authentication tools.

Brevo also delivers well (around 95%), with strong IP warming and GDPR compliance.

**Practical Tip:** Verify domains early. Monitor blacklists with tools like MX Toolbox. Clean lists regularly to maintain rates.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros:**
- Intuitive interface
- Great templates and automations
- Strong e-commerce integrations

**Cons:**
- Expensive for large lists
- Free plan limits
- Recent pricing hikes

### Brevo
**Pros:**
- Unlimited contacts free
- Multichannel (email + SMS)
- Affordable scaling

**Cons:**
- Less polished UI
- Automation learning curve
- Transactional focus over creative

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

**Choose Mailchimp if:** You're a beginner, e-commerce store, or need polished designs. Ideal for lists under 5,000.

**Choose Brevo if:** You have growing lists, need SMS, or prioritize cost. Great for agencies or transactional emails.

**Practical Migration Advice:** Export CSV from current tool. Use built-in importers. Test sends to 10% of list first. Update DNS for authentication.

Run A/B tests on small segments post-switch to measure engagement.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your scale and needs. Mailchimp offers ease for starters; Brevo delivers value for growth. Start with free plans, trial paid features, and track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and clicks (2-5%).

For most SMBs, Brevo edges out on pricing without sacrificing features. Whichever you pick, focus on list hygiene and value-driven content for success.
