# 10 Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2023: Top Email Marketing Platforms for Every Need

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing shifts—from a forever-free plan to tiered paid models—and limitations in advanced automation have many users searching for alternatives. If you're dealing with rising costs, complex workflows, or niche-specific needs like e-commerce or creator audiences, it's time to explore better options.

In this roundup, we highlight 10 proven Mailchimp alternatives based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, focusing on features, pricing, scalability, and ease of migration. Whether you're a small business owner, blogger, or e-commerce store, these platforms offer practical improvements without the hype. We'll break down pros, cons, and who they're best for, plus tips to choose and switch.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's user base exploded thanks to its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and free tier, but changes in 2023 made it less appealing:

- **Pricing Overhaul**: The free plan now caps at 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month, with paid plans starting at $13/month for 500 contacts. Costs scale quickly for growing lists.
- **Feature Gaps**: Basic automations lack depth compared to competitors; no native CRM or advanced segmentation without add-ons.
- **Deliverability Concerns**: Some users report inbox placement issues as volumes grow.

Switching can save 20-50% on costs while unlocking better tools. According to EmailOctopus data, alternatives often deliver 10-30% higher open rates through smarter segmentation.

## Selection Criteria

We evaluated platforms on:
- **Pricing Transparency**: No hidden fees.
- **Core Features**: Automation, templates, A/B testing, analytics.
- **Ease of Use**: Onboarding and editor intuitiveness.
- **Integrations**: With Shopify, WordPress, etc.
- **Support**: 24/7 chat, resources.
- **Scalability**: For lists from 100 to 100k+.

## Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign shines for marketing teams needing CRM integration. It combines email with site tracking, lead scoring, and machine learning automations.

**Key Features**:
- 750+ integrations (Shopify, Salesforce).
- Conditional content and split automations.
- Built-in CRM for sales pipelines.

**Pricing**: Starts at $29/month (1,000 contacts, unlimited emails). Free trial.

**Pros**:
- Deep personalization boosts engagement.
- Excellent reporting dashboards.

**Cons**:
- Learning curve for beginners.

Best for: Mid-sized businesses. Users report 25% higher conversions vs. Mailchimp.

### 2. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Tailored for creators, bloggers, and course sellers. Focuses on simplicity with subscriber tagging over lists.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automation builder.
- Landing pages and forms included.
- Commerce tools for digital products.

**Pricing**: Free for 300 subscribers; paid from $29/month.

**Pros**:
- Creator-friendly tagging.
- One-click upsells.

**Cons**:
- Less robust for e-commerce.

Best for: Solopreneurs. Seamless WordPress integration.

### 3. Klaviyo

E-commerce powerhouse, especially for Shopify users. Excels in behavioral flows like abandoned cart emails.

**Key Features**:
- Predictive analytics.
- SMS integration.
- Revenue tracking per campaign.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts; scales with sends (~$20 for 500).

**Pros**:
- 10x ROI for stores via flows.
- Shopify one-click setup.

**Cons**:
- E-com focused; generalists may overpay.

Best for: Online stores.

### 4. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

All-in-one with transactional emails, SMS, chat, and ads. Strong EU compliance (GDPR).

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts on free plan.
- Automation workflows.
- Sales CRM.

**Pricing**: Free (300 emails/day); paid from €25/month.

**Pros**:
- Cost-effective scaling.
- Multi-channel.

**Cons**:
- Interface feels dated.

Best for: SMBs needing SMS.

### 5. MailerLite

Budget-friendly with drag-and-drop everything. Great for startups.

**Key Features**:
- Pop-ups, forms, websites.
- A/B testing free.
- API access.

**Pricing**: Free to 1,000 subs; $10/month after.

**Pros**:
- Simple migration.
- High deliverability (99%).

**Cons**:
- Basic automations.

Best for: Beginners.

### 6. GetResponse

Conversion-focused with webinars and funnels.

**Key Features**:
- Autoresponders, landing pages.
- E-com cart abandonment.

**Pricing**: From $19/month (1,000 contacts).

**Pros**:
- Webinar integration.

**Cons**:
- Support varies.

Best for: Lead gen.

### 7. AWeber

Veteran platform with reliable delivery.

**Key Features**:
- Tag-based automation.
- E-commerce tools.

**Pricing**: $19/month (500 subs).

**Pros**:
- 24/7 support.

**Cons**:
- Editor outdated.

Best for: Non-profits.

### 8. Constant Contact

User-friendly for events and local biz.

**Key Features**:
- Event management.
- Social ads.

**Pricing**: $12/month (500 contacts).

**Pros**:
- Phone support.

**Cons**:
- Limited automations.

Best for: Small retail.

### 9. Drip

Advanced for e-commerce with revenue experiments.

**Key Features**:
- Customer journey mapping.

**Pricing**: From $39/month.

**Pros**:
- Workflow intelligence.

Best for: DTC brands.

### 10. Omnisend

Omnichannel for Shopify/WooCommerce.

**Key Features**:
- SMS/push notifications.

**Pricing**: Free to 500 subs; $16/month.

**Pros**:
- Pre-built flows.

Best for: Multichannel retail.

## How to Choose and Migrate

### Practical Advice for Selection
1. **Assess Needs**: E-com? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Creators? ConvertKit.
2. **Test Free Trials**: Import a segment, run a campaign.
3. **Check Deliverability**: Use tools like Mail-Tester.
4. **Budget**: Factor sends vs. contacts.

### Migration Steps
1. Export CSV from Mailchimp (Audience > Export).
2. Sign up, import to new platform (most have wizards).
3. Recreate automations (start simple).
4. Update sign-up forms/embeds.
5. Notify subscribers of change.
6. Monitor bounces first 30 days.

Pro Tip: Use Zapier for seamless transfers. Expect 1-2 weeks for full switch.
