# Brevo vs Mailchimp: The Ultimate Comparison for 2024

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both platforms cater to businesses of all sizes, from solopreneurs to enterprises, offering automation, templates, and analytics. But which one fits your needs? This head-to-head breakdown covers pricing, features, usability, integrations, and more to help you decide.

We'll dive into practical advice based on real user scenarios, like starting a newsletter or scaling e-commerce campaigns. No hype—just facts to guide your choice.

## Pricing: Where Value Meets Your Budget

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both offer free plans, but they differ in limits and scaling.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. It's great for testing but restrictive for growth.

Paid tiers (billed monthly):
- Essentials: Starts at $13/mo for 500 contacts (up to 50,000 sends), scaling to $350+ for larger lists.
- Standard: $20/mo base, adds advanced automation and A/B testing.
- Premium: $350/mo base, for high-volume senders with priority support.

**Practical tip:** If your list exceeds 5,000 quickly, costs rise fast—calculate using their [pricing calculator](https://mailchimp.com/pricing/). Watch for contact-based billing; inactive subscribers still count.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts but caps at 300 emails per day (about 9,000/month). Ideal for low-volume users.

Paid plans (monthly, euros converted to USD approx. $):
- Starter: $25/mo for 20,000 emails/month, unlimited contacts.
- Business: $65/mo for 20,000 emails, adds automation and segmentation.
- Enterprise: Custom, with dedicated support and high volumes.

Brevo bills by emails sent, not contacts—cheaper for large lists with moderate sending.

**Practical tip:** For e-commerce with transactional emails (order confirmations), Brevo bundles them free in paid plans. Use their [pricing page](https://www.brevo.com/pricing/) to simulate your volume.

**Winner?** Brevo for cost-conscious teams with big lists; Mailchimp for unlimited sends on small lists.

## Features Face-Off: Automation, Templates, and Segmentation

Both excel in core email marketing, but nuances matter.

### Email Templates and Design

Mailchimp shines with 100+ drag-and-drop templates, mobile-responsive out-of-the-box. High customization via code for pros.

Brevo offers 40+ templates, simpler editor but integrates transactional emails seamlessly. Both support custom HTML.

**Advice:** Beginners pick Mailchimp's intuitive builder; developers prefer Brevo's flexibility.

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp's automation is robust: welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement. Customer Journey Builder visualizes complex flows.

Brevo matches with automation builder for drip campaigns, RSS-to-email, and SMS integration. Includes webhook triggers.

**Pro tip:** Test automations with small segments first. Mailchimp limits free automations; Brevo unlocks more in Business plan.

### List Segmentation and Analytics

Mailchimp: Tag-based segmentation, behavioral triggers. Reports cover opens, clicks, revenue tracking.

Brevo: Advanced segmentation by behavior, custom events. Analytics include heatmaps, deliverability scores.

**Practical advice:** For e-com, Brevo's product recommendations in emails give an edge. Track ROI with UTM parameters in both.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is polished, with tutorials everywhere. Setup takes minutes; AI content generator speeds copywriting.

Brevo is straightforward, especially for transactional + marketing emails. Dashboard feels modern, less cluttered.

**User scenario:** New to email? Mailchimp's guided tours win. Scaling marketers appreciate Brevo's clean lists.

## Integrations and Deliverability

### Integrations

Mailchimp: 300+ native (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics). Zapier expands it.

Brevo: 300+ including WooCommerce, Prestashop, SMS via Twilio. Strong CRM sync.

**Tip:** Check native support for your stack—Brevo edges e-com platforms.

### Deliverability

Both maintain 95%+ rates. Mailchimp's domain authentication is automatic; Brevo requires DKIM/SPF setup but offers tools.

**Advice:** Warm up domains gradually: start 50-100/day, monitor bounces. Use dedicated IPs on higher plans.

## Customer Support and Scalability

Mailchimp: Email/ticket support; chat on paid. 24/7 for Premium.

Brevo: 24/7 chat/email, phone for Business+. Community forums active.

**For agencies:** Brevo's white-label and sub-accounts scale better.

## Real-World Use Cases

- **Solopreneur/Blogger:** Mailchimp free plan + templates.
- **E-commerce Store:** Brevo for transactional emails + automation.
- **Agency:** Brevo's multi-account management.
- **High-Volume:** Compare send limits; Brevo cheaper long-term.

**Migration tip:** Both import CSVs easily. Export from one to the other via API/Zapier to test.

## Conclusion: Pick Your Champion

Brevo vs Mailchimp? **Brevo wins for affordability and transactional needs**, especially if emails > contacts. **Mailchimp excels in user-friendliness and design for beginners**.

Assess your volume, list size, and integrations. Start with free trials—Brevo 3 months free on Starter, Mailchimp forever free limited.

Ultimately, the best platform is the one you use consistently. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and iterate. Ready to choose? Sign up and A/B test campaigns today!
