# Omnisend Review 2023: Is It Right for Your Ecommerce Business?

If you're running an ecommerce store and looking to boost sales through email marketing, you've likely come across Omnisend. This platform specializes in multichannel marketing, focusing on email, SMS, and push notifications tailored for online retailers. In this comprehensive Omnisend review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance to help you decide if it's the best fit for your business.

Whether you're on Shopify, WooCommerce, or another platform, Omnisend promises to simplify customer journeys with automation. But does it deliver? Let's break it down step by step.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an all-in-one marketing automation platform designed specifically for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it has grown to serve over 70,000 stores worldwide. Unlike general email tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes omnichannel campaigns—combining email, SMS, web push, and popups into unified workflows.

Key differentiators include:
- Deep integrations with ecommerce platforms (Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce).
- Pre-built automation workflows for cart abandonment, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells.
- Advanced segmentation based on purchase behavior.

It's particularly popular among small to medium-sized ecommerce businesses aiming to scale without a huge marketing team.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs a punch with features that go beyond basic email sending. Here's a closer look:

### Email Automation and Workflows

Omnisend's drag-and-drop workflow builder is intuitive for beginners yet powerful for pros. You can create automations like:
- **Abandoned cart recovery**: Triggers emails or SMS when customers leave items in their cart. Practical tip: Customize with dynamic product blocks showing exact items abandoned—tests show recovery rates up to 20-30%.
- **Welcome series**: A sequence of 3-5 emails nurturing new subscribers. Advice: Personalize with first-name merge tags and segment by signup source (popup vs. purchase).
- **Win-back campaigns**: Re-engage inactive customers with incentives like discounts.

Pro tip: Use the "if/then" branching in workflows to send SMS only if email opens fail, maximizing reach.

### Omnichannel Messaging

Beyond email:
- **SMS marketing**: Send promotional texts or transactional alerts. Compliance is built-in with opt-in tracking.
- **Web push notifications**: For browser visitors, ideal for flash sales.
- **Popups and forms**: Customizable site popups for list growth, with A/B testing.

Practical advice: Start with email-to-SMS fallback in automations. For a store selling fashion, push notifications for new arrivals can drive 10x higher click rates than email alone.

### List Segmentation and Personalization

Segment contacts by behavior: purchased items, browsing history, or RFM (recency, frequency, monetary value). Omnisend pulls data directly from your store.

Example: Send "frequent buyers" exclusive previews, while "lapsed" get re-engagement offers.

Tip: Use dynamic content blocks to show personalized product recommendations—integrates with apps like Rebuy for upsells.

### Templates and Design

Over 250 responsive templates, editable with a drag-and-drop editor. Mobile-first design ensures high deliverability.

Advice: Always preview on desktop/mobile. Incorporate your brand colors and logo for trust—avoid stock images; use user-generated content for authenticity.

### Analytics and Reporting

Track open rates, clicks, revenue per email, and ROI. Detailed campaign reports show attributed sales.

Practical: Set up custom dashboards for key metrics like automation performance. Export data to Google Analytics for deeper insights.

## Omnisend Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

Omnisend offers a **free plan** for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month—perfect for testing.

Paid plans (billed monthly):
- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts) – All features.
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts) – Adds A/B testing, advanced reporting.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume.

Contacts scale affordably; SMS/push are pay-per-use (e.g., $0.01 per SMS in US).

No contracts, cancel anytime. Compared to Klaviyo (similar ecommerce focus), Omnisend is cheaper for starters.

Tip: Calculate costs based on your list size. Migrate from free Mailchimp tiers to save as you grow.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-centric: Seamless store integrations save hours.
- User-friendly interface: No coding needed.
- Strong automation ROI: Users report 2-5x revenue lifts.
- 24/7 support via chat/email.

### Cons
- Reporting lacks some advanced custom metrics (vs. Klaviyo).
- SMS costs add up for high-volume.
- Free plan limits scalability quickly.

Overall, pros outweigh cons for most ecommerce users.

## How to Get Started with Omnisend: Step-by-Step Guide

1. **Sign up and connect store**: Free account, one-click Shopify integration.
2. **Import contacts**: CSV upload or sync from store.
3. **Build first automation**: Use pre-built abandoned cart template; tweak triggers (e.g., 1-hour delay).
4. **Design campaigns**: Pick template, add products via merge tags.
5. **Test and launch**: Send test emails, monitor in real-time.

Practical advice: A/B test subject lines (e.g., "Forgot something?" vs. "Your cart awaits"). Aim for 20-30% open rates initially; optimize based on data.

Compliance tip: Enable double opt-in for GDPR/CAN-SPAM. Track suppression lists automatically.
