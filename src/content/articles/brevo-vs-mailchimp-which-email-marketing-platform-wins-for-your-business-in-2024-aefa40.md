# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can make or break your email marketing strategy. Both platforms cater to businesses of all sizes, from solopreneurs to enterprises, but they differ in pricing, features, and scalability. This guide breaks down the key differences with practical advice to help you decide based on your needs.

## Pricing: Where Value Meets Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing tool. Brevo and Mailchimp use different models, impacting costs as your list grows.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo offers a free plan with unlimited contacts and 300 emails per day—ideal for startups testing the waters. Paid plans start at €25/month (Starter) for 20,000 emails/month, scaling to €65/month (Business) for advanced automation and 20,000 emails. Enterprise plans are custom.

**Practical Advice:** If you send high-volume transactional emails (e.g., order confirmations), Brevo's pay-per-email model saves money. For a business sending 50,000 emails/month, expect around €65–€100, depending on features. Always calculate based on your projected sends using their pricing calculator.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan covers up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month. The Essentials plan starts at $13/month for 500 contacts (up to 50,000 sends), jumping to $20+ for higher tiers. Premium plans ($350+/month) unlock multivariate testing and unlimited sends.

**Practical Advice:** Mailchimp suits low-volume senders with small lists. For growing lists (e.g., 5,000 contacts), costs rise quickly to $75+/month. Factor in overage fees if you exceed sends—budget 20% extra for scaling.

**Winner:** Brevo for cost-effectiveness on high-volume campaigns; Mailchimp for capped, predictable pricing on small lists.

## Ease of Use and Interface

Both platforms prioritize user-friendliness, but their drag-and-drop editors differ.

Brevo's editor is modern and intuitive, with a clean dashboard integrating email, SMS, chat, and CRM. Setup takes minutes, and automation workflows are visual.

Mailchimp's interface is legendary for beginners—polished templates and a simple signup form builder. However, advanced users may find navigation cluttered as features expand.

**Practical Advice:** Start with Brevo if you're multitasking (email + SMS). Test Mailchimp's free editor for creative campaigns. Import your list via CSV in both; Brevo handles larger imports faster without timeouts.

## Core Features Comparison

### Email Templates and Design

Brevo provides 40+ responsive templates, customizable with HTML access. Mailchimp boasts 100+ industry-specific templates with more creative blocks.

**Tip:** Use Brevo for transactional-heavy designs; Mailchimp for visually rich newsletters.

### Automation and Workflows

Brevo excels in multi-channel automation (email + SMS triggers). Mailchimp's automations are robust for e-commerce (abandoned cart) but segment-limited on lower plans.

**Practical Advice:** For drip campaigns, Brevo's unlimited workflows on paid plans win. Set up a welcome series: Brevo integrates CRM data seamlessly; Mailchimp uses tags effectively.

### List Management and Segmentation

Brevo offers unlimited contacts on free plans with dynamic segments based on behavior. Mailchimp segments by groups/tags, but free limits hinder advanced use.

**Tip:** Clean lists weekly—both have built-in tools. Brevo's CRM auto-enriches contacts.

### Integrations

Mailchimp leads with 300+ native integrations (Shopify, WordPress). Brevo supports 300+ via Zapier, plus native WhatsApp and Facebook leads.

**Practical Advice:** E-commerce users pick Mailchimp for Shopify sync. Agencies love Brevo's all-in-one (no extra tools needed).

## Deliverability and Analytics

Deliverability rates hover around 95% for both, per industry benchmarks. Mailchimp's established IP reputation edges it for cold lists; Brevo shines for warm transactional sends.

Analytics: Brevo tracks opens, clicks, and revenue per email. Mailchimp adds comparative reporting and A/B testing on all plans.

**Practical Advice:** Warm up domains gradually (start 100 sends/day). Use Brevo's heatmaps for mobile optimization; Mailchimp's reports for ROI tracking.

## Customer Support and Resources

Brevo offers email/ticket support (24/7 on higher plans) and a knowledge base. Mailchimp provides 24/7 email, priority phone on Premium, and extensive tutorials.

**Tip:** Leverage communities—Mailchimp's forum is larger. For quick fixes, Brevo's chat widget responds faster.

## Scalability for Growing Businesses

Brevo scales seamlessly for high-volume (millions of emails) without contact limits. Mailchimp caps sends by tier, requiring upgrades.

**Practical Advice:** Solopreneurs: Mailchimp free tier. SMBs (1k–10k contacts): Brevo Starter. Enterprises: Brevo custom for SMS/email bundles.

## Real-World Use Cases

- **E-commerce Store:** Mailchimp for abandoned cart automations and product recommendations.
- **Agency/Service Business:** Brevo for client CRM and multi-channel.
- **High-Volume Newsletter:** Brevo to avoid per-contact fees.

Test both free plans: Export campaigns to compare metrics after 30 days.

## Conclusion: Which One Should You Choose?

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your priorities. Choose **Brevo** if you need affordable scaling, transactional emails, and multi-channel marketing—perfect for budget-conscious growth. Opt for **Mailchimp** if ease, templates, and ecosystem integrations are key, especially for creative or e-commerce beginners.

No one-size-fits-all: Audit your list size, send volume, and features needed. Start free, migrate data easily (both support CSV/JSON), and track KPIs like open rates (aim 20–30%). In 2024, Brevo's momentum makes it a strong challenger, but Mailchimp's polish endures.

Ready to decide? Sign up for both and A/B test your next campaign. Your inbox ROI awaits.
