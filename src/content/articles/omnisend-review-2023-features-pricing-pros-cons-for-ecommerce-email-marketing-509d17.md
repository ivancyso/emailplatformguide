# Omnisend Review: Is It the Best Email Marketing Tool for Your Ecommerce Store?

If you're running an ecommerce business and looking to boost sales through email marketing, you've likely come across Omnisend. This platform specializes in multichannel marketing, focusing on email, SMS, and push notifications tailored for online stores. In this comprehensive Omnisend review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, ease of use, and whether it's the right fit for your needs. Whether you're on Shopify, WooCommerce, or another platform, we'll provide practical advice to help you decide.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an all-in-one marketing automation platform designed primarily for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it helps store owners create targeted campaigns, automate customer journeys, and recover abandoned carts without needing coding skills. It integrates seamlessly with major ecommerce platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, and Magento.

Unlike general email tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes ecommerce-specific features such as product recommendations, order notifications, and pre-built workflows. It's used by over 70,000 brands worldwide, including MVMT and Kate Spade, proving its scalability for small to mid-sized businesses.

**Practical Tip:** Start by connecting your store via the Omnisend dashboard. The setup wizard imports customer data and products automatically, saving hours of manual work.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend shines with its ecommerce-focused toolkit. Here's a breakdown of standout features:

### Email and SMS Campaigns

Create drag-and-drop emails with dynamic content blocks for products, categories, or personalized recommendations. The SMS feature allows opt-in compliant messaging for time-sensitive promotions like flash sales.

**Practical Advice:** Use A/B testing for subject lines and send times. Omnisend's editor lets you segment by purchase history—e.g., target 'recent buyers' with upsell offers. Aim for 20-30% open rates initially; refine based on analytics.

### Automation Workflows

Pre-built workflows cover welcome series, abandoned cart recovery, win-back campaigns, and post-purchase upsells. Customize triggers like 'viewed product but didn't buy' or 'birthday.'

**Practical Tip:** For abandoned carts, set a 1-hour delay for the first email, then SMS after 24 hours. Include urgency with countdown timers. Users report 15-30% recovery rates with this sequence.

### Popups and Forms

On-site popups, spin-to-win wheels, and signup forms capture leads. These integrate with your email lists and trigger automations instantly.

**Pro Tip:** Place exit-intent popups on high-traffic pages. Offer 10-15% discounts for emails, but track conversion impact to avoid list fatigue.

### Web Push Notifications

Send browser notifications for back-in-stock alerts or price drops. Opt-in rates are high since they're less intrusive than email.

### Analytics and Reporting

Track revenue per recipient, ROI, and campaign performance. Ecommerce tracking shows attributed sales from each channel.

**Advice:** Review the 'Customer Insights' dashboard weekly. Identify top segments (e.g., VIP customers spending >$100) for exclusive campaigns.

## Omnisend Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

Omnisend offers a free plan for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month—perfect for testing. Paid plans are contact-based:

- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts) – Includes automations, SMS (pay-per-use), basic reporting.
- **Pro:** $59/month (500 contacts) – Adds advanced segmentation, A/B testing, unlimited data retention.
- **Custom:** For 50k+ contacts, with dedicated support.

SMS credits are extra (~$1 per 100 messages in the US). No contracts; monthly billing with annual discounts.

**Practical Comparison:** Cheaper than Klaviyo for small lists but scales similarly. Calculate costs: If you have 1,000 contacts, Pro plan is ~$116/month.

| Standard | 500+ | $16+ | SMS, popups |
| Pro | 500+ | $59+ | Advanced analytics |

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-centric: Deep integrations and product feeds.
- User-friendly interface: No steep learning curve.
- High deliverability: 99%+ inbox rates reported.
- Multichannel: Email + SMS + push in one dashboard.
- 24/7 support via chat/email.

### Cons
- SMS costs add up for high-volume senders.
- Limited to ecommerce; not ideal for B2B newsletters.
- Free plan lacks advanced automations.
- No native landing pages (use integrations like Unbounce).

**User Feedback:** On G2 and Capterra, it scores 4.6/5 from 1,000+ reviews, praised for ease and ROI.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for DTC ecommerce brands with 500-50,000 customers. If you're on Shopify and need quick wins like cart recovery, it's a top choice. Avoid if you need complex B2B drip campaigns—consider ActiveCampaign instead.

**Setup Guide:**
1. Sign up and connect your store.
2. Import segments (all contacts, buyers, etc.).
3. Build a welcome automation.
4. Launch a popup.
5. Monitor revenue attribution.

Expect 2-4x ROI within months with consistent use.

## Omnisend vs. Competitors

- **vs. Klaviyo:** Omnisend is cheaper for starters; Klaviyo better for massive lists.
- **vs. Mailchimp:** More automation depth; Mailchimp wins for non-ecom.
- **vs. ActiveCampaign:** Simpler for ecommerce; ActiveCampaign for advanced CRM.

For most DTC brands, the investment pays off quickly through increased sales.
