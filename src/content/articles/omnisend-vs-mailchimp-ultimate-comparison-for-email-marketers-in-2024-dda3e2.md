# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools, but they cater to slightly different needs. Omnisend shines in ecommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications, while Mailchimp offers broad appeal for beginners and general marketers with its intuitive interface. This comparison breaks down pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a key factor for most users. Both platforms offer free plans, but their structures differ based on contacts and features.

### Omnisend Pricing

Omnisend provides a generous free plan for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails per month. Paid plans start at $16/month for the Standard plan (up to 500 contacts), scaling to $59/month for Pro (up to 500 contacts). Pricing is contact-based and includes unlimited emails in most plans. SMS credits are extra, which is practical for ecommerce stores testing multichannel campaigns. As your list grows, costs remain predictable without overage fees.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails monthly but limits automation. Essentials starts at $13/month (500 contacts), Standard at $20/month, and Premium at $350/month for advanced features. It's send-volume based in free tiers but switches to contact-based for paid. High-volume senders may find it expensive, especially with audience tiers adding costs for engaged segments.

**Practical Advice:** If you're an ecommerce business with under 2,500 contacts, Omnisend's free plan offers more value with full automation access. Mailchimp suits solopreneurs on a tight budget starting small.

## Features Breakdown

Both platforms excel in core email marketing, but differences emerge in automation and integrations.

### Email Automation and Workflows

Omnisend's automation is ecommerce-centric, with pre-built workflows for abandoned carts, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells. You can trigger emails based on customer behavior like browsing history or product views. Multichannel support (email + SMS + web push) allows unified campaigns.

Mailchimp offers robust automations like customer journeys and behavioral triggers, but they're more general-purpose. Classic automations are being phased out for the newer Customer Journey builder, which requires higher plans.

**Practical Tip:** Use Omnisend for cart recovery—set up a workflow that sends an email reminder 1 hour after abandonment, followed by SMS if no action. In Mailchimp, test tag-based automations for lead nurturing.

### Templates and Design

Mailchimp has a vast library of drag-and-drop templates, mobile-responsive out of the box, ideal for non-designers. Customization is straightforward with content blocks.

Omnisend's editor is also drag-and-drop, with ecommerce-focused templates like product carousels and dynamic blocks that pull real inventory data. It supports AMP for interactive emails.

### Integrations and Ecommerce Focus

Omnisend integrates seamlessly with Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce—syncing orders and customer data automatically. It also connects to Klaviyo alternatives via Zapier.

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps, including Google Analytics, Facebook, and WordPress. Its ecommerce features require plugins or higher plans.

**Pro Tip:** For Shopify users, Omnisend's native app installs in minutes and auto-populates segments. Mailchimp works but needs more setup for transaction data.

### Analytics and Reporting

Omnisend provides detailed revenue tracking, attributing sales to specific campaigns with ROAS metrics. Segment insights show customer lifetime value.

Mailchimp's reports cover opens, clicks, and revenue but are less granular for ecommerce attribution without Premium.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp wins for beginners with its simple dashboard and tutorials. Setup takes minutes—import contacts, pick a template, send.

Omnisend has a steeper learning curve due to advanced features but offers guided onboarding and templates. The interface is clean, with a focus on workflows.

**Advice for New Users:** Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing. Switch to Omnisend once you need omnichannel or deeper ecommerce insights. Both have 14-day trials.

## Customer Support

Omnisend offers 24/7 chat and email support on paid plans, with dedicated managers for Enterprise. Knowledge base is solid for self-help.

Mailchimp provides email support (chat for higher plans), extensive help docs, and a community forum. Response times can vary.

**Tip:** Test support pre-purchase—ask about specific integrations. Omnisend's live chat is responsive for technical queries.

## Scalability and Limitations

Omnisend scales well for growing ecommerce stores, handling high-volume sends without deliverability issues (strong IP reputation). No contact limits on emails.

Mailchimp handles millions of sends but pricing jumps sharply past 10k contacts. Deliverability is good but watch for engagement thresholds.

**Practical Consideration:** Monitor your open rates. Both penalize low engagement, but Omnisend's segmentation tools help maintain lists cleaner.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend?

- Ecommerce brands (Shopify, WooCommerce users)
- Businesses wanting SMS/push alongside email
- Teams focused on revenue-driven automation
- Mid-sized lists needing cost-effective scaling

Example: A dropshipping store uses Omnisend to recover 15-20% of abandoned carts via automated sequences, boosting revenue without ad spend.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Beginners or content creators
- Non-ecommerce businesses (blogs, services)
- Budget-conscious startups
- Users needing broad integrations

Example: A blogger segments subscribers by interests and sends personalized newsletters, leveraging Mailchimp's audience insights.

## Migration Tips

Switching? Export CSVs from one and import to the other. Omnisend has a Mailchimp importer tool. Test campaigns on small segments first to check deliverability. Rebuild automations gradually.

**Best Practice:** Audit your list for engagement before migrating—clean bounces and inactives to avoid blacklists.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce marketers with superior automation, multichannel support, and pricing transparency. Mailchimp remains the go-to for ease and versatility, especially for non-store owners.

Ultimately, test both free plans with your audience. If revenue tracking and cart recovery are priorities, go Omnisend. For simple newsletters and quick starts, Mailchimp delivers.

Your choice depends on business type—ecommerce leans Omnisend, general marketing favors Mailchimp. Whichever you pick, focus on compliant list-building (double opt-ins) and consistent sending for best results.
