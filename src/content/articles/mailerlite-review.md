MailerLite has quietly become one of the most popular email marketing platforms, and for good reason: it offers an impressive feature set at prices that undercut nearly every competitor. But is affordable good enough?

After extensive testing, here's our honest take.

## What Is MailerLite?

MailerLite is an email marketing platform that focuses on simplicity and value. Founded in 2010, it's grown to over 1 million users by offering a clean interface, competitive pricing, and features that rival platforms costing 3-4x more.

## Key Features

### Email Editor
MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is one of the cleanest in the industry. It's intuitive enough for beginners but flexible enough for experienced marketers. You can build emails from scratch or use templates, and the editor supports rich text, images, buttons, video, and countdown timers.

### Automation
MailerLite offers visual automation workflows with triggers, conditions, and actions. On the free plan, you get basic automation (which most competitors lock behind paid plans). The paid plans add advanced features like multivariate automation and auto-resend.

Common automation workflows:
- Welcome sequences for new subscribers
- Abandoned cart recovery
- Re-engagement campaigns
- Birthday/anniversary emails
- RSS-to-email automation

### Website Builder
A unique value-add: MailerLite includes a website builder at no extra cost. It's not a full WordPress replacement, but it's sufficient for simple marketing sites, portfolios, and blogs.

### Landing Pages
The landing page builder includes a solid template library and a drag-and-drop editor. Pages are hosted on MailerLite's domain (free) or your custom domain (paid). The templates are modern and conversion-optimized.

### Subscriber Management
MailerLite uses a hybrid approach: subscribers can be organized into groups (similar to lists) and tagged for finer segmentation. This gives you flexibility without the complexity of a fully tag-based system.

## Deliverability

MailerLite's deliverability rates are consistently above average. They use:
- SPF and DKIM authentication
- Dedicated IP options for high-volume senders
- Built-in list cleaning tools
- Automatic bounce and complaint handling

In our testing, inbox placement rates were comparable to [AWeber](/aweber-review) and Mailchimp -- both known for strong deliverability.

## The Approval Process

One notable quirk: MailerLite has a manual approval process for new accounts. When you sign up, your account is reviewed before you can send campaigns. This can take a few hours to a day. It's mildly annoying but serves a purpose — it keeps spammers out, which helps everyone's deliverability.

## Who Should Use MailerLite?

**Great for:**
- Budget-conscious businesses and solopreneurs
- Bloggers who want email + website in one tool
- Anyone [switching from Mailchimp](/mailchimp-alternatives) to save money
- Small businesses under 10,000 subscribers -- see our [best email marketing for small business](/best-email-marketing-small-business) guide
- Users who want automation on a free plan

**Not ideal for:**
- Large enterprises needing advanced CRM
- Businesses requiring phone support
- Users who need 700+ templates (AWeber)
- Advanced automation power users (ActiveCampaign)

## The Verdict

MailerLite delivers the best value in email marketing. The feature set you get at $9-18/month would cost $30-50+ on competing platforms. The free plan is genuinely useful (not just a teaser), and the interface is a pleasure to use.

The trade-offs are real but minor: fewer templates than Mailchimp, less powerful automation than ActiveCampaign, and no built-in CRM. For 90% of small businesses and creators, these limitations won't matter.

If budget matters to you — and it should — MailerLite is the best choice in 2026. It also tops our list of the [best free email marketing tools](/best-free-email-marketing).
