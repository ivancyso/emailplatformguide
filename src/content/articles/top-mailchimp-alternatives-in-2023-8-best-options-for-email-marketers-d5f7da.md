# Top Mailchimp Alternatives in 2023: 8 Best Options for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing hikes and feature limitations have many users searching for alternatives. If you're dealing with bloated costs, rigid plans, or need more advanced automation, it's time to explore options. This roundup covers eight proven Mailchimp alternatives, focusing on real features, pricing (as of late 2023), and practical use cases for small businesses, e-commerce stores, and agencies.

We'll break down pros, cons, and who each suits best, with tips to migrate smoothly. All data is based on official sites and user reviews from G2 and Capterra.

## Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but scale quickly—up to $350+/month for 50k contacts with automations. Common pain points include:

- **High costs for growing lists**: Essentials plan lacks robust automation.
- **Limited segmentation**: Struggles with complex customer journeys.
- **Deliverability issues**: Some users report higher spam rates.

Switching can save 30-50% on costs while unlocking better tools. Before diving in, audit your needs: list size, automation complexity, integrations (e.g., Shopify, WordPress), and budget.

## 1. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
- Advanced automation with 750+ pre-built workflows.
- Deep CRM integration for sales pipelines.
- Site tracking and predictive sending.

### Pricing
- Lite: $29/month (500 contacts).
- Plus: $49/month (unlimited sends).
- Scales to $259/month for 50k contacts.

### Pros
- Exceptional automation—build if/then branches easily.
- High deliverability (95%+ reported).
- Great for e-commerce with abandoned cart flows.

### Cons
- Steeper learning curve for beginners.
- No free plan beyond 14-day trial.

**Best for**: Agencies and e-com stores needing CRM + email. Migrate by exporting CSV from Mailchimp and importing segments.

## 2. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

### Key Features
- Creator-focused: Visual automations, landing pages, forms.
- Tagging over lists for precise segmentation.
- Native commerce for selling digital products.

### Pricing
- Free up to 300 subscribers.
- Creator: $29/month (500 subs).
- Scales affordably to $79/month for 10k.

### Pros
- Intuitive for solopreneurs/bloggers.
- Unlimited emails on all plans.
- Strong analytics on opens/clicks.

### Cons
- Less robust for large enterprises.
- Fewer templates than Mailchimp.

**Best for**: Content creators and course sellers. Practical tip: Use their "Sequences" for nurture series—set up in 10 minutes vs. Mailchimp's clunky builder.

## 3. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
- SMS + email + chat in one dashboard.
- Transactional emails free up to 300/day.
- Automation with triggers like cart abandonment.

### Pricing
- Free: 300 emails/day.
- Starter: $25/month (20k emails).
- Business: $65/month (unlimited contacts).

### Pros
- Cost-effective for high-volume sends.
- EU-based servers for GDPR compliance.
- Excellent API for developers.

### Cons
- Interface feels dated.
- Support slower on free plan.

**Best for**: SMBs with transactional needs. Tip: Integrate with Zapier for 1-click Mailchimp migration.

## 4. Klaviyo

### Key Features
- E-commerce powerhouse: Revenue tracking, flow builder.
- AI-powered segmentation and A/B testing.
- Shopify/WooCommerce native apps.

### Pricing
- Free up to 250 contacts.
- Scales by usage: ~$20/month for 500, $100+ for 10k.

### Pros
- Best-in-class for Shopify stores (30%+ revenue lift reported).
- Predictive analytics for send times.

### Cons
- E-com focused; weaker for B2B.
- Pricing unpredictable for spikes.

**Best for**: Online stores. Advice: Start with their flow templates—win-back campaigns recover 10-15% lost revenue.

## 5. GetResponse

### Key Features
- All-in-one: Email, webinars, landing pages, funnels.
- Autoresponders and conversion funnels.
- AI email generator.

### Pricing
- Starter: $19/month (1k contacts).
- Pro: $49/month (unlimited).

### Pros
- Webinar integration saves tool stacking.
- High deliverability (99% uptime).

### Cons
- Overwhelming for email-only users.

**Best for**: Marketers running events. Tip: Use funnel builder for lead magnets—boost conversions 20%.

## 6. AWeber

### Key Features
- Drag-and-drop editor, RSS-to-email.
- E-commerce tools like order notifications.
- Strong tagging system.

### Pricing
- Lite: $19/month (500 subs).
- Unlimited: $149/month (scalable).

### Pros
- Reliable since 1998; 99% deliverability.
- Free migration service.

### Cons
- Dated design.
- No native CRM.

**Best for**: Non-profits/small biz. Practical: Schedule RSS campaigns for consistent blogging.

## 7. Constant Contact

### Key Features
- Event management, social ads.
- AI subject line optimizer.
- Mobile app for on-the-go edits.

### Pricing
- Lite: $12/month (500 contacts).
- Premium: $80/month.

### Pros
- Beginner-friendly templates.
- US-based support.

### Cons
- Expensive for large lists.

**Best for**: Local businesses. Tip: Link events to emails for 25% attendance boost.

## 8. Omnisend

### Key Features
- Omnichannel: Email, SMS, push.
- Product pickups and popups.
- Deep Shopify integration.

### Pricing
- Free up to 250 contacts.
- Standard: $16/month.

### Pros
- SMS boosts open rates 6x.
- Automation recipes ready-to-use.

### Cons
- SMS costs extra.

**Best for**: E-com with multi-channel. Advice: Test popups—capture 10% more leads.

## How to Choose and Switch to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: List size >10k? Prioritize unlimited sends (e.g., Brevo).
2. **Test Free Trials**: Spend 1 week building a sample campaign.
3. **Check Integrations**: Ensure Zapier/ native apps for your stack.
4. **Migrate Data**: Export audiences from Mailchimp (CSV), import, and remap tags. Use tools like PieSync for sync.
5. **Monitor Deliverability**: Warm up new domains gradually.

Budget tip: Most offer 14-30 day trials—run A/B tests on your top 2.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like ActiveCampaign and Klaviyo offer superior automation and value for growing businesses. For creators, ConvertKit shines; e-com picks Omnisend or Brevo. Evaluate based on your audience size and goals—switching typically takes 1-2 weeks and pays off in ROI.
