[MailerLite](/mailerlite-review) and [Kit (ConvertKit)](/convertkit-review) are two of the most popular email marketing platforms for creators and small businesses. Both offer free plans, solid automation, and a clean user experience. But they serve slightly different audiences and make different trade-offs.

Here is how they stack up.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | MailerLite | Kit (ConvertKit) |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| Free plan | 1,000 subs, 12K emails/mo | 1,000 subs |
| Starting price | $9/mo | $29/mo |
| Email templates | Many (drag-and-drop) | Few (9 templates) |
| Automation | Good (visual builder) | Excellent (visual builder) |
| Landing pages | Yes (built-in) | Yes (50+ templates) |
| Website builder | Yes | No |
| Commerce | No | Yes (digital products) |
| Subscriber model | List-based | Tag-based |
| Creator Network | No | Yes |

## Pricing

This is the biggest difference. MailerLite is dramatically cheaper.

| Subscribers | MailerLite | Kit (ConvertKit) |
|-------------|-----------|------------------|
| 1,000 | Free / $9 | Free / $29 |
| 2,500 | $15/mo | $49/mo |
| 5,000 | $29/mo | $79/mo |
| 10,000 | $47/mo | $119/mo |
| 25,000 | $139/mo | $199/mo |

At every tier, MailerLite costs 30-60% less than Kit. For budget-conscious creators, this difference is significant.

## Email Design

**MailerLite wins.** Its drag-and-drop editor is one of the best in the industry — intuitive, flexible, and packed with content blocks. You get many professionally designed templates.

Kit takes a minimalist approach with only 9 email templates. The philosophy is intentional: plain-text-style emails convert better for creators. But if you want visually rich newsletters, MailerLite gives you far more options.

## Automation

**Kit wins** — but MailerLite is closer than you might expect.

Kit's visual automation builder is best-in-class for creators. Tag-based subscriber management makes it easy to build complex sequences based on behavior: "If subscriber clicked link X but did not buy product Y, send sequence Z."

MailerLite's automation builder is visual and capable, but the list-based model is less flexible for complex tagging workflows. For straightforward automations (welcome series, product launch sequences), MailerLite handles them well.

## Commerce

Kit lets you sell digital products directly — ebooks, courses, memberships, paid newsletters. No need for Gumroad or Shopify. This is a major advantage for creators who monetize through digital products.

MailerLite does not have built-in commerce. You would need to integrate with an external platform.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- **Budget-conscious creators** who want professional email marketing at the lowest possible price
- **Small businesses** that need landing pages, a website builder, and email in one tool
- **Visual email designers** who want extensive template options and a powerful editor
- **Anyone under 1,000 subscribers** — MailerLite's free plan includes automation, which Kit's free plan does not

See our [full MailerLite review](/mailerlite-review) for more details.

## Who Should Choose Kit?

- **Creators selling digital products** who want built-in commerce without extra tools
- **Bloggers and YouTubers** who need sophisticated tag-based segmentation
- **Automation power users** who build complex conditional sequences
- **Creators who value community** — the Creator Network is unique to Kit

See our [full Kit review](/convertkit-review) for more details.

## The Verdict

**Choose MailerLite** if price matters and you want the best features-to-cost ratio. It does 80% of what Kit does at half the price.

**Choose Kit** if you are a creator who sells digital products and needs sophisticated automation with tag-based segmentation. The higher price buys you tools that MailerLite does not offer.
