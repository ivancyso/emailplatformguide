# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: The Ultimate Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's customer engagement and revenue. With so many options available, pitting Omnisend against Mailchimp—the two heavyweights in the space—helps narrow it down. This comparison dives into their features, pricing, usability, and more, tailored for businesses deciding between them.

Whether you're a small e-commerce store or scaling a larger operation, we'll break down real-world differences based on official documentation and user experiences. No hype, just facts to guide your choice.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer tiered plans, but they cater to different scales.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends—ideal for startups testing the waters. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for enterprise features like advanced automation and unlimited sends.

Key tiers:
- **Essentials**: $13–$350/month (email-focused, basic automation).
- **Standard**: $20–$700/month (A/B testing, behavioral targeting).
- **Premium**: $350+/month (multivariate testing, 24/7 support).

Mailchimp charges based on contacts, not sends in higher tiers, which suits high-volume senders.

### Omnisend Pricing
Omnisend skips a robust free tier (limited to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month) but shines for e-commerce. Paid plans start at $16/month (Standard) for 500 contacts and unlimited emails.

Key tiers:
- **Standard**: $16–$59/month (automation, SMS add-on).
- **Pro**: $59–$222/month (advanced segmentation, A/B testing).
- **Enterprise**: Custom (API access, dedicated support).

Omnisend's unlimited emails in paid plans make it cost-effective for frequent campaigns. Add-ons like SMS ($0.02–$0.015/message) or web push enhance multichannel marketing.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your monthly sends and contact list size. Use Mailchimp's free plan for bootstrapping; switch to Omnisend if e-commerce drives 70%+ of your revenue to avoid per-send fees creeping up.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities

Both excel in email, but Omnisend leans multichannel, while Mailchimp is a broader marketing suite.

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp offers a drag-and-drop builder with 100+ responsive templates, customizable for any industry. It's beginner-friendly with content blocks for images, products, and RSS feeds.

Omnisend's builder is e-commerce optimized, with dynamic product blocks pulling live inventory from Shopify or WooCommerce. Templates focus on carts, orders, and upsells—over 250 pre-built workflows.

**Tip**: Test templates with your branding. Mailchimp suits service-based businesses; Omnisend auto-populates product data, saving hours for online stores.

### Automation and Workflows
Mailchimp's automation includes single and multi-step journeys like welcome series or re-engagement. Customer Journey Builder (Premium) visualizes complex paths.

Omnisend dominates with 200+ pre-built automations for abandoned carts (recovering 15–30% of lost sales per industry benchmarks), post-purchase upsells, and cross-sells. Omnichannel support includes SMS and push notifications triggered by behavior.

**Practical Advice**: Audit your funnel. If cart abandonment plagues you, Omnisend's workflows deploy faster. For nurture sequences, Mailchimp's simplicity wins.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp segments by tags, groups, or behavior (e.g., opened last campaign). Merge fields enable basic personalization.

Omnisend uses predictive segmentation (e.g., "likely to buy") and deep e-commerce data like purchase history. Hyper-personalization inserts ordered products dynamically.

**Pro Tip**: Start with behavioral lists. Export CSV from your store analytics to import; both handle it seamlessly.

### Integrations
Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Strong for non-e-commerce.

Omnisend integrates natively with 140+ platforms, prioritizing Shopify, BigCommerce, Magento. Bonus: Facebook Messenger and Instagram DMs.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is intuitive, with tutorials everywhere—perfect for non-marketers. Onboarding wizards guide setup.

Omnisend's dashboard prioritizes e-commerce metrics (revenue per recipient). Slightly steeper curve but faster ROI for stores.

Support:
- Mailchimp: Email/chat (paid), vast knowledge base.
- Omnisend: 24/7 live chat/email (Pro+), dedicated onboarding.

**Advice**: Do a 14-day trial (both offer). Migrate lists via CSV; test one campaign to gauge learning curve.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- Generous free plan.
- All-in-one (landing pages, ads, postcards).
- Excellent for beginners.

**Cons**:
- Expensive at scale for emails.
- Weaker e-commerce automations.

### Omnisend
**Pros**:
- Unlimited emails, multichannel.
- E-commerce powerhouse.
- High deliverability (99%+ claimed).

**Cons**:
- Limited free tier.
- Less general marketing tools.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Mailchimp** if: You're a solopreneur, blogger, or service business under 5K contacts. Need free tools and broad marketing (social, sites).

- **Pick Omnisend** if: E-commerce is core (Shopify/Woo users). Prioritize SMS/push for 20–50% lift in engagement. Scaling revenue-focused automations.

**Migration Tip**: Both support API/CSV imports. Pause campaigns during switch; use tools like PieSync for sync. A/B test identical campaigns post-migration to benchmark.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for e-commerce with superior automations and multichannel, potentially boosting revenue faster. Mailchimp remains king for versatility and accessibility, especially starters.

Evaluate based on your niche: trial both, track metrics like open rates (aim 20–30%) and revenue per email ($0.50+ goal). The right tool aligns with your goals—neither is universally "best."

Ready to decide? Start with Mailchimp free or Omnisend trial. Your inbox (and bottom line) will thank you.
