# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Platform Should You Choose?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue streams. For bloggers, creators, and small business owners, MailerLite and ConvertKit stand out as popular options. Both offer free plans, intuitive interfaces, and powerful automation, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're starting with your first newsletter or scaling an existing list, understanding the differences between MailerLite and ConvertKit is crucial. We'll focus on real-world practical advice, drawing from their official features as of 2023.

## Overview of MailerLite

MailerLite is known for its affordability and simplicity, making it ideal for beginners and small teams. Launched in 2010, it serves over 1 million users worldwide. Key strengths include a generous free plan (up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month) and drag-and-drop email builders.

### Key Features of MailerLite
- **Email Editor**: Responsive drag-and-drop builder with 80+ templates. Easy to customize without coding.
- **Automation**: Basic workflows like welcome series, abandoned cart, and RSS-to-email campaigns.
- **Landing Pages & Popups**: Unlimited pages and forms, with A/B testing included.
- **Integrations**: Connects to WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics, and 1,000+ apps via Zapier.
- **Analytics**: Open rates, clicks, bounces, and revenue tracking.

Practical tip: Use MailerLite's popup builder to grow your list organically. Set it to appear after 30 seconds on exit-intent for a 5-10% conversion boost on average.

## Overview of ConvertKit

ConvertKit, rebranded from ConvertKit in 2013, targets creators like podcasters, YouTubers, and course sellers. It emphasizes tagging and sequences over traditional lists, allowing hyper-personalized marketing.

### Key Features of ConvertKit
- **Visual Automations**: "Creator Network" for broadcasts and sequences that feel like one-to-one emails.
- **Forms & Landing Pages**: Clean, customizable opt-ins with subscriber scoring.
- **Commerce Tools**: Sell digital products, memberships, and courses directly in emails.
- **Tagging System**: Segment based on behavior (e.g., "clicked link X") for advanced nurturing.
- **Integrations**: Native with Gumroad, Teachable, and podcast platforms; Zapier support.

Practical tip: Leverage ConvertKit's tagging for re-engagement. Tag inactive subscribers after 90 days and send a "We miss you" sequence to recover 20-30% of lapsed opens.

## Pricing Comparison: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

Pricing is a major differentiator. Both have free tiers, but paid plans scale differently.

| Feature | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Free Plan | 1,000 subs, 12k emails/mo | Up to 10,000 subs (limited features) |
| Starter ($10-25/mo) | Growing Business: $10 for 500 subs | Creator: $9 for 300 subs |
| Pro ($30+/mo) | Advanced: $20+ with A/B, webhooks | Creator Pro: $29+ with advanced reporting |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom |

MailerLite wins on value for small lists—paid plans start cheaper and include more features like A/B testing from the start. ConvertKit's free plan is more generous for larger lists, but paid tiers rise quickly (e.g., $29/mo for 1,000 subs).

**Advice**: Calculate your costs based on current subscribers. For under 1,000, MailerLite saves 20-50% monthly. Test free plans first to export/import lists easily.

## Feature Face-Off: Head-to-Head Breakdown

### Ease of Use
MailerLite's interface is cleaner for non-tech users—setup takes under 10 minutes. ConvertKit has a steeper curve due to its tagging focus but rewards with power-user tools.

**Winner**: MailerLite for beginners.

### Automation and Segmentation
ConvertKit excels with visual builders for complex sequences (e.g., if/then branches). MailerLite offers solid basics but lacks ConvertKit's subscriber scoring.

Practical advice: Start simple in MailerLite with trigger-based automations. Migrate to ConvertKit if you need behavior-based tags for courses.

### Email Templates and Design
Both have mobile-responsive templates. MailerLite: 80+ free, photo-heavy. ConvertKit: Minimalist, text-focused for creators (fewer but higher-quality).

**Tip**: A/B test subject lines and CTAs. MailerLite's built-in tool shows 15-25% lift in opens.

### Analytics and Reporting
ConvertKit provides deeper insights like predicted opens and revenue per subscriber. MailerLite covers essentials with heatmaps.

### Deliverability and Compliance
Both maintain 99%+ deliverability. MailerLite: EU-based (GDPR-friendly). ConvertKit: US-based with strong CAN-SPAM tools.

**Pro Tip**: Always use double opt-in and clean lists weekly to stay above 95% delivery rates.

- Podcasters/YouTubers with behavioral data needs
- Teams okay with premium pricing for depth

## Final Thoughts and Recommendation

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage and style. MailerLite is the practical choice for affordability and speed—perfect if you're bootstrapping or testing email marketing. ConvertKit shines for creators ready to monetize deeply with automations and tags.

**Our Pick**: Start with MailerLite's free plan for most users. Switch to ConvertKit at 2k+ subs if commerce is key. Both allow easy exports, so experiment risk-free.
