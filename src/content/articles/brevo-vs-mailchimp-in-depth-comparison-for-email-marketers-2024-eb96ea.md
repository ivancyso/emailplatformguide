# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Tool Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses scaling their email marketing. Both platforms offer robust tools for newsletters, automations, and customer engagement. This comparison dives into pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide based on real capabilities.

## Pricing: Cost Breakdown and Value

Pricing is a top concern for email marketers. Brevo and Mailchimp use different models, affecting budgets differently.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo's model is contact-based with credits for emails/SMS. Plans start free:
- **Free**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day.
- **Starter**: $25/month for 20k emails/month.
- **Business**: $65/month for 20k emails + advanced features.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

No overage fees; you buy credits if needed. Ideal for high-volume senders without contact limits exploding costs.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp charges based on contacts and features:
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1k sends/month.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts + automations.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced segmentation.

Costs rise quickly with audience growth—e.g., 10k contacts on Standard is ~$100/month. Practical tip: Calculate your monthly sends and contacts using their calculators.

**Winner for Budgets**: Brevo for growing lists; Mailchimp's free tier suits tiny lists.

## Ease of Use and Interface

Both have drag-and-drop editors, but interfaces differ.

### Brevo's Interface
Clean, modern dashboard integrates email, SMS, chat, and CRM. Beginner-friendly with templates and AI content generator. Navigation is intuitive; setup takes minutes.

### Mailchimp's Interface
Familiar to many, with a content studio and creative assistant. Slightly busier but powerful for designers. Mobile app is strong for on-the-go edits.

**Practical Advice**: Test free trials. Brevo feels faster for transactional emails; Mailchimp excels in visual campaigns. Migrate data easily via CSV or integrations.

## Core Features Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates
- **Brevo**: 48+ responsive templates, block-based editor. Strong for transactional emails (e.g., order confirmations).
- **Mailchimp**: 100+ templates, highly customizable. Better for creative newsletters.

### Automation Workflows
- **Brevo**: Unlimited automations on paid plans, triggers like cart abandonment, welcome series. Multi-channel (email + SMS).
- **Mailchimp**: Robust journeys on Standard+, behavioral triggers, A/B testing.

**Tip**: Use Brevo for omnichannel; Mailchimp for complex customer journeys.

### List Management and Segmentation
Both segment by behavior, location, tags.
- Brevo: Built-in CRM syncs contacts automatically.
- Mailchimp: Advanced tags and groups.

### Integrations
- Brevo: 300+ (WordPress, Shopify, Zapier).
- Mailchimp: 250+ (eCommerce heavy).

**Pro Tip**: For Shopify users, both integrate seamlessly—test API limits.

## Deliverability and Analytics

Deliverability impacts ROI.

- **Brevo**: 99%+ rates, IP warming, dedicated IPs on Enterprise. Strong blacklist monitoring.
- **Mailchimp**: Excellent reputation (98%+), detailed reports.

Analytics:
- Brevo: Open/click tracking, heatmaps, revenue per email.
- Mailchimp: AI insights, comparative reports.

**Advice**: Warm up domains gradually. Monitor SPF/DKIM setup—both guide you.

## Customer Support and Scalability

- **Brevo**: Email/chat support (paid), knowledge base, academy courses.
- **Mailchimp**: 24/7 phone/email (Premium+), extensive help center.

Scalability: Brevo handles millions of emails; Mailchimp suits enterprises but pricier.

**For Solopreneurs**: Brevo's free tier scales better.
**For Agencies**: Mailchimp's team features shine.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo
**Pros**:
- Affordable scaling.
- Multi-channel.
- Unlimited contacts free.

**Cons**:
- Limited free sends.
- Fewer templates.

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- Intuitive for beginners.
- Superior templates.
- Strong community.

**Cons**:
- Expensive for large lists.
- Feature-locked plans.

## Real-World Use Cases

- **Ecommerce**: Brevo for SMS + email abandoned carts (higher conversions).
- **Bloggers**: Mailchimp for beautiful newsletters.
- **SaaS**: Both, but Brevo's CRM edges transactional flows.

**Practical Test**: Send a campaign to 100 contacts. Track setup time and opens.

## Conclusion: Which to Choose?

Brevo wins for cost-conscious teams needing multi-channel and scalability—perfect if sends exceed contacts. Mailchimp is best for design-focused users with smaller lists valuing polish.

**Recommendation**: Start with Brevo's free plan if budget-tight; Mailchimp if aesthetics priority. Both offer 14-day trials—import lists and run A/B tests.

Ultimately, align with goals: volume (Brevo) or creativity (Mailchimp). Whichever you pick, focus on compliant lists and value-driven content for 20-30% open rates.
