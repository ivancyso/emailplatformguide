# Mailchimp vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform is Right for You?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. Mailchimp and ConvertKit are two popular platforms, but they cater to different needs. Mailchimp is an all-in-one marketing powerhouse ideal for small businesses and e-commerce, while ConvertKit targets creators, bloggers, and course sellers with creator-focused tools. In this comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide.

## Pricing Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but they scale differently based on subscribers.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts with 1,000 monthly sends and basic features like a single audience and landing pages. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for 50,000+ contacts (Premium). Features unlock gradually: automation at $75/month (Standard), advanced reporting at Premium. Overages for sends can add costs, and it's monthly billing only.

Practical tip: If you're under 500 contacts and testing waters, start free. For growing lists, calculate total sends—Mailchimp charges based on contacts and sends.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit's free plan goes up to 300 subscribers (now called Creator plan in free tier post-rebrand to Kit). Paid Creator plan starts at $9/month for up to 300 subscribers, $29 for 1,000, up to $779 for 50,000+. Unlimited emails and sends across all plans—no overage fees. Commerce add-on ($29+/month) for paid newsletters/subscriptions.

Practical tip: ConvertKit shines for unlimited sends, perfect if you email frequently. Annual billing saves 20%.

**Verdict**: ConvertKit is cheaper for mid-sized lists with high send volumes; Mailchimp better for low-volume, feature-heavy needs.

## Feature Comparison

Both platforms excel in core email marketing, but differences emerge in depth.

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive with 100+ mobile-responsive templates. Content blocks for images, products, and social feeds make it e-commerce friendly.

ConvertKit's visual builder is simpler, with clean templates optimized for creators. It emphasizes one-click blocks for buttons, images, and subscriber counts. No product catalogs like Mailchimp.

**Practical advice**: Use Mailchimp for visually complex campaigns; ConvertKit for quick, text-heavy newsletters.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp offers customer journeys (visual automation builder) from Standard plan ($75/month). Triggers like tag added, page visit, or purchase. Single-step automations free.

ConvertKit's automations are tag-based and unlimited even on free. Visual editor for sequences, broadcasts, and subscriber behaviors (e.g., link clicks). Native commerce automations for paid content.

**Practical advice**: Creators love ConvertKit's tagging for segmentation (e.g., tag 'lead-magnet-downloader'). Mailchimp suits behavioral automations like abandoned carts.

### Segmentation and Tagging

Mailchimp uses groups and tags, with merge fields for dynamic segments. Advanced segmentation via custom conditions.

ConvertKit relies on tags for everything—add multiple tags per subscriber, segment by 'has tag X but not Y'. Forms auto-tag based on opt-ins.

**Practical advice**: If you nurture leads via tags (bloggers), ConvertKit wins. For group-based lists (e.g., by location), Mailchimp.

### Landing Pages and Forms

Mailchimp: 300+ templates, pop-ups, forms integrated with sites. Free plan limited.

ConvertKit: Native landing pages, opt-in forms, link pages (great for link-in-bio). Unlimited on paid plans, mobile-optimized.

**Practical advice**: Build a lead magnet funnel in ConvertKit—form > tag > welcome sequence. Mailchimp for full websites.

### Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp: Detailed opens, clicks, revenue tracking, A/B testing, comparative reports.

ConvertKit: Opens, clicks, unsubscribes, revenue per subscriber. A/B on subjects/links, growth tracking.

**Practical advice**: Track ROI with Mailchimp's e-commerce integrations; monitor subscriber value in ConvertKit.

### Integrations

Mailchimp: 300+ native (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics, Zapier).

ConvertKit: 100+ (WordPress, Shopify, Gumroad, Zapier, Teachable).

Both strong via Zapier for custom flows.

**Practical advice**: E-commerce? Mailchimp + Shopify sync. Courses? ConvertKit + Teachable.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's dashboard is colorful and beginner-friendly with guided setup wizards. Steeper curve for advanced automations.

ConvertKit (now Kit) has a minimalist interface, quick onboarding tours focused on creators. Tag system takes practice but streamlines growth.

**Practical advice**: Beginners: Mailchimp tutorials abound. Solopreneurs: ConvertKit's simplicity saves time.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/chat (paid), extensive help center, 24/7 chat for Premium.

ConvertKit: Priority email support (paid), live chat, creator-focused resources like podcast.

**Practical advice**: Free users get forums; upgrade for humans.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros
- Robust templates and e-commerce tools
- Free plan for small lists
- Advanced analytics

### Mailchimp Cons
- Pricing jumps with scale
- Limited free automations
- Send limits

### ConvertKit Pros
- Unlimited sends
- Creator-centric tagging/automation
- Affordable scaling

### ConvertKit Cons
- Fewer design options
- No built-in ads manager
- Free plan caps at 300 subs

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Small businesses/e-commerce stores
- Teams needing collaboration
- High-volume transactional emails

**Tip**: Migrate if you outgrow free tier—export lists easily.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers, podcasters, course creators
- High-engagement newsletters
- Budget-conscious solos with 1k+ subs

**Tip**: Start with free, tag aggressively for personalization.

## Final Thoughts: Mailchimp vs ConvertKit Winner?

No outright winner—depends on you. Mailchimp for versatile marketing suites; ConvertKit for streamlined creator growth. Test free plans: import a segment, build a campaign, run an automation.

For most creators, ConvertKit's value wins at scale. Businesses? Mailchimp's ecosystem. Whichever you pick, focus on list-building: offer value-first magnets, segment ruthlessly, A/B test relentlessly.
