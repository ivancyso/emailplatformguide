# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate 2024 Showdown

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. For creators, bloggers, small businesses, and e-commerce owners, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both tools excel in automation and user-friendly designs, but they cater to slightly different needs.

In this comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and real-world applications. Whether you're on a budget or need advanced creator tools, you'll find practical advice to decide. Data is based on official sites as of mid-2024—always verify current plans.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Your Subscriber Count

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free tiers, but scales differ.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite shines for affordability:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, drag-and-drop editor, and landing pages.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $9/month (500 contacts), $15 (1,000), up to $290 (100k+). Unlimited emails, advanced automations, A/B testing.
- **Advanced**: $20+/month adds webhooks, custom HTML/CSS.

**Practical Tip**: Ideal for bootstrapped solopreneurs. Upgrade only when exceeding free limits—many stay free for years by optimizing sends.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now rebranded with creator focus):
- **Free Plan**: Up to 10,000 subscribers (unlimited emails). Basic broadcasts, forms, landing pages, but limited automations.
- **Creator**: $15/month (300 subs), $29 (1,000), scales to $2,500+ (500k subs). Unlocks sequences, tagging, commerce.
- **Creator Pro**: $25+/month adds advanced subscriber scores, integrations.

**Practical Tip**: Free plan suits growing lists without immediate revenue needs. Paid tiers justify cost for digital product sellers via built-in commerce.

**Winner?** MailerLite for tiny budgets (<1k subs); ConvertKit for larger free lists.

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools Head-to-Head

Both platforms pack essentials, but depth varies.

### Automation and Sequences

- **MailerLite**: Workflow builder with triggers (e.g., tag added, purchase). Multi-step automations, RSS campaigns. Easy for welcome series.
- **ConvertKit**: Visual sequences shine for creators—drip content based on opens/clicks. Tagging automates segmentation (e.g., 'engaged' vs 'cold').

**Advice**: Use ConvertKit for nurture sequences (bloggers love it). MailerLite for e-commerce abandons—set up in 10 minutes via templates.

### Email Editor and Templates

- **MailerLite**: Intuitive drag-and-drop, 80+ responsive templates. Custom blocks, JSON feeds for dynamic content.
- **ConvertKit**: Block-based editor, plain-text focus for high deliverability. Creator templates for newsletters/podcasts.

**Pro Tip**: Test both free editors. MailerLite for visually rich emails; ConvertKit for authentic, mobile-first designs.

### Segmentation and Tagging

- **MailerLite**: Groups, tags, custom fields. Behavioral segments (e.g., opened last 30 days).
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tagging rules, subscriber scores. Predicts engagement.

**Practical Use**: Segment for re-engagement campaigns—boost opens 20-30% by targeting 'inactive' tags.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages/forms/popups on all plans. High-converting templates, exit-intent.
- **ConvertKit**: Forms, landing pages, ephemerals (flyouts). Native checkout for products.

**Tip**: MailerLite popups grow lists faster for websites; ConvertKit's commerce integrates Gumroad/Stripe seamlessly.

### Analytics and Reporting

Both track opens, clicks, bounces. MailerLite adds revenue tracking; ConvertKit shows revenue per subscriber.

**Advice**: Review unsubscribe rates weekly—aim <0.5%. Use A/B tests (both support) to optimize subject lines.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Daily Workflow

MailerLite's interface is minimalist—beginners onboard in minutes. Drag-and-drop everywhere.

ConvertKit feels creator-native: Sequences tab centralizes drips. Slight learning curve for tags, but tutorials abound.

**Real-World Test**: Import CSV lists easily in both. MailerLite wins for non-tech users; ConvertKit for automation pros.

**Migration Tip**: Both import from competitors (e.g., Mailchimp). Export subscribers first, map tags/groups.

## Customer Support and Community

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Highly rated (4.9/5 on Capterra).
- **ConvertKit**: Email support (paid plans), extensive academy/videos. Creator community via Facebook/Slack.

**Advice**: Use free trials (MailerLite 30 days, ConvertKit 14 days) to test support responsiveness.

## Integrations and Deliverability

- **Integrations**: MailerLite (2,500+ via Zapier), WordPress plugin. ConvertKit: Native Gumroad, Teachable; strong Zapier.
- **Deliverability**: Both >95% (industry standard). ConvertKit edges for newsletters; MailerLite for promos.

**Tip**: Warm up domains gradually—start 50/day to avoid spam filters.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite Pros and Cons

**Pros**:
- Cheaper paid plans
- Generous free tier
- Popups/landing pages
- Fast setup

**Cons**:
- Less creator-specific (no native storefront)
- Automation less visual

### ConvertKit Pros and Cons

**Pros**:
- Superior sequences/tagging
- Digital sales tools
- Large free subscriber limit
- Creator resources

**Cons**:
- Higher starting paid price
- Editor less flexible for designs

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs ConvertKit?

- **Pick MailerLite if**: Small business, e-commerce, budget-conscious, need popups. Great for agencies managing multiple clients.
- **Pick ConvertKit if**: Blogger/podcaster/creator selling courses/ebooks. Scales with audience monetization.

**Hybrid Advice**: Start with free plans. Switch if features lack—both allow easy exports.

## Conclusion

**MailerLite vs ConvertKit** boils down to your stage: MailerLite for accessible growth and visuals; ConvertKit for creator automation and sales.

No clear 'winner'—test both. For most starting out, MailerLite's free tools build momentum fast. Scaling creators? ConvertKit's tagging pays off long-term.
