# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your audience engagement and revenue. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small businesses. Both tools excel in automation and user-friendly designs, but they cater to slightly different needs. This in-depth comparison covers pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more to help you decide.

We'll break down real-world use cases, practical tips for setup, and migration advice. No hype—just facts based on their official offerings as of late 2023.

## Overview of MailerLite

MailerLite is a Lithuania-based platform launched in 2010, known for its affordability and simplicity. It's popular among small businesses and creators who want robust features without complexity.

### Key Strengths
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month—ideal for testing.
- **Drag-and-Drop Editor**: Intuitive for non-designers.
- **Built-in Tools**: Landing pages, popups, forms, and websites all in one dashboard.

MailerLite supports unlimited emails on paid plans (banded by subscriber count), making it scalable for growing lists.

## Overview of ConvertKit

ConvertKit, founded in 2013 by Nathan Barry, targets creators like bloggers, podcasters, and YouTubers. It emphasizes sequences (automated email series) and subscriber tagging for personalized marketing.

### Key Strengths
- **Creator-Focused**: Native commerce tools for selling digital products.
- **Visual Automations**: Easy flowchart-style builder.
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (recently reduced from 1,000).

ConvertKit shines in segmentation via tags and broadcasts, with strong integrations for platforms like WordPress and Teachable.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major decider. Here's a side-by-side (monthly, billed annually; check sites for latest).

| Subscribers | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|-------------|------------|------------|
| 0-1,000    | Free / $10| Free / $29|
| 1k-2.5k    | $15        | $49        |
| 2.5k-5k    | $25        | $79        |

**Practical Advice**: Start with MailerLite's free tier if budget-tight. ConvertKit's higher entry ($29/mo) suits those needing advanced tagging from day one. Both offer 30-day refunds—test thoroughly.

MailerLite wins on cost for most, but ConvertKit's Creator plan adds paid newsletter subscriptions.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer responsive, drag-and-drop editors with 100+ templates.
- **MailerLite**: Simpler blocks, photo editor integration. Great for quick newsletters.
- **ConvertKit**: More customization, plain-text options for authenticity.

**Tip**: Import your branding (logos, colors) early. MailerLite's editor feels faster for beginners.

### Automation and Workflows
- **MailerLite**: Trigger-based automations (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart). RSS-to-email for bloggers.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences as pre-built series; tagging for dynamic segmentation (e.g., "lead" vs "buyer").

**Practical Advice**: Map your customer journey first. Use ConvertKit for nurture sequences; MailerLite for e-commerce triggers. Test with 10-20 subscribers to avoid errors.

### Forms, Landing Pages, and Popups
- **MailerLite**: Unlimited popups/modals, high-converting landing pages, full websites.
- **ConvertKit**: Inline/classic forms, landing pages, but no native popups (use integrations).

**Tip**: A/B test forms. MailerLite's popups boost signups by 20-30% in practice—place at content midpoints.

### Analytics and Segmentation
Both track opens, clicks, bounces.
- **MailerLite**: Groups for segmentation; revenue tracking.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tags, subscriber scoring, revenue per subscriber.

**Advice**: Segment weekly (e.g., engaged vs inactive). ConvertKit's tags enable hyper-personalization like "podcast listener."

### Integrations and E-commerce
- **MailerLite**: 250+ (Stripe, WooCommerce, Zapier).
- **ConvertKit**: 100+ focused on creators (Gumroad, MemberPress).

Both handle basic payments, but pair with Zapier for gaps.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

MailerLite's dashboard is cleaner for newbies—import lists in minutes. ConvertKit's learning curve is steeper due to tagging but rewarding for power users.

**Migration Tip**: Export CSV from old tool. MailerLite's importer handles segments; ConvertKit requires tag recreation. Budget 2-4 hours.

## Customer Support and Community

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. High ratings (4.8/5 on G2).
- **ConvertKit**: Email/ticket (no live chat), extensive Creator Network community.

**Advice**: Join Facebook groups for both. MailerLite responds faster for urgent issues.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros**:
- Cheaper scaling.
- All-in-one (sites/popups).
- Generous free plan.

**Cons**:
- Less advanced tagging.
- Automation less visual.

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:
- Superior sequences/tagging.
- Creator tools (subscriptions).
- Strong analytics.

**Cons**:
- Pricier.
- No popups.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Budget-conscious startups.
- E-commerce stores needing popups.
- Beginners wanting free growth to 1,000 subs.

**Real-World Example**: A blogger uses RSS automation to send weekly digests effortlessly.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators with funnels.
- Those selling courses/products.
- Teams needing tag-based personalization.

**Example**: Podcaster tags listeners by episode, sends tailored upsells.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

**MailerLite** edges out for most due to price and versatility—perfect if you're under 5,000 subs. **ConvertKit** is the pick for creators prioritizing automations and monetization.

**Action Plan**:
1. Sign up for both free plans.
2. Build a test campaign/automation.
3. Check deliverability (both excellent, 99%+).
4. Migrate if committed.

Whichever you choose, focus on value-driven emails: 80% education, 20% promo. Track ROI monthly. Your audience will thank you.
