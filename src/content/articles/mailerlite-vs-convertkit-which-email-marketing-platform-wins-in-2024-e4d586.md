# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you connect with your audience. If you're torn between **MailerLite** and **ConvertKit**, this in-depth comparison breaks it down. We'll cover pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide based on your needs—whether you're a solopreneur, blogger, or small business owner.

Both tools are popular for their creator-friendly designs, but they cater to slightly different users. MailerLite shines in affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit excels in advanced automations for content creators. Let's dive in.

## Overview of MailerLite

MailerLite is a Lithuania-based platform launched in 2010, known for its straightforward interface and budget-friendly plans. It's ideal for beginners and small teams who want reliable email marketing without a steep learning curve.

Key strengths include a forever-free plan (up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month), drag-and-drop editor, and built-in tools like popups, landing pages, and surveys. As of 2024, it supports over 1 million users worldwide.

Practical tip: Start with the free plan to test campaigns. Use the A/B testing feature to optimize subject lines—aim for open rates above 20% by personalizing previews.

## Overview of ConvertKit

ConvertKit, founded in 2013 by Nathan Barry, targets creators like bloggers, podcasters, and YouTubers. It's U.S.-based and emphasizes segmentation, automations, and monetization tools.

It offers a free plan for up to 10,000 subscribers (with limits on sends), plus paid plans starting at $15/month for 300 subscribers. Standout features include visual automations, subscriber tagging, and commerce integrations for selling digital products.

Practical advice: Leverage subscriber tags for hyper-targeted sequences. For example, tag users who click podcast links and send tailored follow-ups to boost engagement by 15-30%.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major decider. Here's a side-by-side (based on 2024 rates; always check official sites for updates):

| Subscribers | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|-------------|------------|------------|
| Up to 1,000 | Free      | Free (limited sends) |
| 500         | $10/mo    | $15/mo    |
| 2,500       | $20/mo    | $29/mo    |
| 10,000      | $50/mo    | $79/mo    |

MailerLite wins on cost, especially for growing lists under 5,000. ConvertKit's Creator plan adds perks like unlimited emails but costs more.

**Practical advice**: Calculate your monthly sends. If under 12,000, MailerLite's free tier suffices. For high-volume newsletters, ConvertKit's unlimited sends justify the premium.

## Ease of Use

MailerLite's interface is clean and intuitive—campaign setup takes minutes. The drag-and-drop builder is beginner-proof, with mobile previews.

ConvertKit is also user-friendly but leans toward power users. Its automation builder uses a flowchart style, which is visual but requires planning.

Winner: MailerLite for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for those comfortable with logic flows.

**Tip**: Import contacts via CSV in both. Clean your list first (remove bounces) to maintain deliverability above 95%.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates

Both offer responsive templates. MailerLite has 80+ free ones with a simple editor. ConvertKit's builder focuses on clean, text-heavy designs suited for newsletters.

**Advice**: Use MailerLite for visually rich emails; ConvertKit for minimalist, story-driven content.

### Automations

ConvertKit dominates with unlimited automations and advanced tagging (e.g., "clicked link X"). MailerLite offers basic workflows like welcome series and abandoned carts.

**Pro tip**: In ConvertKit, set up a 7-day nurture sequence: Day 1 welcome, Day 3 value content, Day 7 offer. Track conversions in analytics.

### Landing Pages and Forms

MailerLite includes unlimited popups, forms, and pages (free tier limited). ConvertKit's forms are optimized for lead magnets, with native selling pages.

Both integrate with websites easily via embeds.

### Analytics and Segmentation

ConvertKit's reporting is deeper (revenue tracking, tag performance). MailerLite provides solid basics like opens, clicks, and geography.

**Advice**: Segment by engagement—send re-engagement campaigns to inactive subs quarterly to reduce churn.

### Integrations

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, plus WordPress, Shopify.
ConvertKit: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable, Google Analytics.

## Customer Support

MailerLite: Email, chat (paid), extensive help center.
ConvertKit: Priority email/chat for paid users, creator-focused resources like webinars.

Both score high (4.5+ on G2/Capterra). **Tip**: Use self-serve docs first—search "automation triggers" to save time.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Budget-tight, small list (<5k), need quick setup, visual campaigns.
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Creator selling products, complex sequences, scaling fast.

Test both free plans. Migrate via CSV exports—most users report smooth switches.

## Conclusion

MailerLite edges out for affordability and simplicity, making it ideal for startups. ConvertKit pulls ahead for serious creators needing robust automations and revenue tools. Ultimately, align with your goals: cost vs. growth potential.

Whichever you choose, focus on value-driven emails. Aim for 1:1 reader value ratio, comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM, and monitor metrics weekly. Your audience will thank you.
