# 10 Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Top Picks for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but with rising costs, limited advanced features for scaling businesses, and a shift to a pay-per-contact model, many users are searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're frustrated with unpredictable pricing or need more robust automation, deliverability, or segmentation, this roundup covers the top 10 options. We've focused on platforms that offer real value, based on user reviews from sites like G2 and Capterra, official pricing pages, and feature comparisons as of 2024.

Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or agency, these alternatives provide practical solutions. We'll break down features, pricing, pros/cons, and migration tips to help you choose wisely.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan is generous for beginners (up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month), but paid plans start at $13/month and scale quickly with audience size. Recent changes emphasize performance-based pricing, which can surprise growing lists. Common pain points include:

- **High costs for large lists**: Bills can exceed $500/month for 50k contacts.
- **Limited automation**: Basic workflows compared to competitors.
- **Deliverability dips**: Some users report inbox placement issues post-2023 updates.
- **UI clutter**: Overwhelming for non-marketers.

Switching makes sense if you need better ROI, advanced personalization, or SMS integration. Always export your Mailchimp list (CSV format) before migrating—most platforms support direct imports.

## Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo stands out for its unlimited contacts on paid plans and multi-channel support (email, SMS, chat, ads). Ideal for e-commerce and transactional emails.

**Key Features**:
- Drag-and-drop editor similar to Mailchimp.
- Automation workflows with A/B testing.
- 300+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress).
- Excellent deliverability (99%+ claimed).

**Pricing**: Free plan (300 emails/day); Starter $25/month (20k emails); Business $65/month (unlimited emails, advanced reporting).

**Pros**: Affordable scaling, transactional API, EU-based for GDPR.
**Cons**: Free plan limits daily sends; steeper learning for automation.

**Best For**: SMBs with transactional needs. Migrate by importing CSV and remapping tags.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

A powerhouse for automation, ActiveCampaign excels in CRM-email hybrids with machine learning for predictions.

**Key Features**:
- 700+ pre-built automations.
- Site tracking and lead scoring.
- Split testing and conditional content.

**Pricing**: Lite $29/month (500 contacts); Plus $49 (automation + CRM); Pro $149 (advanced).

**Pros**: Deep personalization, high deliverability.
**Cons**: Complex for beginners; no free plan beyond trial.

**Best For**: Agencies and lead-gen businesses. Practical tip: Use their migration service for seamless transfer.

### 3. Klaviyo

Tailored for e-commerce (Shopify integration shines), Klaviyo focuses on data-driven flows like abandoned cart.

**Key Features**:
- Revenue tracking per campaign.
- SMS and push notifications.
- Predictive analytics.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; scales $20+ based on contacts.

**Pros**: E-com specific templates; strong analytics.
**Cons**: Less ideal for non-stores; pricing rises fast.

**Best For**: Online retailers. Advice: Sync Shopify data first for instant segmentation.

### 4. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Creator-focused with simple tagging over lists.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automations.
- Landing pages and forms.
- Subscriber scoring.

**Pricing**: Free (300 subscribers); Creator $29/month; Creator Pro $59.

**Pros**: Intuitive for solopreneurs; unlimited sends.
**Cons**: Fewer enterprise features.

**Best For**: Bloggers/newsletters. Tip: Tag-based segments ease Mailchimp migration.

### 5. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels.

**Key Features**n- Conversion funnels.
- Webinars (up to 1k attendees).
- Autoresponders.

**Pricing**: Email Marketing $19/month (1k contacts); Marketing Automation $59.

**Pros**: Built-in landing pages; good support.
**Cons**: Interface feels dated.

**Best For**: Course creators.

### 6. AWeber

Reliable veteran with strong deliverability.

**Key Features**:
- RSS-to-email.
- E-com integrations.
- Tag-based automation.

**Pricing**: Lite $19/month (500 subs); Unlimited $149.

**Pros**: Loyal support; spam testing.
**Cons**: Basic reporting.

**Best For**: Non-profits/small biz.

### 7. Constant Contact

User-friendly for beginners.

**Key Features**:
- Event management.
- Social ads.
- AI content generator.

**Pricing**: Lite $12/month (500 contacts); Standard $35.

**Pros**: Easy templates.
**Cons**: Per-contact pricing.

**Best For**: Local businesses.

### 8. Omnisend

E-commerce SMS/email combo.

**Key Features**:
- Omnichannel workflows.
- Product picks in emails.

**Pricing**: Free (500 emails/month); Standard $16/month.

**Pros**: Generous free tier.
**Cons**: E-com focused.

**Best For**: DTC brands.

### 9. Drip

Advanced revenue automation.

**Key Features**:
- Customer journey builder.
- Behavior-based triggers.

**Pricing**: Starts $39/month (500 subs).

**Pros**: Deep segmentation.
**Cons**: Pricey for small lists.

**Best For**: High-volume e-com.

### 10. MailerLite

Budget-friendly with drag-and-drop.

**Key Features**:
- Popups and forms.
- Basic automation.

**Pricing**: Free (1k subs, 12k emails); Growing $10/month.

**Pros**: Cheap; website builder.
**Cons**: Limited advanced flows.

**Best For**: Startups.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

### Key Factors to Evaluate
- **Pricing Model**: Contacts vs. sends? Predict costs with your list size.
- **Deliverability**: Check Sender Score or third-party tests.
- **Integrations**: Must-have for your stack (e.g., Zapier, Shopify).
- **Ease of Use**: Test free trials.
- **Support**: Live chat vs. docs.

### Migration Steps
1. Export Mailchimp list (Audience > Export).
2. Sign up for trial; import CSV.
3. Remap tags/segments.
4. Recreate top campaigns.
5. Update signup forms/domain authentication (DKIM/SPF).
6. Test sends; monitor bounces.

Pro Tip: Run parallel campaigns during transition to compare performance.
