# Best Email Marketing Tools for Nonprofits in 2024: Free Plans, Discounts & Fundraising Features

Email marketing remains one of the most cost-effective ways for nonprofits to connect with donors, volunteers, and supporters. With tight budgets, nonprofits need platforms offering free plans, special discounts, robust segmentation for donor lists, and automation for campaigns like year-end appeals. This roundup focuses on tools with proven nonprofit-friendly features, based on their official pricing pages and user-reported experiences as of 2024.

We'll cover key features to prioritize, review top platforms, provide a comparison table, and share practical advice for implementation. Whether you're a small charity or a larger organization, these options help maximize ROI without breaking the bank.

## Why Email Marketing is Essential for Nonprofits

Nonprofits rely on email to nurture relationships and drive action. According to industry benchmarks from sources like Nonprofit Tech for Good, email open rates for nonprofits average 25-30%, with click-through rates around 2-3%. This outperforms social media for direct fundraising.

Key benefits include:
- **Personalized donor communication**: Segment lists by giving history to send targeted appeals.
- **Event promotion and volunteer recruitment**: Automated reminders boost attendance.
- **Compliance and deliverability**: Tools with built-in CAN-SPAM and GDPR support reduce risks.
- **Measurable impact**: Track donations directly from emails.

In 2024, with rising email fatigue, success hinges on choosing platforms optimized for mobile, A/B testing, and integrations with donation processors like Stripe or PayPal.

## Key Features to Look for in Nonprofit Email Tools

When selecting a platform, prioritize these based on nonprofit workflows:

### Donor Segmentation and List Management
Divide contacts into groups like "monthly donors," "lapsed supporters," or "event attendees." This increases relevance and open rates by 14-20%, per general email marketing studies.

### Automation Workflows
Set up drip campaigns for welcome series, thank-yous post-donation, or re-engagement for inactive subscribers.

### Fundraising Templates and Landing Pages
Pre-built designs for appeals, newsletters, and petitions save time.

### Nonprofit Pricing and Discounts
Look for free tiers (e.g., up to 2,000 contacts) or 15-50% discounts verified on vendor sites.

### Integrations
Connect with CRMs like Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud, donor platforms (Bloomerang, DonorPerfect), or Google Workspace.

### Analytics and Reporting
Track revenue per email, ROI, and suppression lists for unsubscribes.

### Deliverability Tools
Dedicated IP options or authentication (DKIM/SPF) ensure emails land in inboxes.

## Top 7 Email Marketing Platforms for Nonprofits

Here are the best options, selected for affordability, ease of use, and nonprofit-specific perks. All data from official websites as of mid-2024.

### 1. Mailchimp
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month—ideal for startups. Paid plans scale affordably, with nonprofits eligible for discounts via application.

**Pros**:
- Intuitive drag-and-drop editor.
- Advanced segmentation and automation.
- Integrates with 250+ apps, including Zoom for events.

**Cons**:
- Free plan limits creative assets.
- Higher tiers start at $13/month.

**Best for**: Small nonprofits starting out. Practical tip: Use behavioral triggers to automate thank-yous after donations.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)
Free forever plan: 300 emails/day to unlimited contacts. Nonprofits love the transactional email add-ons for receipts.

**Pros**:
- SMS integration for urgent appeals.
- Strong automation with A/B testing.
- EU-based for GDPR compliance.

**Cons**:
- Design templates are basic.
- Paid plans from €25/month for more sends.

**Best for**: International nonprofits. Tip: Segment by location for localized campaigns.

### 3. Constant Contact
Offers 20-30% nonprofit discounts on all plans (apply via their site). Starts at $12/month for 500 contacts.

**Pros**:
- Nonprofit-optimized templates.
- Event management tools.
- Excellent support with live chat.

**Cons**:
- No free plan beyond trial.
- Limited automation on basic tiers.

**Best for**: Event-heavy orgs. Tip: Use their signup form builder for volunteer drives.

### 4. Sender
Free plan: 2,500 subscribers, 15,000 emails/month. Generous for growing lists.

**Pros**:
- Pop-up forms and landing pages included.
- Automation for win-back campaigns.
- Affordable upgrades from $8/month.

**Cons**:
- Fewer integrations than giants.
- Interface feels dated.

**Best for**: Mid-sized teams. Tip: A/B test subject lines for holiday fundraisers.

### 5. Benchmark Email
Special nonprofit pricing (up to 50% off—contact sales). Free trial with robust features.

**Pros**:
- RSS-to-email for newsletters.
- Image editor and polls.
- High deliverability scores.

**Cons**:
- Automation requires Pro plan ($13/month).
- Steeper learning curve.

**Best for**: Storytelling nonprofits. Tip: Embed videos from YouTube for impact stories.

### 6. Moosend
Free for 1,000 subscribers. Lifetime discounts for nonprofits.

**Pros**:
- Advanced automation and personalization.
- Ecommerce tracking for donation funnels.
- White-label option.

**Cons**:
- Support is email-only on free.
- Pricing jumps at $9/month.

**Best for**: Data-driven orgs. Tip: Use JSON triggers for CRM syncs.

### 7. AWeber
Nonprofit discounts available (15-25%). Free trial, plans from $19/month.

**Pros**:
- Legacy tool with reliable delivery.
- Tag-based segmentation.
- Webinars integration.

**Cons**:
- Dated UI.
- No free plan.

**Best for**: Established nonprofits. Tip: Leverage tags for donor stewardship.

## How to Choose the Right Email Marketing Platform for Your Nonprofit

1. **Assess your list size**: Start free if under 1,000 contacts.
2. **Check discounts**: Email sales@platform.com with your EIN.
3. **Test integrations**: Ensure compatibility with your CRM.
4. **Trial campaigns**: Run a sample appeal during free trials.
5. **Budget for scale**: Factor 10-20% annual growth.

Practical advice: Export current lists to CSV and import during trials. Monitor bounce rates under 2%.

## Best Practices for Nonprofit Email Campaigns

- **Subject lines**: Keep under 50 chars, e.g., "Your $10 Helped 5 Kids Today."
- **Frequency**: 1-2/month to avoid fatigue.
- **Mobile optimization**: 60% opens on phones—use single-column layouts.
- **Calls-to-action**: Clear buttons like "Donate Now" linking to secure pages.
- **Compliance**: Always include physical address and unsubscribe links.
- **A/B testing**: Test images vs. no-images for 10-15% lift.
- **Post-campaign analysis**: Calculate ROI as (donations - platform cost) / cost.

Pro tip: Clean lists quarterly to maintain sender reputation.

## Conclusion

For nonprofits, Mailchimp and Brevo stand out for free scalability, while Constant Contact and Benchmark excel with discounts and templates. Evaluate based on your donor volume and tech stack—most offer 14-30 day trials.

Start with a free plan today to test donor engagement. Track metrics like conversion rates to refine strategies. Effective email marketing can increase donations by 20-50% with consistent use. Choose wisely, and watch your impact grow.
