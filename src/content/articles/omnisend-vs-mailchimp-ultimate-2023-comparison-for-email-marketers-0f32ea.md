# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a veteran in the space, known for its user-friendly interface and broad appeal. Omnisend shines in ecommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications. This comparison dives deep into features, pricing, and real-world use cases to help you decide.

## Quick Overview: Omnisend and Mailchimp

**Mailchimp** powers over 12 million businesses with drag-and-drop editors, automation, and audience segmentation. It's ideal for beginners and content-driven campaigns.

**Omnisend** focuses on ecommerce stores (e.g., Shopify users), offering pre-built workflows for abandoned carts, welcome series, and cross-channel messaging (email + SMS + web push).

Practical tip: Start with free trials from both to import your list and test a basic automation.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is a top decider. Here's how they stack up (based on current standard plans as of 2023; always check official sites for updates).

| Feature/Plan | Mailchimp | Omnisend |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| **Free Plan** | Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/mo, basic features | Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/mo, full automation access |
| **Starter/Essentials** | $13/mo (500 contacts), unlimited emails | $16/mo (500 contacts), omnichannel |
| **Standard/Pro** | $20/mo (500 contacts), advanced automation | $59/mo (500 contacts), A/B testing, dedicated IP |
| **Premium/Enterprise** | $350+/mo, full features | Custom, high-volume support |

Mailchimp's free tier is more generous for emails sent, but Omnisend's free plan includes premium automations like cart recovery—perfect if you're testing ecommerce flows.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate costs based on your list size. For 1,000 contacts sending weekly newsletters, Mailchimp Essentials (~$13-29/mo) beats Omnisend Standard (~$20-36/mo). But add SMS? Omnisend bundles it affordably.

## Feature Face-Off: Core Capabilities

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 100+ responsive templates. Customization is straightforward for non-designers.

Omnisend offers similar builders but ecommerce-optimized templates (product blocks, dynamic content). It pulls real-time product data from your store.

**Winner**: Tie for beginners; Omnisend for shops.

**Tip**: Use Mailchimp for blog newsletters; Omnisend for personalized product recs.

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp has behavioral automations (e.g., tag-based triggers) and customer journeys.

Omnisend excels here: 200+ pre-built workflows for abandoned carts (recover 10-30% of lost sales typically), post-purchase upsells, and win-back campaigns. Multi-channel triggers (e.g., SMS after email open).

**Practical Advice**: If your open rates hover below 20%, Omnisend's segmentation by purchase behavior can boost them. Test a cart abandonment flow—set it up in 10 minutes.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Both segment by opens, clicks, purchases. Mailchimp uses tags/merge fields; Omnisend adds predicted behavior (e.g., 'likely to churn').

**Tip**: Segment 'VIP customers' (3+ purchases) for exclusive offers to increase loyalty.

### Omnichannel Features

Mailchimp: Basic SMS add-on ($10+/mo extra).

Omnisend: Native SMS, web push—send push notifications for 8x higher open rates than email.

**Winner**: Omnisend for multi-channel.

## Integrations: Connecting Your Tech Stack

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier, Shopify).

Omnisend: 100+ focused on ecommerce (Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Klaviyo alternatives). Native app for quick setup.

**Practical Advice**: For Shopify users, Omnisend syncs orders/inventory automatically. Mailchimp requires more setup via Zapier. Prioritize based on your CRM/CMS.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp: 4.5/5 on G2 for beginners. Guided tours, vast help center.

Omnisend: Slightly steeper for non-ecom, but ecommerce wizards make setup fast (under 15 mins).

**Tip**: Watch Omnisend's Shopify app demo; Mailchimp's for general lists.

## Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp: Revenue tracking, A/B testing, detailed reports.

Omnisend: Ecommerce-specific ROI (e.g., attributed revenue per campaign), heatmaps for popups.

**Practical Advice**: Track 'revenue per recipient' in Omnisend to justify spend—aim for $0.50+ per email sent.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for paid; chat for higher tiers.

Omnisend: 24/7 live chat, dedicated manager for Pro+.

**Winner**: Omnisend for responsive help.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend?

- Ecommerce stores (Shopify, Woo)
- Need SMS/push
- Focus on automations/cart recovery

Example: A dropshipping store recovered $5K/mo in carts after switching (user-reported averages).

**Action Step**: Integrate with your store, run a 14-day trial workflow.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Bloggers, agencies, non-profits
- Simple newsletters
- Budget-conscious starters

**Action Step**: Build your first audience segment today.

## Final Verdict: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

No one-size-fits-all. **Pick Mailchimp** for ease and free growth. **Choose Omnisend** for ecommerce revenue boosts.

Test both: Export a CSV list, migrate in minutes. Monitor metrics like open rates (target 25%+), click-through (3%+), and revenue.

Whichever you choose, consistent testing wins. Start small, scale with data.
