# Best Email Marketing Software in 2024: Top 10 Picks for Growing Businesses

Choosing the right email marketing software is crucial for businesses looking to nurture leads, boost sales, and retain customers. With dozens of platforms available, the best email marketing software balances ease of use, powerful automation, deliverability rates, and affordable pricing. In this roundup, we'll cover the top 10 options based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, key features to prioritize, and practical advice for selecting the ideal tool for your needs.

Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or enterprise team, these picks cater to various budgets and scales. All data is drawn from official websites and recent reviews as of 2024—no hype, just facts.

## What Makes the Best Email Marketing Software?

Before diving into the list, understand the core criteria for top-tier platforms:

### Essential Features
- **Drag-and-Drop Editors**: Intuitive builders for creating responsive emails without coding.
- **Automation Workflows**: Trigger-based sequences for welcome series, abandoned carts, and re-engagement.
- **Segmentation and Personalization**: Target lists by behavior, location, or purchase history.
- **Analytics and Reporting**: Track open rates (industry avg. 21-25%), clicks (2-3%), and ROI.
- **Integrations**: Connect with Shopify, WordPress, Zapier, CRM like HubSpot.
- **Deliverability**: Aim for 95%+ inbox placement; check tools like GlockApps for testing.

### Pricing Factors
Most offer freemium tiers. Paid plans scale by contacts/subscribers:
- Starter: $10-50/month (up to 1,000 subs)
- Pro: $50-200/month (5,000-10,000 subs)
- Enterprise: Custom (50,000+ subs)

Practical tip: Start with free trials (14-30 days) and import a test list to evaluate workflows.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Platforms

Here's our curated list, ranked by versatility, user ratings (4.5+ on G2), and value.

### 1. Mailchimp

The OG of email marketing, Mailchimp powers 13M+ users. Free for up to 500 contacts/1,000 sends/month.

**Pros**:
- Beginner-friendly editor and 200+ templates.
- Built-in CRM and audience insights.
- E-commerce integrations (Shopify, WooCommerce).

**Cons**:
- Automation limited on free/basic plans.
- Pricing jumps at higher tiers (Essentials: $13/mo for 5,000 subs).

**Best for**: Small businesses, nonprofits. Pricing: Free-$299+/mo.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

Leads with advanced automation (4.6/5 on G2). Starts at $29/mo.

**Pros**:
- Machine learning for predictive sending.
- 900+ integrations, deep CRM features.
- Site tracking for behavioral emails.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve.
- No free plan (15-day trial).

**Best for**: Agencies, B2B marketers. Pricing: $29-$149+/mo.

### 3. Klaviyo

E-commerce powerhouse (4.7/5 G2), free up to 250 contacts.

**Pros**:
- Revenue-focused flows (e.g., 30% lift in AOV reported).
- Shopify native integration.
- Advanced segments like 'viewed product X'.

**Cons**:
- Less ideal for non-ecom.
- Costs rise fast ($20-$700+/mo).

**Best for**: Online stores. Pricing: Free-$2,000+/mo.

### 4. ConvertKit (now Kit)

Creator-focused, free up to 300 subs.

**Pros**:
- Visual automations, tagging system.
- Landing pages and forms included.
- Transparent pricing.

**Cons**:
- Basic reporting.
- Templates need customization.

**Best for**: Bloggers, course creators. Pricing: Free-$29+/mo.

### 5. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars (4.3/5 G2). Free up to 500 subs.

**Pros**:
- Conversion funnels, A/B testing.
- Landing pages, funnels builder.
- High deliverability (99%).

**Cons**:
- Interface feels dated.

**Best for**: SMBs needing extras. Pricing: Free-$49+/mo.

### 6. Constant Contact

User-friendly for beginners (4.4/5 G2). $12/mo starter.

**Pros**:
- AI content generator.
- Event management.
- Strong support (phone/chat).

**Cons**:
- Limited automations.

**Best for**: Local businesses. Pricing: $12-$80+/mo.

### 7. AWeber

Reliable since 1998, free up to 500 subs.

**Pros**:
- Excellent deliverability.
- Tag-based automation.
- 700+ apps via Zapier.

**Cons**:
- Editor lags modern competitors.

**Best for**: Long-term users. Pricing: Free-$19+/mo.

### 8. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Transactional + marketing emails. Free 300/day.

**Pros**:
- SMS integration.
- Custom pricing for high volume.
- EU GDPR compliant.

**Cons**:
- Daily send limits on free.

**Best for**: International teams. Pricing: Free-$25+/mo.

### 9. Drip

E-com automation specialist. $39/mo.

**Pros**:
- Customer journey builder.
- Revenue tracking per campaign.

**Cons**:
- Pricey for starters.

**Best for**: DTC brands.

### 10. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free with CRM (4.4/5 G2).

**Pros**:
- Unlimited sends for 2M contacts (paid lists extra).
- Full inbound suite.

**Cons**:
- Upsell-heavy.

**Best for**: Growing teams using HubSpot.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing Software for You

1. **Assess Your Needs**: E-com? Pick Klaviyo/Drip. B2B? ActiveCampaign.
2. **Test Deliverability**: Use Google Postmaster Tools post-signup.
3. **Budget Check**: Calculate per-subscriber cost (e.g., Mailchimp ~$0.03/sub at scale).
4. **Scale Potential**: Ensure API limits support growth.
5. **Compliance**: Look for CAN-SPAM/GDPR tools.

Practical advice: Migrate with CSV exports. A/B test subject lines (e.g., emoji vs. plain boosts opens 10-20%). Segment lists quarterly to maintain 30%+ open rates.

## Conclusion

The best email marketing software depends on your audience size, goals, and tech savvy. Mailchimp suits starters, while ActiveCampaign or Klaviyo excel for advanced users.
