# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a veteran in the space, known for its beginner-friendly interface and broad appeal. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines for ecommerce businesses with advanced omnichannel features like SMS and push notifications.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down key aspects like pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and support. Whether you're a small business owner, ecommerce store manager, or marketer scaling up, you'll get practical advice to decide which platform fits your goals. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple since 2001, powering email campaigns for millions. It's ideal for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams starting with email marketing.

**Key Strengths:**
- Intuitive drag-and-drop editor.
- Forever-free plan for up to 500 contacts.
- Built-in audience insights and A/B testing.

However, as your list grows, costs rise quickly, and advanced ecommerce automations require higher tiers. Practical tip: Start with Mailchimp's free plan to test basic newsletters, but monitor contact growth to avoid surprises.

## Overview of Omnisend

Launched in 2014, Omnisend targets ecommerce brands, integrating seamlessly with Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce. It emphasizes automation for sales funnels like abandoned cart recovery.

**Key Strengths:**
- Omnichannel campaigns (email, SMS, web push).
- Free plan for up to 250 contacts with 500 emails/month.
- Pre-built workflows for ecommerce events.

Omnisend's focus on revenue recovery makes it a go-to for online stores. Advice: If 70% of your traffic is from an online store, prioritize Omnisend for its popup builders and segmentation tools.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major decider. Both offer free tiers, but scales differently.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic features.
- **Essentials:** $13/mo (5,000 contacts), adds automation.
- **Standard:** $20/mo (5,000), behavioral targeting.
- **Premium:** $350/mo (10,000+), advanced reporting.

Overages for sends/contacts add up fast. For 10,000 contacts with high volume, expect $100+ monthly.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free:** 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, full features.
- **Standard:** $16/mo (500 contacts), unlimited emails.
- **Pro:** $59/mo (500), SMS/push included.
- **Custom:** Enterprise-level.

Omnisend bills by contacts, not sends—better for frequent campaigns. Practical advice: Calculate your monthly sends. If under 15,000 emails to 2,500 contacts, Omnisend Standard ($20/mo equivalent) beats Mailchimp's $45 Essentials.

| Feature | Mailchimp (10k contacts) | Omnisend (10k contacts) |
|---------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| Starting Price | ~$100/mo | ~$105/mo |
| Free Tier | Yes | Yes |
| SMS Included | Premium only | Pro+ |

Winner: Omnisend for ecommerce volume; Mailchimp for low-volume starters.

## Feature Face-Off: Core Capabilities

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's editor is polished with 100+ responsive templates. Omnisend offers 250+ ecommerce-specific ones, plus dynamic product blocks.

**Advice:** Use Mailchimp for content-heavy newsletters; Omnisend for product catalogs.

### Automation Workflows
- **Mailchimp:** Single-step automations in Essentials; customer journeys in Standard+.
- **Omnisend:** Visual builder with multi-channel (e.g., email → SMS fallback). Pre-made flows for welcome series, carts, post-purchase.

Omnisend recovers 15-30% more carts on average (per their data). Tip: Set up abandoned cart automation first—test with a 10% discount trigger.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both segment by behavior/location. Omnisend excels with product-based segments (e.g., 'viewed shoes'). Mailchimp's merge tags are solid but less dynamic.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp: Revenue per email, opens/clicks. Omnisend: Attributed revenue, SMS ROI.

**Pro Tip:** Track ecommerce revenue attribution. Omnisend links sales directly to campaigns.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Interface

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners—setup in minutes, no learning curve. Omnisend requires some ecommerce knowledge but offers guided tours.

**Practical Advice:** Spend 30 minutes on each free trial. Import a test list (100 contacts) and build a workflow. If drag-and-drop feels clunky in Omnisend, stick to Mailchimp.

## Integrations: Connecting Your Tech Stack

- **Mailchimp:** 300+ apps (Google Analytics, WordPress, Zapier). Ecommerce via plugins.
- **Omnisend:** Native Shopify/Woo/Magento; 140+ via Zapier. Stronger for Klaviyo migrants.

Both support Facebook/Google ads. Advice: Check your CMS—Omnisend auto-syncs orders from Shopify; Mailchimp needs manual tags.

## Customer Support and Resources

Mailchimp: 24/7 email/chat (paid), huge knowledge base. Omnisend: Priority chat/email (Pro+), live webinars, migration help.

Ecommerce users praise Omnisend's responsive team. Tip: Join their Facebook group for peer tips.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros
- Free plan generosity.
- Creative tools (landing pages).
- Brand recognition.

**Cons:** Expensive scaling; limited SMS.

### Omnisend Pros
- Ecommerce automations.
- Omnichannel.
- Cost-effective sends.

**Cons:** Steeper for non-ecom; fewer templates.

## Which to Choose? Decision Framework

- **Choose Mailchimp if:** Blogging, services, <5k contacts, budget-tight start.
- **Choose Omnisend if:** Ecommerce store, need SMS/push, revenue-focused automations.

**Quick Quiz:**
1. Do you sell products online? → Omnisend.
2. Need simple newsletters? → Mailchimp.
3. Scaling to 10k+? Compare trials.

Run A/B tests post-signup: Send identical campaigns and measure open/click/revenue rates.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce with superior automations and multichannel support, while Mailchimp remains king for versatile, easy-entry marketing. Neither is 'best' universally—test both free plans for 14 days.

For most growing online stores, Omnisend's ROI tools justify the switch. Track metrics like cart recovery rate (aim for 10-20%) and lifetime value.
