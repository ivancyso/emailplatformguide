# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: In-Depth Comparison

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. For creators, small businesses, and marketers deciding between MailerLite and ConvertKit, this head-to-head comparison breaks down key factors like pricing, features, usability, and support. Both tools excel in automation and segmentation, but they cater to slightly different users. MailerLite shines for budget-conscious beginners with its free plan, while ConvertKit targets content creators with advanced tagging and visual automations.

We'll explore practical advice on setup, campaigns, and scaling, helping you pick the winner for your workflow.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer transparent, subscriber-based plans without hidden fees, but their structures differ.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite starts with a generous **free plan** for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month—ideal for testing without commitment. Paid plans begin at $10/month (billed annually) for 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 1,000, $50 for 2,500, and higher tiers up to enterprise levels.

- **Growing Business**: $9–$25/month (1k–2.5k subs)
- **Advanced**: $18–$50/month (adds A/B testing, webhooks)

No contracts; monthly billing available at a premium. Practical tip: Start free, upgrade seamlessly as your list grows. It's cost-effective for solopreneurs under 5,000 subs.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) has no perpetual free plan—only a 14-day trial. The **Creator** plan starts at $15/month (300 subs, annually), jumping to $29 for 1,000 subs, $79 for 3,000, and $2,500+ for massive lists.

- **Creator**: $15–$2,500/month (basic automations)
- **Creator Pro**: $25–$4,500/month (advanced reporting, one-click upsells)

Annual discounts save 20%. Advice: If you're a podcaster or blogger with 500+ subs, the tagging system justifies the cost; otherwise, it feels steep for startups.

**Winner**: MailerLite for affordability, especially under 2,000 subs. ConvertKit if you need pro features early.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Both have intuitive dashboards, but onboarding varies.

### MailerLite User Experience

Drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly with 80+ responsive templates. Setup takes minutes: connect domain, import contacts via CSV/Google Sheets, and launch. Mobile app for iOS/Android handles campaigns on-the-go.

Practical advice: Use the checklist wizard for first campaign—import, segment, design, send. JSON imports and API access suit tech-savvy users.

### ConvertKit User Experience

Clean interface with a focus on sequences and tags. Visual automation builder (like a flowchart) is powerful but has a learning curve. Forms, landing pages, and subscriber scores are native.

Tip: Tag-based segmentation (e.g., 'lead-magnet-downloader') automates personalization without lists. Great for creators; import from Mailchimp easily.

**Winner**: MailerLite for sheer simplicity; ConvertKit for visual thinkers.

## Core Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite's editor offers blocks for images, buttons, and RSS feeds. Unlimited images/storage. ConvertKit emphasizes commerce with product recommendations and sellable landing pages.

Advice: Test A/B subjects in both (MailerLite free in advanced plans). Use MailerLite for newsletters, ConvertKit for courses.

### Automations and Segmentation

- **MailerLite**: Trigger-based (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart). Basic tags/segments by behavior/location.
- **ConvertKit**: Superior visual automations, subscriber scoring, and tag triggers. Integrates with Zapier deeply.

Practical: For e-commerce, ConvertKit's one-click upsells shine; bloggers love MailerLite's popup A/B testing.

### Landing Pages and Forms

Both include unlimited pages/forms. MailerLite adds popups, countdown timers. ConvertKit excels in incentive forms (e.g., 'subscribe for free chapter').

Tip: Embed forms on WordPress/Webflow—both support it seamlessly.

### Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite: Open/click rates, geography, device breakdowns. Heatmaps in pro plans.
ConvertKit: Revenue tracking, broadcast performance, tag analytics.

**Pro Tip**: Track ROI by linking UTM tags; both handle it well.

### Integrations

Over 1,000 each via Zapier. MailerLite: WooCommerce, Google Analytics. ConvertKit: Teachable, Patreon—creator heaven.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Fast responses (under 1 hour).
ConvertKit: Email/ticket only (no live chat), but extensive academy/videos. Community forum active.

Advice: MailerLite for urgent help; ConvertKit's docs suffice for most.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: You're a beginner, small business, or budget-tight (under $20/month). Perfect for newsletters, lead gen, e-com basics.
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Blogger/podcaster/creator needing sequences, tags, and sales funnels. Scales for 6-figure revenue.

Practical migration advice: Export CSV from one, import to other. Test both trials/free plans side-by-side.

## Conclusion

MailerLite edges out for most users with its free entry and simplicity, saving hundreds yearly while delivering 90% of needed features. ConvertKit pulls ahead for serious creators investing in automations and monetization—its tools pay for themselves via higher conversions.

Ultimately, audit your needs: list size, budget, tech comfort. Start with MailerLite if unsure; switch to ConvertKit at 1,000+ engaged subs. Both deliver 99% deliverability and GDPR compliance. Whichever you choose, focus on value-driven content— that's the real growth hack.
