# Best Email Marketing Software: Top 10 Picks for Businesses in 2024

Email marketing remains one of the most effective digital strategies, with average ROI around $36 for every $1 spent. For businesses choosing email marketing platforms, selecting the best email marketing software is crucial. It needs to handle list building, automation, segmentation, and analytics without overwhelming your team.

This roundup evaluates the best email marketing software based on real-world criteria: ease of use, automation capabilities, template libraries, deliverability rates, integrations, pricing, and customer support. We focus on platforms suitable for solopreneurs, small businesses, and enterprises. All recommendations draw from established features as of 2024—no hype, just practical insights to help you decide.

## Key Factors to Consider When Choosing Email Marketing Software

Before diving into the top picks, understand what matters most.

### Ease of Use and Onboarding

Look for drag-and-drop editors and intuitive dashboards. Platforms with guided tours or free resources reduce setup time. For beginners, prioritize no-coding requirements.

### Automation and Segmentation

Advanced automation workflows (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart) save hours. Segmentation by behavior, location, or purchase history boosts open rates by 14.31% on average.

### Deliverability and Compliance

High inbox placement is non-negotiable. Check for built-in GDPR/CCPA tools, spam testing, and authentication (DKIM/SPF).

### Integrations and Scalability

Seamless connections to CRMs (Salesforce), e-commerce (Shopify), and analytics (Google Analytics) are essential. Ensure it scales with your list size without performance dips.

### Pricing and Free Tiers

Most offer freemium plans. Evaluate cost per subscriber or contacts, plus add-ons for SMS or landing pages.

### Analytics and Reporting

Track opens, clicks, conversions, and revenue per email. A/B testing tools help optimize campaigns.

Practical tip: Start with free trials. Import a test list of 500 contacts and build a sample automation to gauge fit.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Compared

Here's our curated list of the best email marketing software, ranked by versatility, value, and user feedback from sources like G2 and Capterra.

### 1. Mailchimp

**Best for beginners and small businesses.**

Mailchimp powers millions with its all-in-one platform. Key features: 200+ templates, audience segmentation, behavioral automations, and ecommerce integrations.

**Pros:** Free plan up to 500 contacts; excellent templates; creative studio for landing pages.

**Cons:** Automation limited on free/basic plans; pricing jumps at higher tiers.

**Pricing:** Free; Essentials $13/mo (5k contacts); Standard $20/mo.

Ideal if you're starting out. Tip: Use their pre-built journeys for quick wins.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

**Best for marketing automation.**

Deep automation with CRM integration sets it apart. Features: Site tracking, lead scoring, machine learning splits, 900+ integrations.

**Pros:** Powerful if/then logic; split testing; robust reporting.

**Cons:** Steeper learning curve; no free plan.

**Pricing:** Lite $29/mo (1k contacts); Plus $49/mo.

Practical advice: Map customer journeys first—ActiveCampaign shines here for mid-sized teams.

### 3. ConvertKit (now Kit)

**Best for creators and bloggers.**

Creator-focused with unlimited emails, visual automations, and subscriber tagging.

**Pros:** Simple pricing; commerce tools; broadcast scheduling.

**Cons:** Fewer templates; basic analytics.

**Pricing:** Free up to 300 subscribers; Creator $15/mo.

Tip: Tag-based segmentation simplifies newsletters for solopreneurs.

### 4. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for transactional + marketing emails.**

Multichannel (email, SMS, chat) with strong deliverability.

**Pros:** Unlimited contacts on paid plans; free SMS credits; API flexibility.

**Cons:** Interface feels dated; limited templates.

**Pricing:** Free (300 emails/day); Starter $25/mo.

Advice: Pair with transactional APIs for e-commerce stores.

### 5. GetResponse

**Best for webinars and sales funnels.**

All-in-one with landing pages, funnels, and webinar hosting.

**Pros:** Conversion funnel builder; AI email generator; high deliverability.

**Cons:** Overwhelming for simple needs.

**Pricing:** Free (500 contacts); Email Marketing $19/mo.

Tip: Use funnel analytics to optimize high-ticket sales.

### 6. Klaviyo

**Best for e-commerce (Shopify users).**

Data-driven flows with revenue tracking.

**Pros:** Predictive analytics; dynamic product recommendations; Shopify native.

**Cons:** Expensive for non-ecom; complex setup.

**Pricing:** Free up to 250 contacts; scales with revenue.

Practical: Review flow performance weekly to cut churn.

### 7. Constant Contact

**Best for nonprofits and events.**

User-friendly with event management and donations.

**Pros:** AI recommendations; social ads; 24/7 support.

**Cons:** No automation on basic plan; higher cost.

**Pricing:** Lite $12/mo (500 contacts).

Tip: Leverage their contact importer for quick list growth.

### 8. AWeber

**Best for reliable deliverability.**

Veteran platform with strong tagging and RSS campaigns.

**Pros:** Forever free trial; landing pages; high open rates.

**Cons:** Dated design; limited automations.

**Pricing:** Lite $12.50/mo (500 subscribers).

Advice: Test RSS-to-email for consistent content.

### 9. HubSpot Marketing Hub

**Best for enterprises with CRM.**

Free CRM bundled with email tools.

**Pros:** Full inbound suite; personalization; team collaboration.

**Cons:** Costly upgrades; bloated for solos.

**Pricing:** Free tools; Starter $20/mo.

Tip: Sync with sales pipelines for closed-loop reporting.

### 10. Drip

**Best for advanced e-commerce workflows.**

Behavioral automation powerhouse.

**Pros:** Customer journey builder; A/B testing; segmentation.

**Cons:** Pricey; e-com focused.

**Pricing:** Starts at $39/mo (500 subscribers).

Practical: Use UTM tracking for multi-channel attribution.

## How to Choose and Implement the Best Email Marketing Software

1. **Assess Needs:** List size? E-com? Team size?
2. **Trial Multiple:** Export lists to 2-3 platforms.
3. **Migrate Smartly:** Use CSV imports; clean lists first (remove bounces).
4. **Optimize Campaigns:** A/B test subjects (aim 20-30% opens); personalize.
5. **Measure ROI:** Track beyond opens—focus on conversions.

Pro tip: Compliance first—add double opt-in to build quality lists.
