# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. For creators, bloggers, small businesses, and solopreneurs, MailerLite and ConvertKit stand out as popular choices. Both offer powerful features like automation, beautiful templates, and segmentation, but they cater to slightly different needs.

MailerLite is known for its affordability and beginner-friendly interface, with a generous free plan. ConvertKit, on the other hand, is creator-centric, emphasizing tagging, sequences, and monetization tools. In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're starting with a small list or scaling to thousands, read on for practical insights.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms use subscriber-based tiers, but MailerLite edges out with its free plan.

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Ideal for testing or small lists—no credit card required.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 1,000, $30 for 2,500, etc. Annual discounts available (save 20-30%).
- **Advanced**: From $20/month, adds A/B testing, web push notifications, and priority support.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for large lists.

MailerLite keeps costs low even as you grow, making it budget-friendly.

### ConvertKit Pricing (Now Kit)
ConvertKit rebranded pricing tiers to Free, Creator, Creator Pro, etc.
- **Free Plan**: Up to 10,000 subscribers (limited forms and broadcasts). Great for beginners.
- **Creator**: $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, $29 for 1,000, $49 for 3,000.
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month base, adds advanced automation, revenue tracking.
- Annual billing saves 20%.

**Practical Advice**: If you're under 1,000 subscribers, start with MailerLite's free plan to build habits without cost. ConvertKit's free tier suits creators needing tagging early. Calculate your needs: Estimate monthly sends (e.g., weekly newsletters = 4x list size) and project growth. Always factor in overage fees—MailerLite charges extra sends at $0.001/email.

## Feature Breakdown: Core Capabilities

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer drag-and-drop editors.
- **MailerLite**: Intuitive block-based builder with 80+ responsive templates. Strong photo editing tools built-in.
- **ConvertKit**: Clean visual editor, 100+ templates optimized for creators. Commerce blocks for selling digital products directly.

**Tip**: Preview on mobile—MailerLite's editor shines for quick customizations.

### Automation and Workflows
Automation is key for nurturing leads.
- **MailerLite**: Visual automation builder for welcome series, abandoned carts, birthdays. Triggers include opens, clicks, tags.
- **ConvertKit**: Excels with Sequences (drip campaigns) and Visual Automations. Tag-based triggering is powerful for segmentation without lists.

**Practical Advice**: For bloggers, ConvertKit's tagging (e.g., 'lead magnet downloader') automates personalized paths better. Use MailerLite for simple e-commerce flows. Test with 10-20 subscribers first.

### Landing Pages and Forms
- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, popups, forms on free plan. Website builder included in paid plans.
- **ConvertKit**: Forms, landing pages, checkouts. Native video embeds and link sharing.

ConvertKit wins for product launches; MailerLite for lead gen popups.

### Segmentation and Analytics
- **MailerLite**: Groups, tags, custom fields. Opens rates, clicks, revenue tracking.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tagging, subscriber scoring, A/B testing on subjects/lines.

**Tip**: Segment by engagement—send re-engagement campaigns to inactive subs (e.g., 'We miss you' after 90 days no open).

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

MailerLite feels more accessible for non-techies: 5-minute signup, guided tours.
ConvertKit has a steeper curve but excellent tutorials for creators.

**Practical Advice**: Import CSV lists carefully—map fields correctly to avoid bounces. Both support Zapier for 1,000+ integrations (e.g., connect to WordPress, Shopify).

## Customer Support and Deliverability

- **Support**: MailerLite offers live chat (paid), email (free). ConvertKit: Email only, but responsive with creator-focused help docs.
- **Deliverability**: Both >95%. MailerLite authenticates automatically; ConvertKit advises custom tracking domains.

**Tip**: Warm up new domains with 50-100 sends/day. Monitor spam complaints.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
Small businesses, e-commerce stores, beginners needing free tools and popups. If budget < $20/mo and list <5k, go MailerLite.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Bloggers, podcasters, course creators valuing tagging and monetization. Ideal for lists 1k+ focused on long-term nurturing.

**Migration Tip**: Export subscribers as CSV from old tool. Both import easily. Run parallel for 1-2 weeks to compare deliverability.
