# 10 Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Top Email Marketing Platforms Compared

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but rising costs, limited automation for free users, and a shift toward enterprise features have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're a small business owner, freelancer, or marketer tired of bloated pricing tiers, this roundup compares the top options based on real user needs like affordability, ease of use, automation, and deliverability.

We'll break down 10 solid alternatives, highlighting key features, pricing, pros, and cons. Plus, practical tips on migrating and choosing the right one. All info is based on official sites and common user feedback as of 2024—no hype, just facts.

## Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 subscribers and 1,000 emails/month, with paid plans starting at $13/month but jumping quickly (e.g., $75+ for 5,000 contacts). Advanced automations and reporting require higher tiers. Users often switch for:

- **Better pricing**: Forever-free plans or lower costs per contact.
- **Superior automation**: Deeper workflows without upselling.
- **E-commerce focus**: Integrations for Shopify/ WooCommerce users.
- **Beginner-friendly interfaces**: Less overwhelming than Mailchimp's recent redesigns.

Practical advice: Audit your current Mailchimp usage. Export lists via CSV, note key automations, and test 2-3 alternatives with free trials before migrating.

## Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)
Brevo offers transactional emails, SMS, and marketing automation in one platform. Free plan: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day.

**Key Features**:
- Drag-and-drop editor.
- Automation workflows (e.g., abandoned cart).
- CRM integration.

**Pricing**: Free; paid from $25/month (20k emails).

**Pros**: Generous free tier, high deliverability (98%+ claimed), multi-channel.
**Cons**: Editor less template-rich than Mailchimp.

**Best for**: SMBs needing SMS alongside email. Migrate by importing CSV and remapping automations—takes ~1 hour.

### 2. ConvertKit
Creator-focused with tagging over segments. Free up to 300 subscribers.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automations.
- Landing pages/forms included.
- Subscriber scoring.

**Pricing**: Free; Creator $15/month (300 subs), up to $29 for 1k.

**Pros**: Simple for newsletters, strong tagging.
**Cons**: No built-in A/B testing.

**Best for**: Bloggers/influencers. Practical tip: Use tags for personalized sequences—export Mailchimp segments as tags during import.

### 3. ActiveCampaign
Advanced automation powerhouse. No forever-free, but 14-day trial.

**Key Features**:
- Machine learning site tracking.
- 700+ integrations (e.g., WordPress).
- Split testing.

**Pricing**: $29/month (500 contacts).

**Pros**: Deep personalization, CRM included.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

**Best for**: E-commerce with complex funnels. Advice: Start with their automation blueprint templates to replicate Mailchimp journeys.

### 4. Klaviyo
E-commerce specialist, Shopify-native.

**Key Features**n- Predictive analytics.
- Flow builder for SMS/email.
- Revenue tracking.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; scales with revenue.

**Pros**: ROI-focused metrics, A/B testing.
**Cons**: Less ideal for non-ecom.

**Best for**: Online stores. Tip: Connect Shopify first—Klaviyo auto-syncs data, easing Mailchimp migration.

### 5. GetResponse
All-in-one with webinars/landing pages. Free 30-day trial.

**Key Features**:
- Conversion funnels.
- Autoresponders.
- E-com tools.

**Pricing**: $19/month (1k subs).

**Pros**: Webinar integration, affordable.
**Cons**: Interface dated.

**Best for**: Lead gen. Migrate tip: Use their importer wizard for seamless list transfer.

### 6. AWeber
Veteran platform, simple and reliable. Free up to 500 subs.

**Key Features**:
- Tag-based automation.
- 700+ apps.

**Pricing**: Free; $19.99/month unlimited.

**Pros**: Loyal support, high deliverability.
**Cons**: Basic reporting.

**Best for**: Beginners. Advice: Their setup wizard guides new users post-Mailchimp switch.

### 7. Constant Contact
User-friendly for non-profits/SMBs. No free plan, 60-day trial.

**Key Features**:
- Event management.
- Social ads.

**Pricing**: $12/month (500 contacts).

**Pros**: AI builder, easy templates.
**Cons**: Pricey for large lists.

**Best for**: Events. Tip: Test AI content generator for quick campaign starts.

### 8. Moosend
Affordable automation with landing pages.

**Key Features**:
- Behavioral triggers.
- A/B testing.

**Pricing**: Free up to 1k subs; $9/month paid.

**Pros**: Unlimited emails on free, fast support.
**Cons**: Fewer integrations.

**Best for**: Startups. Practical: Unlimited sends mean no overage worries.

### 9. MailerLite
Drag-and-drop simplicity. Free up to 1k subs/12k emails/month.

**Key Features**:
- Popups/websites.
- Automation blocks.

**Pricing**: Free; $10/month (500 subs).

**Pros**: Clean UI, generous free.
**Cons**: No SMS.

**Best for**: Solopreneurs. Tip: Build websites in-app to replace Mailchimp landing pages.

### 10. Omnisend
Omnichannel for e-com (email/SMS/push).

**Key Features**:
- Pre-built workflows.
- Product recommendations.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; $16/month.

**Pros**: E-com focus, templates.
**Cons**: Limited non-ecom use.

**Best for**: DTC brands. Advice: Use workflow simulator before launch.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Match your needs**: E-com? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Creators? ConvertKit. Budget? Brevo/MailerLite.
2. **Check deliverability**: Look for 95%+ rates (all above do).
3. **Trial stack**: Sign up for 3-5 free trials, import a test list (100 subs).
4. **Migration steps**:
   - Export Mailchimp audience as CSV (Audience > Export).
   - Import to new tool (most have wizards).
   - Recreate 2-3 top automations.
   - Update website forms (via embed code).
   - Test sends to yourself.
5. **Compliance**: Ensure GDPR tools like double opt-in.

Budget tip: Aim for $10-30/month for 1k-5k subs across most.

## Conclusion

Switching from Mailchimp doesn't have to be daunting—these **Mailchimp alternatives** offer better value, features tailored to niches, and smoother experiences. Brevo and MailerLite shine for free tiers, while ActiveCampaign and Klaviyo excel in power. Start with a trial, migrate methodically, and track open rates post-switch (expect 20-30% uplift with better tools).
