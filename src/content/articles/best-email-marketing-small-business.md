Small businesses need email marketing that's affordable, easy to use, and effective. You don't need enterprise features — you need something that lets you stay in touch with customers, promote your services, and grow your list without spending hours learning complex software.

Here are the best options for small businesses in 2026.

## What is the best email marketing platform for small business?

The best email marketing platform for small business is MailerLite, which combines an intuitive drag-and-drop editor, generous free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers, and paid plans starting at just $9/month. AWeber is a strong alternative for established businesses that prioritize phone support and proven deliverability.

## How much should a small business spend on email marketing?

Most small businesses can start email marketing for free and should budget $9-50/month as their list grows. A good benchmark is allocating 5-10% of your total marketing budget to email. The return on investment is typically excellent, with email marketing averaging $36-42 in revenue for every $1 spent.

## What features do small businesses need in email marketing software?

Small businesses should prioritize ease of use, affordable scalable pricing, basic automation like welcome sequences and abandoned cart emails, customizable sign-up forms, and strong deliverability rates. Avoid overpaying for enterprise features you will not use, as they add unnecessary complexity and cost to your marketing stack.

## 1. MailerLite — Best Overall for Small Business

[MailerLite](/mailerlite-review) is our top recommendation for most small businesses. It offers the best balance of features, ease of use, and pricing.

**Why small businesses love it:**
- Free plan covers up to 1,000 subscribers
- Paid plans start at just $9/month
- Drag-and-drop email builder is genuinely easy to use
- Includes landing pages and a basic website builder
- Automation is good enough for most small business needs
- Deliverability rates are consistently high

**The catch:** Advanced features like auto-resend and multivariate testing are only on higher plans.

**Best for:** Solopreneurs, local businesses, and early-stage companies that need a professional email presence without the premium price.

## 2. AWeber — Best for Established Small Businesses

If your business has been around for a while and you value reliability over flashy features, [AWeber](/aweber-review) is a strong choice.

**Why small businesses love it:**
- 700+ email templates for any industry
- Phone support available (rare in this price range)
- Smart Designer creates branded templates automatically
- Proven deliverability over 25+ years

**The catch:** The interface feels older than newer competitors, and automation isn't as powerful as [ActiveCampaign](/activecampaign-vs-mailchimp).

**Best for:** Established businesses that want proven reliability, strong support, and professional templates.

## 3. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — Best for Multi-Channel

Brevo stands out by combining email marketing with SMS, WhatsApp, and live chat in a single platform. For small businesses that communicate through multiple channels, this is a significant advantage.

**Why small businesses love it:**
- Email + SMS + WhatsApp + live chat in one platform
- Free plan includes 300 emails per day
- Built-in CRM for contact management
- Transactional email support included
- Pay-by-email pricing (not by subscriber count)

**The catch:** The email builder isn't as polished as MailerLite or Mailchimp.

**Best for:** Businesses that need multi-channel communication and want to consolidate tools.

## 4. Kit (ConvertKit) — Best for Service Businesses with Content

If your small business relies on content marketing -- blogging, newsletters, or educational content -- [Kit's](/convertkit-review) creator-focused approach works well for service businesses too.

**Why small businesses love it:**
- Excellent automation for lead nurturing
- Free plan up to 1,000 subscribers
- Commerce features for selling services/digital products
- Clean interface that's quick to learn

**The catch:** Limited email design options and higher pricing at scale.

**Best for:** Consultants, coaches, and service businesses that use content to attract clients.

## What Small Businesses Should Look For

Before choosing a platform, consider:

1. **List size:** Most platforms offer free plans up to 500-1,000 subscribers. If you're just starting, free is fine.
2. **Budget:** Costs scale with your subscriber count. Plan for growth.
3. **Features you actually need:** Most small businesses need good templates, basic automation, and reliable delivery. Don't pay for features you won't use.
4. **Ease of use:** If you're not technically savvy, prioritize platforms with intuitive interfaces and good support.
5. **Integration:** Make sure it connects with your existing tools (website, CRM, ecommerce platform). If you run an ecommerce store, consider a specialized platform like [Klaviyo](/klaviyo-review) or [Omnisend](/omnisend-review).
