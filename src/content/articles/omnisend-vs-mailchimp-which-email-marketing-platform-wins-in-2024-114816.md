# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Omnisend vs Mailchimp head-to-head helps clarify which tool suits your needs. Both are popular choices for email marketing, but they cater to slightly different audiences. Mailchimp is a veteran in the space, known for its beginner-friendly interface, while Omnisend shines in ecommerce automation.

This comparison dives into pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more, based on official documentation and user-reported experiences as of 2024. Whether you're a small business owner, ecommerce entrepreneur, or marketer scaling up, we'll help you decide.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when evaluating email marketing tools. Both Omnisend and Mailchimp offer free plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends, ideal for testing. Paid plans start at $13/month for Essentials (up to 500 contacts, 5,000 sends), scaling to Premium at $350+/month for advanced features like multivariate testing. Pricing is contact-based, which can get expensive quickly as your list grows. For example, 10,000 contacts on Standard plan costs around $100/month.

### Omnisend Pricing
Omnisend's free plan allows 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, with unlimited SMS credits—a boon for omnichannel testing. Paid plans begin at $16/month for Standard (up to 500 contacts), up to Pro at $59+/month. Like Mailchimp, it's contact-tiered, but Omnisend includes more ecommerce features in base plans. For 10,000 contacts, expect $59/month on Standard.

**Practical Advice:** If you're just starting with a small list, Mailchimp's free tier edges out with more sends. For ecommerce stores planning SMS or popups, Omnisend's free plan offers better multichannel access. Always calculate based on your expected list size and send volume—use their pricing calculators for accuracy.

## Core Features: Automation, Segmentation, and Templates

Both platforms excel in email marketing essentials, but Omnisend pulls ahead in ecommerce-specific tools.

### Email Automation
Mailchimp offers robust automations like welcome series, abandoned cart (via integrations), and behavioral triggers. Its automation builder is drag-and-drop, but complex workflows require higher tiers.

Omnisend specializes in pre-built ecommerce workflows: abandoned cart, back-in-stock, cross-sell, and win-back campaigns. Its automation editor is visual and includes SMS/push notifications natively, making it more powerful for online stores without extra setup.

**Practical Tip:** Test automations with a small segment first. For Mailchimp, use Customer Journey Builder (Premium only). In Omnisend, leverage 250+ templates tailored to Shopify/WooCommerce for quicker setup.

### List Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp segments by tags, groups, or behavioral data. Advanced segmentation needs behavioral data from integrations.

Omnisend's segmentation is ecommerce-native: by purchase history, browsing behavior, or product views. It supports dynamic segments that update in real-time.

### Templates and Design
Mailchimp boasts thousands of templates with a user-friendly editor. Mobile-responsive designs are standard.

Omnisend offers 250+ ecommerce-optimized templates, with a similar drag-and-drop editor plus product blocks that pull live inventory data.

**Practical Advice:** Prioritize platforms with A/B testing on subject lines and content. Both support it, but Omnisend's splits are easier for product-based tests.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners with its intuitive dashboard and extensive tutorials. Setup takes minutes: import contacts, pick a template, send.

Omnisend has a steeper learning curve for non-ecommerce users but excels with guided onboarding for stores. Its dashboard prioritizes revenue metrics like ROAS (return on ad spend).

**Pro Tip:** Use Mailchimp if your team lacks technical skills. For Shopify users, Omnisend's one-click app install simplifies everything.

## Integrations and Ecommerce Focus

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps: Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier, and major CRMs. Ecommerce plugins exist for Shopify, but they're not as seamless.

Omnisend natively supports Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, with deep data sync for orders and products. It also handles SMS via Twilio and push notifications.

**Practical Advice:** Check Zapier compatibility for custom workflows. If you're on Shopify (powers 28% of online stores), Omnisend's integration reduces data silos and boosts automation accuracy.

## Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp provides detailed reports on opens, clicks, revenue (via tracking pixels), and unsubscribe rates. Custom dashboards are available on higher plans.

Omnisend's analytics emphasize ecommerce KPIs: attributed revenue, conversion rates per channel, and customer lifetime value (CLV). Revenue tracking is more accurate out-of-the-box.

**Tip:** Track UTM parameters in both for multi-channel attribution. Export data to Google Sheets for deeper analysis.

## Customer Support Options

Mailchimp offers email support on free plans, chat on paid, and extensive self-help resources (academy, forums).

Omnisend provides 24/7 email/chat on all paid plans, priority for Pro, plus a knowledge base and migration assistance.

**Practical Advice:** Test support before committing—email a pre-sales question. Omnisend's responsiveness shines for urgent ecommerce issues like campaign delays.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Mailchimp if:** You're a blogger, service business, or small team needing simple emails/newsletters. Its ecosystem suits content marketers.

**Pick Omnisend if:** You run an online store (Shopify/Woo) and want automated revenue recovery via email/SMS. It's optimized for driving sales.

**Migration Tip:** Both offer import tools. Export CSV from one, map fields, and test sends. Budget 1-2 days for setup.

## Conclusion

In the Omnisend vs Mailchimp showdown, there's no one-size-fits-all winner—it depends on your business model. Mailchimp remains a solid all-rounder for general email marketing, powering millions of users with reliability. However, Omnisend edges ahead for ecommerce brands seeking integrated omnichannel marketing, often delivering higher ROI through targeted automations.

Evaluate your needs: list size, channels (email-only vs multichannel), and tech stack. Start with free trials—sign up for both, import a test list, and run a sample campaign. Track metrics like open rates (aim for 20-30%) and conversions over a week.

For most growing online stores, Omnisend's specialized features make it the smarter long-term choice. Whichever you select, consistent testing and segmentation will maximize results. Ready to choose? Head to their sites and begin your trial today.
