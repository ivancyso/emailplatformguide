# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive sales. For creators, bloggers, and small businesses, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both tools excel in user-friendliness and powerful automations, but they cater to slightly different needs.

MailerLite emphasizes affordability and simplicity with a generous free plan, making it ideal for bootstrapped startups. ConvertKit, on the other hand, focuses on creators with advanced tagging and commerce features for selling digital products.

In this guide, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more to help you decide. All data is based on official sites as of late 2024—no hype, just facts.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Here's a side-by-side look.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite offers a **free plan** for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month—perfect for testing. Paid plans start at:

- **Growing Business**: $10/month (500 subs, unlimited emails)
- **Advanced**: $20/month (1,000 subs) up to enterprise options.

Features like automation, landing pages, and popups are included across plans. Annual billing saves 30%. No contracts; cancel anytime.

**Practical tip**: Start free to build your list, then scale without breaking the bank. For 5,000 subs, it's about $30/month.

### ConvertKit Pricing (Now Kit)

ConvertKit rebranded to Kit but keeps familiar pricing. No perpetual free plan—14-day trial only. Plans:

- **Creator**: $15/month (300 subs, formerly free tier feel)
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month (500 subs) up to $79/month (3,000 subs).

Commerce features (subscriptions, one-time sales) start at higher tiers. Annual discount: 20%.

**Practical tip**: If you're selling courses or memberships, the Pro plan unlocks payouts. Budget $50+/month for growing lists.

**Winner**: MailerLite for beginners/cost-conscious; ConvertKit for revenue-focused creators.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Interface

Both platforms prioritize intuitive designs, but differences shine in daily workflows.

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

New users can send their first email in minutes. The editor is block-based: add text, images, buttons effortlessly. Mobile-responsive templates out-of-the-box.

Automation builder uses a visual flowchart—connect triggers like "subscribed to list" to actions like "send welcome series."

**Pro tip**: Use the template library for newsletters or product launches. A/B testing is free on all plans.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Dashboard

Tag-based subscriber management feels like a CRM. Visual automations (called Sequences) guide you step-by-step.

Editor is clean but less "drag-heavy"—focuses on content blocks with personalization tags like {first_name}.

**Pro tip**: The subscriber growth dashboard shows opens, clicks, and revenue—great for iterating content.

**Winner**: Tie—MailerLite for visual builders, ConvertKit for tagging power users.

## Key Features Head-to-Head

Let's dive into core capabilities.

### Email Builder and Templates

- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates, full customization. JSON editor for pros.
- **ConvertKit**: Simpler builder with 100+ templates optimized for creators. Strong personalization.

Both deliver 99%+ inbox rates (industry standard).

### Automations and Segmentation

- **MailerLite**: Time-based, behavior-triggered automations. Segments by activity, custom fields. Popups/forms convert 20-30% better with A/B.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences for drip campaigns. Advanced tagging (e.g., "purchased course") enables hyper-personalization. Re-engagement campaigns auto-reactivate.

**Practical advice**: For lead magnets, MailerLite's popups integrate seamlessly. ConvertKit shines in post-purchase upsells.

### Landing Pages and Forms

- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, forms, popups on free plan. HTML customization.
- **ConvertKit**: Forms and landing pages included; commerce-optimized checkouts.

### Integrations and Ecommerce

Both connect to 1,000+ apps via Zapier.

- **MailerLite**: WooCommerce, Shopify, WordPress plugins.
- **ConvertKit**: Gumroad, Teachable native; built-in Creator Network for cross-promos.

**Tip**: If using WordPress, MailerLite's plugin auto-syncs comments to lists.

### Analytics and Reporting

- **MailerLite**: Open/click rates, revenue tracking, heatmaps.
- **ConvertKit**: Subscriber value reports, predicted revenue.

## Support and Community

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive academy/videos. Active Facebook group.
- **ConvertKit**: Email/ticket support (faster on Pro), podcast, webinars. Creator-focused community.

**Practical advice**: MailerLite's chat resolves issues in <5 mins—crucial for non-tech users.

## Pros and Cons Summary

### MailerLite

**Pros**:
- Free plan
- Affordable scaling
- All features unlocked early
- Excellent popups/landing pages

**Cons**:
- Less advanced commerce
- Basic tagging vs segments

### ConvertKit

**Pros**:
- Superior tagging/sequences
- Built for selling
- Revenue analytics
- Creator tools (visuals, payouts)

**Cons**:
- Higher cost, no free tier
- Fewer templates

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Budget < $20/month, need free start, focus on newsletters/popups, small biz/blogger without heavy sales.
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Monetizing content, need tags/sequences, willing to invest $30+, digital product seller.

**Migration tip**: Both import CSVs easily. Export from one, map fields, test automations first. MailerLite offers migration service on higher plans.

## Conclusion: Your Best Pick Depends on Goals

**MailerLite vs ConvertKit** boils down to cost vs creator depth. For most starting out, MailerLite's free plan and features make it the smart entry—scale to 10k subs under $100/month. ConvertKit justifies its price for those earning via email (e.g., $1k+/month revenue).

Test both: MailerLite free forever (under limits), ConvertKit's trial. Track your first campaign's ROI to decide.
