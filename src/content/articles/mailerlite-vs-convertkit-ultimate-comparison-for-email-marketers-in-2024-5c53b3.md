# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. MailerLite and ConvertKit are two popular platforms favored by bloggers, creators, and small businesses. Both offer automation, beautiful templates, and segmentation, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling to thousands of subscribers, this guide provides practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email tool. Both platforms scale with your subscriber list, but their structures differ.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite stands out with its generous free plan: up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. No credit card required to start. Paid plans begin at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers (billed annually) and go up to $230/month for 150,000+ subscribers. Key perks include unlimited emails on paid plans, popups, and landing pages included across all tiers. It's ideal for bootstrapped solopreneurs testing the waters.

Practical tip: If you're under 1,000 subs, stick to free and upgrade seamlessly without data loss.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit offers a 14-day free trial but no perpetual free plan. The Creator plan starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers (annual billing), jumping to $29/month for 1,000. The Creator Pro plan begins at $50/month for advanced commerce features. Prices scale to $1,365/month for massive lists. All plans include unlimited emails and sends.

Practical tip: Use the trial to import your list and test automations before committing. Annual billing saves 20%.

**Winner on Price:** MailerLite for beginners and small lists; ConvertKit competitive for creators willing to invest early.

## Ease of Use and Interface

### MailerLite: Beginner-Friendly Drag-and-Drop

MailerLite's interface is clean and intuitive. The drag-and-drop editor lets you build emails without coding. Campaigns take minutes to design with 80+ responsive templates. Navigation is straightforward: dashboard shows key stats like open rates at a glance.

Users praise its simplicity—perfect if you're new to email marketing. Mobile app available for iOS/Android.

### ConvertKit: Creator-Centric with a Learning Curve

ConvertKit's visual builder is powerful but assumes some familiarity. The "Sequences" and "Automations" tabs are front-and-center for storytelling emails. Tagging system replaces traditional segments, which takes getting used to but excels for personalized flows.

It's optimized for Apple users with a polished Mac-like feel, but the initial setup might overwhelm absolute beginners.

**Practical Advice:** Start with MailerLite if you're non-technical. Switch to ConvertKit once comfortable with subscriber tags for advanced nurturing.

**Winner:** MailerLite for quick setup; ConvertKit for power users.

## Feature Breakdown

### Automation Capabilities

Both shine here, but approaches vary.

**MailerLite:** Workflow builder with triggers like sign-ups or clicks. Supports A/B testing, RSS-to-email, and time-based delays. Unlimited automations on all plans.

**ConvertKit:** Superior for creators with "Visual Automations." Rules-based triggers (e.g., "if subscribed to X, send Y") and subscriber scoring. Pre-built sequences for welcome series or product launches.

Practical tip: Map your customer journey first—use MailerLite for simple drips, ConvertKit for complex funnels like podcast listener segmentation.

### Email Templates and Design

MailerLite: 100+ free templates, photo editors, and custom HTML. Strong for newsletters and promotions.

ConvertKit: Fewer templates (50+), but optimized for long-form content like blogs. Inline images and clean, minimalist designs suit creators.

### Segmentation and Tagging

MailerLite: Groups, tags, and custom fields. Easy clicks-to-segments.

ConvertKit: Tag-based system is its superpower—predictive sending based on behaviors. No groups needed.

Practical tip: Export CSV from one tool to import into the other for testing segments.

### Integrations and Landing Pages

Both integrate with 1,000+ apps via Zapier. MailerLite has built-in popups/forms/websites. ConvertKit excels with commerce (Stripe, Gumroad) and native creator tools (podcasts, YouTube).

MailerLite: Free websites/landing pages on all plans.

ConvertKit: Forms and landing pages included, plus subscriber commerce.

### Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite: Open/click rates, bounces, revenue tracking, heatmap.

ConvertKit: Detailed subscriber activity, revenue per subscriber, A/B insights.

**Overall Feature Winner:** ConvertKit for creators; MailerLite for versatility.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive help center, Facebook group. Fast responses (under 1 hour).

ConvertKit: Email/ticket support (business hours), Creator Network community, webinars. Highly responsive for paid users.

Practical tip: Join both free communities (MailerLite Academy, ConvertKit Facebook group) before deciding.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite Pros:
- Free plan
- Affordable scaling
- All-in-one (popups, sites)
- Intuitive for beginners

**Cons:** Less advanced tagging; no native commerce.

### ConvertKit Pros:
- Creator-focused automations
- Excellent tagging/segmentation
- Monetization tools
- Predictable revenue analytics

**Cons:** No free plan; steeper learning curve; higher cost for small lists.

## Who Should Choose Which?

**Pick MailerLite if:** You're a small business, blogger on a budget, or need quick wins with popups/landing pages. Great for e-commerce starters under 5,000 subs.

**Pick ConvertKit if:** You're a content creator (podcaster, YouTuber) focused on long-term audience building and sales funnels. Ideal for 1,000+ engaged subscribers.

Practical migration advice: Export subscribers as CSV (both support). Recreate top automations first. Test sends to 10% of list. Use Zapier for ongoing syncs during transition.

## Conclusion

MailerLite edges out for affordability and ease, making it perfect for newcomers. ConvertKit dominates for sophisticated creator workflows and monetization. Evaluate based on your list size, budget, and goals—sign up for MailerLite's free plan or ConvertKit's trial today.

Neither is "best" universally; test both with your audience. Track metrics like open rates (aim >25%) and conversions post-switch. Whichever you choose, consistent value-driven emails will drive results.
