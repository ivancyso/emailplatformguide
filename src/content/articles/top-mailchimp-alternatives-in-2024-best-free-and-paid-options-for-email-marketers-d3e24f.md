# Top Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Free and Paid Options for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for alternatives. If you're facing high fees, need advanced segmentation, or want better deliverability, it's time to explore options. This roundup covers the top Mailchimp alternatives, focusing on real features, pricing, and use cases for small businesses, e-commerce stores, and agencies.

We'll break down 10 solid choices, compare them to Mailchimp, and share practical tips for switching. All info is based on current public pricing and features as of 2024—always check official sites for updates.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but jump quickly (e.g., $299/month for 50k contacts). Common pain points include:

- **Limited automation**: Basic triggers only on lower tiers.
- **Deliverability issues**: Some users report higher spam rates.
- **UI clutter**: Overwhelmed by unnecessary features.

Alternatives often offer unlimited sends, better support, or niche strengths like e-commerce integrations. Before switching, audit your current setup: export contacts, note automations, and test imports.

## Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo shines for transactional emails alongside marketing. Unlike Mailchimp's contact-based pricing, Brevo charges per email sent—ideal for sporadic senders.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts on free plan (300 emails/day).
- Advanced automation, SMS, chat, and CRM.
- Drag-and-drop editor similar to Mailchimp.

**Pricing**: Free forever; paid from $25/month (20k emails).

**Pros**: Cost-effective for high-volume, multi-channel. **Cons**: Steeper learning curve for CRM.

**Best for**: SMBs needing SMS/email bundles. Migrate by importing CSV and remapping automations.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

A powerhouse for automation, ActiveCampaign beats Mailchimp with machine learning and deep CRM integration.

**Key Features**:
- Site tracking, predictive sending, split testing.
- 900+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress).
- Conditional content blocks.

**Pricing**: Starts at $29/month (500 contacts); scales to $259 for 50k.

**Pros**: Superior personalization. **Cons**: Complex for beginners.

**Practical Tip**: Use their migration service for seamless transfer. Test automations with a small segment first.

### 3. Klaviyo

E-commerce focused, Klaviyo excels in data-driven flows—perfect if Mailchimp's analytics feel basic.

**Key Features**n
- Revenue tracking, A/B testing on flows.
- Pre-built Shopify/WooCommerce integrations.
- Dynamic product recommendations.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; $45/month for 1k contacts.

**Pros**: ROI-focused metrics. **Cons**: Less ideal for non-ecom.

**Best for**: Online stores. Sync your store data before importing lists.

### 4. ConvertKit (now Kit)

Creator-friendly with simple tagging over lists, outperforming Mailchimp for newsletters.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automations, landing pages, forms.
- Subscriber scoring, one-click upsells.

**Pricing**: Free to 1k subs; $29/month for 3k.

**Pros**: Clean UI, unlimited sends. **Cons**: Fewer templates.

**Tip**: Tag-based segmentation migrates easily—map Mailchimp groups to tags.

### 5. MailerLite

Budget pick with Mailchimp-like ease but better value.

**Key Features**:
- Pop-ups, surveys, A/B testing.
- Website builder included.

**Pricing**: Free to 1k subs/12k emails; $10/month growing plan.

**Pros**: Affordable scaling. **Cons**: Basic reporting.

**Migration Advice**: Their importer handles Mailchimp exports directly.

### 6. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels, more robust than Mailchimp's add-ons.

**Key Features**:
- Conversion funnels, autoresponders.
- E-commerce tools, landing pages.

**Pricing**: Free trial; $19/month (1k contacts).

**Pros**: High deliverability. **Cons**: Overkill for simple lists.

### 7. AWeber

Veteran tool with strong support and RSS-to-email.

**Key Features**:
- Smart send times, legacy-proof stability.

**Pricing**: $16.15/month (500 subs).

**Pros**: Reliable. **Cons**: Dated interface.

### 8. Constant Contact

User-friendly for non-profits/small biz, with event management.

**Key Features**:
- Social ads integration, AI content.

**Pricing**: $12/month (500 contacts).

**Pros**: Beginner-friendly. **Cons**: Pricey for large lists.

### 9. Omnisend

Ecom specialist like Klaviyo but with more channels.

**Key Features**:
- Push notifications, SMS.

**Pricing**: Free to 250 contacts; $16/month.

### 10. Zoho Campaigns

Great if you use Zoho suite—integrates seamlessly.

**Key Features**:
- Workflow automation, RSS.

**Pricing**: Free to 2k subs; $4/month.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: Ecom? Pick Klaviyo/Omnisend. Automation? ActiveCampaign.
2. **Budget Check**: Calculate costs—Brevo/MailerLite win for volume.
3. **Test Free Trials**: Import 10% of list, run a campaign.
4. **Migration Steps**:
   - Export from Mailchimp (CSV/ZIP).
   - Clean data (remove bounces).
   - Remap tags/segments.
   - Recreate top automations.
   - Update website forms.
   - Monitor deliverability week 1.
5. **Common Pitfalls**: Forgetting unsubscribe links or domain auth (DKIM/SPF).

Pro Tip: Use Zapier for quick integrations during transition.

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ActiveCampaign, and Klaviyo offer better value, features, and scalability for most users. Start with your priorities—cost, ease, or power—and trial 2-3 options.
