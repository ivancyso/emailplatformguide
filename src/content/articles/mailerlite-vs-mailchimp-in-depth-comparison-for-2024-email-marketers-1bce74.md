# MailerLite vs MailChimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and MailChimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email list and scaling campaigns. Both are popular tools for small businesses, solopreneurs, and marketers, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, automation, integrations, and deliverability based on their official offerings as of 2024.

Whether you're a beginner or managing growing lists, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both platforms offer free plans, but they differ in limits and paid tiers.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite keeps it simple and affordable:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $9/month for 500 subscribers (billed annually), scaling to $20 for 2,500 subs. Unlimited emails.
- **Advanced**: From $23/month for 500 subs, adding A/B testing and web push notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for large lists.

Practical tip: If your list is under 1,000 and you send monthly newsletters, the free plan suffices. Upgrade only when you hit limits—no overage fees.

### MailChimp Pricing

MailChimp's structure is tiered by contacts and features:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month (recently reduced). Basic templates and landing pages.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts, up to $350 for 50,000. Always-paid emails.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500, adding branching automation and custom reports.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced segmentation and multivariate testing.

Practical tip: MailChimp charges per contact regardless of engagement, so dormant lists cost more. Ideal if you need robust reporting early on.

**Winner**: MailerLite for budget-conscious users; MailChimp for feature-heavy needs despite higher costs.

## Ease of Use: Interface and Onboarding

User-friendliness matters for non-techies.

### MailerLite's Simplicity

Clean, intuitive dashboard. Drag-and-drop editor is straightforward—no steep learning curve. Onboarding includes video tutorials and templates optimized for mobile.

Practical advice: Start with their 30+ responsive templates. Customize blocks easily; test on real devices via preview.

### MailChimp's Feature-Rich Editor

More complex with multipage builders and content optimizer AI. Great for pros, but beginners might need tutorials.

Practical advice: Use the "Content Optimizer" to score emails pre-send. Enable "High-Performers" to reuse top templates.

**Winner**: MailerLite for quick setup; MailChimp for power users.

## Core Features: Templates, Segmentation, and Analytics

### Email Templates and Design

MailerLite: 80+ free templates, HTML editor, and image editor. Focus on clean, conversion-focused designs.
MailChimp: 100+ templates, Creative Assistant AI for quick builds. More variety for e-commerce.

Tip: In MailerLite, use dynamic content blocks for personalized recommendations.

### List Segmentation

Both excel:
- MailerLite: Tags, groups, custom fields, behaviors (e.g., opened last campaign).
- MailChimp: Advanced segments by engagement, location, purchase history.

Practical advice: Segment by 'most engaged' (opened 3+ last emails) to boost open rates 20-30%.

### Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite: Real-time stats, click maps, revenue tracking.
MailChimp: Deeper insights like cohort analysis, A/B reports.

Tip: Track ROI by tagging UTM links in both.

## Automation and Workflows

### MailerLite Automation

Visual builder for welcome series, abandoned carts, re-engagement. Triggers: sign-ups, tags, dates.

Practical advice: Build a 5-email nurture sequence: Welcome > Value tip > Case study > Offer > Follow-up. Set delays based on open rates.

### MailChimp Automations

Branching logic, customer journeys. Integrates with e-com platforms.

Practical advice: Use RSS-to-email for blogs; trigger post-purchase upsells.

**Winner**: Tie—MailerLite simpler, MailChimp more advanced.

## Integrations and Deliverability

Both integrate with 100+ apps (Stripe, Shopify, WordPress).

MailerLite: Native popups, forms, surveys. High deliverability (98%+ inbox rate per reviews).
MailChimp: Extensive API, Zapier hub. Strong authentication.

Tip: Authenticate domain in both for better deliverability. Warm up IPs gradually.

## Customer Support

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Fast responses.
MailChimp: Priority support on higher plans; self-serve for free.

Practical advice: Use chat for setup issues—both resolve in minutes.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Solopreneurs, bloggers, small teams.
- Need affordable scaling, simple automations.
- Focus: Newsletters, lead magnets.

**Pro Tip**: Export lists easily if switching.

## Who Should Choose MailChimp?

- E-commerce stores, agencies.
- Complex campaigns, deep analytics.
- Established brands.

**Pro Tip**: Leverage Creative Assistant for A/B variants.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

Neither is universally 'best'—it depends on your stage.

| Feature | MailerLite | MailChimp |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Free Plan | 1k subs, 12k emails | 500 contacts, 1k emails |
| Starting Paid | $9/mo | $13/mo |
| Best For | Beginners, affordability | Advanced features |

**Recommendation**: Start with MailerLite's free plan for most users—switch to MailChimp if needing e-com depth. Test both free tiers.
