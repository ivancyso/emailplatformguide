# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses scaling their email marketing. Both platforms offer robust tools for newsletters, automations, and customer segmentation, but they cater to different needs. This comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, integrations, and deliverability based on official data and user reports as of 2024.

Whether you're a solopreneur on a budget or a growing team needing advanced CRM, we'll help you decide with practical advice.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both have free plans, but they differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts and 5,000 sends, scaling to $350+/month for Premium (50,000+ contacts). Key notes:
- Overage fees apply if you exceed limits.
- Annual billing saves 15-20%.
- Add-ons like landing pages cost extra ($14/month).

Practical tip: Ideal for beginners testing waters, but costs rise quickly with lists over 5,000—budget $100+/month then.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo's free plan offers unlimited contacts with 300 daily emails (about 9,000/month). Paid plans begin at $25/month (Starter) for 20,000 monthly emails, up to $65/month (Business) for 100,000 emails. Enterprise is custom.
- No contact-based pricing; email volume drives costs.
- Includes SMS and chat in higher tiers.
- 3-month free trial on paid plans.

Practical tip: Better for high-volume senders on a budget. If you have 10,000+ contacts but send infrequently, Brevo saves 50-70% vs Mailchimp.

**Winner: Brevo for most SMBs** due to predictable, volume-based pricing without contact limits exploding costs.

## Ease of Use and Interface

### Mailchimp User Experience

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 100+ mobile-responsive templates. The dashboard feels familiar, like a modern inbox. Onboarding includes tutorials and a content optimizer AI.

Pros: Quick campaign setup (under 10 minutes for basics).
Cons: Cluttered for advanced users; automation builder can feel rigid.

### Brevo User Experience

Brevo's editor is block-based and customizable, with 48 templates. The interface is clean, emphasizing automation workflows from day one. Multi-channel (email + SMS) setup is seamless.

Pros: Simpler for transactional + marketing emails.
Cons: Fewer templates; learning curve for non-email pros.

Practical advice: Start with Mailchimp if you're design-focused and new. Switch to Brevo if you need CRM-like workflows. Test both free plans—export a sample list to compare setup time.

**Winner: Mailchimp** for sheer beginner-friendliness.

## Core Features Comparison

### Email Templates and Design

Mailchimp: 100+ industry-specific templates, AI-assisted content generation, A/B testing on subject lines.
Brevo: 48 templates, but strong customization and HTML editor. Includes dynamic content blocks.

Tip: Use Mailchimp's Creative Assistant for quick wins; Brevo for branded, code-heavy designs.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp: Classic automations (welcome series, abandoned cart) plus customer journey builder in Premium.
Brevo: Advanced journeys with wait steps, A/B testing, and RSS-to-email. Integrates transactional APIs easily.

Practical advice: For e-commerce, Brevo's cart abandonment flows trigger faster. Map your customer lifecycle first—tools like Lucidchart help visualize before building.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Both segment by behavior, tags, and custom fields. Mailchimp excels in reporting (open rates, revenue per email). Brevo shines in deliverability stats and heatmaps.

Tip: Track ROI with UTM parameters in both; Brevo's free web tracker adds form analytics without code.

### Deliverability and Compliance

Mailchimp: 99%+ deliverability, strict authentication (DKIM/SPF). EU GDPR compliant.
Brevo: 99% deliverability, IP warming for new accounts, strong anti-spam tools. Also GDPR/CCPA ready.

Real data: Brevo IPs are shared but warmed; Mailchimp's dedicated IPs cost extra ($50+/month).

**Features Winner: Tie**—Mailchimp for templates/analytics, Brevo for automation/deliverability.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics) via native or Zapier.
Brevo supports 300+ too, with strengths in e-commerce (WooCommerce, Magento) and APIs for devs.

Practical tip: Check Zapier limits—Brevo's native transactional API reduces reliance on zaps, saving tasks/month.

For scaling: Mailchimp handles millions of sends; Brevo's volume pricing suits agencies.

## Pros and Cons Summary

### Mailchimp
**Pros:**
- Intuitive editor
- Excellent support (chat/email)
- Robust free resources (guides, academy)

**Cons:**n- Expensive for large lists
- Limited free sends
- No native SMS

### Brevo
**Pros:**
- Affordable scaling
- Unlimited free contacts
- Multi-channel (SMS, chat, WhatsApp)

**Cons:**
- Fewer templates
- Support slower on free plan
- Less brand recognition

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick Mailchimp if:** You're a content creator, blogger, or small team prioritizing design and ease. Best for <5,000 contacts, infrequent sends.
- **Pick Brevo if:** E-commerce store, agency, or high-volume sender needing SMS/automation. Ideal for 5,000+ contacts on budget.

Migration advice: Export CSV from one, import to other. Clean lists first (remove bounces). Test sends to 100 subscribers.

## Conclusion

Brevo edges out Mailchimp for cost-conscious businesses with growing lists and multi-channel needs, offering better long-term value without sacrificing features. Mailchimp remains king for quick, pretty campaigns.

Ultimately, sign up for both free plans, send a test campaign, and review analytics after 30 days. Your audience engagement data will decide. For most scaling SMBs, Brevo's pricing and automation make it the 2024 pick.
