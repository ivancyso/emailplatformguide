# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp—the industry giant—helps narrow it down. This guide compares their key aspects like pricing, features, usability, and support to help you decide based on your needs, whether you're a small business owner, freelancer, or growing e-commerce store.

We'll dive into practical insights, real-world use cases, and tips to maximize each tool's potential without hype or unsubstantiated claims.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo is a versatile all-in-one platform focused on email, SMS, chat, and CRM features. It's particularly strong for businesses needing transactional emails alongside marketing campaigns. Launched as Sendinblue in 2012, it rebranded to Brevo in 2023 to emphasize its broader marketing automation scope.

Key strengths include unlimited contacts on paid plans, daily email limits rather than subscriber-based pricing, and seamless integration for transactional sends (e.g., order confirmations). It's EU-based, complying with GDPR out of the box, which appeals to privacy-conscious users.

**Practical tip:** If your business sends automated transactional emails like password resets or shipping updates, Brevo's API excels here. Start with their free plan (300 emails/day) to test without commitment.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, founded in 2001, is synonymous with email marketing. It's beginner-friendly with drag-and-drop editors, pre-built templates, and audience segmentation. Its free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends, making it accessible for startups.

Mailchimp shines in creative campaigns, A/B testing, and integrations with e-commerce platforms like Shopify. It also offers advanced analytics and automation workflows. However, pricing scales quickly with audience size.

**Practical tip:** Use Mailchimp's content optimizer to improve open rates— it scores your subject lines and content based on proven best practices.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major differentiator. Here's a breakdown based on current tiers (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates):

### Brevo Pricing
- **Free:** 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts.
- **Starter:** $25/month for 20k emails/month.
- **Business:** $65/month for 20k emails + advanced automation.
- **Enterprise:** Custom.

Brevo charges per email volume, not contacts, so it's cost-effective for large lists with low send frequency.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 500 contacts, 1k sends/month.
- **Essentials:** $13/month for 500 contacts, scales to $350+ for 50k.
- **Standard:** $20/month for 500 contacts, up to $600+ for larger.
- **Premium:** $350+/month.

Mailchimp's contact-based pricing can get expensive fast for growing lists.

**Practical advice:** Calculate your costs. For 10k contacts sending monthly newsletters: Brevo ~$25 (20k emails), Mailchimp ~$100 (Standard). Factor in SMS or automation needs—Brevo bundles them affordably.

## Feature Comparison

Both platforms offer core email marketing, but differences emerge in depth.

### Email Builder and Templates
- **Brevo:** Drag-and-drop editor with responsive templates. Strong for transactional personalization.
- **Mailchimp:** Superior template library (hundreds free), creative blocks, and theme builder.

**Tip:** Mailchimp for visually stunning newsletters; Brevo for functional, data-driven emails.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Brevo:** Multi-channel automations (email + SMS), drip campaigns, abandoned cart recovery.
- **Mailchimp:** Customer Journey Builder for complex triggers like behavioral segmentation.

Both handle basics well, but Brevo edges for e-commerce integrations.

### Analytics and Reporting
- Brevo: Open rates, clicks, bounces; heatmaps for engagement.
- Mailchimp: Detailed reports, comparative analytics, Einstein AI predictions (Premium).

**Practical advice:** Track revenue per email—both integrate with Google Analytics. Set up custom events for ROI measurement.

### Integrations
- Brevo: 300+ including WooCommerce, Zapier.
- Mailchimp: 250+ native, excels with Shopify, WordPress.

## Ease of Use

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners with its intuitive interface and tutorials. Brevo has a slight learning curve for advanced automation but offers clean dashboards.

**Onboarding tip:** Both have 30-day trials. Import your list via CSV and run a test campaign to gauge comfort.

## Customer Support

- **Brevo:** Email/ticket support (paid plans), live chat, knowledge base. 24/7 for higher tiers.
- **Mailchimp:** Extensive help center, email support (paid), community forums. No phone for lower plans.

Brevo responds faster for technical issues, per user reviews on G2/Capterra.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- E-commerce stores (transactional emails).
- Businesses with large, low-engagement lists.
- Multi-channel needs (SMS/chat).
- Budget-conscious teams.

**Case study tip:** A SaaS company saved 40% switching from Mailchimp by leveraging unlimited contacts.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Content creators/marketers prioritizing design.
- Small teams needing quick setup.
- Agencies with diverse clients.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to priorities: Brevo for cost-efficiency and transactional power, Mailchimp for ease and creativity. For most small businesses under 5k contacts, start with Mailchimp's free plan. Scaling up? Brevo's model saves money long-term.

Test both—export your list and compare a sample campaign. Whichever you pick, focus on list hygiene (remove bounces), personalization (use merge tags), and compliance (double opt-in). Your open rates will thank you.
