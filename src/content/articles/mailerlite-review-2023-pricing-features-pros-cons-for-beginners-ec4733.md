# MailerLite Review 2023: Pricing, Features, Pros & Cons for Beginners

If you're a small business owner, blogger, or marketer hunting for an affordable email marketing platform, MailerLite often pops up in recommendations. But does it live up to the hype? In this comprehensive MailerLite review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance to help you decide if it's the right choice for your email campaigns. Whether you're migrating from another tool or starting fresh, this guide provides practical insights based on its core offerings.

MailerLite positions itself as a user-friendly, budget-friendly alternative to giants like Mailchimp. Launched in 2010, it serves over 2 million users worldwide, focusing on simplicity without skimping on essentials like automation and analytics. Let's break it down step by step.

## MailerLite Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

One of MailerLite's biggest draws is its straightforward pricing, which scales with subscribers rather than contacts or sends. No hidden fees for opening rates or bounces—costs are predictable.

- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups. Perfect for testing without commitment.
- **Growing Business ($10/month)**: Up to 500 subscribers, unlimited emails, advanced automation, A/B testing, and webhooks.
- **Advanced ($20/month)**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, adds custom HTML, priority support, and high-volume sending.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for 100k+ subscribers.

**Practical Advice**: Start with the free plan to import contacts and build your first campaign. Upgrade only when you hit limits—many users stay free for months. Annual billing saves 20-30%. Compare to Mailchimp's free tier (500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month); MailerLite wins for higher limits.

Pricing is billed monthly or annually, with a 30-day money-back guarantee. No contracts, so you can downgrade anytime.

## Key Features of MailerLite

MailerLite packs robust tools into an intuitive interface. Here's what stands out:

### Drag-and-Drop Email Editor

The editor is block-based, letting you mix text, images, buttons, and dividers. No coding needed, but custom HTML is available for pros.

**Practical Tip**: Use pre-built templates for newsletters, product launches, or welcome series. Customize with your branding—upload logos and set colors via the theme editor. Test mobile responsiveness before sending; it auto-optimizes but preview on devices.

### Automation Workflows

Build if/then sequences triggered by sign-ups, clicks, or purchases. Single and multi-step automations are free-form.

**Practical Advice**: Set up a welcome series: Email 1 (thanks + freebie), Email 2 (tips), Email 3 (upsell). Use tags to segment (e.g., "opened-email-1"). Monitor drop-offs in analytics to refine timing—aim for 24-48 hour intervals.

### Landing Pages and Popups

Create unlimited pages with forms, optimized for conversions. Popups include exit-intent and scroll-triggered.

**Tip**: A/B test headlines and CTAs. Embed forms on your site via JavaScript snippet. Track conversions with built-in analytics—no Google Analytics needed initially.

### Forms and Surveys

Inline, embedded, or popup forms with conditional logic (show fields based on answers).

**Pro Tip**: Use for lead magnets like ebooks. Integrate with Zapier for CRM syncing.

### Analytics and Reporting

Open rates, clicks, bounces, unsubscribes. Heatmaps show click locations.

**Advice**: Benchmark against industry averages (20-30% opens). Segment reports by campaign type to spot winners.

Other features: RSS campaigns, e-commerce integrations (Shopify, WooCommerce), and API access.

## Integrations and API

**Tip**: Connect to your ESP for abandoned cart emails. Use webhooks for custom triggers like Slack notifications.

## Customer Support and Resources

**Advice**: Free users get community forum access. For urgent issues, upgrade for priority.

**Pros**:

**Cons**:

## Who Should Use MailerLite?
