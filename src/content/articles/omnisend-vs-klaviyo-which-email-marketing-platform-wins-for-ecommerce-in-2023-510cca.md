# Omnisend vs Klaviyo: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins for Ecommerce in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can make or break your ecommerce growth. With Omnisend and Klaviyo leading the pack for online stores, especially those on Shopify and WooCommerce, this head-to-head comparison dives deep into their features, pricing, ease of use, and more. Whether you're a small business owner or scaling a DTC brand, we'll help you decide based on real capabilities and practical use cases. Let's break it down.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing platform designed specifically for ecommerce. It excels in combining email, SMS, and push notifications into unified customer journeys. Launched in 2014, it's popular among Shopify merchants for its drag-and-drop editor and pre-built workflows.

Key strengths include:
- **Omnichannel support**: Seamlessly blend email with SMS and web push for higher engagement rates.
- **Ecommerce integrations**: Native connections to Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, and more.
- **Automation workflows**: Ready-to-use flows for welcome series, cart abandonment, and post-purchase upsells.

Practical tip: Start with Omnisend's free plan (up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month) to test SMS add-ons, which cost extra but boost open rates by 6x compared to email alone.

## Overview of Klaviyo

Klaviyo, founded in 2012, is a powerhouse for data-driven email marketing, particularly for Shopify stores. It's renowned for its advanced segmentation and revenue-focused analytics, powering brands like TaylorMade and Casper.

Standout features:
- **Hyper-personalization**: Uses predictive analytics to suggest products and optimize send times.
- **Deep Shopify sync**: Real-time data pull for abandoned carts, revenue per recipient (RPR), and ROAS tracking.
- **A/B testing**: Built-in tools for subject lines, content, and flows.

Pro advice: Klaviyo's free tier (250 contacts) is generous for testing, but scale-up costs rise quickly. Use their revenue tracking to justify spend—many users report 30%+ revenue lifts from optimized flows.

## Feature Comparison: Head-to-Head

### Email Automation and Workflows

Both platforms shine in automations, but differences emerge in complexity.

- **Omnisend**: 250+ pre-built templates and workflows. Drag-and-drop builder is beginner-friendly. Supports conditional splits based on behavior (e.g., viewed product > $50).
  Practical setup: Create a cart abandonment flow in 10 minutes—add SMS fallback for non-openers.

- **Klaviyo**: More advanced with dynamic segments and revenue-based triggers. Flows can include custom events like "session browsed."
  Practical tip: Segment by predicted CLV (customer lifetime value) for VIP nurturing; expect setup time of 20-30 minutes for pros.

Winner: Klaviyo for advanced users; Omnisend for speed.

### Segmentation and Personalization

- **Omnisend**: List-based segments with ecommerce properties (e.g., purchased X, not Y). Behavioral filters like page visits.

- **Klaviyo**: Superior with SQL-like queries and real-time syncing. Build segments like "high AOV buyers who haven't purchased in 30 days."

Advice: In Omnisend, use pop-up forms for list growth; Klaviyo integrates better with popups via third-party apps like Justuno.

### SMS and Omnichannel

Omnisend includes SMS natively (extra cost: $0.02/message), with unified inboxes. Klaviyo added SMS in 2021 but charges per message ($0.015 base).

Tip: For US stores, test SMS opt-ins—Omnisend's all-in-one dashboard simplifies compliance.

### Templates and Design

Both offer responsive HTML templates. Omnisend's editor is more intuitive for non-designers; Klaviyo's allows custom CSS for brands.

### Analytics and Reporting

Klaviyo edges out with RPR, incremental revenue attribution, and cohort analysis. Omnisend provides solid ROI tracking tied to orders.

Practical: Export Klaviyo reports to Google Sheets for custom dashboards.
