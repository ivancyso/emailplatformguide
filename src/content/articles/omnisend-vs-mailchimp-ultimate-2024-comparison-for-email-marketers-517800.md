# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming, especially with so many features to compare. Both are powerhouse email marketing tools trusted by thousands of businesses. Mailchimp has long been the go-to for beginners and small teams due to its intuitive interface and free plan. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines for ecommerce stores with its omnichannel capabilities like SMS and web push notifications.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, support, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur, ecommerce owner, or scaling agency, you'll get practical advice to decide which fits your needs. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but they scale differently based on contacts and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic features. No automation.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/mo (500 contacts), unlimited sends, basic automation.
- **Standard**: $20/mo (500 contacts), advanced automation, A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350+/mo (10k+ contacts), multivariate testing, optimization.

Mailchimp charges per contacts, so costs rise quickly. For 5,000 contacts on Standard, it's about $75/mo.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, full automation access.
- **Standard**: $16/mo (500 contacts), SMS/push, unlimited emails.
- **Pro**: $59/mo (500 contacts), advanced segmentation, high-volume SMS.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for 100k+ contacts.

Omnisend is more generous on sends (unlimited emails) and cheaper for ecommerce-heavy users. Practical tip: Use Omnisend's free plan to test automations before committing—import your Shopify list and set up a welcome series in minutes.

**Winner**: Omnisend for growing lists; Mailchimp for tiny free starts.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly or Pro-Level?

### Mailchimp Interface
Drag-and-drop editor is legendary. No coding needed for newsletters. Dashboard is clean, with quick stats on open rates. Setup takes under 10 minutes: sign up, import CSV, design template.

**Practical Advice**: For non-tech users, start with pre-built templates. Customize colors via the style editor—test mobile preview always, as 50%+ opens are mobile.

### Omnisend Interface
Similar drag-drop, but ecommerce-optimized. Visual workflow builder for automations is a standout. Dashboard prioritizes revenue metrics like attributed sales.

**Practical Advice**: Connect your store (Shopify/Woo), and Omnisend auto-pulls products for dynamic emails. Use the popup builder for list growth—set exit-intent triggers for 20-30% opt-in boosts.

**Winner**: Tie—both intuitive, but Mailchimp edges for pure email beginners.

## Features Breakdown

### Email Templates and Design
Both have 100+ responsive templates. Mailchimp's are more creative (e.g., holiday themes). Omnisend excels in product-focused blocks.

**Tip**: In Mailchimp, use Content Studio for stock images. Omnisend auto-syncs product feeds—ideal for abandoned cart emails showing exact items.

### Automation Workflows
Omnisend dominates here with pre-built ecommerce flows: cart recovery (recover 15-20% lost sales), win-back, cross-sell. Visual editor lets you drag segments and actions.

Mailchimp has solid automations (e.g., birthday series), but limited triggers vs. Omnisend's 15+.

**Practical Setup Guide**:
1. In Omnisend: Go to Automations > Create > Abandoned Cart. Add SMS branch for 2x recovery rates.
2. Test with your own cart—expect 10-15% recovery initially.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Omnisend: Behavior-based (browsed products, order value). Mailchimp: Tag-based, basic behaviors.

**Tip**: Segment by purchase history in Omnisend for VIP flows—send exclusive discounts to repeat buyers.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp: Campaign performance, revenue tracking (with integrations). Omnisend: Ecommerce-native ROI, attributed revenue per channel.

**Pro Tip**: Track UTM links in both. Omnisend's heatmaps show engagement hotspots.

### Omnichannel Features
Omnisend: Email + SMS + Push. Send SMS for high-urgency (e.g., "Your cart expires soon!"). Mailchimp: Email/SMS add-ons extra cost.

**Real-World Example**: Ecom stores using Omnisend report 30% higher engagement with SMS.

**Winner**: Omnisend for features.

## Integrations: Connect Your Tech Stack

Mailchimp: 300+ native (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Strong for general marketing.

Omnisend: 100+, but laser-focused on ecommerce (Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, Klaviyo alternatives).

**Advice**: If you're on Shopify, Omnisend's one-click sync beats Mailchimp's app. Use Zapier for both to connect CRMs like HubSpot.

## Customer Support and Resources

Mailchimp: 24/7 email/chat for paid, huge knowledge base, free courses.

Omnisend: Live chat/email (all plans), dedicated onboarding for Pro+, active Facebook community.

**Tip**: Join Omnisend's webinars for ecommerce templates—faster ramp-up than Mailchimp's forums.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for volume of resources.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros**:

- Vast template library

**Cons**:
- Expensive scaling
- Limited ecommerce automations
- SMS extra

### Omnisend
**Pros**:
- Omnichannel (SMS/push free on paid)
- Ecommerce automations
- Better ROI tracking

**Cons**:
- Free plan contact limit low
- Less general marketing focus

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Mailchimp if**:
- You're a blogger, service business, or nonprofit.
- Need free forever for <500 contacts.
- Want simple newsletters.

**Pick Omnisend if**:
- Running an online store (Shopify et al.).
- Prioritize automation revenue recovery.
- Want SMS/push without add-ons.

**Migration Tip**: Export Mailchimp lists as CSV, import to Omnisend in 5 mins. Rebuild automations first.

## Conclusion: The Verdict

Mailchimp is the safe, versatile choice for general email marketing—perfect for startups testing waters. Omnisend pulls ahead for ecommerce with superior automations, omnichannel, and value at scale. If your revenue ties to online sales, switch to Omnisend today; expect quicker ROI.

Test both free plans side-by-side. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%), clicks (2-5%), and conversions. Whichever boosts your goals wins.
