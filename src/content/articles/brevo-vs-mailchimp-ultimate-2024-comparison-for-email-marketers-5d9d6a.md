# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses building their email lists. Both are popular platforms with robust features for email campaigns, automations, and audience management. This in-depth comparison breaks down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more to help you decide based on your needs—whether you're a solopreneur, small business, or growing team.

We'll focus on real-world use cases, drawing from official docs and user feedback. No hype, just facts to guide your choice.

## Pricing Breakdown: Free Plans and Paid Tiers

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but they differ significantly in limits and scaling costs.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to **500 contacts** and **1,000 email sends per month**. It's great for testing but quickly limits growing lists. Features like basic automations and landing pages are included, but advanced segmentation is paywalled.

Paid plans (billed monthly):
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/mo for 500 contacts (up to 50,000 sends). Adds A/B testing and mobile-optimized templates.
- **Standard**: $20/mo for 500 contacts (unlimited sends). Includes dynamic content and automation branching.
- **Premium**: $350/mo for 500 contacts. Enterprise-level reporting and dedicated support.

Contacts beyond base tiers add costs (e.g., $50 for 50k contacts on Essentials). Annual billing saves 15-20%. Watch for add-ons like postcards or social ads.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo shines with a **free plan offering unlimited contacts and 300 emails per day** (about 9,000/month). No contact caps make it ideal for list-building without immediate upgrades.

Paid plans (monthly):
- **Starter**: $25/mo for 20,000 emails (unlimited contacts). Removes daily limits, adds sales platform basics.
- **Business**: $65/mo for 20,000 emails. Full automation, transactional emails, and SMS/WhatsApp.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing. Priority support and advanced API.

Brevo's model scales better for high-volume senders without contact penalties. Transactional emails (e.g., order confirmations) are pay-per-use, starting at $0.001/email.

**Practical Advice**: If your list exceeds 500 contacts early, Brevo's free plan saves money. Mailchimp suits low-volume hobbyists. Calculate your monthly sends: use Brevo's pricing calculator for projections.

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools for Campaigns and Growth

Both platforms cover email essentials, but nuances matter.

### Email Builder and Templates
- **Mailchimp**: Drag-and-drop editor with 100+ responsive templates. High customization via code blocks. Pre-built content blocks speed setup.
- **Brevo**: Intuitive block-based editor with 45+ templates. Strong mobile preview and AMP support for interactive emails.

**Winner for Beginners**: Mailchimp's library feels more polished.

### Automation Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Classic automations (welcome series, abandoned cart) with branching logic on Standard+. Customer journey builder on Premium.
- **Brevo**: Unlimited automations on free/paid. Multi-channel (email + SMS), A/B testing in flows, and resale/lead scoring.

**Practical Tip**: For e-commerce, Brevo's cart abandonment sequences integrate natively with Shopify/WooCommerce. Test simple flows first—Brevo's visual editor reduces setup time by 20-30% per user reports.

### Segmentation and Analytics
Both segment by opens, clicks, location. Mailchimp offers behavioral tagging; Brevo excels in custom events and real-time data sync.

Analytics: Mailchimp's reports include revenue tracking; Brevo provides heatmaps and deliverability insights.

### Additional Features
| Feature | Mailchimp | Brevo |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Landing Pages | Yes (basic on free) | Yes (advanced forms) |
| SMS/WhatsApp | Add-on ($10+/mo) | Native on Business+ |
| Integrations | 300+ (Zapier, Google) | 300+ (strong CRM focus) |
| Deliverability | 99%+ claimed | 99%+, EU-hosted for GDPR |

**Advice**: Integrate with your CRM early. Brevo pairs well with HubSpot; Mailchimp with Google Workspace.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is beginner-friendly with guided tours and a content optimizer score. Onboarding emails teach basics in 10 minutes.

Brevo feels modern but has a steeper curve for automations. Dashboard unifies marketing/sales data, great for teams.

**User Feedback**: G2 rates Mailchimp 4.5/5 for ease (easier for non-techies); Brevo 4.6/5 overall but praised for scalability.

**Tip**: Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email. Switch to Brevo once hitting 1,000 subscribers for better ROI.

## Customer Support and Community

- **Mailchimp**: 24/7 email/chat on paid; self-serve knowledge base. Huge community forums.
- **Brevo**: Live chat 24/5 on paid; phone for Enterprise. Active academy with certifications.

Both score high (Mailchimp 4.4/5, Brevo 4.5/5 on support). Brevo responds faster for pricing queries.

**Pro Tip**: Join Reddit's r/Emailmarketing for peer advice. Test support pre-signup via free chat.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros
- Polished templates and AI content generator.
- Strong U.S. deliverability.
- Free plan for tiny lists.

Cons:
- Contact-based pricing hurts growth.
- Automations locked behind tiers.

### Brevo Pros
- Unlimited contacts free.
- Multi-channel (SMS cheap at $0.045/message).
- Transactional + marketing emails.

Cons:
- Fewer templates.
- Daily send limits on free.

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Mailchimp** if: Under 500 contacts, need beautiful designs, U.S.-focused, or prefer all-in-one with ads/social.
- **Pick Brevo** if: Growing lists, e-commerce with SMS, international (GDPR-compliant), or high-volume transactional needs.

**Scaling Advice**: Migrate lists via CSV—both support it. Budget $25-50/mo initially. Track ROI with UTM tags.

## Conclusion

Brevo edges out for most growing businesses with flexible pricing and multi-channel power, especially post-500 contacts. Mailchimp remains king for design-focused beginners on tight lists. Test both free plans: import 100 contacts, send a campaign, build an automation. Your choice depends on volume and channels—Brevo for scale, Mailchimp for simplicity.
