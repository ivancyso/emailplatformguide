# Omnisend Review: Is It the Best Email Marketing Tool for Your Ecommerce Store?

If you're running an online store and looking to boost sales through email marketing, you've likely come across Omnisend. This platform specializes in ecommerce, offering multichannel campaigns via email, SMS, and web push notifications. In this Omnisend review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, usability, and whether it's worth your investment. Whether you're on Shopify, WooCommerce, or another platform, we'll provide practical advice to help you decide.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is a marketing automation platform designed primarily for ecommerce businesses. Launched in 2014, it helps store owners create targeted campaigns that drive revenue. Unlike general email tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend focuses on omnichannel marketing—combining email with SMS and push notifications for higher engagement.

It integrates seamlessly with popular ecommerce platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, and Magento. You can pull customer data directly from your store to segment audiences and automate workflows. Over 70,000 brands use it, including those with high-volume sales.

**Practical Tip:** Start by connecting your store in under 5 minutes via the Omnisend dashboard. This auto-imports customer data, orders, and products for personalized messaging.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs robust features tailored for ecommerce. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Campaigns and Automation

Create drag-and-drop emails with ecommerce-specific blocks like product carousels, order summaries, and dynamic discount codes. Automation workflows include welcome series, abandoned cart recovery, back-in-stock alerts, and post-purchase upsells.

**Practical Advice:** For abandoned carts, set up a 1-hour email reminder with the exact items left behind, followed by a 24-hour SMS nudge. Users report 20-30% recovery rates—test timing with A/B splits.

### SMS and Web Push Notifications

SMS marketing is a standout: send promotional texts or transactional messages with high open rates (up to 98%). Web push works for browser notifications without needing an app.

**Pro Tip:** Use SMS for flash sales (e.g., "20% off ends in 2 hours!") but comply with opt-in rules. Segment by purchase history to avoid spam flags.

### Popups, Forms, and List Building

On-site popups capture emails with exit-intent triggers, spin-to-win wheels, or time delays. These feed into segments for targeted campaigns.

**How to Implement:** Design a popup offering 10% off for email signups. Place it on high-traffic pages like product listings. Track conversion rates in the dashboard.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Advanced segmentation based on behavior, purchases, and custom events. Personalize with first-name merges, recommended products, or location-based offers.

**Advice:** Create segments like "VIP customers" (3+ purchases) for exclusive discounts. Use dynamic content blocks to show different products per segment in one email.

### Reporting and Analytics

Track open rates, clicks, revenue per recipient (RPR), and ROI. Ecommerce-specific metrics show attributed revenue from campaigns.

**Tip:** Focus on RPR over open rates—it's a direct sales indicator. Export reports to CSV for deeper analysis in Google Sheets.

## Omnisend Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

Omnisend offers a free plan for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month—ideal for testing. Paid plans are contact-based:

- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts) up to $59/month (10k). Includes most features.
- **Pro:** $59/month (500) to $542/month (10k). Adds advanced reporting, A/B testing, and API access.

No contracts; monthly or yearly billing with discounts. SMS credits sold separately (e.g., $0.016 per message in US).

**Value Check:** For small stores (<2k contacts), Standard suffices. Compare to Klaviyo (similar pricing but ecommerce-only focus). Scale as your list grows without data migration hassles.

|------|----------|-----------------|------------|
| Free | 250 | $0 | 500 emails |
| Standard | 500-100k | $16-$759 | Unlimited emails |
| Pro | 500-100k | $59-$1,020 | Advanced features |

## Ease of Use and Integrations

The interface is intuitive, even for beginners. Drag-and-drop editor rivals BeeFree or Klaviyo's. Mobile app for on-the-go management.

Integrations: 140+ apps including Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, Zapier, and payment gateways like Stripe.

**Onboarding Advice:** Follow the 7-day setup wizard—it guides you through list import, first workflow, and campaign launch. Use pre-built templates to go live fast.

## Customer Support and Resources

24/7 email/chat support on paid plans; free users get help center access. Extensive knowledge base, video tutorials, and webinars.

**User Feedback:** Response times average 1-2 hours. Community forum for peer tips.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-centric features save time.
- Strong automation ROI (many report 5x returns).
- Generous free plan.
- Multichannel without extra tools.

### Cons
- SMS costs add up for high-volume.
- Advanced reporting gated behind Pro.
- Limited non-ecommerce use cases.

Real user reviews (from G2/Capterra): 4.7/5 stars, praising automation but noting occasional deliverability tweaks needed.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for:
- Shopify/WooCommerce stores with 500+ customers.
- Brands wanting SMS/push alongside email.
- Marketers prioritizing revenue automation.

Alternatives: Klaviyo (deeper Shopify integration), ActiveCampaign (CRM focus), or Mailchimp (budget generalists).

**Decision Framework:** If your store generates $10k+/month and email drives <20% sales, Omnisend can optimize that. Start free to test.

## Conclusion

Omnisend earns its spot as a top ecommerce email marketing tool. Its multichannel approach, ecommerce templates, and automation deliver tangible revenue lifts without complexity. While pricing scales with contacts, the free tier and ROI justify it for growing stores.

Ready to try? Sign up for the free plan, import your list, and launch an abandoned cart flow today. Track results over 30 days—if RPR exceeds $0.50 per recipient, it's a keeper.
