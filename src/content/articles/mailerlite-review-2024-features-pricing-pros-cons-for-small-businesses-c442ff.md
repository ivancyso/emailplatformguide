# MailerLite Review 2024: Features, Pricing, Pros & Cons for Small Businesses

If you're a small business owner, blogger, or marketer searching for an affordable yet powerful email marketing platform, you've likely come across MailerLite. In this in-depth **MailerLite review**, we'll dive into its features, pricing, ease of use, and whether it's the right choice for your needs. With a focus on simplicity and value, MailerLite has gained popularity among users who want professional results without a steep learning curve or high costs.

MailerLite positions itself as an all-in-one solution for email campaigns, automations, landing pages, and more. Founded in 2010 and based in Lithuania, it serves over 2 million users worldwide. But does it live up to the hype? Let's break it down step by step.

## What is MailerLite?

MailerLite is a cloud-based email marketing service designed primarily for small to medium-sized businesses, freelancers, and content creators. It offers tools to build email lists, design campaigns, automate workflows, and track performance. Unlike enterprise-level platforms like Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign, MailerLite emphasizes affordability and user-friendliness.

Key highlights include:
- A forever-free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month.
- Drag-and-drop email editor.
- Built-in website builder, popups, and forms.
- Compliance with GDPR, CAN-SPAM, and other regulations.

It's particularly appealing for those new to email marketing, as it avoids overwhelming dashboards while still providing robust functionality.

## Key Features of MailerLite

MailerLite packs a punch in features without complicating the interface. Here's a closer look at what it offers.

### Drag-and-Drop Email Builder

The email editor is intuitive and responsive. You can choose from 80+ customizable templates or start from scratch. Practical advice: Use the grid-based editor for precise layouts—align images and text blocks easily to maintain brand consistency. Test mobile previews before sending to ensure 100% deliverability on all devices.

### Automation Workflows

Automations are a standout. Create triggers like welcome series, abandoned cart reminders, or re-engagement campaigns. For example, set up a workflow where new subscribers receive a three-email nurture sequence over a week. Tip: Segment your audience by behavior (e.g., opened last email) to boost open rates by 20-30%.

### Landing Pages and Forms

Build unlimited landing pages with a simple builder—no coding needed. Embed subscription forms, popups, or fixed bars on your site. Pro tip: A/B test popup timing (e.g., 30 seconds vs. exit-intent) to optimize opt-in rates. Integrate with WordPress or other CMS seamlessly.

### Website Builder

Newer addition: A full website builder with e-commerce capabilities. Create up to 10 pages on the free plan. It's basic but effective for one-page sites or lead magnets. Advice: Pair it with email automations for a complete funnel.

### Analytics and Reporting

Track opens, clicks, bounces, and unsubscribes with detailed reports. Heatmaps show click locations. Practical use: Review campaign performance weekly and refine subject lines—aim for 20-30% open rates by personalizing with merge tags like *|FNAME|*.

### Integrations

Connects with 1,000+ apps via Zapier, including Shopify, WooCommerce, Google Analytics, and Stripe. For e-commerce users, sync customer data automatically for targeted campaigns.

## Pros and Cons of MailerLite

### Pros
- **Affordable**: Best free plan in the industry.
- **User-Friendly**: 4.6/5 on G2 and Capterra from 2,000+ reviews.
- **High Deliverability**: 99% rates reported.
- **No Hidden Fees**: Unlimited emails on paid plans.
- **Great Support**: 24/7 live chat, extensive knowledge base.

### Cons
- **Limited Advanced Segmentation**: No deep behavioral scoring like ActiveCampaign.
- **Basic E-commerce Tools**: Lacks full CRM features.
- **No Native SMS**: Relies on integrations.
- **Design Templates**: Functional but not as flashy as ConvertKit.

## Who Should Use MailerLite?

Ideal for:
- Solopreneurs and bloggers building lists under 10,000.
- Small e-commerce stores needing basic automations.
- Non-profits or agencies on tight budgets.

Avoid if you need complex multi-channel marketing or enterprise-scale personalization.

## MailerLite vs. Alternatives

- **Vs. Mailchimp**: MailerLite is cheaper with better free tier; Mailchimp has more templates but bloated UI.
- **Vs. ConvertKit**: MailerLite wins on price; ConvertKit excels in creator-focused tagging.
- **Vs. ActiveCampaign**: MailerLite for beginners; ActiveCampaign for advanced automations (but 3x pricier).

Practical comparison tip: Import a test list to each platform and run a sample campaign to feel the interface.
