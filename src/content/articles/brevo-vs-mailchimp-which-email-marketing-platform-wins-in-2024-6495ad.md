# Brevo vs Mailchimp: The Ultimate Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp—the two popular contenders—helps narrow it down. This in-depth Brevo vs Mailchimp comparison covers pricing, features, ease of use, deliverability, support, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or scaling e-commerce store, we'll provide practical advice to guide your decision.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing tool. Both platforms offer free tiers, but their structures differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp uses a freemium model:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Ideal for testing, but limits scale quickly.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/month for 500 contacts (billed annually), scaling to $350+ for 50,000 contacts.
- **Standard**: From $20/month, adds advanced automation.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for large lists and A/B testing.

Mailchimp bills based on contact volume, with overage fees if you exceed limits. It's straightforward but can get pricey for growing lists—expect $100+/month for 10,000 contacts.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo focuses on send volume with unlimited contacts on paid plans:
- **Free Plan**: 300 emails/day (about 9,000/month), no daily cap removal.
- **Starter**: €25/month (~$27 USD) for 20,000 emails/month, unlimited contacts.
- **Business**: €65/month for 20,000 emails, adds automation and segmentation.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high volume.

Brevo shines for high-volume senders on a budget. For example, sending 20,000 emails to unlimited contacts costs less than Mailchimp's equivalent for 10,000 contacts.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your monthly sends and contacts. Use Brevo if you send frequently to large lists (e.g., newsletters). Opt for Mailchimp if under 5,000 contacts and low volume. Always factor in annual discounts—both offer 10-20% off.

## Core Features: Automation, Templates, and Segmentation

Both platforms pack robust features, but their strengths vary.

### Email Templates and Design
- **Mailchimp**: Renowned for drag-and-drop builder with 100+ professional templates. Mobile-responsive out-of-the-box. Great for non-designers.
- **Brevo**: Solid builder with 40+ templates, customizable blocks. Less polished than Mailchimp but includes transactional email templates.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for visual appeal.

### Automation Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Classic automations like welcome series, abandoned cart (with integrations). Customer Journey Builder in higher plans.
- **Brevo**: Advanced workflows including RSS campaigns, drip sequences, and SMS integration. Triggers based on opens, clicks, etc.

Brevo edges out for multi-channel (email + SMS/WhatsApp).

### List Segmentation and Personalization
Both support tags, custom fields, and behavioral segmentation. Mailchimp's merge tags are intuitive; Brevo offers dynamic content blocks for personalization.

**Practical Advice**: Start with simple segments (e.g., by purchase history). Test personalization: "Hi [FirstName]" boosts opens by 20-30% per industry benchmarks. Integrate with Google Analytics for deeper insights.

## Ease of Use and User Interface

- **Mailchimp**: Beginner-friendly dashboard, intuitive navigation. Setup takes minutes—import CSV, design, send.
- **Brevo**: Clean interface, but steeper learning curve for automations. Mobile app for on-the-go management.

**Practical Advice**: If your team lacks tech skills, demo Mailchimp first (14-day trial). Brevo suits users comfortable with CRM-like tools.

## Deliverability and Analytics

High inbox placement is crucial—aim for 95%+ rates.

- **Mailchimp**: Excellent reputation (98%+ deliverability). Detailed reports on opens, clicks, bounces. Heatmaps in premium.
- **Brevo**: Strong 97%+ deliverability, IP warming for new senders. Analytics include revenue tracking and unsubscribe insights.

Both comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM. **Practical Advice**: Warm up domains gradually (start 100/day). Clean lists monthly to avoid spam traps—tools like NeverBounce integrate with both.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **Mailchimp**: 24/7 email/chat for paid users. Extensive help center, webinars, templates galore.
- **Brevo**: Live chat/email (business hours), phone for enterprise. Academy with tutorials, responsive community forum.

Mailchimp wins for self-servers; Brevo for direct help.

**Practical Advice**: Leverage free resources first—Mailchimp's blog has 1,000+ guides. For issues, paid plans unlock priority support.

## Integrations and Scalability

- **Mailchimp**: 300+ native (Shopify, WordPress, Google Workspace). Zapier for more.
- **Brevo**: 300+ including e-commerce (WooCommerce), CRM (HubSpot), and SMS gateways. API for custom builds.

Both scale well: Mailchimp for agencies, Brevo for transactional-heavy sites.

**Practical Advice**: Check your stack—e.g., Brevo + WooCommerce for abandoned carts saves time. Use Zapier as a bridge if needed (free tier: 100 tasks/month).

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

### Choose Brevo If:
- Budget-conscious with high send volumes.
- Need SMS/transactional emails (e.g., order confirmations).
- Unlimited contacts appeal (growing lists).
- Multi-channel marketing (email + chat).

**Example**: E-commerce stores sending 50,000+ emails/month under $100.

### Choose Mailchimp If:
- Beginner needing polished templates.
- Small lists (<10k contacts).
- Value brand ecosystem (Intuit QuickBooks integration).
- Focus on creative campaigns.

**Example**: Bloggers/freelancers starting with newsletters.

**Practical Advice**: Run A/B tests on 100-subscriber lists. Migrate via CSV export/import—both support it seamlessly. Trial both for 14 days.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp isn't a clear knockout; it depends on your needs. Brevo offers better value for scaling senders and multi-channel, while Mailchimp excels in ease and design for beginners. For most small businesses (under 5k contacts), start with Mailchimp's free plan. Growing teams? Brevo's pricing wins long-term.

Assess your volume, tech comfort, and integrations. Whichever you pick, focus on list-building (opt-ins via popups) and compliance. Track ROI: aim for $38 return per $1 spent (DMA benchmark).
