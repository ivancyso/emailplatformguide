# MailerLite vs Mailchimp 2024: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins?

Choosing between MailerLite and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email list or scaling your marketing efforts. Both are popular platforms designed for businesses of all sizes, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp has long been a household name with robust features, while MailerLite emphasizes affordability and simplicity.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down key aspects like pricing, ease of use, features, integrations, support, and deliverability. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or marketer evaluating options, this guide provides practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities as of 2024. By the end, you'll know exactly which tool fits your workflow.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first deciding factor. Both offer free plans, but they differ significantly in limits and scaling costs.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly email sends. Paid plans start at $13/month for Essentials (up to 500 contacts, 5,000 sends), scaling to $350+/month for Premium tiers with advanced automation. They recently simplified pricing into Free, Essentials, Standard, and Premium, but costs rise quickly with audience size. For example, 5,000 contacts on Standard costs around $75/month.

**Practical Tip:** If your list is under 500, Mailchimp's free tier is great for testing basic campaigns. But watch out for send limits—exceeding them forces an upgrade.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite shines here with a generous free plan: unlimited emails to 1,000 subscribers (time-unlimited). Growing plan starts at $10/month for up to 500 contacts, $20 for 1,000, and scales linearly—$150 for 25,000 contacts. No send limits on paid plans, and they include features like popups and forms for free.

**Practical Tip:** For bootstrapped startups or bloggers with 500-2,500 subscribers, MailerLite saves 20-50% over Mailchimp. Use their pricing calculator to project costs based on your growth rate.

**Winner:** MailerLite for most small-to-medium lists due to lower costs and higher free limits.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly Interfaces

### Mailchimp's Drag-and-Drop Editor
Mailchimp's editor is intuitive with a vast template library (hundreds pre-built). New users can launch a campaign in minutes. However, advanced customizations require CSS knowledge, and the interface feels cluttered with upselling prompts.

**Practical Advice:** Start with their guided setup wizard for audience import and first campaign. Ideal if you prefer visual builders without coding.

### MailerLite's Clean Editor
MailerLite's editor is minimalist—drag-and-drop blocks for text, images, buttons. Fewer templates (50+), but they're mobile-responsive out-of-the-box. No clutter, faster loading times.

**Practical Advice:** Perfect for non-designers. Test A/B subjects directly in the editor; embed forms easily on WordPress via shortcodes.

**Winner:** Tie—Mailchimp for template variety, MailerLite for speed and simplicity.

## Core Features Head-to-Head

### Automation and Workflows
- **Mailchimp:** Single-step automations on free plan; multi-step (journeys) from Standard ($75+/mo). Includes abandoned cart, re-engagement.
- **MailerLite:** Full automation builder on free plan (up to 1,000 subs). Drip sequences, tags-based triggers, RSS campaigns.

**Practical Tip:** For welcome series or lead nurturing, MailerLite's free automations let you automate sooner. Mailchimp excels in e-commerce with behavioral triggers.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both support tags, groups, and custom fields. Mailchimp adds behavioral segmentation (opens/clicks). MailerLite's is simpler but powerful for small lists.

**Practical Advice:** Segment by engagement: Send re-engagement flows to inactive subs quarterly. Use dynamic content blocks for personalized recommendations.

### Landing Pages and Forms
- MailerLite: Unlimited free landing pages, popups, forms with A/B testing.
- Mailchimp: Basic on free; advanced in higher tiers.

**Pro Tip:** Drive traffic with MailerLite popups—exit-intent works well for 10-20% opt-in boosts.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp offers detailed reports (revenue tracking). MailerLite provides opens, clicks, unsubscribes with geographic data.

**Practical Advice:** Track ROI by tagging UTM links; both integrate with Google Analytics.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Shopify, WordPress, Zapier). MailerLite supports 1,000+ via Zapier, native WooCommerce/Google Sheets.

**Practical Tip:** If you're on Shopify, Mailchimp's depth wins. For general use, MailerLite + Zapier covers 90% of needs without premium add-ons.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **Mailchimp:** Email/ticket support; chat for paid. Extensive academy, but community forums are hit-or-miss.
- **MailerLite:** 24/7 live chat (all plans), email, knowledge base. Highly rated for responsiveness.

**Practical Advice:** Test chat during your free trial—MailerLite resolves issues in minutes, great for urgent launches.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Both maintain 95%+ deliverability. Mailchimp's domain authentication is automatic; MailerLite requires manual DKIM/DMARC setup.

**Pro Tip:** Warm up new domains gradually (50-100 sends/day) on either to hit inboxes. Comply with GDPR via built-in double opt-in.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros:** Cheaper, generous free plan, simple UI, strong automation.
**Cons:** Fewer templates, less e-com focus.

### Mailchimp
**Pros:** Brand recognition, advanced analytics, vast integrations.
**Cons:** Pricey scaling, limited free automations.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick MailerLite if:** Budget-conscious, small list (<10k subs), need quick automations/popups. Ideal for bloggers, freelancers, agencies.
- **Pick Mailchimp if:** E-commerce heavy, large audiences, need complex journeys. Suits established brands.

**Migration Tip:** Both offer CSV imports. Export from one, clean data (remove bounces), import to test.

## Conclusion: Your Best Pick in 2024

MailerLite edges out for most users—especially startups—thanks to unbeatable pricing and ease. Mailchimp remains king for feature-rich e-commerce. Start with free trials: Sign up for both, import 100 contacts, send a test campaign. Measure setup time and costs against your goals.
