# Best Email Marketing for Ecommerce: Top 7 Platforms for Shopify Stores in 2024

Email marketing remains one of the highest-ROI channels for ecommerce businesses, with average returns of $36 for every $1 spent according to DMA data. For Shopify store owners and other ecommerce platforms, choosing the right email marketing tool is crucial for driving sales through automations like abandoned cart recovery, welcome series, and personalized product recommendations. In this roundup, we evaluate the best email marketing for ecommerce based on integrations, ease of use, pricing, and real-world performance for online stores.

We'll cover key features to prioritize, in-depth looks at top platforms, a comparison table, and practical tips for implementation. Whether you're scaling a dropshipping store or a DTC brand, these tools can help you nurture leads and boost repeat purchases.

## Why Email Marketing is Essential for Ecommerce Success

Ecommerce businesses thrive on repeat customers, and email captures 4x more customers than social media per Campaign Monitor stats. Key benefits include:

- **Personalization at Scale**: Segment lists by purchase history, behavior, and location.
- **Automation Workflows**: Trigger emails for cart abandonment (recovering 10-30% of lost sales), post-purchase upsells, and win-back campaigns.
- **Revenue Attribution**: Track direct sales from emails to measure true impact.

For Shopify users, seamless app integrations pull customer data automatically, making setup straightforward.

## Key Features to Look for in Ecommerce Email Tools

Not all email platforms are built for ecommerce. Prioritize these:

### Abandoned Cart Recovery

Automated sequences that remind shoppers of items left behind. Look for multi-email flows with dynamic product images.

### Product Recommendations

AI-driven suggestions based on browsing or past buys, increasing AOV by 20-30%.

### Deep Integrations

Native support for Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce. Sync orders, inventory, and customer data in real-time.

### Advanced Segmentation & Analytics

RFM analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary value), revenue per email, and A/B testing.

### SMS & Cross-Channel

Many top tools now bundle SMS for higher open rates (98% vs. 20-30% for email).

Pricing typically starts free for small lists (under 1,000 subscribers) and scales with contacts/emails sent.

## Top 7 Best Email Marketing Platforms for Ecommerce

### 1. Klaviyo

Klaviyo dominates ecommerce email marketing, powering brands like Casper and Everlane. Its Shopify integration is unmatched, syncing data every 15 minutes.

**Pros**:
- Built-in flows for abandoned carts, browsed-but-not-bought, and reviews.
- Predictive analytics for churn prediction and lifetime value.
- Visual flow builder for complex automations.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve for beginners.
- Pricing jumps at higher volumes.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts; $20/mo for 500, scales to $100+ for 10k.

**Best For**: Mid-to-large Shopify stores focused on data-driven personalization.

### 2. Omnisend

Omnisend is tailored for ecommerce with omnichannel support (email + SMS + push). Integrates with Shopify, BigCommerce, and Woo.

**Pros**:
- Pre-built ecommerce workflows (e.g., cart recovery averages 17% recovery rate).
- Pop-up forms and popup campaigns for list growth.
- Free plan includes 500 emails/mo.

**Cons**:
- Limited reporting depth compared to Klaviyo.
- SMS credits add up.

**Pricing**: Free for 250 contacts; Standard $16/mo for 500.

**Best For**: Small-to-medium stores wanting SMS alongside email.

### 3. Mailchimp

The OG email tool, Mailchimp has evolved with ecommerce features via Shopify integration.

**Pros**:
- Intuitive drag-and-drop editor.
- Customer Journey Builder for automations.
- Strong A/B testing and analytics.

**Cons**:
- Ecommerce features lag behind specialists.
- Pricing based on contacts, not sends, can be pricey.

**Pricing**: Free up to 500 contacts/1k sends; Essentials $13/mo.

**Best For**: Beginners or multi-channel marketers already using Mailchimp.

### 4. ActiveCampaign

Excels in advanced automations and CRM integration, great for ecommerce with custom fields.

**Pros**:
- Machine learning for send-time optimization.
- Site tracking for behavioral emails.
- Deep Shopify sync.

**Cons**:
- Overkill for simple lists.
- Interface feels dated.

**Pricing**: $29/mo for 1k contacts.

**Best For**: Stores needing CRM + email in one.

### 5. Drip

Ecommerce-specific with revenue tracking and workflow recipes.

**Pros**:
- Smart send times and product feeds.
- Conditional split testing.

**Cons**:
- No free plan.
- Fewer templates.

**Pricing**: $39/mo for 1k.

**Best For**: Agencies managing multiple stores.

### 6. Klaviyo Alternatives: Glew & Retention.com

Glew focuses on analytics-heavy ecommerce; Retention.com on first-party data capture.

### 7. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Budget-friendly with transactional emails.

**Pros**: Unlimited contacts, pay-per-send.

**Pricing**: Free 300/day; $25/mo.

**Best For**: Bootstrapped stores.

## Practical Advice: How to Choose and Implement the Best Email Tool

1. **Audit Your Needs**: List size? Tech stack (Shopify/Woo)? Team size? Start with free trials.

2. **Set Up Core Flows**:
   - **Welcome Series**: 3-5 emails introducing brand, offering 10% off.
   - **Abandoned Cart**: Email 1 at 1hr, 2 at 24hr with discount.
   - **Post-Purchase**: Thank you + upsell at 3 days.

3. **Grow Your List Legally**: Use popups with clear value (e.g., '10% off for signing up'). Comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM.

4. **Optimize for Revenue**:
   - Segment by LTV: VIPs get exclusive previews.
   - A/B test subjects (e.g., 'Your Cart Misses You' vs. 'Forgot Something?').
   - Track UTM parameters for attribution.

5. **Common Pitfalls**: Avoid bought lists (high spam). Test mobile rendering. Monitor deliverability (aim >95%).

6. **Migration Tips**: Export CSV from old tool, map custom fields, test automations.

Integrate with Google Analytics and Facebook Pixel for full-funnel tracking.
