# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for creators, small businesses, and marketers seeking affordable, powerful solutions. This head-to-head comparison dives into pricing, features, usability, and real-world performance to help you decide.

Both platforms excel in automation and list management, but they cater to slightly different audiences. MailerLite shines for budget-conscious beginners with its generous free plan, while ConvertKit targets content creators with advanced tagging and monetization tools. Let's break it down.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Here's how they stack up based on current plans (as of 2024):

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, forms, and landing pages.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers (scales with list size).
- **Advanced**: $21/month for 500 subscribers, adding A/B testing and web push notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for large lists.

MailerLite keeps costs low even as your list grows, with no subscriber-based overages.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (limited features; no advanced automations).
- **Creator**: Starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month, unlocking unlimited forms, landing pages, and commerce tools.
- **Higher Tiers**: Scale up to $2,485/month for massive lists.

ConvertKit bills based on subscribers, which can get pricey quickly. No long-term contracts, and both offer 14-30 day trials.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your costs using their pricing calculators. For lists under 1,000, MailerLite's free tier is unbeatable. Test ConvertKit if you need creator-specific features like paid newsletters.

## Ease of Use and Interface

### MailerLite: Beginner-Friendly Drag-and-Drop
MailerLite's interface is clean and intuitive. The drag-and-drop editor lets you build emails in minutes—no coding required. Key highlights:
- Visual campaign builder with 80+ responsive templates.
- One-click blocks for images, buttons, and social feeds.
- Mobile app for on-the-go management.

New users report setup in under 10 minutes. It's ideal if you're transitioning from basic tools like Mailchimp.

### ConvertKit: Creator-Centric Dashboard
ConvertKit's dashboard emphasizes sequences and subscriber journeys. The visual builder is straightforward but more automation-focused:
- Block-based editor with creator templates (e.g., newsletters, broadcasts).
- Tagging system over traditional segments for smarter personalization.

It's slightly steeper for absolute beginners but rewarding for those building funnels.

**Practical Advice**: Sign up for trials. Import a test list (both support CSV) and send a sample campaign. MailerLite wins for speed; ConvertKit for depth.

## Core Features Breakdown

### Automation and Workflows
- **MailerLite**: Trigger-based automations (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart). Multi-step workflows with delays and conditions. Free plan includes basics; advanced adds RSS campaigns.
- **ConvertKit**: Powerful sequences (automated email series). Tag triggers enable complex behaviors like "if subscriber buys, tag as VIP." Pro plan unlocks unlimited sequences.

ConvertKit edges out for sophisticated creator automations.

### Landing Pages and Forms
Both offer drag-and-drop builders:
- **MailerLite**: Unlimited popups, forms, and landing pages (even on free). Embedded or fixed-position opt-ins with A/B testing.
- **ConvertKit**: Unlimited on paid plans. Strong focus on incentive forms (e.g., lead magnets). Integrates with commerce for paid subscriptions.

**H3: Integrations**
- MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, plus native WordPress, WooCommerce.
- ConvertKit: Deep integrations with Gumroad, Teachable, Shopify. Native commerce for digital products.

### Analytics and Reporting
- MailerLite: Open rates, clicks, bounces, revenue tracking. Heatmaps on free plan.
- ConvertKit: Subscriber activity reports, revenue per subscriber, A/B testing insights.

Both comply with GDPR and offer unsubscribe management.

**Practical Advice**: Prioritize based on needs. Use MailerLite for simple lead gen; ConvertKit for e-commerce funnels. Always enable double opt-in to maintain list health.

## Customer Support and Reliability

MailerLite offers 24/7 live chat, email support, and a knowledge base. High deliverability (99%+ claimed).

ConvertKit provides email support (priority for Pro), extensive guides, and a creator-focused help center. Also boasts excellent deliverability.

**Practical Advice**: Check uptime via tools like UptimeRobot. Both score 99.9%+. Join their Facebook groups for user tips.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
Small businesses, bloggers starting out, or anyone on a tight budget. Perfect if you need quick wins with forms and basic automations. Migrate easily from free tools—export/import is seamless.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Content creators, podcasters, course sellers. If tagging and revenue tracking are key, it's worth the investment. Ideal for scaling newsletters into businesses.

**Migration Tip**: Export subscribers as CSV from your current tool. Test automations with a segment first to avoid errors.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage and goals. MailerLite delivers unbeatable value for beginners and small lists, with simplicity at its core. ConvertKit empowers creators with tools to monetize audiences effectively, though at a premium.

For most users under 5,000 subscribers, start with MailerLite's free plan. Upgrade to ConvertKit if creator features become essential. Whichever you pick, focus on building genuine relationships—consistent value drives opens and conversions.
