# Brevo vs Mailchimp 2024: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins for Your Business?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your customer engagement and drive real revenue growth. In this head-to-head comparison, we'll pit Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp, two of the most popular tools for businesses of all sizes. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or marketing manager, understanding their differences in pricing, features, usability, and support will help you decide.

We'll break down key aspects with practical advice, real-world use cases, and no hype—just facts to guide your choice. By the end, you'll know which platform aligns with your goals, like scaling campaigns or staying within budget.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo is an all-in-one marketing platform that excels in email, SMS, chat, and CRM features. It's particularly strong for transactional emails and businesses needing multi-channel communication. Brevo offers a free plan with unlimited contacts and 300 emails per day, making it accessible for startups.

Key strengths include high deliverability rates (often above 95% based on industry benchmarks), advanced automation workflows, and seamless integration with e-commerce platforms like Shopify and WooCommerce. Practical tip: If your business sends order confirmations or abandoned cart emails alongside newsletters, Brevo's transactional email focus can save you from needing separate tools.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has long been a staple for email marketing, known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and vast template library. Its free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails per month, ideal for beginners testing the waters.

It shines in audience segmentation, A/B testing, and e-commerce integrations via its 'Customer Journey' automations. However, pricing scales quickly with contacts. Advice for users: Mailchimp is great if you're heavily into content creation and need polished, mobile-responsive designs without coding.

Calculate your monthly sends: If over 10K emails, Brevo's pay-as-you-go avoids overages. Small teams should trial both free plans to compare costs realistically.

## Features Face-Off

### Automation and Workflows

Both offer robust automations, but styles differ. Brevo's automation builder is linear and condition-based, perfect for drip campaigns like welcome series or re-engagement. It supports SMS triggers too.

Mailchimp's Customer Journeys use a visual flowchart for complex branching (e.g., based on purchase history). **Tip**: Use Brevo for simple, multi-channel sequences; Mailchimp for behavioral triggers in e-commerce.

### Email Templates and Design

Mailchimp boasts 100+ free, customizable templates with a superior drag-and-drop editor. Brevo has 50+ responsive templates but requires more tweaks for advanced styling.

**Advice**: Beginners love Mailchimp's ease—import content in minutes. Pros using custom HTML? Brevo's editor integrates code cleanly without breaking responsiveness.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Mailchimp excels in behavioral segmentation (e.g., opened last campaign). Brevo offers custom fields, tags, and real-time lists. Both provide open/click rates, bounce handling.

Brevo edges in SMS reporting; Mailchimp in predictive analytics (via Intuit). **Pro tip**: Segment lists weekly—test Brevo's automation scoring vs. Mailchimp's Einstein AI for engagement boosts up to 20%.

### Integrations and Add-Ons

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Brevo supports 300+ too, plus native WhatsApp and CRM sync.

**Practical**: E-commerce users pick Mailchimp for Shopify depth; agencies choose Brevo for SMS/automation bundles.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface feels playful and beginner-friendly, with guided tours. Brevo is straightforward but dashboard-heavy for multi-channel users.

**Advice for newbies**: Start with Mailchimp's tutorials (video-heavy). Scaling teams prefer Brevo's clean lists and quick setup (under 10 minutes for first campaign).

Onboarding: Both have 14-day trials; Brevo's free tier lets you build lists indefinitely.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

Brevo offers email/ticket support (priority for paid), phone for Enterprise, and 99% deliverability via dedicated IPs.

Mailchimp has 24/7 email/chat for paid plans, strong inbox placement (95%+). **Tip**: Warm up domains gradually on both to hit 98% delivery—monitor via Google Postmaster.

Community forums favor Mailchimp for volume, but Brevo's responsive chat wins for urgency.

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Brevo if**:
- Budget-conscious with growing lists
- Need SMS/transactional emails
- Multi-channel marketing (e.g., webinars + email)

**Example**: A SaaS startup sending daily transactional emails—Brevo saves 40% vs. competitors.

**Pick Mailchimp if**:
- Prioritize design and segmentation
- E-commerce with behavioral automations
- Beginner needing hand-holding

**Example**: Boutique store running flash sales—Mailchimp's A/B tools optimize conversions.

**Tiebreaker advice**: Export a sample list to both free plans. Run a test campaign tracking opens/CTRs. Factor in your tech stack—migrate data via CSV/Zapier easily.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp isn't one-size-fits-all. Brevo wins for cost-effective, versatile campaigns ideal for international or SMS-heavy businesses. Mailchimp dominates for creative, analytics-driven marketing with unmatched ease.

For most small businesses (under 5K contacts), start with Brevo's free plan and switch if design needs grow. Larger teams? Compare quotes. Whichever you choose, focus on list hygiene, compliant content (GDPR/CAN-SPAM), and consistent testing—email ROI averages 42:1.
