# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives for 2024: Best Email Marketing Platforms Compared

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its pricing changes, limited automation in lower tiers, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for alternatives. If you're facing similar issues—high fees after 2,000 subscribers, clunky editor, or inadequate support—this guide covers the top Mailchimp alternatives. We'll compare real features, pricing (as of late 2023), pros/cons, and practical tips to help you switch smoothly.

These picks are based on user reviews from G2, Capterra, and Trustpilot, focusing on platforms suitable for solopreneurs, small businesses, and e-commerce stores.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Before diving in, consider common pain points:
- **Pricing**: Mailchimp's Essentials plan jumps to $13/month for 500 contacts, but scales poorly (e.g., $299/month for 50k contacts).
- **Automation**: Basic in free tier; advanced needs higher plans.
- **Deliverability**: Around 85-90% open rates reported, but competitors often edge it out.
- **Editor**: Drag-and-drop is user-friendly but lacks mobile precision.

Switching can save 30-70% on costs while gaining better automations and support. Always export your Mailchimp list via CSV before migrating.

## 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
Brevo offers transactional emails, SMS, chat, and CRM in one dashboard. Unlimited contacts in free plan (300 emails/day).

### Pricing
- Free: 300 emails/day
- Starter: $25/month (20k emails)
- Business: $65/month (20k emails + automation)

### Pros
- Excellent deliverability (98%+ claimed)
- Multi-channel (email + SMS)
- Easy migration from Mailchimp

### Cons
- Automation workflows less visual than Mailchimp

**Best for**: SMBs needing SMS alongside email. Practical tip: Use their import tool to transfer lists in under 10 minutes; test with a small segment first.

## 2. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
Advanced automation, machine learning for split testing, site tracking, and 900+ integrations.

### Pricing
- Starter: $29/month (500 contacts)
- Plus: $49/month (CRM + sales automation)

### Pros
- Deep automations (e.g., if/then branches)
- High deliverability (95%+)
- Detailed reporting

### Cons
- Steeper learning curve

**Best for**: E-commerce and lead nurturing. Tip: Start with their 14-day trial; map Mailchimp tags to ActiveCampaign's custom fields for seamless data transfer.

## 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

### Key Features
Creator-focused: Visual automations, landing pages, subscriber scoring.

### Pricing
- Free: Up to 1k subscribers
- Creator: $29/month (1k subs)

### Pros
- Simple for newsletters
- Native commerce (sell digital products)
- Great support

### Cons
- Fewer templates

**Best for**: Bloggers/content creators. Advice: Use their "broadcast" for one-offs like Mailchimp campaigns; integrate with Zapier for WordPress.

## 4. Klaviyo

### Key Features
E-commerce powerhouse: Flows for abandoned carts, product recommendations, A/B testing.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; scales with revenue (e.g., $60/month for 1k contacts).

### Pros
- Shopify/WooCommerce integration
- Predictive analytics
- 99% deliverability

### Cons
- E-com focused; less for B2B

**Best for**: Online stores. Tip: Connect your store first, then import Mailchimp data—Klaviyo auto-syncs profiles.

## 5. MailerLite

### Key Features

### Pricing

### Pros
- Affordable
- Fast editor
- Website builder

### Cons
- Limited advanced segmentation

**Best for**: Budget-conscious startups. Practical: Their editor mirrors Mailchimp; duplicate campaigns during migration.

## 6. GetResponse

## How to Choose and Migrate: Practical Advice

### Step 1: Assess Needs
- **List size/budget**: MailerLite/Brevo for <10k subs.
- **Automations**: ActiveCampaign/Klaviyo.
- **E-com**: Klaviyo/Omnisend.

### Step 2: Compare Costs
Use tools like EmailToolTester.com. Example: 10k contacts—Brevo $65 vs Mailchimp $129.

### Step 3: Migrate Safely
1. Export CSV from Mailchimp (Audience > Export).
2. Clean data (remove bounces).
3. Import to new tool; remap fields.
4. Replicate top campaigns.
5. Test send to yourself.
6. Update signup forms/ integrations (e.g., via Zapier).

**Pro Tip**: Run parallel for 1-2 weeks. Monitor deliverability with GlockApps.

### Common Pitfalls
- Ignoring compliance (GDPR/CAN-SPAM)—all listed tools help.
- Skipping A/B tests—boost opens by 20%.
- Neglecting mobile—use responsive templates.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ActiveCampaign, and Klaviyo offer better value, features, and scalability. Pick based on your niche: creators to ConvertKit, stores to Klaviyo. Start with free trials, migrate methodically, and track metrics like open rates (aim 25%+) and revenue per email.
