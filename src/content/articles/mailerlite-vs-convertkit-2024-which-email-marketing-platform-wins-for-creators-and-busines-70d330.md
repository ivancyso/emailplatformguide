# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate 2024 Showdown for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. In this head-to-head comparison, we'll pit **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** against each other, focusing on key factors like pricing, features, usability, and support. Whether you're a solopreneur, blogger, or small business owner, this guide provides practical insights to help you decide.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email tool. Both platforms offer free plans, but they scale differently as your subscriber list grows.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite keeps it simple and affordable:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $9/month for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 2,500. Unlimited emails.
- **Advanced**: From $23/month, adds A/B testing, web push notifications, and priority support.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for large lists.

No contracts, monthly billing, and you can downgrade anytime. Practical tip: Start free and upgrade seamlessly—ideal for bootstrapped creators testing the waters.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) targets creators with tiered plans:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers with core features like broadcasts, forms, and landing pages. Limited to 10 sends per subscriber monthly.
- **Creator**: $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, up to $29 for 1,000. Unlimited sends, basic automations.
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month (300 subs) to $79 (3,000). Adds advanced automations, subscriber scoring, and integrations.
- **Enterprise**: Custom for high-volume users.

Annual discounts available (20% off). Advice: ConvertKit's pricing jumps faster for growing lists, so calculate based on your projected growth. Use their pricing calculator for accuracy.

**Winner on Price**: MailerLite for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit if you need creator-specific perks.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop vs Intuitive Interfaces

Both platforms prioritize user-friendliness, but their editors differ.

### MailerLite's Editor

Drag-and-drop builder with 80+ responsive templates. Intuitive blocks for images, buttons, and text. Mobile app for on-the-go management. New users can build a campaign in under 10 minutes.

Practical advice: Customize templates with dynamic content blocks—merge tags pull subscriber data effortlessly. Test on mobile preview before sending.

### ConvertKit's Visual Editor

Clean, block-based builder focused on sequences. Fewer templates (around 50), but highly customizable for newsletters. Strong tagging system simplifies segmentation.

Tip: Use the 'Visual Automations' builder for if/then flows without coding. Great for tagging behaviors like 'opened email X' to personalize follow-ups.

**Winner**: Tie—MailerLite for visual flexibility, ConvertKit for automation simplicity.

## Core Features: Automations, Segmentation, and More

### Email Building and Templates

MailerLite: Photorealistic editor, RSS-to-email campaigns, and HTML options. Supports countdown timers and product blocks.

ConvertKit: Optimized for newsletters with inline forms and commerce integrations (e.g., Gumroad). Native selling via paid newsletters.

### Automations

- **MailerLite**: Workflow builder with triggers like sign-ups or page visits. Multi-step with delays and conditions. Free plan includes basics.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequence automations shine—drip campaigns for courses. Pro adds subscriber scoring and advanced segments.

Advice: For course launches, ConvertKit's tagging automates 'welcome series' based on interests. MailerLite excels in popup-triggered workflows for lead gen.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Both offer tag-based segmentation. MailerLite: Custom fields, click maps, revenue tracking. ConvertKit: Behavior-based reports, open rates by tag.

Tip: Segment by engagement—send re-engagement campaigns to inactive subs quarterly to boost deliverability.

### Landing Pages and Forms

MailerLite: Unlimited pages/forms/popups with A/B testing (paid). High-converting templates.
ConvertKit: Native landing pages, opt-in incentives, and checkout forms.

### Integrations

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, plus WordPress, Shopify.
ConvertKit: Deep with creator tools like Teachable, Patreon, YouTube.

**Winner**: ConvertKit for creators; MailerLite for e-commerce versatility.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive academy with videos. Highly rated (4.9/5 on Capterra).

ConvertKit: Email support (faster on paid), podcast, creator network. 4.6/5 ratings.

Practical tip: Join MailerLite's Facebook group for peer tips; ConvertKit's resources for monetization strategies.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite Pros
- Cheaper scaling
- Feature-rich free plan
- Excellent popups/landing pages

Cons: Fewer creator-specific tools.

### ConvertKit Pros
- Superior automations for sequences
- Built-in commerce
- Creator-focused community

Cons: Higher cost for growth; limited free sends.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: You're starting out, need affordable unlimited emails, or run an online store. Perfect for agencies serving multiple clients.
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: You're a content creator building courses/podcasts, value tagging for personalization, or plan paid newsletters.

Test both free plans: Import a sample list, build a campaign, and run a test automation.

## Conclusion

In the **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** battle, there's no one-size-fits-all winner—it depends on your goals. MailerLite offers unbeatable value and versatility for most users, while ConvertKit empowers creators with tailored monetization tools. Evaluate your subscriber growth, feature needs, and budget. Whichever you choose, focus on consistent value-driven emails to grow opens and clicks. Start today, track metrics, and iterate. Your audience awaits!
