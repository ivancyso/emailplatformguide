# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Omnisend against Mailchimp—the two popular platforms—helps clarify which one suits your needs. This comparison dives into their features, pricing, usability, and more, tailored for businesses deciding between them. Whether you're a small ecommerce store or scaling up, we'll break it down with practical insights.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing since 2001. It's known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and all-in-one marketing platform. Ideal for beginners, it supports email campaigns, automations, landing pages, and social media ads.

Key strengths include a generous free plan (up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month) and extensive templates. However, as your list grows, costs rise quickly.

**Practical tip:** Start with Mailchimp's free tier to test basic newsletters. Use their audience dashboard to segment lists by engagement—open rates above 25% indicate hot leads worth nurturing.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend, launched in 2014, targets ecommerce brands with omnichannel marketing (email, SMS, web push). It integrates seamlessly with Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce, making it a go-to for online stores.

Standout features: Advanced automation workflows like cart abandonment and welcome series, plus popups and forms for list building. Free plan covers 250 contacts and 500 emails/month.

**Practical tip:** If you're on Shopify, connect Omnisend via the app store. Set up a cart recovery flow: Trigger email 1 hour post-abandonment with a 10% discount code—recovery rates often hit 15-20%.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major decider in Omnisend vs Mailchimp.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month.
- **Essentials:** $13/month (5,000 contacts, 50,000 sends).
- **Standard:** $20/month (5,000 contacts, unlimited sends, basic automation).
- **Premium:** $350+/month (large lists, advanced reporting).

Mailchimp charges per contact, with overages adding up. No SMS in lower tiers.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free:** 250 contacts, 500 emails/month.
- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts, unlimited emails, SMS credits extra).
- **Pro:** $59/month (500 contacts, advanced segmentation, A/B testing).
- **Enterprise:** Custom.

Omnisend offers better value for ecommerce with unlimited emails in paid plans. SMS starts at $0.016/message.

**Practical advice:** Calculate total cost. For 5,000 contacts sending weekly newsletters + automations:
- Mailchimp Standard: ~$20/month base + potential overages.
- Omnisend Standard: ~$59/month but includes omnichannel perks.

Use their pricing calculators to project 12 months ahead, factoring in growth.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Ease of Use
Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners—its editor is visual and forgiving. Omnisend's interface is clean but ecommerce-focused, with a slight learning curve for non-shop owners.

**Tip:** Mailchimp users: Drag elements freely. Omnisend: Leverage pre-built workflows to save time.

### Email Templates and Design
Both offer 100+ responsive templates. Mailchimp has more variety (events, nonprofits). Omnisend excels in product-focused designs with dynamic blocks pulling real inventory.

**Practical advice:** Customize with your branding. Test mobile previews—aim for 600px width. Use Omnisend's product feed for personalized recommendations, boosting clicks by 26% per their benchmarks.

### Automation and Workflows
Omnisend shines here with ecommerce-specific triggers (browse abandonment, back-in-stock). Mailchimp's automations are solid but customer journey builder requires Premium.

**Tip:** In Omnisend, chain SMS after email fails (e.g., Day 1 email, Day 3 SMS). Track ROI via revenue per recipient—target $0.50+.

### Integrations
Mailchimp: 300+ (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).
Omnisend: Strong ecommerce (Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives), plus Google, Facebook.

**Advice:** Prioritize native integrations. Migrate from Mailchimp to Omnisend via CSV export/import—map fields carefully to avoid data loss.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp: Detailed opens/clicks, revenue tracking in higher plans.
Omnisend: Ecommerce revenue attribution, SMS performance.

**Tip:** Focus on key metrics: Open rate >20%, click >3%, unsubscribe <0.5%. Use cohort analysis in Omnisend for repeat purchase insights.

### List Building Tools
Omnisend's popups, forms, and wheels convert 2-5x better for shops. Mailchimp has forms but fewer gamified options.

**Advice:** A/B test popup timing (exit-intent works best). Offer lead magnets like 'Top 10 Tips' for 10-15% opt-in rates.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Mailchimp if:** You're a solopreneur, blogger, or service business starting out. Need simple emails without ecommerce focus.

**Pick Omnisend if:** Running an online store. Want SMS/push for higher engagement (open rates 98% for push). Scaling with automations.

**Migration tip:** Export lists as CSV. Test a small campaign first. Omnisend offers free migration help for larger lists.

In the Omnisend vs Mailchimp showdown, there's no one-size-fits-all winner—it depends on your business. Mailchimp suits general marketing with ease, while Omnisend dominates ecommerce with omnichannel depth. Evaluate your needs: If revenue from emails/SMS is key, Omnisend edges out.
