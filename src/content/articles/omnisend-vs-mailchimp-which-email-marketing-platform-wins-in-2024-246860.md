# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With thousands of subscribers relying on timely, personalized emails, tools like Omnisend and Mailchimp stand out. Both cater to small businesses and ecommerce stores, but they differ in focus, features, and pricing. This in-depth comparison breaks down Omnisend vs Mailchimp based on real user experiences, official pricing, and key functionalities to help you decide.

Whether you're a beginner scaling your first list or an established store needing omnichannel automation, we'll cover pricing, features, integrations, and more. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform designed primarily for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it excels in integrating email, SMS, and web push notifications into unified workflows. It's particularly popular among Shopify and WooCommerce users due to seamless native integrations.

Key strengths include:
- **Ecommerce-centric automations**: Cart abandonment, welcome series, back-in-stock alerts, and post-purchase flows.
- **Visual workflow builder**: Drag-and-drop editor for complex automations without coding.
- **Free plan**: Up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, ideal for startups.

Omnisend powers over 70,000 brands, focusing on revenue recovery through targeted messaging. Practical tip: Use its product picker to dynamically insert customer-bought items into emails, boosting relevance and open rates.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, founded in 2001, is a veteran in email marketing with over 13 million users. It's known for its beginner-friendly interface and robust free tier. While it supports ecommerce, it's more general-purpose, serving bloggers, nonprofits, and agencies alongside stores.

Standout features:
- **Intuitive drag-and-drop editor**: Hundreds of templates for quick campaigns.
- **Audience segmentation**: Based on behavior, tags, and purchases.
- **Built-in analytics**: Detailed reports on opens, clicks, and revenue.

Mailchimp's strength lies in scalability for non-ecommerce users, but ecommerce features require add-ons like the abandoned cart integration. Advice: Start with its free plan (up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month) to test basic campaigns before upgrading.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major decider. Both offer free plans, but structures differ.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free**: 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, unlimited SMS/push (fair use).
- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts), scales by contacts; includes automation, SMS.
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts), adds API access, dedicated IP.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

Billed annually for discounts. No send limits beyond contacts; pay per contact.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free**: 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month.
- **Essentials**: $13/month (500 contacts), 5,000 sends; basic automation.
- **Standard**: $20/month (500 contacts), 6,000 sends; better automation.
- **Premium**: $350+/month; advanced segmentation.

Send-volume caps make it pricier for high-volume senders. Practical advice: Calculate based on your list size and send frequency. For 5,000 contacts sending weekly newsletters, Omnisend Standard (~$59) beats Mailchimp Standard (~$75).

| Feature | Omnisend Free | Mailchimp Free | Omnisend Standard | Mailchimp Essentials |
|---------|---------------|----------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Contacts | 250 | 500 | 500+ | 500+ |
| Emails/mo | 500 | 1,000 | Unlimited* | 5,000+ |
| SMS | Limited | No | Yes | No |

## Feature Face-Off: Key Capabilities

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp wins for sheer variety (500+ templates), but Omnisend's are ecommerce-optimized with dynamic blocks (e.g., product carousels). Tip: In Omnisend, use conditional content to show different products based on browsing history.

### Automation Workflows
Omnisend shines here with pre-built ecommerce flows like win-back campaigns. Mailchimp's automations are solid but less visual. Advice: For cart recovery, Omnisend's multi-step sequences (email + SMS) recover 20-30% more on average per their benchmarks—test it yourself.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both offer behavioral segmentation. Omnisend edges with purchase-based tags and predicted CLV (Pro plan). Mailchimp's merge tags are simpler for beginners.

### SMS and Omnichannel
Omnisend includes SMS/push natively (pay-per-message). Mailchimp requires third-party integrations (extra cost). Ideal for mobile-first audiences.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp's dashboard is comprehensive for campaigns. Omnisend focuses on revenue attribution, tracking ROI from each flow.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly?

Mailchimp's interface is the gold standard for newbies—minimal learning curve. Omnisend is intuitive but assumes some ecommerce knowledge. Onboarding: Mailchimp's tutorials are extensive; Omnisend offers store-specific guides.

Practical tip: Import your list via CSV in either, then segment immediately. Mailchimp auto-tags opens/clicks; Omnisend syncs orders automatically.

## Integrations and Compatibility

Omnisend: 160+ apps, native with Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce. Plug-and-play for most ecommerce platforms.

Mailchimp: 300+ integrations, including Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier. Broader but shallower for stores.

Advice: If you're on Shopify, Omnisend installs in minutes with one-click sync. Mailchimp needs MC Store for full ecommerce.

## Customer Support

Omnisend: 24/7 email/chat (paid plans), knowledge base, live chat. Highly rated for responsiveness.

Mailchimp: Email/ticket (paid), self-serve help center. Free users get forums only.

User reviews (G2/Capterra): Omnisend 4.6/5, Mailchimp 4.5/5. Omnisend praised for ecommerce expertise.

## Which One Should You Choose?

- **Choose Omnisend if**: You run an online store (Shopify/Woo), need SMS/automation for recovery, or prioritize revenue tracking. Best for 100-10k contacts.
- **Choose Mailchimp if**: You're a beginner, non-ecommerce (blog/newsletter), or need broad integrations. Great for under 5k contacts with low volume.

Test both free plans: Migrate lists easily. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and revenue per email.

## Conclusion

Omnisend vs Mailchimp boils down to your business type. Omnisend leads for ecommerce with superior automation and omnichannel, while Mailchimp excels in accessibility and templates. Neither is 'best' universally—audit your needs: list size, send volume, integrations.

Start with free trials, run A/B tests on campaigns, and scale what delivers ROI. For most growing stores, Omnisend's focus tips the scale in 2024. Ready to choose? Sign up today and watch engagement soar.
