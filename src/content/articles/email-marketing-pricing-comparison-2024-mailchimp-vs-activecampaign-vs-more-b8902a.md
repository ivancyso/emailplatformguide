# Email Marketing Pricing Comparison: Choose the Right Platform for Your Budget

Selecting the right email marketing platform isn't just about features—pricing plays a pivotal role, especially as your subscriber list grows. In this comprehensive email marketing pricing comparison, we'll break down costs for popular tools like Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, Brevo (formerly Sendinblue), GetResponse, and Moosend. We'll focus on real pricing tiers (as of mid-2024; always verify on official sites as plans change), factors influencing costs, and practical tips to calculate your true expenses. Whether you're a solopreneur with 500 subscribers or scaling to 10,000+, this guide helps you avoid overpaying.

## Why Pricing Matters in Email Marketing

Pricing in email marketing isn't one-size-fits-all. Most platforms charge based on:
- **Contact list size**: The number of subscribers.
- **Email sends/emails per month**: Limits on volume.
- **Features**: Automation, segmentation, A/B testing, e-commerce integrations.
- **Add-ons**: SMS, landing pages, or premium support.

**Practical tip**: Estimate your needs first. Multiply your current subscribers by expected growth (e.g., 20% monthly). Factor in open rates (20-30% average) for send volumes. Free trials (14-30 days) let you test without commitment.

Hidden costs include:
- Deliverability fees if reputation drops.
- Migration costs from old tools.
- Time spent learning complex interfaces.

## Key Pricing Models Explained

### Subscriber-Based Pricing
Most common (e.g., Mailchimp). Pay more as list grows. Good for steady growth.

### Send-Based Pricing
Charge per email sent (e.g., Brevo). Ideal for low-volume, high-engagement lists.

### Flat or Tiered
Fixed monthly for unlimited sends within tiers (e.g., some GetResponse plans).

**Pro advice**: Use calculators on platform sites. For 2,500 contacts sending 10k emails/month, compare apples-to-apples.

## Practical Advice: How to Choose and Save Money

1. **Calculate TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)**: (Monthly fee x 12) + setup time + add-ons. Example: For 5k contacts, Moosend saves $240/year vs Mailchimp.

2. **Scale Projections**: If growing 50%/quarter, pick flexible tiers like Brevo.

3. **Free Tier Strategy**: Start with Mailchimp/Moosend, migrate later (most offer imports).

4. **Negotiate**: Enterprise plans often customizable; mention competitors.

5. **Discounts**: Annual billing, Black Friday deals (20-50% off first year), nonprofits (free tiers).

6. **Test Deliverability**: Pricing worthless if emails hit spam. Monitor with GlockApps (free trial).

7. **ROI Focus**: Track revenue per email. Platforms like Klaviyo (e-comm focused, $20+/mo) pay for themselves via sales.

**Case Study**: A blogger with 3k subs switched from Mailchimp ($45/mo) to ConvertKit ($49/mo) for unlimited sends—increased revenue 25% via better automations.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Overbuying Features**: Skip premium if no automation needs.
- **Ignoring Send Limits**: Exceed = extra fees (e.g., Mailchimp $20/5k overage).
- **Forgetting Compliance**: GDPR/CCPA tools add $10-50/mo elsewhere.
- **Churn Risk**: Lock-in via contacts makes switching costly.
