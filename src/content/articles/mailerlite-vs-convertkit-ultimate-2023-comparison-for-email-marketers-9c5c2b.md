# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business or content creation efforts. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit is a common debate. Both platforms cater to beginners, creators, bloggers, and small businesses looking for affordable, powerful email solutions. This MailerLite vs ConvertKit comparison dives deep into pricing, features, automation, ease of use, and more, based on real user experiences and official specs as of 2023.

Whether you're on a free plan or scaling up, we'll help you decide which fits your audience-building needs.

## Pricing Breakdown: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but they differ in limits and paid tiers.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite's free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—generous for starters. Paid plans start at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20/month for 1,000 subs. Higher tiers like Advanced ($21/month for 500 subs) add A/B testing and web push notifications.

No contracts, monthly billing, and annual discounts (up to 26% off). It's one of the most affordable options, with costs tied strictly to subscriber count, not sends.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit (rebranded elements of Kit) offers a free plan up to 1,000 subscribers with unlimited emails in the first month, then limited sends. Paid Creator plan starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, jumping to $29/month for 1,000. The Creator Pro plan ($25/month base) unlocks advanced automation and revenue features.

ConvertKit emphasizes value for creators with commerce tools, but it's pricier as lists grow—$79/month for 3,000 subs vs MailerLite's $50.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your subscriber growth. Use MailerLite's pricing calculator for long-term savings if you're bootstrapping. ConvertKit suits those monetizing via emails early.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities

Both platforms excel in essentials, but nuances matter.

### Email Builders and Templates

**MailerLite:** Drag-and-drop editor with 80+ responsive templates. Photo blocks, columns, and custom HTML. Classic and new editors for flexibility.

**ConvertKit:** Visual builder focused on clean, mobile-first designs. Fewer templates (around 100), but optimized for creators with commerce integrations.

**Winner:** Tie—MailerLite for variety, ConvertKit for simplicity.

### Automation and Sequences

**MailerLite:** Visual automation builder for workflows like welcome series, abandoned carts. Time delays, A/B splits in advanced plans. Integrates with e-commerce.

**ConvertKit:** Sequences shine here—automated email series triggered by tags or actions. Re-engagement campaigns and subscriber scoring. Pro plan adds one-click upsells.

**Practical Advice:** For nurture sequences, test ConvertKit's tagging system (behavior-based over lists). MailerLite's automations are easier for beginners with flowchart views.

### Forms, Landing Pages, and Popups

**MailerLite:** Unlimited popups, embedded forms, surveys. Website builder for landing pages (free plan included). High customization.

**ConvertKit:** Forms and landing pages with A/B testing. Native checkout pages for digital products. No popups in free plan.

**Tip:** Use MailerLite popups for list growth on blogs; ConvertKit for sales funnels.

### Integrations and E-commerce

Both integrate with WordPress, Shopify, Zapier (1,000+ apps). ConvertKit edges with Gumroad, Teachable for creators. MailerLite supports Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel natively.

## Ease of Use and User Interface

MailerLite's interface is intuitive, dashboard-focused with quick setup wizards. Ideal for non-tech users—migrate lists in minutes.

ConvertKit prioritizes creators: Tag-based segmentation feels natural for podcasters/bloggers. Steeper learning for traditional marketers.

**Practical Advice:** Start a free trial. Import a test list to MailerLite for speed; explore ConvertKit if you use tags heavily.

User ratings (as of 2023): MailerLite 4.7/5 on G2/Capterra; ConvertKit 4.6/5, praised for support.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

**MailerLite:** 24/7 live chat, email support, knowledge base. EU-based (GDPR compliant). 99% deliverability.

**ConvertKit:** Email/ticket support (faster on paid), tutorials, creator community. US-based, strong inbox placement.

Both maintain high deliverability via authentication (DKIM/SPF). Advice: Always warm up domains.
