# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate 2024 Comparison for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit helps solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams decide based on real needs. This guide breaks down pricing, features, usability, and more to guide your choice.

## Why Compare MailerLite and ConvertKit?

Both platforms cater to creators and small businesses but differ in focus. MailerLite emphasizes affordability and simplicity, ideal for beginners building lists. ConvertKit targets content creators with advanced automation for newsletters and courses.

Practical tip: Start by assessing your list size, budget, and automation needs. Both offer free trials—sign up for each to import a test list and send a campaign.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is a top decider. Here's a side-by-side look (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates).

| Feature | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Free Plan | Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month | Up to 1,000 subscribers (limited forms/landing pages) |
| Paid Starts At | $10/month (500 subs) | $15/month (300 subs, Creator plan) |
| Higher Tiers | Growing: $20+; Advanced: $60+ | Creator: $29+; Creator Pro: $59+ |
| Perks | Forever free tier, no contracts | Commerce features in Pro, visual automations |

**MailerLite wins on affordability**: Its free plan suits bootstrappers, and paid plans scale cheaply without subscriber caps on emails sent.

**ConvertKit's edge**: Higher tiers unlock selling digital products directly, valuable for course creators.

Advice: Calculate costs based on your growth. If under 1,000 subs, MailerLite saves money. For e-commerce integration, budget for ConvertKit Pro.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly Interfaces

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

MailerLite's editor is intuitive—no coding needed. Build emails with blocks for images, buttons, and text. The dashboard is clean, with quick-setup wizards for campaigns and popups.

Pros: Fast onboarding (under 10 minutes). Mobile-responsive templates out-of-the-box.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Design

ConvertKit feels tailored for newsletters. The visual builder uses "cards" for sequences, but the classic editor suits traditional emails. Tagging system simplifies segmentation.

Pros: Intuitive for podcasters/bloggers; subscriber search is powerful.

**Verdict**: MailerLite for absolute beginners; ConvertKit if you value tagging over basic drags.

Tip: Watch tutorials on YouTube—MailerLite's are shorter (5-10 mins), ConvertKit's dive deeper into sequences (15+ mins).

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools Head-to-Head

### Email Builders and Templates

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, photo editor included. A/B testing on subjects/lines.

ConvertKit: Fewer templates but optimized for single-column newsletters. Strong preview tools.

Both deliver 95%+ rates, per industry benchmarks.

### Automation and Workflows

**MailerLite**: Basic automations like welcome series, abandoned cart. Drag-and-drop builder for multi-step flows. RSS-to-email for bloggers.

**ConvertKit**: Superior sequences (time-based or action-triggered). Visual automation builder in Pro plans tags behaviors automatically.

Advice: For simple "if subscribe, send X"—MailerLite suffices. Complex nurturing (e.g., lead scoring)? ConvertKit shines.

### Segmentation and Tagging

MailerLite: Groups, custom fields, behaviors (opens/clicks). Easy segments via clicks.

ConvertKit: Tag-based (e.g., "podcast listener"). Automations react to tags dynamically.

Tip: Export CSVs from one to test segmentation in the other.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

MailerLite: Unlimited landing pages/forms/popups on all plans. High-converting templates, exit-intent popups.

ConvertKit: Forms and landing pages included; Commerce for payments. Fewer popup options.

**MailerLite leads** for lead gen tools—great for webinars or ebooks.

### Integrations and E-commerce

Both integrate with Zapier, WordPress, Shopify.

MailerLite: 1,000+ apps, Stripe for payments.

ConvertKit: Native Gumroad/Stripe commerce, podcast hosts.

For WooCommerce users, MailerLite's depth wins; ConvertKit for Teachable/Gumroad.

## Deliverability and Analytics

Both prioritize inbox placement with authentication setup guides (DKIM/SPF).

MailerLite: Clean IP reputation, detailed reports (opens, clicks, revenue tracking).

ConvertKit: Revenue per subscriber metric, unsubscribe insights.

Practical advice: Warm up domains gradually. Monitor bounce rates—aim under 2%.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, knowledge base. Fast responses (under 1 hour).

ConvertKit: Email/ticket support (business hours), extensive help docs, creator community.

**MailerLite faster** for urgent issues; ConvertKit's resources suit self-learners.

Tip: Join Reddit (r/Emailmarketing) or Facebook groups for user stories.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Budget-conscious startups
- Bloggers needing popups/landing pages
- Teams wanting simplicity

Example: A lifestyle blogger with 500 subs grows affordably, using RSS campaigns.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Newsletter writers/podcasters
- Digital product sellers
- Users needing behavioral automations

Example: Course creator segments by engagement, sells via links.

Test both: Migrate lists easily via CSV.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

No clear winner—**MailerLite for affordability and ease (best for 80% of small users)**; **ConvertKit for creators scaling revenue**.

**Action plan**:

2. Import 100 contacts, build a campaign.
3. Set up one automation.
