# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool is crucial for businesses looking to nurture leads, boost sales, and engage customers effectively. In this in-depth Brevo vs Mailchimp comparison, we'll break down key aspects like pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide. Both platforms cater to small businesses, e-commerce stores, and marketers, but they differ in strengths. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) emphasizes affordability and multichannel marketing, while Mailchimp focuses on user-friendly design and robust analytics.

Whether you're on a free plan or scaling up, this guide provides practical insights based on real user experiences and official specs.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first deciding factor. Both offer free tiers, but their structures vary.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo's plans start with a free option allowing 300 emails per day forever, unlimited contacts, and basic features like email campaigns and a drag-and-drop editor. Paid plans begin at $25/month for Starter (20,000 emails/month), scaling to $65/month for Business (unlimited emails, advanced automation). Enterprise is custom. No subscriber-based pricing—it's send-volume based, making it cost-effective for high-volume senders with large lists.

**Practical Advice:** Ideal if you have growing lists but send sporadically. Monitor daily limits to avoid overages.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month, with basic automation. Essentials starts at $13/month (500 contacts, 5,000 emails), Standard at $20/month (advanced automation), and Premium at $350/month for high-volume needs. Pricing is contact-based, so costs rise with list growth.

**Practical Advice:** Great for beginners with small lists. Upgrade early if your audience exceeds 500 to avoid feature limits.

| Feature | Brevo Free | Mailchimp Free | Brevo Starter ($25/mo) | Mailchimp Essentials ($13/mo) |
|---------|------------|----------------|-------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Contacts | Unlimited | 500 | 20k emails/mo | 500 contacts |
| Emails | 300/day | 1k/mo | 20k/mo | 5k/mo |
| Automation | Basic | Basic | Advanced | No server-side tracking |

Brevo often wins for larger lists on a budget.

## Feature Face-Off: Core Capabilities

### Email Builder and Templates
Both have drag-and-drop editors. Mailchimp's is intuitive with 100+ customizable templates, responsive by default. Brevo offers similar ease, plus transactional email support (SMS, WhatsApp). Brevo shines in dynamic content blocks for personalization.

**Tip:** Test templates on mobile—both score high, but Mailchimp's gallery is broader for quick starts.

### Automation Workflows
Mailchimp's automation is beginner-friendly with pre-built journeys (e.g., abandoned cart). Brevo provides multi-step workflows, A/B testing, and CRM integration for lead scoring.

**Practical Advice:** Use Brevo for sales funnels; Mailchimp for simple newsletters. Set up welcome series first—track open rates to refine.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp excels with behavioral segmentation (e.g., purchase history). Brevo's CRM auto-segments based on interactions, free for all plans.

**Tip:** Segment by engagement: send re-engagement campaigns to inactive subscribers quarterly.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp offers detailed revenue tracking and comparative reports. Brevo provides real-time stats, heatmaps, and deliverability insights.

**Advice:** Integrate Google Analytics for deeper ROI measurement on either.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is polished, with guided tours—perfect for non-tech users. Brevo is straightforward but has a slight learning curve for its all-in-one dashboard (email + CRM + chat).

**User Tip:** Start with Mailchimp if you're solo; Brevo if managing sales pipelines. Both import CSV lists easily—clean data first to boost deliverability.

## Customer Support and Community

Mailchimp provides email support (priority for paid), extensive help center, and forums. Brevo offers 24/7 chat/email for paid users, phone for higher tiers, and a knowledge base.

**Practical Advice:** Free users lean on docs. Paid? Brevo's live chat resolves issues faster for urgent campaigns.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Shopify, WordPress, Google Workspace). Brevo supports 300+ too, strong in e-commerce (WooCommerce) and custom APIs.

**Tip:** For scalability, Brevo handles millions of emails; Mailchimp suits mid-sized growth. Use Zapier for gaps.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Both maintain high deliverability (Mailchimp ~90-95%, Brevo similar with dedicated IPs on paid plans). Comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM via built-in tools.

**Advice:** Warm up IPs gradually, maintain <1% complaint rates. Authenticate domains early.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros
- Affordable for high volume
- Built-in CRM and SMS
- Unlimited contacts free

### Brevo Cons
- Daily send limits on free
- Fewer templates

### Mailchimp Pros
- Intuitive editor
- Strong analytics
- Large template library

### Mailchimp Cons
- Expensive for large lists
- Feature-locked free plan

## Who Should Choose Brevo?
E-commerce owners, agencies, or high-volume senders needing CRM. Practical: Migrate if costs rise with Mailchimp subscribers—export lists easily.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
Beginners, content creators, small teams prioritizing design. Advice: Stick if your list <2k; reassess yearly.

## Migration Tips
Switching? Export audiences from one, import to the other. Remap automations manually. Test sends to verify.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to needs: Brevo for cost-efficiency and multichannel (best for scaling SMBs), Mailchimp for polished ease (ideal starters). Evaluate your list size, send frequency, and features. Trial both free plans—run a test campaign tracking opens/CTRs. In 2024, Brevo edges for value, but Mailchimp retains loyalty for UX. Whichever you pick, consistent testing drives 20-30% better results. Ready to choose? Start with your goals.
