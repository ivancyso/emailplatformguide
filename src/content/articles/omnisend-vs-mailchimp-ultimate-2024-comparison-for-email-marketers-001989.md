# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools trusted by thousands of businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp has long been the go-to for beginners with its intuitive interface and free plan, while Omnisend shines for ecommerce stores with advanced automation and omnichannel capabilities.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're a small business owner, ecommerce entrepreneur, or marketing manager, you'll get practical advice to decide which platform fits your goals. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing tool. Both platforms use tiered plans based on contact lists and features, but they differ in transparency and ecommerce focus.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp offers a **free plan** for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends—perfect for startups testing the waters. Paid plans start with:
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts (scales up to $350+ for larger lists).
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, adding advanced automation and A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350/month for 500 contacts, with multivariate testing and segmentation.

**Practical tip**: Mailchimp bills based on contacts, not sends, which can save money for low-volume senders. However, costs rise quickly beyond 5,000 contacts, often exceeding $100/month. Always factor in add-ons like landing pages ($13+ extra).

### Omnisend Pricing
Omnisend's **free plan** supports up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, with full access to core features like automation. Paid tiers include:
- **Standard**: $16/month for 500 contacts (unlimited emails).
- **Pro**: $59/month for 500 contacts, unlocking API access and custom events.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume needs.

**Practical tip**: Omnisend charges per contacts but allows unlimited emails in paid plans, making it cost-effective for frequent ecommerce campaigns like abandoned cart recoveries. SMS adds extra costs (~$1 per 100 messages), but it's optional.

**Winner**: Omnisend for ecommerce with unlimited sends; Mailchimp for true free-tier beginners.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Interface

### Mailchimp's User-Friendly Design
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is legendary for non-techies. New users can create a campaign in minutes with pre-built templates. The dashboard feels familiar, like a simplified Google Workspace.

**Advice**: Ideal if your team lacks design skills. Use the content optimizer for quick improvements based on engagement data.

### Omnisend's Ecommerce-Centric Dashboard
Omnisend prioritizes workflows over basic emails. The interface is clean but assumes some marketing knowledge, with a focus on pop-ups, SMS, and automation builders.

**Advice**: Start with their onboarding wizard—it imports Shopify/WooCommerce data seamlessly. Less intuitive for pure newsletter senders.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for absolute beginners; Omnisend for store owners comfortable with automations.

## Key Features Head-to-Head

### Email Templates and Design
Both offer responsive templates:
- Mailchimp: 100+ free, highly customizable with Creative Assistant (AI suggestions).
- Omnisend: 250+ ecommerce-optimized, including product blocks that pull live inventory.

**Practical advice**: For product-heavy emails, Omnisend's dynamic blocks update prices automatically—test with a welcome series to see real-time personalization.

### Automation Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Single, customer journey, and behavioral automations in Standard+. Classic automations are free-form.
- **Omnisend**: Superior with pre-built ecommerce flows (cart abandonment, win-back, cross-sell). Supports SMS/email/push in one workflow.

**Advice**: Ecommerce brands should map a full funnel: browse → cart → purchase → post-purchase. Omnisend recovers 15-30% more carts on average (based on their benchmarks—verify in your tests).

### Segmentation and Personalization
- Mailchimp: Tags, groups, behavioral data; merge fields for personalization.
- Omnisend: Advanced ecommerce segments like 'viewed product X but didn't buy.' Hyper-personalization with product recommendations.

**Tip**: Segment by purchase history first. Use Omnisend for RFM (recency, frequency, monetary) out-of-the-box.

### Analytics and Reporting
Both track opens, clicks, revenue. Omnisend edges with **ROAS (return on ad spend)** tracking for ecommerce. Mailchimp excels in comparative reports.

**Advice**: Set revenue tracking early—link UTM parameters to see true campaign ROI.

### Integrations
- Mailchimp: 300+ (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).
- Omnisend: Deep with Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce; also Zapier, Google.

**Tip**: If on Shopify, Omnisend installs in one click, syncing orders automatically.

## Customer Support and Resources
Mailchimp provides 24/7 email support (chat in higher plans), extensive help center, and free courses. Omnisend offers live chat 24/5, priority phone in Pro, and ecommerce-specific guides.

**Practical advice**: Join their Facebook communities—Mailchimp's is larger for general tips; Omnisend's for store scaling hacks.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- Generous free plan
- Beginner-friendly
- Strong creative tools

**Cons**:
- Expensive at scale
- Limited ecommerce automations
- No native SMS

### Omnisend
**Pros**:
- Ecommerce powerhouse
- Unlimited emails in paid plans
- Omnichannel (SMS, push)

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve
- Free plan limits
- Less general templates

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Mailchimp if**: You're a blogger, service business, or solopreneur starting with newsletters. Budget under $20/month, no ecommerce.
- **Pick Omnisend if**: Running an online store (Shopify, etc.). Need cart recovery, upsells, SMS. Scaling to 1,000+ orders/month.

**Migration tip**: Export CSV lists easily. Test both free plans for 2 weeks—send identical campaigns and compare metrics.

## Conclusion

Omnisend vs Mailchimp boils down to your business type. Mailchimp remains the safe, versatile choice for broad marketing needs, powering millions with simplicity. Omnisend, however, dominates ecommerce with targeted automations that drive revenue—often 2-3x higher ROAS for stores.

For most choosing in 2024, **Omnisend wins for growth-focused ecommerce**, while Mailchimp suits casual users. Audit your goals: high-volume sends? Go Omnisend. Quick setup? Mailchimp.
