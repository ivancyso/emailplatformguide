# Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Top 10 Compared for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing hikes, limited automation for free users, and steeper learning curve have many businesses searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're dealing with growing lists, complex automations, or tighter budgets, switching platforms could save time and money while boosting deliverability and engagement.

In this guide, we'll compare the top 10 Mailchimp alternatives based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, official pricing pages, and feature sets as of 2024. We'll cover pricing, key features, pros/cons, and practical advice for choosing the right one. Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or agency, there's an option here.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp? Common Pain Points

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 subscribers and 1,000 emails/month, with paid plans starting at $13/month but scaling quickly—up to $350+/month for 50k contacts. Automation is basic on lower tiers, and support is email-only for most users.

Businesses often leave for:
- **Better pricing**: Flat rates or pay-per-use models.
- **Advanced automations**: Behavioral triggers and multi-channel support.
- **Easier segmentation**: Drag-and-drop without code.
- **Superior deliverability**: Higher inbox rates via dedicated IPs.

Practical tip: Audit your current Mailchimp usage. Export your list (CSV format) and test imports on 2-3 alternatives before migrating fully.

Now, deep dives into each.

### 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)
Brevo combines email, SMS, chat, and CRM in one dashboard. Pricing is usage-based: free up to 300 emails/day forever, then $25/month for 20k emails.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts on paid plans.
- Advanced automation workflows.
- Drag-and-drop editor with 50+ templates.

**Pros**: Excellent transactional emails; high deliverability (99% claimed); EU-based for GDPR.
**Cons**: SMS add-ons extra; interface can feel cluttered.

**Practical Advice**: Ideal if you send high-volume transactional emails (e.g., order confirmations). Migrate by mapping custom fields—test with a 100-subscriber segment first.

### 2. MailerLite
Affordable and user-friendly, perfect for newsletters. Free for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month.

**Key Features**:
- Pop-ups and landing pages included.
- A/B testing on all plans.
- Website builder integration.

**Pros**: Fast setup (under 10 minutes); great mobile editor.
**Cons**: Limited automation depth vs. ActiveCampaign.

**Practical Advice**: Use for content creators. Segment lists by engagement—send re-engagement campaigns to inactive subs to boost open rates by 20-30%.

### 3. ConvertKit
Creator-focused with tagging over lists. Free for 300 subscribers.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automations (if/then logic).
- Native sell forms and subscriptions.
- Commerce tools for digital products.

**Pros**: Simple tagging reduces list fatigue.
**Cons**: No phone support; e-commerce limited.

**Practical Advice**: Tag subscribers by behavior (e.g., 'clicked webinar'). Automate welcome sequences to nurture leads into buyers.

### 4. ActiveCampaign
Automation powerhouse with CRM and site tracking.

**Key Features**:
- 700+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress).
- Machine learning for split testing.
- Conditional content blocks.

**Pros**: Deep personalization; predictive sending.
**Cons**: Steep learning curve; $29/month minimum.

**Practical Advice**: For sales teams, set up lead scoring. Track ROI by tagging campaigns—users report 40% higher conversions.

### 5. Klaviyo
E-commerce specialist, integrates seamlessly with Shopify/Magento.

**Key Features**:
- Revenue tracking per flow.
- Built-in A/B testing for subject lines.
- SMS and push notifications.

**Pros**: Free for small lists; top deliverability.
**Cons**: Less ideal for non-ecom.

**Practical Advice**: Build abandoned cart flows—recover 10-15% lost sales. Use dynamic product blocks for personalized recommends.

### 6. GetResponse
All-in-one with webinars and funnels.

**Key Features**:
- Conversion funnels.
- Autoresponders with scoring.
- Landing page builder.

**Pros**: 30% discount on annual; strong support.
**Cons**: Editor less intuitive.

**Practical Advice**: Host webinars via integration. Segment by funnel drop-off for targeted follow-ups.

### 7. AWeber
Reliable for evergreen campaigns.

**Key Features**:
- RSS-to-email.
- Tag-based automation.

**Pros**: Proven 20+ years; high deliverability.
**Cons**: Dated interface.

**Practical Advice**: Automate blog digests to keep engagement high without manual sends.

### 8. Constant Contact
Event-focused with social ads.

**Key Features**:
- Event management.
- AI subject lines.

**Pros**: Beginner-friendly.
**Cons**: Pricey for large lists.

**Practical Advice**: Promote events with RSVPs—track attendance ROI.

### 9. Omnisend
Omnichannel for retail.

**Key Features**: Email + SMS + push.

**Pros**: Free plan generous.
**Cons**: Ecom-only focus.

**Practical Advice**: Time SMS for post-purchase upsells.

### 10. Moosend
Budget automation.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited emails on paid.
- Page builder.

**Pros**: Cheap scaling.
**Cons**: Fewer integrations.

**Practical Advice**: Use for startups; A/B test everything.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Budget**: Under $20? MailerLite/Moosend. Scaling? Brevo/Klaviyo.
2. **Audience Size**: Free forever? Brevo/Omnisend.
3. **Needs**: Ecom? Klaviyo. Automation? ActiveCampaign. Newsletters? ConvertKit.
4. **Migration Steps**:
   - Export Mailchimp list (Audience > Export).
   - Sign up, import CSV.
   - Recreate top automations.
   - Warm new IP with gradual sends (start 20% volume).

Test 2-3 with your list. Monitor open rates (aim >25%) and complaints (<0.1%).

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ActiveCampaign, and Klaviyo offer better value, features, and scalability for most users. Pick based on your niche—ecom loves Klaviyo, creators prefer ConvertKit. Start with a free trial, migrate strategically, and watch engagement soar.
