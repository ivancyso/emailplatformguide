# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With countless options available, two giants stand out: Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp. Both platforms offer robust features for building email lists, automating campaigns, and analyzing performance. But which one is better for your needs? This in-depth Brevo vs Mailchimp comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, integrations, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or marketing manager, we'll provide practical advice based on real user experiences and official platform data. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Free Plans and Scalability

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing tool. Both Brevo and Mailchimp offer free tiers, but they differ significantly in structure.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts and 300 emails per day—ideal for startups testing the waters without subscriber limits. Paid plans start at $25/month for 20,000 emails (Starter), scaling to $65/month for 20,000 emails with advanced automation (Business). Enterprise plans are custom-priced. No contracts required, and you can send transactional emails for free up to 300/day.

**Practical Tip:** If your list is growing fast but volume is low, Brevo's unlimited contacts prevent forced upgrades. Use their credit-based SMS add-on sparingly for high-ROI campaigns.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month (500 sends/month now). Essentials starts at $13/month for 500 contacts (unlimited sends post-send limits), Standard at $20/month for automation, and Premium at $350/month for 10,000+ contacts. High-volume senders pay per email after thresholds.

**Practical Tip:** Mailchimp suits low-volume beginners, but watch contact-based pricing—if your list exceeds tiers without high engagement, costs spike. Audit inactive subscribers quarterly to stay cost-effective.

**Winner:** Brevo for growing lists; Mailchimp for tiny budgets with capped contacts.

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools and Advanced Capabilities

Both platforms excel in email marketing essentials, but nuances matter.

### Email Builders and Templates

Brevo offers a drag-and-drop editor with 48+ responsive templates, plus AI-generated content. Mailchimp's editor is intuitive with 100+ templates and creative assets from its design team.

**Practical Advice:** Customize templates with your brand colors/fonts. Test mobile previews—Brevo's AMP support enables interactive emails like carousels for higher engagement (up to 25% open rate boosts reported by users).

### Automation and Workflows

Brevo shines with multi-channel automation (email, SMS, WhatsApp) and a visual builder for complex journeys like abandoned cart recovery. Mailchimp's automations include single-trigger journeys (e.g., welcome series) in Standard plan, with customer journey builder in Premium.

**Practical Tip:** Start simple—set up a 3-email nurture sequence. Brevo's wait/step triggers handle branching logic better for e-commerce; integrate with Zapier for Mailchimp if needed.

### Landing Pages and Forms
Both have builders: Brevo includes unlimited forms/popups in free plan; Mailchimp requires paid tiers for advanced popups.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp provides detailed heatmaps and A/B testing; Brevo offers real-time tracking with revenue attribution.

**h3 Advanced Features**
- **Brevo:** CRM, chat, ads manager in one dashboard.
- **Mailchimp:** Intuit QuickBooks integration, audience insights.

**Winner:** Tie—Brevo for all-in-one marketing; Mailchimp for email-centric depth.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface feels polished and beginner-friendly, with guided tours and a vast help center (including video tutorials). Brevo's dashboard is clean but has a steeper curve for automation setup.

**Practical Advice:** Import CSV lists first—both handle segmentation well. Mailchimp's merge tags simplify personalization; Brevo's conditional content blocks add dynamic flair without code.

New users report Mailchimp's setup takes 15 minutes vs. Brevo's 30 for full automation. Mobile apps are available for both.

**Winner:** Mailchimp for absolute beginners.

## Integrations and Deliverability

### Integrations
Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps natively (Shopify, Google Analytics, WordPress). Brevo supports 300+ via API/Zapier, plus native e-commerce (WooCommerce, Magento).

**Practical Tip:** Prioritize your stack—use Zapier for gaps. Test webhook setups for real-time data sync.

### Deliverability
Both boast 95%+ rates. Brevo's dedicated IP option (Enterprise) aids high-volume senders; Mailchimp's authentication tools are standard.

**Practical Advice:** Warm up IPs gradually (start 50/day), maintain <0.1% complaints, segment cold/hot lists.

**Winner:** Mailchimp for breadth; Brevo for e-com focus.

## Customer Support and Community

Brevo offers 24/7 email/chat (paid plans), knowledge base, and academy courses. Mailchimp provides email support (priority for Premium), forums, and Twitter help.

**Practical Tip:** Join Reddit (r/MailChimp, r/emailmarketing) or Brevo's community for peer tips. Use live chat for urgent deliverability issues.

**Winner:** Brevo for responsive paid support.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?
Small-to-medium businesses with growing lists, e-commerce stores needing SMS/automation, or multi-channel marketers. Best if you value cost-per-email over per-contact.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
Beginners, content creators, or nonprofits with small lists. Ideal for design-heavy campaigns and if you're already in their ecosystem.

## Conclusion: Make the Right Choice for Your Business

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your stage and needs. Brevo edges out for scalability and affordability (especially post-rebrand improvements), making it our pick for most growing businesses in 2024. Mailchimp remains a safe, user-friendly bet for starters.

**Next Steps:** Sign up for both free plans, migrate a test list, and run A/B campaigns. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and ROI. Whichever you choose, consistent testing and compliance (GDPR/CAN-SPAM) are key to success.
