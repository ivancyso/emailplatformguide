# Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Top 10 Picks for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many businesses seeking alternatives. If you're looking for more affordable, feature-rich, or user-friendly options, this roundup covers the top Mailchimp alternatives. We'll compare key features like automation, segmentation, templates, and integrations, based on official platform data and user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra.

Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or agency, these tools offer scalable solutions. Let's dive in.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but escalate quickly—up to $350/month for 50,000 contacts with advanced automation. Common pain points include:

- **Pricing opacity**: Audience size drives costs, even if you're not emailing everyone.
- **Limited free automation**: No advanced workflows without upgrading.
- **Deliverability issues**: Some users report higher spam rates post-2023 updates.

Alternatives often provide unlimited sends, better automation, or lower costs. Evaluate based on your list size, budget, and needs like e-commerce integrations.

## Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo stands out for transactional emails and marketing automation. It offers a free plan with 300 emails/day (unlimited contacts), scaling to paid plans from $25/month for 20,000 emails.

**Key Features**:
- Drag-and-drop editor with 48 templates.
- Automation workflows for abandoned carts and re-engagement.
- SMS and WhatsApp integration.
- 99% deliverability rate (per Brevo claims).

**Pros**: Generous free tier; multi-channel (email + SMS). **Cons**: Steeper learning curve for automations.

Ideal for e-commerce with transactional needs. Pricing: Free to $65/month for 20k contacts.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

A powerhouse for automation, ActiveCampaign excels in CRM integration and machine learning personalization. Starts at $29/month for 500 contacts.

**Key Features**:
- 750+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress).
- Conditional content and split testing.
- Site tracking for behavioral emails.

**Pros**: Advanced segmentation; high ROI (users report 208% per G2). **Cons**: Overkill for beginners.

Great for agencies. Compare to Mailchimp: Deeper automations at similar mid-tier pricing.

### 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Tailored for creators, ConvertKit focuses on newsletters and creator economies. Free up to 300 subscribers; $15/month for 500.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automations (no coding).
- Tagging and subscriber scoring.
- Native landing pages and forms.

**Pros**: Simple interface; unlimited emails. **Cons**: Fewer e-commerce features.

Perfect for bloggers. Practical tip: Use tagging for personalized broadcasts to boost open rates by 20-30%.

### 4. Klaviyo

E-commerce focused, Klaviyo integrates seamlessly with Shopify and BigCommerce. Free up to 250 contacts; $20/month for 500.

**Key Features**:
- Revenue tracking and product recommendations.
- A/B testing on flows.
- Predictive analytics for churn.

**Pros**: 82% revenue lift for users (Klaviyo data). **Cons**: Email-only (add-ons for SMS).

Switch if you're DTC. Tip: Set up welcome series to recover 10-15% of lost carts.

### 5. MailerLite

Budget-friendly with strong visuals. Free for 1,000 subscribers/12,000 emails/month; $10/month for 500 subs.

**Key Features**n- Pop-up forms and landing pages.
- Basic automation and A/B testing.
- Website builder included.

**Pros**: Cheapest paid plans; easy migration from Mailchimp. **Cons**: Limited advanced CRM.

Best for startups. Migrate lists easily via CSV—test with a small segment first.

### 6. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels. Free trial; $19/month for 1,000 contacts.

**Key Features**:
- Conversion funnels and webinars.
- Autoresponders with e-commerce tools.
- 150+ templates.

**Pros**: High deliverability (99%); sales funnels. **Cons**: Interface feels dated.

For lead gen. Advice: Use funnels to nurture leads, increasing conversions by 40%.

### 7. AWeber

Veteran tool with strong support. Free for 500 subscribers; $19/month unlimited.

**Key Features**:
- RSS-to-email and tagging.
- 700+ integrations.
- Detailed analytics.

**Pros**: Reliable; great support. **Cons**: Basic design editor.

Reliable for small businesses. Tip: Leverage RSS for consistent content delivery.

### 8. Constant Contact

User-friendly for non-profits/SMBs. $12/month for 500 contacts.

**Key Features**:
- Event management and donations.
- Social media tools.

**Pros**: Beginner-friendly. **Cons**: Pricey for large lists.

Good starter alternative.

### 9. Drip

E-commerce automation specialist. $39/month for 500 contacts.

**Key Features**:
- Workflow builder with customer data platform.

**Pros**: Deep segmentation. **Cons**: Higher cost.

For advanced revenue tracking.

### 10. Omnisend

Omnichannel for e-commerce. Free up to 250 contacts; $16/month.

**Key Features**:
- SMS/push notifications.
- Pre-built workflows.

**Pros**: All channels in one. **Cons**: E-com focused.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: E-commerce? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Creators? ConvertKit.
2. **Budget Check**: Free tiers (Brevo, MailerLite) for testing.
3. **Migration**: Export CSV from Mailchimp; import and verify.
4. **Test Deliverability**: Send test campaigns; monitor spam scores via GlockApps.
5. **Integrations**: Ensure compatibility with your stack (e.g., Zapier).

Practical advice: Start with a 14-30 day trial. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and CTR (2-5%) post-switch.

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ActiveCampaign, and ConvertKit offer better value, automation, and scalability.
