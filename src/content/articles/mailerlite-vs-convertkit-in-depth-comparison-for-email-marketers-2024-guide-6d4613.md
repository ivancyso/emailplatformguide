# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can be tough when you're building your email list as a solopreneur, blogger, or small business owner. Both are popular email marketing tools designed for non-technical users, but they cater to slightly different needs. MailerLite emphasizes affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit focuses on creators with advanced automation for sequences and tagging.

In this comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and real-world use cases. By the end, you'll know which tool aligns with your goals—whether you're just starting or scaling up. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both platforms use tiered plans based on subscriber count, with monthly or annual billing options (annual saves 10-20%). No contracts, cancel anytime.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite stands out for its generous free plan and low entry costs:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups. Ideal for testing.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month (500 contacts), $15 (1,000), up to $310 (150k+). Unlimited emails.
- **Advanced**: $20/month base (500 contacts) adds A/B testing, web push notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom for high-volume.

Practical tip: If you're under 1k subs, start free. Upgrade seamlessly without data loss. Annual billing drops to $8/month equivalent.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) targets creators with a free tier but higher paid plans:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 10,000 subscribers (recent update), but limited to 30-day broadcasts. Forms and landing pages included.
- **Creator**: $15/month (300 subs), $29 (1,000), scales to $779 (50k+). Unlimited emails, basic automations.
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month base (300 subs) adds advanced segments, revenue reporting.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

Practical tip: Free plan suits growing lists, but Pro unlocks monetization tracking. No forever-free beyond basics—budget for growth.

**Winner?** MailerLite for bootstrappers; ConvertKit if you need creator-specific analytics.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly Interfaces

Both have intuitive dashboards, but let's compare onboarding and daily workflows.

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is block-based, like building with Lego. Quick setup: import contacts, design in minutes. Mobile app for on-the-go edits.

ConvertKit uses a visual automation builder (like a flowchart) and clean forms. Steeper for sequences but rewarding for tagging fans.

**Practical Advice**:
- Newbies: MailerLite—import CSV, send first campaign in 10 mins.
- Creators: ConvertKit—tag subscribers by behavior (e.g., "opened ebook") for personalized flows.

User ratings: MailerLite 4.7/5 on Capterra; ConvertKit 4.6/5. Minimal learning curve for both.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Email Editor and Templates

- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates, drag-and-drop with JSON editor for pros. Photo editor built-in.
- **ConvertKit**: Simpler block editor, 100+ templates optimized for creators (newsletters, products). Strong plain-text option.

Both deliver 99%+ inbox rates. Tip: Test templates on mobile—MailerLite edges with more customization.

### Automation and Workflows

- **MailerLite**: Triggered automations (welcome series, abandoned cart). RSS-to-email campaigns. Simple but effective.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences shine—drip campaigns based on tags/actions. Bell weather campaigns for re-engagement.

Advice: Use ConvertKit for nurture sequences (e.g., blog subscriber to buyer); MailerLite for e-commerce triggers.

### Segmentation and Tagging

- **MailerLite**: Groups, custom fields, behaviors. Basic but fast.
- **ConvertKit**: Tag-based (e.g., "lead-magnet-downloader"). Advanced subscriber scoring.

Pro tip: Segment by engagement to boost open rates 20-30%.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

Both offer unlimited:
- MailerLite: 100+ templates, exit-intent popups, countdown timers.
- ConvertKit: Inline/epic forms, one-click upsells.

Integrate with WordPress, Shopify seamlessly.

### Integrations and Analytics

- MailerLite: 250+ (Zapier, Google Analytics, Facebook). Basic reporting.
- ConvertKit: 100+, strong Stripe/Patreon for creators. Revenue per subscriber tracking.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email, huge knowledge base, free migration. Community forum.
- **ConvertKit**: Email/ticket (business hours), Creator Network (podcasts, courses). Priority for Pro.

Both excellent; MailerLite faster for free users.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs ConvertKit?

**Pick MailerLite if**:
- Budget < $20/month
- Small biz/e-commerce
- Need popups/landing pages now

Example: Local shop owner—use free plan for newsletters, upgrade for automations.

**Pick ConvertKit if**:
- Blogger/podcaster/creator
- Complex sequences matter
- Monetize via products/courses

Example: Newsletter writer—tag readers, send personalized drips, track sales.

**Switching?** Both import from competitors easily. Test free trials.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage: MailerLite for affordable, all-in-one simplicity; ConvertKit for creator-scale automations and insights. Neither is "best" universally—start with your subscriber count and goals.

If cost rules, go MailerLite (try free today). For growth-focused creators, ConvertKit's sequences pay off long-term.

Ready to choose? Sign up for both free plans, import 10 contacts, and build a test campaign. Track opens/clicks for a week. Your data will decide.
