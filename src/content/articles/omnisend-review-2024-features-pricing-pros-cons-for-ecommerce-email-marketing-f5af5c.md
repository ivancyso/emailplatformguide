# Omnisend Review 2024: Features, Pricing, Pros & Cons for Ecommerce Email Marketing

If you're running an ecommerce store and looking for a robust email marketing platform, Omnisend often comes up in discussions. This Omnisend review dives deep into its capabilities, helping you decide if it's the right fit for your business. Designed specifically for online retailers, Omnisend combines email, SMS, and push notifications into one platform, making it easier to engage customers across channels.

In this guide, we'll cover its key features, pricing structure, real-world pros and cons, and practical tips for getting started. Whether you're on Shopify, WooCommerce, or another platform, read on to see how Omnisend stacks up.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform tailored for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it focuses on helping store owners nurture leads, recover abandoned carts, and drive repeat sales through targeted campaigns. Unlike general-purpose tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes ecommerce-specific workflows, such as product recommendations and order notifications.

It integrates seamlessly with major platforms including Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, and Magento. As of 2024, it powers over 70,000 brands worldwide, with a strong emphasis on automation to save time for busy entrepreneurs.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend shines with features built for ecommerce growth. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Automation Workflows

Omnisend's automation builder is drag-and-drop simple, allowing you to create workflows triggered by customer actions like sign-ups, purchases, or cart abandonment. Practical advice: Start with the pre-built 'Welcome Series' template. Segment your audience by purchase history, then send personalized emails with dynamic product blocks. This can boost open rates by pulling real product data from your store.

For example, set up a cart recovery flow: Send email #1 after 1 hour, SMS #2 after 24 hours. Users report recovery rates of 10-20% when timed right. Always A/B test subject lines—Omnisend makes this easy with built-in tools.

### SMS and MMS Marketing

SMS open rates hover around 98%, making it a powerhouse for time-sensitive promotions. Omnisend lets you blend SMS into automations or standalone campaigns. Tip: Use MMS for rich content like product images in win-back sequences. Comply with TCPA rules by getting opt-ins via popups—Omnisend's popup builder handles double opt-ins automatically.

Pricing note: SMS credits are pay-as-you-go, so budget based on your list size. Start small: Test with a 10% discount code to flash sales audiences.

### Popups, Forms, and Landing Pages

Capture leads with customizable popups, wheels, and forms. These integrate directly into workflows. Best practice: Use exit-intent popups offering 10-15% off for email/SMS sign-ups. Track performance via Omnisend's dashboard, which shows conversion rates and revenue per visitor.

### Web Push Notifications

For browser-based re-engagement, push notifications remind visitors of abandoned items. They're free to send (up to platform limits) and opt-in friendly. Advice: Pair with email for multi-channel reinforcement, but limit frequency to avoid unsubscribes.

### Reporting and Analytics

Omnisend's dashboard provides ecommerce metrics like revenue per recipient (RPR), ROI, and attribution. Drill down by channel or campaign. Pro tip: Use the 'Journey Reports' to see cross-channel impact—vital for justifying ad spend.

Other notables: API access for custom integrations, 250+ templates, and list cleaning tools to maintain deliverability (aim for <0.5% bounce rate).

## Omnisend Pricing Plans

Omnisend offers transparent, ecommerce-friendly pricing:

- **Free Plan**: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month. Includes basic automations and popups. Ideal for testing.
- **Standard ($16/month)**: Unlimited emails, up to 500 contacts, SMS add-on. Most popular for small stores.
- **Pro ($59/month)**: Advanced reporting, unlimited contacts (pay per 500 extra), custom events.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume.

No contracts; monthly billing. SMS costs ~$0.02/message (US). Practical tip: Calculate costs using their pricing calculator. Migrate from free Mailchimp plans seamlessly—Omnisend imports lists in minutes.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-focused automations save setup time.
- Multi-channel (email + SMS + push) in one dashboard.
- Excellent deliverability (via partnerships like SendGrid).
- Free plan with no credit card required.
- 24/7 chat support on paid plans.

### Cons
- SMS pricing adds up for large lists.
- Advanced segmentation requires Pro plan.
- No native Facebook/Instagram ads integration (use Zapier).
- Learning curve for complex workflows if you're new.

From user reviews on G2 and Capterra (4.7/5 average), praise centers on ease for Shopify users, while some note SMS costs as a barrier for bootstrapped brands.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Best for ecommerce stores with 500+ contacts seeking automation. If you're dropshipping on Shopify or scaling DTC, it's a top pick. Not ideal for B2B lead gen or non-ecom (lacks deep CRM features).

Practical setup guide:
1. Sign up and connect your store.
2. Import contacts and clean bounces.
3. Build a welcome automation.
4. Launch a popup for list growth.
5. Monitor RPR weekly and optimize.

Expect 20-30% revenue lift in first months with consistent use.

## Omnisend Alternatives

- **Klaviyo**: More advanced segmentation, but pricier ($20+/month).
- **ActiveCampaign**: Great for general automation, less ecom-specific.
- **Mailchimp**: Cheaper entry, but weaker automations.

Compare based on your volume: Omnisend wins for mid-tier ecom.

If multi-channel automation aligns with your goals, start with a trial—many users see quick wins in cart recovery and repeat sales.
