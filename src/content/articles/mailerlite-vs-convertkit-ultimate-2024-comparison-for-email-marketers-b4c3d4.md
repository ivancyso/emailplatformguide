# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for creators, bloggers, and small business owners. Both platforms offer powerful features for building lists, sending newsletters, and automating campaigns. But they cater to slightly different needs.

MailerLite emphasizes affordability and simplicity, ideal for beginners and budget-conscious users. ConvertKit, on the other hand, targets content creators with advanced segmentation and monetization tools. In this comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're migrating from another tool or starting fresh, this guide provides practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities.

## Quick Overview of MailerLite and ConvertKit

### What is MailerLite?
MailerLite is an all-in-one email marketing platform launched in 2010. It's known for its drag-and-drop editor, pop-up forms, and landing pages. Key strengths include low-cost plans and high deliverability rates. It's great for e-commerce stores, bloggers, and agencies handling multiple clients.

### What is ConvertKit?
Formerly known as ConvertKit, this platform (rebranded but still widely called by its original name) focuses on creators like podcasters, YouTubers, and course sellers. It shines in tagging subscribers, visual automations, and native commerce features. ConvertKit prioritizes revenue generation through emails.

Both support unlimited emails on paid plans and comply with GDPR/CCPA, but your choice depends on your audience size, budget, and goals.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is a top concern for most users. Here's a practical breakdown:

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation and forms.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 1,000 subscribers (scales up to $20 for 2,500, etc.). Unlimited emails.
- **Advanced**: $20+/month with A/B testing, webhooks, and priority support.

MailerLite's free tier is generous, making it perfect for testing. No contracts, cancel anytime.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (recently expanded from 1,000 in some updates—check site for latest). Basic broadcasts and forms.
- **Creator**: Starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, $29 for 1,000. Includes automations and landing pages.
- **Creator Pro**: $29+/month with advanced reporting, subscriber scores, and one-click upsells.

ConvertKit is pricier for larger lists but offers more creator-specific value. Practical tip: Calculate based on your list size. For 5,000 subs, MailerLite costs ~$50/month vs ConvertKit's ~$79.

**Winner**: MailerLite for budgets under $50/month; ConvertKit if you need pro features.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Interface

### MailerLite
Intuitive dashboard with a clean, modern design. Drag-and-drop editor requires no coding. Onboarding includes video tutorials and templates. Users report setup in under 30 minutes.

**Practical Advice**: Use the campaign checklist to launch your first email quickly. Import contacts via CSV seamlessly.

### ConvertKit
Sleek interface geared toward sequences. Visual automation builder is beginner-friendly, but tagging system has a learning curve. Excellent docs and creator-focused guides.

**Practical Advice**: Start with the 'Welcome Sequence' template to nurture new subscribers automatically.

Both score high (4.5+ on G2/Capterra), but MailerLite edges out for absolute beginners.

## Key Features Head-to-Head

### Email Editor and Templates
- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates, photo editor integration. Strong customization without clutter.
- **ConvertKit**: Simpler editor focused on text-heavy newsletters. Fewer templates but optimized for creators.

**Tip**: Test mobile previews—both excel here.

### Automation and Segmentation
- **MailerLite**: Trigger-based automations, RSS campaigns, A/B testing. Basic segmentation by tags/groups.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced visual automations, subscriber scoring, and behavioral tags (e.g., 'opened last email'). Superior for personalized funnels.

**Practical Advice**: For drip campaigns, ConvertKit's sequences save time. Use MailerLite for simple workflows like abandoned cart recovery.

### Forms, Pop-ups, and Landing Pages
Both offer embeddable forms and pop-ups. MailerLite includes a full website builder; ConvertKit has optimized landing pages for lead magnets.

### Integrations
- MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WooCommerce/Stripe.
- ConvertKit: Strong with Teachable, Gumroad; Zapier too.

**Tip**: If you're on WordPress, both plugins work flawlessly.

### Analytics and Deliverability
Both provide open/click rates, bounce handling. MailerLite claims 99% deliverability; ConvertKit around 97-98%. Track revenue per subscriber in ConvertKit Pro.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive academy. Fast responses (under 1 hour).
- **ConvertKit**: Email/ticket support (no live chat on basic), podcasts, and creator community. Slower but in-depth.

**Practical Advice**: Join MailerLite's Facebook group for peer tips; ConvertKit's blog for strategy.

If affordability and simplicity are key, **MailerLite wins**—especially for lists under 5,000. For creators scaling to paid products, **ConvertKit** delivers better ROI long-term.
