# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform is Right for You in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools trusted by thousands of businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp has long been the go-to for beginners and small businesses with its intuitive interface and free tier. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines for ecommerce brands with advanced omnichannel features like SMS and push notifications.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur or scaling an online store, you'll get practical advice to decide which platform fits your goals. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, launched in 2001, is one of the most recognized names in email marketing. It serves over 12 million users worldwide and handles everything from newsletters to complex automations.

**Key Strengths:**
- User-friendly drag-and-drop editor.
- Generous free plan for up to 500 contacts.
- Robust audience segmentation and A/B testing.

**Best For:** General marketers, bloggers, and small businesses not heavily focused on ecommerce.

Practical tip: Start with Mailchimp's free plan to test basic campaigns. Use their pre-built templates to launch your first newsletter in under 30 minutes.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend is a newer player (founded in 2014) but has rapidly grown, especially among Shopify and WooCommerce users. It emphasizes omnichannel marketing, integrating email, SMS, and web push seamlessly.

**Key Strengths:**
- Ecommerce-specific automations like abandoned cart recovery.
- Pop-up forms and list-building tools.
- All-in-one dashboard for multi-channel campaigns.

**Best For:** Online stores needing automated customer journeys across channels.

Practical tip: If you run a Shopify store, Omnisend's native integration syncs customer data automatically, saving hours on manual imports.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major factor when choosing between Omnisend vs Mailchimp. Both offer tiered plans based on contacts and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month (basic features).
- **Essentials:** $13/month (5,000 contacts, no advanced automation).
- **Standard:** $20/month (5,000 contacts, automation, A/B testing).
- **Premium:** $350+/month (large lists, advanced reporting).

Mailchimp gets expensive quickly for growing lists—expect 20-30% higher costs at scale compared to competitors.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month (full features except SMS).
- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts, unlimited emails, SMS add-on).
- **Pro:** $59/month (500 contacts, advanced segmentation, A/B testing).
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing.

Omnisend often provides better value for ecommerce with unlimited emails on paid plans and pay-per-use SMS.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your costs using both pricing calculators. For 2,500 contacts sending weekly emails, Mailchimp Standard costs ~$20/month, while Omnisend Standard is $36 but includes SMS. Factor in your channel needs.

| Feature | Mailchimp (Standard) | Omnisend (Standard) |
|---------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Contacts | 5,000 | 500 (scales up) |
| Emails | 50,000/month | Unlimited |
| SMS | Extra integration | Included pay-per-use |

## Feature Comparison

### Automation Workflows
Mailchimp offers solid automations like welcome series and re-engagement, but they're linear and less ecommerce-focused.

Omnisend excels with pre-built workflows for cart abandonment (recovering 15-30% of lost sales on average), product recommendations, and cross-sells. Use conditional splits based on behavior for personalized journeys.

**Tip:** In Omnisend, set up a cart abandonment flow: Trigger on exit-intent → Send email reminder → Follow with SMS if no purchase.

### Email Templates and Design
Both have drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp's library is vast (hundreds of themes), ideal for non-designers. Omnisend focuses on mobile-responsive, product-focused templates with dynamic blocks for inventory sync.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp provides detailed reports on opens, clicks, and revenue. Omnisend adds omnichannel attribution, showing SMS vs email ROI clearly.

**Practical Advice:** Track revenue per recipient (RPR) in both. Omnisend's ecommerce tracking auto-links sales to campaigns via UTM parameters.

### List Building Tools
Omnisend's pop-ups, signup forms, and wheels convert 2-5x better for stores. Mailchimp has forms but lacks native gamification.

## Ease of Use

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners—its interface feels like a simple app. Setup takes minutes.

Omnisend has a slight learning curve for omnichannel but intuitive workflows. Dashboard unifies channels, reducing tool-switching.

**Tip for Switchers:** Export lists as CSV from Mailchimp and import to Omnisend in one click. Test a small segment first to avoid deliverability issues.

## Integrations

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).

Omnisend targets ecommerce: Native Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce. Also supports Klaviyo-like pop-ups and SMS via Twilio.

**Practical Advice:** If you're on Shopify, Omnisend installs in 5 minutes. For WordPress, Mailchimp's plugin is seamless.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for paid users; self-serve knowledge base.

Omnisend: 24/7 live chat, dedicated onboarding for Pro plans, and ecommerce experts.

Users report faster resolutions with Omnisend (average 1-2 hours vs Mailchimp's 24+).

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros:** Free plan, easy, huge template library.
**Cons:** Pricey scaling, limited ecommerce automations, no native SMS.

### Omnisend
**Pros:** Omnichannel, ecommerce focus, unlimited emails.
**Cons:** Smaller free plan, less general templates.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp if:** You're a beginner, non-ecommerce business, or need a free starter tool. Great for newsletters and basic segmentation.

- **Choose Omnisend if:** You sell online, want SMS/push, or prioritize automations. Ideal for recovering revenue from abandoned carts.

**Migration Tip:** Audit your current campaigns. Rebuild high-performers first. Use both free trials (14-30 days) to A/B test identical campaigns.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out Mailchimp for ecommerce marketers needing omnichannel power and automation depth, while Mailchimp remains unbeatable for simplicity and free entry. Neither is 'best' universally—test based on your audience size, channels, and budget.
