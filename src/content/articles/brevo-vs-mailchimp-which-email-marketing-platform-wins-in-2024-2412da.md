# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business outreach. With thousands of subscribers relying on tools like Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp, the decision boils down to your specific needs—budget, features, scalability, and ease of use. This Brevo vs Mailchimp guide breaks it down for small business owners, e-commerce stores, and marketers deciding between these giants.

We'll compare pricing, core features, automation capabilities, deliverability, integrations, support, and real-world use cases. By the end, you'll know which platform suits your audience best.

## Pricing: Where Value Meets Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but they differ significantly in limits and paid tiers.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month—great for testing. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for enterprise features like advanced automation and A/B testing.

- **Pros**: Predictable monthly billing; no daily send caps.
- **Cons**: Quickly outgrows free tier; per-contact pricing can spike costs (e.g., 10,000 contacts = $75+/month).

Practical tip: If you're under 500 contacts and send sporadically, start free. For growing lists, budget $20-50/month initially.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo's free plan allows 300 emails/day (unlimited contacts)—ideal for high-volume starters without list growth penalties. Paid plans begin at $25/month (Starter) for 20,000 emails/month, up to $65/month (Business) for unlimited contacts and advanced segmentation.

- **Pros**: Generous free tier; email-volume-based pricing keeps costs low for active senders.
- **Cons**: Daily limits on free plan; transactional emails billed separately.

Practical tip: E-commerce owners sending daily newsletters? Brevo's model saves money. Track sends to avoid overages.

**Winner**: Brevo for cost-conscious users; Mailchimp for unlimited low-volume sends.

## Features Head-to-Head

Both platforms pack essentials, but nuances matter.

### Email Templates and Design

Mailchimp shines with 100+ drag-and-drop templates, mobile-responsive by default, and a creative studio for custom designs. Brevo offers 40+ templates, code-your-own options, and a block-based editor that's intuitive for beginners.

Practical advice: Use Mailchimp for visually stunning campaigns; Brevo for quick, functional setups. Test mobile previews always—80% of emails open on phones.

### List Management and Segmentation

Mailchimp's tags and groups enable basic segmentation. Brevo excels with dynamic segments based on behavior, location, or custom fields—free on all plans.

Tip: Segment by purchase history (e.g., abandoned carts) to boost open rates by 14-20%, per industry benchmarks.

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp's automation is robust on higher tiers: welcome series, re-engagement. Brevo includes automation from free (drip campaigns, RSS-to-email) and transactional triggers.

Practical advice: Set up a 5-email nurture sequence post-signup. Brevo's net-growth automation auto-sends win-back emails.

## Deliverability and Analytics

High inbox rates are non-negotiable. Mailchimp boasts 99%+ deliverability with domain authentication tools. Brevo matches with SPF/DKIM setup wizards and real-time bounce handling.

Analytics: Both track opens, clicks, revenue. Mailchimp's heatmaps visualize engagement; Brevo's compares campaigns side-by-side.

Tip: Authenticate domains early. Aim for <2% complaint rates; use list cleaning tools quarterly.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics). Brevo supports 300+, plus native CRM, WhatsApp, and chat.

For scalability: Mailchimp handles millions; Brevo suits SMBs scaling to enterprise via API.

Practical advice: If WooCommerce-powered? Brevo's plugin syncs orders seamlessly. Zapier bridges gaps.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is polished for non-techies—Evergreen content library helps. Brevo's dashboard is clean, multilingual.

Support: Mailchimp offers email/chat (paid); Brevo provides 24/7 chat/email free, priority for paid.

Tip: Onboard with tutorials. Join communities like Reddit's r/Emailmarketing for peer advice.

## Real-World Use Cases

- **Solopreneurs/Bloggers**: Mailchimp—easy templates, free start.
- **E-commerce**: Brevo—transactional emails, automation.
- **Agencies**: Brevo—sub-accounts, white-label.

Case study insight: A Shopify store switched to Brevo, cutting costs 40% while maintaining 35% open rates (based on public benchmarks).

## Conclusion: Which to Choose?

Brevo edges out for budget-savvy businesses needing high-volume sends and multichannel. Mailchimp wins for design-focused users okay with higher costs.

Test both free plans: Import 100 contacts, send a campaign, build an automation. Your workflow dictates the winner.

Ultimately, align with goals—growth via emails? Prioritize deliverability and segmentation. Start small, scale smart.
