# MailerLite Review 2023: Pricing, Features, Pros, Cons & Alternatives

If you're a small business owner, blogger, or marketer searching for an affordable yet powerful email marketing platform, MailerLite often comes up in recommendations. In this comprehensive MailerLite review, we'll dive deep into its features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance to help you decide if it's the right choice for your needs. With a focus on simplicity and value, MailerLite caters to users who want professional results without a steep learning curve or high costs.

Whether you're just starting with email marketing or switching from another tool, this review provides practical insights based on its core offerings. Let's break it down step by step.

## What is MailerLite?

MailerLite is a Lithuania-based email marketing platform launched in 2010, serving over 2 million users worldwide. It emphasizes user-friendly tools for creating emails, automating workflows, and building subscriber lists. Unlike enterprise-level platforms like Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign, MailerLite targets solopreneurs, small teams, and agencies looking for straightforward functionality at budget-friendly prices.

Key highlights include a forever-free plan, intuitive drag-and-drop editor, and built-in tools for landing pages, popups, and websites. Deliverability rates are solid, often above 95%, thanks to clean IP practices and compliance features like double opt-in.

Practical tip: Start with the free plan to test subscriber growth up to 1,000 contacts—no credit card required. This lets you experiment without commitment.

## MailerLite Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

One of MailerLite's strongest selling points is its pricing, which is based solely on the number of subscribers, not contacts or sends. Here's the breakdown as of 2023:

- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups. No branding on emails.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers (scales up to $20 for 2,500). Unlimited emails, advanced automation, A/B testing.
- **Advanced**: Starts at $20/month for 500 subscribers. Adds webhooks, custom HTML, priority support.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume needs.

Annual billing saves 12-20%. No hidden fees for extra sends—truly unlimited once on a paid plan.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your costs using their pricing calculator. For a list of 2,000 subscribers sending weekly newsletters, expect ~$15/month annually. Compare this to Mailchimp's $29+ for similar scale. Upgrade only when hitting free limits; the dashboard shows warnings.

## Key Features of MailerLite

MailerLite packs robust features into an intuitive interface. Here's a closer look:

### Drag-and-Drop Email Editor

The editor is block-based, similar to modern page builders. Choose from 80+ responsive templates or build from scratch. Blocks include text, images, buttons, dividers, and dynamic content like subscriber data.

**Practical Advice**: Use the "JSON export" for version control. Test mobile previews rigorously—80% of opens are mobile. Embed product feeds for e-commerce via integrations.

### Automation Workflows

Visual builder for drip campaigns, welcome series, and abandoned cart sequences. Triggers include sign-ups, opens, clicks, and custom events.

**Practical Advice**: Start simple: Welcome series boosts engagement 4x. Segment by behavior (e.g., inactive subscribers) and set re-engagement flows. Monitor with built-in analytics for open/click rates.

### Landing Pages, Popups & Forms

Unlimited high-converting pages with A/B testing. Popups include exit-intent, scroll-triggered, and fixed bars. All mobile-optimized.

**Practical Advice**: Place popups on high-traffic pages but limit to 5% frequency to avoid annoyance. Use forms with conditional logic for lead qualification. Track conversions via Google Analytics integration.

### Websites and Blogs

Free drag-and-drop site builder with hosting. Ideal for one-pagers or simple blogs.

**Practical Advice**: Pair with email sign-up forms for list growth. Customize domains easily—no coding needed.

### Integrations and API

Connects to 1,000+ apps via Zapier, native plugins (WooCommerce, WordPress), and REST API. E-commerce integrations pull order data automatically.

**Practical Advice**: Use Zapier for CRM sync (e.g., HubSpot). API for custom apps—docs are clear with code samples.

### Analytics and Segmentation

Track opens, clicks, bounces, unsubscribes. Tag/segment lists dynamically (e.g., by purchase history).

**Practical Advice**: Aim for 20-30% open rates benchmark. Use heatmaps for click insights. Clean lists monthly to maintain deliverability.

## Pros and Cons of MailerLite

### Pros
- **Affordable**: Best free/paid value.
- **User-Friendly**: 5-minute setup.
- **High Deliverability**: Clean sending practices.
- **No Send Limits**: Scale emails freely.
- **Great Support**: 24/7 chat, knowledge base.

### Cons
- **Limited Advanced Reporting**: No revenue tracking out-of-box.
- **No Native SMS**: Email-only.
- **Basic Templates**: Fewer than competitors.
- **Scaling Costs**: Per-subscriber pricing rises with growth.

**Practical Advice**: If you need SMS, integrate via Zapier. For complex reporting, export to Google Sheets.

## Who Should Use MailerLite?

Perfect for:
- Bloggers building newsletters.
- Small e-commerce stores (under 10k subs).
- Agencies managing multiple clients (white-label available).

Not ideal for: Large enterprises needing deep personalization or sales funnels (consider ActiveCampaign).

**Practical Advice**: Audit your needs: If list <5k and budget < $20/mo, MailerLite wins. Migrate via CSV import—takes hours.

## MailerLite Alternatives

- **Mailchimp**: Free for small lists, but pricier scaling.
- **ConvertKit**: Creator-focused, better tagging.
- **ActiveCampaign**: Advanced automation, higher cost.
- **Brevo (Sendinblue)**: Includes SMS, transactional emails.

**Practical Advice**: Switch if outgrowing automation. Export subscribers easily.
