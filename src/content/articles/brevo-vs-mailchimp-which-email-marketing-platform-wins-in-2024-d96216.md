# Brevo vs Mailchimp: The Ultimate Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp—the two giants in the space—helps narrow it down. This Brevo vs Mailchimp guide dives into pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or scaling marketer, we'll provide practical advice based on real-world use cases. No hype, just facts to guide your choice.

## Pricing: Where Value Meets Your Budget

Pricing is often the make-or-break factor. Both platforms offer free tiers, but they differ significantly as you scale.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for enterprise tiers. Key notes:
- Charges based on contacts, not sends.
- Automation and advanced segmentation cost extra.
- Overages can surprise you if lists grow unexpectedly.

**Practical Advice:** If your list is under 500, start free. Monitor contact growth monthly to avoid billing shocks. Use their pricing calculator for projections.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo's free plan allows 300 emails/day (unlimited contacts). Paid plans begin at $25/month for 20,000 emails, with higher tiers up to enterprise. Highlights:
- Billed by emails sent, not contacts—ideal for low-volume senders.
- Includes SMS and chat in higher plans.
- No overage fees; roll over unused credits.

**Practical Advice:** Perfect for transactional emails or irregular campaigns. Track daily limits to avoid throttling. Upgrade only when hitting 300/day consistently.

**Winner?** Brevo for cost-conscious users with growing lists; Mailchimp for high-volume senders okay with contact-based pricing.

## Features Face-Off: Core Capabilities

Both excel in email marketing, but nuances matter.

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp shines with drag-and-drop editors and 100+ responsive templates. Customization is intuitive, with A/B testing built-in.

Brevo offers a similar block-based editor with 40+ templates, plus dynamic content blocks for personalization.

**Practical Tip:** Test templates on mobile first—80% of emails are opened there. Use Brevo's preview for transactional-personalized hybrids.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp's automation is robust for e-commerce (abandoned cart, welcome series). Classic Automations are free; advanced require higher plans.

Brevo's automation includes multi-step journeys, RSS feeds, and sales CRM integration—all available from free tier.

**Practical Advice:** Map your customer journey first. Start with Brevo for simple triggers; switch to Mailchimp if needing deep e-com integrations like Shopify.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Mailchimp: Tag-based segmentation, behavioral tracking, detailed reports (open rates, clicks).

Brevo: Advanced segmentation (custom events, RFM), heatmaps, and revenue tracking.

**Pro Tip:** Segment by engagement—re-engage inactive subscribers quarterly to boost deliverability.

### Additional Tools

- **Mailchimp:** Landing pages, forms, postcards, social ads.
- **Brevo:** SMS/WhatsApp, live chat, meetings scheduler, full CRM.

Brevo edges out for all-in-one marketing; Mailchimp for content-focused extras.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is beginner-friendly, with guided tours and vast resources (tutorials, blog).

Brevo is clean but has a steeper curve for automations due to more options.

**Practical Advice:** Spend Day 1 importing contacts and sending a test campaign. Use Mailchimp if non-techy; Brevo if comfortable with CRM.

Onboarding: Both offer 14-day trials. Mailchimp feels polished; Brevo more functional.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Deliverability rates: Mailchimp ~90-95%; Brevo ~98% (strong IP reputation).

Both GDPR/CCPA compliant. Brevo auto-handles bounces; Mailchimp requires manual cleaning.

**Practical Tip:** Warm up domains gradually (start 50/day). Authenticate SPF/DKIM early. Monitor blacklists weekly.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp: 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Zapier, WordPress).

Brevo: 300+ including e-com (WooCommerce), CRM (HubSpot), and custom API.

**Advice:** List your stack first. Both scale well—Mailchimp to millions; Brevo handles high-volume transactional.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/chat (paid), huge knowledge base.

Brevo: 24/7 chat/email (all plans), phone for premium.

Brevo wins for responsive support.
