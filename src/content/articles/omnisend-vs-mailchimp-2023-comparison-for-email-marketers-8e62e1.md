# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming, especially if you're building or scaling an email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a veteran in the space, known for its beginner-friendly interface and broad appeal. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines in ecommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down features, pricing, ease of use, and more. Whether you're a small business owner, ecommerce seller, or marketer, this guide provides practical advice to make an informed decision. Let's dive in.

## Mailchimp Overview

Mailchimp has been around since 2001 and powers millions of campaigns. It's ideal for newsletters, basic automations, and audience management.

### Key Mailchimp Features
- **Email Templates**: Drag-and-drop builder with hundreds of responsive templates.
- **Automation**: Journey builder for welcome series, birthdays, and re-engagement.
- **Segmentation**: Tag-based lists and behavioral triggers.
- **Integrations**: Connects with Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics, and 300+ apps via Zapier.
- **Analytics**: Open rates, clicks, revenue tracking.

Practical tip: Start with Mailchimp's free plan (up to 500 contacts) to test audience growth. Use A/B testing on subject lines to boost open rates by 20-30%—a proven tactic for beginners.

## Omnisend Overview

Launched in 2014, Omnisend targets ecommerce stores, integrating seamlessly with platforms like Shopify and BigCommerce. It emphasizes recovery flows and multichannel messaging.

### Key Omnisend Features
- **Omnichannel**: Email, SMS, web push—unified inbox for all channels.
- **Automation Workflows**: Pre-built for cart abandonment, welcome series, post-purchase upsells.
- **Product Recommendations**: Dynamic blocks pulling from your store.
- **Segmentation**: Advanced RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) and custom filters.
- **Integrations**: Native with Shopify, WooCommerce, Klaviyo alternatives.

Practical advice: For Shopify users, enable Omnisend's cart recovery automation immediately. It recovers 10-15% of abandoned carts on average by sending timed emails and SMS—set it up in under 10 minutes via their workflow editor.

## Feature-by-Feature Comparison

### Templates and Design
Mailchimp offers a vast library and intuitive editor, perfect for non-designers. Omnisend's templates are ecommerce-optimized with product feeds, but fewer overall options. **Winner: Mailchimp** for versatility.

### Automation and Workflows
Omnisend excels here with visual workflow builders tailored for sales funnels. Mailchimp's is solid but less ecommerce-specific. Example: Omnisend's Win-Back flow nurtures inactive subscribers across channels. **Winner: Omnisend**.

Practical tip: In either tool, segment by purchase history. For Mailchimp, use tags; for Omnisend, RFM scoring. Test workflows with small segments first to refine triggers like 'no purchase in 30 days.'

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both support dynamic content. Omnisend's RFM and predictive analytics give it an edge for targeting high-value customers. Mailchimp relies more on manual tags. **Winner: Omnisend**.

### Integrations
Mailchimp has broader app ecosystem (6,000+ via Zapier). Omnisend focuses on ecommerce (50+ native). **Winner: Mailchimp** for general use.

### Analytics and Reporting
Omnisend tracks revenue per email/SMS with attribution. Mailchimp provides standard metrics plus ecommerce tracking. Both integrate with Google Analytics. **Tie**—use built-in dashboards for quick insights, export for deeper analysis.

### Deliverability and Compliance
Both maintain strong sender reputations (Mailchimp ~99%, Omnisend similar). Always warm up domains and follow GDPR/CAN-SPAM with double opt-ins.

## Pricing Breakdown

### Mailchimp Pricing
- Free: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month.
- Essentials: $13/mo (50k emails, 5k contacts).
- Standard: $20/mo (basic automation).
- Premium: $350/mo (advanced features).

Costs scale with contacts; overages add up quickly.

### Omnisend Pricing
- Free: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month.
- Standard: $16/mo (unlimited emails, 500 contacts).
- Pro: $59/mo (advanced automation, A/B testing).
- Custom for enterprises.

SMS/push are pay-per-use (~$0.01/message). **Winner: Omnisend** for value in ecommerce, as unlimited emails prevent scaling surprises.

Practical advice: Calculate TCO. For 10k contacts sending weekly: Mailchimp ~$100/mo; Omnisend ~$59 + SMS. Migrate during free trials to test costs.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is polished for beginners—onboarding wizards guide you. Omnisend has a steeper curve but ecommerce shortcuts speed setup.

Support: Mailchimp offers 24/7 chat/email (paid plans); Omnisend provides priority chat, knowledge base, and dedicated managers on Pro.

**Winner: Mailchimp** for newbies. Tip: Use Omnisend's templates to bypass learning; watch Mailchimp's free academy videos for quick wins.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp** if: You're a blogger, service business, or beginner needing simple newsletters. Great for non-ecommerce with its free plan.
- **Choose Omnisend** if: You run an online store (Shopify/Woo). Prioritize cart recovery and SMS for 2-5x higher engagement.

Migration tip: Export CSV from one, import to the other. Clean lists first (remove bounces). Set up automations Day 1 to retain revenue.

Practical advice: Audit your needs. Ecommerce? Omnisend. General marketing? Mailchimp. Run split tests post-switch: Track open rates (aim 20%+), clicks (3%+), revenue per recipient.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce marketers with superior automation and multichannel tools, while Mailchimp remains the go-to for beginners and general use. Neither is 'best' universally—align with your goals.

Test both free plans. For most online stores, Omnisend delivers faster ROI via recoveries. Small teams? Mailchimp's simplicity wins.
