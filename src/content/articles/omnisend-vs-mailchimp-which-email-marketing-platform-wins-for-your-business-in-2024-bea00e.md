# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: The Ultimate Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's customer engagement and revenue. In the **Omnisend vs Mailchimp** debate, both tools are popular, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a veteran in general email marketing, ideal for beginners and small businesses. Omnisend shines in e-commerce with omnichannel features like SMS and push notifications.

This in-depth **Omnisend vs Mailchimp** comparison covers pricing, features, ease of use, automation, integrations, and more. Whether you're a startup, e-commerce store owner, or marketer scaling up, we'll help you decide. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, launched in 2001, powers over 12 million users worldwide. It's known for its intuitive interface and robust free plan.

### Key Strengths
- **User-Friendly Editor**: Drag-and-drop builder makes campaign creation simple, even for non-designers.
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month—perfect for testing.
- **Audience Insights**: Behavioral data helps segment lists effectively.

### Practical Advice
Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing. Use its templates for quick newsletters. Set up a welcome series: Go to Automations > Classic Automations > Welcome series. Test subject lines with A/B testing to boost open rates by 20-30% on average.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend is an e-commerce-focused platform emphasizing omnichannel marketing. It integrates seamlessly with Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce.

### Key Strengths
- **Omnichannel Support**: Email, SMS, web push—unified customer data.
- **E-commerce Automations**: Pre-purchase, cart abandonment, post-purchase flows out-of-the-box.
- **Free Plan**: Up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, with SMS credits.

### Practical Advice
For online stores, Omnisend's product recommendation blocks pull real inventory data. Implement a cart recovery workflow: Workflows > Create Workflow > Abandoned Cart. Personalize with dynamic blocks to recover 10-15% of lost sales.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major factor in **Omnisend vs Mailchimp**.

| Feature | Mailchimp | Omnisend |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Free Plan | 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/mo | 250 contacts, 500 emails/mo + SMS |
| Paid Starts | $13/mo (Essentials, 500 contacts) | $16/mo (Standard, unlimited emails) |
| High Volume | $350+/mo (10k contacts) | $59/mo (10k contacts, Pro) |

**Mailchimp** bills per contacts + features (e.g., automation in higher tiers). **Omnisend** offers unlimited emails in paid plans, better for growing lists. Practical tip: Calculate TCO—Omnisend saves on high-volume sends; Mailchimp's free tier suits bootstrappers.

## Feature Face-Off: Core Capabilities

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer drag-and-drop editors. Mailchimp has 100+ templates; Omnisend focuses on 250+ e-commerce optimized ones with product blocks.

**Advice**: For newsletters, Mailchimp's variety wins. For shops, Omnisend's dynamic content (e.g., "Buy again" blocks) drives upsells.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journey Builder (paid), single/multiple automations.
- **Omnisend**: Visual workflow builder with SMS triggers, splits, and goals.

**Practical Advice**: Build a win-back campaign in Omnisend: Segment inactive users (>90 days), send email + SMS. In Mailchimp, use tags for similar logic. Omnisend edges for complex e-com flows.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp uses tags, groups, merge fields. Omnisend leverages purchase history, behavior scores.

**Advice**: Score leads in Omnisend (RFM analysis built-in) for targeted sends. Mailchimp's predictive segmentation (paid) is comparable but less e-com native.

### Analytics and Reporting
Both track opens, clicks, revenue. Omnisend's Revenue Track™ attributes sales across channels; Mailchimp reports ROI per campaign.

**Advice**: Use Omnisend for ROAS tracking in Shopify stores. Export Mailchimp data to Google Analytics for deeper insights.

## Integrations: Which Connects Better?

Mailchimp: 300+ apps (Google Workspace, Facebook, Zapier).
Omnisend: 160+, e-com heavy (Klaviyo alternatives, Judge.me reviews).

**Practical Advice**: Shopify users—Omnisend syncs orders/customers automatically. Generalists—Mailchimp's ecosystem is broader. Test Zapier for custom bridges.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp: Beginner-friendly, extensive help center, email/chat support (paid).
Omnisend: Slightly steeper for non-e-com, 24/7 live chat, knowledge base.

**Advice**: Onboard with Mailchimp's tours. Omnisend's migration tool imports Mailchimp lists seamlessly—use it to switch.

### Omnisend

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Mailchimp** if: Small business, bloggers, non-e-com. Budget under $50/mo, need simple emails.
- **Pick Omnisend** if: E-commerce store (Shopify/Woo), want SMS/push, scale revenue.

**Practical Advice**: Audit your needs—e-com revenue >20%? Omnisend. List <1k? Start Mailchimp free. Migrate via CSV or native tools; A/B test both for 1 month.

## Conclusion

In **Omnisend vs Mailchimp**, there's no one-size-fits-all. Mailchimp excels for general marketing with ease and affordability at entry level. Omnisend dominates e-commerce with powerful automations and omnichannel reach, often yielding higher ROI for online stores.

Test free plans: Sign up for both, import a segment, run a campaign. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20%+), CTR (2-5%), revenue per email ($0.50+ goal).
