# Omnisend Review 2023: Features, Pricing, Pros & Cons for Ecommerce Email Marketing

If you're running an ecommerce store and looking for an email marketing platform that goes beyond basic newsletters, Omnisend might be on your radar. Designed specifically for online retailers, Omnisend combines email, SMS, and push notifications into one dashboard. In this comprehensive review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, ease of use, and whether it's the right choice for your business. Whether you're migrating from Mailchimp or starting fresh, this guide provides practical insights to help you decide.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an all-in-one marketing automation platform tailored for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it powers over 70,000 stores worldwide, with strong integrations for Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce. Unlike generalist tools, Omnisend focuses on omnichannel campaigns—email, SMS, web push—to drive revenue. It emphasizes pre-built workflows for cart abandonment, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells, making it beginner-friendly yet scalable for growing stores.

Practical tip: If your store has under 500 contacts, start with their free plan to test automations without commitment.

## Omnisend Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

Omnisend offers a free plan for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, perfect for testing. Paid plans scale by contact volume:

- **Standard**: Starts at $16/month (500 contacts), includes automations, SMS, and basic reporting.
- **Pro**: From $59/month (500 contacts), adds API access, advanced segmentation, and A/B testing.
- **Custom**: For enterprises with high volumes.

No contracts, and you only pay for active contacts. SMS credits are extra (e.g., $0.015 per message in the US). Compared to Klaviyo, Omnisend is more affordable for small teams.

**Pro Tip**: Use the pricing calculator on their site to forecast costs. Import contacts gradually to stay under limits during trials.

## Key Features of Omnisend

### Powerful Automation Workflows

Omnisend shines in automations. Pre-built templates for abandoned carts recover 10-30% of lost sales on average. Set up a workflow in minutes: Trigger on cart abandonment → Send reminder email after 1 hour → Follow with SMS if no action.

**Step-by-Step Setup**:
1. Go to Automations > Create Workflow.
2. Select 'Cart Abandonment' template.
3. Customize delay timers and content.
4. Add conditions like 'high-value cart' for segmentation.
5. Launch and monitor in real-time dashboard.

Practical advice: Test workflows with a small segment first. Use dynamic blocks to personalize with product images from your store.

### Email and SMS Builder

Drag-and-drop editor with 250+ responsive templates. Embed product feeds directly—no coding needed. SMS editor supports short links and personalization like {{first_name}}.

For ecommerce, the Product API pulls real-time inventory, ensuring accurate recommendations.

**Best Practice**: Keep emails under 100kb for fast loading. A/B test subject lines (e.g., 'Your Cart Misses You' vs. 'Forgot Something? 20% Off!'). Aim for 20-30% open rates initially.

### Advanced Segmentation and List Management

Segment by behavior: purchased X, viewed Y, or RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary). Create tags automatically via integrations.

Example: Segment 'VIP customers' (spent >$100 last 30 days) for exclusive offers.

### Analytics and Reporting

Track revenue per email, ROI, and attribution across channels. Custom dashboards show CLV impact.

**Practical Tip**: Set up UTM tracking for emails linking to your site. Review 'Campaign Comparison' weekly to refine strategies.

### Popups, Forms, and Web Push

On-site tools capture emails with exit-intent popups (conversion rates up to 5%). Web push for re-engaging visitors (even if cookies are blocked).

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Intuitive interface with a 5-minute signup. Onboarding includes video tutorials and a dedicated manager for paid plans. Mobile app for campaign approvals on-the-go.

New users: Import lists via CSV or connect your store in one click. No steep learning curve—designers and marketers alike appreciate it.

**User Feedback**: High ratings (4.7/5 on G2) for simplicity, though advanced users note limited custom CSS in free tier.

## Integrations and Compatibility

Seamless with 140+ apps: Shopify, Magento, Google Analytics, Facebook, Klaviyo (for migration). Zapier support extends possibilities.

**Pro Tip**: For Shopify users, enable the app for real-time order syncing. Test syncs with a staging store first.

## Customer Support

24/7 email/chat for all plans, priority phone for Pro+. Knowledge base with 100+ guides and webinars.

Practical advice: Join their Facebook community for peer tips. Use live chat during peak hours (US/EU mornings).

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

**Pros**:
- Ecommerce-focused automations save time.
- Affordable pricing with free tier.
- Strong omnichannel (email + SMS + push).
- Excellent Shopify integration.

**Cons**:
- SMS costs add up for high-volume senders.
- Limited advanced reporting vs. Klaviyo.
- No native landing pages.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for: DTC brands, Shopify merchants (under 50k contacts), teams wanting quick ROI from automations.

Not for: Non-ecommerce (better with ActiveCampaign), ultra-high volume without budget.

**Practical Advice for Choosing**: Trial Omnisend alongside 1-2 alternatives. Measure recovery rates from your first abandoned cart flow.

## Conclusion

Omnisend earns its spot as a top email marketing tool for ecommerce, blending power with affordability. Its automations deliver tangible revenue lifts—many users report 20-40% increases in repeat sales. If your store thrives on Shopify and you need SMS alongside email, sign up for the free plan today. For larger operations, weigh Pro features against costs.

Ready to optimize? Focus on one workflow weekly, segment rigorously, and track revenue attribution. Omnisend isn't perfect, but for most growing stores, it's a smart investment.
