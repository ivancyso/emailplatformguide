# Top Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: 8 Best Options for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for alternatives. If you're facing similar issues—high fees after 2,000 subscribers, clunky editor, or basic reporting—it's time to explore better fits. This roundup covers eight proven Mailchimp alternatives, focusing on features like automation, segmentation, and deliverability. We'll break down practical advice for switching, based on real user needs for small businesses, e-commerce, and creators.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Before diving in, consider your pain points:
- **Pricing**: Mailchimp's Essentials plan jumps to $13/month for 500 contacts, scaling fast.
- **Automation**: Free plan limits to basic sends; advanced workflows cost extra.
- **Editor**: Drag-and-drop is okay but lacks mobile-first precision.
- **Analytics**: Solid basics, but lacks deep revenue tracking.

Practical tip: Audit your current campaigns. Export subscriber data (CSV from Mailchimp) and test imports on new platforms. Check deliverability scores via tools like Mail-Tester.

## 1. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
Brevo offers transactional emails, SMS, chat, and CRM in one dashboard. Unlimited contacts on paid plans, with automation workflows rivaling ActiveCampaign.

### Pricing
Free: 300 emails/day. Starter: $25/month for 20k emails.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Affordable scaling, excellent deliverability (98%+), EU-based for GDPR. **Cons**: Interface can feel busy for beginners.

**Practical Advice**: Ideal for e-commerce with abandoned cart emails. Migrate by mapping Mailchimp tags to Brevo lists—takes under an hour.

## 2. ConvertKit

### Key Features
Creator-focused: Visual automations, landing pages, subscriber tagging, and commerce tools for selling digital products.

### Pricing
Free up to 1,000 subs. Creator: $29/month for 1k-3k subs.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Intuitive for newsletters, one-click upsells. **Cons**: No phone support; e-commerce limited vs. Klaviyo.

**Practical Advice**: Use for solopreneurs. Segment by 'open rates' for re-engagement sequences. Test broadcasts with A/B subjects.

## 3. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
Advanced automation with machine learning site tracking, conditional content, and 900+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress).

### Pricing
Starts at $29/month for 500 contacts (Lite plan).

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Powerful CRM-email combo, predictive sending. **Cons**: Steep learning curve; overkill for simple lists.

**Practical Advice**: For sales teams, set up 'if/then' branches (e.g., if clicked link A, send nurture B). Export Mailchimp segments directly.

## 4. Klaviyo

### Key Features
E-commerce powerhouse: Revenue tracking, dynamic product feeds, flow builder for SMS/email.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 sends. Scales with revenue (e.g., $60/month for $10k revenue).

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Shopify integration supreme, ROI-focused analytics. **Cons**: E-com only; pricey for non-stores.

**Practical Advice**: Sync product catalogs first. Build win-back flows targeting lapsed buyers—boosts revenue 20-30% typically.

## 5. MailerLite

### Key Features
Drag-and-drop editor, pop-ups, landing pages, A/B testing, and basic automation.

### Pricing
Free up to 1,000 subs/12k emails. Growing: $10/month.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Budget-friendly, fast setup, high deliverability. **Cons**: Automation less advanced than competitors.

**Practical Advice**: Great starter alternative. Use website forms to grow lists organically. Schedule campaigns during peak open times (Tues-Thurs 10AM).

## 6. GetResponse

### Key Features
Webinars, funnels, AI email generator, conversion funnels, and e-commerce tools.

### Pricing
Free trial; Email Marketing: $19/month for 1k contacts.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: All-in-one (autoresponders, landing pages). **Cons**: Upsells during signup.

**Practical Advice**: Leverage webinar integrations for lead gen. Migrate automations by recreating sequences step-by-step.

## 7. AWeber

### Key Features
Reliable delivery, tagging, canned responses, and integrations with 700+ apps.

### Pricing
Lite: $12.50/month for 500 subs.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: 20+ years experience, strong support. **Cons**: Dated interface.

**Practical Advice**: For non-profits/small biz. Use 'Smart Send' for optimal timing. Clean lists quarterly to maintain sender reputation.

## 8. Constant Contact

### Key Features
Event management, social ads, AI subject lines, and robust reporting.

### Pricing
Lite: $12/month for 500 contacts.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros**: Beginner-friendly, US-based support. **Cons**: Limited automation depth.

**Practical Advice**: Pair with social for events. Test mobile previews before sends.

## Practical Advice for Choosing and Migrating

1. **Match Your Needs**: E-com? Klaviyo. Creators? ConvertKit. Budget? MailerLite.
2. **Test Deliverability**: Send test campaigns to your domain.
3. **Migration Steps**:
   - Export CSV from Mailchimp (Audience > Export).
   - Import to new tool; map fields (email, name, tags).
   - Recreate top 3 automations first.
   - Update signup forms/landing pages.
   - Monitor bounces first 30 days.
4. **Compliance**: Ensure GDPR/CCPA tools (double opt-in).
5. **Scale Check**: Calculate costs at 5k/10k subs.

Avoid common pitfalls: Don't rush full switch—run parallel for 2 weeks.

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ConvertKit, and ActiveCampaign offer better value, automation, and flexibility for most users.
