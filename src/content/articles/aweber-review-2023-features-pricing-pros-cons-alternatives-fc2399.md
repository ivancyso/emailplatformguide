# AWeber Review 2023: Is It Still Worth It for Email Marketers?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can make or break your business's growth. With so many options like Mailchimp, ConvertKit, and ActiveCampaign, AWeber has maintained a solid reputation since 1998. This AWeber review dives deep into its features, pricing, usability, and more to help you decide if it's the right fit for your needs. We'll cover practical tips based on real-world usage, without hype or false promises.

Whether you're a solopreneur building your first list or a small business scaling campaigns, read on for honest insights.

## What is AWeber?

AWeber is a longstanding email marketing service focused on automation, list management, and deliverability. Founded in 1998, it powers over 100,000 customers worldwide. It's known for its reliability rather than flashy new features, making it a safe choice for beginners and established users alike.

Key strengths include high inbox placement rates (often above 99% with proper setup) and robust compliance tools for GDPR and CAN-SPAM. However, its interface feels a tad dated compared to modern competitors.

**Practical Advice:** Start with AWeber if you prioritize stability over cutting-edge design. Import your existing list via CSV and use their segmentation tools from day one to avoid spam complaints.

## Key Features of AWeber

AWeber packs essential tools for most email marketers. Here's a breakdown:

### Drag-and-Drop Email Builder

AWeber's editor is straightforward with 150+ responsive templates. Customize with blocks for images, text, buttons, and RSS feeds. It's not as intuitive as Beehiiv's but gets the job done.

**Pro Tip:** Use the "Classic Builder" for complex layouts if the drag-and-drop glitches. Test emails on mobile—80% of opens happen there—and leverage A/B testing for subject lines to boost open rates by 20-30%.

### Automation and Workflows

Build autoresponders, welcome series, and behavioral triggers. Tag subscribers based on actions like opens or clicks, then send personalized follow-ups.

**Practical Example:** Set up a 7-day nurture sequence for new signups: Day 1 welcome, Day 3 value tip, Day 7 offer. This can increase engagement by nurturing leads automatically.

### Landing Pages and Signup Forms

Create pop-ups, inline forms, and full landing pages without coding. Integrates with WordPress via plugins.

**Advice:** Embed forms on high-traffic pages like blog posts. Use exit-intent pop-ups sparingly (under 5% of visitors) to maintain user experience.

### Integrations and Analytics

Connects to 6,000+ apps via Zapier, Shopify, WordPress, and more. Track opens, clicks, bounces, and revenue with detailed reports.

**Tip:** Link to Google Analytics for full-funnel tracking. Monitor unsubscribe rates—aim for under 0.5% monthly by cleaning inactive lists quarterly.

### E-commerce Tools

For Shopify users, sync orders and abandoned carts. Send post-purchase upsells.

## AWeber Pricing: Transparent but List-Size Dependent

AWeber uses subscriber-based pricing with a free plan for up to 500 subscribers (limited to 3 campaigns/month). Paid plans start at:

- Lite: $19/mo (500 subs) up to $149/mo (50,000 subs)
- Plus: $29/mo (500 subs) up to $179/mo (50,000 subs) – adds webhooks, custom JS

No contracts; monthly or annual billing (10-20% discount). Overages cost extra.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate costs using their pricing calculator. For growing lists, budget for tiers—e.g., 2,500 subs is ~$29/mo on Lite. Switch to annual for savings if commitment is low-risk.

Compared to Mailchimp (free up to 500, then $13+), AWeber is pricier for small lists but includes more automation.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Onboarding includes video tutorials and a 30-minute live setup call (free). The dashboard is functional but cluttered with legacy menus.

**User Experience Tip:** Bookmark key sections like Campaigns > Automations. Spend 1-2 hours weekly reviewing stats. New users: Import lists first, then build one campaign to learn the ropes.

Mobile app for iOS/Android handles basic sends and stats—great for on-the-go checks.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

24/7 email/ticket support, live chat (business hours US), and a vast knowledge base. Phone support for Plus plans.

Deliverability shines: AWeber's IP reputation ensures high inbox rates. They provide authentication guides (DKIM/SPF).

**Pro Tip:** Warm up new domains by starting with 50 emails/day, ramping to 500. Authenticate early to hit 95%+ delivery.

## Pros and Cons of AWeber

### Pros:
- Excellent deliverability and compliance tools
- Reliable automation for beginners
- Generous free plan trial
- Strong integrations

### Cons:
- Dated interface (slow on older browsers)
- Pricing jumps with list growth
- Limited advanced segmentation vs. Klaviyo
- No native video/email support

**Real Talk:** If your list is under 10k, pros outweigh cons. For e-com heavyweights, consider ActiveCampaign.

## Who Should Use AWeber?

Ideal for:
- Bloggers and course creators (easy autoresponders)
- Non-profits (affordable compliance)
- Small e-com stores (Shopify sync)

Avoid if: You need hyper-personalization or serve 100k+ subs affordably.

**Decision Framework:** Score your needs: Automation (high? Yes), Budget (tight? Check free tier), Scale (rapid? Look elsewhere).

## AWeber Alternatives

- **Mailchimp:** Free starter, better templates, but weaker automation.
- **ConvertKit:** Creator-focused, unlimited sends, $29/mo flat.
- **ActiveCampaign:** Advanced CRM, $29/mo, steeper learning curve.
- **GetResponse:** Webinars included, similar pricing.

**Switching Tip:** Export lists as CSV; most platforms import seamlessly. Test 2-3 via free trials.

## Conclusion: Should You Choose AWeber?

AWeber remains a dependable choice in 2023 for reliable email marketing without bells and whistles. Its strengths in deliverability, support, and core automation make it perfect for small businesses focused on consistent growth. However, if modern UI or flat pricing matters more, explore alternatives.

**Next Steps:** Sign up for the free plan, build a test campaign, and track metrics for 30 days. Weigh costs against ROI—email ROI averages $36 per $1 spent.
