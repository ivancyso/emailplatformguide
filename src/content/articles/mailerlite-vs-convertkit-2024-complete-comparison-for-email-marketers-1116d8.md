# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can be tough when you're building your email list as a blogger, small business owner, or content creator. Both are popular for their user-friendly interfaces and powerful features, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide based on real-world use cases.

Whether you're just starting with a small list or scaling to thousands of subscribers, understanding these differences ensures you pick a tool that grows with you without unnecessary costs or limitations.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both platforms offer straightforward plans, but MailerLite edges out for budget-conscious users.

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups—ideal for testing.
- **Growing Plan**: Starts at $10/month for up to 500 contacts, scaling to $70/month for 50,000. Unlimited emails, advanced automation.
- **Advanced Plan**: From $20/month for 500 contacts, adds A/B testing, custom HTML, and priority support.

No contracts, cancel anytime. Practical tip: Start free and upgrade as your list grows; it's perfect if you're under 1,000 subs.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **14-Day Free Trial**: Full access to test everything.
- **Creator Plan**: Starts at $15/month (up to 300 subscribers), up to $29/month for 1,000. Includes broadcasts, sequences, forms, and landing pages.
- **Creator Pro Plan**: Same pricing tiers but adds advanced segmentation, revenue tracking, and subscriber scoring.

ConvertKit bills based on subscribers, with annual discounts up to 20%. Advice: If you have fewer than 300 subs, the entry price is low, but it jumps quickly—budget for growth.

| Feature | MailerLite Free | ConvertKit Creator (Entry) |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| Subs Limit | 1,000 | 300 |
| Emails/mo | 12,000 | Unlimited |
| Automation | Basic | Full sequences |

MailerLite wins for free tier generosity; ConvertKit for unlimited sends on paid plans.

## Feature Face-Off

Both platforms shine in core email marketing, but let's dive into specifics.

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 80+ responsive templates. Customize blocks easily—no coding needed. Practical advice: Use their photo editor for quick image tweaks directly in emails.

ConvertKit's Visual Builder is block-based too, optimized for creators. Pre-built templates for newsletters, sales pages. Tip: Its commerce block integrates one-click checkouts for digital products.

Winner: Tie—both beginner-friendly, but ConvertKit feels more modern for long-form content.

### Automation and Workflows

#### MailerLite Automations
Workflows trigger on tags, opens, clicks. Examples: Welcome series, abandoned cart (with integrations). Unlimited steps on paid plans.

#### ConvertKit Sequences and Automations
Tag-based segmentation is king here. Build if/then rules like "If subscribed to podcast, send bonus episode." Visual automation builder prevents overwhelm.

Advice: For simple drips, MailerLite suffices. Complex creator funnels (e.g., lead magnet → course upsell)? ConvertKit's tagging system saves hours weekly.

### Segmentation and List Management

MailerLite uses groups and segments based on activity/behavior. Easy clicks to filter.

ConvertKit relies on tags for hyper-personalization—add multiple tags per sub, automate based on them. Pro plan adds scoring.

Tip: If your audience has niches (e.g., bloggers vs podcasters), ConvertKit's tags enable precise targeting, boosting open rates 20-30% in tests.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

Both offer unlimited pages/forms.
- MailerLite: Drag-drop builder, popups, exit-intent. Embed anywhere.
- ConvertKit: Native forms for incentives, sticky bars. Strong for lead magnets.

Practical: A/B test headlines on forms—MailerLite's free plan includes this.

### Integrations

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress, Google Analytics, Stripe.
ConvertKit: Deep WooCommerce, Teachable, Gumroad. Zapier too.

Creators: ConvertKit integrates seamlessly with podcast hosts like Buzzsprout.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

MailerLite: Clean dashboard, 5-minute setup. Live chat support (paid), email (free). Extensive help center, video tutorials.

ConvertKit: Steeper for newbies due to tag system, but onboarding emails guide you. Email support only, priority for Pro. Creator-focused academy with courses.

Advice: Spend 30 mins in each trial—MailerLite for speed, ConvertKit for depth. Check migration tools; both import CSVs easily from Mailchimp.

## Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite: Open/click rates, bounce handling, revenue tracking (paid). Clean charts.
ConvertKit: Detailed reports + revenue per subscriber. Pro unlocks A/B insights.

Tip: Track campaign ROI—use UTM links and Google Analytics integration on both.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to simplicity vs sophistication. MailerLite is the affordable all-rounder for startups, while ConvertKit empowers creators with pro-level tools for revenue growth.

No clear winner—audit your needs: subscriber size, automation complexity, budget. Start with MailerLite's free plan if unsure; switch to ConvertKit as you scale and sell.

Both deliver 40%+ open rates with good practices like value-first content. Whichever you pick, focus on list-building via lead magnets and consistent sending.
