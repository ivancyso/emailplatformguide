# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming, especially if you're scaling your email marketing efforts. Both are powerful tools trusted by thousands of businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp has long been the go-to for beginners with its intuitive interface and free plan, while Omnisend shines in ecommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll dive into pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, support, and more. Whether you're a small business owner, ecommerce store manager, or marketing team lead, you'll get practical advice to decide which fits your workflow. Let's break it down.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both platforms offer free tiers, but costs scale with contacts and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's plans start with a forever-free option for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Paid plans include:
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/month (500 contacts), unlimited sends, basic automation.
- **Standard**: From $20/month, adds behavioral targeting and custom landing pages.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced A/B testing and automation.

Overage fees apply if you exceed contact limits, and pricing jumps significantly beyond 10,000 contacts. It's cost-effective for small lists but can become expensive for growing businesses.

### Omnisend Pricing
Omnisend's free plan supports up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, with full access to core features like automation. Paid tiers:
- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts), includes SMS and push notifications.
- **Pro**: $59/month, unlocks advanced segmentation, A/B testing, and high-volume sends.
- **Custom**: Enterprise-level pricing.

Omnisend charges per contact but offers unlimited emails in most plans, making it more predictable for high-volume senders. SMS credits are extra but competitively priced.

**Practical Tip**: Calculate your monthly sends and contact growth. Use Mailchimp's pricing calculator or Omnisend's to forecast 6-12 months ahead. For ecommerce with 5,000 contacts, Omnisend often undercuts Mailchimp on total cost.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

Both platforms prioritize user-friendliness, but Mailchimp edges out for absolute beginners.

### Mailchimp's Interface
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is legendary—add blocks for images, text, buttons without coding. The dashboard is clean, with quick-start templates for newsletters and promotions. New users can launch a campaign in under 30 minutes.

### Omnisend's Workflow
Omnisend matches with a visual builder tailored for ecommerce flows (e.g., cart abandonment). It's slightly more advanced, with workflow canvases for automations. Steeper for non-ecommerce users but rewarding for pop-up forms and product recommendations.

**Practical Advice**: Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing. Switch to Omnisend if you need pre-built ecommerce templates. Test both free plans to gauge your comfort—most users adapt within a week.

## Feature Face-Off: Core Capabilities

Here's where differences shine. We'll compare key areas with real-world use cases.

### Email Builder and Templates

**Mailchimp**: 100+ responsive templates, high customization. Great for blogs and general newsletters.

**Omnisend**: Ecommerce-focused templates with dynamic product blocks that pull live inventory. Ideal for personalized recommendations.

Winner: Omnisend for shops; Mailchimp for content creators.

### Automation Workflows

**Mailchimp**: Solid single-step automations (welcome series, re-engagement). Customer Journey Builder in higher plans for branching logic.

**Omnisend**: Superior with visual workflow editor for complex sequences like abandoned cart + browse abandonment + back-in-stock. Triggers include page views and purchases.

**Practical Tip**: For a cart recovery flow, Omnisend's multi-touch automation recovers 15-30% more carts on average (based on user reports). Set up in 10 steps: Trigger > Wait > Send Email > SMS fallback.

### Segmentation and Personalization

**Mailchimp**: Tags, groups, and behavioral segments. Merge fields for basic personalization.

**Omnisend**: Advanced Hyros segmentation by purchase history, browsing behavior, and predicted CLV. Deep personalization like "{{first_name}}'s Recommended Products".

### Omnichannel: SMS, Push, and More

Mailchimp offers basic SMS in Premium plans. Omnisend excels here—seamless email + SMS + web push. Send a cart reminder via email, follow-up SMS if no open.

**Pro Tip**: If your audience prefers mobile (e.g., 40%+ open rates on SMS), Omnisend boosts ROI. Integrate pop-ups for list growth without leaving your site.

### Integrations and Apps

**Mailchimp**: 300+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics). Robust Zapier support.

**Omnisend**: Native Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce apps. Syncs real-time order data. Also Zapier, but ecommerce depth wins.

**Advice**: Check your stack. Ecommerce platforms? Omnisend. General CRM? Mailchimp.

## Customer Support and Resources

**Mailchimp**: Extensive free academy, forums, email support (chat for paid). 24/7 phone in Premium.

**Omnisend**: 24/7 live chat for all plans, dedicated managers in Pro. Highly rated for responsiveness (4.7/5 on G2).

**Practical Note**: Test support pre-purchase. Ask about migration—both offer CSV imports, but Omnisend's Shopify app migrates automatically.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick Mailchimp if**: You're a solopreneur, blogger, or service business with <5,000 contacts. Need simple newsletters and social ads.

- **Pick Omnisend if**: Running an online store (Shopify/Woo). Want SMS/push for higher engagement. Scaling automations for revenue recovery.

**Migration Advice**: Export lists as CSV. Recreate top automations first. Run A/B tests post-switch to measure deliverability (both have strong IPs).

## Conclusion: Make the Right Choice for Growth

Neither is 'best' universally—Mailchimp suits broad marketing needs with ease, while Omnisend dominates ecommerce with revenue-focused tools. For most growing stores, Omnisend's automation and omnichannel edge deliver better ROI.
