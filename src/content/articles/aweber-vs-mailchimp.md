[AWeber](/aweber-review) and Mailchimp are two of the oldest names in email marketing. AWeber launched in 1998, Mailchimp in 2001. Both have evolved significantly, but they now serve slightly different audiences.

This comparison covers pricing, features, automation, deliverability, and ease of use to help you decide.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | AWeber | Mailchimp |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Best for** | Small businesses, affiliate marketers | Beginners, ecommerce |
| **Free plan** | 500 subscribers, all features | 500 contacts, 1K emails/mo |
| **Templates** | 700+ | 100+ |
| **Automation** | Good | Good |
| **CRM** | No | Basic |
| **Phone support** | Yes | No (paid plans only for chat) |
| **Starting price** | $19/mo | $13/mo |
| **Affiliate friendly** | Yes | No |

## Templates and Email Design: AWeber Wins

AWeber offers 700+ email templates across every industry and style. The Smart Designer feature automatically generates branded templates from your website URL. For users who need polished, professional emails without hiring a designer, AWeber delivers.

Mailchimp has 100+ templates and a strong drag-and-drop editor with a brand kit feature. The editor is polished, but the template selection is significantly smaller.

**Winner:** AWeber.

## Automation

Both platforms offer visual automation builders with standard triggers: welcome series, tag-based sequences, and time-based workflows.

**Mailchimp's Customer Journey Builder** is slightly more visual and supports pre-built journey templates. It integrates with Mailchimp's audience data for behavior-based triggers.

**AWeber's automation** is straightforward and reliable. Campaigns, tag triggers, and conditional workflows cover most use cases. It lacks the visual polish of Mailchimp's builder but gets the job done.

For advanced automation needs, neither platform is the best choice — consider [ActiveCampaign](/activecampaign-vs-mailchimp) instead.

**Winner:** Mailchimp (slightly).

## Deliverability: AWeber Wins

AWeber has been optimizing deliverability for over 25 years. Independent tests consistently show inbox placement rates above 95%. AWeber actively manages its sender infrastructure and maintains strong ISP relationships.

Mailchimp also delivers well, but its open platform model means deliverability can vary more depending on the behavior of other senders on shared IPs.

Both support SPF, DKIM, and DMARC authentication. Both provide list hygiene tools.

**Winner:** AWeber (slight edge).

## Pricing

| Subscribers | AWeber (Plus) | Mailchimp (Standard) |
|-------------|---------------|---------------------|
| 500 | Free | Free |
| 1,000 | $30/mo | $26/mo |
| 2,500 | $50/mo | $45/mo |
| 5,000 | $70/mo | $75/mo |
| 10,000 | $100/mo | $110/mo |
| 25,000 | $150/mo | $230/mo |

AWeber is cheaper at 5,000+ subscribers, and the gap widens at scale. At 25,000 contacts, AWeber saves you $960/year compared to Mailchimp.

AWeber's free plan also includes all features (automation, templates, landing pages), while Mailchimp's free plan restricts automation to paid tiers.

**Winner:** AWeber at scale. Mailchimp for smaller lists.

## Ease of Use: Mailchimp Wins

Mailchimp's interface is more modern and intuitive. The onboarding process is smooth, and new users can launch a campaign within minutes. The dashboard is clean with clear navigation.

AWeber's interface is functional but feels slightly dated compared to Mailchimp. The learning curve is still gentle, but Mailchimp wins on overall user experience.

**Winner:** Mailchimp.

## Reporting and Analytics

Mailchimp offers deeper analytics: click maps, revenue attribution, audience demographics, engagement scoring, and predictive insights on higher plans.

AWeber covers the essentials well — opens, clicks, subscriber growth, and campaign performance — but lacks the analytical depth that Mailchimp provides.

**Winner:** Mailchimp.

- Small businesses that value phone support
- Affiliate marketers (Mailchimp restricts affiliate content)
- Users who need extensive email templates
- Businesses with 5,000+ subscribers (better pricing)
- Anyone who prioritizes proven deliverability

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Beginners who want the easiest onboarding
- Ecommerce stores (strong Shopify/WooCommerce integration)
- Users who need a built-in CRM
- Teams that want deep analytics and reporting
- Small lists under 5,000 contacts (cheaper entry point)

## The Verdict

**AWeber** is the better choice for small businesses that value templates, deliverability, and customer support. It costs less at scale and does not restrict affiliate marketing content.

**Mailchimp** is better for beginners, ecommerce stores, and users who want a modern interface with CRM and analytics built in.
