# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp makes sense for many users. Both are popular tools designed for businesses of all sizes, from solopreneurs to enterprises. This Brevo vs Mailchimp guide breaks down key aspects like pricing, features, usability, and deliverability to help you decide.

Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling up, we'll provide practical advice based on real user experiences and official platform details. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Where Value Meets Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing tool. Both Brevo and Mailchimp offer free tiers, but their structures differ significantly.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo uses a contact-based pricing model with daily email send limits on lower plans. The free plan allows unlimited contacts but caps at 300 emails per day. Paid plans start at $25/month for 20,000 emails/month (Starter), scaling to $65/month for 20,000 emails with more features (Business). Enterprise plans are custom.

**Practical Advice:** Ideal for businesses with sporadic sending needs. If you send daily newsletters, calculate your volume—Brevo's unlimited contacts prevent extra fees as your list grows.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp charges based on contacts and features. The free Essentials plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month. Paid tiers begin at $13/month for 500 contacts (Essentials), up to $350+/month for large lists on Premium. All-emails plans remove send limits but cost more.

**Practical Advice:** Great for beginners with small lists. Monitor contact growth closely—costs rise quickly beyond 5,000 contacts. Use the forever-free plan for testing basic campaigns.

**Winner:** Brevo for high-volume senders on a budget; Mailchimp for low-volume starters.

## Features: Core Capabilities Head-to-Head

Both platforms pack robust features, but their strengths vary.

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp shines with its drag-and-drop editor and 100+ professional templates. Customization is intuitive, even for non-designers.

Brevo offers a similar builder with mobile-responsive templates and a theme library. It includes transactional email templates, a plus for e-commerce.

**Practical Advice:** Test Mailchimp if visual appeal is key for branded newsletters. Use Brevo for integrating promotional and transactional emails seamlessly.

### Automation Workflows
Brevo's automation is powerful, supporting multi-step journeys like welcome series, abandoned cart, and re-engagement. No-code setup with triggers like opens or clicks.

Mailchimp's automations are user-friendly for basics (e.g., birthday emails) but require higher plans for advanced branching.

**Practical Advice:** For e-commerce stores, Brevo's SMS integration enhances automations. Start simple in Mailchimp and upgrade as needs grow.

### Audience Segmentation
Both excel here. Mailchimp uses tags, groups, and behavioral data. Brevo leverages CRM-like segmentation with custom fields and real-time syncing.

**Practical Advice:** Segment by purchase history or engagement to boost open rates (aim for 20-30% improvements). Brevo's unlimited contacts make large segments cost-effective.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp provides detailed reports on opens, clicks, and revenue tracking. A/B testing is standard.

Brevo offers heatmaps, link tracking, and deliverability stats. Its dashboard integrates SMS performance.

**Practical Advice:** Track ROI with UTM parameters in both. Use Brevo's stats for troubleshooting bounces.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

Mailchimp connects with 300+ apps via Zapier, including Shopify, WordPress, and Google Analytics.

Brevo supports 300+ native integrations, plus API for custom builds. Strong with e-commerce (WooCommerce, Magento).

**Practical Advice:** Inventory your stack—Mailchimp for marketing tools; Brevo for sales/CRM syncs like HubSpot.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is polished and beginner-friendly, with guided tours.

Brevo is straightforward but has a slight learning curve for advanced automations.

**Practical Advice:** Spend 30 minutes on tutorials. Import contacts via CSV in both for quick starts.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Both prioritize inbox placement. Mailchimp reports 80-90% rates; Brevo similar, with dedicated IPs on higher plans.

**Practical Advice:** Warm up domains gradually (start with 50-100 sends/day). Comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM using built-in tools.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp offers email support on free plans, chat on paid.

Brevo provides 24/7 chat/email, phone for premium.

**Practical Advice:** Paid users get faster resolutions—factor this for time-sensitive campaigns.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?
Small to mid-sized businesses, e-commerce, agencies needing SMS. Perfect if budget < $50/month and sends >10k/month.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
Beginners, content creators, non-profits with <5k contacts. Ideal for design-focused campaigns.

## Conclusion: Your Next Steps

Brevo edges out for cost-efficiency and versatility, especially for growing lists. Mailchimp wins for simplicity and aesthetics.

**Practical Advice:** Sign up for both free plans. Migrate a test list (use built-in importers). Run A/B campaigns tracking opens/CTRs. Switch based on data—most users commit after 1-2 months.
