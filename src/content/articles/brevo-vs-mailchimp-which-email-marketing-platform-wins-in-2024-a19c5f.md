# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp—the industry giant—helps narrow it down. This guide compares their pricing, features, usability, deliverability, and more to help you decide based on your needs, whether you're a small business, e-commerce store, or growing team.

## Pricing: Where Value Meets Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer free tiers, but they differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for enterprise. Features like automation and A/B testing unlock at higher tiers. Overages for extra contacts can add up quickly—expect 15-20% more per tier.

**Practical Advice:** Ideal for solopreneurs testing waters. Calculate your contact growth; if you exceed 5,000 quickly, costs rise fast.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo's free plan allows 300 emails/day (unlimited contacts). Paid plans begin at $25/month for 20,000 emails, with contacts billed separately above 100,000. No send limits per day on paid plans, and transactional emails are free up to your plan's volume.

**Practical Advice:** Great for high-volume senders or those combining marketing/transactional emails. Use their pricing calculator to forecast based on your list size and send frequency.

**Winner:** Brevo for cost-conscious users with growing lists; Mailchimp for very small lists under 500 contacts.

## Features Head-to-Head

Both excel in core email marketing, but nuances matter.

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 100+ responsive templates. Customization is straightforward, even for beginners.

Brevo offers a similar block-based editor with 48 templates, plus dynamic content blocks for personalization.

**Practical Tip:** Test both free editors. Mailchimp suits visual creators; Brevo for data-driven personalization like product recommendations.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp's automation shines with behavioral triggers (e.g., abandoned cart) and multi-step journeys on higher plans.

Brevo provides robust automation, including RSS campaigns and WhatsApp/SMS integration, available from starter plans.

**H3: Best Use Case**
- Mailchimp: Welcome series or re-engagement.
- Brevo: E-commerce automations with transactional follow-ups.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Mailchimp uses tags and groups for segmentation; analytics cover opens, clicks, and revenue tracking.

Brevo's contact syncing and advanced segmentation (e.g., by purchase history) pair with heatmaps and revenue per email metrics.

**Practical Advice:** Export CSV lists to test segmentation logic before committing. Brevo edges for e-commerce analytics.

### Additional Channels
Brevo includes SMS, WhatsApp, and chat—free SMS credits on paid plans. Mailchimp adds social ads and landing pages.

**Winner:** Tie—Brevo for omnichannel, Mailchimp for content ecosystem.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is polished, with guided tours and a vast help center. Setup takes minutes.

Brevo's dashboard is clean but slightly more technical, with strong API docs for developers.

**Practical Tip:** Beginners: Start with Mailchimp's free trial. Tech-savvy users: Brevo's API for custom integrations (Shopify, WooCommerce native).

Onboarding scores: Mailchimp 9/10, Brevo 8/10.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Deliverability is crucial—neither wants your emails in spam.

Mailchimp boasts 99%+ deliverability via dedicated IPs on premium plans and strict list hygiene tools.

Brevo claims 99% deliverability, with domain authentication (DKIM/SPF) from day one and automatic bounce handling.

**Practical Advice:** Always warm up IPs gradually (start with 50-100 sends/day). Verify domains early. Both comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM; Brevo adds CCPA support.

Real-world tests (from public benchmarks like Mail-Tester): Both score 9.5+/10.

**Winner:** Slight edge to Brevo for out-of-box setup.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Zapier, Facebook).

Brevo supports 300+ too, with strengths in e-commerce (BigCommerce, Magento) and CRMs (HubSpot).

**H3: Scaling Considerations**
For 10k+ contacts, Brevo's unlimited contacts (billed high-volume) scale better. Mailchimp caps features by tier.

**Practical Tip:** List your top 5 tools (e.g., WordPress). Check native plugins—Brevo wins for transactional-heavy stacks.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for free; priority chat/phone on paid ($299+).

Brevo: 24/7 chat/email from free plan, phone for premium.

**Winner:** Brevo for accessible support.

## Who Should Choose What?

- **Pick Mailchimp if:** Small team (<2,500 contacts), need quick setup, value design over volume.
- **Pick Brevo if:** E-commerce, high sends, budget under $50/month, need SMS/transactionals.

**Practical Advice:** Run a 14-day trial of both. Import 100 contacts, send a test campaign, and track metrics. Factor in your projected growth—Brevo saves long-term for most.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp isn't one-size-fits-all. Mailchimp dominates for simplicity and brand recognition, but Brevo offers superior value, especially for scaling businesses blending marketing and transactional emails. In 2024, with rising costs, Brevo pulls ahead for 70% of users based on pricing flexibility and features.

Ready to choose? Sign up for Brevo's free plan today—unlimited contacts await. For Mailchimp loyalists, their free tier remains unbeatable for starters.
