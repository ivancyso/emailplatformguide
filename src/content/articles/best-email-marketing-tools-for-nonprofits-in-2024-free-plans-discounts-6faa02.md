# Best Email Marketing Tools for Nonprofits in 2024: Free Plans & Discounts

Nonprofits rely on effective communication to engage donors, volunteers, and supporters. Email marketing remains one of the most cost-effective ways to nurture relationships, share impact stories, and drive donations. With budget constraints common in the sector, platforms offering free plans or nonprofit discounts are essential. This roundup highlights the best email marketing tools for nonprofits, focusing on features like segmentation, automation, donation integrations, and compliance with CAN-SPAM and GDPR. We'll compare pricing, pros, cons, and practical tips to help you choose.

## Why Email Marketing is Crucial for Nonprofits

Email delivers high ROI—studies from nonprofits like the Red Cross show open rates up to 30% for targeted campaigns. Key benefits include:
- **Personalized donor stewardship**: Segment lists by giving history to send thank-yous or updates.
- **Event promotion**: Boost attendance for fundraisers.
- **Advocacy campaigns**: Mobilize supporters for petitions.

In 2024, tools must support mobile optimization, A/B testing, and analytics to track metrics like click-through rates (CTR) and conversion to donations.

## Key Features to Prioritize for Nonprofits

When selecting an email marketing platform, look for:

### Free or Discounted Plans
Many offer nonprofit pricing upon verification (e.g., via GuideStar or 501(c)(3) status).

### Donor-Focused Integrations
Connect with Stripe, PayPal, Donorbox, or CRM like Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud.

### Automation and Segmentation
Drip campaigns for lapsed donors; segment by engagement level.

### Compliance Tools
Built-in unsubscribe management and list cleaning.

### Reporting
Track revenue per email and donor lifetime value (LTV).

## Top Email Marketing Tools for Nonprofits

Here are seven standout platforms, evaluated for nonprofit suitability based on user reviews from G2 and Capterra, official pricing pages, and sector case studies.

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends—ideal for small nonprofits starting out.

**Pros**:
- Intuitive drag-and-drop editor.
- 250+ integrations, including donation forms.
- Automation for welcome series and re-engagement.

**Cons**:
- Free plan limits advanced reporting.
- Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) but nonprofits get 15% off annually.

**Practical Advice**: Use behavioral segmentation to target high-value donors. A church nonprofit reported 25% donation uplift via automated thank-yous. Verify nonprofit status for discounts.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Free plan: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day.

**Pros**:
- SMS integration for urgent appeals.
- Advanced automation workflows.
- Nonprofits eligible for custom discounts.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve for automations.
- Paid starts at $25/month.

**Practical Advice**: Set up transactional emails for donation receipts. Pair with Facebook Ads for retargeting—users note 40% open rates on mobile.

### 3. Constant Contact

Offers 20-30% nonprofit discounts on all plans (from $12/month).

**Pros**:
- Event management tools.
- Robust templates for newsletters.
- AI-powered subject line generator.

**Cons**:
- No free plan beyond 60-day trial.
- Limited automation on basic tiers.

**Practical Advice**: Leverage the nonprofit toolkit for volunteer sign-ups. A food bank saw 18% CTR on storytelling emails.

### 4. MailerLite

Free up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month.

**Pros**:
- Pop-up forms for list growth.
- Landing pages included.
- High deliverability (99%).

**Cons**:
- Fewer integrations than competitors.
- Growing plan: $10/month.

**Practical Advice**: Use website pop-ups at virtual events. Track opens to refine content—one animal shelter boosted recurring gifts by 15%.

### 5. Benchmark Email

Nonprofit discounts up to 50%; free up to 250 contacts.

**Pros**:
- RSS-to-email for blogs.
- Image editor.
- Polls and surveys.

**Cons**:
- Interface feels dated.
- Starts at $13.99/month post-discount.

**Practical Advice**: Automate impact reports via RSS. Great for content-heavy orgs like museums.

### 6. ActiveCampaign

Nonprofit pricing from $9/month (lite plan).

**Pros**:
- Powerful CRM features.
- Site tracking for personalized emails.
- Machine learning for predictions.

**Cons**:
- Complex for beginners.
- No perpetual free plan.

**Practical Advice**: Build donor journeys—e.g., if/then logic for upgrades. Education nonprofits report 3x engagement.

### 7. ConvertKit (now Kit)

Free up to 300 subscribers; nonprofits get 25% off ($9/month starter).

**Pros**:
- Creator-focused for storytelling.
- Native selling (one-click upsells).
- Visual automations.

**Cons**:
- Less emphasis on large lists.

**Practical Advice**: Use for newsletters driving peer-to-peer fundraising. Creators in the space see strong LTV growth.

## How to Choose the Right Platform

1. **Assess your list size**: Start free if under 1,000.
2. **Budget check**: Apply for discounts early.
3. **Test integrations**: Ensure CRM/donation sync.
4. **Trial run**: Send a test campaign measuring opens/CTR.
5. **Scale potential**: Check API access for growth.

## Practical Tips for Nonprofit Email Success

- **List hygiene**: Remove bounces quarterly to protect sender reputation.
- **Content strategy**: 80/20 rule—80% value (stories/updates), 20% asks.
- **Timing**: Tuesdays/Thursdays, 8-10 AM for 20% higher opens.
- **A/B test**: Subjects, send times, CTAs—aim for 2-5% CTR.
- **Mobile-first**: 60% opens on phones; use single-column designs.
- **Measure ROI**: Track (donations - cost) / cost. Tools like Google Analytics UTM tags help.

Case study: A wildlife nonprofit using Mailchimp automation retained 35% more donors year-over-year.
