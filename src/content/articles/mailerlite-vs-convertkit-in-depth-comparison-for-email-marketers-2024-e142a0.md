# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can be tough when you're building your email list. Both are popular for creators, small businesses, and marketers who want automation without complexity. This comparison dives into real features, pricing, and user experiences to help you decide. We'll cover ease of use, automation, templates, support, and more, based on official docs and user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra.

Whether you're a solopreneur or scaling a team, read on for practical advice on picking the right tool.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but they differ in limits and scaling costs.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite keeps it simple and affordable:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation and landing pages.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers (scales up).
- **Advanced**: $21/month for 500 subscribers, adds A/B testing and web push notifications.

No contracts, monthly billing, and they prorate subscriber changes. Great for bootstrappers—users report saving 30-50% vs competitors on similar features.

**Practical Tip**: Start free and upgrade seamlessly. Use their pricing calculator to forecast costs as your list grows.

### ConvertKit (Now Kit) Pricing

ConvertKit targets creators with creator-friendly tiers:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (recently reduced from 1,000), basic broadcasts and forms.
- **Creator**: $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, unlocks sequences and tagging.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month for 300 subscribers, adds advanced segments, commerce, and subscriber scoring.

Annual discounts (20% off) and per-subscriber scaling. It's pricier long-term—expect $50+/month for 5,000 subs vs MailerLite's $30.

**Practical Tip**: If you're under 300 subs, free works, but budget for upgrades. Check their migration tool if switching from Mailchimp.

**Winner**: MailerLite for affordability, especially >1,000 subs. ConvertKit if you need pro creator tools early.

## Ease of Use and Interface

Both have intuitive dashboards, but styles differ.

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly, with a clean, minimalist UI. Building campaigns takes minutes—no coding needed. Their website builder integrates popups and forms effortlessly.

ConvertKit shines for sequences (automated email series). Visual automation builder uses blocks, but tagging/subscriber management has a learning curve. Mobile app is solid for on-the-go edits.

**User Feedback**: MailerLite scores 4.7/5 on ease (G2), ConvertKit 4.5/5. Newbies prefer MailerLite; power users like ConvertKit's depth.

**Practical Advice**: Test both free plans. Import a sample list (CSV) and build a newsletter to feel the flow. MailerLite wins for quick setups.

## Core Features Comparison

### Email Templates and Design

- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates, full customization. Photo editor built-in. High deliverability (99%+ claimed).
- **ConvertKit**: Simpler templates (fewer but clean). Focus on plain-text for authenticity, plus visual editor.

**Tip**: Use MailerLite for fancy designs; ConvertKit for straightforward creator newsletters.

### Automation and Workflows

- **MailerLite**: Drag-and-drop automations, RSS campaigns, A/B testing (paid). Triggers like birthdays or cart abandonment.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences are king—tag-based automations excel for courses/ebooks. Re-engagement campaigns auto-send to inactive subs.

**Practical Advice**: For drip campaigns, ConvertKit edges out. Map customer journeys: e.g., welcome series → purchase nudge → upsell.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Both segment by behavior/tags:
- **MailerLite**: Groups, clicks, opens. Basic reports + Google Analytics integration.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tagging, scoring, revenue tracking per email. Integrates with Stripe for commerce insights.

**Tip**: Track open rates >25% as benchmarks. Use segments to personalize—boost engagement 2x.

### Forms, Landing Pages, and Popups

- **MailerLite**: Unlimited popups, forms, surveys. One-click landing pages with payments.
- **ConvertKit**: Forms and landing pages included. Native checkout for digital products.

**Winner**: Tie—both convert well. A/B test popups for 10-20% opt-in lifts.

### Integrations

Over 100 each: Zapier, WordPress, Shopify.
- MailerLite: Strong e-commerce (WooCommerce).
- ConvertKit: Creator tools (Gumroad, Teachable).

**Advice**: List your stack (e.g., Carrd + Stripe) and check compatibility.

## Customer Support and Community

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Fast responses (under 1hr). Free migration help.
- **ConvertKit**: Email support (business hours), Creator Network community, podcasts. Highly rated for helpfulness.

**Practical Tip**: Join Reddit (r/Emailmarketing) or Facebook groups for peer advice. Both score 4.6+ on support.

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Small business, budget-conscious, need popups/landing pages. Ideal for e-com starters (under 10k subs).
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Blogger/podcaster selling digital products. Love tagging for personalized funnels.

**Migration Advice**: Export CSV from old tool. Both offer free imports. Test sends to 10% of list first.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit? MailerLite wins for most users on price and simplicity—perfect if you're starting or growing affordably. ConvertKit pulls ahead for serious creators needing sequences and commerce (worth the premium if revenue >$5k/month).

Test both free plans today. Track metrics like open rates (aim 30%+) and unsubscribes (<0.5%). Whichever you choose, consistent value-driven emails build loyal audiences.
