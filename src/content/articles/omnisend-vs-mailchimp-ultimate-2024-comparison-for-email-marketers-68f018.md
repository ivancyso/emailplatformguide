# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a longstanding favorite for beginners and small businesses with its intuitive interface and free tier. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines for ecommerce brands with advanced automation and omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down key factors like pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and support. Whether you're scaling an online store or nurturing leads for a service business, this guide provides practical insights to make an informed decision. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing since 2001. It's known for its drag-and-drop editor, extensive template library, and audience segmentation tools. With over 12 million users, it's ideal for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams who want quick setup without a steep learning curve.

**Key Strengths:**
- Free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month.
- Robust automation for welcome series and abandoned cart emails.
- Built-in landing pages and forms.

**Practical Tip:** Start with Mailchimp's free plan to test basic campaigns. Use their audience insights to segment lists by engagement, sending personalized content that boosts open rates by focusing on active subscribers first.

## Overview of Omnisend

Launched in 2014, Omnisend targets ecommerce marketers with seamless integrations for Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce. It excels in omnichannel marketing, combining email, SMS, and web push for higher engagement. It's praised for pre-built workflows that save time on common ecommerce tasks.

**Key Strengths:**n- Free plan for up to 250 contacts with 500 emails/month.
- Advanced segmentation based on purchase behavior.
- Pop-up forms and product recommendation engines.

**Practical Tip:** If you run an online store, connect Omnisend to your ecommerce platform right away. Set up automated cart recovery flows—these can recover 10-20% of lost sales with minimal effort, based on industry benchmarks from similar tools.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major decider. Both offer free tiers, but costs scale with contacts and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 500 contacts, basic features.
- **Essentials:** $13/mo (5,000 contacts) – no advanced automation.
- **Standard:** $20/mo (5,000 contacts) – single-step automations.
- **Premium:** $350/mo (10,000 contacts) – full features, A/B testing.

Mailchimp gets expensive quickly for growing lists, especially above 10,000 contacts.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 250 contacts, core features.
- **Standard:** $16/mo (500 contacts) – unlimited emails, SMS add-on.
- **Pro:** $59/mo (500 contacts) – advanced reporting, unlimited omnichannel.

Omnisend is more affordable for ecommerce users needing SMS, with pay-as-you-go for messages.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your monthly sends and contacts. For under 2,500 contacts, Mailchimp's free plan edges out. For ecommerce with 1,000+ contacts, Omnisend's Standard plan offers better value with automation depth. Always factor in SMS costs—Omnisend charges ~$0.02 per message.

| Feature | Mailchimp Free | Omnisend Free | Mailchimp Standard | Omnisend Standard |
|---------|----------------|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Contacts | 500 | 250 | 5,000 | 500 |
| Emails/mo | 1,000 | 500 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| SMS | No | No | Add-on | Add-on |

## Feature Face-Off: Key Capabilities

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly with 100+ responsive templates. Omnisend offers similar ease but adds dynamic product blocks for ecommerce, pulling live inventory data.

**Winner:** Omnisend for stores; Mailchimp for general use.

**Tip:** Customize templates with your brand colors and test mobile previews—80% of emails are opened on phones.

### Automation Workflows

#### Mailchimp Automations
Customer journeys, tag-based triggers, and behavioral emails. Limited to single-step in lower plans.

#### Omnisend Automations
Pre-built workflows for welcome series, win-back, and cross-sells. Omnichannel branching (email to SMS fallback).

**Practical Tip:** Use Omnisend's cart abandonment workflow: Trigger email 1 hour post-abandon, SMS after 24 hours. Track recovery rates in reports to refine timing.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both segment by opens/clicks. Omnisend adds purchase history and predicted CLV (Customer Lifetime Value). Mailchimp uses behavioral scoring.

**Tip:** Segment high-value customers (last purchase >$100) for VIP discounts—personalization lifts revenue 20% per industry studies.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp: Revenue tracking, A/B tests. Omnisend: Omnichannel ROI, heatmaps for pop-ups.

**Winner:** Omnisend for ecommerce metrics.

## Ease of Use: User Interface Breakdown

Mailchimp feels like a creative studio—intuitive for non-techies. Onboarding wizards guide setup.

Omnisend's dashboard prioritizes ecommerce KPIs, with a slight learning curve for SMS setup but faster for automations.

**Practical Advice:** Spend 15 minutes on each free trial. Mailchimp suits if you design from scratch; Omnisend if you import customer data for instant workflows.

## Integrations: Connecting Your Tech Stack

Mailchimp: 300+ apps (Google Analytics, WordPress, Zapier).

Omnisend: Deep ecommerce ties (Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives), plus Facebook/Instagram for ads.

**Tip:** Use Zapier for custom bridges. For Shopify users, Omnisend's native sync auto-syncs orders—no manual CSV imports.

## Customer Support and Resources

Mailchimp: 24/7 email/chat (paid plans), vast knowledge base, forums.

Omnisend: Priority chat/email, live webinars, dedicated ecommerce guides.

**Practical Tip:** Join both communities—Mailchimp's forums for general tips, Omnisend's Slack for store-specific hacks.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros:** Free tier, easy editor, creative tools.
**Cons:** Pricing jumps, limited automation on basics.

### Omnisend
**Pros:** Ecommerce focus, omnichannel, affordable scaling.
**Cons:** Smaller free tier, less non-ecom templates.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Mailchimp if:** You're a beginner, blogger, or service business under 5,000 contacts needing simple newsletters.
- **Pick Omnisend if:** You sell products online, need SMS/push, or want advanced ecommerce automations.

**Migration Tip:** Export lists as CSV. Test a small campaign first to check deliverability—warm up domains gradually.

## Conclusion: Your Next Steps

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce growth with superior automations and channels, while Mailchimp remains king for straightforward email needs. Evaluate based on your audience size, industry, and budget.

Start free trials today: [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com) or [Omnisend](https://omnisend.com). Track metrics like open rates (aim >20%) and ROAS post-switch. The right tool amplifies your marketing—choose wisely to drive real revenue.
