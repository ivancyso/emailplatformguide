# MailerLite vs ConvertKit 2024: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business or content creation efforts. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit is a common debate among solopreneurs, bloggers, and small business owners. Both platforms offer free plans, powerful automation, and user-friendly interfaces, but they cater to slightly different needs.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, automation capabilities, integrations, customer support, and more. Whether you're a creator building an audience or a marketer driving sales, you'll get practical advice to decide which tool fits your workflow. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email platform. Both MailerLite and ConvertKit offer generous free tiers, making them accessible for beginners.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite's free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. This is ideal for testing without commitment. Paid plans start at $10/month (billed monthly) for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $20/month for 1,000, $30 for 2,500, and so on. Annual billing discounts bring it to $9/month starting price.

Key perks:
- Unlimited emails on paid plans (fair usage applies).
- No contracts; cancel anytime.
- Additional features like advanced automation unlock at higher tiers.

**Practical advice**: If your list is under 1,000 and you're budget-conscious, stick with free. Upgrade only when you hit limits—MailerLite's pricing stays affordable even at 10,000+ subs (around $50/month).

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit's free plan covers up to 1,000 subscribers with core features like broadcasts, forms, and landing pages. The Creator plan starts at $15/month (monthly) or $9/month annually for 300-1,000 subs, then $25/month for 1,000-3,000. It scales with subscriber count: $50 for 5,000, up to enterprise levels.

Key perks:
- Unlimited emails and sends.
- Commerce tools included (subscriptions, products).
- Higher tiers add priority support.

**Practical advice**: ConvertKit suits growing creators; the free plan lacks some advanced tagging/segmentation. Budget for annual savings if committing long-term—perfect if revenue from emails justifies $15+ monthly.

**Winner on Price**: MailerLite edges out for sheer affordability at scale, especially for non-creators. ConvertKit's value shines if you need built-in selling features.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities Head-to-Head

Both tools excel in essentials, but differences emerge in design flexibility and creator tools.

### Email Editor and Templates
- **MailerLite**: Drag-and-drop editor with 80+ responsive templates. Classic and photo editors available. Strong A/B testing (free on all plans) and RSS campaigns.
- **ConvertKit**: Simple visual editor focused on clean, mobile-first designs. Fewer templates (around 50), but excels in personalization blocks like subscriber previews.

**Practical advice**: Use MailerLite for visually rich newsletters (e.g., e-commerce promos). ConvertKit for minimalist creator updates—test both free editors with your branding.

### Forms, Popups, and Landing Pages
MailerLite offers popups, fixed bars, and embedded forms with triggers (exit-intent, scroll). Landing pages are basic but customizable.

ConvertKit shines with subscriber-focused forms (inline, flyout) and unlimited landing pages optimized for signups.

**Practical advice**: For aggressive lead gen (e.g., blog popups), MailerLite's variety wins. Creators: ConvertKit's forms integrate seamlessly with content upgrades.

### Segmentation and Tagging
ConvertKit's tagging system is legendary—tag subscribers based on actions for hyper-personalization.

MailerLite uses groups, segments, and click/activity tracking, but tagging is less granular.

**Practical advice**: If nurturing leads via behaviors (e.g., "read ebook → tag interested"), go ConvertKit. Simple lists? MailerLite suffices.

## Automation: Building Workflows

Automation is where both platforms deliver ROI.

### MailerLite Automations
Visual builder for workflows like welcome series, abandoned carts. Triggers: time, actions, custom events. Free on all plans, with A/B testing.

**Example workflow**: New subscriber → delay 2 days → send blog roundup → if opens, tag "engaged."

### ConvertKit Automations (Visual Automations)
Rule-based sequences with if/then logic. Native support for courses, one-click upsells.

**Example workflow**: Purchase → trigger thank-you → upsell related product 7 days later.

**Practical advice**: Start simple—both handle drip campaigns well. ConvertKit for complex creator funnels (e.g., podcast listener paths). MailerLite for e-com triggers like cart recovery. Test with 10-20 subs first.

**Winner**: ConvertKit for advanced logic; MailerLite for ease and cost.

## Integrations and E-commerce

- **MailerLite**: 100+ integrations (WooCommerce, WordPress, Zapier). Built-in store for digital/physical products.
- **ConvertKit**: Native commerce (subscriptions, digital products), strong with Teachable, MemberPress. Zapier hub.

**Practical advice**: WordPress users: Both plugins work great. Sell courses? ConvertKit. Physical goods? MailerLite + Woo.

## Customer Support and Ease of Use

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Beginner-friendly UI.

ConvertKit: Email support (priority on paid), extensive academy/resources. Steeper for non-creators.

**Practical advice**: Import your list (CSV works on both) and send a test campaign Day 1. MailerLite's chat resolves issues faster.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
Small businesses, e-commerce stores, agencies needing affordable scale. If popups and A/B tests drive your strategy, start here.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Bloggers, podcasters, course creators relying on sequences and tags. If monetizing audiences directly, it's a powerhouse.

**Migration Tip**: Both import from competitors easily. Export lists anytime—no lock-in.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your goals: MailerLite for budget-friendly versatility and quick wins; ConvertKit for creator depth and long-term audience building. Both free plans let you trial risk-free—sign up, import 100 subs, build one automation, and send a campaign.

For most starting out, **MailerLite wins on value**. Scaling creators? **ConvertKit pulls ahead**. Whichever you pick, consistent sending (weekly newsletters) yields 20-30% open rates. Track metrics like opens/clicks, refine, and grow.
