# Brevo vs Mailchimp 2024: The Ultimate Comparison Guide

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp—the two giants in the space—helps narrow it down. This in-depth comparison focuses on key factors like pricing, features, ease of use, and scalability, tailored for small businesses, startups, and growing teams deciding between Brevo and Mailchimp.

Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling to enterprise levels, we'll break down real-world pros, cons, and practical advice to make an informed choice. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo is a versatile all-in-one platform that combines transactional emails, marketing automation, SMS, chat, and CRM features. It's particularly popular among e-commerce stores and SMBs due to its unlimited contacts on paid plans and strong transactional email capabilities.

### Key Strengths of Brevo
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 emails per day with no subscriber limit—ideal for testing.
- **Pricing Model**: Contact-based but offers unlimited send limits on higher tiers.
- **Integrations**: Seamless with Shopify, WooCommerce, WordPress, and Zapier.

Practical tip: If your business sends a mix of marketing and transactional emails (like order confirmations), Brevo's unified inbox prevents deliverability issues common in siloed tools.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has long been the go-to for beginners, known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and creative templates. It's evolved into a full marketing platform with audience insights, ads, and landing pages.

### Key Strengths of Mailchimp
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends.
- **User-Friendly Interface**: Perfect for non-technical users.
- **Analytics**: Robust reporting on opens, clicks, and revenue tracking.

Practical tip: Mailchimp shines for content creators who prioritize beautiful designs without coding. Use its Creative Assistant for AI-generated campaign ideas to save time.

Brevo wins for high-volume senders on a budget—its Starter plan supports unlimited emails for 20k contacts, while Mailchimp charges per send beyond base limits. For example, if you have 10k subscribers and send weekly newsletters, Brevo could save you 30-50% annually.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your needs using Brevo's pricing calculator or Mailchimp's. Factor in add-ons like SMS (Brevo: $10/5k credits; Mailchimp: extra via integrations).

## Features Head-to-Head

### Ease of Use and Editor
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop builder is legendary for its simplicity—new users can create a campaign in under 10 minutes. Brevo's editor is solid but more code-friendly, with responsive templates out-of-the-box.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for beginners; Brevo for developers.

Practical tip: Test both free plans. Import a sample list and design one email to gauge the learning curve.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Brevo**: Advanced triggers like abandoned cart, welcome series, and resends for non-openers. Multi-channel (email + SMS).
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journey Builder for complex automations, but limited on free plan.

Brevo edges out for e-commerce with built-in cart recovery. Example workflow: Trigger SMS 1 hour after cart abandonment, follow with email 24 hours later—boosts conversions by 15-20% per industry benchmarks.

### Deliverability and Analytics
Both boast high deliverability (Brevo ~99%, Mailchimp ~98%). Brevo's dedicated IP option on Enterprise plans suits high-volume senders. Mailchimp excels in behavioral analytics, tracking purchase data.

Practical tip: Warm up your IP gradually (start with 50-100 emails/day) on either platform to avoid spam folders.

### Integrations and CRM
Brevo includes a free CRM with sales pipelines. Mailchimp integrates deeply with Google Analytics and Facebook Ads.

**Practical Advice**: Use Zapier for custom connections. Brevo's native WhatsApp/SMS integration is a game-changer for global audiences.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros
- Affordable scaling
- Transactional + marketing in one
- Unlimited contacts

### Brevo Cons
- Steeper learning curve
- Fewer design templates

### Mailchimp Pros
- Intuitive interface
- Extensive template library
- Strong community support

### Mailchimp Cons
- Expensive for large lists
- Send limits on all plans

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- E-commerce owners needing transactional emails.
- Businesses with 5k+ contacts prioritizing cost.
- Teams wanting SMS/automation bundles.

**Migration Tip**: Export Mailchimp lists via CSV, import to Brevo, and remap automations—takes 2-4 hours.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Solopreneurs and content marketers.
- Brands focused on design and quick setup.
- Users already in the Intuit ecosystem (QuickBooks).

**Migration Tip**: Brevo's import wizard handles Mailchimp tags seamlessly.

## Conclusion: Which One is Right for You?

Neither is universally 'better'—it depends on your stage. For cost-effective scaling and multi-channel marketing, **Brevo** is the smart pick, especially post-rebrand with improved UI. If ease and creativity top your list, stick with **Mailchimp**.

**Final Recommendation**: Start with free trials. Send test campaigns to your list and track engagement. Many switch to Brevo after outgrowing Mailchimp's pricing.
