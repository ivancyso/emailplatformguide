# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses scaling their email marketing. Both are popular platforms with robust features, but they cater to slightly different needs. Brevo excels in transactional emails and affordability, while Mailchimp is known for its intuitive interface and creative tools. This comparison dives deep into pricing, features, usability, support, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or e-commerce manager, we'll provide practical advice based on real-world use cases. Let's break it down.

## Quick Overview

### What is Brevo?
Brevo is an all-in-one CRM platform offering email marketing, SMS, chat, and transactional messaging. It's praised for its pay-as-you-go pricing and high deliverability rates. Ideal for businesses sending both marketing and transactional emails.

### What is Mailchimp?
Mailchimp is a longstanding leader in email marketing with drag-and-drop editors, automation, and audience insights. It has a generous free plan and strong e-commerce integrations, making it great for beginners and growing teams.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a key decider. Both offer free tiers, but structures differ.

### Brevo Pricing
- **Free Plan**: 300 emails per day (unlimited contacts), basic features.
- **Starter**: $25/month for 20,000 emails/month.
- **Business**: Starts at $65/month with advanced automation and segmentation.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume senders.

Brevo uses a credits system for emails/SMS, which is flexible for irregular sending. No overage fees—buy more credits as needed. Practical tip: For startups under 5,000 subscribers sending sporadically, the free plan suffices.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month, basic templates.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts (scales up).
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, with automation.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for large lists and advanced reporting.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts and sends, with higher tiers unlocking features. Tip: If your list grows beyond 2,000, costs rise quickly—budget accordingly.

**Winner?** Brevo for cost-conscious users; Mailchimp for predictable monthly budgets.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp shines with its drag-and-drop builder and 100+ free templates optimized for mobile. Customization is beginner-friendly.

Brevo offers a block-based editor with responsive templates. It's solid but less visually polished than Mailchimp.

**Advice**: Test both free editors. Use Mailchimp for creative newsletters; Brevo for transactional receipts.

### Automation Workflows
- **Brevo**: Advanced triggers like abandoned cart, welcome series, with A/B testing. Integrates SMS for multi-channel.
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journey Builder for complex paths, behavioral triggers.

Both handle drip campaigns well. Tip: For e-commerce, Brevo's transactional focus pairs automation seamlessly.

### List Segmentation and Contacts
Brevo allows unlimited contacts on paid plans with dynamic segments based on behavior.
Mailchimp's tags and groups are powerful, with merge fields for personalization.

**Pro Tip**: Segment lists by engagement (open rates >20%) to boost deliverability on either platform.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp provides detailed insights like click heatmaps and revenue tracking.
Brevo offers campaign performance, bounce rates, and ROI calculators.

Both integrate Google Analytics. Advice: Track open rates weekly—aim for 20-30% industry average.

### Additional Features
- Brevo: Built-in SMS, WhatsApp, live chat, landing pages.
- Mailchimp: Forms, pop-ups, social ads manager.

## Ease of Use

Mailchimp's interface is polished and intuitive—perfect for non-tech users. Onboarding wizards guide you step-by-step.

Brevo has a steeper learning curve due to its CRM depth but improves with tutorials.

**Practical Advice**: Start with Mailchimp if you're new; switch to Brevo for scaling multichannel needs.

## Customer Support

- **Mailchimp**: 24/7 email/chat on paid plans; extensive help center.
- **Brevo**: Priority support on Business+; live chat, phone for enterprises.

Both have academies with courses. Tip: Use community forums for quick wins before contacting support.

## Integrations

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Shopify, WordPress, Google Workspace).
Brevo supports 250+ (Zapier, WooCommerce, HubSpot).

**Advice**: Check Zapier compatibility for custom workflows—both score high.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Brevo boasts 99%+ deliverability with dedicated IPs on higher plans.
Mailchimp maintains strong rates via authentication tools (DKIM, SPF).

Both are GDPR/CCPA compliant. Tip: Always warm up IPs gradually and clean lists monthly to avoid blacklists.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros
- Affordable scaling
- Transactional + marketing in one
- Multi-channel (SMS/chat)

### Brevo Cons
- Editor less intuitive
- Free plan email limits

### Mailchimp Pros
- User-friendly design
- Generous free tier
- Robust analytics

### Mailchimp Cons
- Expensive for large lists
- Feature paywalls

## Who Should Choose Brevo?
- E-commerce stores needing transactional emails.
- Businesses wanting SMS integration.
- High-volume senders on budgets.

**Example**: A Shopify store with 10k subscribers—Brevo saves 40% vs Mailchimp.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
- Content creators and bloggers.
- Teams prioritizing ease and templates.
- Small lists under 5k contacts.

**Example**: A freelancer building newsletters—free plan + drag-drop wins.

## Practical Migration Tips
Switching? Export CSV lists, recreate automations. Test sends to 10% of list first. Use both free trials (Brevo 3 days, Mailchimp 1 month).

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your needs: Pick Brevo for affordability and multichannel power; Mailchimp for simplicity and creativity. For most small businesses, start with Mailchimp's free plan and migrate to Brevo as you scale emails/SMS.
