# Email Deliverability Best Practices: Essential Guide for Choosing Top Marketing Platforms

In the competitive world of email marketing, getting your messages into subscribers' inboxes is crucial. Poor email deliverability can sink even the best campaigns, leading to lost revenue and frustrated teams. For those evaluating email marketing platforms, understanding deliverability best practices is non-negotiable. This guide covers proven strategies to improve inbox placement, with a focus on platform selection.

Whether you're a small business owner or a marketing manager, these insights will help you choose tools that prioritize deliverability. We'll dive into fundamentals, actionable tips, and evaluation criteria.

## What Is Email Deliverability and Why Does It Matter?

Email deliverability refers to the percentage of emails that successfully land in the recipient's inbox rather than spam folders or getting blocked entirely. Industry benchmarks show average deliverability rates hover around 85-95%, but top performers exceed 98%.

For platform choosers, deliverability directly impacts ROI. High rates mean more opens, clicks, and conversions. Low rates waste budget on undelivered sends. Email service providers (ESPs) like those you're considering play a pivotal role through their infrastructure, reputation, and features.

Consider this: If 20% of your list bounces or lands in spam, you're effectively shrinking your audience by a fifth. Platforms with strong deliverability tools help mitigate this.

## Core Email Deliverability Best Practices

Implementing these practices requires both sender-side efforts and a capable platform. Here's practical advice drawn from industry standards.

### 1. Set Up Proper Email Authentication

Authentication protocols are the foundation of trust with email providers like Gmail and Outlook.

- **SPF (Sender Policy Framework)**: Authorizes your domain to send emails via the platform's servers. Verify your DNS records match the ESP's guidelines.
- **DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)**: Adds a digital signature to emails, proving they haven't been tampered with.
- **DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance)**: Builds on SPF/DKIM, instructing receivers on handling failures and providing reports.

**Practical Tip**: Most platforms (e.g., Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign) offer one-click setup wizards. Always enable them during onboarding. Test with tools like MXToolbox or Google's Postmaster Tools.

Without authentication, even premium platforms can't guarantee delivery—ISPs flag unauthenticated mail as suspicious.

### 2. Maintain List Hygiene

Dirty lists kill deliverability. Regularly clean your subscriber base to keep bounce rates under 2% and complaints below 0.1%.

- Remove hard bounces immediately (invalid addresses).
- Suppress soft bounces after 3-5 attempts.
- Use double opt-in to ensure genuine subscribers.
- Segment out inactive users (no opens/clicks in 6 months) and re-engagement campaigns.

**Practical Advice**: Choose platforms with built-in list cleaning, like zero-bounce integrations or automatic suppression lists. Klaviyo and ConvertKit excel here with AI-driven hygiene.

Manual audits: Export lists quarterly and validate with NeverBounce or ZeroBounce services.

### 3. Optimize Content to Avoid Spam Filters

Spam traps are everywhere. Craft emails that ISPs love.

- Balance text-to-image ratio (60/40 text-heavy).
- Avoid spammy words: "Free," "Guaranteed," all caps.
- Use natural links and alt text.
- Keep subject lines under 50 characters, personalized where possible.

**Pro Tip**: Platforms with spam score checkers (e.g., Sendinblue) preview emails pre-send. Test with Litmus or Email on Acid for cross-client rendering.

Monitor blacklists via Talos Intelligence or SenderScore.

### 4. Build and Monitor Sender Reputation

Your sending IP and domain reputation determine placement. Shared IPs (common in starter plans) inherit others' behavior—risky for high-volume senders.

- Warm up new IPs gradually (start low volume, ramp up).
- Switch to dedicated IPs for scale.
- Track metrics: open rates >20%, click rates >2%, unsubscribe <0.5%.

**Platform Selection Advice**: Look for ESPs publishing deliverability stats (e.g., via Return Path certifications). ActiveCampaign and Drip offer reputation dashboards.

### 5. Prioritize Engagement and Segmentation

ISPs favor engaged lists. Segment by behavior for relevance.

- Send to active subscribers first.
- Use behavioral triggers (e.g., cart abandonment).
- Frequency: 1-2 emails/week max for most lists.

**Implementation Steps**:
1. Analyze past campaign data.
2. Create segments (e.g., "high-engagement" vs. "at-risk").
3. A/B test send times.

Platforms like Klaviyo shine with advanced segmentation.

### 6. Leverage Feedback Loops and Analytics

Sign up for ISP feedback loops (Yahoo, Gmail) to auto-remove complainers.

Use platform analytics for:
- Delivery rates
- Bounce/complaint breakdowns
- Placement tracking (via GlockApps tests)

**Routine**: Review weekly, adjust quarterly.
