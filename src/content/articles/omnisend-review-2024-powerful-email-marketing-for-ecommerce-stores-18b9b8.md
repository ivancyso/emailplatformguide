# Omnisend Review 2024: Is It the Right Email Marketing Tool for Your Ecommerce Store?

Choosing the best email marketing platform can make or break your ecommerce growth. With so many options like Mailchimp, Klaviyo, and ActiveCampaign, Omnisend stands out for its focus on online stores. In this in-depth Omnisend review, we'll explore its features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance. Whether you're a Shopify seller or running WooCommerce, we'll help you decide if Omnisend fits your needs.

Omnisend targets ecommerce businesses with tools for email, SMS, and push notifications. It emphasizes automation and segmentation to boost revenue. Let's dive in.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform launched in 2014, designed specifically for ecommerce. Unlike general-purpose tools, it integrates seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, and Magento. It helps store owners send targeted emails, SMS, and web push notifications to recover abandoned carts, welcome new customers, and drive repeat sales.

Key differentiator: Built-in ecommerce data syncing. No need for manual CSV uploads—product data, orders, and customer behavior pull directly from your store.

**Practical Advice:** Start by connecting your store during signup. Omnisend's setup wizard guides you through API integration in under 5 minutes for most platforms.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs robust features without overwhelming beginners. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Builder and Templates

Drag-and-drop editor with 250+ ecommerce-optimized templates. Responsive designs ensure emails look great on mobile (where 50%+ of opens happen). Customize with dynamic blocks for products, order history, and personalized recommendations.

**Practical Tip:** Use the Product Picker to insert shoppable product blocks. Test A/B subject lines directly in the editor—aim for 20-30% open rates by personalizing with first names and recent purchases.

### Automation Workflows

Pre-built workflows for cart abandonment (average recovery: 10-20%), welcome series, post-purchase upsells, and re-engagement. Trigger based on behavior like page views or time since last purchase.

**Pro Tip:** Segment by purchase history (e.g., VIPs who spent >$100). Set win-back flows for inactive subscribers after 90 days—offer 10-15% discounts to re-engage.

### SMS and Push Notifications

SMS campaigns for high-ROI messaging (up to 6x email). Push notifications for cart reminders via browser. MMS support for product images.

**Advice:** Comply with TCPA by getting express consent. Use SMS for urgent promos like flash sales; limit to 1-2/week to avoid opt-outs.

### Popups, Forms, and Landing Pages

Wheel of Fortune, spin-to-win popups, signup forms, and API-driven landing pages. Capture emails with exit-intent triggers.

**Best Practice:** A/B test popup copy. Offer 10% off for emails—expect 3-5% conversion on popups. Integrate with Facebook Pixel for retargeting.

### Segmentation and Analytics

360° customer profiles with purchase data. RFM segmentation (Recency, Frequency, Monetary). Reports on revenue per email, ROI tracking.

**Tip:** Build segments like "abandoned cart >$50" for targeted recovery. Track attribution to see emails driving 30-40% of revenue.

### Integrations

Native with Shopify, WooCommerce, etc. Plus Zapier for 200+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook Ads).

## Omnisend Pricing

Transparent, contact-based pricing:

- **Free:** Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month. All core features.
- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts), unlimited emails, removes branding.
- **Pro:** $59/month (500 contacts), advanced reporting, A/B testing.
- **Enterprise:** Custom, for 100k+ contacts.

No contracts; scales by contacts. SMS/push extra ($0.01-$0.03/message).

**Value Check:** Cheaper than Klaviyo for small stores. Free plan ideal for testing.

**Practical Advice:** Monitor contact growth. Upgrade when hitting limits—import costs $0.002/contact overage.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-focused: Deep product integrations.
- Omnichannel: Email + SMS + push in one dashboard.
- User-friendly: 4.7/5 on G2, intuitive for non-tech users.
- Strong automation: Time-saving workflows.
- Excellent support: 24/7 chat, onboarding calls.

### Cons
- Limited non-ecommerce use: Not ideal for B2B/service businesses.
- SMS costs add up for high-volume.
- No native video email support (embed via links).
- Advanced customization needs CSS tweaks.
