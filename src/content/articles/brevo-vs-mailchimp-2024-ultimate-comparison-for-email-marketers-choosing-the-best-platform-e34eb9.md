# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool is crucial for businesses aiming to nurture leads, boost engagement, and drive sales. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp are two popular platforms, but they cater to slightly different needs. This in-depth Brevo vs Mailchimp comparison breaks down pricing, features, ease of use, deliverability, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or scaling marketer, we'll provide practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities as of 2024.

## Pricing: Free Plans and Scalability Compared

Pricing is often the first factor when comparing Brevo vs Mailchimp. Both offer free tiers, but they differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly email sends. Paid plans start at $13/month for Essentials (up to 500 contacts, 5,000 sends), scaling to Premium at $350/month for high-volume users. Additional contacts cost extra, and features like advanced automation are locked behind higher tiers.

**Practical Advice:** Ideal for beginners testing the waters with small lists. However, if your list grows beyond 5,000 contacts, costs can escalate quickly—budget $100+ monthly.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts but limits you to 300 emails per day. Paid plans begin at $25/month for Starter (20,000 emails/month), with Business at $65/month unlocking more automation. It's contact-based scaling is more generous for growing lists.

**Practical Advice:** Choose Brevo if you have a large but low-engagement list, as unlimited contacts prevent overage fees. For high-volume senders, Brevo often proves cheaper long-term.

| Feature | Mailchimp Free | Brevo Free |
|---------|----------------|------------|
| Contacts | 500 | Unlimited |
| Monthly Sends | 1,000 | 9,000 (300/day) |

In summary, Mailchimp suits tiny lists; Brevo wins for scalability without punishing growth.

## Features Head-to-Head: Automation, Templates, and Integrations

Both platforms excel in core email marketing, but let's dive deeper.

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp offers a vast library of drag-and-drop templates with strong mobile optimization. Brevo provides customizable templates via its builder, plus SMS and WhatsApp integration for multichannel campaigns.

**Practical Tip:** Use Mailchimp for visually stunning newsletters; switch to Brevo if you need transactional emails or SMS alongside email.

### Automation Workflows
Mailchimp's automation is user-friendly with pre-built journeys like welcome series. Brevo shines with advanced branching logic and API-triggered automations, better for e-commerce.

**Pro Tip:** Test automations with a small segment first. Brevo's unlimited workflows in higher plans save time for complex funnels.

### Landing Pages and Forms
Mailchimp includes basic landing pages in paid plans. Brevo offers more robust forms, pop-ups, and pages with A/B testing built-in.

### Integrations
Both integrate with Shopify, WordPress, and Zapier. Mailchimp has 300+ native apps; Brevo emphasizes CRM and sales tools like HubSpot.

**Advice for Users:** E-commerce stores should prioritize Brevo for abandoned cart recovery; content creators may prefer Mailchimp's content studio.

## Ease of Use and User Interface

Mailchimp's interface is intuitive, with a colorful dashboard perfect for non-tech users. Onboarding includes guided tours.

Brevo's modern UI is clean but has a steeper learning curve for automation setup. Mobile apps are available for both.

**Practical Advice:** Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing—import contacts and send your first campaign in under 30 minutes. Brevo rewards experienced users with deeper customization.

User reviews (from G2 and Capterra) rate Mailchimp 4.4/5 for ease of use vs Brevo's 4.3/5, but Brevo edges out in value for money (4.5/5).

## Deliverability and Analytics

Deliverability matters for ROI. Mailchimp boasts 99%+ rates with dedicated IPs on premium plans. Brevo matches this, with strong EU compliance (GDPR-focused).

Analytics: Mailchimp tracks opens, clicks, and revenue. Brevo adds heatmaps and SMS metrics.

**Tip:** Warm up your IP by starting with engaged segments. Monitor bounce rates—aim under 2%. Both platforms provide domain authentication guides (DKIM/SPF).

## Customer Support and Community

Mailchimp offers 24/7 email support on paid plans, plus extensive resources. Brevo provides live chat, priority support from $65/month, and a helpful knowledge base.

**Advice:** Free users get forums for both. Paid Brevo users report faster resolutions for technical issues.

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Mailchimp if:** You're a small team (<1,000 contacts), prioritize design, or need quick setup.
- **Pick Brevo if:** Scaling mid-sized lists (1k-50k), multichannel campaigns, or cost-efficiency.

**Migration Tip:** Both allow CSV imports. Test with a subset before full switch—use Zapier for automation transfer.

## Conclusion: The Verdict on Brevo vs Mailchimp

Neither is universally 'better'—it depends on your stage. For most growing businesses in 2024, **Brevo edges out Mailchimp** due to pricing flexibility and multichannel features, especially post-rebrand improvements.

Start with free trials: Sign up for both, import 100 contacts, and run a test campaign. Track metrics like open rates (target 20-30%) and decide based on your workflow.

Ready to choose? Brevo suits ambitious marketers; Mailchimp fits creative starters. Whichever you pick, consistent segmentation and value-driven content will skyrocket your results.
