# Omnisend Review 2023: Is It the Right Email Marketing Tool for Your Ecommerce Store?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can make or break your ecommerce growth. With so many options like Mailchimp, Klaviyo, and ActiveCampaign, Omnisend stands out for its focus on ecommerce-specific features. In this comprehensive Omnisend review, we'll dive into its capabilities, pricing, real-world usability, and whether it's worth your investment. Tailored for store owners evaluating platforms, this guide provides practical advice based on verified features and user feedback.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform designed primarily for ecommerce businesses. Launched in 2014, it powers over 70,000 stores worldwide, integrating seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce. Unlike general-purpose tools, Omnisend emphasizes automated workflows for cart recovery, welcome series, and cross-selling via email, SMS, and web push notifications.

Practical tip: If you're on Shopify, Omnisend's one-click app install syncs customer data instantly, saving hours of setup time. Start by connecting your store to pull in orders, products, and subscriber lists automatically.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend's strength lies in its ecommerce-centric toolkit. Here's a breakdown of standout features with hands-on advice.

### Drag-and-Drop Email and SMS Builder

The visual editor lets you create responsive emails without coding. Pre-built templates for product blocks, countdown timers, and dynamic content blocks pull live product data from your store.

**Practical advice:** Use dynamic blocks for personalized recommendations. Segment by purchase history (e.g., 'last bought shoes') and insert 'Buy again' carousels. Test mobile previews—80% of ecommerce opens are mobile. A/B test subject lines like 'Your cart misses you!' vs. 'Complete your purchase' to boost open rates by 20-30%.

### Automation Workflows

Omnisend excels in pre-built automations: abandoned cart (recovers 10-20% on average), welcome series, post-purchase upsells, and re-engagement campaigns.

**Practical advice:** Set up a 3-email abandoned cart flow: Email 1 (1 hour post-abandon) with cart recap; Email 2 (24 hours) with discount; Email 3 (72 hours) with free shipping. Track recovery rate in the dashboard—aim for under 1% unsubscribe rate by personalizing with first name and items.

### Omnichannel Messaging: Email, SMS, and Push

Combine channels in one workflow. SMS open rates hit 98%, ideal for time-sensitive promos.

**Practical advice:** Trigger SMS after email non-opens in flash sales. Use web push for cart abandoners—install the snippet on your site for browser notifications. Compliance tip: Always include opt-in checkboxes and SMS keywords like 'JOIN' to meet TCPA regs.

### Advanced Segmentation and Analytics

Segment by behavior (page views, purchases), RFM (recency, frequency, monetary), or custom events. Reports show revenue per email, ROI, and attribution.

**Practical advice:** Create segments like 'VIPs: spent >$500 last 90 days' for exclusive offers. Use the heatmap to spot high-engagement times (e.g., evenings for fashion). Export CSV for deeper Excel analysis if needed.

### Integrations

Native with Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento, Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, and Zapier for 200+ apps.

**Practical advice:** Link to Google Analytics for UTM tracking on links. Use Zapier to sync with inventory tools like Dear Inventory to pause campaigns during stockouts.

## Omnisend Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

Omnisend offers a free plan (up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month) and paid tiers:

- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts) up to $59/month (10k contacts). Unlimited emails, basic automations, SMS add-on.
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts) scaling to $1,544/month (150k). Adds A/B testing, custom events, API access, priority support.

No contracts; monthly billing. SMS costs extra (~$0.02/message in US).

**Practical advice:** Start free to test automations. Upgrade when hitting 250 contacts—Standard suits most small stores (<$100k/month revenue). Calculate ROI: If one campaign generates $500 revenue at 30% margin, it pays for Pro.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-first: Deep product sync and recovery tools outperform generalists.
- User-friendly: 4.7/5 on G2, intuitive for non-marketers.
- High deliverability: 99% inbox rate via dedicated IPs on Pro.
- Omnichannel: Rare all-in-one for email/SMS/push.

### Cons
- SMS pricey for high-volume (better for targeted blasts).
- Limited non-ecommerce use (not ideal for B2B newsletters).
- Learning curve for advanced segmentation.

**Practical advice:** If SMS volume >1k/month, compare Twilio integrations elsewhere. For B2B, consider ActiveCampaign.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for DTC brands, Shopify stores, and mid-sized ecommerce ($10k-$1M/month) needing automation without a specialist. Skip if you're a service business or on a tiny budget (free tier limits scale).

**Real user insights:** Shopify owners report 25% revenue lift from cart recovery. Agencies praise white-label reporting.
