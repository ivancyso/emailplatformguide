# Brevo vs Mailchimp: 2024 Comparison Guide for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting **Brevo vs Mailchimp** is a common debate among small business owners, e-commerce stores, and marketing teams. Both tools offer robust features for building lists, sending campaigns, and automating workflows, but they cater to slightly different needs.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, customer support, and scalability. Whether you're starting from scratch or switching platforms, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. All data is based on official pricing and features as of late 2024.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) is an all-in-one marketing platform emphasizing affordability and multichannel communication. It's particularly strong for transactional emails, SMS marketing, and CRM integrations. Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts with 300 emails per day, making it ideal for bootstrapped startups.

Key strengths:
- Multichannel support (email, SMS, WhatsApp, chat)
- Advanced automation with triggers
- Built-in CRM and sales tools

Practical tip: If your business relies on e-commerce, Brevo's real-time transactional emails (like order confirmations) integrate seamlessly with platforms like Shopify and WooCommerce.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has long been a household name in email marketing, known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and creative templates. It offers a generous free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month. Mailchimp excels in audience segmentation and analytics, with strong e-commerce features via its abandoned cart recovery.

Key strengths:
- Beautiful, mobile-responsive templates
- Comprehensive audience insights
- Extensive third-party integrations

Practical tip: For content creators or agencies, Mailchimp's design-focused tools let you craft visually stunning newsletters without coding skills.

## Pricing Comparison: Brevo vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major factor when choosing between Brevo and Mailchimp. Both have free tiers, but paid plans scale differently.

### Brevo Pricing
- **Free**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day
- **Starter**: $25/mo (20K emails/mo)
- **Business**: $65/mo (20K emails/mo + automation/SMS)
- **Enterprise**: Custom

Brevo charges based on email volume, not contacts, which favors growing lists.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free**: 500 contacts, 1K sends/mo
- **Essentials**: $13/mo (5K contacts)
- **Standard**: $20/mo (5K contacts + automation)
- **Premium**: $350/mo (10K+ contacts)

Mailchimp's costs rise quickly with contact growth.

**Verdict**: Brevo is more cost-effective for high-volume senders or large lists. For example, 50K contacts with moderate sends could cost $65/mo on Brevo vs. $350+ on Mailchimp. Always calculate based on your projected volume using their pricing calculators.

Practical advice: Audit your current list size and send frequency. If you're under 10K contacts and low volume, Mailchimp's free tier might suffice initially.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp shines with its drag-and-drop editor and 100+ customizable templates. Brevo offers a similar builder but with more focus on dynamic content blocks for personalization.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for design-heavy campaigns.

### Automation Workflows
Both support drip campaigns and behavioral triggers.
- Brevo: Multi-step automations with A/B testing and SMS branching.
- Mailchimp: Customer Journey Builder for complex paths.

Practical tip: Test automations with small segments first. Brevo's interface is linear and beginner-friendly for simple welcome series.

### Segmentation and Analytics
Mailchimp's predictive segmentation (e.g., predicted demographics) gives deeper insights. Brevo provides solid RFM analysis and real-time reporting.

**H3: Integrations**
Brevo: Native with Shopify, WordPress, Zapier (500+ apps).
Mailchimp: 300+ native integrations, including Google Analytics and Facebook.

Both score high here, but Mailchimp edges for marketing stacks.

### Additional Features
- Brevo: SMS/WhatsApp, live chat, ads manager.
- Mailchimp: Landing pages, postcards, social ads.

Practical advice: If omnichannel is key (e.g., SMS for cart recovery), choose Brevo. For pure email + social, Mailchimp fits.

## Ease of Use: Which is More Beginner-Friendly?

Mailchimp's interface feels polished and intuitive, with guided onboarding. Brevo is straightforward but has a slight learning curve for its CRM dashboard.

User reviews (from G2/Capterra) rate Mailchimp 4.5/5 for ease of use vs. Brevo's 4.4/5.

Practical tip: Start with Mailchimp's free tutorials if you're new. Brevo's setup wizard is great for importing lists quickly.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **Brevo**: 24/7 email/chat for paid plans, extensive knowledge base, community forum.
- **Mailchimp**: Priority support on higher tiers, phone for Premium, vast help center with videos.

Brevo responds faster for free users. Both offer academies for advanced training.

Practical advice: Check response times via live chat before committing. Join their Facebook groups for peer tips.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros
- Affordable scaling
- Multichannel
- Unlimited contacts free

### Brevo Cons
- Template variety limited
- Less polished analytics

### Mailchimp Pros
- Stunning designs
- Advanced segmentation
- Strong free plan

### Mailchimp Cons
- Expensive at scale
- No SMS native

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Brevo** if: You're e-commerce focused, need SMS, or have growing lists on a budget. Ideal for solopreneurs or SMBs sending frequently.
- **Choose Mailchimp** if: Design matters most, you need deep analytics, or integrate with creative tools. Best for agencies or content marketers.

Migration tip: Both support CSV imports. Use Zapier for seamless data transfer. Test with a subset to avoid deliverability issues.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your priorities: Brevo wins on cost and multichannel for transactional-heavy businesses, while Mailchimp leads in design and insights for creative campaigns. For most small businesses in 2024, start with Brevo's free plan to test scalability.

Evaluate with a 14-day trial of both. Track metrics like open rates (aim >20%) and ROI post-launch. Whichever you pick, consistent testing and list hygiene are key to success.
