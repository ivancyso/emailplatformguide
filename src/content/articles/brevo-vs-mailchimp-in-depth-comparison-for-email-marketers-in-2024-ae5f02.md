# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses scaling their email marketing. Both platforms offer robust tools for newsletters, automations, and customer engagement, but they cater to different needs. This comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, support, and deliverability based on current offerings as of 2024. We'll help you decide based on your audience size, budget, and goals.

## Pricing: Free Plans and Scalability

Pricing is a top concern for email marketers. Both platforms have free tiers, but their structures differ significantly.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo uses a contact-based model with unlimited emails on most plans. The free plan supports up to 300 emails per day (about 9,000 monthly) for unlimited contacts, ideal for startups testing waters.

Paid plans start at $25/month for Starter (20,000 emails/month, 1,000 contacts), scaling to Business ($65/month for 20,000 emails, unlimited contacts) and Enterprise (custom). Add-ons for SMS and advanced automation are available. No contracts required, and annual billing saves 10-20%.

Practical tip: If your list exceeds 2,000 contacts but sends are low-volume, Brevo's unlimited contacts keep costs predictable.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp charges based on contacts and features. The free Essentials plan limits you to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month, with basic templates.

Standard plan starts at $20/month (500 contacts, 5x sends up to 100,000), Pro at $350/month for advanced features. High-volume senders face steep jumps—e.g., 50,000 contacts on Standard costs ~$750/month.

Practical tip: Mailchimp suits small lists (<5,000) on free/Standard, but growing businesses often outgrow it due to per-contact pricing.

**Winner for Budget:** Brevo for mid-sized lists; Mailchimp for tiny audiences.

## Core Features: Emails, Automations, and Beyond

Both excel in email campaigns, but Brevo edges multichannel marketing.

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop builder is intuitive with 100+ responsive templates. High customizability suits designers.

Brevo offers a similar block-based editor with 40+ templates, plus dynamic content blocks for personalization. Both ensure mobile optimization.

Practical advice: Test A/B subject lines in both—Mailchimp auto-optimizes sends; Brevo requires manual setup.

### Automations and Workflows
Mailchimp's automation builder is visual for welcome series, abandoned carts (with e-commerce integrations). Classic Automations handle behavioral triggers.

Brevo shines with transactional emails, journey builder for complex sequences (e.g., lead nurturing across email/SMS). Free plan includes basic automations.

Practical tip: For e-commerce, integrate Mailchimp with Shopify for cart recovery; Brevo pairs well with WooCommerce via native SMS.

### Additional Tools
- **Landing Pages & Forms:** Mailchimp includes basic pages/forms on paid plans. Brevo offers unlimited pages/forms on all plans, plus pop-ups.
- **SMS & Chat:** Brevo bundles SMS (pay-per-use) and live chat; Mailchimp adds SMS on Premium ($350+).
- **Analytics:** Both track opens/clicks. Mailchimp's heatmaps are superior; Brevo's revenue tracking integrates with Google Analytics.

**Feature Winner:** Brevo for multichannel; Mailchimp for polished email-only.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface feels familiar, like a content editor, with quick-start wizards. Great for non-tech users.

Brevo's dashboard is clean but denser with tabs for email, SMS, CRM. Steeper learning for multichannel but simpler for transactional sends.

Practical advice: Start with Mailchimp if solo; Brevo if managing sales pipelines. Both have 10-15 min setup for first campaign.

## Integrations and CRM

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Workspace, Zapier, Facebook). Strong for content creators.

Brevo's 300+ integrations include HubSpot, WordPress, Stripe. Built-in CRM tracks leads without extra cost.

Practical tip: Use Zapier for custom flows—e.g., Brevo SMS on form submits; Mailchimp tags from Google Sheets.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Both maintain 95%+ rates. Mailchimp's legacy boosts reputation; Brevo improved post-rebrand (ISP compliance).

Practical advice: Warm up domains gradually. Authenticate SPF/DKIM in both setups. Brevo's dedicated IPs on Enterprise aid high-volume.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/chat on paid plans; priority for Pro.

Brevo: 24/7 chat/email on all plans, phone for Business+.

**Support Winner:** Brevo—faster responses reported in user forums.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros:
- Affordable scaling
- Multichannel (SMS, chat)
- Unlimited contacts

Cons:
- Fewer templates
- Automation less visual

### Mailchimp Pros:
- User-friendly builder
- Excellent analytics
- Vast template library

Cons:
- Expensive for growth
- Feature-locked free plan

## Who Should Choose What?

- **Pick Brevo if:** Growing list (2k+), need SMS/CRM, budget-conscious. E.g., e-commerce stores sending transactional emails.
- **Pick Mailchimp if:** Small teams, design-focused newsletters, under 5k contacts. E.g., bloggers/podcasters.

Practical advice: Export lists easily from both. Trial both free plans for 14 days to test deliverability on your domain.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to scale and channels. Brevo offers better value for businesses expanding beyond email, with flexible pricing and extras like SMS. Mailchimp remains king for simplicity and creative campaigns but can drain budgets as you grow.

For most users choosing platforms in 2024, **Brevo edges out** due to cost-efficiency and features. Audit your needs: list size, send frequency, integrations. Start with Brevo's free plan if multichannel appeals, or Mailchimp for pure email polish.
