# Top Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Email Marketing Platforms for Growing Businesses

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its pricing structure, limited automation in lower tiers, and recent changes have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're dealing with escalating costs, needing advanced automation, or better ecommerce integrations, it's time to explore options. This roundup covers the top alternatives, focusing on real features, pricing, and practical use cases for businesses choosing email platforms.

We'll compare platforms based on ease of use, automation capabilities, deliverability, pricing, and scalability. All recommendations draw from official sites and user-reported experiences—no hype, just facts to help you decide.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month, with paid plans starting at $13/month but jumping to $75+ for 5,000 contacts. Advanced features like behavioral automation require higher tiers. Common pain points include:

- **High costs for scaling**: Essentials plan lacks robust automation.
- **Deliverability dips**: Recent reports show average rates around 85-90%.
- **Clunky editor**: Drag-and-drop is basic compared to rivals.

Switching makes sense if you need unlimited sends, better segmentation, or SMS/multichannel support. Always export your list via CSV before migrating—most platforms offer import tools.

## Top Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for**: SMBs wanting transactional emails + marketing.

Brevo stands out with **unlimited contacts** on paid plans and free tier up to 300 emails/day. Pricing: Free forever; Starter $25/month (20k emails); Business $65/month.

**Key Features**:
- Drag-and-drop editor with 45+ templates.
- Automation workflows for abandoned carts, welcome series.
- SMS, WhatsApp, chat integrations.
- API for transactional emails (high deliverability ~98%).

**Pros**: Affordable scaling, excellent for ecommerce. **Cons**: Automation builder less visual than Mailchimp.

**Practical Tip**: Use Brevo's site tracking pixel for behavioral triggers. Migrate by mapping custom fields—takes ~1 hour for 10k contacts.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

**Best for**: Advanced automation and CRM integration.

ActiveCampaign excels in **machine learning automation**, ideal if Mailchimp's conditional logic feels limited. Pricing: Lite $29/month (500 contacts); Plus $49; Pro $149.

**Key Features**:
- 750+ integrations (Shopify, Salesforce).
- Predictive sending and split testing.
- Site messaging and deep CRM.

**Pros**: Powerful for lead nurturing. **Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

**Practical Tip**: Start with their 14-day trial. Build a 'lead scoring' automation: score opens/clicks to prioritize hot leads, boosting ROI by 20-30% per user reports.

### 3. Klaviyo

**Best for**: Ecommerce stores (Shopify users).

Klaviyo is **ecommerce-first**, with revenue tracking that ties emails to sales. Free up to 250 contacts/500 sends; paid from $20/month based on contacts.

**Key Features**:
- Flow builder for post-purchase sequences.
- A/B testing on subject lines.
- Built-in analytics showing revenue per campaign.

**Pros**: 99% deliverability for Shopify. **Cons**: Less ideal for non-ecom.

**Practical Tip**: Install the Klaviyo pixel on your store. Set up 'won't buy' suppression lists to avoid burning leads—users see 15-25% uplift in revenue.

### 4. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

**Best for**: Creators, bloggers, course sellers.

ConvertKit focuses on **creator tools** like visual automations and subscriber tagging. Free up to 300 subs; Creator $29/month (1k subs).

**Key Features**n- Commerce for digital products.
- Broadcasts and sequences.
- Forms with subscriber value scoring.

**Pros**: Simple, no contracts. **Cons**: Basic reporting.

**Practical Tip**: Use 'subscriber segments' for personalized sends. Tag based on page visits via integrations—great for nurturing free-to-paid conversions.

### 5. MailerLite

**Best for**: Budget-conscious startups.

Unlimited sends on all plans, free up to 1,000 subs/12k emails/month. Growing Business $10/month (500 subs).

**Key Features**:
- Popups, landing pages included.
- Basic automation and A/B tests.
- High deliverability (95%+).

**Pros**: Cheapest scaling. **Cons**: Fewer advanced automations.

**Practical Tip**: Leverage free websites builder for lead magnets. Segment by engagement to re-engage dormant lists—simple and effective.

### 6. GetResponse

**Best for**: Webinars and sales funnels.

Pricing: Email Marketing $19/month (1k contacts); Marketing Automation $59.

**Key Features**:
- Webinar hosting up to 1,000 attendees.
- Funnel builder.
- Autoresponders with conditional logic.

**Pros**: All-in-one. **Cons**: Interface feels dated.

**Practical Tip**: Host a webinar sequence: invite > reminder > replay. Track conversions directly—ideal for course launches.

### 7. Constant Contact

**Best for**: Non-profits and local businesses.

Starter $12/month (500 contacts); Premium $80.

**Key Features**:
- Event management.
- Social media tools.
- AI-powered recommendations.

**Pros**: Beginner-friendly. **Cons**: Expensive for large lists.

**Practical Tip**: Use 'contact management' for AI cleaning. Schedule social posts alongside emails for multi-channel reach.

### 8. AWeber

**Best for**: Long-term reliability.

Free up to 500 subs; Lite $19/month.

**Key Features**:
- Tag-based automation.
- Landing pages.
- Strong support.

**Pros**: Proven deliverability. **Cons**: Editor basic.

**Practical Tip**: Set up 'smart send times' based on subscriber timezones—increases opens by 10-20%.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: Ecom? Klaviyo/Brevo. Automation? ActiveCampaign.
2. **Budget Check**: Calculate based on list size—e.g., 5k contacts: Brevo ~$25 vs Mailchimp $75.
3. **Test Deliverability**: Send test campaigns to Gmail/Yahoo.
4. **Migration Plan**: Export Mailchimp list > Import > Remap tags > Test automations.
5. **Trial Everything**: Most offer 14-30 days free.

**Pro Tip**: Prioritize ESPs with Zapier integrations for seamless workflows. Monitor metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and click-through (2-5%).
