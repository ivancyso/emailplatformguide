# Email Deliverability Best Practices: Ultimate Guide for Choosing Email Marketing Platforms

In the competitive world of email marketing, getting your messages into subscribers' inboxes is paramount. Poor email deliverability can tank your campaigns, leading to lost revenue and frustrated teams. For those evaluating email marketing platforms, understanding email deliverability best practices is crucial. This guide breaks down actionable strategies, platform features to prioritize, and real-world tips to boost your inbox placement rates.

Whether you're a small business owner or a marketing manager scaling operations, these best practices will help you choose and optimize platforms like Mailchimp, Klaviyo, ActiveCampaign, or Sendinblue effectively.

## What Is Email Deliverability and Why Does It Matter?

Email deliverability refers to the ability of your emails to reach the recipient's inbox rather than landing in spam or being blocked entirely. It's influenced by factors like sender reputation, content quality, list hygiene, and technical setup.

### The Impact on Your Business
When choosing an email marketing platform, consider that high deliverability directly correlates with open rates (typically 20-30% for well-deliverable campaigns) and click-through rates. Platforms with strong deliverability tools can improve ROI by ensuring more subscribers engage with your content. Low deliverability wastes ad spend on list building and erodes trust with ISPs like Gmail, Yahoo, and Outlook.

For platform shoppers, look for transparency in deliverability metrics—reputable ones share average rates above 95%.

## Core Email Deliverability Best Practices

Implementing these practices isn't optional; they're foundational for any platform.

### 1. Set Up Email Authentication Protocols
Email authentication is non-negotiable. Without it, ISPs flag you as suspicious.

- **SPF (Sender Policy Framework)**: Authorizes your sending domain to prevent spoofing. Verify your platform supports easy SPF record setup.
- **DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)**: Adds a digital signature to emails. Platforms like ActiveCampaign automate DKIM signing.
- **DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance)**: Builds on SPF/DKIM with reporting. Choose platforms with DMARC monitoring dashboards.

**Practical Tip**: During platform trials, test authentication setup time. Klaviyo excels here with one-click domain verification.

### 2. Maintain List Hygiene Religiously
Dirty lists kill deliverability. Remove inactive subscribers to keep engagement high.

- Suppress hard bounces, complaints, and unopens after 90-180 days.
- Use double opt-in to ensure genuine subscribers.
- Segment lists by engagement levels.

**Platform Advice**: Opt for tools with automated suppression lists and re-engagement campaigns. Mailchimp's list cleaning feature is user-friendly for beginners.

### 3. Monitor and Improve Sender Reputation
Your IP and domain reputation determine inbox access.

- Warm up new IPs gradually (start with 50-100 emails/day).
- Avoid purchased lists—they're reputation killers.
- Track blacklists via tools like MX Toolbox.

**Choosing Platforms**: Shared IP pools (e.g., Mailchimp) suit low-volume senders; dedicated IPs (e.g., SendGrid integration in platforms) for high-volume with better control.

### 4. Craft Spam-Compliant Content
Content triggers spam filters.

- Subject lines: Keep under 60 chars, avoid ALL CAPS, spam words like "free money."
- Body: Balance text-to-image ratio (60/40), personalize with merge tags.
- Avoid excessive links or attachments.

**Practical Advice**: Use platform preview tools to simulate inbox rendering. Test with Litmus or Email on Acid integrations available in advanced platforms like Klaviyo.

### 5. Prioritize Engagement and Timing
ISPs reward engaged lists.

- Send to active subscribers first.
- Optimal send times: Tuesdays-Thursdays, 8-10 AM recipient time.
- Encourage opens/clicks with value-driven content.

**Platform Feature Spotlight**: Automation sequences in ActiveCampaign nurture engagement automatically.

- **Seed Testing**: Send to test inboxes across providers.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid When Switching Platforms

- Migrating without cleaning lists first.
- Ignoring legacy authentication on old domains.
- Overlooking mobile optimization (50%+ opens on mobile).
- Neglecting post-send monitoring.

Always export/import with subscriber consent history.
