# Omnisend Review 2023: Is It the Right Email Marketing Platform for Your Ecommerce Store?

If you're in the market for an email marketing tool tailored to ecommerce, you've likely come across Omnisend. This Omnisend review dives deep into its capabilities, helping you decide if it's the best fit for growing your online store. With a focus on omnichannel marketing—combining email, SMS, and web push notifications—Omnisend stands out for Shopify and WooCommerce users. We'll cover features, pricing, real-world use cases, and practical setup tips based on its documented offerings.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an ecommerce-focused marketing automation platform launched in 2014. It's designed for online retailers who want to engage customers across multiple channels without needing advanced technical skills. Unlike general-purpose tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes ecommerce-specific automations, such as abandoned cart recovery, welcome series, and post-purchase flows.

Key differentiators include its drag-and-drop editor, pre-built workflows, and seamless integrations with platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce. As of 2023, it serves over 70,000 brands, processing millions of messages daily. It's SOC 2 certified for data security, which is crucial for handling customer data.

**Practical Advice:** Start by signing up for the free plan (up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month). Connect your store in under 5 minutes via native integrations—no coding required.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs a punch with features that streamline ecommerce marketing. Here's a breakdown:

### Email and Campaign Builder

The drag-and-drop editor lets you create responsive emails without HTML knowledge. It includes ecommerce blocks for product recommendations, order summaries, and dynamic content pulled from your store.

**Practical Tip:** Use the Product Picker to insert live product data. For example, in a win-back campaign, show recently viewed items to boost relevance and open rates by up to 20-30% (based on Omnisend's case studies).

### Automation Workflows

Pre-built templates cover 80% of common scenarios: cart abandonment (sends after 1 hour, then 24 hours), browse abandonment, welcome series, and reorders. Custom workflows use triggers like purchases or page visits.

**Practical Advice:** Set up a cart abandonment flow first. Segment by cart value—send high-value carts ($100+) a discount code via SMS for higher recovery rates (Omnisend reports 40% recovery on average).

### SMS and MMS Marketing

Integrate SMS for time-sensitive messages like shipping updates or flash sales. U.S. and Canada numbers are supported, with compliance tools for TCPA.

**Tip:** Combine email + SMS in omnichannel campaigns. Test A/B splits: Email open rates hover at 20-30%, while SMS clicks can hit 19%.

### List Segmentation and Analytics

Segment by behavior (purchased, viewed), demographics, or custom fields. The dashboard shows revenue per email, ROI, and unsubscribe trends.

**Practical Advice:** Build segments like 'VIP customers' (3+ purchases). Target them with exclusive offers to increase lifetime value. Use the reporting to track attributed revenue—link opens/clicks to sales.

### Integrations and Apps

Over 150 integrations, including Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, Klaviyo alternatives, and Zapier. Shopify app has 4.9/5 stars from 2,000+ reviews.

**Tip:** Sync with Google Analytics for UTM tracking. Install the Omnisend popup app for list building—offers spin-to-win or exit-intent popups with 5-10% conversion rates.

## Omnisend Pricing: Transparent and Scalable

Omnisend uses a contact-based model:

- **Free Plan:** $0/month, up to 250 contacts, 500 emails, unlimited SMS (pay-per-use).
- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts), unlimited emails, basic automations.
- **Pro:** $59/month (500 contacts), advanced segmentation, A/B testing, dedicated support.

SMS costs ~$0.02/message in the U.S. No contracts; scales with contacts (e.g., $120/month for 10k contacts on Pro).

**Practical Advice:** Calculate costs using their pricing calculator. For a store with 1,000 contacts sending weekly newsletters + automations, expect $30-50/month on Standard. Upgrade to Pro if you need API access for custom data syncs.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-centric: Pulls real-time data from your store.
- Omnichannel: Email + SMS + push in one platform.
- User-friendly: 95% of users report easy setup (per G2 reviews).
- Strong ROI: Customers see 2.4x revenue lift on average.

### Cons

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Perfect for small-to-medium ecommerce stores (DTC brands, Shopify merchants) with 500-50,000 contacts. If you sell physical/digital products and want quick wins on recovery flows, it's ideal. Avoid if you're in non-ecommerce (e.g., SaaS) or need enterprise-level deliverability guarantees.

**Case Study Example:** A fashion brand using Omnisend recovered $45k in abandoned carts in one month via automated emails + SMS (from Omnisend blog).

**Practical Advice for Onboarding:** 
1. Import contacts via CSV or integration.
2. Build your first workflow (use templates).
3. A/B test subject lines (e.g., 'Don't Miss Out' vs. 'Your Cart Awaits').
4. Monitor spam rates—stay under 0.1% with clean lists.
5. Comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM using built-in tools.
