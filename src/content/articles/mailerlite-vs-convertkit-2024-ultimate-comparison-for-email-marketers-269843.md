# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. MailerLite and ConvertKit are two popular platforms, especially for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small businesses. MailerLite offers affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit targets creators with advanced automation. This in-depth comparison covers pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide.

## Pricing: Affordability vs Value

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms scale with subscribers, but their structures differ.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite starts with a generous free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Paid plans begin at $10/month for 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20 for 1,000, and higher tiers like Advanced at $25/month with extras like A/B testing and web push notifications. No contracts, cancel anytime. It's ideal for bootstrapped users testing waters.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (rebranded as Kit but still widely known as ConvertKit) has no free plan beyond a 14-day trial. Creator plan starts at $29/month for up to 300 subscribers, jumping to $49 for 1,000. The Creator Pro plan at $49/month adds advanced reporting and migrations. Prices rise steeply: $79 for 3,000 subscribers. It's pricier but includes unlimited emails.

**Practical Tip:** Calculate your monthly cost based on list size. For under 1,000 subscribers, MailerLite saves hundreds yearly. Use their pricing calculators for projections.

| Subscribers | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|-------------|------------|------------|
| 0-1,000    | Free/$10+ | $29+      |
| 1,001-3,000| $20-$40   | $49-$79   |

## Feature Comparison

Both excel in core email marketing, but nuances matter.

### Email Editor and Templates

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive with 80+ responsive templates. Classic and blocks editors suit beginners. ConvertKit's visual editor uses blocks too, with creator-focused templates for newsletters and products. Both ensure mobile optimization.

**Advice:** Preview emails on devices. MailerLite edges for sheer template variety.

### Automation and Workflows

ConvertKit shines with visual automation builder for sequences, tags-based triggers (e.g., if subscribed to X, send Y). Supports broadcasts and evergreen sequences. MailerLite offers automation with triggers like birthdays or abandons, plus RSS campaigns. Lacks ConvertKit's tag depth but simpler for basics.

**Pro Tip:** Start with simple welcome series. ConvertKit suits complex funnels; MailerLite for quick setups.

### Forms, Landing Pages, and Popups

Both build opt-ins. MailerLite includes popups, fixed bars, embed forms, and unlimited landing pages (paid plans). ConvertKit has forms, ephemerals (inline), and landing pages with commerce integration.

MailerLite wins on pop-up variety for list growth.

### Segmentation and Tagging

ConvertKit's tag system is powerful for behavioral segmentation (e.g., opened email X). Groups allow list splits. MailerLite uses segments based on activity, custom fields, but tags are basic.

**Advice:** If personalizing by actions, ConvertKit; for simple lists, MailerLite.

### Integrations and E-commerce

Both integrate with Stripe, PayPal, Zapier (1,000+ apps). ConvertKit natively supports Gumroad, Teachable for creators. MailerLite connects to WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics.

ConvertKit better for digital products.

### Analytics and Reporting

ConvertKit provides opens, clicks, revenue tracking per subscriber. MailerLite offers similar plus heatmap, device stats. Both track unsubscribes, spam rates.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

MailerLite's interface is clean, beginner-friendly with quick-start guides, video tutorials. Setup under 10 minutes. ConvertKit's dashboard focuses on creators, with commerce tabs, but steeper for non-creators.

**Practical Advice:** Watch onboarding videos. MailerLite for speed; ConvertKit for depth after trial.

User ratings: MailerLite 4.7/5 on Capterra; ConvertKit 4.6/5.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat (paid), email support, knowledge base. High deliverability (99%+ claimed). ConvertKit: Email support, chat for Pro, extensive help center. Strong inbox placement via authentication guides.

**Tip:** Always verify domain, warm up IP. Both recommend best practices.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Beginners/small lists (<5k subs)
- Budget-conscious bloggers
- Need quick popups/forms

Practical example: A new blogger grows to 2,000 subs on free/paid MailerLite, sending weekly newsletters with RSS automation.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators/podcasters
- Selling digital products/courses
- Complex subscriber journeys

Example: A course creator uses tags for "interested in ebook" segments, automating upsells.

**Migration Tip:** Both offer CSV imports. Test with small lists first.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit? MailerLite wins for affordability and simplicity, perfect for startups testing email marketing. ConvertKit excels for serious creators needing segmentation and sales tools, justifying the premium.

Assess your list size, budget, and features. Start MailerLite's free plan or ConvertKit trial. Track opens/revenue post-switch. The best choice aligns with your goals—affordable growth or creator power.
