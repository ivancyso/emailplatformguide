# Omnisend Review 2023: Features, Pricing, Pros & Cons for Ecommerce Email Marketing

If you're an ecommerce store owner or marketer searching for a reliable email marketing platform, Omnisend likely popped up in your searches. With its focus on ecommerce, Omnisend stands out by combining email, SMS, and push notifications into one dashboard. But is it the right choice for your business? In this detailed Omnisend review, we'll break down its features, pricing, usability, and more to help you decide.

We'll cover real-world practical advice on setup, automation best practices, and integration tips. Whether you're migrating from Mailchimp or starting fresh, this guide provides actionable insights based on Omnisend's actual capabilities as of 2023.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an all-in-one marketing automation platform designed specifically for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it powers over 70,000 stores worldwide, with strong integrations for Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, and Magento. Unlike generalist tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes omnichannel campaigns—email, SMS, web push notifications—to drive revenue.

**Practical Advice:** Start by connecting your store via one-click integrations. For Shopify users, this syncs customer data, orders, and products automatically, saving hours of manual import work. Test the free plan (up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month) to import your list and send a test campaign before committing.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend shines in automation and segmentation, making it ideal for scaling ecommerce marketing.

### Automation Workflows

Omnisend's pre-built workflows cover cart abandonment, welcome series, post-purchase upsells, and re-engagement. You can drag-and-drop to customize triggers like 'added to cart but didn't purchase' or 'browsed category X'.

**Practical Advice:** For cart recovery, enable SMS alongside email—data shows SMS boosts recovery rates by 20-30%. Set up a workflow like: Email at 1 hour → SMS at 24 hours → Win-back discount at 72 hours. Use dynamic product blocks to show abandoned items automatically. Always A/B test subject lines; Omnisend's tool makes this seamless.

### Email and SMS Builder

The drag-and-drop editor supports responsive templates with ecommerce-specific blocks (product carousels, order summaries). SMS editor is straightforward for short, compliant messages.

**Practical Advice:** Use the 'Product' block for personalized recommendations—pull from recent purchases or views. For SMS, keep under 160 characters and include opt-out links to comply with TCPA regulations. Segment by purchase history (e.g., 'VIP customers >$500 LTV') for higher open rates (up to 40% vs. 20% average).

### List Building Tools

Popups, signup forms, and landing pages convert visitors into subscribers. API wheels and spin-to-win gamify signups.

**Practical Advice:** Place exit-intent popups on high-traffic pages like product listings. Offer 10-15% discounts for emails, but tier SMS incentives (rarer for higher value). Track conversion rates in the dashboard—aim for 3-5% popup signup rate initially, optimize by testing copy like 'Get 10% off your first order!'.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Hyper-targeted segments based on behavior, location, or custom events. Detailed reports on revenue per recipient, ROI, and attribution.

**Practical Advice:** Create segments like 'inactive 90 days + past buyer' for win-back campaigns. Use the Revenue Tracker to measure true impact—link campaigns to sales. Export data to Google Analytics for deeper funnel analysis.

### Omnichannel Support

Web push notifications reach 10x more users than email alone. SMS open rates average 98%.

**Practical Advice:** After opt-in, send push for flash sales. Combine channels in workflows: Email → Push → SMS for multi-touch nurturing.

## Omnisend Pricing Plans

Omnisend uses a contact-based model, pay-as-you-grow:

- **Free:** Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month. Limited automations.
- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts), unlimited emails/automations, SMS add-on from $0.016/message.
- **Pro:** $59/month (500 contacts), advanced reporting, A/B testing, dedicated support.
- **Custom:** Enterprise features for high-volume.

No contracts; monthly billing. SMS/web push billed separately.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate costs: For 1,000 contacts + 10k emails + 1k SMS (~$16 base + $16 SMS). Scale gradually—most start on Standard. Watch for overages if lists grow fast; proactive segmentation prevents bloat.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-focused with deep store integrations.
- Powerful automations save time and boost revenue (users report 2-3x ROI).
- User-friendly interface, quick setup (under 30 mins).
- Excellent support via chat/email.
- Compliant with GDPR/CAN-SPAM.

### Cons
- SMS costs add up for high-volume senders.
- Free plan limits scalability.
- No native social media posting.
- Advanced features (like custom events) require Pro plan.

**Practical Advice:** If SMS is core, budget accordingly. For social, integrate with Buffer or Hootsuite.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Perfect for:
- Shopify/WooCommerce stores with 500+ contacts.
- Brands prioritizing automation over basic newsletters.
- Multi-channel marketers (email + SMS + push).

Not ideal for non-ecommerce (better: ActiveCampaign) or tiny lists (MailerLite free tier).

**Practical Advice:** Audit your needs: If 70%+ revenue from online sales, Omnisend excels. Migrate lists via CSV; use their checklist for zero downtime.

## Omnisend vs. Competitors

- **Vs. Klaviyo:** Similar ecommerce focus, but Omnisend cheaper for SMS, easier UI.
- **Vs. Mailchimp:** More automation depth, better ecommerce blocks (Mailchimp feels dated).
- **Vs. ActiveCampaign:** Omnisend wins on omnichannel; AC better for complex CRM.

**Practical Advice:** Trial both Omnisend and Klaviyo—export a workflow to compare setup time.
