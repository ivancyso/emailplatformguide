# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Ultimate 2024 Comparison for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's customer engagement and revenue. In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down **Omnisend vs Mailchimp** based on real features, pricing, and user experiences. Whether you're running an online store or a service-based business, this guide helps you decide which tool fits your needs.

We'll cover overviews, key features, pricing, ease of use, integrations, support, and more. By the end, you'll have practical advice to make an informed choice.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing since 2001. It's known for its user-friendly interface and all-in-one marketing tools. With a generous free plan, it's ideal for beginners and small businesses starting out.

Key strengths include drag-and-drop email builders, audience segmentation, and basic automation. It supports email campaigns, landing pages, and social media ads. However, as lists grow, costs can rise quickly due to its pricing model based on contacts.

Practical tip: If you're under 500 subscribers, Mailchimp's free tier lets you test campaigns without commitment. Use it for newsletters or simple promotions.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend, launched in 2014, specializes in ecommerce marketing. It's designed for platforms like Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce, offering omnichannel campaigns across email, SMS, and web push notifications.

It excels in pre-built workflows for abandoned carts, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells. Omnisend's focus on automation saves time for store owners, with hyper-targeted segmentation based on purchase behavior.

Practical tip: Integrate Omnisend with your ecommerce store to recover lost sales automatically—many users report 20-30% recovery rates on abandoned carts without custom coding.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major factor when pitting **Omnisend vs Mailchimp**.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic features.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/mo (5,000 contacts), adds automation.
- **Standard**: $20/mo (5,000 contacts), behavioral targeting.
- **Premium**: $350/mo (10,000+ contacts), advanced reporting.

Mailchimp charges per contact, so scaling lists increases costs exponentially. No SMS in lower plans.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, core automations.
- **Standard**: $16/mo (500 contacts), unlimited emails, SMS add-on.
- **Pro**: $59/mo (500 contacts), advanced segmentation, A/B testing.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing.

Omnisend offers unlimited emails in paid plans, making it cheaper for high-volume senders. SMS starts at $0.02/message.

**Verdict**: Mailchimp wins for tiny free lists; Omnisend for growing ecommerce with frequent sends. Calculate your costs using their pricing calculators.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp has 100+ templates; Omnisend focuses on ecommerce-optimized ones with dynamic product blocks.

Practical advice: Use Omnisend's product feed integration to auto-populate emails with real inventory—saves hours vs manual updates in Mailchimp.

### Automation Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Single, customer journey, and classic automations. Good for basics like welcome emails.
- **Omnisend**: 200+ pre-built workflows, omnichannel (email + SMS + push). Excels in cart recovery and win-back campaigns.

Tip: Test Omnisend's abandoned cart flow first if you have an online store—set it up in minutes with triggers like 'cart viewed 1+ hours ago'.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp uses tags and groups; Omnisend leverages purchase data for behavioral segments (e.g., 'VIP buyers'). Both support merge tags for personalization.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp provides revenue tracking in higher plans; Omnisend shines with ecommerce ROI metrics like attributed revenue per campaign.

## Ease of Use: Which is More Beginner-Friendly?

Mailchimp's interface is intuitive for non-marketers, with guided setups. Omnisend has a slight learning curve for omnichannel but ecommerce users adapt quickly due to store-specific dashboards.

Practical advice: Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing. Switch to Omnisend once you need advanced automations—its popup builder converts visitors 2-3x better than basic forms.

## Integrations and Apps

- **Mailchimp**: 300+ integrations (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Strong for general marketing stacks.
- **Omnisend**: Native ecommerce apps (Shopify, Magento), plus Klaviyo-like integrations. Supports Google Tag Manager for tracking.

Tip: For Shopify users, Omnisend's one-click install syncs orders/customers automatically. Mailchimp requires more setup for deep ecommerce data.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp offers email support (priority in paid plans), chat, and resources. Omnisend provides 24/7 live chat, dedicated managers in Pro plans, and migration help.

User reviews praise Omnisend's responsive team for ecommerce troubleshooting. Mailchimp's support scales with plan tier.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp** if: You're a blogger, service business, or small team needing simple emails/newsletters. Best for under 2,000 contacts.
- **Choose Omnisend** if: You run an ecommerce store (Shopify/Woo), need cart recovery, or want SMS/push. Scales better for sales-focused campaigns.

Practical decision framework:
1. List size <500? Mailchimp free.
2. Ecommerce? Omnisend.
3. Budget for high volume? Omnisend.
4. General marketing? Mailchimp.

## Conclusion

In the **Omnisend vs Mailchimp** showdown, there's no one-size-fits-all winner—it depends on your business. Mailchimp remains king for beginners and broad marketing, while Omnisend dominates ecommerce with powerful automations and omnichannel reach.

Test both free plans: Send a campaign, set up an automation, and check integrations. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and revenue per email.

For most online stores, Omnisend edges out due to ROI-focused features. Whichever you pick, consistent testing and segmentation will boost results. Ready to choose? Sign up today and start scaling your email marketing.
