# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams. Both platforms excel in user-friendly interfaces and powerful features, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, automation, templates, segmentation, analytics, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're starting with a small subscriber list or scaling up, understanding MailerLite vs ConvertKit is key. We'll provide practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities as of 2023.

## Quick Overview: MailerLite

MailerLite is a budget-friendly email marketing platform loved by small businesses and beginners. Launched in 2010, it emphasizes simplicity and affordability.

**Key Features:**
- Drag-and-drop email builder
- Automation workflows
- Popups, forms, and landing pages
- A/B testing
- Basic e-commerce integrations

**Pricing:** Free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Paid plans start at $10/month for 500 contacts (billed annually), scaling to $20/month for 2,500 contacts. No contracts, easy cancellation.

MailerLite shines for cost-conscious users who need solid basics without complexity.

## Quick Overview: ConvertKit

ConvertKit, formerly known as ConvertKit, targets creators like bloggers, podcasters, and online course sellers. Founded in 2013, it focuses on advanced automation and subscriber relationships.

**Key Features:**
- Visual automation builder
- Tag-based segmentation
- Optimized landing pages and forms
- Commerce tools (sell digital products)
- Detailed subscriber insights

**Pricing:** Free plan up to 1,000 subscribers (limited features). Paid Creator plan starts at $29/month for up to 1,000 subscribers, jumping to $79/month for 3,000. Annual discounts available.

ConvertKit is ideal for those prioritizing long-term subscriber engagement over low costs.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is a major battleground in MailerLite vs ConvertKit.

| Subscribers | MailerLite (Monthly) | ConvertKit (Monthly) |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Up to 1,000 | Free                | Free (basic) / $29  |
| Up to 2,500 | $20                 | $50                 |
| Up to 5,000 | $30                 | $79                 |

**Practical Advice:** If your list is under 1,000, both free plans work, but MailerLite sends more emails (12k vs ConvertKit's limits). For growth, MailerLite stays cheaper—saving $50+/month early on. Test free tiers first: import a sample list and send a campaign to compare deliverability.

ConvertKit justifies higher costs with creator-specific tools like one-click upsells.

## Ease of Use and User Interface

Both platforms prioritize intuitive designs, but differences emerge.

### MailerLite's Interface
Clean, minimalist dashboard. Drag-and-drop editor feels like Canva—perfect for non-designers. Onboarding includes video tutorials.

**Tip:** Use the template library (100+ responsive designs) to launch campaigns in under 30 minutes.

### ConvertKit's Interface
Sleek, creator-focused with a focus on sequences. Automation builder is visual (like Zapier), but steeper learning curve for tags.

**Tip:** Spend time on the 'Sequences' tab—set up welcome series to boost open rates by 20-30%.

**Winner:** MailerLite for beginners; ConvertKit for power users.

## Features Breakdown

### Email Templates and Design
MailerLite: 80+ free templates, HTML editor, image optimization. Mobile-first.

ConvertKit: Fewer templates (50+), but highly customizable with Commerce blocks for product embeds.

**Advice:** Preview on mobile always—80% of emails are opened there. MailerLite edges for variety.

### Automation and Workflows

#### MailerLite Automations
Trigger-based: welcome emails, abandoned carts. Simple if/then logic.

**Pro Tip:** Build a 7-day nurture sequence: Day 1 welcome, Day 3 value tip, Day 7 offer. Track with built-in analytics.

#### ConvertKit Automations
Advanced tagging: 'Subscribed to podcast' → send episode links. Re-engagement campaigns auto-tag inactive users.

**Pro Tip:** Use 'Actions' for dynamic content—personalize based on past opens, increasing clicks by 15%.

ConvertKit wins for complexity.

### Segmentation and Tagging
MailerLite: Groups, custom fields, behaviors.

ConvertKit: Tag everything (e.g., 'lead-magnet-downloader'). More flexible for creators.

**Advice:** Segment by engagement: top 20% openers get exclusive content. Start simple in MailerLite, scale to ConvertKit tags.

### Landing Pages and Forms
Both offer unlimited pages/forms.

MailerLite: Popups, embedded forms, 20+ templates.

ConvertKit: High-converting pages with countdown timers, optimized for lead magnets.

**Tip:** A/B test headlines—'Free Guide' vs 'Ultimate Guide' can double conversions.

### Analytics and Reporting
MailerLite: Opens, clicks, bounces, revenue tracking.

ConvertKit: Subscriber growth, tag reports, revenue per subscriber.

**Advice:** Focus on 'revenue per email' metric. Integrate Google Analytics for deeper insights.

### Integrations and Deliverability
Both integrate with Stripe, Zapier, WordPress.

Deliverability: Both ~95%+ (industry standard). MailerLite slightly better for cold lists per user reports.

**Tip:** Warm up domains gradually—start with 50 emails/day.

## Customer Support

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Fast responses (under 1 hour).

ConvertKit: Email support (business hours), Creator Academy videos, community forum.

**Winner:** MailerLite for immediacy.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs ConvertKit?

**Pick MailerLite if:**
- Budget < $20/month
- Small business/blogger needing basics
- Quick setup priority

**Practical Steps:** Sign up free, import CSV, send test broadcast. Migrate from others via built-in importer.

**Pick ConvertKit if:**
- Creator selling courses/podcasts
- Need tag-based journeys
- Willing to invest in growth

**Practical Steps:** Use 14-day trial, map automations from current tool. Export tags carefully.

**Migration Tip:** Both support CSV imports. Clean your list first (remove inactives) to avoid bounces.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to needs: affordability and simplicity (MailerLite) or creator power (ConvertKit). For most starting out, MailerLite's value wins—scale to ConvertKit later.
