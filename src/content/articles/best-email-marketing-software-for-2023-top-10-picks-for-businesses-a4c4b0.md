# Best Email Marketing Software for 2023: Top 10 Picks for Businesses

Email marketing remains one of the most effective digital strategies, with average ROI around $36 for every $1 spent, according to industry benchmarks from Litmus and DMA reports. For businesses choosing email marketing platforms, the right software can streamline campaigns, boost open rates, and drive conversions. This roundup focuses on the best email marketing software options, evaluated based on real user feedback from G2, Capterra, and Trustpilot, key features like automation and segmentation, pricing transparency, and scalability for small to enterprise teams.

Whether you're a solopreneur starting with a free plan or a growing e-commerce brand needing advanced analytics, we'll cover practical advice to help you select the ideal tool. No hype—just data-driven insights into top performers.

## Key Criteria for Evaluating Email Marketing Software

Before diving into the list, understand what makes software stand out. Prioritize these factors when choosing:

### 1. Ease of Use and Templates

Beginner-friendly drag-and-drop editors save time. Look for 100+ responsive templates optimized for mobile, as 46% of emails are opened on phones (Statista).

### 2. Automation and Segmentation

True automation workflows (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart) and dynamic segmentation based on behavior increase engagement by up to 760% (Experian).

### 3. Deliverability and Analytics

High inbox placement rates (above 95%) and detailed metrics like open rates, click-throughs, and revenue per email are essential.

### 4. Integrations and Pricing

Seamless connections to CRM (e.g., Salesforce), e-commerce (Shopify), and Zapier. Value pricing scales with subscribers, not contacts.

### 5. Support and Compliance

24/7 chat support and GDPR/CAN-SPAM tools prevent legal issues.

Practical tip: Start with a free trial. Test 2-3 campaigns with your audience segment to gauge deliverability.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Picks

Here's our curated list of the best email marketing software, ranked by overall value for different business sizes.

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp leads with its intuitive interface and free plan for up to 500 contacts/1,000 sends monthly. Features include automation, A/B testing, and audience insights. Pricing starts at $13/month for Essentials (up to 5,000 contacts).

**Pros:** Generous free tier, 300+ integrations, strong analytics.
**Cons:** Advanced automation locked behind Premium ($350+/month).

Ideal for: Small businesses. Users report 20-30% open rates with behavioral segmentation. Integrate with Google Analytics for full-funnel tracking.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

Best for automation pros. Machine learning predicts customer behavior; deep CRM integration. Starts at $29/month (Lite, 500 contacts).

**Pros:** Powerful if/then automations, site tracking, split testing.
**Cons:** Steeper learning curve.

Practical advice: Use "conditional content" for personalized emails, boosting clicks by 14% per their case studies.

### 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Creator-focused with visual automations and landing pages. Free up to 300 subscribers; Creator plan $29/month.

**Pros:** Tagging system for segmentation, subscriber scoring.
**Cons:** Limited reporting for enterprises.

Great for bloggers. Tip: Broadcasts with fan feedback loops improve retention.

### 4. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels. Starts at $19/month (2,000 contacts).

**Pros:** Conversion funnel builder, high deliverability (99%).
**Cons:** Interface feels dated.

Advice: Leverage abandoned cart recovery—recovers 10-15% lost sales.

### 5. Constant Contact

User-friendly for non-profits/SMBs. $12/month starter (500 contacts).

**Pros:** Event management, social ads integration.
**Cons:** No native automation depth.

Tip: AI-powered subject lines test variations automatically.

### 6. AWeber

Reliable since 1998, strong list building. $19/month (500 subscribers).

**Pros:** Unlimited emails, spam testing.
**Cons:** Basic design tools.

Advice: Use RSS-to-email for consistent newsletters.

### 7. Klaviyo

E-commerce powerhouse, Shopify native. Free up to 250 contacts; $20/month after.

**Pros:** Predictive analytics, revenue tracking.
**Cons:** Expensive at scale ($100s/month).

Practical: Flow builder for post-purchase sequences yields 30% uplift.

### 8. Sendinblue (Brevo)

Transactional + marketing emails. Free 300/day; $25/month Premium.

**Pros:** SMS/chat add-ons, EU hosting.
**Cons:** Template variety limited.

Tip: Multichannel campaigns (email + SMS) boost opens 6x.

### 9. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free CRM-integrated tool. Paid from $20/month.

**Pros:** Free forever for basics, smart content.
**Cons:** Full power needs Marketing Hub ($800+/month).

Advice: Align with sales pipelines for lead nurturing.

### 10. Drip

Advanced for WooCommerce. $39/month (500 subscribers).

**Pros:** Customer journey mapping.
**Cons:** Pricey for starters.

Tip: Multivariant testing refines campaigns iteratively.

## Practical Advice for Choosing and Implementing Email Marketing Software

1. **Audit Your Needs:** Calculate list size growth (aim 20-30% quarterly). Match to pricing tiers.

2. **Migration Tips:** Export CSV from old tools; use built-in importers. Clean lists with zero-bounce verification.

3. **Campaign Best Practices:** Segment by engagement (active/inactive). Personalize with merge tags. Send 1-2x/week; optimal time 10 AM Tuesdays (CoSchedule data).

4. **Measure Success:** Track beyond opens—focus on revenue per recipient. Use UTM tags for attribution.

5. **Scale Smart:** Start simple, upgrade for automations. Budget $10-50/month initially.

Common pitfalls: Ignoring mobile optimization or buying lists (hurts deliverability).

## Conclusion

The best email marketing software depends on your stage: Mailchimp for starters, ActiveCampaign for growth, Klaviyo for e-com. Test trials, prioritize automation and analytics.
