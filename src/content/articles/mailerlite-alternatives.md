[MailerLite](/mailerlite-review) is one of the best value email marketing platforms available — affordable, clean, and feature-rich for the price. But it is not the right fit for everyone.

If you need advanced automation, multi-channel marketing, a built-in CRM, or ecommerce-specific features, there are strong alternatives worth considering.

## Why Switch from MailerLite?

MailerLite excels at affordable email marketing, but it has limitations:

- **No SMS or multi-channel marketing.** MailerLite is email-only. If you need SMS, WhatsApp, or push notifications, you need a different platform.
- **No CRM.** MailerLite has subscriber profiles with tags, but no deal pipelines, sales automation, or account management.
- **Basic reporting.** MailerLite covers opens, clicks, and unsubscribes, but lacks cohort analysis, predictive analytics, and deep audience insights.
- **Limited ecommerce depth.** MailerLite supports basic ecommerce integrations, but it does not offer product-level segmentation, predictive CLV, or native Shopify integration depth.
- **Automation ceiling.** The automation builder handles standard workflows well, but complex multi-path automations with CRM triggers or predictive branching require a more powerful platform.

If any of these limitations affect your business, here are the best alternatives.

## The Best MailerLite Alternatives

### 1. Brevo — Best for Multi-Channel Marketing

**Why switch:** Brevo offers email, SMS, WhatsApp, and live chat in one platform — something MailerLite cannot match.

**Pros:**
- Email + SMS + WhatsApp + live chat in one tool
- Built-in CRM with deal pipelines
- Unlimited contacts on all plans (pay by email volume)
- Transactional email included (SMTP relay)
- Free plan: 300 emails/day with unlimited contacts

**Cons:**
- Email editor less polished than MailerLite's
- Template library is smaller and less modern
- No website builder

**Pricing:** Starter plan from $9/mo for 5,000 emails. See our [MailerLite vs Brevo](/mailerlite-vs-brevo) comparison for a detailed breakdown.

**Best for:** Businesses that communicate across multiple channels and want everything in one platform.

### 2. Kit (ConvertKit) — Best for Creators

**Why switch:** Kit is built for content creators with tag-based subscriber management, powerful automation, and built-in commerce for digital products.

**Pros:**
- Subscriber-centric model (no duplicate charges)
- Best-in-class visual automation builder
- Built-in commerce (sell digital products, paid newsletters)
- Creator Network for audience growth
- Free plan: 1,000 subscribers

**Cons:**
- Significantly more expensive than MailerLite ($29/mo vs $9/mo at 1,000 subs)
- Only 9 email templates (plain-text focus)
- No website builder

**Pricing:** Creator plan from $29/mo. Read our [Kit review](/convertkit-review) and [ConvertKit vs MailerLite](/convertkit-vs-mailerlite) comparison.

**Best for:** Bloggers, podcasters, course creators, and newsletter operators who monetize their audience.

### 3. ActiveCampaign — Best for Advanced Automation

**Why switch:** ActiveCampaign offers the most powerful automation builder in email marketing, plus a built-in CRM — two things MailerLite lacks.

**Pros:**
- Best-in-class automation with unlimited branching
- Built-in CRM with deal pipelines and sales automation
- Lead scoring and site tracking
- 900+ integrations
- Goal-based automation

**Cons:**
- No free plan (14-day trial only)
- Starts at $29/mo — 3x MailerLite's price
- Steeper learning curve

**Pricing:** Starter plan from $29/mo. See our [ActiveCampaign vs Mailchimp](/activecampaign-vs-mailchimp) comparison for context on how it stacks up.

**Best for:** Growing businesses with sales teams, B2B companies, and anyone who has outgrown MailerLite's automation.

### 4. Mailchimp — Best for Features and Ecosystem

**Why switch:** Mailchimp offers a broader feature set including CRM, deep analytics, extensive templates, and the widest integration ecosystem.

**Pros:**
- Built-in CRM
- 100+ templates with a polished drag-and-drop editor
- Deep analytics (audience demographics, engagement scoring, click maps)
- 300+ integrations
- Content Optimizer for email improvement suggestions

**Cons:**
- 2-3x more expensive than MailerLite at every tier
- Free plan is limited (500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month)
- Restricts affiliate marketing content

**Pricing:** Standard plan from $13/mo. See our [MailerLite vs Mailchimp](/mailerlite-vs-mailchimp) comparison for a side-by-side breakdown.

**Best for:** Businesses that need a CRM, extensive design tools, and deep analytics, and are willing to pay the premium.

### 5. Omnisend — Best for Ecommerce

**Why switch:** Omnisend is purpose-built for ecommerce with pre-built automation flows, SMS, push notifications, and native Shopify integration.

**Pros:**
- Pre-built ecommerce workflows (cart abandonment, post-purchase, win-back)
- Email + SMS + push notifications in one platform
- Native Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce integration
- Product picker and dynamic discount codes
- Free plan: 250 contacts

**Cons:**
- Not useful for non-ecommerce businesses
- Fewer general email templates
- No website builder

**Pricing:** Standard plan from $16/mo. Read our [Omnisend review](/omnisend-review) and [Omnisend vs Mailchimp](/omnisend-vs-mailchimp) comparison.

**Best for:** Online stores that want ecommerce-specific email and SMS marketing in one tool.

### 6. AWeber — Best for Templates and Support

**Why switch:** AWeber offers 700+ email templates, phone support on all plans, and affiliate-friendly policies — areas where MailerLite is weaker.

**Pros:**
- 700+ email templates (the largest library)
- Smart Designer auto-creates branded templates
- Phone support available
- Affiliate marketing friendly
- 25+ year deliverability track record

**Cons:**
- Interface feels dated compared to MailerLite
- Less intuitive automation builder
- No website builder

**Pricing:** Plus plan from $19/mo. Read our [AWeber review](/aweber-review) and [AWeber vs Mailchimp](/aweber-vs-mailchimp) comparison.

**Best for:** Small businesses that need extensive templates, phone support, or affiliate-friendly email marketing.

## How to Migrate from MailerLite

Switching from MailerLite is straightforward:

1. **Export your subscribers** from MailerLite (Subscribers > Export)
2. **Import into your new platform** (CSV upload works universally)
3. **Recreate your automations** (screenshot your MailerLite workflows first)
4. **Update embedded forms** on your website
5. **Redirect landing pages** if you used MailerLite's landing page builder
6. **Test everything** before canceling your MailerLite account

Most platforms offer free migration assistance for accounts above a certain size.

## The Bottom Line

MailerLite remains one of the best values in email marketing. If you are happy with its features and pricing, there is no reason to switch.

But if you have outgrown MailerLite or need capabilities it does not offer, the best alternative depends on your specific gap: **Brevo** for multi-channel, **Kit** for creator tools, **ActiveCampaign** for automation and CRM, **Mailchimp** for features and ecosystem, **Omnisend** for ecommerce, and **AWeber** for templates and support.
