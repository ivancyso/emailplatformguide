# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses starting or scaling their email marketing. Both are popular platforms with robust features, but they cater to slightly different needs. This in-depth Brevo vs Mailchimp comparison covers pricing, features, usability, support, integrations, and deliverability to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or e-commerce manager, we'll provide practical advice based on real user scenarios. Let's dive in.

## Quick Overview of Brevo and Mailchimp

### What is Brevo?
Brevo is an all-in-one CRM and marketing platform focused on email, SMS, chat, and transactional messaging. It's known for its generous free plan (300 emails per day, unlimited contacts) and pay-as-you-go pricing, making it ideal for startups and growing businesses. Brevo excels in combining transactional emails with marketing campaigns.

### What is Mailchimp?
Mailchimp is a veteran in email marketing, famous for its drag-and-drop editor and beginner-friendly interface. It offers a free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Mailchimp shines in creative templates, audience insights, and e-commerce integrations, popular among content creators and online stores.

## Pricing Comparison: Brevo vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a key decider in Brevo vs Mailchimp debates. Both have free tiers, but structures differ significantly.

### Brevo Pricing
- **Free Plan**: 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts, basic automation, forms, and landing pages.
- **Starter**: Starts at €25/month (20,000 emails), removes daily limit, adds removal of Brevo branding.
- **Business**: From €65/month (20,000 emails), includes advanced automation, A/B testing, SMS.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume senders.

Brevo uses a credits system for emails/SMS, with overages at low rates (e.g., €0.001 per extra email). No contact-based limits—great for list growth.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic templates, no automation.
- **Essentials**: $13/month (5,000 contacts, 50,000 sends), single-step automations.
- **Standard**: $20/month (5,000 contacts), behavioral targeting, A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350/month (10,000+ contacts), advanced segmentation, multivariate testing.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts and sends, with costs rising quickly as lists grow (e.g., 50k contacts on Premium exceeds $1,000/month).

**Practical Advice**: If you have a large, growing list with moderate sends, Brevo saves money (e.g., 100k emails/month costs ~€50 vs Mailchimp's $300+). For small lists (<5k contacts) with creative needs, Mailchimp's free tier suffices longer.

## Feature Comparison: Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive for non-designers, with 100+ mobile-responsive templates. Brevo's builder is solid but more code-friendly, with 40+ templates focused on conversions.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for beginners; Brevo for customization.

### Automation Workflows
- Brevo: Unlimited contacts in automations (even free), drip campaigns, webhooks.
- Mailchimp: Limited on lower plans; single-step on Essentials, full journeys on Standard+.

**Practical Advice**: Use Brevo for welcome series or abandoned cart flows without upgrading.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both offer tag-based segmentation. Mailchimp's 'Audience Insights' predicts behavior. Brevo integrates CRM data for dynamic content.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp provides heatmaps, open rates, revenue tracking. Brevo offers real-time stats, bounce management, goal tracking.

### Additional Features
- Brevo: SMS, WhatsApp, live chat, transactional API (high deliverability for receipts).
- Mailchimp: Social ads, postcards, advanced e-commerce (Shopify sync).

**Practical Advice**: E-commerce users pick Mailchimp for product recommendations; transactional-heavy sites (SaaS) choose Brevo.

## Ease of Use: Brevo vs Mailchimp User Experience

Mailchimp feels like a creative studio—perfect for marketers without tech skills. Onboarding wizards guide you.

Brevo has a steeper curve due to its multi-channel dashboard but rewards with power-user tools like custom JSON fields.

**Practical Advice**: Test both free plans. Spend 30 minutes building a campaign: Mailchimp for speed, Brevo for depth.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **Mailchimp**: 24/7 email/chat on paid plans, extensive help center, templates galore. Free users get forums.
- **Brevo**: Live chat/email (business hours), phone for higher tiers, academy with certifications.

Both score 4.5+ on G2/Capterra. Mailchimp edges for volume of tutorials.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp: 300+ native (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics). Zapier for more.
Brevo: 300+ (WooCommerce, HubSpot), strong API for developers.

Scalability: Brevo handles millions of emails affordably; Mailchimp suits mid-sized but pricey at scale.

**Practical Advice**: Check your stack—Brevo for EU compliance (GDPR-focused), Mailchimp for US e-com ecosystems.

## Deliverability and Sender Reputation

Both maintain 95%+ rates. Brevo's IP warming and dedicated IPs (paid) help new senders. Mailchimp's legacy boosts trust but strict policies suspend inactive accounts.

**Practical Advice**: Warm up lists gradually: clean bounces weekly, segment engaged users.

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Brevo if**: Startup with growing lists, need SMS/transactionals, budget-conscious (e.g., agencies sending 50k+ emails/mo).
- **Choose Mailchimp if**: Creative marketer, small e-com store (<10k contacts), prioritize templates/insights.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your costs: Input contacts/sends into both calculators. Migrate via CSV—test small campaigns first.

## Conclusion

In the Brevo vs Mailchimp showdown, there's no one-size-fits-all. Brevo wins on value and scalability for most growing businesses, while Mailchimp excels in user-friendliness and design. Start with free trials: sign up for Brevo at brevo.com and Mailchimp at mailchimp.com. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20%+) and ROI after 3 months.

For your email marketing success, align the tool with your goals—affordability (Brevo) or creativity (Mailchimp). Which will you try first?
