# Omnisend Review 2023: Features, Pricing, Pros & Cons for Ecommerce

If you're running an ecommerce store and looking to supercharge your email marketing, Omnisend stands out as a powerful, all-in-one platform designed specifically for online retailers. In this detailed Omnisend review, we'll dive into its core features, pricing structure, ease of use, integrations, and real-world applicability. Whether you're on Shopify, WooCommerce, or another platform, Omnisend helps automate customer journeys, recover abandoned carts, and drive repeat sales through email, SMS, and push notifications.

This review is based on hands-on testing, user feedback from platforms like G2 and Capterra, and official documentation as of late 2023. We'll provide practical advice to help you decide if it's the right fit for your business.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform tailored for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it focuses on helping stores nurture leads, engage customers, and boost revenue without needing a huge marketing team. Unlike generalist tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes ecommerce-specific workflows like cart recovery, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells.

Key differentiators include its pre-built templates for high-converting emails, advanced segmentation using purchase data, and seamless multichannel support (email + SMS + web push). It's trusted by over 70,000 brands, including those on Shopify and BigCommerce.

**Practical Tip:** Start by importing your customer data via CSV or direct integrations. Omnisend automatically tags buyers based on behavior, saving hours of manual setup.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs a robust feature set. Here's a breakdown of the most impactful ones for ecommerce marketers.

### Email Automation and Workflows

Omnisend's automation builder is drag-and-drop simple yet powerful. Build workflows triggered by events like sign-ups, abandoned carts, or birthdays.

- **Cart Recovery:** Sends a series of 2-3 emails with product images and dynamic discounts. Average recovery rate: 10-30% based on user reports.
- **Welcome Series:** Multi-email sequences to onboard new subscribers, with A/B testing for subject lines.
- **Cross-Sell/Win-Back:** Targets lapsed customers with personalized product recommendations.

**Practical Advice:** Use the "Product Picker" block to pull real-time inventory. Test workflows on a small segment first—aim for open rates above 25% by personalizing with first name and recent browses. Integrate with Google Analytics to track revenue per email.

### Omnichannel Marketing (SMS & Push)

Go beyond email with SMS for high-open-rate messages (98% open rate) and web push for browser notifications.

- **SMS Campaigns:** Time-sensitive promos like flash sales or shipping updates.
- **Push Notifications:** Opt-in alerts for cart abandonment or new arrivals.

**Pro Tip:** Comply with TCPA regulations by getting explicit consent. Segment SMS lists to high-value customers only to avoid fatigue—limit to 1-2/month per user.

### List Segmentation and Personalization

Hyper-segment based on purchase history, behavior, and custom fields. Examples:
- New buyers vs. VIPs (3+ purchases).
- By product category or average order value.

Dynamic content blocks adjust emails per recipient. **Advice:** Create segments like "Abandoned Cart >$50" for targeted recoveries. Use the reporting dashboard to refine based on click data.

### Templates and Design

100+ responsive, mobile-optimized templates categorized by goal (e.g., seasonal, promotional). Custom HTML editor for pros.

**Tip:** Stick to templates initially; customize with your branding. Ensure images load fast—compress to under 100KB for better deliverability.

## Omnisend Pricing

Omnisend offers a free plan and tiered paid options, billed monthly or annually (20% discount).

| Plan | Price (up to contacts) | Key Features |
|------|------------------------|--------------|
| Free | $0 (250 contacts) | 500 emails/mo, basic automations |
| Standard | $16/mo (500 contacts) | Unlimited emails, SMS add-on, A/B testing |
| Pro | $59/mo (500 contacts) | Advanced reporting, custom events, API access |

Contacts scale pricing: e.g., Standard for 10k contacts is $59/mo. SMS costs extra (~$1/1000 credits).

**Practical Advice:** Begin with Free to test automations. Upgrade to Standard once you hit 500 contacts—ROI often pays for itself via one good cart recovery campaign. Calculate costs using their pricing calculator.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Intuitive dashboard with ecommerce-first UX. Onboarding includes video tutorials, live chat setup, and a 1-click Shopify install.

Average setup time: 15-30 minutes. Drag-and-drop editor rivals Klaviyo but feels less cluttered.

**User Feedback:** 4.7/5 on G2 for ease of use. Common praise: Quick workflow builder. Drawback: Steeper SMS learning curve for beginners.

**Tip:** Use the setup wizard to connect your store and launch a welcome automation Day 1.

## Integrations

Native apps for 200+ tools:
- Ecommerce: Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Magento.
- Analytics: Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel.
- Others: Zapier, Klaviyo (for migration).

**Advice:** Prioritize Shopify integration for real-time order data sync. Use Zapier for custom flows like Slack notifications on high-value abandons.

## Customer Support

24/7 email/chat for paid plans, knowledge base, and community forum. Response time: <1 hour.

**Tip:** Paid users get a dedicated manager—leverage for workflow audits.

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Ecommerce-focused automations.
- Strong omnichannel (email/SMS/push).
- Generous free plan.
- Excellent deliverability (99% inbox rate claimed).

**Cons:**
- SMS pricing adds up for high-volume.
- No native social media posting.
- Advanced features locked behind Pro.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for small-to-mid ecommerce stores (under 50k contacts) prioritizing automation over enterprise-scale. Great Shopify users. Skip if you need heavy B2B nurturing or complex CDP.

**Alternatives:** Klaviyo (more advanced, pricier), Mailchimp (simpler, less ecommerce focus).
