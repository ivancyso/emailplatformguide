# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can be tough when you're building your email list as a blogger, creator, or small business owner. Both are popular for their user-friendly interfaces and powerful features, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, automation, deliverability, and more to help you decide.

We'll focus on real-world use cases, drawing from user feedback and official documentation. Whether you're on a budget or need advanced creator tools, read on for practical advice.

## Pricing Breakdown: Affordability vs Value

Pricing is often the first deciding factor. Both platforms offer transparent, subscriber-based plans without hidden fees.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite shines for budget-conscious users:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation and landing pages.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers, scaling to $30/month for 10,000.
- **Advanced**: $20/month base for more features like A/B testing and custom HTML.

No contracts, and you can downgrade anytime. Practical tip: Start free to test list growth before committing.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit targets creators with higher-tier value:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 10,000 subscribers (recent update), but limited to 30-day broadcasts.
- **Creator**: $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, $29 for 1,000.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month base, adding advanced reporting and one-click upsells.

ConvertKit bills annually for discounts (up to 20% off). Tip: If selling digital products, the commerce features justify the cost.

**Winner**: MailerLite for startups; ConvertKit for scaling creators. Calculate your needs: If under 1,000 subs, both free tiers work, but MailerLite sends more emails.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop vs Intuitive Flows

Both have intuitive dashboards, but differences emerge in setup.

### MailerLite's User-Friendly Editor
MailerLite's drag-and-drop builder is beginner-friendly. Add blocks for images, buttons, or RSS feeds effortlessly. Mobile-responsive templates out-of-the-box. Onboarding includes video tutorials.

Practical advice: Import contacts via CSV in minutes. Use popups and forms for list building—customize without code.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Design
ConvertKit uses a visual automation builder with sequences (drip campaigns). The editor is block-based, similar to MailerLite, but tagging system simplifies segmentation.

Tip: For podcasters, the subscriber interview form auto-tags interests. Less hand-holding, but 14-day trial with guided setup.

**Winner**: Tie for beginners; ConvertKit edges for power users familiar with tagging.

## Feature Comparison: Core Tools Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates, photo editor integration. Strong for newsletters.
- **ConvertKit**: Commerce-focused templates with product blocks. Visual editor predicts send times.

Both support custom HTML for advanced users.

### Automation and Segmentation

#### MailerLite Automations
Trigger-based: Welcome series, abandoned cart (via integrations). Behavioral segmentation by opens/clicks.

Practical tip: Set up a 5-email nurture sequence in 30 minutes. Integrates with Google Analytics for tracking.

#### ConvertKit Sequences
Rule-based automations shine here. Tag subscribers (e.g., "podcast listener") for hyper-targeted sends. Re-engagement campaigns built-in.

Advice: Use subscriber scores to prioritize engaged leads. Ideal for course launches.

### Landing Pages and Forms
- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, popups, and surveys on all plans. A/B testing included.
- **ConvertKit**: Forms, landing pages, and checkouts. Pro plan unlocks link pages for products.

Tip: MailerLite for lead magnets; ConvertKit for sales funnels.

### Integrations and Analytics
Both connect to 1,000+ apps via Zapier.
- MailerLite: Native WordPress, Shopify; detailed open/click heatmaps.
- ConvertKit: WooCommerce, Teachable; revenue tracking per subscriber.

**Deliverability**: Both boast 99%+ rates. MailerLite uses dedicated IPs on higher plans; ConvertKit emphasizes authentication setup (DKIM/SPF)—follow their guides.

## Pros and Cons: Honest Breakdown

### MailerLite Pros
- Cheaper scaling.
- Generous free plan.
- Excellent popups/forms.

Cons:
- Fewer e-commerce tools.
- Basic tagging.

### ConvertKit Pros
- Superior automations for creators.
- Built-in selling features.
- Strong community resources.

Cons:
- Higher cost for small lists.
- No phone support.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Solopreneurs/bootstrappers.
- Newsletter publishers.
- Businesses under 5,000 subs.

**Migration Tip**: Export from competitors via CSV. MailerLite's importer handles segments cleanly. Test with a small batch first.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers/podcasters selling courses.
- Teams needing revenue analytics.
- Lists over 1,000 with high engagement.

**Setup Advice**: During trial, build one sequence and tag 100 subs to simulate. Check deliverability with GlockApps integration.

## Testing Both: Practical Next Steps

1. Sign up for free trials/plans.
2. Import 50-100 test subscribers.
3. Build a welcome automation and send a broadcast.
4. Compare analytics after 48 hours.
5. Factor in support: MailerLite offers live chat; ConvertKit has email/tickets.

Don't overlook compliance: Both support GDPR double opt-in. Always include unsubscribe links.

## Conclusion: Your Best Pick Depends on Goals

If affordability and simplicity top your list, **MailerLite** is the clear choice—perfect for growing lists without breaking the bank. For creators monetizing through emails, **ConvertKit** delivers unmatched automation and sales tools.

Ultimately, test both. Email marketing ROI comes from engagement, not just features. Whichever you pick, focus on value-driven content to hit 30%+ open rates.
