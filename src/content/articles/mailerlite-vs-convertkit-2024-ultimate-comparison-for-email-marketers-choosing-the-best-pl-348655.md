# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can feel overwhelming when you're building your email list and scaling your marketing efforts. Both are popular email service providers (ESPs) favored by solopreneurs, bloggers, and small businesses for their user-friendly interfaces and powerful automation. But which one fits your needs?

In this in-depth MailerLite vs ConvertKit comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and real-world use cases. Whether you're migrating from another tool or starting fresh, this guide provides practical advice to make an informed decision. Let's dive in.

## MailerLite Overview

MailerLite is a Lithuania-based ESP launched in 2010, known for its affordability and simplicity. It's ideal for beginners and small teams who want robust features without a steep learning curve.

### Key Features
- **Drag-and-Drop Editor**: Intuitive builder for emails, landing pages, popups, and forms.
- **Automation**: Visual workflow builder for welcome series, abandoned cart, and RSS-to-email campaigns.
- **Segmentation**: Tag-based and custom field segmentation for targeted sends.
- **Analytics**: Open rates, clicks, revenue tracking, and A/B testing.
- **Additional Tools**: Built-in website builder, surveys, and one-click signup forms.

MailerLite supports unlimited emails on paid plans and has a strong focus on GDPR compliance, making it great for EU-based users.

### Pricing
MailerLite offers a generous free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Paid plans start at $10/month for 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20 for 1,000, with higher tiers up to Enterprise at custom pricing. No contracts, monthly billing available.

Practical tip: Start with the free plan to test imports and basic automations before upgrading.

## ConvertKit Overview

ConvertKit (rebranded elements to Kit but still widely known as ConvertKit) targets creators like bloggers, podcasters, and course sellers. Founded in 2013, it emphasizes sequences and subscriber tagging for personalized nurturing.

### Key Features
- **Visual Automations**: Tag-triggered sequences, broadcasts, and commerce integrations.
- **Forms & Landing Pages**: Native creators with checkout for digital products.
- **Tagging System**: Advanced subscriber management beyond basic segments.
- **Creator Network**: Monetization tools like paid newsletters and subscriptions.
- **Analytics**: Subscriber growth, revenue per subscriber, and link tracking.

ConvertKit shines in content-driven marketing with features like subscriber scoring and one-click upsells.

### Pricing
Free plan for up to 10,000 subscribers (limited to basic features). Creator plan starts at $9/month for up to 300 subscribers, $29 for 1,000, up to $79 for 10,000. Enterprise at $779+/month. Annual discounts available.

Practical tip: Use the free tier for tagging experiments if you're a content creator testing audience engagement.

## Pricing Comparison: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

| Subscribers | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|-------------|------------|------------|
| 0-1,000    | Free ($10 after) | Free ($9-29) |
| 1,000-3,000| $20-30/mo | $29-49/mo |

MailerLite is cheaper for growing lists under 5,000 subscribers, while ConvertKit's free plan scales higher but paid tiers cost more for advanced creator tools. Factor in your expected growth: MailerLite saves money short-term; ConvertKit justifies costs with monetization features.

**Advice**: Calculate total cost including add-ons. MailerLite has no transaction fees on its site builder; ConvertKit charges 3.5% + Stripe fees for checkouts under $50k/year.

## Feature Face-Off: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

### Email Editor and Templates
Both offer drag-and-drop editors. MailerLite's is simpler with 80+ responsive templates. ConvertKit focuses on clean, mobile-first designs optimized for creators, with fewer but highly customizable templates.

**Winner**: Tie—test both free editors for your brand style.

### Automation Capabilities
MailerLite's automation is visual and beginner-friendly, supporting multi-step workflows like birthday emails or re-engagement.

ConvertKit excels in sequence automations (drip campaigns) triggered by tags, perfect for course funnels.

**Practical Advice**: For e-commerce, MailerLite's cart abandonment wins. For lead nurturing, ConvertKit's tagging provides precision.

### Landing Pages and Forms
MailerLite includes unlimited landing pages and popups on all plans. ConvertKit offers digital product delivery and subscription forms.

**Tip**: Use MailerLite for lead gen sites; ConvertKit for selling info products directly.

### Integrations
MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress, Google Analytics.
ConvertKit: Strong with Teachable, Gumroad, Stronger Zapier (2,000+ apps).

Both integrate with Stripe, but ConvertKit has deeper commerce ties.

### Analytics and Reporting
ConvertKit edges out with subscriber value metrics. MailerLite provides solid basics plus heatmaps.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

MailerLite scores higher for non-tech users—setup in minutes, 24/7 live chat, email support, and a knowledge base.

ConvertKit has a steeper curve for tagging but excellent email support, community forums, and migration services.

**Advice**: Import a test list (both allow CSV uploads) to compare dashboards. MailerLite feels more 'plug-and-play'.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs ConvertKit?

**Pick MailerLite if**:
- You're a small business or blogger on a budget.
- Need quick websites/forms/popups.
- Prioritize ease and EU compliance.

**Example**: A local service provider using popups for appointments.

**Pick ConvertKit if**:
- You're a creator selling courses/newsletters.
- Want advanced sequences and tagging.
- Scale to 10k+ subs free initially.

**Example**: Podcaster automating episode follow-ups with upsells.

**Migration Tip**: Both offer free imports. Export from old ESP as CSV, map fields, and test sends. Use Zapier for ongoing syncs.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your goals: MailerLite for affordable, all-in-one simplicity; ConvertKit for creator-focused power. Neither is 'best' universally—MailerLite suits cost-conscious beginners, while ConvertKit empowers revenue-focused pros.

Start with free trials: sign up for both, import 100 subs, build a sample automation, and send a test campaign. Track your metrics for a week to decide.
