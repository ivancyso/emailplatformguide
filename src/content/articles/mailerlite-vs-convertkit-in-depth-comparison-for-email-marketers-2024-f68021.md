# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business or content creation efforts. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams. Both platforms excel in automation and subscriber management, but they cater to slightly different audiences. MailerLite shines with affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit targets creators with advanced tagging and commerce features.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and real-world use cases. Whether you're on a budget or need creator-specific tools, you'll get practical advice to decide.

## Pricing: Affordability vs Value

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer transparent plans without hidden fees, but structures differ.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite keeps it simple and budget-friendly:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, forms, and landing pages.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers (scales up).
- **Advanced**: From $20/month with A/B testing and web push notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume users.

No contracts; upgrade/downgrade anytime. Annual billing saves 20-30%. Practical tip: Start free to test integrations like WordPress plugins.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now Kit) focuses on creators:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (limited forms/landing pages).
- **Creator**: $15/month for up to 300 subscribers (unlimited emails).
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month adds advanced automations, subscriber scoring, and one-click upsells.
- Higher tiers scale by subscriber count, up to Enterprise.

14-day trial on paid plans. Annual discounts available. Tip: If you're under 300 subs, free plan suffices for basic newsletters.

**Winner?** MailerLite for budgets under $10/month; ConvertKit if you need unlimited sends early on.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

Both are intuitive, but interfaces vary.

### MailerLite User Experience

Clean dashboard with drag-and-drop editor. No coding needed for responsive emails. Features like photo blocks and countdown timers speed up design. Mobile app for on-the-go management. Onboarding wizard guides new users. Pro tip: Use pre-built templates for newsletters to launch in minutes.

### ConvertKit User Experience

Creator-centric with visual automations builder. Tag-based segmentation feels natural for bloggers. Email editor is block-based, similar to modern builders. Learning curve for sequences, but tutorials help. Tip: Leverage the broadcast vs sequence distinction—broadcasts for one-offs, sequences for nurture.

**Verdict**: MailerLite edges for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for those comfortable with tagging.

## Core Features Comparison

Let's dive into what matters: building lists, sending campaigns, and automating.

### Email Builder and Templates

- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates. Drag-and-drop with JSON exports for devs. Embed videos, RSS feeds.
- **ConvertKit**: Simpler builder with 100+ templates. Strong on personalization via tags.

Both ensure mobile optimization—test via preview tools.

### Automation and Segmentation

### MailerLite Automations

Workflow builder for triggers like sign-ups or purchases. Behavioral automation tracks opens/clicks. Segment by custom fields, activity, or tags. Practical advice: Set up a welcome series to boost open rates by 20-30% (industry avg).

### ConvertKit Automations

Sequences shine for drip campaigns. Advanced tagging (e.g., 'lead-magnet-downloader') enables hyper-personalization. Re-engagement campaigns auto-tag inactive subs. Tip: Use subscriber score to prioritize high-value leads.

**Edge**: ConvertKit for complex creator funnels; MailerLite for straightforward workflows.

### Landing Pages and Forms

- **MailerLite**: Unlimited popups, forms, landing pages on all plans. A/B testing included.
- **ConvertKit**: Forms and pages unlimited on paid; Pro adds upsells.

Build opt-ins with incentives like lead magnets. Integrate with quizzes via third-parties.

### Analytics and Reporting

Both track opens, clicks, bounces. MailerLite offers revenue tracking; ConvertKit excels in revenue per subscriber (RPS) for e-commerce.

## Integrations and E-commerce

### MailerLite Integrations

Native with WooCommerce, Shopify, WordPress. Zapier for 1,000+ apps. Stripe for payments.

### ConvertKit Integrations

Gumroad, Teachable, Patreon focus. Strong podcast tools (transistor.fm). Zapier too.

Tip: Check native vs Zapier to avoid extra costs ($20+/mo).

## Customer Support and Community

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Highly rated (4.8/5 on Capterra).
- **ConvertKit**: Email support (24-48hr), creator community, webinars. 4.6/5 ratings.

Both have academies. MailerLite responds faster for urgent issues.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros**:
- Cheapest entry point.
- Feature-rich free plan.
- Intuitive for non-techies.

**Cons**:
- Limited advanced commerce.
- Automation less visual.

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:
- Creator-optimized tagging.
- Unlimited emails.
- Strong monetization tools.

**Cons**:
- Pricier for small lists.
- Steeper learning for basics.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite if**: You're a beginner, small business, or budget-conscious. Ideal for e-commerce stores needing popups/forms.
- **Pick ConvertKit if**: You're a blogger/podcaster selling digital products. Tagging and sequences nurture audiences effectively.

Test both: MailerLite's free plan is generous; ConvertKit's trial lets you import lists.

Practical migration tip: Export CSV from one, import to the other. Clean tags first to avoid duplicates.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit? No clear winner—it's about fit. MailerLite democratizes email marketing with low costs and ease (perfect for startups). ConvertKit empowers creators to monetize via smart automations.

For most choosing platforms, start with your subscriber count and goals. If under 1,000 subs and simple needs, MailerLite saves money. Scaling creators? ConvertKit's tools pay off long-term.
