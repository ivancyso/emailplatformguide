## What is ConvertKit?

ConvertKit is an email marketing platform designed specifically for creators. Launched in 2013 by Nathan Barry, it started as a tool for his own newsletter and evolved into a full-featured service. Unlike generalist platforms like Mailchimp, ConvertKit emphasizes visual automations, subscriber tagging, and commerce integrations tailored for selling digital products, courses, and memberships.

Key highlights:
- **Audience size**: Over 100,000 creators use it.
- **Core focus**: Building relationships with subscribers through personalized emails.
- **Integrations**: Native with WordPress, Shopify, Gumroad, and more.

**Practical tip**: If you're just starting, ConvertKit's free plan supports up to 10,000 emails to 300-1,000 subscribers (depending on sign-ups), making it ideal for testing without commitment.

## Key Features: What ConvertKit Does Best

### Visual Automations
ConvertKit's drag-and-drop automation builder is intuitive. Create sequences triggered by actions like "subscribed to form" or "purchased product."

**H3: How to Build Your First Automation**
1. Go to Automations > New Automation.
2. Choose a trigger (e.g., form submission).
3. Add steps: Send email > Wait 3 days > If/then branch (e.g., opened email?).
4. Test with a sample subscriber.

Practical tip: Use tagging for segmentation—tag buyers vs. freebie downloaders for targeted follow-ups. This boosts open rates by 20-30% based on industry benchmarks.

### Forms and Landing Pages
Unlimited customizable forms (inline, popups, flyouts) and landing pages. No coding needed.

**Pro tip**: Embed forms on your blog via simple code snippets. A/B test headlines for 10-15% conversion lifts.

### Tagging and Segmentation
Instead of complex lists, use tags like "podcast listener" or "course interested." Segments pull active subscribers dynamically.

**H3: Segmentation Best Practices**
- Tag based on behavior: "clicked link to pricing page."
- Create segments: "Tags include 'VIP' AND opened last 5 emails."
- Send re-engagement campaigns to "inactive >90 days" to reduce churn.

### Commerce and Selling
Sell digital products directly: one-click upsells, paywalls, subscriptions. Integrates with Stripe seamlessly.

Practical example: Launch a course—set up a sequence: Welcome > Free lesson > Upsell offer > Feedback survey.

### Analytics and Reporting
Track opens, clicks, revenue per subscriber. Clean dashboards show what's working.

**Tip**: Focus on "revenue per subscriber" metric to justify tool costs.
