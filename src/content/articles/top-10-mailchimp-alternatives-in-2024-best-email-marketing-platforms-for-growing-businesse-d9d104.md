# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Email Marketing Platforms for Growing Businesses

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its pricing changes, limited automation in lower tiers, and steeper costs for larger lists have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're dealing with rising costs, need advanced automations, or want better ecommerce integrations, it's time to explore options.

This roundup compares 10 proven Mailchimp alternatives based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, official pricing pages, and feature sets as of 2024. We'll cover pricing, key features, pros/cons, and practical tips for switching. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business, or scaling ecommerce store, there's a platform here for you.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month (recently updated), but paid plans start at $13/month for essentials and jump to $350+/month for advanced features. Common pain points include:

- **High costs for large lists**: Billed by contacts, not sends.
- **Basic automations** in entry plans.
- **Deliverability dips** for some users post-2023 updates.

Alternatives often offer unlimited sends, better segmentation, or niche strengths like ecommerce. Before migrating, export your Mailchimp list (CSV via Audience > Export), clean it (remove bounces), and test imports.

## 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
- Unlimited contacts in free plan (300 emails/day).
- Marketing automation, SMS, chat, and transactional emails.
- Drag-and-drop editor, A/B testing, landing pages.

### Pricing
Free forever; Starter $25/month (20k emails); Business $65/month (unlimited).

### Pros
Affordable scaling, excellent deliverability (98%+), multi-channel.

### Cons
Advanced segmentation requires Business plan.

**Practical Advice**: Ideal for transactional-heavy businesses. Use their migration tool for seamless Mailchimp import.

## 2. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
- Advanced automations with if/then branching.
- CRM integration, site tracking, machine learning scoring.
- 900+ app integrations via Zapier.

### Pricing
Lite $29/month (500 contacts); Plus $49; Professional $149.

### Pros
Powerful for sales funnels, high customization.

### Cons
Steep learning curve for beginners.

**Practical Advice**: Start with a 14-day trial. Map your Mailchimp automations to AC's 'recipes' for quick setup. Great for B2B.

## 3. Klaviyo

### Key Features
- Ecommerce-first: Flows for abandoned carts, product recommendations.
- Revenue tracking, dynamic segments based on behavior.
- Integrates with Shopify, BigCommerce.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; scales with revenue (e.g., $20/month for small stores).

### Pros
Best ROI for DTC brands (users report 30%+ uplift).

### Cons
Less ideal for non-ecommerce.

**Practical Advice**: Connect your store in minutes. Use pre-built flows to replicate Mailchimp welcome series.

## 4. MailerLite

### Key Features
- Popups, forms, landing pages included.
- Automation blocks, A/B testing, RSS campaigns.

### Pricing
Free (1k subs, 12k emails); Growing $10/month (500 subs unlimited sends).

### Pros
Super affordable, beginner-friendly editor.

### Cons
No native CRM.

**Practical Advice**: Perfect Mailchimp swap for newsletters. Their API makes migration straightforward.

## 5. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

### Key Features
- Creator-focused: Visual automations, tagging over lists.
- Paid newsletters, digital product sales.

### Pricing
Free (300 subs); Creator $29/month; Creator Pro $59.

### Pros
Intuitive for bloggers/influencers.

### Cons
Limited reporting.

**Practical Advice**: Tag subscribers by interests during import for better targeting than Mailchimp lists.

## 6. GetResponse

### Key Features
- Webinars, funnels, ecommerce tools.
- AI email generator, conversion funnels.

### Pricing
Free (500 contacts); Email Marketing $19/month; Marketing Automation $59.

### Pros
All-in-one with sales tools.

### Cons
Interface feels dated.

**Practical Advice**: Use for webinar sequences post-Mailchimp.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: Ecommerce? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Automations? ActiveCampaign.
2. **Budget Check**: Calculate based on list size (e.g., 5k subs: Brevo ~$65 vs Mailchimp $75+).
3. **Trial & Test**: Most offer 14-30 days free. Send test campaigns.
4. **Migrate Smartly**:
   - Export Mailchimp CSV.
   - Import to new tool (all support it).
   - Recreate top campaigns/automations first.
   - Update signup forms/domain authentication (SPF/DKIM).
5. **Monitor Metrics**: Track open rates (aim 20-30%), clicks (2-5%), deliverability.

Pro Tip: Use Zapier for hybrid setups during transition.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ActiveCampaign, and Klaviyo offer better value, features, and flexibility for most users.
