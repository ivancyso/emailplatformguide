# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for Email Marketers

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can make or break your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful platforms, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a veteran in the space, known for its beginner-friendly interface and broad appeal. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines in eCommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

In this Omnisend vs Mailchimp guide, we'll break down pricing, features, integrations, and real-world use cases to help you decide. Whether you're a small business owner or scaling an online store, this comparison provides practical advice based on their core offerings.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been around since 2001 and powers millions of campaigns. It's ideal for newsletters, basic automations, and audience segmentation.

### Key Strengths
- **User-Friendly Editor**: Drag-and-drop builder makes it easy for non-designers.
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month—perfect for startups.
- **Analytics**: Detailed reports on opens, clicks, and revenue.

Practical tip: Start with the free plan to test audience engagement before upgrading.

## Overview of Omnisend

Launched in 2014, Omnisend targets eCommerce brands with pre-built workflows for abandoned carts, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells. It supports email, SMS, and web push in one dashboard.

### Key Strengths
- **Omnichannel**: Combine email with SMS for higher engagement rates.
- **Ecommerce Integrations**: Seamless with Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce.
- **Automation Templates**: Ready-to-use flows save setup time.

Practical tip: Use Omnisend's popup builder to grow your list with incentives like discounts.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major factor in Omnisend vs Mailchimp debates. Both use tiered plans based on contacts and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
| Plan | Contacts | Price/Month |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Free | 500 | $0 |
| Essentials | 500-50k | $13+ |
| Standard | 500-100k | $20+ |
| Premium | 500-10k+ | $350+ |

Mailchimp charges extra for advanced automations and SMS in lower tiers. Legacy plans are being phased out, so new users get these rates.

### Omnisend Pricing
| Plan | Contacts | Price/Month |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Free | 250 | $0 |
| Standard | 500-100k | $16+ |
| Pro | Unlimited | $59+ |

Omnisend includes SMS credits in paid plans and offers pay-as-you-go for extras. No contracts, and you can switch plans easily.

**Verdict**: Mailchimp wins for tiny lists with its generous free tier. Omnisend is more cost-effective for growing eCommerce lists over 5,000 contacts due to unlimited emails and multichannel.

Practical advice: Calculate your costs using their pricing calculators. Factor in SMS if you plan multichannel campaigns.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Ease of Use
Mailchimp's interface feels intuitive for beginners, with a clean dashboard. Omnisend has a slight learning curve for omnichannel but excels in automation setup.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for pure email; Omnisend for advanced flows.

### Email Templates and Design
- Mailchimp: 100+ responsive templates, custom HTML option.
- Omnisend: Product-focused blocks (e.g., dynamic product feeds), A/B testing built-in.

Practical tip: In Mailchimp, use content optimizer for better deliverability. Omnisend's dynamic content pulls real inventory data.

### Automation and Workflows
Mailchimp offers single, customer journey, and classic automations (Premium only). Omnisend's 200+ templates include win-back and cross-sell sequences.

**Practical Example**: For abandoned carts, Omnisend sends email #1, SMS #2, push #3 automatically. Mailchimp requires separate tools for SMS.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both segment by behavior, location, and tags. Omnisend adds predictive segmentation based on purchase data.

### Integrations
- Mailchimp: 300+ including Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier.
- Omnisend: 160+, strong in eCommerce (Shopify pulls orders in real-time).

**Tip**: If you're on Shopify, Omnisend installs in minutes with one-click sync.

### Deliverability and Support
Mailchimp boasts high deliverability (98%+ claimed). Omnisend focuses on eCommerce with dedicated IP options in Pro.
Support: Mailchimp has 24/7 chat for paid; Omnisend offers priority email and chat.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros
- Established brand with vast resources (tutorials, community).
- Excellent for content-heavy newsletters.
- Mobile app for on-the-go management.

### Mailchimp Cons
- Expensive for large lists.
- Limited multichannel without add-ons.
- Automation locked behind higher plans.

### Omnisend Pros
- All-in-one for email + SMS + push.
- ROI-focused for eCommerce (tracks revenue per campaign).
- Generous free trial (14 days).

### Omnisend Cons
- Less ideal for non-eCommerce (fewer newsletter templates).
- SMS costs add up for high-volume.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Mailchimp if**:
- You're a blogger, agency, or service business focused on newsletters.
- Budget is tight and list <2,500.
- You need robust A/B testing and landing pages.

**Pick Omnisend if**:
- You run an online store (Shopify, etc.).
- Want to boost revenue with automations and SMS.
- Scaling beyond 5,000 contacts.

**Migration Tip**: Both offer CSV imports. Test with a small segment first to check deliverability.

## Real-User Insights

From forums like Reddit and G2:
- Mailchimp users love simplicity but complain about 2023 pricing hikes.
- Omnisend scores high (4.6/5 on G2) for eCommerce ROI, with users reporting 30%+ cart recovery lifts.

Practical advice: Run a 14-day Omnisend trial alongside Mailchimp to A/B test campaigns.

## Conclusion

In the Omnisend vs Mailchimp showdown, there's no one-size-fits-all winner—it depends on your business. Mailchimp suits general email marketing with its ease and free start. Omnisend dominates for eCommerce with superior automations and multichannel.

For most online stores, Omnisend edges out due to revenue-tracking and integrations. Small non-eCommerce teams? Stick with Mailchimp.

Ready to choose? Sign up for free trials today and track metrics like open rates (aim >20%) and revenue per recipient. Whichever you pick, consistent testing and segmentation will drive results.
