# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can be tough when you're building your email list. Both are popular for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. MailerLite shines with affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit excels in creator-focused tools like advanced automations and tagging.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, and real-world advice to help you decide. Whether you have 100 subscribers or 10,000, we'll guide you based on practical scenarios.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Your Subscriber Count

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both platforms scale with subscribers, but MailerLite starts cheaper with a generous free plan.

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups.
- **Growing Business**: $10/month for up to 500 subs (billed annually), scales to $20 for 1,000 subs.
- **Advanced**: $21/month for 500 subs, up to $58 for 10,000 subs.

MailerLite keeps costs low even at scale—under $100/month for 25,000 subs. No transaction fees on payments.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free Trial**: 14 days, no card required.
- **Creator**: $15/month for up to 300 subs (annually), $29 for 1,000.
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month for 300 subs, $79 for 1,000. Adds advanced reporting, subscriber scoring.
- **Enterprise**: Custom for 100k+ subs.

ConvertKit charges 20-50% more than MailerLite at most levels. It includes commerce features (one-time fees of 3.5% + $0.30 on payouts).

**Practical Advice**: If you're under 1,000 subs and budget-tight, start with MailerLite's free plan to test waters without commitment. ConvertKit suits those willing to invest $30+/month for premium creator tools from day one. Always calculate based on your growth: use their pricing calculators.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

Both have intuitive interfaces, but MailerLite feels more beginner-friendly.

### MailerLite Interface
Clean dashboard with quick access to campaigns, automations, and sites. Drag-and-drop editor is responsive, with 80+ templates. Mobile app for on-the-go management.

### ConvertKit Interface
Sleek, creator-oriented with a focus on sequences and broadcasts. Visual automation builder is powerful but has a slight learning curve for tagging. No mobile app yet.

**Practical Advice**: Newbies or non-techies—go MailerLite for its shallower curve. Spend 30 minutes in each demo account. ConvertKit rewards familiarity with its tagging system for personalized flows.

## Feature Face-Off: Automation, Segmentation, and More

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer drag-and-drop with HTML options.
- **MailerLite**: 100+ responsive templates, photo editor, RSS campaigns. Strong for newsletters.
- **ConvertKit**: Commerce-friendly templates, inline product recommendations. Ideal for selling digital products.

**Winner**: Tie—test templates for your brand.

### Automation and Workflows
- **MailerLite**: Visual workflow builder for triggers like sign-ups, purchases. A/B testing, drip sequences.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences (automated series), visual automations with actions/loops. Tagging for complex nurturing (e.g., tag 'interested' after webinar).

**Practical Advice**: For simple welcome series, MailerLite suffices. Creators running courses/podcasts need ConvertKit's tagging—e.g., automate upsells based on opens/clicks.

### Forms, Popups, and Landing Pages
- **MailerLite**: Unlimited popups, forms, surveys. Website builder with themes.
- **ConvertKit**: Native landing pages, opt-in forms, link pages (great for podcasts). Fewer popup options.

Grow lists faster with MailerLite's popups on WordPress/Webflow.

### Segmentation and Personalization
- **MailerLite**: Groups, segments by behavior/time. Basic scoring.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tagging, subscriber scores, predictive analytics in Pro.

**Practical Advice**: Segment early—e.g., MailerLite for 'active vs inactive'; ConvertKit for nuanced creator funnels like 'podcast listener score'.

### Analytics and Deliverability
Both track opens/clicks (95%+ deliverability).
- MailerLite: Heatmaps, revenue tracking.
- ConvertKit: Revenue per subscriber, churn prediction.

ConvertKit edges for e-commerce insights.

## Integrations and Customer Support

### Integrations
- **MailerLite**: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress, WooCommerce, Google Analytics.
- **ConvertKit**: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable, Patreon. Zapier too.

Both integrate seamlessly with Stripe for payments.

### Support
- MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, help center.
- ConvertKit: Email (business hours), chat for Pro+, extensive academy.

**Practical Advice**: MailerLite for instant help; ConvertKit's docs suffice for power users.

**Pros**:

- Unlimited everything
- Excellent popups/sites

**Cons**:
- Less advanced tagging
- Fewer creator-specific tools

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:
- Superior automations/tagging
- Built for monetization
- Great for courses/podcasts

**Cons**:
- Higher cost, no free plan
- Steeper curve for basics

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs ConvertKit?

- **Pick MailerLite if**: Small business, blogger on budget, need popups/landing pages. Perfect for 0-5k subs focusing on growth.
- **Pick ConvertKit if**: Creator selling products/courses, need tagging/sequences. Worth it for 1k+ engaged subs.

**Test Both**: MailerLite free, ConvertKit trial. Export lists easily (CSV). Consider churn: switch if features underdeliver.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to budget vs power. MailerLite wins for affordability and ease—ideal for most starting out. ConvertKit dominates for serious creators monetizing audiences.
