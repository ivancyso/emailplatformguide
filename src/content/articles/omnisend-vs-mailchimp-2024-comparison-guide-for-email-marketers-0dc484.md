# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools trusted by thousands of businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a long-standing favorite for its user-friendly interface and broad appeal, while Omnisend shines in ecommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're a small business owner, ecommerce entrepreneur, or marketer scaling campaigns, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, launched in 2001, is one of the most recognized names in email marketing. It serves over 12 million users worldwide and offers an all-in-one platform for email, automation, landing pages, and social ads.

**Key Strengths:**
- Beginner-friendly drag-and-drop editor.
- Generous free plan for startups.
- Robust audience segmentation and A/B testing.

**Best For:** General businesses, bloggers, and nonprofits needing simple, scalable email tools. However, advanced ecommerce users often find its automation less specialized.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend is a newer player (founded 2014) focused on ecommerce brands. It integrates seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce, emphasizing omnichannel marketing.

**Key Strengths:**
- Pre-built workflows for abandoned carts, welcome series, and post-purchase emails.
- SMS, web push, and popup tools included.
- Advanced segmentation based on purchase behavior.

**Best For:** Online stores wanting to recover lost sales and engage customers across channels without juggling multiple apps.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is a top concern for platform choosers. Both offer free tiers, but scales differ based on contacts and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic features.
- **Essentials:** Starts at $13/mo (5,000 contacts), adds automation and A/B testing.
- **Standard:** $20/mo (5,000 contacts), includes dynamic content.
- **Premium:** $350/mo (10,000+ contacts), advanced reporting.

Mailchimp bills monthly or annually (20% discount). Overages for sends can add up quickly.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, core automations.
- **Standard:** $16/mo (500 contacts), unlimited emails, SMS add-on.
- **Pro:** $59/mo (500 contacts), advanced segmentation, A/B testing.
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing for high-volume.

Omnisend charges per contact, with unlimited emails in paid plans—ideal for frequent senders. SMS credits are extra (~$1 per 100).

**Practical Advice:** If you're under 500 contacts and testing waters, Mailchimp's free plan edges out. For ecommerce with 1,000+ customers sending daily, Omnisend's unlimited sends save money long-term. Calculate your needs: Estimate monthly sends x open rates to project costs.

## Feature Face-Off: Automation, Templates, and Segmentation

### Email Automation
Mailchimp offers behavioral automations like tag-based triggers and customer journeys. It's solid for newsletters but lacks ecommerce depth.

Omnisend excels here: Drag-and-drop workflow builder with 200+ templates for cart recovery (up to 30% recovery rates reported by users), win-back, and cross-sells. Practical tip: Use Omnisend's cart abandonment flow—set it to trigger 1 hour post-abandon with a 10% discount code.

### Templates and Design
Both have drag-and-drop editors. Mailchimp's library is vast (hundreds of themes), mobile-responsive by default. Omnisend focuses on product-focused blocks, syncing real inventory from your store—perfect for dynamic emails.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp: Groups, tags, merge fields. Predicts customer lifetime value.

Omnisend: Deeper ecommerce segments like 'viewed product X but didn't buy.' Hyper-personalization with product recommendations boosts opens by 20-30% typically.

**Winner:** Omnisend for ecommerce; Mailchimp for general lists.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners—intuitive dashboard, extensive tutorials, and 24/7 chat support (paid plans). Setup takes minutes: Import CSV, design first email.

Omnisend has a steeper curve due to advanced features but ecommerce wizards guide setup. Onboarding emails teach workflows step-by-step.

**Practical Advice:** Start with Mailchimp if non-techy. Switch to Omnisend once integrating store data—its Shopify app auto-syncs orders in seconds.

## Integrations and Apps

Mailchimp: 300+ integrations (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Native forms and popups.

Omnisend: 160+ but ecommerce-heavy (Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives, Google Ads). Unique: SMS via Twilio, push notifications.

Both support Zapier for custom flows. Pro tip: Test integrations early—Omnisend's Shopify sync pulls customer data without manual exports, saving hours weekly.

## Customer Support and Resources

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for free; chat/phone for paid. Huge knowledge base, courses.

Omnisend: 24/7 live chat/email for all plans, dedicated manager for Pro+. Active Facebook community, migration service from Mailchimp.

**Practical Advice:** Omnisend's responsive support shines for urgent campaign tweaks—users report <10min responses.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros:
- Free plan generosity.
- All-in-one (ads, sites).
- Proven scalability.

Cons:
- Pricing jumps steeply.
- Ecommerce automations basic.
- Deliverability dips with high volume.

### Omnisend Pros:
- Omnichannel (SMS/push boosts engagement 6x).
- Ecommerce-first automations.
- Cost-effective for sends.

Cons:
- Smaller free plan.
- Less suited for non-ecom.
- Fewer templates.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Mailchimp if:** General marketing, small lists, budget under $20/mo, need social tools.
- **Pick Omnisend if:** Ecommerce store, prioritize recovery/upsells, want SMS/push, scaling sends.

Test both free plans: Import 100 contacts, build an automation, send a test campaign. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and clicks.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce-focused businesses with superior automations and omnichannel reach, potentially increasing revenue 15-25% via recoveries. Mailchimp remains king for versatile, easy entry-level use.

Your choice depends on goals—audit your store type, list size, and channels. Whichever you select, commit to consistent testing: A/B subjects, segment ruthlessly, analyze reports weekly.
