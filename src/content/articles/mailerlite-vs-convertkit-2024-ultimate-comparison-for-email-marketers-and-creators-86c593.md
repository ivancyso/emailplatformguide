# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Platform Should You Choose in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool is crucial for growing your audience, nurturing leads, and driving revenue. MailerLite and ConvertKit are two popular platforms, each appealing to different users. MailerLite is known for its affordability and simplicity, making it ideal for beginners and small businesses. ConvertKit, on the other hand, caters to creators like bloggers and podcasters with advanced automation and tagging features.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur deciding between MailerLite vs ConvertKit or scaling your business, this guide provides practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities as of 2024.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first factor when comparing MailerLite and ConvertKit. Both offer straightforward plans, but they differ in starting costs and free tiers.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite shines with its generous free plan, allowing up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Paid plans start at $10/month for up to 500 contacts (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20/month for 1,000 contacts, $30 for 2,500, and so on. Advanced features like A/B testing and web push notifications are available on higher tiers starting at $20/month. Annual billing saves 30%.

**Practical Tip:** Start with the free plan to test campaigns. It's perfect if you're under 1,000 subscribers and want no-risk entry.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit no longer has a traditional free plan but offers a 14-day free trial. The Creator plan starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers (billed annually at $9/month effectively), $29/month for 1,000, and scales up. The Creator Pro plan ($45/month for 300 subs) adds advanced features like subscriber scoring and integrations. They emphasize unlimited emails sent.

**Practical Tip:** If you have fewer than 300 subscribers, the low entry point is attractive. Use the trial to import contacts and run a sequence before committing.

| Feature | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Free Plan | Up to 1k subs, 12k emails/mo | 14-day trial only |
| Starting Paid | $10/mo (500 subs) | $15/mo (300 subs) |
| Annual Discount | 30% | Yes, effective lower rates |

**Winner:** MailerLite for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit for unlimited sends on paid plans.

## Features Comparison: Core Capabilities

Both platforms offer essential email marketing tools, but their strengths vary.

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 80+ responsive templates. You can customize blocks easily, and it supports HTML for advanced users.

ConvertKit's visual builder is block-based, optimized for creators. It includes pre-built templates for newsletters, broadcasts, and sales pages, with strong mobile previews.

**Practical Advice:** Import your existing HTML emails into MailerLite for quick setup. For ConvertKit, use their template library to match your brand in minutes.

### Automation and Sequences

ConvertKit excels here with its "Sequences" (automated email series) and tagging system over traditional lists. Visual automation builder lets you create if/then flows based on opens, clicks, or purchases.

MailerLite offers automation workflows with triggers like sign-ups or purchases. It's simpler but powerful for drip campaigns and abandoned cart recovery.

**Practical Tip:** In ConvertKit, tag subscribers by behavior (e.g., "read-book") for hyper-personalization. MailerLite users: Set up a welcome series in under 10 minutes using templates.

### Segmentation and Analytics

ConvertKit uses tags and segments for precise targeting. Analytics show opens, clicks, revenue per subscriber, and unsubscribe rates.

MailerLite segments by custom fields, activity, or groups. Detailed reports include click maps and revenue tracking.

**Practical Advice:** Test segments with A/B splits (available in both). Track revenue attribution to justify your tool choice.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

Both have built-in builders:
- MailerLite: Unlimited landing pages, popups, and forms on all plans.
- ConvertKit: Forms, landing pages, and checkout pages (Pro plan for advanced).

**Tip:** Use MailerLite popups for exit-intent on blogs; ConvertKit forms integrate seamlessly with WordPress.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

MailerLite's interface is clean and beginner-friendly, with a shallow learning curve. Onboarding includes video tutorials and migration tools.

ConvertKit is creator-oriented, with a dashboard focused on growth metrics. It's straightforward but assumes some marketing knowledge.

**Practical Advice:** Migrate from other tools using CSV imports or direct integrations (both support Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign). MailerLite's one-click import is faster for large lists.

**Winner:** MailerLite for absolute beginners.

## Integrations and Deliverability

Integrations:
- MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress, WooCommerce, Google Analytics.
- ConvertKit: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable, Zapier; focuses on creator tools like Patreon.

Deliverability: Both maintain high rates (MailerLite ~99%, ConvertKit ~98% per industry benchmarks). Use double opt-ins and clean lists.

**Tip:** Connect to Google Analytics in both for UTM tracking. Test deliverability with tools like GlockApps.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive knowledge base, Facebook group.

ConvertKit: Email support (priority on Pro), help docs, creator community forums.

**Practical Advice:** Join communities—MailerLite's Facebook group for quick tips; ConvertKit's podcast for strategies.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros:** Affordable, free plan, easy editor, unlimited images/emails.
**Cons:** Fewer advanced creator features, tagging less robust.

### ConvertKit
**Pros:** Superior automations, revenue tracking, creator-focused.
**Cons:** Higher cost, no perpetual free plan.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs ConvertKit?

- **Choose MailerLite if:** You're a small business, blogger starting out, or need low-cost with popups/landing pages. Ideal for 1k+ subs on budget.
- **Choose ConvertKit if:** You're a content creator selling digital products, need tagging/sequences, and value unlimited sends.

**Migration Tip:** Export lists as CSV, map fields, and re-engage with a "we've moved" campaign to retain opens.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit comes down to your stage and needs. MailerLite wins for affordability and simplicity, perfect for bootstrapped ventures. ConvertKit edges out for creators prioritizing automations and monetization.

Test both: Sign up for MailerLite's free plan and ConvertKit's trial. Run a sample campaign, analyze metrics, and scale with the one fitting your workflow. In 2024, the best tool boosts engagement without breaking the bank—choose wisely to grow your email list effectively.
