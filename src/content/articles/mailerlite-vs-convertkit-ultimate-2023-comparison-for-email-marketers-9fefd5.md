# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for creators, small businesses, and marketers seeking affordable, powerful platforms. This in-depth comparison covers pricing, features, usability, and more to guide your decision.

## Overview of MailerLite

MailerLite is a Lithuania-based platform launched in 2010, known for its simplicity and cost-effectiveness. It caters to bloggers, e-commerce stores, and nonprofits with a free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Key strengths include an intuitive drag-and-drop editor, responsive templates, and built-in tools like popups, forms, and landing pages.

Practical tip: Start with MailerLite's free tier to test subscriber growth without upfront costs. Use their A/B testing for subject lines to boost open rates by experimenting with personalized previews.

## Overview of ConvertKit

ConvertKit, founded in 2013 by Nathan Barry, targets creators like bloggers, podcasters, and YouTubers. It emphasizes automation sequences, subscriber tagging, and commerce integrations. There's no perpetual free plan, but a 14-day trial lets you explore. Pricing begins at $29/month for up to 1,000 subscribers.

Practical advice: Leverage ConvertKit's visual automation builder for welcome series. Tag subscribers based on behavior (e.g., 'clicked podcast link') to send targeted follow-ups, increasing conversions.

## Pricing Comparison

| Feature | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Free Plan | Yes (1,000 subs, 12k emails/mo) | No (14-day trial) |
| Starter Plan | $10/mo (500 subs) | $29/mo (1,000 subs) |
| Growth Plan | $20/mo (2,500 subs) | $29/mo (300 subs, scales up) |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom |

MailerLite wins on affordability, especially for bootstrapped users. ConvertKit's pricing includes unlimited emails but jumps quickly with subscriber count.

**Pro Tip:** Calculate costs based on your list size. For 5,000 subs, MailerLite is ~$50/mo vs ConvertKit's $79/mo. Factor in add-ons like ConvertKit's Creator Network ($29 extra).

## Ease of Use and Interface

MailerLite's dashboard is clean and beginner-friendly. The email editor mimics popular builders like Canva—drag elements, customize blocks, no coding needed. Navigation is straightforward for campaigns, automations, and analytics.

ConvertKit feels more 'creator-centric' with a sidebar for broadcasts, sequences, and tags. The editor is block-based but requires more setup for complex flows. Steeper learning curve for non-creators.

**Practical Advice:** If you're new, import contacts to MailerLite and send a test campaign in under 30 minutes. For ConvertKit, watch their free tutorials on tagging to segment lists effectively from day one.

## Key Features Breakdown

### Email Templates and Design
Both offer responsive, mobile-optimized templates. MailerLite has 80+ free ones with photo editing tools. ConvertKit provides customizable blocks but fewer pre-mades—focus on simplicity.

### Automation and Workflows
ConvertKit excels with unlimited sequences triggered by actions (e.g., purchase). MailerLite supports multi-step automations, RSS-to-email, and abandoned cart flows.

**Tip:** Use MailerLite's automation for e-commerce drip campaigns. In ConvertKit, set up 'if/then' branches for personalized subscriber journeys.

### Segmentation and Tagging
ConvertKit's tagging is powerful—auto-tag opens/clicks. MailerLite uses segments based on custom fields, activity, or groups.

### Landing Pages and Forms
MailerLite includes unlimited popups, forms, and sites. ConvertKit offers landing pages and forms optimized for lead magnets.

### Analytics and Reporting
Both track opens, clicks, bounces. ConvertKit adds revenue tracking per subscriber; MailerLite shows campaign revenue fields.

**Pro Tip:** Integrate Google Analytics in both for deeper insights. Track unsubscribe rates and refine content to stay under 0.5% for healthy lists.

## Integrations and Support

MailerLite integrates with 1,000+ apps via Zapier, plus native Stripe, WooCommerce, WordPress. Support: Email, chat (paid plans), extensive help center.

ConvertKit connects to Shopify, Gumroad, Teachable; strong Zapier support. Help: Email, chat, Creator Network community.

**Advice:** For WordPress users, MailerLite's plugin auto-syncs subscribers. ConvertKit shines with podcast tools like Transistor.

## Pros and Cons

**MailerLite Pros:** Cheap/free plan, easy editor, all-in-one (sites/forms), high deliverability.
**Cons:** Limited advanced tagging, fewer creator-specific tools.

**ConvertKit Pros:** Robust automations, tagging, commerce focus, unlimited sends.
**Cons:** Pricier, no free plan, basic templates.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Beginners/small lists (<5k subs)
- Budget-conscious e-commerce/nonprofits
- Need for websites/popups

**Practical Step:** Sign up free, build a popup for your site, aim for 10% opt-in rate.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators (blogs/podcasts)
- Growing lists needing sequences
- Monetization-focused (digital products)

**Practical Step:** Trial it, import list, create a 7-email sequence for new subs to nurture leads.

## Conclusion

MailerLite edges out for affordability and simplicity, ideal for starters scaling cost-effectively. ConvertKit suits creators prioritizing automations and tagging for higher engagement. Test both—MailerLite free, ConvertKit trial—to match your workflow. Ultimately, the 'best' depends on your audience size, budget, and goals. Prioritize deliverability (both >95%) and compliance (GDPR tools in both). Whichever you pick, consistent value-driven emails will grow your business. Start today and watch your open rates soar!
