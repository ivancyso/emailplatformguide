# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for creators, small businesses, and marketers seeking affordable, powerful automation. This head-to-head comparison dives into pricing, features, usability, and real-world performance to help you decide.

## Pricing: Affordability vs Value

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms scale with subscribers, but their models differ significantly.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite shines in accessibility:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month—ideal for startups testing waters.
- **Growing Business**: $10/month for up to 500 subs (scales to $20 for 1,000, etc.).
- **Advanced**: $21/month starting, adds A/B testing, custom HTML.
- **Enterprise**: Custom quotes for high-volume needs.

No contracts, monthly billing, and a 30-day money-back guarantee. Practical tip: Start free to build your list before upgrading.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit targets creators with premium features:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (limited forms/landing pages).
- **Creator**: $15/month for up to 300 subs ($29 for 1,000).
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month starting (unlimited features like commerce).
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

Annual discounts up to 20%. Advice: If you're under 300 subs, the free tier works, but growth quickly pushes costs higher than MailerLite.

**Winner**: MailerLite for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit if you need creator-specific tools justifying the premium.

## Ease of Use: Intuitive Interfaces for Beginners

Both are drag-and-drop friendly, but user experience varies.

### MailerLite User Experience

Clean, minimalist dashboard. Key strengths:
- Intuitive editor with 80+ responsive templates.
- One-click popups/websites builder.
- Simple automation workflows via visual builder.

New users onboard in minutes. Pro tip: Use the template library to launch campaigns fast without design skills.

### ConvertKit User Experience

Creator-centric with tagging over lists. Highlights:
- Visual automation builder (like a flowchart).
- Sequences for drip campaigns.
- Commerce dashboard for selling digital products.

Steeper for non-creators due to tagging system. Advice: Watch ConvertKit's tutorials if switching from list-based tools.

**Winner**: MailerLite for sheer simplicity; ConvertKit for power users comfortable with segments.

## Core Features: Automations, Segmentation, and More

Features drive engagement. Here's a breakdown.

### Email Builder and Templates

- **MailerLite**: Drag-and-drop with JSON editor for coders. Mobile-optimized templates.
- **ConvertKit**: Block-based builder, strong for newsletters. Less template variety.

Both support custom HTML/CSS.

### Automations and Workflows

- **MailerLite**: Trigger-based (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart). Multi-step with delays/if-then logic.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences and automations excel for creators. Tag triggers automate subscriber journeys.

Practical advice: For e-commerce, MailerLite's integrations (WooCommerce, Shopify) pair well with automations. Creators love ConvertKit's subscriber scoring.

### Segmentation and Analytics

- **MailerLite**: Tags, groups, custom fields. Open/click tracking, heatmaps.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tagging, subscriber reports, revenue tracking.

Both offer A/B testing (MailerLite free on higher plans).

### Landing Pages and Forms

- **MailerLite**: Unlimited popups, forms, landing pages (even on free plan).
- **ConvertKit**: Forms, landing pages, but limited on free tier.

Tip: Use MailerLite popups for lead gen on blogs; ConvertKit for checkout-optimized pages.

### Integrations

Both connect to 100+ apps:
- MailerLite: WordPress, Zapier, Google Analytics.
- ConvertKit: Gumroad, Teachable, Stronger Zapier support.

**Winner**: Tie—MailerLite for broad use, ConvertKit for creator ecosystem.

## Deliverability and Support

Reliable delivery matters.

- **Deliverability**: Both maintain 99%+ rates. MailerLite uses Amazon SES; ConvertKit its infrastructure. Authenticate domains for best results.
- **Support**: MailerLite offers live chat (paid), email, help center. ConvertKit: Email support, extensive docs, priority for Pro.

Advice: Always warm up IPs and clean lists to hit inboxes.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite

**Pros**:
- Cheaper at scale.
- Free plan generosity.
- All-in-one (sites, popups).

**Cons**:
- Fewer creator-specific tools.
- Basic commerce.

### ConvertKit

**Pros**:
- Superior tagging/sequences.
- Built-in selling features.
- Creator community.

**Cons**:
- Higher cost.
- Limited free features.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Small business, blogger on budget, need popups/landing pages. Great for 1,000-10,000 subs.
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Monetizing audience, digital products, complex nurtures. Ideal for course creators.

Test both: MailerLite's free plan, ConvertKit's trial.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit? MailerLite wins for affordability and ease, perfect for most growing lists. ConvertKit edges out for creators prioritizing sequences and sales. Assess your subscriber count, budget, and goals—export/import subscribers easily between them.

Ultimately, start with your needs: Budget? MailerLite. Creator tools? ConvertKit. Both deliver ROI through engaged lists. Ready to choose? Sign up today and automate your growth.
