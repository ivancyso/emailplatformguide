# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are popular platforms designed for businesses of all sizes, from solopreneurs to enterprises. This in-depth comparison breaks down key aspects like pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and deliverability to help you decide.

We'll focus on real-world use cases for audiences like small business owners, e-commerce stores, and marketing teams looking to scale their email campaigns.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but they differ in limits and scalability.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo's model is contact-based but emphasizes daily email sends on lower tiers. 
- **Free Plan**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails per day (about 9,000/month).
- **Starter**: $25/month for 20,000 emails/month.
- **Business**: Starts at $65/month for 20,000 emails with advanced automation.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing.

Brevo shines for transactional emails (like order confirmations) alongside marketing, with no extra fees for SMS or WhatsApp in higher plans. It's cost-effective for high-volume senders without massive lists.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp uses a contact-based model with send limits tied to subscribers.
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts, scaling up.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, with better automation.
- **Premium**: Starts at $350/month for advanced features.

Mailchimp gets pricey fast as your list grows—expect $100+/month for 10,000 contacts. It's better for low-volume, design-focused users but less ideal for scaling without budget hikes.

**Practical Advice**: If you're a startup sending frequent transactional emails, Brevo saves money long-term. For occasional newsletters with under 2,000 subscribers, Mailchimp's free tier suffices. Calculate your monthly sends: Brevo caps daily, Mailchimp caps per billing cycle.

## Features: Core Capabilities Compared

Both platforms offer drag-and-drop editors, but their strengths vary.

### Email Builder and Templates
- **Brevo**: Responsive templates with a clean editor. Strong in transactional emails via API. Includes SMS, chat, and WhatsApp marketing.
- **Mailchimp**: Superior design library with 100+ templates. Excellent for creative campaigns, landing pages, and postcards.

Brevo edges out for multi-channel (email + SMS), while Mailchimp wins for visual polish.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Brevo**: Unlimited automations on paid plans. Triggers like abandoned cart, welcome series, with A/B testing.
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journey Builder on Standard+. Behavioral automations are intuitive but limited on lower tiers.

**Tip**: E-commerce users—test Brevo's cart recovery flows; they integrate seamlessly with Shopify/WooCommerce.

### Segmentation and Analytics
Both segment by opens, clicks, location. Brevo offers heatmaps and real-time reporting. Mailchimp's analytics include revenue tracking per campaign.

**Pro Advice**: Use segmentation early. For example, in Brevo, tag subscribers by purchase history for targeted re-engagement—boost open rates by 20-30% typically.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface feels more polished for beginners, with guided tours and a creative vibe. Brevo is straightforward but leans functional—great if you want quick setup without fluff.

**Getting Started Tip**: Import your list via CSV in either. Brevo auto-verifies emails during import, reducing bounces from day one. Mailchimp requires manual list cleaning.

For non-tech users, Mailchimp's templates reduce setup time. Agencies prefer Brevo's sub-accounts for client management.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

- **Brevo**: 300+ integrations (Google Workspace, WordPress, Zapier). Native CRM and e-commerce plugins.
- **Mailchimp**: 250+ apps, strong with Facebook/Instagram ads and Shopify.

Both connect via Zapier for custom workflows. **Advice**: If you're on WordPress, Brevo's plugin handles forms + emails effortlessly. Mailchimp excels in ad retargeting.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Deliverability matters—poor rates kill ROI.
- **Brevo**: 99%+ inbox rates reported, with dedicated IPs on Enterprise. EU-based servers aid GDPR compliance.
- **Mailchimp**: Strong reputation (98%+), but shared IPs on lower plans can hurt new senders.

Both enforce CAN-SPAM/GDPR. **Best Practice**: Warm up your IP gradually (start with 50-100 sends/day). Use double opt-in to maintain lists under 0.5% complaint rates.

## Customer Support

- **Brevo**: Email/chat support on paid plans, 24/7 for Business+. Knowledge base is solid.
- **Mailchimp**: Priority support on Premium only; free users get forums.

Brevo responds faster for growing businesses.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- High-volume senders (newsletters + transactional).
- Multi-channel marketers (SMS/email).
- Budget-conscious teams scaling to 50k+ emails/month.

**Case**: A SaaS company uses Brevo for onboarding sequences + support tickets, saving 40% vs Mailchimp.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Design-heavy brands.
- Beginners needing templates/ads.
- Small lists (<5k contacts).

**Case**: Bloggers leverage Mailchimp's audience insights for content-driven emails.

## Migration: Switching Platforms

Both offer import tools. Export from Mailchimp (CSV/API), import to Brevo seamlessly. Test a small batch first to check formatting.

**Step-by-Step**:
1. Export audience from current tool.
2. Clean data (remove inactives).
3. Map custom fields.
4. Replicate top automations.
5. A/B test new sends.

## Final Verdict: Brevo vs Mailchimp

**Brevo wins for most users in 2024** due to flexible pricing, multi-channel support, and scalability. It's practical for real growth without escalating costs. Mailchimp suits creative, low-volume needs but lags in value.

Assess your needs: Calculate sends/contacts for 6 months. Trial both free plans—Brevo for volume, Mailchimp for design.

Ready to choose? Start with Brevo if versatility matters. Your email ROI depends on the fit.
