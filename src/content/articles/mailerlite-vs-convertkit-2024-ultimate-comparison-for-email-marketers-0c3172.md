# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. For solopreneurs, bloggers, small business owners, and growing teams deciding between MailerLite and ConvertKit, this in-depth comparison breaks down key factors like pricing, features, usability, and support. Both platforms cater to users building email lists for newsletters, courses, or e-commerce, but they shine in different areas. MailerLite offers affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit targets creators with advanced automation. Let's dive in to help you pick the best fit.

## Quick Overview of Each Platform

### What is MailerLite?

MailerLite is a user-friendly email marketing service launched in 2010, popular among beginners and small businesses. It emphasizes drag-and-drop editors, responsive templates, and essential features like automation, landing pages, popups, and forms. With a generous free plan, it's ideal for startups testing the waters. Strengths include low costs, high deliverability (via dedicated IPs on higher plans), and an intuitive interface. Over 2 million users trust it for reliable campaigns.

### What is ConvertKit?

ConvertKit, formerly known as ConvertKit, is creator-centric software designed for bloggers, podcasters, and online course sellers since 2013. It focuses on sequences (automated email series), subscriber tagging, and commerce tools like digital product sales. No traditional drag-and-drop builder—instead, it uses a block-based editor for clean, mobile-friendly emails. It's pricier but offers powerful segmentation and integrations for scaling audiences. Popular among 100,000+ creators for its 'set it and forget it' automations.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is a top decider for your audience. Both use subscriber-based tiers with unlimited email sends on paid plans (timeframe limits apply on free tiers).

### MailerLite Pricing

- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, forms, and landing pages. Perfect for testing.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month (up to 500 subscribers), $20 (2,500), scaling to $310+ for 250,000+. Annual discounts ~20%.
- **Advanced**: From $21/month, adds A/B testing, web push notifications, and priority support.

MailerLite wins on affordability—paid plans are 50-70% cheaper than competitors for similar subs. No contracts; cancel anytime.

### ConvertKit Pricing

- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers (limited to basic broadcasts and forms; no advanced automations).
- **Creator**: $15/month (300 subs), $29 (1,000), up to $779 (50,000+). Annual billing saves 20%.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month start (300 subs), adds revenue tracking, subscriber scoring, and one-click upsells.

ConvertKit lacks a robust free tier for growth and starts higher. Practical tip: Calculate your current list size x growth rate. If under 2,500 subs, MailerLite saves $100s yearly.

**Winner**: MailerLite for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit if you need pro creator tools.

## Ease of Use and Editor Comparison

### MailerLite's Interface

Drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly with 80+ templates. Classic or new editor options. Mobile app for on-the-go management. Dashboard is clean, with quick access to campaigns, automations, and analytics. Onboarding wizards guide setup in minutes.

Practical advice: Import contacts via CSV, segment by engagement, and launch your first campaign in under 30 minutes.

### ConvertKit's Interface

Simpler block-based editor—no drag-drop, but excels at text-heavy newsletters. Visual automation builder uses flowcharts for sequences. Steeper learning curve for tagging system, but tutorials abound.

Practical advice: Use 'subscribe tags' for lead magnets; automate welcome series to nurture new subs.

**Winner**: MailerLite for visual builders; ConvertKit for automation pros.

## Key Features Breakdown

### Automation and Workflows

- **MailerLite**: Workflow builder with if/then triggers (e.g., birthday emails, abandoned carts). Unlimited automations on all plans. Integrates with e-com like Shopify.
- **ConvertKit**: Superior sequences and visual automations. Tag-based triggers (e.g., 'purchased course' tag leads to upsell). Commerce beta for one-click sales.

Tip: For bloggers, ConvertKit's sequences convert 20-30% better per user reports.

### Segmentation and Personalization

- **MailerLite**: Groups, tags, custom fields. Behavioral segmentation (opens/clicks).
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tagging (manual/auto). Subscriber scoring predicts value.

### Landing Pages and Forms

Both offer unlimited pages/forms. MailerLite has popups, surveys; ConvertKit focuses on optimized checkout pages.

### Integrations

- MailerLite: 100+ (Zapier, WordPress, Google Analytics).
- ConvertKit: 50+ native (Gumroad, Teachable, Strong integrations for creators).

**Integrations Tip**: Use Zapier for custom workflows on either.

### Analytics and Deliverability

MailerLite: Open/click rates, revenue tracking, heatmaps. 99% deliverability.
ConvertKit: Advanced reporting, A/B testing on Pro. Strong inbox placement.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 email/chat, extensive academy, Facebook community.
- **ConvertKit**: Email support (faster on Pro), huge creator-focused guides, podcast.

Both score 4.5+ on review sites. MailerLite edges free users.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite Pros
- Cheaper/free plan
- Drag-drop ease
- Built-in websites/popups

Cons: Less creator-specific (no native product sales).

### ConvertKit Pros
- Creator automations
- Tagging power
- Monetization tools

Cons: Higher cost, basic editor.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Small businesses/e-com stores under 5k subs.
- Beginners wanting quick setup.
- Budget-focused teams needing popups/landing pages.

Example: A local shop uses MailerLite for cart recovery, saving 15% on tools.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers/podcasters selling courses.
- Creators with 1k+ engaged subs.
- Teams prioritizing sequences over design.

Example: Newsletter writer automates funnels, boosting revenue 2x.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

**Overall Winner**: Depends on needs. MailerLite for affordability and versatility (best for 70% of small users). ConvertKit for creator scaling (if budget allows).

Practical steps:
1. Audit your list size/needs.
2. Start MailerLite free trial (full features).
3. Test ConvertKit 14-day trial.
4. Migrate via CSV if switching.
5. Track ROI after 3 months.

Both deliver value—focus on consistent sending (weekly newsletters grow lists 4x faster). Whichever you choose, prioritize compliance (GDPR/CAN-SPAM) and value-driven content. Ready to grow? Pick one today!
