# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools trusted by thousands of businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp has long been the go-to for beginners and general marketers, while Omnisend shines in e-commerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll dive into pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and real-world performance. Whether you're a small business owner, e-commerce store manager, or scaling marketer, you'll get practical advice to decide which fits your workflow. Let's break it down.

## Pricing: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both offer free plans, but they differ in scalability and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends—great for testing. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for larger lists (up to 100k+ contacts). Advanced features like automation and A/B testing require the Standard ($20+/month) or Premium ($350+/month) tiers.

**Practical Tip:** If your list is under 2,000, the free plan works well for basic newsletters. Watch out for send limits; exceeding them pauses your account until the next cycle.

### Omnisend Pricing

Omnisend's free plan covers 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, with full access to automation and SMS (limited credits). The Standard plan starts at $16/month for 500 contacts, Pro at $59/month for advanced segmentation. No hard send limits—charged by contacts and credits used.

**Practical Tip:** E-commerce users love Omnisend's pay-per-use SMS; it's cheaper for low-volume texters. Calculate your needs using their pricing calculator to avoid surprises.

**Winner:** Omnisend for e-commerce scaling (better per-contact pricing); Mailchimp for tiny free lists.

## Features: Core Capabilities Compared

Both platforms excel in email, but Omnisend edges out in multichannel.

### Email Templates and Design

Mailchimp offers 100+ drag-and-drop templates, customizable with its editor. High-res images and responsive designs are standard.

Omnisend provides 250+ e-commerce-focused templates (product blocks, countdown timers). Its editor includes dynamic content blocks that pull real product data.

**Practical Advice:** Test templates on mobile—80% of emails are opened there. Use Mailchimp for creative freedom; Omnisend for shoppable emails.

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp's automations include welcome series, abandoned cart (Premium only), and behavioral triggers. Simple builder, but limited branching.

Omnisend's automation is powerhouse: 360° customer profiles, pre-built workflows for cart recovery (80%+ open rates reported), win-back, and cross-sells. Multichannel splits (email to SMS fallback).

**Practical Tip:** Start with Omnisend's templates for quick wins—import Shopify data for personalized carts. In Mailchimp, tag segments manually for similar results.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Mailchimp uses tags, groups, and basic behavioral data. Advanced segmentation needs Premium.

Omnisend auto-segments by purchase history, browsing, and predicted CLV. Hyper-personalization with product recommendations.

**Practical Advice:** Segment by RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) in both. Omnisend automates this; export CSV from Mailchimp for deeper analysis in Google Sheets.

### Omnichannel: Beyond Email

Mailchimp added SMS ($10/1,500 credits) and basic popups. Limited push.

Omnisend bundles email, SMS, push notifications, and web push natively. One dashboard for all.

**Practical Tip:** For DTC brands, Omnisend's SMS open rates (98%) boost revenue 20-30%. Integrate popups early to capture 10-15% more leads.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is intuitive for newbies—minimal learning curve. Extensive help center, tutorials.

Omnisend is user-friendly but e-com tailored; onboarding wizard connects stores in minutes.

**Practical Advice:** Spend 30 mins on Mailchimp's free tour. For Omnisend, link your store first—auto-syncs orders/customers.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp: 300+ apps (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics). Robust API.

Omnisend: Deep e-com focus (Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce). Zapier, 140+ apps.

**Practical Tip:** Use Zapier for custom flows if native lacks. Both scale to millions; Omnisend handles high-volume transactional emails better.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket (paid), chat (higher tiers). Community forums.

Omnisend: 24/7 email/chat (all plans), dedicated managers (Pro). Highly rated (4.7/5 G2).

**Practical Advice:** Test support pre-signup via live chat. Omnisend responds faster for urgent campaigns.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Mailchimp if:** You're a blogger, service business, or solopreneur starting out. Need simple newsletters and don't send SMS.

**Pick Omnisend if:** You run an online store (Shopify/Woo). Want automation that drives sales via email/SMS/push.

**Migration Tip:** Export lists from one to the other via CSV. Rebuild automations—test on 10% segment first to avoid deliverability dips.

## Conclusion

Omnisend vs Mailchimp? Omnisend takes the crown for e-commerce (superior automation, multichannel), while Mailchimp suits generalists with its polish and free tier. Evaluate your goals: revenue-focused? Omnisend. Brand building? Mailchimp.

**Next Steps:** Sign up for both free plans. Run a test campaign to 100 contacts. Track open rates, clicks, and revenue. The data will decide.
