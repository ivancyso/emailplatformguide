# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: 2024 Comparison Guide for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Omnisend against Mailchimp—the two popular choices—helps clarify which tool suits your needs. This in-depth comparison focuses on key aspects like pricing, features, ease of use, and more, tailored for small business owners, ecommerce entrepreneurs, and marketers deciding between these platforms.

Whether you're scaling an online store or nurturing leads, understanding the differences ensures you pick a tool that drives results without unnecessary costs. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing since 2001. It's known for its intuitive interface and robust free plan, making it accessible for beginners. Mailchimp supports email campaigns, automations, landing pages, and social media ads from one dashboard.

**Key strengths:**
- User-friendly drag-and-drop editor.
- Extensive template library.
- Strong analytics for tracking opens, clicks, and revenue.

It's ideal for general businesses, bloggers, and nonprofits. However, as lists grow, pricing can escalate, and advanced ecommerce features lag behind specialized tools.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend, launched in 2014, targets ecommerce brands with multichannel marketing. It excels in email, SMS, and push notifications, integrating seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce.

**Key strengths:**n- Omnichannel campaigns (email + SMS + web push).
- Pre-built workflows for cart abandonment and welcome series.
- Popups and signup forms optimized for conversions.

Omnisend shines for online retailers needing automated recovery flows and personalized messaging across channels.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major decision factor. Both offer free tiers, but structures differ.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month, basic features.
- **Essentials:** $13/month (5,000 contacts), adds automation.
- **Standard:** $20/month (5,000 contacts), includes A/B testing.
- **Premium:** $350+/month (10,000+ contacts), advanced segmentation.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts and sends emails monthly. Overages apply for extra sends.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, core features.
- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts), unlimited emails.
- **Pro:** $59/month (500 contacts), advanced reporting, SMS.
- **Enterprise:** Custom, for high-volume needs.

Omnisend's plans are contact-based with unlimited emails in paid tiers—a boon for frequent senders. SMS credits are add-ons.

**Practical advice:** For under 500 contacts, start free on either. Ecommerce stores sending weekly newsletters benefit from Omnisend's unlimited sends. Budget-conscious solopreneurs may prefer Mailchimp's lower entry paid plan.

| Feature | Mailchimp | Omnisend |

| Paid Starts | $13/mo | $16/mo |

## Feature Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly with 100+ responsive templates. Customization is straightforward, but code access requires higher plans.

Omnisend offers a similar editor with ecommerce-focused templates (product blocks, dynamic content). It pulls product data automatically from integrations.

**Winner:** Tie for ease; Omnisend for product-heavy emails.

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp provides single-step automations on lower plans, branching on higher ones. Classic automations include welcome series and re-engagement.

Omnisend's workflows are visual and ecommerce-centric: abandoned cart (email + SMS), back-in-stock alerts, cross-sells. Supports multi-channel triggers.

**Practical advice:** Use Omnisend for cart recovery—set up a workflow in minutes to recover 10-20% of lost sales via automated emails/SMS.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Both segment by behavior, location, and tags. Mailchimp uses behavioral triggers; Omnisend adds product-based segments (e.g., viewed items).

Omnisend's dynamic content blocks personalize based on purchase history without coding.

### Multichannel Capabilities

Mailchimp focuses on email + ads. Omnisend adds SMS (high open rates ~98%) and web push—crucial for mobile shoppers.

**Practical tip:** For DTC brands, Omnisend's SMS automation boosts urgency (e.g., "Your cart expires in 1 hour").

### Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp tracks ROI, opens, clicks. Omnisend emphasizes revenue per recipient, campaign ROI, and cross-channel attribution.

## Ease of Use

Mailchimp's interface feels polished for non-tech users. Onboarding includes tutorials.

Omnisend is equally intuitive but ecommerce-oriented—quick setup if you use Shopify.

**Advice:** Test both free plans. Mailchimp suits content creators; Omnisend clicks for store owners.

## Integrations

Mailchimp: 300+ (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).

Omnisend: 100+, excels with ecommerce (Shopify, Magento) and SMS gateways.

**Tip:** Check native integrations first to avoid Zapier costs.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket (paid), chat (higher plans), vast knowledge base.

Omnisend: 24/7 live chat/email, dedicated manager (Pro+), active Facebook community.

Omnisend edges out for responsive help.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros:**
- Generous free plan.
- All-in-one (forms, sites, ads).
- Mature platform.

**Cons:**
- Send limits inflate costs.
- Ecommerce automations basic.

### Omnisend
**Pros:**
- Unlimited emails.
- Multichannel focus.
- Ecommerce power.

**Cons:**
- Smaller free plan.
- Less general-purpose.

## Which Should You Choose?

- **Choose Mailchimp if:** General marketing, small lists, budget free tier, need ads/landing pages.
- **Choose Omnisend if:** Ecommerce store, want SMS/push, recovery automations, unlimited sends.

**Practical advice:** Audit your needs. For Shopify users, Omnisend installs in 5 minutes. Migrate via CSV exports. A/B test campaigns post-switch to measure uplift.

## Conclusion

Omnisend vs Mailchimp boils down to your business type. Mailchimp offers broad appeal and simplicity; Omnisend delivers targeted ecommerce firepower. Both are reliable—no platform is "perfect," but matching features to goals maximizes ROI.

Start with free trials: sign up, import 100 contacts, build a test automation. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and conversions. For growing ecommerce, Omnisend's edge in multichannel often justifies the switch.
