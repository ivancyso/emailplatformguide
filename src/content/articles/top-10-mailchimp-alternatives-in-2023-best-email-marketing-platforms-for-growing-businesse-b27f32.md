# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2023: Best Email Marketing Platforms for Growing Businesses

Mailchimp has long been a popular choice for email marketing, but as businesses grow, many users find its pricing structure, limited automation, or customer support lacking. If you're searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**, you're not alone. With rising costs—especially after Mailchimp's 2023 pricing changes—and the need for advanced features like deeper automations, segmentation, and e-commerce integrations, switching platforms can save money and boost results.

In this roundup, we'll cover the top 10 Mailchimp alternatives tailored for small to medium businesses, solopreneurs, and e-commerce stores. Each includes key features, pricing basics, pros/cons, and practical tips for implementation. We'll focus on real-world usability to help you decide based on your audience size, budget, and goals.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp? Practical Reasons to Explore Alternatives

Before diving into options, consider these common pain points:

- **Pricing jumps**: Mailchimp's free plan limits you to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month. Paid plans scale quickly, with Essentials at $13/month for 500 contacts but no automation.
- **Automation limits**: Basic plans lack site tracking or advanced triggers.
- **Deliverability and support**: Some users report inbox placement issues and slow support.

Alternatives often offer better value: unlimited sends, advanced automations from day one, or e-commerce focus. A switch can reduce costs by 30-50% while improving open rates through better segmentation.

**Practical tip**: Audit your current Mailchimp usage. Export contact lists and campaigns to compare import ease. Most alternatives support CSV imports seamlessly.

## 1. ActiveCampaign: Best for Advanced Automation

### Key Features
ActiveCampaign shines in marketing automation with over 750 integrations, including Shopify and WordPress. Build complex customer journeys with if/then branching, predictive sending, and machine learning for content recommendations.

### Pricing
Starts at $29/month (Starter, 500 contacts). Scales to $149/month (Plus) for sales CRM.

### Pros
- Deep automation without coding.
- Excellent split testing and personalization.
- Strong reporting on revenue per email.

### Cons
- Steeper learning curve for beginners.

**Practical advice**: Use the free migration service. Start with a welcome series automation to nurture leads—expect 20-30% higher engagement than Mailchimp basics.

## 2. Klaviyo: Ideal for E-commerce

### Key Features
Built for Shopify/WooCommerce, Klaviyo offers flow automations (abandoned cart, post-purchase), revenue tracking, and A/B testing on subject lines.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 sends. Paid from $20/month based on contacts.

### Pros
- E-commerce metrics like ROAS (return on ad spend).
- Integrates natively with 350+ apps.
- High deliverability (98%+ claimed).

### Cons
- Less suited for non-e-com B2B.

**Practical advice**: Install the Klaviyo pixel on your site for behavioral data. Set up abandoned cart flows immediately—recover 10-15% of lost sales.

## 3. ConvertKit: Perfect for Creators and Bloggers

### Key Features
Creator-focused with visual automations, tagging, and landing pages. Sell digital products directly via emails.

### Pricing
Free up to 300 subscribers. Creator plan $29/month (500 subs).

### Pros
- Simple interface, no bloat.
- Unlimited emails/sends.
- Strong subscriber scoring.

### Cons
- Limited templates compared to Mailchimp.

**Practical advice**: Tag subscribers by content downloads. Automate sequences for lead magnets—great for newsletters with 40%+ open rates.

## 4. GetResponse: All-in-One Marketing Suite

### Key Features
Email, webinars, landing pages, and funnels. AI-driven optimization for send times.

### Pricing
Free up to 500 contacts. Email Marketing $19/month (1,000 contacts).

### Pros
- Conversion funnel builder.

### Cons
- Interface feels dated.

**Practical advice**: Use webinar integrations for lead gen. Test AI send times to boost opens by 15-20%.

## 5. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue): Budget-Friendly with SMS

### Key Features
Transactional + marketing emails, SMS, chat. Unlimited contacts on free plan.

### Pricing
Free (300 emails/day). Starter $25/month.

### Pros
- Multi-channel (email + SMS).
- High deliverability.

### Cons
- Automation limits on lower plans.

**Practical advice**: Combine SMS with email for cart recovery—doubles response rates.

## 6. MailerLite: Simple and Affordable

### Key Features
Drag-and-drop editor, popups, automation. Website builder included.

### Pricing
Free up to 1,000 subs/12,000 emails. Growing Business $10/month.

### Pros
- Fast setup.
- A/B testing free.

### Cons
- Fewer advanced e-com features.

**Practical advice**: Embed popups on high-traffic pages. Segment by engagement for targeted re-engagement campaigns.

## 7. AWeber: Reliable for Small Businesses

### Key Features
Templates, automations, landing pages. Strong tagging system.

### Pricing
Lite $12.50/month (500 subs).

### Pros
- 24/7 support.
- Easy imports.

### Cons
- No free plan.

**Practical advice**: Use legacy automations for quick wins. Focus on list cleaning quarterly.

## 8. Constant Contact: Beginner-Friendly

### Key Features
Event management, social ads. AI email generator.

### Pricing
Lite $12/month (500 contacts).

### Pros
- Intuitive editor.
- Local business tools.

### Cons
- Expensive at scale.

**Practical advice**: Leverage social integrations for cross-promotion.

## 9. Omnisend: E-commerce Powerhouse

### Key Features
Omnichannel (email, SMS, push). Product picks in emails.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts. Standard $16/month.

### Pros
- Pre-built workflows.

### Cons
- E-com focused.

**Practical advice**: Automate post-purchase upsells.

## 10. Drip: Advanced E-commerce Automation

### Key Features
Behavioral triggers, revenue tracking. Shopify focus.

### Pricing
$39/month (500 subs).

### Pros
- Workflow builder.

### Cons
- Pricier.

**Practical advice**: Track UTM sources for attribution.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Match your needs**: E-com? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Automation? ActiveCampaign.
2. **Budget check**: Calculate based on contacts (e.g., 5,000 subs: ActiveCampaign ~$70 vs. Mailchimp $75+).
3. **Trial everything**: Most offer 14-30 day trials.
4. **Migration steps**:
   - Export lists from Mailchimp (CSV).
   - Import and map fields.
   - Recreate top campaigns.
   - Update website forms (plugins like Zapier help).
   - Test sends to avoid bounces.

**Pro tip**: Monitor deliverability post-switch with tools like GlockApps. Aim for 95%+ inbox rates.
