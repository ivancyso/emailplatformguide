# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Tool Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With countless options available, two giants stand out: Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp. Both cater to businesses of all sizes, from solopreneurs to enterprises, but they differ in pricing, features, and scalability.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down key aspects like pricing, ease of use, automation capabilities, integrations, and customer support. Whether you're migrating from one to the other or starting fresh, this guide provides practical insights based on real user experiences and official documentation to help you decide.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing tool. Both Brevo and Mailchimp offer free plans, but their paid tiers vary significantly.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo uses a contact-based pricing model with no send limits on most plans, which is ideal for high-volume senders.

- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts, basic features.
- **Starter**: $25/month for 20,000 emails/month, 500 contacts.
- **Business**: Starts at $65/month for 20,000 emails, with advanced automation.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume needs.

Brevo shines for transactional emails and SMS, bundling them affordably. For example, adding SMS marketing doesn't spike costs dramatically.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp charges based on contacts and features, with monthly send limits.

- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts, 5x contacts in sends.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, with automation.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced segmentation.

Mailchimp's free plan is more restrictive for growing lists. Costs escalate quickly as your audience expands—e.g., 10,000 contacts on Standard costs around $100/month.

**Practical Advice**: If you have a large, engaged list and send frequently, Brevo's model saves money (e.g., 10k contacts might cost $25 vs Mailchimp's $75+). Test free plans first to gauge fit.

## Ease of Use and Interface

User-friendliness matters for non-technical marketers.

### Brevo's Drag-and-Drop Editor
Brevo's editor is intuitive, with a clean dashboard resembling modern tools like Canva. New users can build campaigns in under 10 minutes. It supports responsive templates out-of-the-box and A/B testing on higher plans.

### Mailchimp's Classic Editor
Mailchimp's interface is polished but can feel cluttered with legacy features. The drag-and-drop builder is powerful for complex designs, but beginners report a steeper learning curve due to nested menus.

**Practical Advice**: Start with Brevo if you're a solo marketer prioritizing speed. Mailchimp suits design-heavy teams willing to invest time. Both offer tutorials—Brevo's video library is more concise (under 5 minutes per topic).

## Core Features Comparison

### Email Templates and Design
- **Brevo**: 48+ free responsive templates, customizable blocks. Strong for e-commerce with product blocks.
- **Mailchimp**: 100+ templates, excels in creative freedom with code-your-own options.

Brevo edges out for quick setups; Mailchimp for branded depth.

### Automation and Workflows
Both support drip campaigns, but:
- **Brevo**: Unlimited automations on paid plans, including webhooks and transactional triggers. Great for abandoned cart sequences.
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journeys on Standard+ plans, with branching logic. More visual but limited to 50,000 sends/month on mid-tier.

**Practical Tip**: Use Brevo for sales funnels (e.g., trigger emails on form submissions). Mailchimp for nurture sequences with behavioral triggers.

### List Management and Segmentation
- **Brevo**: Unlimited contacts on paid plans, dynamic segments based on behavior.
- **Mailchimp**: Tags and groups, advanced with Premium merge fields.

Brevo handles large lists without extra fees, perfect for scaling.

### SMS and Multichannel
Brevo integrates SMS natively (pay-per-use, ~$0.01/message), plus WhatsApp on Enterprise. Mailchimp offers SMS on Premium ($10+/month base).

## Integrations and Deliverability

### Integrations
- **Brevo**: 300+ via Zapier, native with Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics. API is developer-friendly.
- **Mailchimp**: 250+ apps, strong e-commerce (WooCommerce, BigCommerce).

Both sync seamlessly with CRMs like HubSpot.

### Deliverability Rates
Brevo reports 99%+ inbox rates with dedicated IPs on Business+. Mailchimp averages 97-98%, bolstered by its massive sender network. Monitor your domain's reputation—warm up IPs gradually on either.

**Practical Advice**: Integrate with Google Analytics for ROI tracking. Use Brevo's IP warming guide for new senders to hit 95%+ delivery from day one.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **Brevo**: 24/7 chat/email on paid plans, extensive knowledge base, free migrations.
- **Mailchimp**: Email/ticket support (chat on Premium), vast blog/academy.

Brevo responds faster (under 1 hour), ideal for urgent issues.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- Growing e-commerce stores needing SMS/email bundles.
- Budget-conscious teams with large lists.
- Businesses focused on automation and transactional emails.

**Pro Tip**: Leverage Brevo's free account migration to switch painlessly—export/import lists in CSV format.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Creative agencies building stunning newsletters.
- Brands with complex audience segments.
- Users invested in Mailchimp's ecosystem (e.g., landing pages).

**Pro Tip**: Optimize sends with Mailchimp's predictive analytics to boost open rates by 20-30%.

## Conclusion: Your Next Steps

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to priorities: Brevo wins for cost-effective scaling and multichannel (especially under 10k contacts), while Mailchimp excels in polished design and established tools for creative campaigns.

**Recommendation**: Trial both free plans for 14 days. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%), click-through (2-5%), and unsubscribes (<0.5%). For most small-to-medium businesses, Brevo offers better long-term value in 2024.
