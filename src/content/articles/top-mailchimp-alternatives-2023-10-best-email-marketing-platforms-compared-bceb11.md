# Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp has long been a popular choice for email marketing, but as businesses grow, many users seek alternatives due to pricing changes, limited automation features, or better integrations. In 2023, Mailchimp's free plan limits and higher costs for advanced features push users toward more flexible options. This guide compares 10 top Mailchimp alternatives, focusing on practical aspects like ease of migration, deliverability, and scalability for small businesses, e-commerce stores, and agencies.

We'll break down each platform's key strengths, pricing (based on public data as of late 2023), pros/cons, and who it's best for. All recommendations are based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, emphasizing tools with strong deliverability rates above 95%.

## ActiveCampaign: Best for Advanced Automation

### Key Features
ActiveCampaign excels in marketing automation with deep CRM integration, behavioral tracking, and site messaging. It offers split testing, dynamic content, and over 900 app integrations via Zapier.

### Pricing
Starts at $29/month for 500 contacts (Lite plan). No free plan, but 14-day trial.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Powerful automations save time; excellent reporting; high deliverability (~99%).
**Cons:** Steep learning curve; pricier for large lists.

### Who It's For
E-commerce brands and agencies needing CRM-like features. Practical tip: Use its automation recipes to replicate Mailchimp journeys—import segments via CSV for quick setup.

## ConvertKit (Now Kit): Creator-Focused Simplicity

### Key Features
Tailored for creators with landing pages, forms, subscriber tagging, and visual automations. Native commerce tools for selling digital products.

### Pricing
Free for up to 300 subscribers; Creator plan $29/month for 500.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Intuitive interface; unlimited emails; strong for newsletters.
**Cons:** Limited e-commerce beyond basics; fewer enterprise features.

### Who It's For
Bloggers, podcasters, and solopreneurs. Advice: Migrate by exporting Mailchimp audiences and using ConvertKit's tagging to segment—test with a small group first to check deliverability.

## Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue): Affordable All-in-Rounder

### Key Features
Transactional emails, SMS, chat, and CRM in one. Advanced segmentation, A/B testing, and API access.

### Pricing
Free for 300 emails/day; Starter $25/month for 20k emails.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Generous free tier; multi-channel; EU-based for GDPR ease.
**Cons:** Interface feels dated; support slower on free plan.

### Who It's For
Startups on budgets. Tip: Leverage its email validation to clean Mailchimp lists pre-migration, reducing bounce rates.

## Klaviyo: E-Commerce Powerhouse

### Key Features
AI-driven flows, revenue tracking, and Shopify/WooCommerce integrations. Predictive analytics for churn prediction.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; scales with revenue (e.g., $20/month base + %).

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Unmatched e-com ROI; dynamic product recommendations.
**Cons:** E-com focused; overkill for non-shops.

### Who It's For
Online stores. Practical advice: Connect Klaviyo to your store first, then sync Mailchimp data—focus on abandoned cart flows for quick wins.

## MailerLite: Budget-Friendly with Drag-and-Drop

### Key Features
Popups, landing pages, automation, and A/B testing. Website builder included.

### Pricing
Free for 1,000 subscribers/12k emails; Growing Business $10/month.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Cheap scaling; fast setup; high deliverability.
**Cons:** Basic reporting; no native CRM.

### Who It's For
Small businesses/SMBs. Tip: Use its migration tool to import directly from Mailchimp—customize templates to match branding instantly.

## GetResponse: Webinar and Conversion Tools

### Key Features
Email, funnels, webinars (up to 1,000 attendees), and e-com storefronts.

### Pricing
Free trial; Email Marketing $19/month for 1,000 contacts.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** All-in-one sales tools; conversion funnels.
**Cons:** Clunky mobile editor; upsells.

### Who It's For
Course creators/marketers. Advice: Test webinars post-migration to engage lists effectively.

## AWeber: Reliable for Long-Term Users

### Key Features
Drag-and-drop editor, tagging, and integrations. Strong focus on deliverability.

### Pricing
Lite $12.50/month for 500 subscribers.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** 20+ years experience; easy lists.
**Cons:** Dated design; limited automations.

### Who It's For
Non-profits/small lists. Tip: Segment by engagement scores from Mailchimp exports.

## Constant Contact: Beginner-Friendly

### Key Features
Event management, social ads, and AI content generator.

### Pricing
Lite $12/month for 500 contacts.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** US-based support; simple.
**Cons:** Expensive scaling; basic automations.

### Who It's For
Local businesses. Advice: Use AI tools for quick campaigns.

## Drip: Behavioral Marketing

### Key Features
Workflows based on user actions, revenue tracking.

### Pricing
Starts at $39/month for 500 contacts.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** E-com automations; customization.
**Cons:** No free plan.

### Who It's For
Advanced e-com. Tip: Map Mailchimp behaviors to Drip workflows.

## Omnisend: Omnichannel E-Com

### Key Features
SMS, push, email with Shopify focus.

### Pricing
Free for 250 contacts/500 emails; Standard $16/month.

### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Multi-channel; templates.
**Cons:** E-com centric.

### Who It's For
Stores wanting SMS. Advice: Start with omnichannel tests.

## How to Choose and Migrate from Mailchimp

1. **Assess Needs:** List size? E-com? Automation depth?
2. **Trial Multiple:** Most offer 14-30 days free.
3. **Migrate Data:** Export CSV from Mailchimp (Audience > Export). Import to new tool; verify segments.
4. **Test Deliverability:** Send to test lists; monitor spam rates.
5. **Train Team:** Use tutorials; start simple.

Practical checklist: Backup lists, notify subscribers of change, update website forms.

## Conclusion

Switching from Mailchimp doesn't have to be daunting. For most, MailerLite or Brevo offer the best value; e-com users pick Klaviyo or Omnisend. Evaluate based on your audience size and goals—always trial before committing. These alternatives provide comparable or superior features at often lower costs, helping you scale email marketing effectively in 2023. Ready to migrate?
