[Omnisend](/omnisend-review) and Mailchimp are both popular email marketing platforms, but they come from different worlds. Omnisend is purpose-built for ecommerce stores. Mailchimp is a general-purpose marketing platform that supports ecommerce among many other use cases.

If you run an online store, here is how they compare.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Omnisend | Mailchimp |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Best for** | Ecommerce stores | General marketing |
| **Free plan** | 250 contacts, 500 emails | 500 contacts, 1K emails |
| **SMS marketing** | Built-in | Add-on |
| **Push notifications** | Yes | No |
| **Product picker** | Yes | Basic |
| **Pre-built ecommerce flows** | Yes | Limited |
| **CRM** | No | Basic |
| **Starting price** | $16/mo | $13/mo |
| **Shopify integration** | Native | Third-party |

## Ecommerce Features: Omnisend Wins

This is Omnisend's core strength. Every feature is designed around selling products online.

**Omnisend ecommerce features:**
- Pre-built automation workflows (cart abandonment, browse abandonment, post-purchase, welcome, win-back)
- Product picker that pulls items directly from your store catalog
- Dynamic discount codes in emails
- Product recommendation blocks
- SMS and push notifications in the same automation flow
- Back-in-stock notifications

**Mailchimp ecommerce features:**
- Product recommendations (paid plans)
- Purchase-based automation triggers
- Revenue reporting
- Basic cart abandonment emails
- Shopify integration via third-party connector

Mailchimp supports ecommerce but it is not built around it. You can create cart abandonment emails, but the setup requires more manual configuration compared to Omnisend's pre-built flows.

**Winner:** Omnisend.

## Multi-Channel Marketing: Omnisend Wins

Omnisend combines email, SMS, and web push notifications in a single platform. You can build automation workflows that send an email first, follow up with an SMS if unopened, and trigger a push notification as a final touchpoint — all in one flow.

Mailchimp recently added SMS as a paid add-on, but it is not as deeply integrated into the automation builder. Push notifications are not available.

**Winner:** Omnisend.

## Ease of Use: Mailchimp Wins

Mailchimp's interface is familiar to millions of users. The onboarding is smooth, the dashboard is clean, and sending a campaign takes minutes. For general email marketing, the user experience is hard to beat.

Omnisend's interface is focused on ecommerce workflows, which makes it efficient for store owners but can feel narrow for non-ecommerce tasks. The learning curve is still gentle, but Mailchimp is more intuitive for first-time users.

**Winner:** Mailchimp.

## Email Design

**Mailchimp** offers 100+ templates and one of the best drag-and-drop editors in the industry. Brand kit, Content Optimizer, and creative tools make it a strong design platform.

**Omnisend** has fewer templates but includes ecommerce-specific content blocks: product picker, discount codes, and product recommendation widgets. The editor is functional and clean but not as polished as Mailchimp's.

**Winner:** Mailchimp for general design. Omnisend for ecommerce-specific elements.

## Pricing

| Contacts | Omnisend (Standard) | Mailchimp (Standard) |
|----------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 500 | $16/mo | $13/mo |
| 1,000 | $20/mo | $26/mo |
| 5,000 | $65/mo | $75/mo |
| 10,000 | $115/mo | $110/mo |
| 25,000 | $209/mo | $230/mo |
| 50,000 | $330/mo | $385/mo |

Pricing is close at lower tiers, but Omnisend is cheaper at 1,000+ contacts — and includes SMS and push notifications in the price. Mailchimp charges extra for SMS.

At 50,000 contacts, Omnisend saves $660/year before accounting for Mailchimp's SMS add-on costs.

**Winner:** Omnisend (better value when SMS is included).

## Reporting and Analytics

Mailchimp offers deeper general analytics: audience demographics, click maps, engagement scoring, social media performance, and predictive insights on higher tiers.

Omnisend's reporting is focused on ecommerce metrics: revenue per campaign, revenue per automation flow, conversion rates, and product performance. It tells you exactly how much money each email made.

**Winner:** Mailchimp for general analytics. Omnisend for revenue-focused reporting.

## Shopify Integration

Omnisend has a native Shopify integration with one-click installation, automatic product syncing, and pre-built workflows that activate immediately.

Mailchimp's native Shopify integration was removed in 2019. You now need a third-party connector (like ShopSync) which adds cost and complexity. Data syncing is not as seamless.

**Winner:** Omnisend.

## The Verdict

**Omnisend** is the better choice for ecommerce stores. It is purpose-built for online selling with pre-built flows, SMS, push notifications, and native Shopify integration. For most store owners, it delivers more ecommerce value than Mailchimp at a similar or lower price.

**Mailchimp** is the better choice for general marketing. If your business is not primarily ecommerce, Mailchimp's broader feature set — CRM, design tools, analytics — makes it more versatile.

If you run an online store, start with Omnisend. If you run a service business or content site, start with Mailchimp. Both offer free plans to test.
