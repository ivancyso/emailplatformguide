# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With thousands of platforms available, two stand out for small to medium-sized businesses: Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp. Both offer robust features for building lists, sending campaigns, and automating workflows. But which one fits your needs? This Brevo vs Mailchimp comparison dives into pricing, features, usability, and real-world performance to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur launching your first newsletter or a growing team scaling automations, we'll break it down with practical advice based on user experiences and platform capabilities.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo is an all-in-one marketing platform emphasizing affordability and multichannel communication. It started as a transactional email service and evolved into a full CRM with email, SMS, chat, and ads.

**Key Strengths:**
- Unlimited contacts in free/paid plans (pay per email sent).
- Built-in SMS and WhatsApp integration.
- Advanced automation with triggers like abandoned cart.

Practical tip: If your business involves e-commerce, Brevo's transactional emails (e.g., order confirmations) integrate seamlessly with Shopify or WooCommerce via API. Start with their free plan to test list imports without limits.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple since 2001, known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and beginner-friendly interface. It's ideal for content creators and nonprofits with its creative templates.

**Key Strengths:**n- Excellent audience segmentation and A/B testing.
- Extensive template library (over 100 free options).
- Integrations with 300+ apps like Google Analytics and Facebook.

Practical tip: Use Mailchimp's landing page builder for lead magnets. Import your CSV contacts and segment by engagement to boost open rates—aim for personalized subject lines like "[Name], your exclusive offer awaits."

## Pricing Comparison: Brevo vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major decider. Here's a side-by-side (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates):

| Feature | Brevo | Mailchimp |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Free Plan | 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts | 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month |
| Starter | $25/mo (20k emails) | $13/mo (500 contacts, 5k emails) |
| Mid-Tier | $65/mo (100k emails) | $20/mo (50k contacts, unlimited emails) |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom |

Brevo shines for growing lists—pay only for sends, not contacts. Mailchimp caps free users quickly but offers predictable monthly pricing.

**Advice:** Calculate your monthly sends. If under 10k emails with a large list, Brevo saves money. For high-volume senders with small lists, Mailchimp's Essentials plan is cost-effective. Track ROI by monitoring cost per acquisition (CPA) post-campaign.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Ease of Use
Mailchimp wins for beginners with its visual editor—no coding needed. Brevo's editor is solid but slightly steeper for automation setups.

**Tip:** Spend 15 minutes in each demo account. Mailchimp feels like Canva; Brevo like a CRM dashboard.

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp: 200+ responsive templates, custom HTML option.
Brevo: 40+ templates, strong mobile optimization.

**Practical Advice:** Customize with your brand colors. Test on Litmus or Email on Acid for cross-client rendering. Use dynamic content blocks for personalization.

### Automation and Workflows
Brevo: Multi-step journeys with wait nodes, SMS branches.
Mailchimp: Classic automations (welcome series, re-engagement).

**Tip:** Set up a 7-day nurture sequence. Brevo's split testing in automations gives data-driven tweaks—e.g., if SMS boosts conversions 20%, prioritize it.

### Analytics and Reporting
Both offer open/click rates, bounce handling. Mailchimp's heatmaps show click paths; Brevo tracks revenue per email.

**Advice:** Integrate Google Analytics UTM tags. Benchmark opens at 20-30%; aim higher with clean lists (remove inactives quarterly).

### Integrations and CRM
Brevo: Native CRM, Zapier, WordPress plugin.
Mailchimp: Shopify, Eventbrite, deeper e-com focus.

**Tip:** For WooCommerce users, Brevo's API syncs orders instantly. Mailchimp's MC API suits developers.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

Brevo: 24/7 chat/email, phone for premium. Deliverability ~98% (IP warming advised).
Mailchimp: Email/ticket only (free), priority for paid. Industry-leading 99%+ deliverability.

**Practical Advice:** Warm IPs by starting with 50 sends/day, ramp up. Use double opt-in to maintain sender reputation—monitor via Google Postmaster Tools.

## Pros and Cons

**Brevo Pros:**
- Cost-effective scaling.
- Multichannel (email + SMS).
- No contact limits.

**Brevo Cons:**
- Fewer templates.
- Automation learning curve.

**Mailchimp Pros:**
- User-friendly.
- Creative tools.
- Strong community resources.

**Mailchimp Cons:**
- Contact-based pricing hurts growth.
- Limited SMS.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- E-commerce stores needing transactional emails/SMS.
- Businesses with large lists on budgets.
- Teams wanting CRM integration.

**Migration Tip:** Export Mailchimp lists as CSV, import to Brevo. Remap automations in 2-3 hours.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Beginners/content marketers.
- Nonprofits (discounts available).
- Brands prioritizing design.

**Scaling Tip:** Upgrade before hitting limits; use tags for segmentation.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to priorities: Brevo for affordability and multichannel power, Mailchimp for simplicity and creativity. Test both free plans—send a sample campaign to 100 contacts and compare metrics.

For most SMBs, start with Brevo if scaling lists; Mailchimp if ease trumps cost. Track KPIs like open rates (target 25%), CTR (3-5%), and unsubscribes (<0.5%). Whichever you pick, consistent testing and list hygiene drive success.
