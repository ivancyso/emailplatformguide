# Omnisend Review 2023: Is It the Best Email Marketing Tool for Your Ecommerce Store?

If you're an ecommerce business owner scouting for an email marketing platform, Omnisend often pops up in recommendations. Designed specifically for online stores, it combines email, SMS, and push notifications into one omnichannel solution. In this detailed Omnisend review, we'll break down its features, pricing, usability, and real-world performance to help you decide if it's right for your needs.

Whether you're on Shopify, WooCommerce, or another platform, Omnisend aims to boost revenue through targeted campaigns and automations. We'll cover practical setup tips, strengths, limitations, and comparisons—no hype, just facts based on its official capabilities and user feedback.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an ecommerce-focused marketing automation platform launched in 2014. It caters to over 100,000 stores worldwide, integrating seamlessly with major platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, and Magento. Unlike generalist tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes omnichannel messaging: emails, SMS, web push notifications, and popups.

Key differentiator: Its pre-built workflows for common ecommerce scenarios, such as cart abandonment, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells. This makes it ideal for stores wanting to recover lost sales without coding expertise.

**Practical Advice:** Start by connecting your store via a one-click integration. Omnisend pulls product data, customer info, and order history automatically, saving hours of manual setup.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs robust tools for growing ecommerce brands. Here's a deep dive into standout features with tips on using them effectively.

### Drag-and-Drop Email Builder

The visual editor lets you create responsive emails without HTML knowledge. It includes ecommerce-specific blocks like product carousels, dynamic discount codes, and countdown timers.

**Practical Tip:** Use the 'Product' block to showcase recently viewed items. Test mobile previews—over 50% of opens happen on phones. A/B test subject lines like "Your Cart Misses You" vs. "Complete Your Purchase" to lift open rates by 20-30%.

### Advanced Automations

Omnisend shines here with 200+ templates for workflows like:
- **Cart Recovery:** Triggers emails/SMS 1-3 hours post-abandonment.
- **Browse Abandonment:** Targets users who viewed but didn't add to cart.
- **Win-Back Campaigns:** Re-engages inactive subscribers.

**Practical Advice:** Set up the Cart Recovery workflow first—it's responsible for 15-20% revenue recovery in many stores. Customize delays (e.g., email at 1h, SMS at 24h) and segment by cart value for higher conversions. Monitor the dashboard's revenue attribution to tweak timing.

### Omnichannel Messaging

Beyond email:
- **SMS:** Two-way messaging with opt-in compliance (TCPA/GDPR).
- **Web Push:** Browser notifications for cart reminders.
- **Popups & Forms:** On-site capture with gamified wheels or spin-to-win.

**Tip:** Combine channels—send email first, follow with SMS for 6x higher open rates. Use popups on exit-intent to grow lists ethically, targeting 5-10% conversion rates.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Segment by purchase history, behavior, or custom attributes. Hyper-unique personalization pulls first name, last order, or recommended products.

**Practical Advice:** Create segments like "VIPs" (3+ purchases) for exclusive offers. Use dynamic content blocks to show personalized product recs, boosting click-through rates by 2-3x.

### Analytics and Reporting

Track ROI with campaign revenue, unsubscribe rates, and customer lifetime value (CLV). Integrates with Google Analytics.

**Tip:** Focus on the 'Revenue per Recipient' metric. Aim for $0.50+ per email sent; adjust content if lower.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-centric: Deep store integrations and product data sync.
- Strong automations: Easy setup, high ROI potential.
- Omnichannel: Unified inbox for all channels.
- Generous free plan and intuitive UI.
- 24/7 support via chat/email.

### Cons
- Limited non-ecommerce features (e.g., no event management).
- SMS costs add up for high-volume senders ($0.01-$0.03/message).
- Learning curve for advanced segmentation.
- No native landing pages (use integrations like Unbounce).

**User Feedback Note:** Trustpilot rates it 4.6/5 from 1,000+ reviews, praising ease but noting occasional sync delays with non-Shopify stores.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Perfect for:
- Shopify/WooCommerce stores under $1M revenue.
- Brands prioritizing automation over basic newsletters.
- Teams wanting SMS/push without multiple tools.

Not ideal for B2B lead gen or massive enterprises needing white-labeling.

**Practical Advice:** If your average order value (AOV) is $50+, automations can pay for itself quickly. Calculate potential: 10% recovery on 100 abandoned carts/month at $50 AOV = $500 revenue.
