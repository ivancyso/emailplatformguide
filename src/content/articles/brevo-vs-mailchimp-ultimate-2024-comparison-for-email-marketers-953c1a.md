# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are popular platforms designed for businesses of all sizes, offering automation, templates, and analytics. This in-depth comparison breaks down key aspects like pricing, features, usability, and support to help you decide. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or scaling marketer, we'll provide practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo is an all-in-one marketing platform that excels in email, SMS, chat, and CRM tools. It's particularly strong for transactional emails alongside marketing campaigns. Launched as Sendinblue in 2012 and rebranded in 2023, Brevo targets cost-conscious users with unlimited contacts on most plans.

**Key Strengths:**
- Generous free plan (300 emails/day).
- Built-in transactional email API.
- Multi-channel support (email, SMS, WhatsApp).

Practical tip: If your business sends order confirmations or invoices, Brevo's SMTP relay is reliable and scales well without extra costs.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, founded in 2001, is a household name in email marketing with a freemium model. It's known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and robust audience segmentation. Mailchimp serves over 13 million users and integrates deeply with e-commerce platforms like Shopify.

**Key Strengths:**
- Beginner-friendly interface.
- Advanced automation journeys.
- Extensive template library.

Practical tip: Start with Mailchimp's free tier if you're testing email marketing for the first time—it's great for building your initial subscriber list.

## Pricing Comparison: Brevo vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major decider. Here's a side-by-side look at their plans as of 2024 (always check official sites for updates).

### Brevo Pricing
- **Free:** 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts.
- **Starter:** $25/mo for 20K emails/mo.
- **Business:** $65/mo for 20K emails/mo + advanced features.
- **Enterprise:** Custom.

Brevo charges per email volume, not contacts, making it cheaper for large lists.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** 500 contacts, 1K emails/mo.
- **Essentials:** $13/mo for 500 contacts, 5K emails.
- **Standard:** $20/mo for 500 contacts + automation.
- **Premium:** $350/mo for advanced reporting.

Mailchimp limits by contacts, which can get expensive as your list grows.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your monthly sends and list size. For 10K contacts sending 50K emails/mo, Brevo Business ($65) beats Mailchimp Standard (~$100+). Use Brevo's pricing calculator to simulate your needs.

## Feature Comparison

Both platforms offer core features, but differences shine in specifics.

### Email Builder and Templates
- **Brevo:** Drag-and-drop editor with responsive templates. Custom HTML support.
- **Mailchimp:** Superior creative tools, including AI-generated content and 100+ industry templates.

Winner: Mailchimp for design newbies.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Brevo:** Basic automations (welcome series, abandoned cart) plus API triggers.
- **Mailchimp:** Advanced customer journeys with branching logic and behavioral triggers.

Practical tip: Map your customer lifecycle first—use Mailchimp if you need complex A/B testing in automations.

### Segmentation and Analytics
- **Brevo:** Tag-based segmentation, real-time reporting, heatmaps.
- **Mailchimp:** Predictive segmentation (e.g., predicted demographics), detailed revenue tracking.

### Integrations
Both connect to 100+ apps via Zapier. Brevo edges with native CRM; Mailchimp with e-commerce (WooCommerce, BigCommerce).

### Deliverability
Brevo: 99%+ rates, IP warming tools.
Mailchimp: Excellent reputation, but stricter spam policies.

**Practical Advice:** Monitor your sender score with tools like GlockApps. Warm up domains gradually on either platform by starting with engaged segments.

## Ease of Use: Brevo vs Mailchimp

Mailchimp's interface feels more polished for beginners—think colorful dashboards and guided tours. Brevo is straightforward but has a steeper learning curve for automation setup.

**Onboarding Tip:** Mailchimp's pop-up tutorials help quick starts. For Brevo, watch their 5-minute setup videos to import contacts via CSV seamlessly.

Migration advice: Export lists from one to the other using standard formats. Test a small batch first to avoid deliverability hits.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **Brevo:** 24/7 chat/email on paid plans, extensive knowledge base, community forum.
- **Mailchimp:** Priority support on higher tiers, huge help center, webinars.

Practical tip: Join both free trials and test support responsiveness. Free users get email-only on both.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros:
- Affordable scaling.
- Unlimited contacts.
- Multi-channel.

Cons:
- Fewer templates.
- Basic reporting on lower plans.

### Mailchimp Pros:
- Intuitive design.
- Strong automations.
- E-commerce focus.

Cons:
- Pricey for large lists.
- Free plan limits.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- Businesses with large contact lists.
- Those needing SMS/transactional emails.
- Budget-focused SMBs sending high volumes.

Example: An online store with 50K subscribers sending weekly newsletters—Brevo saves hundreds monthly.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Design-oriented marketers.
- E-commerce beginners.
- Teams needing advanced analytics.

Example: A content creator with 5K engaged followers running drip campaigns—Mailchimp's tools shine.

**Decision Framework:**
1. List size >10K? Brevo.
2. Heavy automation? Mailchimp.
3. Multi-channel? Brevo.
4. Trial both for 14-30 days.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your priorities: Brevo for cost-efficiency and scale, Mailchimp for user-friendliness and creativity. Neither is universally 'better'—test with your data. Start free trials, import a segment, and send a test campaign. Track open rates, clicks, and costs over 30 days. For most growing businesses, Brevo offers better long-term value, but Mailchimp's ecosystem keeps it competitive.
