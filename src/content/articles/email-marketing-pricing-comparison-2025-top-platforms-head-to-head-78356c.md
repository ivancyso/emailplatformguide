# Email Marketing Pricing Comparison 2025: Choose the Best Plan for Your Needs

Selecting the right email marketing platform isn't just about features—it's about value for money. With subscriber lists growing and budgets tight, understanding pricing is crucial. This 2025 comparison breaks down costs for popular tools like Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, Brevo (formerly Sendinblue), GetResponse, and Constant Contact. We'll focus on starter plans, scaling costs, and hidden fees, helping you pick based on your audience size and goals.

Whether you're a solopreneur with 500 subscribers or scaling to 10,000+, we'll provide real pricing data (as of early 2025; always check official sites for updates), practical tips, and calculators to estimate your spend.

## Why Pricing Matters More Than Ever in Email Marketing

Email marketing delivers ROI up to 42x, but overpaying kills profits. Platforms charge based on contacts (subscribers), sends, or features. Free tiers lure beginners, but costs spike at 1,000+ contacts.

**Practical Advice:**
- Estimate your list growth: 20-30% monthly for active campaigns.
- Factor in add-ons: Automation, A/B testing, or SMS can add 20-50%.
- Trial all: Most offer 14-30 day free trials without cards.

Average small business spend: $20-100/mo. Enterprises: $500+.

## Key Pricing Models Explained

### Contacts-Based Pricing
Most common (e.g., Mailchimp). Pay per subscriber, not sends. Good for engaged lists.

### Send-Based Pricing
Brevo charges per email volume. Ideal for low-frequency newsletters.

### Flat or Tiered
ConvertKit: Flat per creator tier, unlimited sends.

**Hidden Costs to Watch:**
- Overage fees: $X per extra 500 contacts.
- Agency plans: 20-50% markup.
- Integrations: Zapier ($20/mo extra).

**Tip:** Use this formula: Monthly Cost = Base + (Contacts / Tier Limit) * Overage Rate.

### Mailchimp Pricing Deep Dive

Mailchimp's freemium model suits beginners. Essentials ($13/mo for 500): Basic automations. Standard ($75 for 2,500): A/B testing, custom landing pages. Premium scales steeply.

**Pros:** Intuitive, huge templates library.
**Cons:** No free after 500; behavioral automations locked high.

**Advice:** Best for ecom starters under 5k lists. Migrate if automation-heavy.

### ActiveCampaign Pricing

Starts at $29/mo (500 contacts): Email + basic automation. Plus ($49 for 2,500): Site tracking. Pro ($149+): Machine learning.

**Standout:** Advanced CRM/automation at mid-price.

**Practical Tip:** Calculate ROI: Saves hours on segments. Ideal for SaaS/service businesses.

### ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Creator ($29/mo): Unlimited sends, forms. Scales flat until Pro ($79 for 10k+ advanced).

**Why Love It:** Creator-focused, no send limits.

**Advice:** Perfect for bloggers/newsletters. Pair with Gumroad for sales.

### Brevo Pricing

Free forever (300 emails/day). Starter $25 (20k/mo). Business $65 (unlimited contacts, 20k emails).

**Tip:** Volume senders win. EU GDPR compliant bonus.

### GetResponse and Constant Contact

GetResponse: All-in-one ($19 start), webinars included higher.
Constant Contact: Beginner-friendly ($12 Lite), strong support.

**Quick Pick:** GetResponse for marketing funnels; Constant for nonprofits.

## Feature vs. Price: What You Get

Don't chase cheap—match needs:

- **Basic Newsletters:** Brevo or Mailchimp free.
- **Automations:** ActiveCampaign ($29 wins).
- **Unlimited Sends:** ConvertKit.

**Cost-Saving Hacks:**
1. Clean lists quarterly (remove bounces) to stay in tiers.
2. Annual billing: 15-20% off.
3. Start free, upgrade via trial insights.
4. Compare with tools like EmailToolTester.

**Scenario Examples:**
- 1k subs, weekly newsletter: Brevo $0-25.
- 5k ecom: ActiveCampaign $89 (growth automations pay off).
- 20k enterprise: Custom quotes (negotiate).
