# The Best Email Marketing Software for 2024: Top 10 Picks for Growing Businesses

Email marketing remains one of the most effective digital strategies, with average ROI around $36 for every $1 spent, according to industry benchmarks from Litmus and DMA reports. For businesses choosing email marketing platforms, the right software can streamline automation, personalize campaigns, and drive conversions. In this roundup, we evaluate the best email marketing software based on real user feedback from G2, Capterra, and Trustpilot, focusing on ease of use, deliverability, integrations, and value for money.

Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or scaling agency, these tools cater to diverse needs. We prioritize platforms with proven track records, avoiding hype with data-backed insights.

## Key Features to Consider When Choosing Email Marketing Software

Before diving into the list, understand what makes top-tier email marketing software stand out. Here's practical advice on essential features:

### 1. Automation Capabilities
Look for drag-and-drop builders for workflows like welcome series, abandoned cart recovery, and re-engagement campaigns. Tools with behavioral triggers (e.g., based on opens/clicks) save time and boost engagement rates by up to 80%, per EmailMonday studies.

### 2. List Management and Segmentation
Robust segmentation by demographics, behavior, or purchase history is crucial. Expect built-in cleaning tools to maintain list hygiene and comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM.

### 3. Templates and Design Tools
Mobile-responsive templates and AI-assisted design speed up creation. Test for customization without coding.

### 4. Analytics and Reporting
Track open rates (industry avg. 21%), click-through (2.5%), and revenue per email. A/B testing and heatmaps provide actionable insights.

### 5. Integrations and Deliverability
Seamless connections to Shopify, WordPress, CRM like HubSpot, and payment gateways. High deliverability scores (above 95%) from Return Path or GlockApps matter.

### 6. Pricing and Scalability
Free tiers for starters, then per-subscriber or feature-based plans. Avoid hidden fees for sends or support.

Budget tip: Start with free plans under 1,000-2,000 subscribers, then scale. Factor in support quality—live chat beats email tickets.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Platforms

Here's our curated list of the best email marketing software, ranked by overall performance for most users. Each includes pros, cons, pricing (as of 2024), and ideal use cases.

### 1. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign excels in advanced automation and CRM integration, serving 180,000+ businesses.

**Pros:**
- Powerful automation with if/then logic.
- Deep CRM features for sales pipelines.
- Excellent split testing and site tracking.

**Cons:**
- Steeper learning curve for beginners.
- Higher cost for large lists.

**Pricing:** Starts at $29/mo (Essentials) for 500 contacts; up to $149/mo (Professional).

**Best for:** E-commerce and B2B with complex funnels. Users report 30%+ open rate improvements.

### 2. Klaviyo

Klaviyo dominates e-commerce with Shopify-native flows, powering brands like Uniqlo.

**Pros:**
- Superior revenue tracking and product recommendations.
- Pre-built flows for SMS/email synergy.
- Predictive analytics.

**Cons:**
- Limited non-e-com features.
- Pricing scales quickly.

**Pricing:** Free up to 250 contacts; $20/mo+ based on sends.

**Best for:** Online stores. Delivers 3x higher revenue per recipient vs. averages.

### 3. Mailchimp

The OG platform with 13M+ users, now with AI tools like Intuit Assist.

**Pros:**n- Intuitive interface and vast template library.
- Free plan forever.
- Strong analytics dashboard.

**Cons:**
- Automation limited in lower tiers.
- Recent pricing hikes post-Intuit acquisition.

**Pricing:** Free for 500 contacts/1,000 sends; $13/mo (Essentials).

**Best for:** Beginners and small teams. Easy A/B testing boosts CTR by 20-50%.

### 4. ConvertKit (now Kit)

Creator-focused with visual automations.

**Pros:**n- Unlimited emails on all plans.
- Tagging-based segmentation.
- Landing page builder included.

**Cons:**
- Fewer enterprise features.
- Design less polished.

**Pricing:** Free up to 300 subscribers; $15/mo (Creator).

**Best for:** Bloggers and course creators. High deliverability (99%).

### 5. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels.

**Pros:**
- Conversion funnels and landing pages.
- AI email generator.
- Competitive pricing.

**Cons:**
- Interface feels dated.
- Support varies.

**Pricing:** Free for 500 contacts; $19/mo (Starter).

**Best for:** Marketers needing webinars. 94% deliverability.

### 6. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Transactional + marketing emails hybrid.

**Pros:**
- Pay-per-send model.
- SMS and chat integrations.
- EU-based for privacy.

**Cons:**
- Automation builder clunky.
- Limited templates.

**Pricing:** Free 300 emails/day; $25/mo (Starter).

**Best for:** Transactional volume. Cost-effective for spikes.

### 7. Drip

E-commerce automation specialist.

**Pros:**
- Workflow timelines.
- Customer data platform.
- Zero minimums.

**Cons:**
- Expensive for non-ecom.
- Complex setup.

**Pricing:** Starts at $39/mo for 2,500 subscribers.

**Best for:** Advanced DTC brands.

### 8. Constant Contact

User-friendly for non-profits/small biz.

**Pros:**
- Event management.
- Social ads integration.
- 60-day free trial.

**Cons:**
- Basic automations.
- Per-contact pricing.

**Pricing:** $12/mo (Lite) for unlimited emails.

**Best for:** Events and locals.

### 9. AWeber

Reliable since 1998.

**Pros:**
- High deliverability.
- Tag-based automations.
- Loyal support.

**Cons:**
- Dated UI.
- Fewer integrations.

**Pricing:** $12.50/mo (Free tier limited).

**Best for:** Long-term lists.

### 10. Omnisend

Omnichannel for e-com.

**Pros:**
- Free plan generous.
- Pop-ups and forms.
- Unified inbox.

**Cons:**
- Reporting basic.
- SMS add-on extra.

**Pricing:** Free up to 250 contacts; $16/mo.

**Best for:** Multi-channel starters.

## How to Choose and Implement the Best Email Marketing Software

1. **Assess Your Needs:** List size? E-com? Budget under $50/mo?
2. **Trial Multiple Tools:** Use 14-30 day trials. Import a test list.
3. **Test Deliverability:** Send to Gmail/Yahoo; check spam.
4. **Migrate Smartly:** Export CSV, map fields, suppress bounces.
5. **Optimize Campaigns:** Start with 1-2 sends/week, segment rigorously, personalize subjects.

Pro tip: Integrate with Google Analytics for full-funnel tracking. Aim for 20-30% open rates initially.
