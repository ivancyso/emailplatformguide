# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2023: Best Email Marketing Platforms for Every Budget

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many businesses searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're dealing with rising fees, complex interfaces, or need better segmentation and automation, it's time to explore options.

This roundup compares 10 proven **Mailchimp alternatives** based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, official pricing pages, and feature sets as of late 2023. We'll cover free plans, scalability, ease of use, and migration tips. Whether you're a solopreneur, e-commerce store, or agency, there's a fit here.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but jump quickly (e.g., $299/month for 50k contacts). Common pain points include:
- **Limited automations**: Basic triggers only on lower tiers.
- **UI clutter**: Overwhelming for beginners.
- **Deliverability dips**: Recent reports show variance.

Alternatives often offer better value: unlimited sends on some plans, advanced AI tools, and seamless integrations with Shopify, WordPress, etc. Before switching, export your Mailchimp list via CSV and test imports.

## 1. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
Advanced automation workflows, CRM integration, machine learning for send times, and site tracking. Ideal for sales teams.

### Pricing
Essays plan: $29/month (500 contacts). Scales to Enterprise at custom pricing.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Deep personalization, 90%+ deliverability, 700+ integrations.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve; no free plan beyond 14-day trial.

**Practical Advice**: Use for complex customer journeys. Migrate via their importer tool—takes ~1 hour for 10k contacts.

## 2. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

### Key Features
Creator-focused: Visual automations, landing pages, subscriber scoring, and commerce tools.

### Pricing
Free for 300 subscribers; Creator at $29/month (1k subs).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Simple drag-and-drop, unlimited emails, strong for newsletters.
**Cons**: Less robust reporting than enterprise tools.

**Practical Advice**: Perfect for bloggers. Tag-based segmentation beats Mailchimp's groups. Free migration support available.

## 3. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
Transactional emails, SMS, chat, unlimited contacts on free plan, automation with conditions.

### Pricing
Free forever (300 emails/day); Starter $25/month (20k emails).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Generous free tier, EU-based for GDPR ease, multi-channel.
**Cons**: Email builder less intuitive.

**Practical Advice**: Great for e-commerce with WhatsApp integration. Import CSV directly; test deliverability with their warmup tool.

## 4. Klaviyo

### Key Features
E-commerce powerhouse: Revenue tracking, flow builder for abandoned carts, A/B testing, predictive analytics.

### Pricing
Free for 250 contacts/500 emails; scales by contacts (e.g., $60/month for 1k).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Shopify native, 45% avg revenue lift reported by users.
**Cons**: E-com focused; pricier for non-shop owners.

**Practical Advice**: Connect to your store in minutes. Use pre-built flows to replicate Mailchimp campaigns quickly.

## 5. MailerLite

### Key Features
Drag-and-drop editor, popups, landing pages, A/B testing, webhooks.

### Pricing
Free up to 1k subscribers/12k emails; Growing Business $10/month.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Affordable, fast editor, high deliverability (99%).
**Cons**: Fewer advanced CRM features.

**Practical Advice**: Best for startups. One-click Mailchimp import; focus on website forms for list growth.

## 6. GetResponse

### Key Features
Autofunnels, webinars, conversion funnels, AI email generator.

### Pricing
Free for 500 contacts; Email Marketing $19/month (1k subs).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: All-in-one (landing pages, ads), strong automation.
**Cons**: Interface feels dated.

**Practical Advice**: Use for lead gen. Bulk import and map tags to avoid data loss.

## 7. AWeber

### Key Features
Classic editor, tagging, integrations with 700+ apps, spam testing.

### Pricing
Lite $12.50/month (500 subs); no free plan but 30-day trial.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Reliable 25+ years, excellent support.
**Cons**: Less modern automations.

**Practical Advice**: For legacy users. Their migration service handles everything.

## 8. Constant Contact

### Key Features
Event management, social ads, AI content optimizer, robust reporting.

### Pricing
Lite $12/month (500 contacts).

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Beginner-friendly, US-based support.
**Cons**: No free plan; higher costs at scale.

**Practical Advice**: Good for nonprofits. Easy template swaps from Mailchimp.

## 9. Omnisend

### Key Features
Omnichannel (email, SMS, push), pre-built workflows, product recommendations.

### Pricing
Free for 250 contacts/500 emails; Standard $16/month.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: E-com optimized, high ROI.
**Cons**: SMS add-ons extra.

**Practical Advice**: Segment by purchase history post-migration.

## 10. Drip

### Key Features
Behavioral automation, revenue tracking, A/B testing, API access.

### Pricing
Starts at $39/month (500 subs); custom for larger.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Advanced for DTC brands.
**Cons**: No free tier.

**Practical Advice**: For high-volume senders. Use Zapier for initial sync.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: List size? E-com? Automation depth?
2. **Trial Everything**: Most offer 14-30 days free.
3. **Check Integrations**: Zapier/ native apps?
4. **Migration Steps**:
   - Export audience from Mailchimp (CSV).
   - Sign up, import, verify emails.
   - Recreate top campaigns.
   - Update website forms/CRM links.
   - Monitor first sends for deliverability.

**Pro Tip**: Start with 10% of list to test. Tools like Mailchimp's API or third-party (e.g., Zapier) speed this up.

Budget under $20? MailerLite or Brevo. Scaling sales? ActiveCampaign. E-com? Klaviyo.
