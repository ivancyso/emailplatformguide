# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate Comparison for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business or content creation efforts. In this MailerLite vs ConvertKit showdown, we'll break down key features, pricing, usability, and more to help you decide. Both tools cater to creators, bloggers, and small businesses, but they shine in different areas. Whether you're starting free or scaling up, read on for practical insights.

## Pricing: Where Value Meets Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle when picking an email marketing tool. Both MailerLite and ConvertKit offer free plans, but their paid tiers differ significantly.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite keeps it simple and affordable:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation and landing pages.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers (scales up).
- **Advanced**: $20/month for 500 subscribers, adds A/B testing and web push notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume needs.

No contracts, monthly billing, and they charge based on subscribers at send time—practical for growing lists without overpaying.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (rebranded as Kit but still widely known as ConvertKit) focuses on creators:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers (limited to 10,000 emails/year—much lower than MailerLite).
- **Creator**: Starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers, includes unlimited emails and basic selling features.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month for 300 subscribers, adds advanced automations, subscriber scoring, and visual automations.
- Higher tiers scale to $2,995/month for massive lists.

ConvertKit's pricing jumps faster as your list grows, but it includes commerce tools like paid newsletters.

**Practical Advice**: If you're bootstrapping with a small list (<1,000), MailerLite's free plan sends more emails. For creators monetizing via newsletters, ConvertKit's built-in selling justifies the cost. Always calculate based on your projected growth—use their pricing calculators.

## Ease of Use and Editor Experience

A steep learning curve kills momentum. Both platforms prioritize intuitive designs.

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Editor

MailerLite's editor is beginner-friendly:
- Classic or new blocks-based editor.
- 80+ responsive templates.
- Photo editor integrated.

Setup takes minutes: Import contacts via CSV, segment, and send. Mobile app for on-the-go management.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Focused Interface

ConvertKit emphasizes sequences over one-off blasts:
- Clean, minimalist editor with blocks for text, images, buttons.
- Visual automation builder (Pro plan).

It's tag-based segmentation shines for personalization, but the free plan limits broadcasts.

**Practical Advice**: Test both free trials. MailerLite suits visual marketers needing popups/forms quickly. ConvertKit rewards those building nurture sequences—import your list and set up a welcome series in under 30 minutes.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Automation and Segmentation

- **MailerLite**: Workflow automation with triggers (e.g., birthday emails), RSS campaigns, A/B testing (paid). Segments by behavior, custom fields.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tagging, sequences, and subscriber scoring. Commerce automations like one-click upsells.

Winner: ConvertKit for complex funnels; MailerLite for straightforward automations.

### Landing Pages and Forms

- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, popups, forms with 50+ templates. Embedded or fixed-position popups.
- **ConvertKit**: Forms, landing pages, and checkouts integrated. Strong for digital products.

**Practical Advice**: Use MailerLite popups on exit-intent to capture 10-20% more leads. ConvertKit's forms excel in gated content for courses.

### Integrations and Analytics

Both integrate with Zapier, WordPress, Shopify.
- MailerLite: Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, 1,000+ apps.
- ConvertKit: Native commerce (Stripe, Gumroad), detailed reporting on opens, clicks, revenue.

Analytics in both track opens (30-40% avg), clicks (2-5%). ConvertKit edges with revenue attribution.

### Deliverability and Support

Both boast high deliverability (95%+). MailerLite offers 24/7 live chat; ConvertKit email/ticket with creator resources.

**Practical Advice**: Warm up your domain with gradual sends. Monitor spam complaints—stay under 0.1%.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite

**Pros**:
- Cheaper scaling.
- Generous free plan.
- Excellent popups/landing pages.

**Cons**:
- Fewer commerce features.
- Automation less visual.

### ConvertKit

**Pros**:
- Creator tools (subscriptions, products).
- Powerful tagging/sequences.
- Great support resources.

**Cons**:
- Pricier for large lists.
- Free plan email limits.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Small business/blogger, budget-conscious, need popups/landing pages. Ideal for e-commerce beginners (starts at $10/mo).
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Content creator/podcaster selling digital products, need sequences and monetization. Worth it from 1,000+ subscribers.

**Practical Advice**: Migrate painlessly—both support CSV exports. Start with free plans: Build a 100-subscriber test list, run campaigns, compare open rates.
