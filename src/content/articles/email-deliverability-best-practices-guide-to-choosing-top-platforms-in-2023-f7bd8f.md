# Email Deliverability Best Practices: Guide to Choosing Top Platforms in 2023

In the competitive world of email marketing, **email deliverability** is the cornerstone of success. For businesses and marketers choosing email marketing platforms, understanding best practices ensures your messages land in the inbox, not the spam folder. Poor deliverability can tank open rates, engagement, and ROI. This guide dives into proven **email deliverability best practices**, practical advice, and what to look for in platforms to maximize your campaigns.

Whether you're a small business owner or a growth marketer, these strategies will help you select and optimize platforms like Mailchimp, Klaviyo, or ActiveCampaign for superior performance.

## What is Email Deliverability and Why Does It Matter?

Email deliverability refers to the ability of your emails to reach the recipient's inbox rather than being blocked, bounced, or routed to spam. Major email service providers (ESPs) like Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo use sophisticated algorithms to filter emails based on sender reputation, content quality, and user engagement.

### Key Metrics to Track
- **Inbox Placement Rate**: Percentage of emails landing in the inbox (aim for 95%+).
- **Bounce Rate**: Hard bounces (invalid addresses) and soft bounces (temporary issues). Keep under 2%.
- **Spam Complaint Rate**: User-reported spam. Target below 0.1%.
- **Open and Click Rates**: Indicators of engagement that influence future deliverability.

Poor deliverability costs marketers billions annually. According to Return Path, 18% of emails fail to reach the inbox. For platform choosers, prioritize tools with built-in deliverability monitoring.

## Core Email Deliverability Best Practices

Implementing these practices is essential before selecting a platform. They form the foundation for any successful email strategy.

### 1. Build and Maintain a Clean Email List

Start with quality over quantity. Never buy lists—they're a fast track to blacklists.

**Practical Advice:**
- Use double opt-in: Confirm subscriptions to verify genuine interest.
- Implement list hygiene: Regularly remove inactive subscribers (no opens/clicks in 6 months).
- Segment lists: Tailor content to user behavior for higher engagement.

**Platform Tip:** Look for platforms with automated list cleaning tools, like ZeroBounce integration in ConvertKit or Mailchimp's built-in suppression lists.

### 2. Authenticate Your Domain with SPF, DKIM, and DMARC

Authentication protocols prove you're a legitimate sender.

- **SPF (Sender Policy Framework)**: Authorizes sending IPs in DNS records.
- **DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)**: Adds cryptographic signatures to emails.
- **DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance)**: Builds on SPF/DKIM with reporting and policy enforcement.

**Practical Steps:**
1. Set up records in your DNS provider (e.g., GoDaddy, Cloudflare).
2. Use tools like MX Toolbox to verify setup.
3. Monitor DMARC reports for issues.

Most platforms like Sendinblue (Brevo) or Postmark offer one-click authentication setup, making this easier for beginners.

### 3. Optimize Email Content and Design

Content flags spam filters. Avoid spammy tactics.

**Best Practices:**
- **Subject Lines**: Keep under 50 characters, avoid ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation (!!!).
- **Preheaders**: Craft compelling previews.
- **Body Copy**: Balance text-to-image ratio (60/40), use personalized greetings.
- **Avoid Spam Triggers**: Words like "free money," "guaranteed," or too many links.

Test with tools like Litmus or GlockApps. Platforms with A/B testing (e.g., ActiveCampaign) help refine this.

### 4. Warm Up Your Sending Domain

New domains start with zero reputation. Gradually increase volume.

**Practical Advice:**
- Begin with 50-100 emails/day, ramp up 20-50% weekly.
- Focus on engaged segments first.
- Use dedicated IP for high-volume senders (10k+ emails/month).

Services like Warmup Inbox or platforms with warmup features (e.g., Mailreach integrations) automate this.

### 5. Monitor Sender Reputation

Your IP and domain reputation dictates deliverability.

**Tools to Use:**
- Google Postmaster Tools: Free Gmail insights.
- Sender Score (Return Path): 0-100 reputation score.
- Talos Intelligence: Cisco's blacklist checker.

Choose platforms with reputation dashboards, like Klaviyo or Omnisend.

### 6. Encourage Engagement and Handle Complaints

Engaged users signal quality to ISPs.

**Tips:**
- Send relevant, timely content.
- Include clear unsubscribe links (required by CAN-SPAM).
- Honor opt-outs within 10 days.
- Add "mark as spam" handling.

Re-engagement campaigns for dormant users can revive lists.

## Choosing an Email Marketing Platform for Optimal Deliverability

When evaluating platforms, focus on deliverability features. Here's what to prioritize:

### Essential Platform Features

| Feature | Why It Matters | Examples |

| **Deliverability Tools** | Built-in monitoring, IP reputation | Klaviyo, ActiveCampaign |

| **Dedicated IPs** | For scale | SendGrid, Amazon SES |
| **Analytics** | Bounce/spam tracking | HubSpot, GetResponse |

### Top Platforms for Deliverability (Based on Industry Benchmarks)

- **Klaviyo**: Excels in e-commerce with 99%+ deliverability, advanced segmentation.
- **ActiveCampaign**: Robust automation, detailed reporting.
- **Brevo (Sendinblue)**: Affordable, strong transactional email support.
- **Mailchimp**: User-friendly, but monitor for shared IP issues at scale.

Test with free trials: Send test campaigns to various inboxes and check placement.

## Advanced Strategies for Long-Term Success

### Blacklist Monitoring and Removal
Regularly scan with MultiRBL or MX Toolbox. Dispute false positives promptly.

### Feedback Loops (FBLs)
Sign up with ISPs like Yahoo and Microsoft for complaint notifications.

### IP Rotation and Shared vs. Dedicated
Shared IPs are fine for <10k emails/month; switch to dedicated for volume.

### Compliance with Regulations
Adhere to GDPR, CAN-SPAM, CASL. Platforms like HubSpot offer compliance checklists.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Over-sending to cold lists.
- Ignoring mobile optimization (50%+ opens on mobile).
- Neglecting A/B testing.
- Using free webmail for sending (poor reputation).
