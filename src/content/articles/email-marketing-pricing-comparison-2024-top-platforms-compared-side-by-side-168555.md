# Email Marketing Pricing Comparison: Choose the Right Platform for Your Budget

Selecting the right email marketing platform isn't just about features—it's about value for money. With prices ranging from free tiers to enterprise-level plans costing hundreds per month, understanding email marketing pricing is crucial for businesses of all sizes. In this comprehensive comparison, we'll break down costs from top providers like Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, Brevo (formerly Sendinblue), and Moosend. We'll focus on starter plans, mid-tier options, and scaling costs based on subscriber counts (e.g., 500, 2,500, 10,000 contacts). All pricing is monthly unless noted, billed annually for discounts where applicable. Data is current as of 2024—always check official sites for updates.

## Why Pricing Matters More Than You Think

Email marketing delivers an average ROI of $42 for every $1 spent, but overspending on unused features or hidden fees can eat into profits. Key factors influencing cost:

- **Subscriber Count**: Most platforms charge based on contacts, not sends.
- **Sending Volume**: Some bill per email sent (rare now).
- **Features**: Automation, A/B testing, and analytics bump up tiers.
- **Deliverability Add-ons**: Premium IP pools or dedicated IPs cost extra.

Practical tip: Start with your current list size and projected growth. Calculate lifetime value (LTV) per subscriber to justify spends—e.g., if a subscriber generates $50/year, a $1/mo platform fee is fine.

**Pros**: Intuitive drag-and-drop editor, free plan. **Cons**: Automation limited in lower tiers; high jump to Premium. Best for solopreneurs. Tip: Use free for testing, upgrade only for behavioral automations.

## Best Plans by Budget and Use Case

### Under $30/mo: Best Free/Cheap Options

- **Free**: Mailchimp (limited), Brevo (300/day), ConvertKit (up to 300).
- **Starter Budget**: Moosend or Brevo for unlimited-ish sends. Advice: Track open rates—if under 20%, optimize lists first before upgrading.

### $30-100/mo: Growing Businesses

ConvertKit or Mailchimp Standard. Practical: Audit features needed—do you require e-commerce tracking? Skip if not.

### $100+/mo: Enterprise Scale

ActiveCampaign Pro. Tip: Negotiate custom plans; add dedicated IPs ($10-50/mo) for better deliverability.

## Hidden Costs and How to Avoid Them

- **Overages**: Mailchimp charges for extra contacts.
- **Add-ons**: SMS ($0.01/message), dedicated IPs ($50+/mo).
- **Migration Fees**: Some charge for imports.

Pro Tip: Use tools like EmailListVerify (free tier) to clean lists pre-import, reducing contact bloat and costs. Always opt for annual billing (10-20% savings). Monitor churn—platforms like ActiveCampaign help with win-back campaigns.

## How to Choose the Right Email Marketing Pricing Plan

1. **Estimate Subscribers**: Current + 20% growth/year.
2. **List Needs**: Automation? E-com? Pick accordingly.
3. **Trial Everything**: Most offer 14-30 days.
4. **Calculate TCO**: Total cost over 12 months, factor ROI.
5. **Read Reviews**: Focus on support quality.

Example: A SaaS with 5k users might pick ActiveCampaign ($109/mo starter) for lead scoring, projecting $10k+ revenue lift.

## Conclusion: Find Your Perfect Email Marketing Pricing Fit

No one-size-fits-all—Moosend wins on pure affordability, ActiveCampaign on power, Brevo on flexibility. For most choosing platforms, start with your budget tier and scale up. Prioritize deliverability (aim for 95%+) over bells-and-whistles. Reassess quarterly as lists grow. Ready to pick? Trial two comparables and A/B your welcome series to see real impact.
