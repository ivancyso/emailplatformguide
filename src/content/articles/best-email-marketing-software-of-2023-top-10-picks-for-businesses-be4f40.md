# Best Email Marketing Software of 2023: Top 10 Picks for Businesses

Choosing the **best email marketing software** can transform how you connect with your audience. With so many options available, it's essential to find a platform that matches your business size, budget, and goals. This roundup reviews the top 10 email marketing tools based on real user feedback, key features, and practical performance. Whether you're a solopreneur or running a large enterprise, these picks offer reliable automation, segmentation, and analytics to boost your campaigns.

We'll cover what makes great software, detailed breakdowns of each tool, a comparison, and tips to select the right one. All recommendations draw from established industry standards and official platform details—no hype, just facts.

## What to Look for in the Best Email Marketing Software

Before diving into the list, consider these core factors:

- **Deliverability Rates**: Aim for platforms with 95%+ inbox placement. Tools with dedicated IPs and authentication support (DKIM, SPF) perform best.
- **Ease of Use**: Drag-and-drop editors save time for non-designers.
- **Automation and Segmentation**: Conditional workflows and audience splitting drive engagement.
- **Integrations**: Connect with CRMs like Salesforce, e-commerce like Shopify, or analytics tools.
- **Pricing**: Free tiers for starters, scalable plans for growth.
- **Analytics**: Open rates, clicks, revenue tracking.
- **Support**: 24/7 chat, knowledge bases.

Practical advice: Start with a free trial to test list imports and send a sample campaign. Monitor your first send's deliverability.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Picks

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp remains a leader for beginners due to its intuitive interface. It offers a forever-free plan for up to 500 subscribers and 1,000 emails/month. Key features include drag-and-drop templates, basic automations (welcome series, abandoned cart), A/B testing, and integrations with 250+ apps like WordPress and Google Analytics.

**Pros**: User-friendly, extensive templates (500+), audience insights.
**Cons**: Advanced automations require higher plans ($13+/mo for Essentials).
**Best for**: Small businesses, bloggers. Pricing starts free, scales to $299+/mo for Premium.

Real-world tip: Use behavioral triggers for re-engagement emails to recover 10-20% of inactive subscribers.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign excels in automation with its CRM integration and machine learning for predictive sending. Plans start at $29/mo (Lite) for 500 contacts. Features: 700+ integrations, site tracking, split automations, and detailed reporting.

**Pros**: Powerful if/then logic, high deliverability (99% reported).
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve.
**Best for**: E-commerce, agencies. Up to $259/mo for Enterprise.

Tip: Set up conditional content blocks to personalize emails dynamically based on past opens.

### 3. Klaviyo

Tailored for e-commerce, Klaviyo shines with Shopify integration and revenue-focused flows. Starts at free for 250 contacts, then $20+/mo. Features: Advanced segmentation, SMS add-on, A/B testing on subject lines.

**Pros**: ROI tracking shows email-attributed revenue.
**Cons**: Less ideal for non-e-com.
**Best for**: Online stores. Scales with revenue.

Practical: Build lookalike audiences from high-LTV customers to expand reach.

### 4. ConvertKit (now Kit)

Creator-focused with clean automations and landing pages. Free up to 300 subs, then $9/mo. Visual automation builder, tagging system, and subscriber scoring.

**Pros**: Simple broadcasts, commerce tools.
**Cons**: Basic templates.
**Best for**: Creators, newsletters. $29+/mo Creator plan.

Tip: Use sequences for lead nurturing, delivering value over 7-10 emails.

### 5. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Affordable with transactional emails included. Free for 300 emails/day, $25/mo Starter. Multichannel (email, SMS, chat), automation workflows.

**Pros**: Unlimited contacts on paid plans, EU hosting.
**Cons**: Editor less polished.
**Best for**: SMBs, transactional needs.

Advice: Combine transactional receipts with promotional upsells for higher conversions.

### 6. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels. Free 30-day trial, $19/mo Email Marketing plan. Conversion funnels, autoresponders, e-com features.

**Pros**: Landing pages included.
**Cons**: Overwhelming dashboard.

Best for: Marketers needing extras. $49+/mo Marketing Automation.

Tip: A/B test funnels end-to-end for optimization.

### 7. MailerLite

Budget-friendly with drag-and-drop and popups. Free to 1,000 subs, $10/mo Growing Business. Website builder, surveys.

**Pros**: High deliverability, fast support.
**Cons**: Limited advanced CRM.

Best for: Startups. $20+/mo Advanced.

Practical: Embed forms on sites for 20-30% list growth.

### 8. Constant Contact

Established for nonprofits/SMBs. $12/mo Lite (500 contacts). Event management, social ads.

**Pros**: AI subject lines, easy migration.
**Cons**: No free plan.

Best for: Non-tech users. $35+/mo Standard.

Tip: Schedule social promotes alongside emails.

### 9. Drip

E-com automation specialist. $39/mo for 500 subs. Workflow builder, personalization.

**Pros**: Customer journey mapping.
**Cons**: Pricier.

Best for: DTC brands.

Advice: Use revenue per visitor metrics to refine segments.

### 10. AWeber

Reliable veteran with tagging. Free trial, $19/mo Lite (500 subs). Webinars, landing pages.

**Pros**: Strong support, spam testing.
**Cons**: Dated interface.

Best for: Long-term lists.

Tip: Run legacy cleanups quarterly.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing Software for You

1. **Assess Needs**: Solo? Mailchimp. Complex? ActiveCampaign.
2. **Budget Check**: Free tiers for testing.
3. **Trial Run**: Import 100 contacts, send test campaign.
4. **Scale Plan**: Ensure pricing grows with subs linearly.
5. **Compliance**: GDPR/CCPA tools built-in.

Practical advice: Calculate CAC vs. LTV—aim for emails contributing 30%+ revenue. Integrate with your stack early.

## Practical Tips for Success with Any Platform

- **List Hygiene**: Remove bounces monthly; use double opt-in.
- **Content Strategy**: 80/20 value-to-promo ratio.
- **Timing**: Test sends (Tues-Thurs, 8-10AM user time).
- **Mobile Optimization**: 50%+ opens on mobile—responsive templates.
- **Analytics Dive**: Track forward rates, unsubscribes under 0.5%.
- **A/B Everything**: Subjects, CTAs, send times.

Start small: One welcome automation yields quick wins.

The **best email marketing software** depends on your stage—Mailchimp for ease, ActiveCampaign for power, Klaviyo for sales. Test 2-3 via trials, prioritize deliverability and automations.
