# Best Email Marketing Software 2023: Top 10 Platforms Compared for Small Businesses

Choosing the **best email marketing software** can transform your business's communication strategy. With email delivering an average ROI of $42 for every $1 spent (according to DMA data), the right platform is essential for small businesses, solopreneurs, and growing teams. This guide compares the top 10 email marketing tools based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, focusing on features like automation, deliverability, and integrations.

We'll break down key criteria, profile each platform, and provide practical advice to help you select the ideal fit. Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling to enterprise levels, these options cover newsletters, e-commerce campaigns, and lead nurturing.

## What Makes the Best Email Marketing Software?

Before diving into comparisons, understand the core factors:

### Essential Features to Prioritize

- **Automation Workflows**: Drip campaigns, abandoned cart recovery, and behavioral triggers save time.
- **Deliverability Rates**: Aim for 95%+ to avoid spam folders. Tools with dedicated IPs excel here.
- **Templates and Design**: Drag-and-drop editors for non-designers.
- **Analytics**: Open rates, clicks, revenue tracking.
- **Integrations**: Connect to Shopify, WordPress, Zapier, etc.
- **List Management**: Segmentation, tagging, compliance (GDPR, CAN-SPAM).

### Pricing Considerations

Most offer freemium models. Free tiers suit <2,000 subscribers; paid plans scale by contacts and sends. Expect $10–$100/month for starters.

### Scalability and Support

Look for 24/7 chat, onboarding webinars, and API access for growth.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Compared

Here's a head-to-head of leading platforms. Data reflects 2023 averages from official sites and reviews.

### 1. Mailchimp

**Best for**: Beginners and small lists.

Mailchimp powers 13 million+ users with intuitive drag-and-drop. Free for up to 500 contacts/1,000 sends/month.

**Pros**:
- 300+ templates.
- Basic automations (welcome series).
- Integrates with Google Analytics, Facebook.

**Cons**:
- Advanced automation locked behind pricier plans ($13–$350+/mo).
- Deliverability dips for high-volume sends.

**Pricing**: Essentials $13/mo (50k sends), Standard $20/mo.

**Practical Tip**: Use for one-off newsletters; migrate if needing sales funnels.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

**Best for**: Advanced automation and CRM.

Top-rated for marketing automation (4.5/5 on G2). Machine learning predicts customer behavior.

**Pros**:
- 900+ integrations (Shopify, Salesforce).
- Site tracking and split testing.
- Conditional content.

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning curve.
- Starts at $29/mo (no free plan beyond trial).

**Pricing**: Lite $29/mo (up to 2,500 contacts).

**Practical Tip**: Segment lists by purchase history for 20–30% higher opens.

### 3. Klaviyo

**Best for**: E-commerce (Shopify users).

Excels in revenue-focused flows; powers brands like Gymshark.

**Pros**:
- Built-in revenue analytics.
- A/B testing on subject lines.
- SMS integration.

**Cons**:
- Less ideal for non-ecom.
- $20/mo minimum.

**Pricing**: Free to 250 contacts; $20–$100+/mo.

**Practical Tip**: Set up abandoned cart flows—recover 10–15% lost sales.

### 4. ConvertKit (now Kit)

**Best for**: Creators and bloggers.

Creator-focused with visual automations.

**Pros**:
- Unlimited emails on free plan (up to 1,000 subs).
- Tagging for segmentation.
- Native landing pages.

**Cons**:
- Basic reporting.
- $15/mo for paid.

**Pricing**: Free; Creator $15/mo.

**Practical Tip**: Use broadcasts for newsletters; automate lead magnets.

### 5. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for**: Multichannel (email + SMS).

Generous free plan: 300 emails/day.

**Pros**:
- Transactional emails.
- Chat support.
- EU-based servers for GDPR.

**Cons**:
- Template library smaller.

**Pricing**: Starter $25/mo.

**Practical Tip**: Combine with WhatsApp for global reach.

### 6. GetResponse

**Best for**: Webinars and funnels.

All-in-one with landing pages, webinars.

**Pros**:
- Autoresponders.
- Conversion funnels.
- Free 30-day trial.

**Cons**:
- Interface dated.

**Pricing**: $19/mo (Email Marketing plan).

**Practical Tip**: Host webinars to boost engagement 3x.

### 7. Constant Contact

**Best for**: Nonprofits and events.

User-friendly for events and donations.

**Pros**:
- Social media tools.
- List-building tools.
- 60-day trial.

**Cons**:
- Expensive for scale ($12–$100+/mo).

**Practical Tip**: Use event registration forms.

### 8. MailerLite

**Best for**: Budget-conscious users.

Feature-rich free plan up to 1,000 subs.

**Pros**:
- Pop-ups and forms.
- A/B testing.
- High deliverability.

**Cons**:
- Limited automations on free.

**Pricing**: Growing Business $10/mo.

**Practical Tip**: Embed forms on-site for organic growth.

### 9. AWeber

**Best for**: Long-term reliability.

25+ years; strong tagging.

**Pros**:
- 150+ integrations.
- Spam testing.

**Cons**:
- Dated design.

**Pricing**: Lite $12.50/mo.

**Practical Tip**: Tag opens/clicks for re-engagement.

### 10. HubSpot Marketing Hub

**Best for**: Growing teams with CRM.

Free tools; scales to enterprise.

**Pros**:
- Full CRM integration.
- Smart content.

**Cons**:
- Free limited to 2,000 emails/mo.

**Pricing**: Starter $20/mo.

**Practical Tip**: Align sales/marketing for closed-loop reporting.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing Software for Your Needs

1. **Assess Your List Size**: Free for <1,000; paid for growth.
2. **Test Deliverability**: Send test campaigns to Gmail/Yahoo.
3. **Trial Multiple**: Most offer 14–30 days free.
4. **Check Integrations**: Must-have for your stack (e.g., WooCommerce).
5. **Focus on ROI**: Track metrics like $ revenue per email.

**Practical Advice**:
- Start simple: Import CSV, segment by engagement.
- A/B test subjects: Emojis boost opens 10–20%.
- Clean lists quarterly to maintain 95%+ deliverability.
- Comply: Double opt-in reduces complaints.

The **best email marketing software** depends on your goals—Mailchimp for ease, ActiveCampaign for power, Klaviyo for sales. Test 2–3 via trials, prioritize automation and analytics.
