# Brevo vs Mailchimp 2024: In-Depth Comparison for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp—the two popular choices—helps narrow it down. This Brevo vs Mailchimp guide focuses on key factors like pricing, features, usability, and more, tailored for businesses deciding between them. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or scaling marketer, we'll provide practical advice to make an informed choice.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo is an all-in-one CRM and marketing platform emphasizing affordability and multichannel communication. It excels in transactional emails, marketing automation, SMS, and chat tools. Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts with 300 emails per day, making it ideal for startups testing waters without commitment.

Practical tip: If your business sends order confirmations or invoices alongside newsletters, Brevo's API for transactional emails integrates seamlessly with e-commerce sites like Shopify or WooCommerce. Start by importing contacts via CSV and segmenting by purchase history for targeted campaigns.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has long been a staple in email marketing, known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and robust audience management. It offers a free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends, with strong analytics and A/B testing. It's particularly popular among content creators and e-commerce stores for its creative templates.

Practical advice: Use Mailchimp's audience dashboard to tag subscribers based on engagement. For example, create a 'high-engagement' segment for users opening 80%+ of emails, then nurture them with exclusive offers to boost retention.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major differentiator in Brevo vs Mailchimp.

| Feature | Brevo | Mailchimp |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Free Plan | Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day | 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month |
| Starter | $25/mo (20k emails) | $13/mo (5k contacts) |
| Growing Businesses | $65/mo (20k contacts) | $20/mo (10k contacts, limited automation) |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom |

Brevo scales better for high-volume senders without contact limits crippling costs, while Mailchimp's Essentials plan adds features like automation at a lower entry but charges per contact.

Practical tip: Calculate your needs using Brevo's pricing calculator—input your list size and send frequency. For Mailchimp, watch for 'overages' on paid plans; budget 20% extra if lists grow unexpectedly.

## Feature Face-Off

### Email Templates and Design

Both offer drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp shines with 100+ responsive templates optimized for mobile. Brevo provides customizable blocks with a focus on personalization variables.

Advice: Test templates on Litmus or Email on Acid before launch. In Brevo, use dynamic content blocks to show product recommendations based on past opens.

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp's automation is classic customer journeys (welcome series, abandoned cart). Brevo offers advanced triggers like SMS follow-ups and webhooks.

Practical: Build a Brevo automation for post-purchase upsells—trigger SMS 3 days after delivery. In Mailchimp, layer behaviors like 'clicked link' for re-engagement.

### Segmentation and List Management

Brevo uses tags and custom fields for granular segments. Mailchimp's groups and tags are user-friendly for behavioral data.

Tip: Clean lists monthly. Export to Google Sheets, remove bounces/hard fails, then re-import to either platform to maintain deliverability above 95%.

### Integrations

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook). Brevo integrates with Zapier, WordPress, and has native e-commerce plugins.

Advice: Prioritize Zapier for custom flows. Connect Brevo to Google Sheets for real-time lead syncing from forms.

### SMS and Multichannel

Brevo includes SMS in higher plans; Mailchimp adds it as an upgrade.

Tip: For US businesses, SMS open rates hit 98%—use Brevo's combo emails + SMS for cart recovery, timing texts 1 hour after abandonment.

## Ease of Use

Mailchimp's interface feels more polished for beginners, with onboarding tours. Brevo's dashboard is straightforward but leans functional.

Practical advice: Spend 30 minutes on each free trial. Mailchimp suits visual creators; Brevo for developers needing API access. Migrate via CSV export/import, mapping fields carefully to avoid data loss.

## Deliverability and Analytics

Both boast high deliverability (Mailchimp ~99%, Brevo ~97% per industry benchmarks). Mailchimp's analytics track revenue per email; Brevo reports on SMS opens.

Tip: Warm up domains gradually—start with 50 sends/day, ramp to full volume over 2 weeks. Monitor spam complaints under 0.1%.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp offers 24/7 email/chat on paid plans. Brevo provides priority support from Starter ($25/mo) with live chat and resources.

Advice: Use community forums first (Reddit's r/Emailmarketing). For urgent issues, Brevo's faster response suits growing teams.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros
- Unlimited contacts on free plan
- Affordable scaling
- Multichannel (email + SMS + chat)

### Brevo Cons
- Daily send limits on free
- Fewer design templates

### Mailchimp Pros
- Intuitive editor
- Excellent analytics
- Vast template library

### Mailchimp Cons
- Contact-based pricing escalates
- Automation locked behind higher tiers

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

Opt for Brevo if you're e-commerce focused, need transactional emails, or prioritize cost per send. Small teams sending 10k+ emails/month save 30-50% vs Mailchimp.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

Choose Mailchimp for creative campaigns, strong reporting, or if you're already in their ecosystem. Ideal for agencies managing multiple clients with varying list sizes.

Practical migration tip: Audit current performance first—export metrics from your old tool. Test parallel campaigns (50/50 split) for 2 weeks post-switch to compare results.

## Conclusion

In Brevo vs Mailchimp, there's no one-size-fits-all. Brevo wins on affordability and multichannel for transactional-heavy businesses; Mailchimp excels in user-friendliness and design for content-driven marketers. Start with free trials, align with your volume and goals, and scale confidently. For most small businesses, Brevo's value proposition edges out in 2024. Whichever you pick, focus on compliant list-building (double opt-in) and consistent testing for 20-30% open rates.
