# Top Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Options for Email Marketing

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're experiencing deliverability issues, need advanced automations, or want more affordable scaling, it's time to explore other platforms.

This roundup covers the top **Mailchimp alternatives** tailored for small businesses, creators, e-commerce stores, and agencies. We'll break down features, pricing, pros/cons, and practical tips to help you switch smoothly. All info is based on current official pricing and features as of 2024.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but escalate quickly (e.g., $299/month for 10k contacts on Essentials). Common pain points include:

- **High costs for larger lists**: Billing based on contacts, not sends.
- **Limited automations**: Advanced workflows require Premium ($350+/month).
- **Deliverability dips**: Recent reports show average rates around 85-90%.
- **Steep learning curve for complex campaigns**.

**Practical advice**: Audit your current setup. Export your list via CSV, check engagement rates (aim for 20-30% open rates), and prioritize platforms with better segmentation and A/B testing.

## Top 7 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. ActiveCampaign

**Best for**: Marketing automation and CRM integration.

ActiveCampaign shines with powerful automations, site tracking, and machine learning for lead scoring. It supports unlimited users and integrates with 900+ apps.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automation builder.
- Split testing and predictive sending.
- Built-in CRM and messaging (SMS, WhatsApp).

**Pricing**: Starts at $29/month (1k contacts, Lite plan). Scales to $149/month for Plus (advanced automations).

**Pros**: Excellent deliverability (95%+), deep personalization.
**Cons**: Overwhelming for beginners.

**Who it's for**: Teams needing sales funnels. **Migration tip**: Use their importer for seamless list transfer; test automations with a subset first.

### 2. ConvertKit (now Kit)

**Best for**: Creators, bloggers, and newsletters.

ConvertKit focuses on simplicity with creator-friendly tools like visual automations, subscriber tagging, and commerce integrations (Gumroad, Teachable).

**Key Features**:
- One-click sign-up forms.
- Broadcasts and sequences.
- Advanced segmentation by behavior.

**Pricing**: Free for 10k subs (limited). Creator plan $29/month (300 subs), scales affordably.

**Pros**: Intuitive UI, great for monetization.
**Cons**: Fewer e-commerce tools than Klaviyo.

**Who it's for**: Solopreneurs. **Practical advice**: Segment by 'purchased' tags for re-engagement campaigns; aim for 1-2 sends/week to avoid fatigue.

### 3. Klaviyo

**Best for**: E-commerce and Shopify users.

Klaviyo excels in data-driven flows for abandoned carts, post-purchase, and SMS. It pulls real-time data from Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.

**Key Features**:
- 350+ integrations.
- AI-optimized send times.
- Revenue tracking per campaign.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails. Paid from $20/month based on contacts.

**Pros**: Top deliverability (97%+), ROI-focused analytics.
**Cons**: E-com heavy; less for non-stores.

**Who it's for**: Online stores. **Migration tip**: Connect your store first, then import historical data for flow backfilling.

### 4. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for**: All-in-one marketing with transactional emails.

Brevo combines marketing, sales CRM, and chat at low costs. Unlimited contacts on higher plans.

**Key Features**:
- SMS, WhatsApp, ads.
- Automation workflows.
- Remove duplicates automatically.

**Pricing**: Free (300/day). Starter $25/month (20k emails).

**Pros**: Cost-effective for high volume, EU-compliant.
**Cons**: UI feels dated.

**Who it's for**: SMBs with multi-channel needs. **Advice**: Use their API for transactional emails to boost deliverability.

### 5. MailerLite

**Best for**: Budget-conscious beginners.

MailerLite offers drag-and-drop editors, pop-ups, and landing pages with high deliverability.

**Key Features**:
- A/B testing free.
- Webinars and surveys.
- RSS-to-email campaigns.

**Pricing**: Free (1k subs, 12k emails/month). Growing Business $10/month (500 subs).

**Pros**: Affordable, fast setup.
**Cons**: Limited advanced CRM.

**Who it's for**: Startups. **Tip**: Leverage pop-ups for list growth; track unsubscribe rates (<0.5% ideal).

### 6. GetResponse

**Best for**: Webinar-focused marketing.

GetResponse includes funnels, landing pages, and conversion funnels alongside email.

**Key Features**:
- Autoresponders and webinars (up to 1k attendees free).
- E-commerce tools.

**Pricing**: $19/month (1k contacts). Scales reasonably.

**Pros**: All-in-one, good support.
**Cons**: Automation builder clunky.

**Who it's for**: Course creators. **Advice**: Start with basic autoresponders, then add webinars for engagement.

### 7. Constant Contact

**Best for**: Non-profits and local businesses.

Simple interface with event management and social ads.

**Key Features**:
- List-building tools.
- AI text generator.

**Pricing**: $12/month (500 contacts).

**Pros**: Easy, AI helpers.
**Cons**: Pricey for scale.

**Who it's for**: Traditional SMBs.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

### Key Factors to Consider

1. **Pricing Model**: Contacts vs. sends (Brevo wins for volume).
2. **Deliverability**: Check third-party benchmarks (e.g., Mail-Tester scores).
3. **Integrations**: Zapier, Shopify, etc.
4. **Support**: Live chat vs. docs.
5. **Scalability**: Free tiers for testing.

**Practical Migration Steps**:

1. Export Mailchimp list (Audience > Export).
2. Sign up for trial, import CSV.
3. Recreate top campaigns/automations.
4. Update website forms (embed codes).
5. Send test campaign to 10% list.
6. Monitor bounces (keep <2%).
7. Cancel Mailchimp after 30 days.

**Pro Tip**: Use tools like Zapier for one-time syncs. Track metrics: opens, clicks, revenue.
