# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp has long been a staple for small businesses and beginners with its user-friendly interface and free plan. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines in omnichannel marketing, especially for ecommerce stores needing SMS, push notifications, and advanced automations.

In this in-depth Omnisend vs Mailchimp comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more. Whether you're scaling an online store or running a service-based business, you'll get practical advice to pick the right one. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free tiers, but their paid plans differ based on contacts and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's plans start with a **free plan** for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Paid tiers include:
- **Essentials**: $13/month (billed annually) for 500 contacts, basic automations.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, advanced automations, A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350/month for 500 contacts, multivariate testing, VIP support.

Costs scale with subscriber count—expect $100+ monthly for 10k contacts on higher plans. Mailchimp charges per contact, even inactive ones.

### Omnisend Pricing
Omnisend's **free plan** supports 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, with core features like automations.
- **Standard**: $16/month (annual) for 500 contacts, unlimited emails, SMS add-on.
- **Pro**: $59/month for 500 contacts, advanced segmentation, high-volume SMS.

Omnisend bills based on contacts but offers unlimited emails in paid plans—a big win for frequent senders. SMS credits are extra (~$1 per 100).

**Practical Advice**: For under 2,500 contacts, Mailchimp's free plan edges out. Ecommerce brands sending daily? Omnisend's unlimited emails save money long-term. Calculate your needs using their pricing calculators.

## Feature Comparison: Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop builder is intuitive, with 100+ responsive templates. It's great for quick newsletters.

Omnisend's builder is ecommerce-focused, with dynamic product blocks that pull from Shopify/WooCommerce. Templates emphasize pop-ups and carts.

**Winner**: Tie for beginners (Mailchimp), Omnisend for stores.

### Automation Workflows
Mailchimp offers customer journeys in Standard+ plans—welcome series, abandoned carts.

Omnisend excels with pre-built ecommerce automations: cart recovery, win-back, cross-sell. It supports omnichannel (email + SMS + push).

**Practical Tip**: Test automations with a small segment first. Omnisend's 9 automation templates save setup time for Shopify users.

### List Segmentation and Personalization
Both segment by behavior, tags, purchases. Mailchimp uses merge fields; Omnisend adds product-based segments (e.g., 'viewed shoes').

Omnisend's behavioral data is richer for retail.

### Integrations
Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).

Omnisend integrates deeply with ecommerce: Shopify, BigCommerce, Klaviyo alternatives, plus SMS gateways.

**Advice**: Check your stack. WordPress bloggers? Mailchimp. DTC brands? Omnisend.

### SMS and Push Notifications
Mailchimp added SMS recently (Premium only, extra cost).

Omnisend bundles SMS/push natively, cheaper for multichannel.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Interface

Mailchimp's dashboard is colorful and beginner-friendly—ideal if you're new to email marketing.

Omnisend feels more professional, with a focus on campaigns and automations tabs. Slight learning curve for non-ecom users.

**Pro Tip**: Use free trials (14-30 days). Mailchimp suits solopreneurs; Omnisend rewards those comfortable with tech.

## Customer Support and Resources

Mailchimp: Email/ticket support (Premium chat/phone). Huge knowledge base, free courses.

Omnisend: 24/7 live chat/email, dedicated managers on Pro. Ecommerce academy with webinars.

**Practical Advice**: Growing pains? Omnisend's responsive chat helps faster. Mailchimp's community forums fill gaps.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Mailchimp if**:
- You're a beginner or small service business.
- Budget under $20/month.
- Need blogging/newsletter focus.

**Pick Omnisend if**:
- Running an ecommerce store (Shopify, etc.).
- Want SMS/push for higher engagement.
- Scale with unlimited sends.

**Migration Tip**: Export lists via CSV. Test imports on free plans. Redirect tracking pixels to avoid deliverability dips.

## Real-World Performance

Benchmarks show Omnisend's ecommerce automations boost revenue 20-30% via SMS (per their case studies). Mailchimp averages 20-30% open rates for newsletters.

**Advice**: Track ROI with UTM tags. A/B test subject lines—personalized ones lift opens 26%.

## Conclusion

Omnisend vs Mailchimp? **Omnisend wins for ecommerce** with superior automations and multichannel. **Mailchimp takes it for beginners** with ease and free access.

Assess your audience: Newsletters? Mailchimp. Sales funnels? Omnisend. Start with trials, monitor metrics like open rates (aim 20%+), clicks (2-5%).
