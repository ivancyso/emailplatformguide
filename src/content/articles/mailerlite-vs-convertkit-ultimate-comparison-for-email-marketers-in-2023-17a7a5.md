# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business. With over 4 billion email users worldwide, email remains the top channel for customer engagement, boasting ROI up to 42:1. But between MailerLite and ConvertKit, which one fits your needs? This in-depth comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, and more for creators, bloggers, and small business owners deciding between these popular platforms.

Both MailerLite and ConvertKit cater to users seeking affordable, powerful email solutions without the complexity of enterprise tools like Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign. MailerLite shines with its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and budget-friendly plans, while ConvertKit excels in creator-focused automation and monetization. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer free plans, making them accessible for startups and solopreneurs.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite's structure is straightforward and subscriber-based:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $9/month for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $59/month for 25,000. Unlimited emails.
- **Advanced**: From $20/month for 1,000 subscribers, adding features like A/B testing and custom HTML.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume needs.

No contracts, monthly billing, and annual discounts up to 16%. Practical tip: Start free and upgrade seamlessly as your list grows—no data migration hassles.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit uses a tiered model focused on creators:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers (recently expanded), with forms, landing pages, and basic broadcasts.
- **Creator**: $15/month for 300-1,000 subscribers, up to $29/month for 3,000. Includes sequences and tagging.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month starting, adding advanced reporting, subscriber scoring, and integrations.
- **Enterprise**: Custom for large lists.

Annual billing saves 20%. Note: ConvertKit charges based on active subscribers, which can save costs if you clean your list regularly.

**Verdict**: MailerLite edges out for sheer affordability, especially for larger lists under 10,000 subscribers. ConvertKit's free plan limits advanced automations, so budget-conscious users may prefer MailerLite. Always check official sites for current rates, as they evolve.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly Interfaces

No one wants a steep learning curve. Both platforms prioritize simplicity.

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Magic
MailerLite's editor is a standout—intuitive blocks for text, images, buttons, and dividers. No coding needed. Dashboard navigation is clean, with quick access to campaigns, automations, and analytics. Onboarding includes video tutorials and templates galore.

**Practical Advice**: Use the popup builder for lead magnets. Set exit-intent triggers to capture 10-20% more subscribers without annoying visitors.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Design
ConvertKit feels like it was built for bloggers. The visual builder uses sections and cards, similar to WordPress. Tagging system simplifies segmentation—no complex lists required.

**Practical Advice**: Leverage the 'one-click upsell' in sequences to boost conversions by 15-30% for course creators.

**Verdict**: MailerLite wins for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for those familiar with content tools.

## Key Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
- **MailerLite**: 80+ responsive templates, full customization. Photo editor built-in.
- **ConvertKit**: Fewer templates (50+), but optimized for newsletters. Strong mobile previews.

**Tip**: Test templates on multiple devices—both deliver 99% inbox rates with authentication.

### Automation and Workflows
- **MailerLite**: Single and multi-step automations, RSS campaigns, A/B testing (paid). Trigger on tags, opens, clicks.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences shine—automated email series based on actions. Visual automation builder in Pro.

**Practical Advice**: For welcome series, MailerLite's simplicity saves time; ConvertKit's tagging automates re-engagement (e.g., win-back flows for inactive subs).

### Landing Pages and Forms
Both offer unlimited pages/forms:
- MailerLite: Popups, embedded forms, countdown timers.
- ConvertKit: Native checkout for products, incentive forms.

**Tip**: A/B test headlines—expect 20-50% lift in opt-ins.

### Segmentation and Analytics
- **MailerLite**: Groups, tags, custom fields. Opens/clicks tracking, revenue reports.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced tagging, subscriber scoring, revenue per subscriber.

**Practical Advice**: Segment by engagement: Send re-engagement campaigns to opens <20%—recover 5-10% of lost subs.

### Integrations
- **MailerLite**: 100+ via Zapier, native WordPress, Google Analytics.
- **ConvertKit**: Deep WooCommerce, Teachable, Zapier (1,000+ apps).

**Tip**: Use Zapier for cross-tool automations, like syncing leads from Typeform to either platform.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, knowledge base, Facebook group.
ConvertKit: Email/ticket support (business hours), extensive Creator Academy videos, podcast.

**Verdict**: MailerLite for instant help; ConvertKit for self-serve resources.

**Cons**:

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:

**Cons**:

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
Small businesses, e-commerce stores, agencies needing popups and websites. Ideal if budget < $20/mo and list <10k.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Bloggers, podcasters, course creators focused on sequences and sales funnels. Great for 1k+ engaged subscribers.

**Practical Advice**: Trial both free plans. Import a test list (under 100 subs) and build a sample automation. Measure setup time and feature fit.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to priorities: MailerLite for versatile, low-cost growth; ConvertKit for creator revenue streams. Neither requires contracts, so experiment. For most beginners, start with MailerLite's free plan and migrate if needed—both support easy exports.

Whichever you pick, focus on list-building: Offer value-packed lead magnets, nurture with consistent value, and analyze opens/clicks weekly. Email marketing success is 80% strategy, 20% tool.
