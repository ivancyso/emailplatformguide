# Top Mailchimp Alternatives for 2024: Best Email Marketing Platforms Compared

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're a small business owner, freelancer, or marketer feeling squeezed by Mailchimp's structure, this roundup explores top contenders that offer better value, advanced features, or simpler interfaces.

We'll compare key players based on real user needs: ease of use, automation capabilities, pricing, deliverability, and integrations. Whether you need something affordable for startups or robust tools for e-commerce, there's an option here. All recommendations draw from official platform docs and common user feedback—no hype, just practical insights.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans jump to $13/month for 500 contacts (Essentials), escalating quickly with audience size. Advanced automations and reporting require higher tiers ($20+). Common pain points include:

- **High costs for scaling**: Legacy pricing grandfathered some users, but new ones face audience-based billing.
- **Limited free automation**: No advanced triggers without upgrading.
- **Deliverability dips**: Recent reports note spam complaints affecting inbox rates.

Switching makes sense if your list exceeds 1,000 or you need e-commerce focus. **Practical tip**: Before migrating, export your Mailchimp audience CSV (under Audience > Export) and test imports on new platforms.

## Top 7 Mailchimp Alternatives

Here are seven strong alternatives, selected for diverse needs like creators, e-commerce, and agencies. Each includes pricing (as of 2024), key features, pros/cons, and who it's best for.

### 1. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

**Pricing**: Free up to 300 emails/day (unlimited contacts); Starter $25/month for 20k emails.

Brevo shines with transactional emails alongside marketing, plus a full CRM. Features include drag-and-drop editor, SMS, chat, and automation workflows.

**Pros**:
- Generous free tier for unlimited contacts.
- High deliverability (98%+ claimed).
- Multi-channel (email, SMS, WhatsApp).

**Cons**:
- Email limits on free plan can constrain high-volume senders.
- Interface feels cluttered for beginners.

**Best for**: SMBs wanting CRM integration without extra tools. **Advice**: Use their automation builder for welcome series—set triggers by sign-up date for quick wins.

### 2. ConvertKit

**Pricing**: Free up to 300 subscribers; Creator $15/month (500 subs), scales to $29 for 1k.

Tailored for creators, ConvertKit excels in newsletters, courses, and tagging. Native commerce for selling digital products.

**Pros**:
- Intuitive for non-marketers.
- Advanced tagging/segmentation.
- One-click unsubscribes boost compliance.

**Cons**:
- No phone support.
- E-commerce features premium-only.

**Best for**: Bloggers and course creators. **Advice**: Leverage 'Visual Automations' for subscriber journeys—tag based on link clicks to personalize follow-ups.

### 3. ActiveCampaign

**Pricing**: Starts at $29/month (1k contacts), up to $149 for 10k.

Powerhouse for automation with CRM, site tracking, and machine learning predictions.

**Pros**:
- Deep automations (if/then branches).
- Lead scoring and split testing.
- 500+ integrations.

**Cons**:
- Steep learning curve.
- No free plan beyond trial.

**Best for**: Agencies and sales teams. **Advice**: Start with their 'Automation Recipes' templates for abandoned cart flows—customize with custom fields from your Mailchimp export.

### 4. Klaviyo

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; scales $20+/month.

E-commerce focused, with revenue tracking and dynamic product recommendations.

**Pros**:
- Shopify/WooCommerce native.
- Predictive analytics for churn.
- A/B testing on subject lines.

**Cons**:
- Less ideal for non-ecom.
- Pricing ties to contacts.

**Best for**: Online stores. **Advice**: Sync customer data via API for personalized flows—focus on post-purchase sequences to boost LTV.

### 5. GetResponse

**Pricing**: Free up to 500 contacts; $19/month (1k subs), includes webinars.

All-in-one with landing pages, funnels, and autoresponders.

**Pros**:
- Built-in webinar hosting.
- Conversion funnels.
- Solid templates.

**Cons**:
- Overwhelming dashboard.
- Support varies by plan.

**Best for**: Marketers needing funnels. **Advice**: Use 'Perfect Timing' delivery to send at peak open times, tested against your Mailchimp data.

### 6. AWeber

**Pricing**: $16.15/month (500 subs), Lite $19 for 1k.

Veteran platform with strong deliverability and simple automations.

**Pros**:
- Reliable support (phone/chat).
- Tag-based automations.
- Spam testing tools.

**Cons**:
- Dated editor.
- Fewer advanced analytics.

**Best for**: Non-tech users. **Advice**: Import segments directly and recreate campaigns— their 'Smart Send' schedules based on subscriber timezones.

### 7. Moosend

**Pricing**: Free up to 1k subs; Pro $9/month (unlimited emails for 500 subs).

Affordable with unlimited emails on paid plans and e-com automations.

**Pros**:
- Flat-rate pricing post-500 subs.
- Popups and landing pages.
- API access.

**Cons**:
- Smaller integration library.
- Newer player.

**Best for**: Budget-conscious startups. **Advice**: Set up 'Behavior-based' triggers for engagement scoring to nurture inactive lists.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: List size? E-com? Automation depth? Match to platforms above.
2. **Trial First**: Most offer 14-30 day trials—import a test list.
3. **Check Integrations**: Zapier covers gaps; native is better.
4. **Migration Steps**:
   - Export Mailchimp lists/segments.
   - Map tags/fields.
   - Recreate top campaigns.
   - Update signup forms (plugins like Zapier help).
   - Monitor deliverability post-switch.
5. **Budget Tip**: Calculate TCO—factor support and features, not just per-contact fees.

**Pro Tip**: Clean your list pre-migration (remove inactives >6 months) to lower costs and boost rates.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ConvertKit, and ActiveCampaign offer flexibility for every budget and scale. For creators, go ConvertKit; e-com picks Klaviyo; all-rounders Brevo or Moosend. Evaluate with your data—most switches pay off in months via lower costs and better engagement.
