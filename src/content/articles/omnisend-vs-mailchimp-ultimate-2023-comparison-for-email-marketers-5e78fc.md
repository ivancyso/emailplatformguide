# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools designed for businesses looking to engage customers through email campaigns, automations, and more. But they cater to slightly different needs: Mailchimp excels in general marketing for small businesses, while Omnisend shines in ecommerce with multichannel support.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and real-world applications. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or ecommerce store manager, you'll get practical advice to decide which fits your workflow. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing since 2001. It's known for its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and free plan, making it accessible for beginners. With over 12 million users, it supports email, landing pages, ads, and basic automation.

**Key Strengths:**
- User-friendly interface ideal for non-technical users.
- Extensive template library.
- Integrates with popular tools like Shopify, WordPress, and Google Analytics.

**Best For:** Blogs, service-based businesses, and nonprofits starting out.

Practical tip: Start with Mailchimp's free plan (up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month) to test basic campaigns before scaling.

## Overview of Omnisend

Launched in 2014, Omnisend targets ecommerce brands with omnichannel marketing—email, SMS, web push notifications. It's built for stores on Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce, emphasizing automation for cart recovery and customer journeys.

**Key Strengths:**
- Advanced ecommerce automations like abandoned cart emails.
- Multichannel campaigns (email + SMS + push).
- Pop-up builders for list growth.

**Best For:** Online stores focused on sales funnels and retention.

Practical tip: Use Omnisend's free plan (up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month) for Shopify stores to set up your first abandoned cart flow in under 30 minutes.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major factor when choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp. Both offer free tiers, but paid plans scale differently.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month.
- **Essentials:** $13/month (50k sends) to $350+/month (500k sends).
- **Standard:** $20/month+ with automations and A/B testing.
- **Premium:** $350+/month for advanced segmentation.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts and sends, with overages adding up quickly.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month.
- **Standard:** $16/month (500 contacts) to $59/month (10k contacts).
- **Pro:** $59/month (500 contacts) to $1,044/month (100k contacts), includes SMS.

Omnisend is contact-based, often cheaper for ecommerce lists under 10k.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your monthly sends and contacts. For 2,500 contacts with high-volume ecommerce emails, Omnisend Standard ($36/month) beats Mailchimp Standard ($50+). Always check current pricing on their sites as it evolves.

## Feature Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly with 100+ templates. Omnisend offers ecommerce-specific blocks (product carousels, order tables) and responsive designs.

**Winner:** Omnisend for stores; Mailchimp for general use.

### Automation Workflows
- **Mailchimp:** Single, customer journey, and tag-based automations. Great for welcome series.
- **Omnisend:** Pre-built workflows for abandoned carts, win-back, cross-sell. Supports SMS triggers.

Practical tip: In Omnisend, segment by purchase behavior for 20-30% higher open rates in recovery emails.

### Segmentation and Analytics
Both offer list segmentation. Mailchimp has behavioral tracking; Omnisend excels in revenue-per-recipient metrics, tying emails to sales.

**H3: A/B Testing**
Mailchimp includes it in Standard+ plans. Omnisend offers unlimited in all paid plans.

### Multichannel Features
Omnisend supports SMS (pay-per-use) and push notifications. Mailchimp focuses on email with add-ons for ads.

**Practical Advice:** If your audience prefers mobile, Omnisend's SMS can boost engagement by 98% (industry average). Test with small batches.

## Ease of Use

Mailchimp scores high for simplicity—setup in minutes. Omnisend has a steeper learning curve due to advanced features but includes guided onboarding and video tutorials.

**Practical Tip:** Mailchimp suits non-marketers; spend 1-2 hours learning Omnisend's dashboard for ecommerce ROI.

## Customer Support

- **Mailchimp:** Email/ticket support; chat for Premium. Community forums are robust.
- **Omnisend:** 24/7 live chat/email, dedicated manager for Pro. Higher satisfaction ratings (4.7/5 on G2).

**Winner:** Omnisend for responsive help.

Practical advice: Use free trials (14-30 days) to test support before committing.

## Integrations

Both integrate with 100+ apps.
- Mailchimp: Zapier, Facebook, QuickBooks.
- Omnisend: Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives, Google Tag Manager.

**Practical Tip:** For Shopify users, Omnisend's native app syncs orders automatically—save hours vs manual CSV imports in Mailchimp.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros:**
- Free plan generosity.
- Brand recognition.
- Simple analytics.

**Cons:**
- Expensive at scale.
- Limited ecommerce automations.
- No native SMS.

### Omnisend
**Pros:**
- Ecommerce-focused features.
- Cost-effective for mid-size lists.
- Omnichannel.

**Cons:**
- Smaller template variety.
- Less ideal for non-ecommerce.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp if:** You're a beginner, service business, or need basic newsletters. Ideal for lists under 5k with low sends.
- **Choose Omnisend if:** You run an online store, need cart recovery, or want SMS/push. Perfect for revenue-driven campaigns.

Practical advice: Audit your goals. If 40%+ revenue from email, prioritize Omnisend. Migrate data via CSV for easy switch.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce with superior automations and multichannel, while Mailchimp remains king for simplicity and free entry. Test both free plans: send a campaign, build an automation, check analytics.

Ultimately, the best choice aligns with your business—general marketing (Mailchimp) or sales-focused (Omnisend). Whichever you pick, consistent testing and segmentation will drive results. Ready to choose? Start your free trial today and scale your email marketing.
