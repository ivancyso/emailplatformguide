# Best Email Marketing Tools for Nonprofits: Free and Discounted Options to Boost Donor Engagement

Email marketing remains one of the most cost-effective ways for nonprofits to connect with donors, volunteers, and supporters. With tight budgets, nonprofits need platforms that offer robust features without breaking the bank. This roundup highlights the best email marketing tools tailored for nonprofits, focusing on free tiers, discounts, and features like donor segmentation, compliance with CAN-SPAM and GDPR, and integration with fundraising CRMs.

We'll cover key considerations, top platforms, and practical implementation tips to help you select and launch campaigns that drive real results.

## Why Email Marketing is Essential for Nonprofits

Nonprofits rely on storytelling to inspire action. Email delivers personalized messages directly to inboxes, with average open rates of 20-30% for nonprofit campaigns—far higher than social media. According to benchmarks from platforms like Mailchimp, nonprofit emails see strong engagement when segmented by donor history.

Key benefits include:
- **Cost efficiency**: Many tools offer free plans for small lists (under 1,000-2,000 contacts).
- **Measurable ROI**: Track opens, clicks, and donations linked to emails.
- **Automation**: Welcome series for new donors or year-end appeals.

In 2023, nonprofits using email saw up to 4x higher donation rates from automated nurture sequences.

## Key Features to Prioritize in Nonprofit Email Platforms

When evaluating tools, focus on these nonprofit-specific needs:

### Donor Segmentation and Personalization
Divide lists by giving history, location, or interests. Tools with dynamic content blocks adapt messages automatically.

### Fundraising Integrations
Seamless connections to Stripe, PayPal, Donorbox, or CRMs like Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud.

### Compliance and Deliverability
Built-in unsubscribe tools, list cleaning, and high sender scores to avoid spam folders.

### Reporting and Analytics
Metrics on revenue per email, donor retention, and A/B testing.

### Mobile Optimization and Templates
Responsive designs for quick campaign builds, with nonprofit-themed templates (e.g., impact reports).

Budget tip: Start with free plans and upgrade as your list grows.

## Top Email Marketing Tools for Nonprofits

Here are seven standout platforms, selected for their nonprofit discounts, ease of use, and proven track records. All offer free trials or tiers.

### 1. Mailchimp
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month—ideal for startups. Nonprofits qualify for 15% discounts via TechSoup. Features include audience segmentation, automation, and A/B testing. Integrates with 250+ apps, including Classy and EveryAction.

**Pros**: Intuitive drag-and-drop editor, strong analytics.
**Cons**: Free plan limits advanced automations.
**Best for**: Small nonprofits under 5,000 contacts.

### 2. Constant Contact
Offers a 60-day free trial and nonprofit discounts up to 30% through TechSoup. Excels in event management and surveys, perfect for volunteer drives. High deliverability (99%) and one-click Google Analytics integration.

**Pros**: Excellent customer support, easy list imports.
**Cons**: Pricing starts at $12/month post-trial.
**Best for**: Event-focused orgs.

### 3. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)
Free plan for 300 emails/day with unlimited contacts. Nonprofit pricing upon request. Standout transactional emails for donation receipts and SMS integration.

**Pros**: Generous free tier, advanced automation.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve.
**Best for**: Growing lists needing SMS.

### 4. ActiveCampaign
Powerful automation for donor journeys. Discounts for nonprofits via partnerships. Starts at $29/month, but robust CRM features justify it.

**Pros**: Deep personalization, site tracking.
**Cons**: Overkill for beginners.
**Best for**: Mid-sized nonprofits with complex funnels.

### 5. HubSpot Email Marketing
Free tools within the CRM suite for up to 2,000 emails/month. Nonprofits get premium features via grants. Integrates with fundraising pipelines.

**Pros**: All-in-one CRM, free forever basics.
**Cons**: Upsell pressure.
**Best for**: Orgs using HubSpot ecosystem.

### 6. DonorPerfect Online Express
CRM with built-in email, tailored for nonprofits. Custom pricing, often discounted. Tracks gifts directly from emails.

**Pros**: End-to-end donor management.
**Cons**: Less flexible for non-CRM users.
**Best for**: Fundraising-heavy missions.

### 7. Keap (formerly Infusionsoft)
Automation-focused with nonprofit templates. Discounts available. Handles sales pipelines for earned income.

**Pros**: Lead scoring for major donors.
**Cons**: Higher cost ($199/month+).
**Best for**: Larger orgs.

## How to Choose the Right Email Marketing Platform

1. **Assess Your List Size**: Free for <1,000? Mailchimp or Brevo.
2. **Test Integrations**: Import a sample CSV and connect to your CRM.
3. **Run a Trial Campaign**: Send to 100 contacts, monitor metrics.
4. **Check Discounts**: Apply via TechSoup or direct nonprofit programs.
5. **Scale Plan**: Ensure pricing grows predictably.

Compare costs: A 5,000-contact nonprofit might pay $0-50/month on free/discounted plans.

## Practical Advice for Launching Nonprofit Email Campaigns

### Build Your List Legally
Use double opt-in forms on your website. Offer lead magnets like impact reports or volunteer guides. Comply with GDPR/CCPA by segmenting international lists.

### Craft High-Engagement Emails
- **Subject Lines**: "Your Gift Changed 5 Lives This Month" (personal, urgent).
- **Content**: 60/40 story-to-ask ratio. Use images of real impact.
- **Frequency**: Monthly newsletters, quarterly appeals.

### Segment for Better Results
- New donors: Welcome series.
- Lapsed: Re-engagement with incentives.
- High-value: Exclusive updates.

### A/B Test Everything
Test send times (Tues-Thurs, 8-10 AM), images, and CTAs. Tools like Mailchimp make this one-click.

### Analyze and Optimize
Aim for 25%+ open rates. Track donation links with UTM parameters. Clean lists quarterly to maintain deliverability.

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Over-sending: Cap at 4-6 emails/year for appeals.
- Generic content: Always personalize.
- Ignoring mobile: 50%+ opens on phones.

Real-world example: A food bank using Constant Contact segmentation increased repeat donations by 35%.
