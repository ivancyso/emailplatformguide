# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your audience engagement and revenue. MailerLite and ConvertKit are popular choices for creators, bloggers, and small businesses. Both offer automation, landing pages, and subscriber management, but they cater to slightly different needs. This in-depth comparison covers pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide.

## Pricing Breakdown

Pricing is often the first factor when selecting an email tool. Both platforms scale with subscriber count, but their structures differ.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite stands out for affordability:
- **Free plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Ideal for testing.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers (unlimited emails).
- **Advanced**: From $21/month, adds features like A/B testing and custom HTML.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for large lists.

No contracts, monthly billing, and high deliverability (95%+ claimed). Practical tip: Start free to build your list before upgrading.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now Kit) focuses on creators with tiered plans:
- **Free trial**: 14 days, no card needed.
- **Creator**: $15/month (billed annually) for up to 300 subscribers; $29/month for 1,000.
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month annually for 300 subs; adds advanced automations, revenue tracking.
- **Enterprise**: Custom for 10k+ subs.

Unlimited emails and sends. Tip: Annual billing saves 20%. No perpetual free plan, so budget for growth.

**Verdict**: MailerLite wins for beginners/small lists; ConvertKit for serious creators willing to invest.

## Ease of Use and User Interface

### MailerLite

Intuitive drag-and-drop editor, clean dashboard. Setup takes minutes: import contacts, design campaigns visually. Mobile app for on-the-go management. Great for non-tech users—practical advice: Use pre-built templates to launch your first campaign in under 30 minutes.

### ConvertKit

Creator-centric interface with visual automations builder. Steeper learning curve for tagging/sequences but powerful once mastered. Forms and landing pages are simple to embed. Tip: Watch their tutorials for quick onboarding; ideal if you're comfortable with workflows.

**Verdict**: MailerLite for simplicity; ConvertKit for depth.

## Email Editor and Design Features

Both offer responsive templates.

### MailerLite

Drag-and-drop with 80+ templates. Custom blocks for images, buttons, RSS feeds. Photo editor built-in. Supports HTML for pros. Practical: Test mobile previews before sending to avoid formatting issues.

### ConvertKit

Clean editor focused on text-rich emails for creators. Fewer templates (20+), but customizable. Strong for newsletters with commerce blocks. Tip: Use 'Commerce' for one-click product sales in emails.

**Verdict**: Tie—MailerLite for variety, ConvertKit for storytelling.

## Automation and Workflows

Automation drives engagement.

### MailerLite

Basic to advanced: Welcome series, abandoned cart, RSS campaigns. Trigger-based with delays/if conditions. Time-saving tip: Automate re-engagement for inactive subscribers to boost open rates.

### ConvertKit

Excels here—visual builder for complex sequences. Tag-based triggers (e.g., 'purchased' tag starts upsell). One-click upsells. Practical advice: Map customer journeys with tags for personalized drips, increasing conversions by nurturing leads.

**Verdict**: ConvertKit superior for sophisticated automations.

## Segmentation and List Management

Targeted sends improve results.

### MailerLite

Groups, segments by activity, custom fields. Simple but effective. Tip: Segment by engagement (opens/clicks) for higher ROI campaigns.

### ConvertKit

Tag-based system shines—auto-apply tags from actions/forms. Advanced scoring. Advice: Use tags for behavior (e.g., 'lead magnet downloader') to send hyper-relevant content.

**Verdict**: ConvertKit for precision targeting.

## Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

Grow lists without a website.

### MailerLite

Unlimited landing pages/forms/popups. Drag-and-drop, A/B testing. High-converting templates. Tip: Embed popups with exit-intent to capture 10-20% more leads.

### ConvertKit

Native pages, forms, ephemerals (flyouts). Commerce integration. Practical: Use 'incentive' forms for lead magnets like ebooks.

**Verdict**: MailerLite edges with popups; both strong.

## Integrations and E-commerce

Connect to your stack.

Both integrate with Stripe, PayPal, Zapier (1,000+ apps). MailerLite: WooCommerce, WordPress. ConvertKit: Gumroad, Teachable—creator-focused. Tip: Use Zapier for custom flows, like syncing podcast listens to tags.

## Analytics and Reporting

Track performance.

### MailerLite

Opens, clicks, bounces, revenue tracking. Heatmaps, link analytics. Export data easily.

### ConvertKit

Detailed: Subscriber growth, revenue per subscriber, A/B results. Tag activity reports. Advice: Monitor 'predicted revenue' in Pro to optimize sequences.

**Verdict**: ConvertKit for revenue insights.

## Customer Support

### MailerLite

24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Fast responses (under 1 hour).

### ConvertKit

Email, chat (business hours), extensive academy/videos. Community forum.

**Verdict**: MailerLite for always-on support.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite Pros
- Affordable/free tier
- User-friendly
- Popups/landing pages

Cons: Less advanced tagging.

### ConvertKit Pros
- Powerful automations/tags
- Creator tools (commerce)
- Revenue analytics

Cons: Pricier, no free plan.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Beginners/bootstrappers
- Small businesses under 5k subs
- Need popups/simple automations

Practical: If budget < $20/mo and quick setup matter.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers/podcasters/creators
- Scaling lists with complex journeys
- Monetization focus

Tip: Invest if you sell digital products.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit? MailerLite for cost-effective, easy email marketing—perfect starters. ConvertKit for creators needing automation depth and monetization. Test both: MailerLite's free plan, ConvertKit's trial. Consider your list size, tech comfort, and goals. For most solopreneurs, start with MailerLite; upgrade to ConvertKit at 1k+ subs. Whichever you pick, consistent value-driven emails will grow your business. (1,248 words)
