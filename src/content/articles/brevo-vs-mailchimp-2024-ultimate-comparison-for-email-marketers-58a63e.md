# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are popular platforms trusted by millions, but they cater to slightly different needs. Brevo shines with its transactional email focus and flexible pricing, while Mailchimp is renowned for its user-friendly interface and robust marketing automation.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or marketing manager scaling up, you'll get practical advice to pick the right tool. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the deciding factor. Both offer free plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo uses a pay-as-you-go model based on emails sent, not contacts stored. This is ideal if you have a large list but send infrequently.

- **Free Plan**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails per day.
- **Starter**: Starts at €25/month for 20,000 emails/month.
- **Business**: €65/month for 20,000 emails + advanced automation.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing with dedicated support.

Practical tip: If your list exceeds 10,000 contacts, Brevo saves money long-term. Overages are just €0.001 per email, avoiding surprise fees.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp tiers by contacts and features, which suits consistent senders.

- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 5,000 contacts, 50,000 emails.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 5,000 contacts + automation.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for large lists and advanced segmentation.

Practical tip: Mailchimp's free tier is great for testing, but costs rise quickly with growth. Use their pricing calculator to forecast.

**Winner**: Brevo for large lists; Mailchimp for beginners under 5,000 contacts.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp offers 100+ drag-and-drop templates with high customization. Brevo has 40+ responsive templates, plus a builder for transactional emails.

Advice: Start with Mailchimp if design is key—no coding needed. Brevo integrates better for e-commerce transactional flows like order confirmations.

### Automation and Workflows
Both excel here.
- **Brevo**: Multi-channel automations (email, SMS, WhatsApp). Classic and advanced workflows with A/B testing.
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journey Builder for complex sequences, behavioral triggers.

Practical advice: Use Brevo for omnichannel (e.g., abandoned cart SMS). Mailchimp for nurture sequences based on opens/clicks. Test simple drip campaigns first.

### List Segmentation and Analytics
Segmentation is crucial for relevance.
- **Brevo**: Tags, custom fields, real-time segmentation. Analytics include open rates, bounces, revenue tracking.
- **Mailchimp**: Advanced segments by behavior/purchases. Detailed reports with AI insights.

Tip: Segment by engagement (e.g., last opened >30 days) on either to boost deliverability above 90%.

### Additional Features
Brevo: Built-in CRM, landing pages, chat. Mailchimp: Forms, popups, ads integration.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's intuitive dashboard feels like Canva—perfect for non-marketers. Drag-and-drop everything.

Brevo is straightforward but has a slight learning curve for transactional features. Onboarding includes tutorials.

Advice: If your team lacks tech skills, go Mailchimp. Spend 15 minutes on Brevo's academy for quick mastery.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Both maintain high deliverability (95%+). Mailchimp's established IP reputation helps new users. Brevo excels in EU compliance (GDPR-focused).

Tip: Warm up IPs gradually: Start with 100 emails/day, monitor bounces <2%. Use double opt-in always.

## Customer Support Options

- **Brevo**: Email, chat (paid plans), 24/7 phone for Enterprise.
- **Mailchimp**: 24/7 email/chat, extensive help center.

Practical: Mailchimp's self-serve resources are superior for free users. Upgrade Brevo for priority help.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Shopify, Google Analytics). Brevo: 300+ via Zapier, native e-commerce plugins.

Both scale well—Brevo handles millions of emails; Mailchimp powers big brands like Intuit.

Advice: Check Zapier for niche tools. Brevo's API is developer-friendly for custom builds.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros
- Unlimited contacts
- Affordable for high-volume
- Multi-channel

Cons: Fewer templates, steeper curve.

### Mailchimp Pros
- Beginner-friendly
- Rich analytics
- Creative assets

Cons: Expensive scaling, contact limits.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?
E-commerce stores, agencies with large lists, international teams needing SMS/WhatsApp. Save 50%+ vs Mailchimp at 50k contacts.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
Starters, content creators, nonprofits. Free plan + ease wins for quick wins.

Practical advice: Trial both (14-30 days free). Import a test list, send a campaign, compare metrics.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp? No one-size-fits-all. Brevo edges for cost-efficiency and versatility; Mailchimp for simplicity and polish. Assess your list size, send frequency, and tech comfort.

For most small businesses (under 5k contacts), start with Mailchimp. Scaling? Switch to Brevo.
