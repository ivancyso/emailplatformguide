# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools trusted by thousands of businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a veteran in the space, ideal for beginners and general marketers, while Omnisend shines for ecommerce brands with its omnichannel capabilities.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, customer support, and real-world performance. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or scaling ecommerce store, you'll get practical advice to pick the right one. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends—great for testing. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for Premium (50,000+ contacts). Key features like advanced automation and A/B testing unlock at higher tiers.

**Pros:** Predictable monthly pricing, no overage fees for sends.
**Cons:** Quickly outgrows free plan; customer support is email-only on lower tiers.

**Practical Tip:** If your list is under 2,000 and you send monthly newsletters, stick to Essentials ($13-$20/month). Calculate costs using their [pricing calculator](https://mailchimp.com/pricing/).

### Omnisend Pricing

Omnisend's free plan allows 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, plus unlimited popups. Paid plans begin at $16/month (Standard) for 500 contacts, up to $59/month for Pro (2,500 contacts). It's contact-based, with no send limits—ideal for high-volume ecommerce.

**Pros:** Generous free plan for popups/SMS; scales well for stores.
**Cons:** SMS credits extra ($0.02-$0.04 each); higher tiers needed for advanced reporting.

**Practical Tip:** Ecommerce sellers: Use the free plan for popups to build lists fast. Budget $16-36/month initially. Check [Omnisend pricing](https://www.omnisend.com/pricing/) for your contact volume.

**Winner:** Omnisend for ecommerce (unlimited sends); Mailchimp for low-volume beginners.

## Core Features: Automation, Templates, and Segmentation

### Email Automation

Mailchimp offers single, classic, and multi-step automations. Journey Builder (Premium) creates complex customer journeys. Practical for abandoned cart or welcome series.

Omnisend excels with pre-built ecommerce workflows: cart recovery, win-back, cross-sell. Omnichannel automation includes SMS/push—triggers like 'browsed but didn't buy' boost revenue 20-30% per their case studies.

**Advice:** Test Omnisend's workflows if you have Shopify/WooCommerce; Mailchimp suits service-based newsletters.

### Templates and Design

Mailchimp: 100+ drag-and-drop templates, mobile-responsive. Content Studio aids creation.

Omnisend: 250+ ecommerce-optimized templates, product blocks auto-pull from stores. Editor is intuitive for non-designers.

**Practical Tip:** Import your branding in both. Use Mailchimp for custom HTML; Omnisend for dynamic product feeds.

### List Segmentation and Analytics

Mailchimp: Tags, groups, behavioral segments. Reports show opens/clicks/revenue.

Omnisend: Advanced segments (e.g., 'high-value repeat buyers'). Revenue tracking per campaign—crucial for ROI.

**Winner:** Omnisend for data-driven ecommerce segmentation.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is beginner-friendly, with tutorials everywhere. Setup takes minutes.

Omnisend focuses on ecommerce setup: One-click Shopify integration, guided onboarding.

**Practical Advice:** Newbies pick Mailchimp. Ecom owners: Omnisend's wizard imports orders/customers instantly. Both score 4.5+ on G2/Capterra for usability.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp: 300+ integrations (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Scales to enterprises.

Omnisend: Native Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce. Plus Klaviyo-like SMS/push via Zapier.

**Tip:** Verify your stack. Mailchimp for broad CRM; Omnisend for store platforms.

## Customer Support and Community

Mailchimp: 24/7 email/chat on paid plans; huge knowledge base.

Omnisend: Live chat/email, priority for Pro. Active Facebook community.

Both responsive, but Omnisend praised for personalized ecommerce help.

## Real User Reviews and Performance

From G2 (2024 data):
- Mailchimp: 4.4/5, loved for simplicity (10M+ users).
- Omnisend: 4.6/5, tops for ecommerce automation.

Case Study: Gymshark uses Mailchimp for mass campaigns; beauty brands like Glossier prefer Omnisend for personalization.

**Pro Tip:** Start free trials (14-30 days). Track metrics: Aim for 20-30% open rates, 2-5% clicks.

## Who Should Choose Which?

**Pick Mailchimp if:**
- General marketing/blogging.
- Budget under $20/month.
- Need creative templates/community.

**Pick Omnisend if:**
- Ecommerce/Shopify store.
- Want SMS/push omnichannel.
- Focus on revenue recovery.

**Migration Advice:** Export CSV from old tool. Both import seamlessly. Test campaigns first.

## Conclusion: Your Next Steps

Neither is 'best' overall—Mailchimp wins for versatility and beginners; Omnisend dominates ecommerce with superior automation and ROI tools. For most choosing between them, assess your niche: General SMB? Mailchimp. Online store? Omnisend.

**Action Plan:** 1. Sign up for free trials. 2. Import 100 contacts, run a test automation. 3. Compare your results after 2 weeks.
