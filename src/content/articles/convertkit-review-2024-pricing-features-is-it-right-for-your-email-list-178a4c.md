## What is ConvertKit?

ConvertKit, formerly known as ConvertKit, is an email marketing platform designed specifically for creators. Launched in 2013 by Nathan Barry, it started as a simple newsletter tool and has evolved into a full-featured automation and commerce platform. Unlike general-purpose tools like Mailchimp, ConvertKit prioritizes tagging subscribers, visual automations, and selling digital products directly through email.

Key differentiators:
- **Creator-centric**: Built by a creator for creators, with features like subscriber tagging instead of rigid segments.
- **No caps on sends**: Send to your entire list without extra fees.
- **Commerce tools**: Sell courses, ebooks, and memberships seamlessly.

ConvertKit powers newsletters for over 100,000 creators, including big names like Pat Flynn and Amy Porterfield. It's not ideal for e-commerce stores with massive transactional volumes but shines for audience-building.

## Key Features of ConvertKit

ConvertKit packs a punch with features that make email marketing straightforward yet powerful. Here's a breakdown:

### Forms and Landing Pages

ConvertKit's forms are embeddable on your site or standalone. Use inline, pop-up, or fixed bar forms with customizable designs—no coding required.

**Practical advice**: Start with a simple opt-in form offering a lead magnet like a free ebook. Use the form builder's A/B testing (available in Pro plans) to test headlines. For landing pages, drag-and-drop templates convert well for webinars or product launches. Pro tip: Integrate with your website via JavaScript snippet for 20-30% higher opt-in rates.

### Email Sequences and Broadcasts

Broadcasts are one-off emails; sequences are automated drip campaigns. The visual editor mimics Gmail, making it intuitive.

**Practical advice**: For onboarding, create a 5-email welcome sequence: Email 1 thanks them, Email 2 delivers the lead magnet, Emails 3-5 nurture with value. Tag opens/clicks to segment automatically—e.g., tag 'interested-in-course' for targeted follow-ups.

### Automations

ConvertKit's automation builder is flowchart-style, easy for beginners.

**Practical advice**: Set up a 'cart abandonment' automation: If someone clicks a product link but doesn't buy, trigger a reminder 24 hours later. Use subscriber scores (Pro feature) to prioritize engaged users. Test automations with a small segment first to avoid spam flags.

### Tagging and Segmentation

Forget complex segments—ConvertKit uses tags applied via actions (opens, clicks, purchases).

**Practical advice**: Tag 'podcast-listener' for audio fans, then send tailored content. This behavioral segmentation boosts open rates by 15-25% based on user reports.

### Commerce and Integrations

Sell digital products with one-click upsells. Integrates with Stripe, Gumroad, Teachable, Zapier (1,000+ apps), and WordPress plugins.

**Practical advice**: For course creators, link ConvertKit to Teachable for automated enrollment emails. Use Zapier to sync podcast listens from Spotify to tags.

### Analytics

Track opens, clicks, revenue per subscriber. Heatmaps show engagement.

**Practical advice**: Aim for 40%+ open rates; if lower, clean your list by removing inactives quarterly.

## Pros and Cons of ConvertKit

### Pros

- Strong for creators (tagging, commerce).

### Cons
- No native A/B for broadcasts (Pro only for forms).

- No phone support.
- Steeper for non-creators.
