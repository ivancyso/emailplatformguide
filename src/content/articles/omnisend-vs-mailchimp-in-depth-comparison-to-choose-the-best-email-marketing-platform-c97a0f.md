# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison to Choose the Best Email Marketing Platform

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can be tricky when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are popular platforms, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp has long been a go-to for beginners and general marketers with its user-friendly interface and robust email tools. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines in omnichannel marketing, especially for ecommerce stores integrating email, SMS, and push notifications.

In this omnisend vs mailchimp comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're a small business owner, ecommerce entrepreneur, or marketing manager, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. We'll focus on real-world use cases, drawing from official platform details and user feedback trends as of 2024.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, launched in 2001, powers over 12 million users worldwide. It's known for its drag-and-drop email builder, audience segmentation, and automation workflows. Ideal for bloggers, nonprofits, and service-based businesses, it offers a free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month.

**Key strengths:**
- Intuitive editor for non-designers.
- Advanced A/B testing.
- Built-in CRM for contact management.

However, as your list grows, costs rise quickly, and advanced ecommerce features require higher tiers or integrations.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend targets ecommerce brands with seamless Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce integrations. It emphasizes omnichannel campaigns—combining email, SMS, and web push for higher engagement. Free for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails monthly, it scales well for growing stores.

**Key strengths:**
- Pre-built ecommerce automations like cart abandonment.
- SMS and push notifications included.
- Pop-up forms for list building.

Omnisend's focus on revenue recovery makes it a favorite for online retailers aiming to boost sales directly from campaigns.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major factor in the omnisend vs mailchimp debate. Both use tiered plans based on contacts and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing (as of 2024):
| Plan | Contacts | Price/Month | Key Features |
|------|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Free | Up to 500 | $0 | Basic emails, templates |
| Essentials | Up to 5k | $13+ | Automation, A/B testing |
| Standard | Up to 5k | $20+ | Behavioral targeting |
| Premium | Up to 5k | $350+ | Advanced analytics |

Mailchimp charges extra for SMS ($10+ per 500 messages) and removes features like landing pages from lower plans.

### Omnisend Pricing:
| Plan | Contacts | Price/Month | Key Features |
|------|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Free | Up to 250 | $0 | 500 emails, basic SMS |
| Standard | Up to 500 | $16 | Unlimited emails, automations |
| Pro | Up to 500 | $59 | Advanced segmentation, A/B |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | API access, dedicated support |

**Practical advice:** For small lists (<1k contacts), Mailchimp's free plan edges out. But Omnisend becomes cheaper for ecommerce with SMS included in Pro plans. Calculate your needs using their pricing calculators—factor in sends, not just contacts.

## Features Head-to-Head: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop builder is beginner-friendly with 100+ responsive templates. Customize with code if needed. Omnisend offers product-focused blocks (e.g., dynamic product feeds) and 250+ ecommerce templates, pulling live data from your store.

**Tip:** Test templates on mobile—both score high, but Omnisend's product carousels convert better for shops.

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp supports single, recurring, and date-based automations with branching logic in higher plans. Omnisend excels here with 12+ pre-built ecommerce flows (welcome series, back-in-stock, win-back). Add SMS/push for multi-channel journeys.

**Practical advice:** For cart recovery, Omnisend's omnichannel flows recover 20-30% more revenue per industry benchmarks. Set up Mailchimp's if you're email-only.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Both allow tag-based and behavioral segmentation. Mailchimp uses predictive segmentation in Premium. Omnisend leverages purchase history for hyper-personalized sends, like 'bought X, recommend Y'.

### Integrations

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Omnisend focuses on 140+ ecommerce tools (Klaviyo alternatives aren't needed), plus Google/FB ads.

**Pro tip:** If you're on Shopify, Omnisend installs in minutes—no Zapier required.

### Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp provides revenue tracking, heatmaps, and compare tools. Omnisend's dashboard highlights ROI with attributed revenue from all channels.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners—its interface feels like a simple newsletter tool. Onboarding tutorials abound.

Omnisend has a steeper curve for non-ecommerce users but guided setup wizards simplify automations.

Support: Mailchimp offers 24/7 chat/email (paid plans); priority phone in Premium. Omnisend provides live chat, email, and a knowledge base; phone for Pro+.

**Advice:** Start with free trials. Migrate lists via CSV—both support it seamlessly.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros:
- Generous free plan.
- Strong community resources.
- Versatile for non-ecommerce.

**Cons:**
- Expensive scaling.
- Limited omnichannel.
- Feature paywalls.

### Omnisend Pros:
- Ecommerce-first automations.
- Cost-effective SMS/push.
- High deliverability (99%+).

**Cons:**
- Less ideal for non-stores.
- Fewer general templates.
- Smaller user base.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Bloggers, agencies, or B2B marketers focused on newsletters.
- Teams needing creative freedom without store data.
- Budget-conscious starters under 1k contacts.

**Migration tip:** Export from current tools and import segments directly.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend?

- Ecommerce stores (Shopify, etc.) wanting cart recovery.
- Brands scaling to SMS/push for 2-3x engagement.
- Revenue-focused teams tracking ROAS.

**Setup advice:** Install the store app first, then build a cart abandonment flow—test with a small segment.

## Conclusion

In the omnisend vs mailchimp showdown, there's no one-size-fits-all winner. Mailchimp suits versatile, email-centric campaigns with its polish and free tier. Omnisend dominates for ecommerce with omnichannel power and better value at scale.

Assess your business: Pure email/content? Go Mailchimp. Online store with abandoned carts? Omnisend. Both offer 14-day trials—sign up, import 100 contacts, and run a test campaign. Track open rates, clicks, and revenue to decide.
