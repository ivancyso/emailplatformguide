# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2023

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options, pitting Omnisend against Mailchimp is a common debate, especially for small businesses and ecommerce stores. Both tools offer powerful features, but they cater to slightly different needs. In this guide, we'll break down their key differences in pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're scaling an online store or nurturing leads for a service-based business, understanding Omnisend vs Mailchimp head-to-head will save you time and money.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing since 2001. It's known for its intuitive interface and all-in-one marketing platform. Ideal for beginners, Mailchimp supports email campaigns, automations, landing pages, and social media ads.

Key strengths include a generous free plan (up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month) and extensive templates. However, as your list grows, costs rise quickly, and advanced ecommerce features lag behind competitors.

Practical tip: Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing. Use their audience dashboard to segment contacts by behavior, like purchase history, for targeted campaigns.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend, launched in 2014, focuses on ecommerce brands. It excels in omnichannel marketing, combining email, SMS, and push notifications. Integrations with Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce make it a favorite for online retailers.

Unlike Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes pre-built workflows for cart abandonment, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells. Pricing is contact-based with no send limits, offering better value for high-volume senders.

Practical advice: If your business relies on ecommerce, connect Omnisend to your store for automatic product recommendations in emails, boosting revenue without manual work.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major factor in Omnisend vs Mailchimp debates.

### Mailchimp Pricing

- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month.
- **Essentials**: $13/month (5,000 contacts), limited automation.
- **Standard**: $20/month (5,000 contacts), better reporting.
- **Premium**: $350+/month (10,000+ contacts), advanced segmentation.

Mailchimp charges per contacts and features, with overages for high engagement.

### Omnisend Pricing

- **Free**: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month.
- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts), unlimited emails/SMS.
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts), advanced segmentation/A/B testing.
- **Enterprise**: Custom.

Omnisend's model scales better for growing lists—no send caps mean predictable costs.

**Verdict**: Mailchimp wins for tiny lists; Omnisend for ecommerce with frequent sends. Calculate your needs: If sending weekly to 5,000 contacts, Omnisend Standard ($59) beats Mailchimp Standard ($350+ with sends).

Practical tip: Audit your contact list size and send frequency. Tools like Omnisend's calculator help forecast costs accurately.

## Feature Comparison

Both platforms pack features, but focus differs.

### Email Templates and Design

Mailchimp offers 100+ drag-and-drop templates, mobile-responsive out-of-the-box. Omnisend has ecommerce-specific ones with dynamic product blocks.

**Winner**: Tie—choose based on industry.

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp's automations are linear (e.g., welcome series). Omnisend shines with multi-channel journeys, like email → SMS if no open.

Practical advice: For cart recovery, Omnisend's workflows recover 15-30% more carts on average (based on their benchmarks). Test with a small segment first.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Mailchimp uses tags and groups. Omnisend leverages purchase data for hyper-personalization, like "bought X, recommend Y."

### Analytics and Reporting

Both provide open rates, clicks, revenue tracking. Omnisend edges with SMS ROI and cross-channel attribution.

**Overall Feature Winner**: Omnisend for ecommerce; Mailchimp for general marketing.

## Ease of Use: Which is More Beginner-Friendly?

Mailchimp's interface is famously simple—drag-and-drop everything. Setup takes minutes.

Omnisend is user-friendly but ecommerce-heavy, with a slight learning curve for non-retailers.

Practical tip: Watch free tutorials. Mailchimp's are abundant on YouTube; Omnisend offers store-specific guides.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for absolute beginners.

## Integrations and Apps

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).

Omnisend focuses on ecommerce: native Shopify, Klaviyo-like product syncs, plus SMS providers.

Practical advice: List your tools (e.g., Shopify + Google Ads). Omnisend auto-pulls orders; Mailchimp requires manual imports sometimes.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for paid plans; huge knowledge base.

Omnisend: 24/7 live chat, dedicated managers for Pro+.

**Winner**: Omnisend—faster responses for urgent campaigns.

Practical tip: Test support pre-signup with a question. Time their response.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick Mailchimp if**: Solopreneur, blogger, service business. Need simple emails and free start.
- **Pick Omnisend if**: Ecommerce store, high-volume sends, want SMS. Prioritize revenue recovery.

Practical advice: Run a 14-day trial of both. Migrate a test list and send one campaign. Track metrics like deliverability (both ~95%+).

## Conclusion

Omnisend vs Mailchimp boils down to your business model. Mailchimp's versatility suits diverse users, while Omnisend dominates ecommerce with smarter automations and value pricing.

For most growing online stores, Omnisend pulls ahead—its omnichannel approach drives 2-3x higher engagement. But test both to confirm.

Ready to choose? Start with your goals: general marketing (Mailchimp) or sales-focused (Omnisend). Whichever you pick, consistent testing and segmentation will skyrocket results.
