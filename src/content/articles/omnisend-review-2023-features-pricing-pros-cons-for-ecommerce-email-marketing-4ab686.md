# Omnisend Review 2023: Is It the Right Email Marketing Platform for Your Ecommerce Store?

If you're an ecommerce business owner scouting for a robust email marketing tool, you've likely stumbled upon Omnisend in your search for an "Omnisend review." This platform stands out for its focus on ecommerce, offering seamless email, SMS, and push notification capabilities designed to boost sales through automation and personalization. In this comprehensive Omnisend review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, usability, and whether it delivers value for stores on platforms like Shopify or WooCommerce.

We'll cover practical setup tips, real-world use cases, and comparisons to help you decide if Omnisend fits your needs. Whether you're migrating from Mailchimp or starting fresh, this guide provides actionable advice based on the platform's documented capabilities and user feedback.

## What is Omnisend and Who is It For?

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform tailored for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it powers over 70,000 stores worldwide, emphasizing cross-channel communication to drive revenue. Unlike generalist tools, Omnisend shines in recovering abandoned carts, welcoming new subscribers, and nurturing repeat customers.

**Ideal audience:** Small to mid-sized ecommerce stores (e.g., Shopify, BigCommerce users) with 500+ contacts looking for automation without a steep learning curve. It's less suited for non-ecommerce businesses or enterprises needing advanced B2B CRM.

Practical tip: Start with their free plan to test integrations before committing. Signup takes under 5 minutes via email—no credit card required.

Getting started is straightforward:

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs ecommerce-specific tools into an intuitive dashboard. Here's a breakdown:

### Drag-and-Drop Email Builder

The visual editor rivals top platforms like Klaviyo. Choose from 250+ responsive templates or build from scratch. Dynamic blocks pull product images, prices, and recommendations automatically.

**Practical advice:** Use product-based blocks for personalized recommendations. Test A/B subject lines (up to 10 variants) to optimize open rates—aim for 20-30% opens by segmenting by purchase history.

### Automation Workflows

Pre-built templates cover 80% of ecommerce needs:
- Welcome series
- Abandoned cart (multi-email/SMS sequences)
- Win-back campaigns
- Post-purchase upsells

Custom workflows use if/else logic for segmentation (e.g., send SMS if email opens fail). Trigger on events like page views or tag additions.

**Real-world tip:** For abandoned carts, set email 1 at 1 hour, SMS at 24 hours. Users see average recovery rates of 10-15% with personalization.

### Omnichannel Marketing: SMS and Push

Beyond email, integrate SMS (U.S./Canada focus) and web push. SMS open rates hit 98%, ideal for time-sensitive promos.

**Setup guide:**
1. Buy credits or subscribe to Pro.
2. Get a dedicated number.
3. Add opt-in checkboxes to popups.

Push notifications work on browsers—no app needed. Combine channels in one workflow for 3x engagement.

### List Building Tools

Wheel popups, signup forms, and landing pages convert 5-10% of visitors. Use gamified wheels for higher opt-ins.

**Best practice:** A/B test popup timing (e.g., exit-intent) and incentives (10% off vs. free shipping). Comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM via built-in unsubscribe links.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Segment by behavior: purchased items, location, engagement. RFM analysis tags high-value customers.

Reporting dashboard tracks revenue per email ($5-10 average ROI reported). Heatmaps show click hotspots.

**Advice:** Export segments to CSV for external tools. Set revenue goals in automations to pause underperformers.
