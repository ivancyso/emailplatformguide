# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate 2024 Comparison

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, grow your list, and drive revenue. For creators, bloggers, small business owners, and marketers, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both are popular for their user-friendly interfaces and powerful features, but they cater to slightly different needs.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, core features, automations, segmentation, integrations, ease of use, customer support, and more. We'll provide practical advice to help you decide which tool fits your workflow. Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling to thousands of subscribers, this guide has you covered.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when picking an email tool. Both MailerLite and ConvertKit offer transparent, subscriber-based plans without hidden fees.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite stands out for affordability:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, landing pages, and popups.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers (scales up).
- **Advanced**: $20/month for 500 subscribers, adding A/B testing and web push notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume users.

Practical tip: If you're bootstrapping or testing email marketing, start with MailerLite's free plan. Upgrade only when you hit limits—no credit card required.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) focuses on creators:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 subscribers (limited broadcasts, no advanced automations).
- **Creator**: Starts at $15/month for 300 subscribers.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month for 300 subscribers, unlocking unlimited emails, advanced reporting, and subscriber scoring.
- **Enterprise**: Custom for large lists.

ConvertKit charges more for premium features, but it's unlimited sends on paid plans.

**Winner for Budget**: MailerLite for most users under 5,000 subscribers. ConvertKit justifies higher costs for serious creators needing deep analytics.

| Feature | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Free Tier | 1,000 subs | 300 subs |
| Starter Price | $10/mo | $15/mo |
| Unlimited Emails | Paid plans | All paid |

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly Interfaces

Both platforms prioritize simplicity, but differences emerge.

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Editor
MailerLite's editor is intuitive with blocks for text, images, buttons, and dynamic content. No coding needed. It supports responsive designs out-of-the-box.

**Pro Tip**: Use pre-built templates for newsletters, product launches, or re-engagement campaigns. Customize with merge tags for personalization.

### ConvertKit's Visual Builder
ConvertKit's builder is clean, with a focus on sequences and incentives. It's block-based but optimized for storytelling (e.g., welcome series).

**Practical Advice**: If you're a podcaster or blogger, ConvertKit's inline editing shines for embedding audio/video. Test both free trials to feel the flow.

MailerLite edges out for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for content creators.

## Core Features: Emails, Forms, and Landing Pages

### Email Builder and Templates
- **MailerLite**: 80+ templates, RSS-to-email, and photo editor integration.
- **ConvertKit**: Commerce-focused templates, one-click upsells.

Both deliver high open rates (industry avg. 20-30%) with mobile optimization.

### Signup Forms and Popups
- MailerLite: Unlimited popups, exit-intent, scroll boxes. A/B test forms.
- ConvertKit: Inline forms, flyouts, embedded. Strong incentives like lead magnets.

**Advice**: Place popups on high-traffic pages but limit to 5% frequency to avoid annoyance. Track conversions via UTM tags.

### Landing Pages
Both offer unlimited pages:
- MailerLite: Drag-and-drop, custom domains, countdown timers.
- ConvertKit: Optimized for courses/digital products, with checkout integration.

## Automations and Segmentation: Power User Tools

### Automations
- **MailerLite**: Visual builder for workflows like welcome series, abandoned carts. Triggers: tags, opens, clicks.
- **ConvertKit**: Advanced sequences, broadcasting, tagging. Rules for "if/then" logic (e.g., if opens email A, send B).

**Practical Tip**: Automate your top 3 funnels first—welcome, re-engagement, purchase follow-up. Use tags for behavior-based sends (e.g., "opened-promo").

### Segmentation
ConvertKit excels with tags and segments (e.g., "active vs inactive"). MailerLite uses groups and custom fields effectively.

**Winner**: ConvertKit for complex nurturing; MailerLite for simple lists.

## Integrations and Analytics

Both integrate with 1,000+ apps via Zapier.
- **MailerLite**: Native WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics.
- **ConvertKit**: WooCommerce, Teachable, Gumroad. Superior revenue tracking.

Analytics:
- Open/click rates, bounce handling.
- ConvertKit adds subscriber value scoring.

**Tip**: Connect to Google Analytics for full-funnel insights. Monitor list health—aim for <0.5% complaints.

## Customer Support and Community

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Highly rated (4.9/5 on Capterra).
- **ConvertKit**: Email support (priority on Pro), Creator Network community.

Both have academies with tutorials.

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:

**Cons**:

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
- Beginners/small lists (<2,000 subs).
- Budget-conscious solopreneurs.
- Need for popups/websites.

**Migration Tip**: Export CSV from old ESP, import to MailerLite. Reconfirm consents to comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
- Bloggers/podcasters/creators.
- Scaling to paid products.
- Complex automations.

**Advice**: Use ConvertKit's audit tool during trial to score your list.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

**MailerLite wins for affordability and simplicity**—ideal if you're starting or keeping costs low. **ConvertKit is better for creators monetizing audiences** with its depth.

Test both: MailerLite free forever (up to limits), ConvertKit 14-day trial. Track metrics like deliverability (both >99%), open rates, and ROI.

Whichever you pick, focus on value-driven emails: 80% education, 20% promotion. Build habits like weekly sends and A/B testing.
