# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can be tricky for businesses scaling their email marketing efforts. Both platforms offer robust tools for automation, segmentation, and campaign management, but they cater to slightly different audiences. Mailchimp is a long-standing favorite for general marketers and small businesses, while Omnisend shines in ecommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

In this Omnisend vs Mailchimp comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and support. Whether you're a beginner or running an online store, you'll get practical advice to decide which fits your workflow. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when pitting Omnisend vs Mailchimp. Both offer free plans, but their paid tiers differ based on contacts and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for larger lists. Key notes:
- Automation and A/B testing require higher tiers.
- Overage fees apply if you exceed contact limits.
- Annual billing saves 15-20%.

Practical tip: If you're under 500 contacts and need basic emails, Mailchimp's free tier is generous. But watch costs as you grow—segmentation and behavioral triggers jump to $75+/month.

### Omnisend Pricing
Omnisend's free plan covers 250 contacts and 500 emails monthly, with full automation access. Paid plans begin at $16/month (Standard) for 500 contacts, up to $59/month for 10,000. Highlights:
- Unlimited emails in all plans (fair usage applies).
- SMS and push notifications extra but bundled efficiently.
- No overage fees; upgrade seamlessly.

Practical tip: Ecommerce stores benefit from Omnisend's pricing since automation workflows (like cart recovery) are free from day one. Calculate your ROI: Omnisend often delivers higher open rates via omnichannel, justifying the slight premium.

**Winner: Omnisend for ecommerce scaling; Mailchimp for absolute beginners.** Use their pricing calculators to simulate your list size.

## Features Head-to-Head

Features define long-term usability in Omnisend vs Mailchimp debates.

### Email Templates and Design
Both provide drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp has 100+ responsive templates, strong for newsletters. Omnisend offers ecommerce-specific ones (product blocks, dynamic content) with better mobile optimization.

Practical advice: Test templates with your branding. Import custom HTML in both, but Omnisend's product feed integration auto-populates store data.

### Automation and Workflows
Mailchimp's automations include welcome series and re-engagement, but advanced branching needs Pro ($350+). Omnisend excels with pre-built ecommerce flows: abandoned cart (35% recovery rate industry avg), win-back, and cross-sell— all available on free/standard plans.

h3 Practical Workflow Setup
- **Mailchimp**: Single-step triggers; good for simple sequences.
- **Omnisend**: Multi-channel (email + SMS), behavioral triggers like page views.

Tip: Start with Omnisend if 20%+ of revenue comes from email/SMS recoveries. Map customer journeys first.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp uses tags/groups; behavioral data limited to higher plans. Omnisend leverages purchase history, browsing behavior for hyper-personalization (e.g., "bought X, recommend Y").

Practical advice: Segment by RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) in both. Omnisend's dashboard visualizes this better for quick insights.

### Omnichannel Capabilities
Mailchimp focuses on email; SMS via third-party. Omnisend natively supports SMS, web push, popups—ideal for Shopify/WooCommerce users.

**Feature Verdict: Omnisend for advanced ecommerce; Mailchimp for email-only simplicity.**

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is intuitive for non-techies, with guided tours. Omnisend has a steeper curve due to omnichannel but ecommerce wizards simplify setup.

Practical tip: Spend 30 mins on each demo account. Mailchimp suits solopreneurs; Omnisend rewards teams with store integrations (auto-syncs orders/customers).

h3 Migration Advice
- Export CSV from old tool.
- Mailchimp: Easy import, clean up bounces.
- Omnisend: Matches orders for segmentation.

Test a small campaign post-migration to verify deliverability.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook). Omnisend focuses on ecommerce: Shopify, BigCommerce, Klaviyo alternatives, plus Zapier.

Practical advice: Prioritize native integrations. For DTC brands, Omnisend's one-click Shopify sync saves hours vs Mailchimp's manual tags.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

Mailchimp: Email/chat (paid), vast resources. Omnisend: 24/7 live chat/email, priority for paid. Both boast 99%+ deliverability.

Tip: Check blacklist monitors like MX Toolbox. Warm up domains gradually (start 50/day).

h3 Real-User Insights
From G2/Capterra (as of 2024): Mailchimp 4.4/5 (ease), Omnisend 4.6/5 (features/value). Common gripe: Mailchimp pricing opacity; Omnisend SMS costs.

## Who Should Choose What?

- **Pick Mailchimp if**: General marketing, blog newsletters, budget under $20/month initially, no ecommerce focus.
- **Pick Omnisend if**: Online store, need SMS/push, automation ROI critical, scaling past 1k contacts.

Practical decision framework:
1. List size/contact volume.
2. Revenue from automated flows?
3. Team size/tech comfort.

Run a 14-day trial of both—track metrics like open rates (aim 25%+), clicks (3%+).

## Conclusion: The Clear Choice Depends on Your Business

In the Omnisend vs Mailchimp showdown, there's no one-size-fits-all. Mailchimp remains a solid, beginner-friendly staple for straightforward email campaigns. However, Omnisend pulls ahead for ecommerce pros with superior automation, omnichannel reach, and transparent pricing—often yielding 2-3x better engagement.

Assess your goals: If driving sales via carts is key, switch to Omnisend today. For content-driven lists, stick with Mailchimp. Whichever you choose, focus on list hygiene, compliant opt-ins (GDPR/CAN-SPAM), and A/B testing for sustained growth.
