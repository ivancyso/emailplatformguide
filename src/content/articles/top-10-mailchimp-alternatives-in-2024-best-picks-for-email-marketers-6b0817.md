# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Picks for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its rising prices, limited automation in lower tiers, and recent changes have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're facing high costs after scaling or need better segmentation and workflows, this roundup covers the top 10 options. Tailored for small businesses, e-commerce stores, and creators choosing email platforms, we'll compare real features, pricing, and use cases with practical advice.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month, but paid plans jump to $13/month for Essentials (with no automation) and $20+ for Standard. Advanced features like deep segmentation require Premium at $350+/month. Common pain points include:

- **Pricing hikes**: Bills balloon with contact growth.
- **Limited free tier**: Restrictive for testing.
- **Automation gaps**: Basic in entry plans.
- **Deliverability issues**: Reported by some users post-2023 updates.

Switching can save 30-50% on costs while unlocking better tools. Before migrating, export your list via CSV and test imports.

## Top Mailchimp Alternatives

Here's a curated list of **Mailchimp alternatives**, ranked by value for different audiences. All offer free trials or plans.

### 1. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for**: Transactional emails + marketing automation.

Brevo combines SMTP with marketing tools. Key features:
- Unlimited contacts on free plan (300 emails/day).
- Advanced automation workflows.
- SMS and chat integration.
- Drag-and-drop editor.

**Pricing**: Free forever; Starter $25/month (20k emails); Business $65/month.

**Pros**: Cheaper than Mailchimp; excellent deliverability (98%+ claimed); multi-channel.
**Cons**: Interface less polished for beginners.

**Practical tip**: Use for e-commerce with transactional triggers. Migrate by mapping custom fields in CSV import.

### 2. MailerLite

**Best for**: Beginners and creators on a budget.

Simple, visual platform with strong free tier.
- Free up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month.
- Pop-ups, forms, and landing pages.
- Basic automation and A/B testing.

**Pricing**: Free; Growing Business $10/month (1k subs); Advanced $20/month.

**Pros**: Intuitive; high deliverability; website builder included.
**Cons**: No deep e-commerce integrations.

**Practical tip**: Ideal for newsletters. Set up a welcome series automation first—takes 10 minutes via templates.

### 3. ConvertKit

**Best for**: Creators and bloggers.

Focuses on subscriptions with tagging.
- Visual automations (no coding).
- Native landing pages and forms.
- Subscriber scoring.

**Pricing**: Free (1k subs); Creator $15/month; Creator Pro $29/month.

**Pros**: Creator-friendly; unlimited emails in paid plans.
**Cons**: Less suited for large e-com lists.

**Practical tip**: Tag subscribers by behavior (e.g., 'opened last 3 emails') for personalized sends. Export Mailchimp tags directly.

### 4. ActiveCampaign

**Best for**: Advanced automation and CRM.

Enterprise-level without the price tag.
- 800+ integrations (Shopify, etc.).
- Machine learning for predictions.
- Site tracking and split testing.

**Pricing**: Free trial; Lite $29/month (500 contacts); Plus $49/month.

**Pros**: Powerful CRM; deep personalization.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

**Practical tip**: Build 'if/then' branches for cart abandonment—boost recovery by 15-20% typically.

### 5. Klaviyo

**Best for**: E-commerce (Shopify users).

Data-driven with revenue focus.
- Built-in analytics and revenue tracking.
- Dynamic product recommendations.
- Flow builder for omnichannel.

**Pricing**: Free (250 contacts); scales with sends ($20+).

**Pros**: E-com optimized; high ROI reported.
**Cons**: E-com heavy; pricey for non-shops.

**Practical tip**: Sync Shopify data automatically. Test flows like 'browsed but didn't buy' for quick wins.

### 6. GetResponse

**Best for**: All-in-one marketing.

Webinars, funnels, and autoresponders.
- Conversion funnels.
- AI email generator.
- Landing page builder.

**Pricing**: Free (500 contacts); Email Marketing $19/month; Marketing Automation $59/month.

**Pros**: Versatile; good for courses.
**Cons**: Overwhelming features.

**Practical tip**: Use funnel builder for lead magnets. A/B test subject lines weekly.

### 7. AWeber

**Best for**: Reliable delivery for small lists.

Veteran platform with loyalty.
- Tag-based automation.
- E-com integrations.

**Pricing**: Free (500 subs); Lite $12.50/month.

**Pros**: 99% deliverability; simple.
**Cons**: Dated interface.

**Practical tip**: Schedule consistent newsletters. Monitor open rates >25% as benchmark.

### 8. Constant Contact

**Best for**: Non-profits and events.

Event management and social ads.
- AI recommendations.
- Contact management.

**Pricing**: Lite $12/month (500 contacts); Standard $35/month.

**Pros**: Support-heavy; easy.
**Cons**: Limited automation.

**Practical tip**: Promote events with integrated RSVPs.

### 9. Omnisend

**Best for**: Omnichannel retail.

SMS + email for shops.
- Pre-built workflows.
- Pop-ups and carts.

**Pricing**: Free (500 emails/month); Standard $16/month.

**Pros**: Mobile-first; Shopify sync.
**Cons**: Retail-focused.

**Practical tip**: Layer SMS on email for 6x open rates.

### 10. Drip

**Best for**: Behavioral e-commerce.

Workflows based on actions.
- Customer lifetime value tracking.
- A/B testing.

**Pricing**: Starts $39/month (500 subs).

**Pros**: Advanced segmentation.
**Cons**: No free plan.

**Practical tip**: Score leads by pages visited.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess needs**: Newsletters? Pick MailerLite. E-com? Klaviyo.
2. **Budget check**: Free tiers for testing (Brevo, MailerLite).
3. **Trial run**: Import 100 contacts; send test campaign.
4. **Key metrics**: Aim for 20-30% opens, 2-3% clicks.
5. **Integrations**: Ensure CRM/CMS compatibility.

## Practical Migration Tips

- **Backup data**: Export audiences, campaigns, automations from Mailchimp.
- **Clean list**: Remove inactives (opens <3 in 6 months).
- **Re-engage**: Send 'we're moving' email with incentive.
- **Test sends**: Check formatting on mobile/desktop.
- **Compliance**: Update privacy policy for new provider.

Expect 1-2 weeks for full switch. Monitor bounces first month.

## Conclusion

The best **Mailchimp alternative** depends on your scale and goals—Brevo for versatility, Klaviyo for shops, MailerLite for simplicity. All outperform Mailchimp in pricing or features for most users.
