# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Best Picks for Email Marketers

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its pricing structure—especially after recent changes—has many users searching for Mailchimp alternatives. With costs rising sharply beyond 500 subscribers and limitations on advanced features in lower tiers, businesses are turning to platforms offering more value, better automation, and scalable pricing.

In this roundup, we'll explore 10 reliable Mailchimp alternatives tailored for solopreneurs, small businesses, and growing teams. Each includes key features, pricing highlights, pros/cons, and ideal use cases based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra. Whether you need ecommerce focus, advanced automations, or budget-friendly options, there's a fit here.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Before diving in, consider why alternatives make sense:

- **Pricing jumps**: Mailchimp's Essentials plan starts at $13/month for 500 contacts but scales to $350+ for 50k contacts. Many alternatives offer unlimited sends or flatter pricing.
- **Feature gaps**: Advanced segmentation and automations require higher tiers in Mailchimp.
- **Deliverability and support**: Some rivals excel in inbox placement and 24/7 support.

Practical tip: Audit your current Mailchimp usage. Export subscriber data (CSV) and test imports in 2-3 alternatives via free trials to check migration ease.

## 1. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
Brevo combines transactional emails, marketing automation, SMS, and CRM in one dashboard. It offers unlimited contacts on paid plans with send limits based on volume.

### Pricing
Free: 300 emails/day. Starter: $25/month for 20k emails. No contact-based overages.

### Pros
- Excellent deliverability (98%+).
- Multi-channel (email + SMS + WhatsApp).

### Cons
- Automation builder less visual than Mailchimp's.

Ideal for: Ecommerce stores needing transactional emails. Migrate by mapping custom fields in Brevo's import tool.

## 2. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
Deep automation with machine learning for predictive sending, site tracking, and 900+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress).

### Pricing
Lite: $29/month for 1k contacts. Scales to $149 for 10k.

### Pros
- Powerful conditional automations (if/then logic).
- Built-in CRM.

### Cons
- Steeper learning curve.

Ideal for: Agencies or teams wanting sales funnels. Tip: Use their automation templates to replicate Mailchimp journeys quickly.

## 3. MailerLite

### Key Features
Drag-and-drop editor, pop-ups, landing pages, and automation workflows. Strong on segmentation.

### Pricing
Free: 1k subs, 12k emails/month. Growing Business: $10/month for 1k subs (unlimited emails).

### Pros
- Affordable forever.
- High deliverability without warm-up needed.

### Cons
- Fewer ecommerce-specific tools.

Ideal for: Bloggers/content creators. Practical advice: Leverage their A/B testing for subject lines to boost open rates 20-30% post-migration.

## 4. ConvertKit (now Kit)

### Key Features
Creator-focused with subscriber tags, visual automations, and commerce tools like one-click upsells.

### Pricing
Free: 300 subs. Creator: $29/month for 1k subs.

### Pros
- Simple tagging over lists.
- Native selling features.

### Cons
- Limited reporting depth.

Ideal for: Newsletter writers. Tip: Tag-based segmentation migrates seamlessly from Mailchimp lists.

## 5. Klaviyo

### Key Features
Ecommerce powerhouse with revenue tracking, dynamic product feeds, and AI-optimized flows (abandoned cart recovery averages 15% lift).

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails. Scales with revenue (0.25% of tracked revenue + $20/month base).

### Pros
- Shopify integration perfection.
- Predictive analytics.

### Cons
- Best for revenue-generating lists.

Ideal for: Online stores. Advice: Connect Klaviyo to your store first, then import Mailchimp data to auto-populate profiles.

## 6. GetResponse

### Key Features
All-in-one: Email, webinars, funnels, and landing pages. AI email generator.

### Pricing
Free: Basic features. Email Marketing: $19/month for 1k contacts.

### Pros
- Webinar integration.
- Conversion funnels.

### Cons
- Interface feels dated.

Ideal for: Course creators. Tip: Use their migration service for zero-downtime switch.

## 7. AWeber

### Key Features
Reliable automation, tagging, and 700+ apps. Strong spam testing.

### Pricing
Lite: $19/month unlimited subs (with send caps).

### Pros
- 99% deliverability guarantee.
- Legacy support.

### Cons
- Editor less modern.

Ideal for: Non-profits/small biz. Advice: Test their Smart Send for optimal timing.

## 8. Constant Contact

### Key Features
Event management, social ads, and AI content optimizer.

### Pricing
Lite: $12/month for 500 contacts.

### Pros
- Beginner-friendly.
- Local business tools.

### Cons
- Expensive at scale.

Ideal for: Offline businesses. Tip: Integrate with Google Workspace for seamless contact sync.

## 9. Omnisend

### Key Features
Omnichannel for ecommerce: Email, SMS, push. Pre-built flows.

### Pricing
Free: 500 emails/month. Standard: $16/month for 500 subs.

### Pros
- SMS bundles cheaply.

### Cons
- Ecommerce-only focus.

Ideal for: DTC brands.

## 10. Zoho Campaigns

### Key Features
Integrates with Zoho suite, AI personalization, RSS campaigns.

### Pricing
Free: 2k subs/6k emails. Standard: $4/month per 500 subs? Wait, $3/user/month bundled.

### Pros
- Cheap for Zoho users.

### Cons
- Best in ecosystem.

Ideal for: SMBs on Zoho CRM.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Match your needs**: Ecommerce? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Automation? ActiveCampaign.
2. **Budget check**: Calculate TCO—Brevo/MailerLite win for volume.
3. **Trial stack**: Sign up for 3 free trials, import 100 test subs, send a campaign.
4. **Migration checklist**:
   - Export Mailchimp lists/groups.
   - Map fields (name, email, custom).
   - Rebuild automations (start with welcomes).
   - Update website forms/integrations.
   - Warm up new IP if high volume.

Expect 1-2 weeks for full switch. Tools like Zapier bridge gaps.

## Practical Tips for Success

- **Boost deliverability**: Use double opt-in, clean lists quarterly (remove inactives).
- **A/B test religiously**: Subjects, send times—aim for 25%+ opens.
- **Compliance**: Honor GDPR/CAN-SPAM with easy unsubscribe.
- **Analytics deep-dive**: Track revenue per email, not just opens.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like Brevo, ActiveCampaign, and MailerLite offer superior pricing and features for most users.
