# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool is crucial for businesses looking to nurture leads, drive sales, and build customer loyalty. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp are two popular platforms, but they cater to slightly different needs. This Brevo vs Mailchimp comparison breaks down pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide based on your business size, budget, and goals.

Whether you're a solopreneur, small business owner, or scaling marketer, we'll provide practical advice on migration, setup tips, and when to switch platforms.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing platform. Both Brevo and Mailchimp offer free plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo uses a contact-based pricing model with daily email limits on lower tiers:
- **Free Plan**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails per day (about 9,000/month). Ideal for testing or very small lists.
- **Starter**: Starts at $25/month for 20,000 emails/month, up to 5,000 contacts.
- **Business**: From $65/month, removes send limits, adds features like A/B testing and automation.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume senders.

Brevo shines for transactional emails and SMS, with pay-as-you-go options. No contact limits on paid plans mean scalability without tier jumps.

**Practical Tip**: If your list grows beyond 500 contacts but sends are low-volume, Brevo's free plan stretches further than Mailchimp's.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp bills based on contacts and monthly sends:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month. Basic features only.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts (scales up).
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, adds automation and A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced segmentation and support.

Mailchimp gets pricey fast for larger lists—e.g., 10,000 contacts on Standard is around $100/month.

**Practical Tip**: Calculate your monthly sends x contact size. Mailchimp suits consistent high-volume senders; Brevo for bursty campaigns.

**Winner**: Brevo for affordability at scale; Mailchimp for simple, low-contact needs.

## Ease of Use and User Interface

Both platforms prioritize intuitive designs, but user feedback highlights differences.

Brevo's dashboard is modern and streamlined, with a drag-and-drop editor similar to Canva. Navigation is logical for automation workflows and multi-channel campaigns (email + SMS + chat).

Mailchimp's interface is legendary for beginners—polished templates and a forgiving editor make it quick to launch campaigns. However, advanced users report clutter in automations.

**Practical Advice**: Start with Brevo if you're handling sales funnels; Mailchimp if you're new to email and want zero learning curve. Test both free plans: import a CSV list and send a test campaign to compare setup time (typically 15-30 mins each).

## Key Features Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp offers 100+ professional templates with easy customization. Brevo provides 40+ responsive templates, focusing on conversion-optimized designs.

**Tip**: Use Mailchimp for visually stunning newsletters; Brevo for e-commerce product emails.

### Automation and Workflows

Brevo excels with multi-step automations, including webhooks and transactional triggers. Mailchimp's automations are robust but limited on lower plans (e.g., single-step on Essentials).

### Segmentation and Analytics

Both segment by behavior, opens, and custom fields. Brevo includes real-time analytics and heatmaps; Mailchimp has comparative reporting.

**Advanced Tip**: For drip campaigns, Brevo's wait nodes and branching save time. Track ROI by integrating UTM parameters in both.

### Additional Channels

Brevo bundles SMS, WhatsApp, and live chat—perfect for omnichannel. Mailchimp focuses on email with basic landing pages and ads.

## Integrations and Scalability

Brevo integrates with 300+ apps (Shopify, WordPress, Zapier) and has a strong API for developers.

Mailchimp connects to 1,000+ via native/Zapier, popular with e-commerce (e.g., WooCommerce).

**Practical Advice**: Check your CRM/CMS compatibility. For custom builds, Brevo's SMTP relay edges out. Migrate lists using built-in importers—export CSV from one, clean data (remove bounces), import to the other.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

Brevo offers email/ticket support (priority on paid), with 99% deliverability rates.

Mailchimp provides 24/7 chat/email on paid plans, boasting 99.5% inbox placement.

**Tip**: Warm up new domains gradually (start 50-100 sends/day) on either to boost deliverability. Use dedicated IPs on enterprise tiers.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros:
- Cheaper for large lists
- All-in-one (email/SMS/chat)
- Unlimited contacts on free

**Cons**:
- Daily send caps on free
- Fewer templates

### Mailchimp Pros:
- Beginner-friendly
- Vast template library
- Strong community resources

**Cons**:
- Expensive scaling
- Feature-locked plans

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Brevo** if: You're budget-conscious, need SMS/automation, or run transactional emails (e.g., SaaS, e-com).
- **Choose Mailchimp** if: Visual design is key, small list (<5k), or you value polish over cost.

**Migration Tip**: Export subscribers, recreate automations (map triggers), A/B test sends post-switch. Expect 1-2 days downtime.

## Conclusion

In the Brevo vs Mailchimp showdown, Brevo pulls ahead for growing businesses seeking value and versatility, while Mailchimp remains the go-to for ease and aesthetics. Evaluate your monthly budget, list size, and features needed—sign up for free trials today.

Ultimately, the best platform aligns with your workflow. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and CTR (2-5%) regardless. For most users in 2024, Brevo's pricing and multi-channel edge make it the smarter long-term pick.
