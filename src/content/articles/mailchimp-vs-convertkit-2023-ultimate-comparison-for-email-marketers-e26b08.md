# Mailchimp vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business outreach. With thousands of subscribers relying on consistent, engaging emails, platforms like Mailchimp and ConvertKit stand out. Mailchimp, a veteran since 2002, powers millions with its user-friendly interface. ConvertKit, launched in 2013, targets creators with advanced automation for newsletters and courses.

This in-depth Mailchimp vs ConvertKit comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, and more. Aimed at solopreneurs, small businesses, and bloggers deciding between them, we'll provide practical advice to help you choose without buyer's remorse. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: Mailchimp vs ConvertKit

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free tiers, but scales differ based on subscriber count.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Includes basic templates and automation.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/month for 500 contacts (up to $350 for 200k+).
- **Standard**: $20/month base, adds A/B testing and behavioral targeting.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced analytics and unlimited sends.

Mailchimp bills monthly or annually (20% discount). Overages for sends can add costs.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers (10,000 emails/month). Full access to core features like forms and sequences.
- **Creator**: $15/month for 300 subs, scales to $29 for 1,000.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month base, adds advanced reporting, subscriber scoring.

ConvertKit is subscriber-based, no send limits. Annual billing saves 20%. No overage fees—predictable costs.

**Practical Advice**: Start with free plans. If under 1,000 subs, ConvertKit's free tier edges out. For e-commerce scaling past 5,000, Mailchimp's tiers might fit better. Calculate your projected growth: use tools like their pricing calculators.

| Automation | Basic     | Full sequences  |

## Ease of Use and Interface

Mailchimp shines for beginners with its drag-and-drop editor. No coding needed—intuitive for non-tech users. Dashboard feels familiar, like Canva meets email.

ConvertKit prioritizes simplicity for creators. Visual automations (like flowcharts) make tagging and sequences easy. Less bloated than Mailchimp for newsletter-focused users.

**Winner for Beginners**: Mailchimp. **For Power Users**: ConvertKit's clean tagging system reduces list fatigue.

**Practical Tip**: Import a test list (CSV from Google Sheets). Build one campaign in each. Time yourself—under 30 minutes? It's a keeper.

## Core Features Comparison

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp: 100+ responsive templates, customizable blocks. Strong for promotional emails.
ConvertKit: Simpler templates optimized for newsletters. Commerce add-on for selling digital products.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journeys for multi-step automations, triggers like opens/clicks. E-commerce integrations shine.
- **ConvertKit**: Sequences and visuals for drip campaigns. Tag-based segmentation (e.g., 'lead' vs 'buyer') is superior for personalization.

**Advice**: If selling courses, ConvertKit's one-click upsells win. For abandoned carts, Mailchimp integrates better with Shopify.

### Segmentation and Analytics
Mailchimp: Groups, tags, merge fields. Detailed reports on revenue per email.
ConvertKit: Advanced tagging, subscriber scores. Open rates, clicks, and revenue tracking.

Both comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM. ConvertKit's 'predictive analytics' forecasts churn.

### Forms, Landing Pages, and Integrations
- Forms: Both embeddable popups/opt-ins.
- Landing Pages: Mailchimp basic; ConvertKit excels with checkout pages.
- Integrations: Mailchimp (6,000+ via Zapier); ConvertKit (native with WordPress, Teachable).

**Tip**: Check Zapier compatibility. Test webhook setups for your CRM.

## Customer Support and Community

Mailchimp: 24/7 email/chat for paid; huge knowledge base, forums.
ConvertKit: Priority email support, live chat, creator-focused resources (podcasts, webinars).

**Practical Advice**: Join communities—Mailchimp's Facebook group vs ConvertKit's creator Slack. Support response under 2 hours? Prioritize that.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros
- Free plan generosity.
- Robust e-commerce tools.
- Beginner-friendly.

Cons:
- Send limits on free.
- Cluttered for large lists.
- Higher costs at scale.

### ConvertKit Pros
- Unlimited sends.
- Creator-centric automations.
- Predictable pricing.

Cons:
- No phone support.
- Less visual templates.
- E-com weaker without add-ons.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
Small businesses, e-commerce stores, agencies needing all-in-one marketing. Ideal if you value templates and broad integrations. Start here if you're new to email.

**Migration Tip**: Export lists easily; use their importer wizard.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Bloggers, podcasters, course creators focused on newsletters. Perfect for tag-based nurturing and monetization.

**Migration Tip**: Map tags during import. Test sequences with 10% of list first.

## Testing and Switching: Practical Steps
1. **Sign Up Free**: Both offer no-card trials.
2. **Import Subscribers**: Use CSV; clean data first (remove bounces).
3. **Build a Campaign**: A/B test subject lines.
4. **Set Up Automation**: One welcome series.
5. **Analyze**: Track opens after 48 hours.
6. **Scale Test**: Send to 100 subs.

Budget 1-2 weeks. Tools like EmailOctopus for hybrids if neither fits.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp vs ConvertKit? No one-size-fits-all. Mailchimp suits marketing teams with its polish and scale. ConvertKit empowers creators with smart automations and simplicity.

For most starting out: Try ConvertKit's generous free plan. Growing e-com? Mailchimp.

Ultimately, test both. Your audience behavior dictates the winner. Focus on deliverability (both excellent) and engagement. Whichever boosts opens by 20%+ is yours.
