# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can be tough when you're building your email list. Both are popular for solopreneurs, small businesses, and content creators, but they cater to slightly different needs. This MailerLite vs ConvertKit comparison breaks down pricing, features, ease of use, and real-world performance to help you decide.

We'll look at key aspects like automation, segmentation, deliverability, and support. Whether you're a blogger scaling sequences or a shop owner needing popups, read on for practical advice.

## Pricing: Free Plans vs Creator-Focused Tiers

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Here's how they stack up.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite shines with affordability:
- **Free plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation and drag-and-drop editor.
- **Growing Business**: $10/month for up to 500 subs (billed annually), scales to $20 for 2,500 subs.
- **Advanced**: $20/month starting, adds A/B testing and web push notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom for large lists.

No contracts, monthly billing option. Practical tip: Start free to test imports and campaigns without risk.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now Kit) targets creators:
- **Free plan**: Up to 300 subs (recently reduced), limited to basic broadcasts.
- **Creator**: $15/month for up to 300 subs (annual), $29 for 1,000.
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month starting, unlocks advanced automations, segments, and commerce.

14-day trial. It's pricier for larger lists—$79/month for 10k subs vs MailerLite's $50.

**Verdict**: MailerLite wins for budget-conscious users or testing waters. ConvertKit justifies cost for revenue-focused creators with built-in selling tools.

| Feature | MailerLite | ConvertKit |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Free Tier | 1,000 subs | 300 subs |
| 1,000 Subs | ~$15/mo | $29/mo |
| 10k Subs | ~$50/mo | $79/mo |

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop vs Visual Automations

Both have intuitive dashboards, but interfaces differ.

### MailerLite's User-Friendly Editor

MailerLite's drag-and-drop builder is beginner gold:
- Responsive templates, no coding needed.
- One-click popups, forms, and landing pages.
- Mobile app for on-the-go management.

Practical advice: Use their template library for quick newsletters. Import contacts via CSV seamlessly—tested with Gmail exports.

### ConvertKit's Creator Workflow

ConvertKit emphasizes sequences:
- Visual automation builder (like flowchart).
- Tag-based subscriber management (no lists/segments confusion).
- Native landing pages and checkout forms.

It's steeper for newbies but powerful once learned. Tip: Set up a welcome sequence first—tag new subs for personalized drips.

**Winner**: MailerLite for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for those comfortable with tagging systems.

## Core Features: Automations, Segmentation, and Integrations

### Email Editor and Templates

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, photo editor integration. Rich text or HTML options.

ConvertKit: Clean editor focused on text-heavy newsletters. Fewer templates but optimized for creators.

### Automations

- **MailerLite**: Workflow builder for triggers like signups, purchases. Time-based delays, A/B splits in paid plans.
- **ConvertKit**: Superior sequences—automated email courses. Re-engagement campaigns via tags.

Practical tip: For course launches, ConvertKit's subscriber scoring predicts engagement better.

### Segmentation and Analytics

MailerLite uses groups and segments (custom fields). Open/click tracking, heatmaps.

ConvertKit: Tag everything (e.g., "purchased", "opened-3x"). Advanced reports on revenue per subscriber.

### Integrations

Both connect to 1,000+ via Zapier. MailerLite has WooCommerce, WordPress plugins. ConvertKit excels with Gumroad, Teachable for creators.

**Pro Tip**: If you're on Shopify, MailerLite's native app abandons cart emails effortlessly.

## Deliverability and Support

Deliverability: Both score high (MailerLite ~99%, ConvertKit ~98% per InboxAlly). Use double opt-ins to maintain.

Support:
- MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Fast responses (under 1 hour).
- ConvertKit: Email/ticket only (no live chat), priority for Pro. Creator-focused academy videos.

Advice: MailerLite for urgent help; ConvertKit's docs suffice for power users.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Small businesses/e-commerce under 5k subs.
- Need popups/websites on budget.
- Free plan testers.

**Real Example**: A cafe owner uses popups to grow from 200 to 2k subs, sending weekly promos at $10/mo.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers/podcasters with courses/products.
- Tag-heavy automations.
- Monetization via one-click upsells.

**Real Example**: Newsletter writer automates 7-day sequence, tags buyers for upsells, hitting $5k MRR.

## Migration and Getting Started

Switching? Both offer CSV imports. MailerLite's migration tool handles other platforms. Test small batches first.

Steps:
1. Export from current tool.
2. Clean data (remove bounces).
3. Map fields, import.
4. Replicate top campaigns.

## Conclusion: MailerLite vs ConvertKit—Your Pick

**MailerLite** is the affordable all-rounder for beginners and SMBs needing quick wins with popups and basics. **ConvertKit** dominates for creators prioritizing sequences, tagging, and sales funnels.

If budget < $20/mo and list <5k: Go MailerLite. Scaling to products/courses: ConvertKit.
