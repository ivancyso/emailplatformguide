# Best Email Marketing Platforms for Nonprofits: Free and Discounted Options in 2024

Nonprofits rely on effective communication to engage donors, volunteers, and supporters. Email marketing is a cost-effective way to nurture relationships, share impact stories, and drive fundraising. With tight budgets, nonprofits need platforms offering free tiers, discounts, or affordable pricing alongside features like list segmentation, automation, and analytics.

This roundup highlights the best email marketing tools tailored for nonprofits. We focus on platforms with verified nonprofit-friendly pricing, ease of use for non-tech teams, and integrations with donation tools like Donorbox or Classy. All recommendations are based on current pricing and features as of 2024—no hype, just practical insights.

## Why Email Marketing is Essential for Nonprofits

Email delivers high ROI: for every $1 spent, nonprofits average $42 in return (DMA data). It outperforms social media for direct engagement. Key benefits include:

- **Personalized donor stewardship**: Segment lists by giving history to send tailored thank-yous.
- **Event promotion and RSVPs**: Automate reminders to boost attendance.
- **Fundraising campaigns**: Time-sensitive appeals with urgency.
- **Compliance tools**: Built-in unsubscribe and CAN-SPAM/GDPR features.

Choose platforms with mobile-responsive templates, drag-and-drop editors, and A/B testing to maximize open rates (aim for 20-30% industry average for nonprofits).

## Key Features Nonprofits Should Prioritize

When selecting a tool, evaluate these must-haves:

### Free or Discounted Plans
Look for generous free tiers (e.g., 1,000+ contacts) or nonprofit-specific discounts (10-50% off).

### Integrations
Seamless connections to CRMs like Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud, donation platforms (PayPal, Stripe), or Google Workspace.

### Automation and Segmentation
Trigger emails based on behavior, like post-donation follow-ups or lapsed donor re-engagement.

### Analytics and Reporting
Track opens, clicks, conversions, and ROI to refine campaigns.

### Deliverability and Compliance
High sender scores and suppression lists to avoid spam folders.

## Top Email Marketing Platforms for Nonprofits

Here are seven standout options, ranked by affordability and nonprofit suitability.

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends—perfect for small nonprofits starting out. Paid plans scale affordably from $13/month.

**Pros for Nonprofits**:
- Intuitive drag-and-drop builder with nonprofit templates (e.g., newsletters, appeals).
- Automation for welcome series and birthday thank-yous.
- Integrates with 250+ apps, including DonorPerfect and Eventbrite.
- Detailed reports on donor engagement.

**Cons**: Free plan limits advanced automation; nonprofit discount not standard but available via partnerships.

**Practical Tip**: Use audience segments to tag 'major donors' vs. 'volunteers' for personalized content. Test subject lines like 'Your Impact Last Month' for 25%+ open rates.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Free forever plan: unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day. Paid from $25/month.

**Pros for Nonprofits**:
- SMS integration for urgent appeals.
- Advanced automation workflows (e.g., abandoned cart for event tickets).
- CRM built-in for tracking donor journeys.
- EU-based with strong GDPR compliance.

**Cons**: Daily send limit on free plan may constrain large blasts.

**Practical Tip**: Set up transactional emails for instant donation receipts to build trust and encourage repeats.

### 3. MailerLite

Free up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month—most generous free tier.

**Pros for Nonprofits**:
- Pop-up forms for sign-ups on your website.
- Landing pages for campaign microsites.
- A/B testing and click maps.

**Cons**: Fewer integrations than Mailchimp.

**Practical Tip**: Embed signup forms on impact report pages to grow lists organically.

### 4. Constant Contact

Nonprofit pricing: 20-30% off standard rates (from $12/month for 500 contacts).

**Pros for Nonprofits**:
- Event management tools with RSVPs.
- Social media integrations for cross-promotion.
- AI-powered subject line suggestions.

**Cons**: No free plan beyond 60-day trial.

**Practical Tip**: Use their nonprofit templates for year-end appeals; track ROI by linking to donation pages.

### 5. Benchmark Email

Free up to 250 contacts, 3,500 sends/month.

**Pros for Nonprofits**:
- Image editor and RSS-to-email for blogs.
- Basic automation included free.

**Cons**: Dated interface.

**Practical Tip**: Automate monthly newsletters from your blog RSS to keep supporters informed effortlessly.

### 6. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free with CRM: unlimited sends to 2,000 contacts (marketing contacts limited).

**Pros for Nonprofits**:
- Full CRM for donor management.
- Smart content personalization.
- Free forever for basics.

**Cons**: Advanced features require paid CRM upgrade.

**Practical Tip**: Use contact properties to score donors and nurture high-value ones.

### 7. ActiveCampaign

From $29/month; nonprofit discounts via TechSoup.

**Pros for Nonprofits**:
- Powerful machine learning for predictions.
- Site tracking and lead scoring.

**Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

**Practical Tip**: Build 'if/then' automations for donor lifecycle stages.

## Practical Advice for Launching Your Nonprofit Email Program

1. **Build Your List Legally**: Use double opt-in forms on donation pages, events, and newsletters. Offer lead magnets like 'Annual Impact Report'.

2. **Segment Ruthlessly**: Group by engagement (opens/clicks), recency (last donation), and demographics. Tools like Mailchimp make this drag-and-drop easy.

3. **Craft Compelling Content**: Keep emails 50% storytelling (your mission's impact) and 50% calls-to-action. Subject lines under 50 chars: 'Join Our Mission Today'.

4. **Schedule Wisely**: Tuesdays-Thursdays, 8-10 AM recipient time. Test with platform tools.

5. **Measure Success**: Track open rates (25%+ goal), click-through (2-5%), and conversions (donations). Use UTM tags for Google Analytics.

6. **Stay Compliant**: Always include physical address, clear unsubscribe. Monitor bounce rates under 2%.

7. **Scale with Automation**: Start simple—welcome series, then nurture sequences.

Integrate with tools like Zapier for no-code connections to accounting software.

## Conclusion

For nonprofits, Mailchimp or MailerLite offer the best free entry points, while Constant Contact shines for discounts and events. Larger orgs may prefer Brevo or ActiveCampaign for depth. Start with a free trial, import your list, and send a test campaign. Focus on value over volume to foster long-term donor loyalty. The right platform amplifies your mission without breaking the bank—choose based on your contact volume and tech comfort. Ready to boost engagement? Pick one and iterate based on data.
