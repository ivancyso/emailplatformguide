# Best Email Marketing Software for 2023: Top 10 Picks for Businesses

Choosing the **best email marketing software** can transform your business's communication strategy. With email remaining one of the highest-ROI channels—delivering an average return of $36 for every $1 spent—selecting the right platform is crucial for small businesses, e-commerce stores, and enterprises alike. This roundup evaluates top tools based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, key features like automation and segmentation, pricing transparency, and scalability.

We'll cover what makes software stand out, detailed reviews of the top 10, a comparison table, practical selection tips, and a conclusion to help you decide.

## Key Criteria for Evaluating Email Marketing Software

When hunting for the **best email marketing software**, focus on these essentials:

### Essential Features
- **Drag-and-Drop Builders**: Intuitive editors for non-designers.
- **Automation Workflows**: Trigger emails based on user behavior, like abandoned carts.
- **Segmentation & Personalization**: Target lists by demographics, purchases, or engagement.
- **Analytics & Reporting**: Track open rates (industry avg. 21%), clicks (2.3%), and conversions.
- **Integrations**: Connect with Shopify, WordPress, Zapier, or CRMs like Salesforce.
- **Deliverability**: High inbox placement rates (aim for 95%+).
- **Compliance Tools**: Built-in GDPR, CAN-SPAM support, and double opt-in.

### Pricing Considerations
Look beyond free tiers—factor in contacts, sends, and add-ons. Most start free for 0-2,000 subscribers but scale with usage.

### User Experience & Support
Prioritize mobile apps, 24/7 chat, and onboarding resources for quick wins.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Platforms

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp leads with its user-friendly interface and all-in-one marketing hub. Ideal for beginners.

**Key Features**:
- Free plan up to 500 contacts/1,000 sends/month.
- Advanced automations, A/B testing, and behavioral targeting.
- 300+ integrations, including Google Analytics.

**Pricing**: Free; Essentials $13/mo (5,000 contacts); Premium $350/mo (150k contacts).

**Pros**: Excellent templates (200+), high deliverability (99%).
**Cons**: Automation limits on lower plans; can get pricey.

Practical Advice: Use its audience dashboard to segment by purchase history for 20-30% uplift in opens.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

Best for automation-heavy users, blending email with CRM.

**Key Features**:
- Deep automation (if/then logic, site tracking).
- Lead scoring and machine learning predictions.
- Split testing across subjects, content.

**Pricing**: Lite $29/mo (500 contacts); Plus $49/mo; Professional $149/mo.

**Pros**: Powerful CRM integration; detailed reporting.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

Practical Advice: Set up 'win probability' scoring to nurture leads automatically, boosting conversions by 15-25%.

### 3. Klaviyo

E-commerce powerhouse, especially for Shopify users.

**Key Features**:
- Revenue-focused flows (e.g., post-purchase sequences).
- Advanced segmentation by predicted CLV.
- SMS integration.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; scales ~$20/1,000 contacts.

**Pros**: 1-click Shopify sync; A/B testing on flows.
**Cons**: Less ideal for non-ecom.

Practical Advice: Clone high-performing flows like welcome series, which average 4x ROI.

### 4. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Affordable with transactional emails included.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts on paid plans.
- Multichannel (email, SMS, chat).
- Sales CRM and landing pages.

**Pricing**: Free (300/day); Starter $25/mo.

**Pros**: Cost-effective scaling.
**Cons**: Template library smaller.

Practical Advice: Use transactional APIs for order confirmations to maintain 40%+ open rates.

### 5. ConvertKit

Creator-focused with simple tagging.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automations, subscriber tags.
- One-click upsells, digital product delivery.

**Pricing**: Free (300 subs); Creator $29/mo.

**Pros**: Clean UI; great for newsletters.
**Cons**: Limited reporting.

Practical Advice: Tag subscribers by content downloads for personalized broadcasts.

### 6. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels.

**Key Features**:
- Conversion funnels, webinars (up to 1,000 attendees).
- Autoresponders, ecom tools.

**Pricing**: $19/mo (1,000 contacts).

**Pros**: Robust landing pages.
**Cons**: Interface feels dated.

### 7. MailerLite

Budget-friendly with strong visuals.

**Key Features**:
- Popups, forms, automation.
- Free plan robust (1,000 subs).

**Pricing**: Free; Growing Business $10/mo.

**Pros**: Fast setup.
**Cons**: Fewer advanced automations.

### 8. Constant Contact

Small business staple with event tools.

**Key Features**:
- Event management, social ads.

**Pricing**: $12/mo (500 contacts).

**Pros**: AI subject lines.
**Cons**: No free plan.

### 9. Drip

Ecom automation specialist.

**Key Features**:
- Workflow builder, revenue tracking.

**Pricing**: $39/mo (500 people).

**Pros**: Deep Shopify integration.
**Cons**: Pricey for startups.

### 10. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free CRM-integrated option.

**Key Features**:
- Smart content, full CRM.

**Pricing**: Free; Marketing Hub $20/mo.

**Pros**: Scales to enterprise.
**Cons**: Overkill for email-only.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing Software for Your Needs

1. **Assess Your Audience Size**: Start with free plans if under 1,000 subs.
2. **Match Features to Goals**: Ecom? Klaviyo/Drip. Leads? ActiveCampaign.
3. **Test Integrations**: Ensure compatibility with your stack (e.g., WooCommerce).
4. **Trial Periods**: All offer 14-30 days—import lists and run test campaigns.
5. **Monitor Metrics**: Aim for 20%+ opens, 3%+ clicks; switch if below.
6. **Scalability**: Check contact limits and support tiers.

Practical Tip: Start with a simple nurture sequence: Welcome > Educational content > Offer. Track with UTM tags for attribution.

## Conclusion

The **best email marketing software** depends on your business stage and goals—Mailchimp for ease, ActiveCampaign for power, Klaviyo for sales. Prioritize deliverability and automation to maximize ROI. Sign up for trials, migrate gradually (export CSV from old tools), and focus on list hygiene (remove bounces quarterly). With the right platform, you'll engage subscribers effectively and drive growth. Ready to pick? Mailchimp or Brevo are safe starters for most.
