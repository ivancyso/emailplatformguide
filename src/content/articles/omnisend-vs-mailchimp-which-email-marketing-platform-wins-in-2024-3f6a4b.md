# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform is Right for You?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's customer engagement and revenue. In 2024, Omnisend and Mailchimp stand out as two popular platforms, each catering to different needs. Omnisend excels in ecommerce with omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications, while Mailchimp offers broad appeal for beginners with its intuitive interface and free tier.

This head-to-head comparison dives into pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and real-world performance to help you decide. Whether you're running an online store or a service-based business, we'll provide practical advice based on their core strengths.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms use tiered plans based on subscriber count and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends—ideal for startups testing the waters. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for enterprise. Key limits:
- Essentials: Basic automations, no advanced reporting.
- Standard ($20+/month): Behavioral targeting, custom landing pages.
- Premium ($350+/month): A/B testing, multivariate testing.

Overages for extra sends can add up quickly. For ecommerce, expect $100-300/month for 5,000-10,000 subscribers.

### Omnisend Pricing
Omnisend offers a generous free plan for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, plus unlimited SMS trials. Paid plans begin at $16/month (Standard) for 500 contacts, up to $59/month (Pro) for advanced segmentation. No send limits on emails—pay per contact:
- Standard: Omnichannel campaigns, basic automations.
- Pro: Advanced workflows, product recommendations.

For 10,000 contacts, Pro costs around $116/month. SMS and push are usage-based (e.g., $0.015/SMS), making it cost-effective for high-volume ecommerce.

**Practical Advice:** If you're under 500 contacts, start with Mailchimp's free plan. For growing ecommerce lists (1,000+), Omnisend's pricing shines due to unlimited emails and omnichannel without steep overages. Calculate your needs using their pricing calculators.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities

### Email Builders and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop builder is beginner-friendly with 100+ responsive templates. Customization is straightforward, but advanced users may find it rigid for complex designs.

Omnisend's builder is ecommerce-optimized with dynamic product blocks that pull live inventory from Shopify or WooCommerce. It offers 250+ templates focused on cart recovery and upsells. Both support AMP emails for interactive elements.

**Winner:** Omnisend for stores needing product feeds; Mailchimp for general marketing.

### Automations and Workflows
Mailchimp's automations include welcome series, abandoned cart (Premium only), and RSS campaigns. Single-step triggers are free; customer journeys require Standard+.

Omnisend dominates with pre-built ecommerce workflows: cart abandonment (sends in 35 mins on average), win-back, cross-sell. Supports multi-channel (email + SMS + push) and splits tests natively.

**Practical Advice:** Map your customer journey first. Use Omnisend for post-purchase sequences (e.g., browse abandonment recovers 12-15% carts per industry benchmarks). Mailchimp suits nurture sequences for leads.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp uses tags, groups, and behavioral data for segments. Predictive segmentation (Premium) scores leads.

Omnisend leverages purchase history for hyper-targeted segments like 'VIP buyers' or 'frequent abandoners.' Deep ecommerce data integration enables 'bought X, recommend Y.'

**Tip:** Test segments weekly. Omnisend's 360° profiles reduce unsubscribe rates by unifying channels.

## Integrations and Ecommerce Focus

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier) but ecommerce syncing requires third-party tools like Zapier for Shopify.

Omnisend natively connects to Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce—auto-syncing orders, products, customers. Also supports Klaviyo-like popups and forms.

**Practical Advice:** For Shopify users, Omnisend installs in minutes with one-click. Mailchimp works via apps but may lag on real-time data.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is polished for non-marketers; onboarding tutorials abound.

Omnisend's dashboard prioritizes ecommerce metrics (revenue per recipient). Slightly steeper curve but guided setup wizards help.

Support: Mailchimp offers email/chat (paid plans); Omnisend provides 24/7 live chat, priority phone for Pro.

**Pro Tip:** Use free trials (14-30 days). Join their Facebook communities for user tips.

## Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp tracks opens, clicks, revenue (with tracking code). Detailed reports in higher plans.

Omnisend's revenue tracking is automatic for integrated stores, showing ROI per campaign/channel. Heatmaps and cohort analysis in Pro.

**Advice:** Focus on revenue per email sent (RPE). Omnisend reports 2-3x higher RPE for ecommerce.

**Pros:**

### Omnisend

**Cons:**

- SMS costs extra.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Omnisend if:** You're in ecommerce (Shopify/Woo), need SMS/push, prioritize automations recovering revenue (e.g., 20-30% cart recovery rates reported by users).

**Pick Mailchimp if:** Solopreneur, blogger, service business wanting simple emails, or budget under $20/month initially.

**Migration Tip:** Both import CSVs easily. Test parallel campaigns during trials to compare deliverability (both ~95% inbox rates).

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce growth with superior automations and omnichannel, potentially boosting revenue faster. Mailchimp remains king for versatile, low-barrier entry.
