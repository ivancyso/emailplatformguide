# MailerLite Review 2024: Is It the Best Email Marketing Tool for You?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business outreach. With so many options like Mailchimp, ConvertKit, and ActiveCampaign, MailerLite stands out for its simplicity, affordability, and robust features tailored for small to medium-sized businesses, bloggers, and e-commerce stores. In this in-depth MailerLite review, we'll break down its features, pricing, ease of use, and more to help you decide if it's worth your time and money.

MailerLite has been around since 2010 and serves over 2 million users worldwide. It's known for its user-friendly interface and no-frills approach to email marketing. Whether you're just starting or scaling up, this review covers everything you need to know.

## What is MailerLite?

MailerLite is a cloud-based email marketing service that helps you create, send, and track email campaigns. It goes beyond basic emails with tools for automation workflows, landing pages, popups, and subscription forms. The platform emphasizes ease of use, making it ideal for non-technical users.

Key highlights:
- **Drag-and-drop editor**: No coding required.
- **Automation**: Build customer journeys without hassle.
- **Integrations**: Connects with Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics, and more.
- **Analytics**: Track opens, clicks, bounces, and revenue.

MailerLite's free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month, which is generous for beginners.

## Key Features of MailerLite

MailerLite packs a punch with features that rival pricier competitors. Here's a deep dive into the standout ones.

### Email Campaign Builder

The drag-and-drop editor is intuitive. Start with 80+ responsive templates categorized by industry (e.g., e-commerce, newsletters). Customize with blocks for text, images, buttons, and dividers.

**Practical advice**: Use the "JSON editor" for advanced tweaks if needed. Always preview on mobile—MailerLite auto-optimizes, but test subject lines A/B for higher opens (aim for 20-30% open rates).

### Automation Workflows

Build multi-step automations like welcome series, abandoned cart reminders, or re-engagement campaigns. Triggers include sign-ups, purchases, or custom events.

**Practical tip**: Segment by behavior—send a "We miss you" email to inactive subscribers after 30 days. MailerLite's visual builder makes it drag-and-drop simple. Set delays, conditions (e.g., if opened email X), and A/B tests.

### Landing Pages and Popups

Create unlimited landing pages with the same editor. Add forms, countdown timers, and payment integrations via Stripe.

Popups include exit-intent, scroll-triggered, and fixed bars. **Pro tip**: Place popups on high-traffic pages but limit to 5% of visitors to avoid annoyance—MailerLite lets you target by page URL or device.

### Forms and Surveys

Embedded signup forms, inline, or popups. Use surveys for feedback with multiple-choice or open-ended questions.

**Advice**: Embed forms on sidebars or footers. Offer lead magnets like ebooks to boost conversions—track form performance in the dashboard.

### E-commerce and Integrations

Native Shopify and WooCommerce support tracks purchases and revenue. Integrates with Zapier for 1,000+ apps.

**Tip for stores**: Automate post-purchase upsells. Link to Google Analytics for full-funnel tracking.

### Analytics and Reporting

Detailed stats on campaigns: open rates, click maps, bounce rates, unsubscribes. Revenue tracking for e-com.

**Best practice**: Review weekly reports. If clicks are low (<2%), refine CTAs. Use heatmaps to see what links perform best.

## Pros and Cons of MailerLite

### Pros

- **High deliverability**: 99%+ inbox placement.
