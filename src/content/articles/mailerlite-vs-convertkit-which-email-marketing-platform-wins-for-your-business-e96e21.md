# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: In-Depth Comparison for 2023

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business. If you're torn between **MailerLite vs ConvertKit**, this guide breaks it down with real features, pricing, and practical advice. Both are popular for creators, bloggers, and small businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. MailerLite shines in affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit excels in creator-focused automations.

We'll compare pricing, ease of use, key features, integrations, support, and scalability. By the end, you'll know which fits your audience—whether you're a solopreneur or growing team.

## Pricing: Affordability vs Value

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer straightforward plans, but **MailerLite** is more budget-friendly, especially for starters.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite has a **free plan** for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month—perfect for testing. Paid plans start at $10/month (Growing Business) for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 1,000, $50 for 2,500, and so on. Enterprise options go higher with custom features like dedicated IPs.

Practical tip: If you're under 1,000 subs, stick to free and upgrade seamlessly. No contracts, monthly billing, and annual discounts (up to 20% off).

### ConvertKit Pricing

No free plan, but a 14-day trial. Creator plan starts at $15/month (up to 300 subs), $29 for 1,000, $49 for 3,000, up to $779 for 50,000+. Creator Pro adds advanced reporting and revenue optimizations from $45/month.

Practical tip: ConvertKit's pricing jumps faster for larger lists. Ideal if you need pro features early, but calculate based on subscriber growth—use their pricing calculator.

**Winner for Budgets:** MailerLite for free tier and lower entry. ConvertKit justifies cost for serious creators.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

Both are intuitive, but user interfaces differ.

### MailerLite Interface

Clean dashboard with drag-and-drop editor. Build campaigns in minutes—no coding. Visual automation builder uses blocks for triggers/actions. Mobile-responsive templates (100+ free).

Practical advice: Newbies, start with their tutorials. Import contacts via CSV easily; segmentation by tags/groups is straightforward.

### ConvertKit Interface

Creator-centric with sequences (email series) as core. Tag-based subscriber management over lists. Form builder is simple, but editor feels more text-focused.

Practical advice: If you're a blogger, sequences automate welcome series effortlessly. Steeper for non-creators due to tagging system.

**Winner:** MailerLite for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for those comfortable with tags.

## Core Features: Automation, Templates, and More

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, photo editor integration. Classic or drag-and-drop. A/B testing built-in.

ConvertKit: Fewer templates (focus on plain-text for authenticity), but visual editor improving. Strong A/B on subjects.

Practical tip: Test open rates—MailerLite's images boost engagement for e-commerce.

### Automation and Workflows

**MailerLite**: Block-based builder for if/then logic, RSS campaigns, A/B automations. Unlimited sends on paid plans.

**ConvertKit**: Sequences shine for drip campaigns. Tag triggers (e.g., "purchased" tag starts upsell). Commerce tools for digital sales.

Practical advice: Creators selling courses? ConvertKit's one-click upsells save time. For newsletters, MailerLite's RSS-to-email automates content.

### Forms, Popups, and Landing Pages

Both offer embeddable forms/popups. MailerLite includes high-converting popups (exit-intent, scroll-triggered) and unlimited landing pages. ConvertKit has forms/landing pages with better analytics.

Practical tip: Use MailerLite popups for list growth—conversion rates often 5-10% higher per their docs.

### Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite: Open/click rates, geography, device breakdowns. Heatmaps on emails.

ConvertKit: Revenue tracking, subscriber value, advanced reports in Pro.

Practical tip: Track ROI—ConvertKit if monetizing emails directly.

## Integrations and Scalability

**MailerLite**: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress/Stripe. E-commerce friendly (WooCommerce).

**ConvertKit**: Strong with Gumroad, Teachable for creators. Zapier too.

Both scale well—MailerLite to millions of subs, ConvertKit optimized for high-value lists.

Practical advice: Check Zapier limits; MailerLite's native integrations reduce costs.

## Customer Support and Resources

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base, Facebook group.

ConvertKit: Email support (faster on higher plans), podcasts, creator community.

Practical tip: MailerLite's chat resolves issues in minutes—great for non-tech users.

## Pros and Cons Summary

### MailerLite Pros
- Free plan
- Cheaper scaling
- Popups/landing pages

Cons: Less creator-specific commerce.

### ConvertKit Pros
- Powerful sequences/tags
- Monetization tools
- Creator community

Cons: No free plan, pricier.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Small business, budget-conscious, need popups/free tier. Ideal for e-commerce/newsletters.
- **Pick ConvertKit** if: Blogger/podcaster selling digital products, value sequences/revenue tracking.

Practical advice: Trial both. Export lists easily (CSV). Start with your list size—under 1k? MailerLite free.

## Conclusion

**MailerLite vs ConvertKit** boils down to needs: MailerLite wins for affordability and ease (best for starters), ConvertKit for creator automations and sales (premium choice). Neither is "best" universally—audit your goals. Growing lists? MailerLite saves money long-term. Selling courses? ConvertKit's tools pay off.

Ready to decide? Sign up for MailerLite's free plan or ConvertKit trial today. Track metrics after 30 days and switch if needed. Your email strategy thrives with the right fit.
