# Omnisend Review 2023: Is It the Best Email Marketing Tool for Ecommerce?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can make or break your ecommerce business. With countless options available, Omnisend stands out for its focus on online stores, offering omnichannel marketing that includes email, SMS, and web push notifications. In this comprehensive Omnisend review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, ease of use, and whether it's worth your investment. Whether you're a Shopify seller or running WooCommerce, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an ecommerce-focused marketing automation platform launched in 2014. It's designed specifically for online retailers, integrating seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, and Magento. Unlike general-purpose tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes omnichannel campaigns—combining email, SMS, and push notifications to boost customer engagement.

Key differentiators include pre-built ecommerce workflows (e.g., cart abandonment, welcome series) and a drag-and-drop editor tailored for product-focused content. It serves over 70,000 stores worldwide, with a free plan for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails per month. Practical tip: If you're starting small, the free tier lets you test automations without upfront costs.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs powerful features for ecommerce marketers. Here's a breakdown:

### Email and Campaign Builder

The drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with ecommerce-specific blocks for product recommendations, order summaries, and countdown timers. You can create responsive emails without coding. 

**Practical advice:** Use dynamic content blocks to personalize emails based on purchase history. For example, segment by "recently viewed products" to increase click-through rates by up to 20-30% (based on Omnisend's reported benchmarks).

### Automation Workflows

Omnisend shines in automations with 200+ pre-built templates for scenarios like:
- Welcome series
- Cart recovery (sends emails/SMS if carts are abandoned)
- Win-back campaigns for inactive customers
- Post-purchase upsells

**Setup tip:** Start with the cart abandonment workflow. Connect your store, set triggers (e.g., 1 hour post-abandonment), and A/B test subject lines. Users report recovery rates of 10-15% on average.

### SMS and Push Notifications

Omnisend's omnichannel approach lets you send SMS (via integrations like Twilio) and web push. SMS open rates hover around 98%, far surpassing email's 20-30%.

**Pro tip:** Combine channels—follow up an email with SMS for high-value carts. Ensure compliance with TCPA by getting opt-ins via signup forms.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Advanced segmentation uses behavioral data: purchases, browsing history, tags. The dashboard shows revenue per email, ROI tracking, and heatmaps.

**Advice:** Create segments like "VIP customers" (3+ purchases) for exclusive offers. Track metrics like attributed revenue to justify spend.

### Integrations

Native apps for Shopify, WooCommerce, Klaviyo alternatives, Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel. API access for custom needs.

## Omnisend Pricing Plans

Omnisend offers transparent, contact-based pricing:

| Plan | Price (monthly, billed annually) | Contacts | Key Features |
|------|---------------------------------|----------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | Up to 250 | 500 emails/mo, basic automations |
| Standard | $16 (500 contacts) | 500-100k | Unlimited emails, SMS, A/B testing |
| Pro | $59 (500 contacts) | 500-100k | Advanced reporting, API, custom events |

No contracts; monthly billing available at a premium. SMS costs extra (~$0.02/message in US). **Budget tip:** Scale from free to Standard as your list grows—most small stores fit under $50/mo.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-centric: Tailored for stores, not generic businesses.
- Excellent automations: Save hours on setup.
- High deliverability: 95%+ inbox rates.
- 24/7 chat/email support (Pro plan).
- Free plan with robust features.

### Cons
- SMS/push limited to paid plans.
- No built-in landing pages (use integrations).
- Steeper learning curve for non-ecommerce users.
- Pricing jumps with contacts (e.g., 10k contacts ~$105/mo Standard).

Real user feedback from G2/Capterra: 4.7/5 stars, praised for ease and ROI, critiqued for occasional editor glitches.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for:
- Shopify/WooCommerce stores with 100-50k customers.
- Brands prioritizing automation and omnichannel.
- Marketers seeking revenue-focused metrics.

Not for: B2B lead gen or non-ecommerce (try ActiveCampaign). **Decision framework:** If 50%+ revenue from ecommerce, Omnisend fits; else, consider Klaviyo or Mailchimp.

## Practical Setup Guide

1. **Sign up and integrate:** Free account > Connect store (5 mins).
2. **Import contacts/sync data:** Use CSV or native sync.
3. **Build first automation:** Cart recovery—edit template, set delays (1h email, 24h SMS).
4. **Design signup forms:** Popups with incentives (10% off).
5. **Test and launch:** Send previews, monitor opens.
6. **Optimize:** A/B test weekly, review revenue attribution.

**Common pitfalls to avoid:** Over-segmenting early (start broad), ignoring mobile previews, skipping compliance (GDPR/CAN-SPAM).

## Omnisend vs. Competitors

- **Vs. Klaviyo:** Omnisend cheaper for <10k contacts, better SMS; Klaviyo deeper Shopify flows.
- **Vs. Mailchimp:** Omnisend ecommerce superior; Mailchimp easier for beginners.
- **Vs. ActiveCampaign:** Omnisend omnichannel wins; AC better for complex CRM.

## Conclusion

Omnisend earns its spot as a top ecommerce email marketing tool, especially for automation-driven stores. Its free plan, intuitive workflows, and revenue tracking deliver real value—many users see 2-5x ROI within months. If you're scaling an online store, start with the free trial today. For larger enterprises, weigh Pro plan support.

**Final verdict:** 4.8/5. Highly recommended for ecommerce. Sign up via [Omnisend](https://www.omnisend.com) and reclaim abandoned carts.
