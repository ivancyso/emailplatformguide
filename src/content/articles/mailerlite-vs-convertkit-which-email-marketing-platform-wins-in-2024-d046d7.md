# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive sales. If you're torn between MailerLite and ConvertKit, this head-to-head comparison breaks it down based on real user needs. Both are popular for creators, bloggers, and small businesses, but they cater to slightly different workflows. We'll cover pricing, features, ease of use, support, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're on a tight budget or need advanced creator tools, read on for practical insights.

## Pricing: Affordability vs Value

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer free plans, but their paid tiers differ significantly.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite starts free for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Paid plans begin at $10/month (Growing Business, up to 500 subs) and scale to $20 for 1,000 subs. Enterprise options go higher but remain cost-effective. No contracts, monthly billing, and annual discounts up to 30%.

**Practical Tip:** Ideal for bootstrapped solopreneurs. Upgrade only when you hit limits—many users stay free for months.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit's free plan covers up to 1,000 subscribers with basic features (no advanced automations). Creator plan starts at $15/month (300 subs), scaling to $29 for 1,000. Pro plan adds commerce at $29/month (300 subs). Prices jump quickly with growth.

**Practical Tip:** Factor in subscriber count projections. ConvertKit's higher tiers suit those monetizing via emails, but test the free plan first.

**Winner:** MailerLite for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit if you need built-in selling tools.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop vs Intuitive Flows

### MailerLite Interface
Clean, beginner-friendly dashboard. Drag-and-drop editor with 80+ templates. Quick setup for campaigns, popups, and landing pages. Mobile-responsive everything.

**Practical Advice:** Start with templates for newsletters. Customize blocks (images, buttons) without coding. A/B testing is one-click.

### ConvertKit Interface
Visual automation builder shines for sequences. Simpler editor than MailerLite but powerful tagging/subscriber management. Forms and landing pages are straightforward.

**Practical Advice:** Use the 'Visual Builder' for drip campaigns. Tag-based segmentation (e.g., 'opened email') automates personalization effortlessly.

**Winner:** MailerLite for visual designers; ConvertKit for automation enthusiasts.

## Key Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
MailerLite: Responsive drag-and-drop, JSON exports, plain-text options. Strong for popups/websites.
ConvertKit: Clean, mobile-first builder. Focuses on content blocks; less flashy but conversion-oriented.

**Tip:** MailerLite if you embed forms everywhere; ConvertKit for clean creator newsletters.

### Automations and Segmentation
MailerLite: Trigger-based automations, behaviors, RSS-to-email. Basic tagging.
ConvertKit: Sequences (automated series), advanced tagging, subscriber scoring. Re-engagement campaigns built-in.

**Practical Advice:** For welcome series, ConvertKit's tagging (e.g., 'podcast listener') segments better. MailerLite suffices for simple workflows—set up in 10 minutes.

### Landing Pages and Forms
Both offer unlimited pages/forms. MailerLite includes popups, countdown timers. ConvertKit has commerce checkouts on Pro.

**Tip:** Use MailerLite for lead magnets with popups; ConvertKit for selling digital products directly.

### Analytics and Deliverability
MailerLite: Open/click rates, revenue tracking, heatmaps. 99%+ deliverability.
ConvertKit: Detailed subscriber activity, revenue per subscriber. Strong inbox placement.

**Practical Advice:** Track unsubscribe rates weekly. Both integrate Google Analytics for deeper insights.

### Integrations
MailerLite: 250+ (WordPress, Shopify, Zapier).
ConvertKit: 100+ focused on creators (Gumroad, Teachable).

**Tip:** Zapier bridges gaps—automate from Google Sheets to either.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Fast responses (under 1 hour).
ConvertKit: Email/ticket support (business hours), extensive academy/videos. Active creator community.

**Practical Advice:** MailerLite for immediate help; ConvertKit's resources teach long-term.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros:** Cheaper, feature-rich free plan, websites/popups included.
**Cons:** Less advanced tagging, no native commerce.

### ConvertKit
**Pros:** Creator-focused automations, monetization tools.
**Cons:** Pricier, steeper learning for non-creators.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
- Beginners/small teams on budgets.
- Need popups, sites, high-volume sends.
- Example: Bloggers building lists quickly.

**Get Started:** Sign up free, import CSV, send first campaign in 30 mins.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
- Content creators (podcasters, course sellers).
- Rely on sequences/tags for nurturing.
- Example: Newsletter writers monetizing subs.

**Get Started:** Free trial sequences for your audience funnel.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

MailerLite edges out for most due to value and simplicity—perfect if you're starting or scaling affordably. ConvertKit wins for serious creators prioritizing automations and sales.

**Actionable Steps:** 1. Audit your needs (subs, budget, features). 2. Test both free plans. 3. Migrate via CSV export/import. 4. A/B test campaigns post-switch.

Both deliver results with consistent use. Focus on list-building and value-driven content over tools. Questions? Check their docs or comment below.
