# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: The Ultimate Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options, pitting Omnisend against Mailchimp makes sense for many users. Both are popular tools, but they cater to slightly different needs. Omnisend shines in ecommerce automation, while Mailchimp offers a broader all-in-one marketing suite.

This in-depth comparison covers pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, support, and more. Whether you're running an online store or a service-based business, we'll help you decide which platform fits your goals. Data is based on official websites and user reviews from sites like G2 and Capterra as of late 2023.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Your Budget

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer free plans, but scales differ based on subscribers and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic automation.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/month (500 contacts), unlimited sends.
- **Standard**: $20/month (500 contacts), advanced automation, A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350+/month (10k+ contacts), personalization, priority support.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts, with overage fees. It's cost-effective for small lists but pricey for growing ones.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, basic workflows.
- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts), unlimited emails, SMS/push add-ons.
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts), advanced segmentation, unlimited workflows.
- **Pay-as-you-go**: No contact limits, pay per email/SMS sent.

Omnisend is more flexible for ecommerce with usage-based options. For high-volume senders, it can be cheaper than Mailchimp's tiered model.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your monthly sends and list size. Use free trials (14 days for both) to test costs. If you're ecommerce-focused with abandoned carts, Omnisend's add-ons might save money long-term.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities

Both platforms handle email campaigns well, but differences emerge in automation and multichannel support.

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp boasts 100+ professional templates, drag-and-drop builder, and content optimizer. It's beginner-friendly with mobile previews.

Omnisend offers 250+ ecommerce-optimized templates, dynamic product blocks, and popup builders. Templates auto-pull product data from Shopify/WooCommerce.

**Winner**: Mailchimp for general use; Omnisend for stores.

### Automation Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Single, classic, and customer journey automations. Good for welcome series, birthdays. Limited branching.
- **Omnisend**: Omnichannel workflows (email + SMS + push). Pre-built for cart recovery, back-in-stock, cross-sells. Deep segmentation by behavior.

Practical tip: For abandoned cart recovery, Omnisend recovers 20-30% more on average (per their case studies). Set up workflows early—test with small segments to refine triggers.

### Segmentation and Analytics
Both segment by opens/clicks. Mailchimp's analytics include revenue tracking; Omnisend excels in ecommerce ROI with attributed sales.

Omnisend's dashboard shows SMS/email performance side-by-side.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is intuitive, with guided tours. Ideal for non-marketers.

Omnisend has a steeper curve but ecommerce wizards simplify setup.

**Advice**: If your team is new to email, start with Mailchimp. For Shopify users, Omnisend's one-click integration cuts setup time to minutes.

## Integrations and Ecommerce Focus

- **Mailchimp**: 300+ integrations (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Basic ecommerce via plugins.
- **Omnisend**: Native Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce sync. Plus Klaviyo-like popups, SMS via Twilio.

**Pro Tip**: Check Zapier for custom flows. Migrate lists carefully—export CSV from old tool, import, then remap tags to avoid deliverability drops.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

Mailchimp: Email/ticket support (priority on higher plans). Deliverability ~85-90% (per Mail-Tester).

Omnisend: 24/7 chat/email, dedicated managers on Pro. Strong deliverability (90%+), IP warming tools.

User reviews praise Omnisend's responsive team for ecommerce troubleshooting.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros
- Generous free plan
- Vast template library
- All-in-one (social, ads)

Cons:
- Expensive scaling
- Weaker ecommerce automation

### Omnisend Pros
- Ecommerce powerhouse
- Multichannel (SMS/push)
- Flexible pricing

Cons:
- Smaller free plan
- Less non-ecom focus

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Omnisend if**:
- You run an online store (Shopify users love it).
- Need SMS/push for higher engagement.
- Prioritize automation ROI.

**Pick Mailchimp if**:
- General marketing/blog newsletters.
- Budget for small lists.
- Want simplicity over depth.

**Migration Advice**: Both support CSV imports. Clean your list first (remove bounces). Run parallel campaigns during switch to compare performance.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce businesses with superior automation and multichannel tools, potentially boosting revenue faster. Mailchimp remains a solid, user-friendly choice for beginners or non-store owners.

Test both free plans side-by-side. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%), clicks (2-5%), and revenue per email. The right tool aligns with your audience and goals—there's no one-size-fits-all.
