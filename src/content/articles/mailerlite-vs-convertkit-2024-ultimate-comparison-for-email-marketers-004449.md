# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business or content creation efforts. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams. Both platforms excel in automation and deliverability, but they cater to slightly different needs. This in-depth comparison covers pricing, features, user experience, and real-world use cases to guide your decision.

## Pricing: Affordability vs Value

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email tool. Here's how MailerLite and ConvertKit stack up based on their current plans (as of 2024).

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite offers a generous free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—perfect for testing without commitment. Paid plans start at $10/month for 500 subscribers (Growing Business plan), scaling to $20 for 1,000, and higher tiers like Advanced at $21/month for 500 subs with extras like A/B testing and web push notifications.

- **Pros**: Low entry cost, monthly billing, no contracts.
- **Cons**: Advanced features require upgrading.

Practical tip: Start free and upgrade as your list grows. Use their subscriber calculator to forecast costs accurately.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit) has no perpetual free plan, only a 14-day trial. The Creator plan starts at $15/month for up to 300 subscribers and 10,000 emails, jumping to $29 for 500-1,000 subs. The Creator Pro plan begins at $25/month for 300 subs, adding advanced automation and revenue tracking.

- **Pros**: Scales with features like commerce tools.
- **Cons**: Higher starting price, annual discounts only (15% off).

Practical tip: If you're a creator monetizing via products, the Pro plan's checkout features justify the cost. Compare your projected subscriber growth against both calculators.

**Verdict**: MailerLite wins for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit for those needing creator-specific tools.

## Ease of Use and Editor Comparison

Both platforms prioritize intuitive interfaces, but differences emerge in customization.

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Editor

MailerLite's editor is beginner-friendly with blocks for text, images, buttons, and dynamic content. It supports responsive designs and HTML for pros. Features like photo editing and RSS campaigns simplify workflows.

Practical advice: Use the template library (100+ free) to launch campaigns quickly. Test mobile previews before sending to ensure 99% deliverability rates.

### ConvertKit's Visual Builder

ConvertKit uses a block-based builder with strong personalization (e.g., subscriber tags). It's clean but less visual than MailerLite—no inline photo editing. Focuses on sequences over one-off emails.

Practical advice: Leverage the "inline forms" for seamless opt-ins on blogs. Segment lists via tags for hyper-targeted sends, reducing unsubscribes by 20-30%.

**Verdict**: MailerLite for visual designers; ConvertKit for tag-based segmentation pros.

## Automation and Segmentation Features

Automation is where both shine, but approaches differ.

### MailerLite Automations

Single-trigger automations with if/else logic, plus A/B testing on subjects/lines. Integrates with Facebook leads and surveys for list building.

Practical tip: Set up welcome series with behavior triggers (e.g., link clicks) to boost open rates. Use popups and forms with 5+ targeting options for 15-25% conversion lifts.

### ConvertKit Automations (Sequences)

Visual automation builder with unlimited sequences. Tag-based subscriber journeys excel for creators (e.g., nurture leads to sales).

Practical tip: Build "one-click upsells" in sequences. Integrate with Gumroad or Teachable for automated product delivery, streamlining e-commerce.

**Verdict**: ConvertKit edges out for complex creator funnels; MailerLite suffices for straightforward automations.

## Integrations and Additional Tools

### MailerLite Integrations

200+ via Zapier, native WordPress plugin, Google Analytics, Stripe. Includes landing pages, forms, and popups—no extra cost.

Practical advice: Embed forms on Shopify or custom sites. Track ROI with UTM parameters in analytics.

### ConvertKit Integrations

Native with WordPress, Ghost, Squarespace; commerce with Shopify, Patreon. Strong podcast subscriber tools.

Practical advice: Use webhooks for custom CRMs. Creator Network connects you to affiliates effortlessly.

**Verdict**: Tie—choose based on your tech stack (MailerLite for broad, ConvertKit for creator ecosystem).

## Analytics and Support

MailerLite provides opens, clicks, bounces, and revenue tracking (Advanced plan). 24/7 live chat, email support, and a knowledge base.

ConvertKit offers detailed reports on earnings per subscriber, A/B insights. Priority email support; chat for Pro.

Practical tip: Both achieve 95%+ deliverability—warm up lists gradually (start 200/day) and clean bounces monthly.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Beginners/small businesses under 5k subs.
- Need free tier, landing pages, popups.
- Budget under $20/month.

Real-world example: A local bakery uses MailerLite for event promos, achieving 40% open rates with simple automations.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers/podcasters/creators.
- Complex sequences, tagging, monetization.
- Willing to pay for pro features.

Real-world example: A course creator scales to 10k subs with tag-driven upsells, hitting $5k/month revenue.

## Migration and Getting Started

Both offer CSV imports. MailerLite has a migration wizard; ConvertKit guides tag mapping.

Practical steps:
1. Export from current tool.
2. Map fields (name, email, tags).
3. Test a small send.
4. Replicate top campaigns.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage and style. MailerLite offers unbeatable value and simplicity for startups (free plan + low costs), ideal if you're building lists affordably. ConvertKit dominates for creators with its tagging, sequences, and commerce focus, worth the premium for revenue growth.

Test both: MailerLite's free plan and ConvertKit's trial. Track metrics like open rates (aim 25-40%) and conversions post-switch. For most under 2k subs, start with MailerLite; scale to ConvertKit for advanced needs.

Ultimately, the "best" is what fits your workflow—prioritize a trial today.
