Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp are both established email marketing platforms, but they price and structure their plans very differently. Mailchimp charges by contacts. Brevo charges by emails sent. That single difference changes the math for many businesses.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Brevo | Mailchimp |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| Pricing model | Pay per email | Pay per contact |
| Free plan | 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts | 500 contacts, 1K emails/mo |
| Starting price | $9/mo (5K emails) | $13/mo (500 contacts) |
| SMS | Built-in | Add-on |
| Transactional email | Built-in | Separate product (Mandrill) |
| CRM | Built-in (free) | Built-in |
| Automation | Visual builder | Customer Journey Builder |
| Landing pages | Yes | Yes (paid) |

## The Pricing Difference That Matters

This is the biggest distinction. Mailchimp charges based on how many contacts are in your list. Brevo charges based on how many emails you send.

**If you have a large list but send infrequently** (quarterly updates, event announcements), Brevo saves you money. You can have 50,000 contacts and only pay for the emails you actually send.

**If you have a small list but send frequently** (daily newsletters, drip campaigns), Mailchimp's per-contact model may be cheaper.

| Scenario | Brevo Cost | Mailchimp Cost |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| 10,000 contacts, 1 email/week | ~$25/mo (40K emails) | $110/mo |
| 1,000 contacts, daily emails | ~$25/mo (30K emails) | $26/mo |
| 50,000 contacts, 2 emails/mo | ~$25/mo (100K emails) | $385/mo |

For [nonprofits](/email-marketing-for-nonprofits) and organizations with large but infrequently contacted lists, Brevo is dramatically cheaper.

## Transactional Email

This is Brevo's other major advantage. Transactional emails (order confirmations, password resets, shipping notifications) are built into the same platform as marketing email. No separate tool, no extra cost.

Mailchimp separates transactional email into Mandrill, a separate paid product that requires a Standard plan or higher. This adds complexity and cost.

If you need both marketing and transactional email, Brevo gives you both in one platform at one price.

## SMS Marketing

Both platforms offer SMS. Brevo includes SMS in all plans (pay per message). Mailchimp offers SMS as an add-on feature available on Standard plans and above.

Brevo's SMS is more tightly integrated — you can mix email and SMS in the same automation workflow, which is useful for appointment reminders, flash sales, and abandoned cart recovery.

## Automation

Both platforms offer visual automation builders. Mailchimp's Customer Journey Builder is more polished and includes pre-built templates. Brevo's automation is functional and covers the same use cases (welcome series, abandoned cart, date triggers) but feels less refined.

For advanced automation needs, both platforms fall behind [ActiveCampaign](/activecampaign-vs-mailchimp), which offers the most powerful automation builder in the industry.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- Businesses with large contact lists and moderate send frequency
- Anyone who needs transactional + marketing email in one platform
- Budget-conscious teams (Brevo is cheaper for most use cases)
- Businesses that need built-in SMS
- [Small businesses](/best-email-marketing-small-business) that want CRM + email + SMS in one tool

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Businesses that send high-frequency emails to small lists
- Teams that want extensive templates and design tools
- Users who need deep audience analytics and reporting
- Businesses already using Mailchimp's ecosystem (ads, social, landing pages)

## The Verdict

**Brevo wins on value** for most businesses, especially those with larger lists. The pay-per-email model, built-in transactional email, CRM, and SMS make it the more complete platform at a lower price.

**Mailchimp wins on polish** — better templates, deeper analytics, and a more refined user experience. If design and reporting matter more than cost, Mailchimp justifies the premium.
