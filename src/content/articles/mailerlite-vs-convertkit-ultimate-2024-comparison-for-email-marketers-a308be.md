# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business or content creation efforts. With so many options, pitting MailerLite vs ConvertKit head-to-head helps clarify which fits your needs. Both are popular for their user-friendly interfaces and powerful features, but they cater to slightly different audiences. MailerLite shines for budget-conscious beginners and small teams, while ConvertKit targets creators and bloggers with advanced automation.

In this comparison, we'll dive into pricing, features, ease of use, support, and real-world use cases. Whether you're migrating from another tool or starting fresh, this guide provides practical advice to make an informed decision. Let's break it down.

## Overview of MailerLite

MailerLite is a Lithuania-based email service provider (ESP) launched in 2010, known for its affordability and simplicity. It offers a forever-free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—ideal for solopreneurs testing the waters.

Key strengths include a drag-and-drop editor, popups, landing pages, and basic automation. It's compliant with GDPR and CAN-SPAM, with strong deliverability rates around 99%. Integrations cover WordPress, Shopify, and Zapier.

**Practical tip:** If you're on a tight budget, start with MailerLite's free tier. Use their A/B testing to optimize subject lines early—data shows open rates improve by 20-30% with iterative testing.

## Overview of ConvertKit

ConvertKit, founded in 2013 by Nathan Barry, is creator-centric. It emphasizes sequences (automated email series), tagging for segmentation, and commerce tools like digital product sales. No free plan, but a 14-day trial lets you explore fully.

It's praised for its clean interface and focus on subscriber relationships. Features like visual automations and subscriber scoring help nurture leads effectively. Deliverability is excellent, often exceeding 98%, with robust anti-spam measures.

**Practical tip:** Creators should leverage ConvertKit's tagging system. Assign tags based on behavior (e.g., 'opened welcome email') to trigger personalized follow-ups, boosting engagement by segmenting your list dynamically.

## Pricing Comparison: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

Pricing is a major differentiator.

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month.
- **Growing Business:** $10/month for 500 subscribers (scales up; e.g., $20 for 1,000).
- **Advanced:** $20/month base, adds automation and A/B testing.
- **Enterprise:** Custom.

No subscriber caps beyond plans; pay per subscriber band. Annual billing saves 30%.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free (Creator):** Up to 300 subscribers (limited features; trial for full access).
- **Creator:** $15/month for 300-1,000 subscribers.
- **Creator Pro:** $29/month adds advanced reporting, subscriber scoring.
- **Enterprise:** Custom from $679/month.

Billed per subscriber; annual discount 20%.

**Verdict:** MailerLite wins for affordability, especially under 1,000 subs. ConvertKit's higher tiers justify costs for creators monetizing via emails (e.g., courses). **Advice:** Calculate your list size growth. If under 500 subs, MailerLite saves $100+/year. Export lists easily from both for switching.

## Feature Comparison

Both offer core ESP functions, but depth varies.

### Email Builder and Templates
- **MailerLite:** Intuitive drag-and-drop with 80+ responsive templates. Photo blocks and custom HTML supported.
- **ConvertKit:** Block-based editor, simpler but less visual flair. 100+ templates optimized for creators.

**Tip:** Test mobile previews—80% of emails open on phones. MailerLite's editor excels here for quick iterations.

### Automation and Workflows
- **MailerLite:** Basic automations (triggers like sign-ups), RSS campaigns. No complex branching.
- **ConvertKit:** Advanced sequences, visual builder with if/then logic, ending loops. Superior for nurture series.

**Practical advice:** For a welcome series, ConvertKit's tagging automates better (e.g., tag 'interested-in-course' → send upsell). MailerLite suits simple drip campaigns.

### Segmentation and Analytics
- **MailerLite:** Groups, tags, custom fields. Solid open/click tracking, heatmaps.
- **ConvertKit:** Tags, segments, scoring. Deeper revenue tracking for commerce.

### Additional Features
- Landing pages/popups: Both strong; MailerLite more templates.
- Integrations: ConvertKit edges with native commerce (Stripe, Gumroad); MailerLite via Zapier.
- Forms: ConvertKit's inline forms convert 15-20% higher per user reports.

**Overall:** ConvertKit for sophisticated automations; MailerLite for all-in-one basics.

## Ease of Use and Learning Curve

MailerLite feels more beginner-friendly with its dashboard and tutorials. Setup takes minutes—import CSV, design campaign, send.

ConvertKit's interface is clean but assumes email savvy. Visual automations reduce clicks long-term.

**Tip:** Watch onboarding videos (both free). Migrate lists via CSV: clean data first (remove bounces) to avoid deliverability hits.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

- **Support:** MailerLite: Email/chat (paid plans), knowledge base. ConvertKit: Email, chat (Pro+), creator-focused help.
- **Deliverability:** Both top-tier; warm up IPs gradually. Use double opt-in.

**Advice:** Monitor spam complaints (<0.1%). Test sends to Gmail/Yahoo.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros:** Cheap/free plan, easy editor, websites/popups.
**Cons:** Limited advanced automation, fewer commerce tools.

### ConvertKit
**Pros:** Powerful sequences, tagging, monetization.
**Cons:** Pricier, no true free plan.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs ConvertKit?

- **Pick MailerLite if:** Small business, blogger on budget, need popups/landing pages. Great for e-commerce starters.
- **Pick ConvertKit if:** Content creator, course seller, need deep segmentation. Scales with audience growth.

**Migration tip:** Both support CSV exports/imports. Test parallel campaigns for 1-2 months before full switch.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit? It depends on your stage. MailerLite offers unbeatable value for starters (free plan + features), while ConvertKit empowers creators with pro-level tools worth the premium.

For most under 1,000 subs, start with MailerLite—upgrade if automations bottleneck. Creators: ConvertKit's ROI shines via higher conversions.
