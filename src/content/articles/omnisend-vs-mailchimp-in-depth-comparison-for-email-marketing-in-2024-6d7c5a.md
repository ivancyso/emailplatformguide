# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options, pitting Omnisend against Mailchimp makes sense for many users. Both platforms cater to businesses looking to nurture leads, drive sales, and engage customers via email. But they shine in different areas.

Mailchimp has long been a staple for beginners and small businesses, known for its user-friendly interface and robust free plan. Omnisend, on the other hand, targets ecommerce brands with advanced automation and omnichannel features like SMS and web push notifications.

In this comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur or scaling an online store, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, launched in 2001, powers over 13 million users worldwide. It's ideal for newsletters, promotional campaigns, and basic automations. Key strengths include:

- **Intuitive Drag-and-Drop Editor**: Create emails without coding.
- **Audience Segmentation**: Tag contacts and segment based on behavior.
- **Content Studio**: AI-powered tools for subject lines and copy.

However, Mailchimp shifted to a pay-as-you-grow model in 2023, which increased costs for growing lists. It's best for non-ecommerce businesses focused on content marketing.

### Practical Advice for Mailchimp Users

Start with the free plan (up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends). Use behavioral segmentation for targeted campaigns—e.g., send re-engagement emails to inactive subscribers. Integrate with Google Analytics for tracking ROI.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend specializes in ecommerce, integrating seamlessly with Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce. It supports email, SMS, and push notifications in one dashboard. Standout features:

- **Pre-Built Workflows**: Cart abandonment, welcome series, and cross-sells.
- **Product Recommendations**: Dynamic blocks pulling from your store.
- **Popups and Forms**: Capture leads on-site without extra tools.

Omnisend's free plan covers 250 contacts and 500 emails per month, scaling affordably for stores.

### Practical Advice for Omnisend Users

Leverage omnichannel campaigns: Pair email with SMS for 98% open rates on reminders. Set up workflows triggered by purchase history—e.g., upsell high-LTV customers. Use A/B testing on send times to optimize delivery.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major differentiator. Here's a side-by-side (billed annually, as of 2024):

| Feature/Plan | Mailchimp | Omnisend |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| **Free** | 500 contacts, 1k sends/mo | 250 contacts, 500 emails/mo + unlimited SMS credits (fair use) |
| **Starter/Standard** | Essentials: $13/mo (5k contacts) | Standard: $16/mo (500 contacts) |
| **Growth** | Standard: $20/mo (5k) | Pro: $59/mo (500 contacts, advanced reporting) |
| **Enterprise** | Premium: $350+/mo | Custom |

**Key Insight**: Mailchimp charges based on contacts only, while Omnisend factors in sends too—but ecommerce users save with unlimited sends in higher plans. For 5,000 contacts sending 50k emails/mo, Omnisend Pro (~$120) beats Mailchimp Standard (~$75 but limited automations).

**Practical Tip**: Calculate your TCO. If under 500 contacts, both free plans work. For ecommerce with high volume, Omnisend's SMS inclusion adds value without extra fees.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Ease of Use

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners—its editor feels like Canva. Omnisend is close but ecommerce-focused, so learning workflows takes 1-2 hours.

**Advice**: Test both free trials. Mailchimp for quick newsletters; Omnisend for store-synced automations.

### Email Templates and Design

Both offer 100+ responsive templates. Mailchimp emphasizes creative freedom; Omnisend excels in product-focused designs with dynamic content.

**Pro Tip**: Use Omnisend's blocks for personalized product grids—boosts clicks by 20-30% in tests.

### Automation and Workflows

Omnisend dominates with 200+ templates for ecommerce (e.g., back-in-stock alerts). Mailchimp's automations are solid but ecommerce-limited without add-ons.

**Advice**: Map your customer journey. Automate post-purchase thank-yous in Omnisend to reduce refunds.

### Integrations

Mailchimp: 300+ (Google Workspace, Facebook, Zapier).
Omnisend: 100+ focused on ecommerce (Klaviyo alternatives, Google Ads, Zendesk).

**Tip**: Prioritize native Shopify integration—Omnisend syncs orders real-time.

### Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp: Revenue tracking, heatmaps.
Omnisend: Omnichannel ROI, predicted CLV.

**Practical Advice**: Track attributed revenue. Omnisend's dashboard shows SMS lift on email campaigns.

### Deliverability and Compliance

Both score 99%+ inbox rates. Mailchimp has stronger global compliance tools; Omnisend shines in GDPR for EU stores.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:

- Beginner-friendly
- Strong social ads integration

**Cons**:
- Expensive scaling
- Limited ecommerce automations
- Recent UI changes frustrated users

### Omnisend
**Pros**:
- Ecommerce powerhouse
- Omnichannel (SMS/push free in pro)
- Affordable for high-volume sends

**Cons**:
- Smaller free plan
- Less ideal for non-store newsletters
- Fewer general templates

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp if**: You're a blogger, agency, or B2B with low-volume emails. Great for one-off campaigns and teams needing collaboration.

- **Choose Omnisend if**: Running an online store (Shopify/Woo). Perfect for recovery flows, personalization, and multi-channel.

**Decision Framework**:
1. List size <1k? Free plans suffice.
2. Ecommerce revenue >20% from email? Omnisend.
3. Need advanced reporting? Test both trials.

Migrate tip: Export CSV from one, import to the other—takes under an hour.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce growth, offering superior automations and channels at competitive prices. Mailchimp remains king for versatile, beginner marketing.

Ultimately, sign up for free trials. Send test campaigns to your list and monitor open rates, clicks, and revenue. The best choice aligns with your goals—ecommerce automation favors Omnisend, while broad marketing suits Mailchimp.

Ready to choose? Start with Omnisend for stores or Mailchimp for starters. Track results quarterly and reassess.
