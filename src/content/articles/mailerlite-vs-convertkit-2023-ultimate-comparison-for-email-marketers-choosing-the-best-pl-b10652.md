# MailerLite vs ConvertKit 2023: Ultimate Comparison for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams. Both are popular for their user-friendly interfaces and powerful features, but they cater to slightly different needs. MailerLite shines with affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit excels in creator-focused automation.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, and more. Whether you're migrating from another tool or starting fresh, you'll get practical advice to decide. Let's dive in.

## Pricing: Which Offers Better Value?

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms use subscriber-based tiers, but MailerLite edges out with a more generous free plan.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite's free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—ideal for testing without commitment. Paid plans start at $10/month (billed monthly) for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 1,000, $30 for 2,500, and so on. Annual billing drops prices by 16%, e.g., $8/month for 500 subs. No hidden fees, and you can send unlimited emails on paid plans.

**Practical Tip:** Start free, import contacts, and build campaigns. Upgrade only when you hit limits—perfect for bootstrapped creators.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit offers a free plan up to 300 subscribers (recently expanded from 1,000 emails/month limit). Paid Creator plans begin at $29/month for 300-1,000 subscribers, jumping to $49 for 1,000-3,000. Higher tiers like Creator Pro ($49+) add advanced reporting. Annual discounts apply (20% off). Note: Email volume is unlimited on paid plans, but the free tier caps sends.

**Practical Tip:** If you're under 300 subs, ConvertKit's free plan works for basics. Budget $29+ monthly for growth—calculate ROI by tracking open rates post-signup.

**Winner:** MailerLite for cost-conscious users; ConvertKit if you need pro features early.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly Dashboards

Both platforms prioritize intuitive designs, but MailerLite feels more straightforward for absolute beginners.

MailerLite's dashboard is clean, with drag-and-drop everything. Setup takes minutes: verify domain, import CSV lists, and launch. No steep learning curve.

ConvertKit uses a visual builder too, but its tagging system adds complexity. Great for power users segmenting audiences by behavior.

**Practical Advice:** Test both free trials. Time your first newsletter creation—MailerLite often clocks under 10 minutes. Use ConvertKit if you plan heavy personalization from day one.

## Email Editor and Templates

### MailerLite Editor
Drag-and-drop editor with 80+ responsive templates. Customize blocks for images, buttons, text. JSON editor for coders. A/B testing built-in.

**Pro Tip:** Duplicate winning templates. Use dynamic content blocks for subscriber-specific recommendations.

### ConvertKit Editor
Similar drag-and-drop, 100+ templates optimized for creators. Strong on mobile previews. Visual automation builder integrates seamlessly.

**Pro Tip:** Leverage pre-built sequences for welcome series—saves hours.

Both deliver high deliverability (99%+ claimed), but test with tools like Mail-Tester.com.

## Automation and Segmentation

Automation is where ConvertKit pulls ahead for advanced users.

### MailerLite Automations
Workflow builder for triggers like signups, purchases. Basic segmentation by tags/groups. Time-based delays, A/B splits.

**Practical Advice:** Build a 7-day nurture sequence: Day 1 welcome, Day 3 value tip, Day 7 offer. Track drop-offs to refine.

### ConvertKit Automations
Superior tagging/segmentation. Actions based on opens/clicks/tags. Re-engagement campaigns auto-trigger for inactive subs.

**Practical Advice:** Tag by content interest (e.g., "podcast-fan"). Segment broadcasts for 20-30% higher opens.

**Winner:** ConvertKit for complex funnels; MailerLite for simple setups.

## Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

Both include these without add-ons.

MailerLite: Unlimited landing pages/forms/popups. Drag-and-drop builders, 20+ templates. Embed anywhere.

ConvertKit: Native pages/forms with A/B testing. Strong for lead magnets like ebooks.

**Practical Tip:** A/B test headlines on forms (e.g., "Free Guide" vs "Unlock Secrets"). Aim for 5-10% conversion; optimize with heatmaps via integrations.

## Integrations and Zapier Support

MailerLite: 1,000+ via native/Zapier. Connects to WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics.

ConvertKit: Excellent for creators—native WooCommerce, Teachable. Zapier-heavy.

**Advice:** List your stack (e.g., Carrd site + Stripe). Check compatibility docs before committing.

## Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite: Opens, clicks, bounces, revenue tracking. Basic heatmaps.

ConvertKit: Deeper insights—subscriber value, revenue per sub, churn prediction (Pro).

**Tip:** Focus on revenue per email sent. Both export CSVs for Google Sheets analysis.

## Customer Support

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base. Fast responses.

ConvertKit: Email/ticket (business hours), Creator Network community, extensive help docs.

**Practical:** Use chat for urgent DNS issues; communities for strategy.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
Small businesses, bloggers under 1k subs, budget-focused teams. If simplicity > complexity, pick this.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Content creators, podcasters, e-commerce with heavy automation needs. Scale-ready for 1k+ subs.

**Migration Tip:** Export lists as CSV. Test automations pre-switch. Run parallel campaigns for 2 weeks.

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit? MailerLite wins for affordability and ease (best starter), ConvertKit for automation depth (growth-focused). Evaluate based on subs, budget, tech comfort.

**Next Steps:** Sign up for both free plans. Build one campaign each, compare metrics. Your audience will dictate the winner.
