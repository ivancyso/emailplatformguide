# Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Top Picks for Email Marketing

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for alternatives. If you're facing high fees, need advanced automations, or want better deliverability, it's time to explore options. This roundup covers the best Mailchimp alternatives, focusing on features, pricing, and real-world use cases for small businesses, e-commerce stores, and creators.

We'll compare key players like ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, Brevo, and others based on practical factors: ease of setup, automation capabilities, customer support, and scalability. All recommendations draw from official pricing pages and user-reported experiences as of 2024—no hype, just facts to help you switch confidently.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but jump quickly (e.g., $75+ for 5,000 contacts with pro features). Common pain points include:

- **Limited automations**: Basic journeys require upgrades.
- **Deliverability dips**: Recent reports note lower open rates for some users.
- **Editor limitations**: Drag-and-drop is solid but lacks advanced segmentation.

Alternatives often offer unlimited sends, better tagging/segmentation, and integrations with tools like Shopify or WordPress. Before switching, export your Mailchimp list (CSV via Audience > Export) and test imports.

## Top 7 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign shines for marketing automation. It's ideal for businesses needing CRM integration and advanced workflows.

**Key Features**:
- 750+ integrations (Shopify, Salesforce).
- Site tracking and lead scoring.
- Conditional content and split testing.

**Pricing**: Starts at $29/month (Essentials, 500 contacts). Scales to $149 for 2,500 contacts (Professional). 14-day free trial, no credit card.

**Practical Advice**: Use for e-commerce with abandoned cart automations. Setup tip: Map tags during import to leverage machine learning for predictions. Users report 20-30% higher engagement vs. Mailchimp due to better personalization.

### 2. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Perfect for creators and bloggers. Focuses on simplicity with powerful tagging.

**Key Features**n- Visual automations (no coding).
- Native landing pages and forms.
- Subscriber scoring and one-click upsells.

**Pricing**: Free for 10,000 subscribers (limited). Creator plan $29/month unlimited. Free migration service.

**Practical Advice**: Segment by tags (e.g., 'podcast listener') for targeted broadcasts. Integrate with Gumroad for product launches. Great if your list is under 10k—switchers save 50%+ on costs.

### 3. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

Budget-friendly with SMS and chat included. Strong for transactional emails.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts on free plan (300 emails/day).
- Multi-channel (email, SMS, WhatsApp).
- Detailed analytics and A/B testing.

**Pricing**: Free forever tier. Starter $25/month (20k emails). Free migration tool.

**Practical Advice**: Use for e-commerce with order confirmations. Monitor deliverability via dedicated IP add-on ($20/month). Ideal starter alternative—many Mailchimp users migrate here for cost savings without losing features.

### 4. Klaviyo

E-commerce powerhouse, especially for Shopify users.

**Key Features**:
- Predictive analytics and revenue tracking.
- Flow builder for automations.
- UGC and review integrations.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails. Scales $20+/month based on contacts. Free Shopify import.

**Practical Advice**: Build flows for post-purchase upsells. Track ROI directly in dashboard. If you sell online, Klaviyo's data-driven segments outperform Mailchimp's basic lists.

### 5. GetResponse

All-in-one with webinars and funnels.

**Key Features**:
- Conversion funnels and landing pages.
- Autoresponders and webinars (up to 100 attendees free).
- E-commerce tools.

**Pricing**: Free for 500 contacts. Basic $19/month. 30-day trial.

**Practical Advice**: Host webinars to nurture leads. Use perfect timing feature for sends. Good for solopreneurs scaling beyond Mailchimp's limits.

### 6. AWeber

Reliable for small businesses with strong support.

**Key Features**:
- Drag-and-drop builder.
- RSS-to-email.
- Spam testing.

**Pricing**: $12.50/month (500 subscribers). Free trial.

**Practical Advice**: Automate newsletters from your blog RSS. Excellent phone/chat support—call during migration for help.

### 7. Constant Contact

User-friendly for non-techies.

**Key Features**:
- Event management.
- Social ads integration.
- AI subject lines.

**Pricing**: $12/month (500 contacts). 60-day trial.

**Practical Advice**: Great for events/local businesses. Use AI tools to boost opens by testing lines.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: List size? E-com? Automations? Match to features above.
2. **Test Trials**: Sign up for 14-30 day trials. Import a segment of your list.
3. **Check Deliverability**: Review sender scores on sites like Mail-Tester.
4. **Migration Steps**:
   - Export CSV from Mailchimp.
   - Use built-in importers (most offer this).
   - Remap tags/segments.
   - Update website forms (e.g., via Zapier).
5. **Budget Tip**: Calculate TCO—factor sends, contacts, add-ons.

Prioritize platforms with easy unsubscribes and GDPR compliance to avoid blacklists.

## Conclusion

Mailchimp alternatives like ActiveCampaign for power users, Brevo for budgets, or Klaviyo for shops offer better value in 2024. Start with a free trial matching your needs—most make switching seamless. You'll likely see improved engagement and lower costs. Evaluate two or three, run a test campaign, and commit. Your audience will thank you for more relevant emails.
