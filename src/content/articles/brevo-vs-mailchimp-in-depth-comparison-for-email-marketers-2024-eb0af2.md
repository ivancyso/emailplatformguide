# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming with so many features to compare. Both are popular platforms for email marketing, but they cater to slightly different needs. Brevo excels in multichannel marketing with SMS and chat, while Mailchimp is renowned for its user-friendly interface and robust automation.

This guide breaks down the key differences based on real user experiences, official pricing, and feature sets as of 2024. Whether you're a small business owner, freelancer, or growing team, we'll help you decide with practical advice.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo uses a contact-based model:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts, basic features.
- **Starter**: $25/month for 20,000 emails/month (500 contacts).
- **Business**: Starts at $65/month for 20,000 emails (10,000 contacts), adds automation and transactional emails.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high volume.

Brevo shines for transactional emails (like order confirmations) included in all paid plans without extra fees. No overage charges—emails roll over if unused.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp shifted to a tiered model:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month, limited automation.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts, 5,000 emails.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, adds advanced automation.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for larger lists and A/B testing.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts and sends, with overages applying. It's pricier for growing lists—e.g., 10,000 contacts on Standard costs ~$100/month.

**Practical Advice**: If your list is under 2,000 and you send sporadically, Brevo's free plan is more generous. For high-volume senders, Brevo saves money long-term. Calculate your needs using their pricing calculators.

## Ease of Use and Interface

### Brevo's Dashboard
Brevo's interface is modern and intuitive, with a unified inbox for emails, SMS, and WhatsApp. Drag-and-drop editor is straightforward, but the learning curve is steeper for automation workflows.

### Mailchimp's Dashboard
Mailchimp wins for beginners—its drag-and-drop builder is polished, with templates optimized for mobile. The audience dashboard provides quick insights.

**Practical Advice**: Test both free plans. Mailchimp suits non-tech users; Brevo appeals if you need SMS integration from day one.

## Core Features Head-to-Head

### Email Templates and Design
- **Brevo**: 40+ responsive templates, custom HTML support, dynamic content blocks.
- **Mailchimp**: 100+ industry-specific templates, high customization, content optimizer tool.

Mailchimp edges out for design variety.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Brevo**: Advanced automation with triggers like cart abandonment, includes SMS/WhatsApp. Multi-step journeys are visual and easy to edit.
- **Mailchimp**: Customer Journey Builder for complex automations, behavioral triggers. Single-step on lower plans.

Brevo offers more channels in automations.

### List Management and Segmentation
Both segment by behavior, location, etc. Brevo's real-time list cleaning prevents bounces; Mailchimp's tagging is more flexible.

### Landing Pages and Forms
- **Brevo**: Unlimited landing pages on paid plans, pop-ups, forms with A/B testing.
- **Mailchimp**: Basic sites on higher plans, superior form customization.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp's reports are visually rich with revenue tracking. Brevo focuses on deliverability metrics (95%+ rates reported).

**Practical Advice**: For e-commerce, use Brevo's transactional APIs. Track open rates—aim for 20-30% benchmarks. Integrate Google Analytics on both for deeper insights.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Brevo boasts 99% deliverability with dedicated IPs on Enterprise. Mailchimp's is strong (97%+) but has faced spam complaints in the past.

Both are GDPR/CCPA compliant. Brevo's double opt-in is stricter.

**Practical Advice**: Warm up domains gradually (start with 50/day). Use authentication (DKIM/SPF) setup guides provided by both.

## Integrations and Scalability

- **Brevo**: 300+ integrations (Shopify, WordPress, Zapier), strong API.
- **Mailchimp**: 250+ (similar ecosystem), excels with CRMs like HubSpot.

Brevo scales better for transactional volume; Mailchimp for creative agencies.

## Customer Support

Brevo: 24/7 chat/email on paid plans, knowledge base.
Mailchimp: Email/ticket only (priority on Premium), extensive help center.

**Practical Advice**: Join communities like Reddit's r/Emailmarketing for peer tips.

## Pros and Cons Summary

### Brevo Pros
- Affordable scaling
- Multichannel (email + SMS)
- Transactional emails included

### Brevo Cons
- Fewer templates
- Automation setup less visual

### Mailchimp Pros
- Beginner-friendly
- Stunning designs
- Robust analytics

### Mailchimp Cons
- Expensive for large lists
- Limited free automation

## Who Should Choose What?

- **Pick Brevo** if: You're budget-conscious, need SMS, or send transactional emails. Ideal for e-commerce/SaaS.
- **Pick Mailchimp** if: Design matters most, you're new to marketing, or need agency-level reporting.

Migrate data easily via CSV—both support it.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your priorities: Brevo for cost-effective multichannel growth, Mailchimp for polished ease. Start with free trials (Brevo: 300-day equivalent via limits; Mailchimp: forever free tier). Test campaigns with 100 subscribers first.

For most small businesses, Brevo offers better ROI in 2024 due to pricing flexibility. Monitor metrics quarterly and switch if needed—neither locks you in.
