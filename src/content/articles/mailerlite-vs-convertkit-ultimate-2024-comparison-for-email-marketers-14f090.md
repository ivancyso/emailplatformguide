# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for creators, bloggers, and small business owners. Both platforms offer robust features for building lists, sending newsletters, and automating campaigns. But which one fits your needs?

This in-depth MailerLite vs ConvertKit comparison covers pricing, features, usability, support, and real-world use cases. We'll help you decide based on practical advice tailored to your audience size and goals.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both tools have free plans, but they differ in limits and scaling.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite starts with a generous free plan: up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. No credit card required. Paid plans begin at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers (billed annually), scaling to $20 for 1,000, $50 for 2,500, and higher tiers. All plans include unlimited emails after the free tier, plus features like automation and landing pages. It's ideal for bootstrapped users—affordable even at scale.

**Practical Tip:** If you're under 1,000 subs, stick to free. Upgrade annually for 20% savings.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit (rebranded as Kit) offers a free plan up to 300 subscribers with core features like broadcasts and forms. Paid plans start at $15/month for up to 300 subs (monthly billing), $9/month annually. It jumps to $29/month for 1,000 subs. Higher tiers include advanced commerce tools. Note: Unlimited emails on all plans, but costs rise quickly past 1,000 subs.

**Practical Tip:** Creators with paid products love ConvertKit's commerce features, but budget-conscious users may outgrow it faster.

**Winner:** MailerLite for affordability, especially for growing lists under 10,000 subs.

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop vs Visual Builders

User-friendliness matters for non-techies.

### MailerLite's Intuitive Interface
MailerLite shines with a clean, drag-and-drop editor. Building emails takes minutes—no coding needed. The dashboard is straightforward: segments, campaigns, automations all in one place. New users report setup in under 10 minutes.

**Practical Advice:** Use pre-made templates for newsletters or product launches. Test mobile responsiveness before sending.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Focused Design
ConvertKit uses a visual builder with blocks for text, images, and buttons. It's simple but optimized for sequences (automated email series). The tagging system for segmentation feels more advanced, though the learning curve is steeper for beginners.

**Practical Advice:** Start with the "New Broadcast" flow. Customize subscriber tags early for better personalization.

**Winner:** MailerLite for sheer simplicity; ConvertKit for power users comfortable with tags.

## Core Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer responsive templates. MailerLite has 80+ free ones, highly customizable. ConvertKit focuses on clean, minimalist designs suited for bloggers.

### Forms, Popups, and Landing Pages
- **MailerLite:** Unlimited popups, forms, and websites/landing pages on all plans. A/B testing built-in.
- **ConvertKit:** Forms and landing pages standard; popups via integrations. Strong on inline forms for blogs.

**Practical Tip:** Embed MailerLite popups on exit-intent for 20-30% list growth. ConvertKit excels in WordPress via official plugin.

### Segmentation and Tagging
ConvertKit leads with dynamic tags and scoring. MailerLite uses simple segments based on behavior or custom fields—effective but less granular.

### Integrations
Both integrate with Stripe, Zapier, WordPress, Shopify. MailerLite adds Google Analytics; ConvertKit has native commerce for Gumroad/SendOwl.

## Automation Capabilities

Automation is where emails shine.

### MailerLite Automations
Visual workflow builder for welcome series, abandoned carts, re-engagement. Triggers include opens, clicks, purchases. Free on all plans.

**Practical Advice:** Set a 7-email welcome sequence: Day 1 intro, Day 3 value, Day 7 offer. Track with analytics.

### ConvertKit Sequences
"Sequences" are pre-built drip campaigns. Advanced with if/then branching based on tags. Perfect for courses or funnels.

**Practical Advice:** Use subscriber interviews to build sequences. Segment by engagement to boost open rates 15-20%.

**Winner:** ConvertKit for complex funnels; MailerLite for straightforward automations.

## Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite provides open/click rates, revenue tracking, geographic data. ConvertKit adds subscriber revenue per email and A/B insights.

**Practical Tip:** Aim for 20-30% opens. Use heatmaps in both to refine CTAs.

## Customer Support and Resources

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive academy, migration service (free for 1,000 subs).
ConvertKit: Email/ticket support (priority for paid), huge creator community, podcast, templates.

**Practical Advice:** Leverage MailerLite's chat for quick fixes; ConvertKit's resources for long-term strategies.

**Winner:** MailerLite for responsive help.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros:** Cheap, feature-rich free plan, websites included, easy migrations.
**Cons:** Less advanced tagging, no native video emails.

### ConvertKit
**Pros:** Creator tools, powerful sequences, commerce focus.
**Cons:** Pricier, smaller free plan, fewer templates.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?
Small businesses, e-commerce stores, agencies needing landing pages and popups. If budget < $20/month and list < 5,000, go MailerLite.

**Practical Advice:** Migrate from Mailchimp via CSV—takes 30 mins. Focus on popups for lead gen.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Bloggers, course creators, podcasters with digital products. If sequences and tagging are key, and you're okay with $29+/month.

**Practical Advice:** Interview 10 subscribers for tag ideas. Use commerce for one-click upsells.

## Final Verdict: MailerLite Edges Out for Most Users

In MailerLite vs ConvertKit, MailerLite wins for value, ease, and features at a lower cost—perfect for beginners to mid-sized lists. ConvertKit suits niche creators prioritizing automation depth.

Test both free plans. Track metrics like open rates (target 25%) and growth. Whichever boosts revenue wins.
