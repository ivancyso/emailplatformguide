# MailerLite vs Mailchimp: The Ultimate 2024 Comparison Guide

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options available, pitting MailerLite against Mailchimp—the two most popular tools for small businesses and solopreneurs—helps narrow it down. This in-depth comparison covers pricing, features, ease of use, support, integrations, and more. Whether you're a beginner or scaling up, we'll provide practical advice to decide which fits your needs.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first factor when selecting an email tool. Both offer free plans, but they differ significantly in limits and scaling costs.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite keeps it simple and affordable:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes basic automation, drag-and-drop editor, and landing pages.
- **Growing Business**: Starts at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers (unlimited emails), scaling to $20 for 1,000, and higher tiers up to $310 for 150,000+.
- **Advanced**: From $21/month, adds A/B testing and web notifications.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for large lists.

Practical tip: If you're under 1,000 subscribers, the free plan is generous with unlimited sends within the email cap. Upgrade only when you hit limits—no surprises.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's structure is tiered by features:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month. Basic templates and automation.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts (6,000 emails), up to $350+ for larger lists.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, adds behavioral targeting and custom landing pages.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced automation and multivariate testing.

Practical tip: Mailchimp charges based on contacts *and* emails sent, which can get pricey fast for high-volume senders. Always check their pricing calculator for your list size.

| Feature | MailerLite Free | Mailchimp Free |

**Verdict**: MailerLite wins for startups and small lists due to higher free limits and unlimited emails on paid plans.

## Features Face-Off: What Can They Do?

Both platforms pack robust features, but focus areas differ.

### Email Builder and Templates

- **MailerLite**: Intuitive drag-and-drop editor with 80+ responsive templates. Classic and pop-up editors for flexibility.
- **Mailchimp**: Highly visual builder with 100+ templates, creative studio for custom designs.

Practical advice: Test both free editors. MailerLite suits minimalist designs; Mailchimp excels in flashy, image-heavy campaigns.

### Automation Workflows

- **MailerLite**: Visual automation builder for welcome series, abandoned carts, and RSS campaigns. Single and multi-step triggers.
- **Mailchimp**: Advanced automations with customer journeys, A/B testing on journeys (paid plans).

Tip: For e-commerce, integrate with Shopify—both support it, but MailerLite's simpler flows reduce setup time.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

- **MailerLite**: Unlimited landing pages, signup forms, and popups on all plans. High-converting templates.
- **Mailchimp**: Landing pages and forms on paid plans; popups via third-party often needed.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Both offer list segmentation by behavior, tags, and custom fields. Analytics include open rates, clicks, bounces.
- MailerLite: Revenue tracking, heatmaps.
- Mailchimp: Detailed reports, comparative analytics.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly?

MailerLite's clean, no-frills interface shines for quick setup—ideal for non-tech users. Onboarding wizards guide you step-by-step.

Mailchimp's dashboard is feature-rich but can overwhelm beginners with menus. It's more intuitive once familiar.

**Practical Advice**: Start with MailerLite if you're new; switch to Mailchimp if you need creative tools. Import lists easily from CSV in both.

## Customer Support and Community

- **MailerLite**: 24/7 live chat, email support, extensive knowledge base, and Facebook group. Fast responses (under 1 hour).
- **Mailchimp**: Email/ticket support (paid plans get priority), huge help center, forums.

Tip: Free users get community help. Paid MailerLite users rave about chat speed—crucial during launches.

## Integrations and Deliverability

Both integrate with 1,000+ apps via Zapier, native plugins for WordPress, Shopify, etc.

Deliverability: Both maintain strong sender reputations (MailerLite ~99%, Mailchimp ~98%). Use double opt-ins and clean lists for best results.

Practical tip: Monitor spam complaints. MailerLite's dedicated IPs (enterprise) boost large senders.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite vs Mailchimp?

- **Pick MailerLite** if: Solopreneur, blogger, small e-com (under 10k subs). Budget-conscious, value simplicity.
  *Example*: Newsletter sender—free plan handles 12k emails easily.

- **Pick Mailchimp** if: Creative agency, need advanced automations, established brand. Willing to pay for polish.
  *Example*: Marketing team running A/B tests on journeys.

Migration tip: Both allow easy exports/imports. Test with a small list first.

MailerLite edges out for most small businesses with superior pricing and simplicity, while Mailchimp suits feature-heavy users despite higher costs.
