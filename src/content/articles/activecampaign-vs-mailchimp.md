ActiveCampaign and Mailchimp are two of the most popular email marketing platforms, but they target different user profiles. Mailchimp prioritizes ease of use and design, while ActiveCampaign focuses on automation and CRM capabilities.

Here's how they compare across every dimension that matters.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | ActiveCampaign | Mailchimp |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| **Best for** | Automation-focused businesses | Small businesses, beginners |
| **Free plan** | No (14-day trial) | Yes (500 contacts) |
| **Automation** | Best-in-class | Good |
| **CRM** | Built-in | Basic |
| **Templates** | 250+ | 100+ |
| **Starting price** | $29/mo | $13/mo |
| **Learning curve** | Steeper | Easier |

## Automation: ActiveCampaign Wins

This is where ActiveCampaign pulls ahead decisively. Its automation builder is the most powerful in the email marketing space:

**ActiveCampaign automation features:**
- Visual workflow builder with unlimited branching
- If/else conditions based on any contact data
- Goal-based automation (waits until a condition is met)
- Split actions for A/B testing automations
- Site tracking triggers (what pages contacts visit)
- Lead scoring to identify hot prospects
- CRM deal automation (sales pipeline triggers)

**Mailchimp automation features:**
- Customer Journey Builder (visual)
- Pre-built journey templates
- Basic conditional logic
- Time-based triggers
- Purchase behavior triggers

Mailchimp's automation works well for standard scenarios (welcome series, cart abandonment). But if you need complex, multi-path automations with conditional logic, ActiveCampaign is in a different league.

**Winner:** ActiveCampaign.

## Ease of Use: Mailchimp Wins

Mailchimp is designed for people who don't want to think about email marketing more than they have to. The interface is friendly, the onboarding is smooth, and you can send your first campaign within minutes.

ActiveCampaign is more powerful but requires more time to learn. The interface has more depth, which means more options — but also more decisions to make.

**Winner:** Mailchimp.

## Email Design: Mailchimp Wins

Mailchimp's email editor is one of the best:
- 100+ professionally designed templates
- Flexible drag-and-drop builder
- Brand kit for consistent styling
- Content Optimizer suggests improvements

ActiveCampaign has 250+ templates and a capable editor, but the design experience isn't quite as polished. Mailchimp still sets the standard for email design tools.

**Winner:** Mailchimp (slightly).

## CRM and Sales: ActiveCampaign Wins

ActiveCampaign includes a built-in CRM with deal pipelines, contact scoring, and sales automation. This means your sales and marketing teams can work from one platform.

Mailchimp has basic contact profiles but no real CRM functionality. You'd need to integrate with a separate CRM like HubSpot or Pipedrive.

**Winner:** ActiveCampaign.

## Pricing

| Contacts | ActiveCampaign (Starter) | Mailchimp (Standard) |
|----------|------------------------|---------------------|
| 500 | $29/mo | $13/mo |
| 1,000 | $29/mo | $26/mo |
| 2,500 | $49/mo | $46/mo |
| 5,000 | $79/mo | $75/mo |
| 10,000 | $139/mo | $110/mo |

ActiveCampaign is pricier at lower tiers but competitive at higher subscriber counts. The built-in CRM means you may save money by not needing a separate CRM subscription.

**Winner:** Mailchimp on raw pricing. Tie when you factor in CRM value.

## Integrations

Both platforms integrate with hundreds of apps:
- **ActiveCampaign:** 900+ integrations, strong Salesforce and CRM connections
- **Mailchimp:** 300+ integrations, strong ecommerce connections (Shopify, WooCommerce)

**Winner:** ActiveCampaign has more integrations overall, but Mailchimp has deeper ecommerce integrations.

## Who Should Choose ActiveCampaign?

- Businesses that rely on marketing automation
- B2B companies with sales pipelines
- Teams that want CRM + email in one platform
- Growing businesses that will use advanced features
- Anyone who's outgrown Mailchimp's automation

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Small businesses and beginners
- Ecommerce stores (strong platform integrations)
- Users who prioritize email design
- Budget-conscious users (cheaper entry point — or consider [MailerLite](/mailerlite-review) for even lower pricing)
- Anyone who wants a simple, familiar interface

## The Verdict

**Mailchimp** is the better starting point for small businesses and beginners. It's easier to learn, more affordable at small scales, and has excellent design tools.

**ActiveCampaign** is the better investment for growing businesses that need serious automation and CRM capabilities. Yes, it costs more and takes longer to learn, but the ROI from better automation typically outweighs the price difference.
