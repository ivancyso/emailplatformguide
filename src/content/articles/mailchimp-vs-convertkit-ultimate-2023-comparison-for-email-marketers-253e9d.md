# Mailchimp vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing between Mailchimp and ConvertKit can feel overwhelming, especially if you're a small business owner, blogger, or content creator building your email list. Both are popular email marketing tools, but they cater to slightly different audiences. Mailchimp is an all-in-one powerhouse great for beginners and e-commerce, while ConvertKit (now rebranded as Kit in some contexts) shines for creators with its focus on automations and subscriber tagging.

In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and support. We'll provide practical advice to help you decide based on your list size, goals, and budget. Whether you're migrating from one to the other or starting fresh, this guide has you covered. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: Mailchimp vs ConvertKit

Pricing is often the first make-or-break factor. Both platforms use tiered plans based on subscriber count, but their structures differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp offers a generous free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. This is ideal for testing waters without commitment. Paid plans start at:
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts (basic automation, templates).
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts (advanced automation, A/B testing).
- **Premium**: $350/month for 10,000+ contacts (priority support, multivariate testing).

Practical tip: If your list is under 2,000 and you're on a tight budget, Mailchimp's free tier lets you send basic campaigns indefinitely. Watch for send limits, though—exceeding them requires upgrading.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit skips a robust free plan but offers a 14-day trial. Plans are:
- **Creator**: $15/month for up to 300 subscribers (forms, landing pages, basic automations).
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month for 300-1,000 subscribers (advanced tagging, segments).
- Higher tiers scale up to $79/month for 10,000 subscribers.

ConvertKit emphasizes unlimited emails on all plans, making it cost-effective for frequent senders. Practical advice: For solopreneurs with growing lists (300-3,000), ConvertKit's flat-rate sending saves money long-term compared to Mailchimp's per-send fees on free plans.

**Winner?** Mailchimp for bootstrappers; ConvertKit for serious creators planning high-volume sends.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities Head-to-Head

Both tools handle email campaigns, but their strengths vary.

### Email Templates and Design

#### Mailchimp
Drag-and-drop builder with 100+ responsive templates. Excellent for non-designers—add images, buttons, and product blocks easily. E-commerce integrations shine here, pulling in Shopify data automatically.

Practical advice: Use Mailchimp's content optimizer to score your emails for engagement before sending. Great for promotional blasts.

#### ConvertKit
Simpler visual editor focused on clean, mobile-first designs. Fewer templates (around 50), but they're optimized for newsletters. Includes commerce tools for selling digital products directly.

Practical tip: ConvertKit's one-click upsells in emails are perfect for course creators—set up a sequence where subscribers buy on autopilot.

### Automation and Segmentation

#### Mailchimp
Robust automations like welcome series, abandoned cart, and RSS-to-email. Segmentation by tags, behavior, or purchases. Customer Journey Builder visualizes complex flows.

Advice: For e-commerce stores, set up behavioral triggers (e.g., "viewed product but didn't buy") to recover 10-20% more sales.

#### ConvertKit
Superior tagging system—tag subscribers based on actions (e.g., "clicked link A"). Automations are rule-based and powerful for nurturing leads. Visual automation builder rivals ActiveCampaign.

Practical advice: Use ConvertKit's subscriber scores to prioritize hot leads. If you're a podcaster, automate episode deliveries based on interests.

### Landing Pages and Forms
Mailchimp: Basic pop-ups, forms, and landing pages included in higher plans.
ConvertKit: Standout feature—unlimited landing pages and opt-in forms on all plans, with A/B testing.

**Pro Tip:** ConvertKit users convert 20-30% better on forms due to incentive tagging (e.g., free lead magnet delivery).

## Ease of Use and User Interface

Mailchimp's dashboard is colorful and intuitive, like a marketing hub with ads, social, and postcards. Onboarding tutorials guide beginners step-by-step.

ConvertKit keeps it minimalist—focus on emails and growth tools. Steeper for absolute newbies but rewarding for power users.

**Practical Advice:** Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing. Spend a weekend importing contacts and building your first automation. For ConvertKit, import via CSV and map tags immediately to segment effectively.

Switching? Both support easy exports. Mailchimp to ConvertKit: Export segments as CSV. Reverse: Use Zapier for seamless migration.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

Mailchimp: 300+ native integrations (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics). Huge app marketplace.

ConvertKit: Strong with creator tools (Gumroad, Teachable, podcast hosts). Zapier covers gaps.

Advice: E-commerce? Mailchimp + WooCommerce. Blogging? ConvertKit + Ghost or Substack.

## Customer Support and Resources

Mailchimp: Email/ticket support; chat on paid plans. Extensive help center, DNC emails, and webinars.

ConvertKit: Priority email support; live chat for Pro. Creator-focused academy with templates.

**Tip:** Join communities—Mailchimp's Facebook group vs ConvertKit's Slack for peer advice.

## Pros and Cons Summary

### Mailchimp Pros
- Free plan
- Beginner-friendly
- E-commerce focus

Cons: Complex pricing at scale; automation limits on cheap plans.

### ConvertKit Pros
- Unlimited sends
- Creator automations
- Clean tagging

Cons: No free plan; less visual flair.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
Small businesses, e-commerce shops, agencies needing all-in-one. If under 5,000 subs and promotional emails dominate, it's unbeatable.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?
Bloggers, podcasters, course creators. If nurturing relationships via sequences is key, and your list is 300+, go ConvertKit.

## Conclusion: Make the Right Choice for Your Email Strategy

Mailchimp vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage and style. Beginners and sellers: Mailchimp's free entry and templates win. Creators scaling automations: ConvertKit's depth pays off.

Test both—Mailchimp free, ConvertKit trial. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%), clicks (3-5%), and conversions. Whichever boosts revenue without hassle is your winner.
