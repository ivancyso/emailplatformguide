# Best Email Marketing Software: Top 10 Picks for Businesses in 2023

Choosing the right email marketing software is crucial for businesses looking to nurture leads, boost sales, and engage customers effectively. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to pick the best email marketing software that fits your budget, audience size, and goals. In this roundup, we'll explore the top 10 platforms based on real user feedback, core features, and practical considerations. We'll focus on deliverability, automation, ease of use, and scalability—key factors for anyone selecting an email marketing platform.

Whether you're a solopreneur starting with a free plan or a growing team needing advanced automations, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. All recommendations are drawn from established industry standards and widely reported features as of 2023.

## Why Email Marketing Software Matters

Email remains one of the highest-ROI channels, with average returns of $36 for every $1 spent. The best email marketing software streamlines list management, campaign creation, segmentation, and analytics. Look for tools with high deliverability rates (above 95%), mobile-responsive templates, and compliance with laws like GDPR and CAN-SPAM.

**Practical Tip:** Before signing up, audit your needs: subscriber count, sending frequency, e-commerce integration, and team collaboration features.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Platforms

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp is a go-to for beginners due to its intuitive drag-and-drop editor and generous free plan (up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month).

#### Key Features
- Automation workflows for welcome series and abandoned carts.
- Built-in audience insights and A/B testing.
- Integrations with Shopify, WordPress, and Google Analytics.

#### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** User-friendly, extensive templates (hundreds free), strong reporting.
**Cons:** Advanced automations locked behind higher tiers; pricing jumps after 2,000 subscribers.

**Pricing:** Free tier; paid starts at $13/month (Essentials).

**Best For:** Small businesses and freelancers testing email marketing.

### 2. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign excels in advanced automation and CRM integration, making it ideal for sales-driven teams.

#### Key Features
- Deep segmentation based on behavior and tags.
- Machine learning for split testing and predictive sending.
- Site tracking and lead scoring.

#### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Powerful automations (e.g., if/then branches), excellent support.
**Cons:** Steeper learning curve; no free plan.

**Pricing:** Starts at $29/month (Lite plan).

**Best For:** Mid-sized businesses focused on personalized nurturing.

**Practical Advice:** Use its automation builder to map customer journeys—start simple with a 3-email drip sequence.

### 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Tailored for creators, ConvertKit emphasizes creator economy tools like digital product sales.

#### Key Features
- Visual automations and subscriber tagging.
- Native landing pages and forms.
- One-click upsells.

#### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Creator-friendly pricing, unlimited emails on free plan (up to 300 subscribers).
**Cons:** Limited reporting compared to enterprise tools.

**Pricing:** Free for basics; $29/month (Creator).

**Best For:** Bloggers, podcasters, and course creators.

### 4. Constant Contact

A reliable choice for non-profits and local businesses with robust event management.

#### Key Features
- AI-powered subject line generator.
- Social media ads integration.
- Mobile app for on-the-go management.

#### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** 60-day free trial, excellent customer support (phone/chat).
**Cons:** Higher pricing for large lists.

**Pricing:** $12/month (Lite).

**Best For:** Event organizers and SMBs needing simplicity.

### 5. GetResponse

GetResponse stands out with built-in webinar hosting and e-commerce tools.

#### Key Features
- Conversion funnels and abandoned cart recovery.
- Autoresponders and webinars (up to 100 attendees free).

#### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** All-in-one (email + landing pages + funnels).
**Cons:** Interface feels dated.

**Pricing:** Free for 500 contacts; $19/month (Starter).

**Best For:** E-commerce stores running webinars.

**Practical Tip:** Leverage funnels for lead gen—test with a free webinar signup sequence.

### 6. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

Cost-effective with transactional email strengths.

#### Key Features
- Unlimited contacts on free plan (300 emails/day).
- SMS and chat integration.
- Custom API for developers.

#### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Affordable scaling, high deliverability.
**Cons:** Automation limits on lower plans.

**Pricing:** Free; $25/month (Starter).

**Best For:** Budget-conscious teams with transactional needs.

### 7. Klaviyo

E-commerce powerhouse, especially for Shopify users.

#### Key Features
- Revenue tracking per campaign.
- Dynamic product recommendations.
- Advanced segments (e.g., purchased X but not Y).

#### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Deep analytics, Shopify native.
**Cons:** Expensive for non-ecom; geared toward high-volume senders.

**Pricing:** Free up to 250 contacts; scales with sends.

**Best For:** Online stores optimizing revenue.

### 8. AWeber

Veteran platform with strong deliverability.

#### Key Features
- Tag-based automation.
- RSS-to-email campaigns.

#### Pros and Cons
**Pros:** Reliable, good for newsletters.
**Cons:** Basic design tools.

**Pricing:** $19/month (Lite).

**Best For:** Long-term newsletter publishers.

### 9. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free CRM-integrated option.

#### Key Features
- Personalization tokens.
- Smart content based on CRM data.

**Best For:** Teams already using HubSpot.

### 10. Drip

Behavioral automation for e-com.

#### Key Features
- Workflow timelines.
- Customer lifetime value tracking.

**Best For:** Advanced DTC brands.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing Software

### Key Factors to Evaluate
1. **Pricing Model:** Per-subscriber (e.g., Mailchimp) vs. per-send (e.g., Brevo). Calculate based on your list growth.
2. **Deliverability:** Check tools like Mail-Tester scores; prioritize established ESPs.
3. **Integrations:** Must-have for your stack (CRM, e-com, analytics).
4. **Ease of Use:** Test free trials—focus on template editors and mobile previews.
5. **Support:** Live chat vs. docs only.

**Practical Advice:** Start with a free trial. Import a test list, build a sample campaign, and run an automation. Monitor open rates (aim >20%) and spam complaints (<0.1%). Segment lists early to improve engagement.

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Overlooking mobile optimization (50%+ opens on mobile).
- Ignoring list hygiene—clean bounces quarterly.
- Skipping A/B tests on subjects and send times.
