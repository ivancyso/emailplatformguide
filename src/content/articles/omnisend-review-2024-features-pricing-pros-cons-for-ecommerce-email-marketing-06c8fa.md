# Omnisend Review 2024: The Ultimate Guide for Ecommerce Marketers

If you're an ecommerce store owner scouting for a reliable email marketing platform, Omnisend often tops the list. This Omnisend review dives deep into its capabilities, helping you decide if it's the right fit for your business. Specializing in omnichannel marketing, Omnisend combines email, SMS, and push notifications seamlessly, making it a powerhouse for online stores.

With integrations for platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce, it's designed to boost customer engagement and sales. We'll cover features, pricing, real-world pros and cons, and practical tips to maximize its potential. Let's get started.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an all-in-one marketing automation platform tailored for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it focuses on helping businesses nurture leads, recover abandoned carts, and drive repeat purchases through targeted campaigns.

Unlike general-purpose tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend emphasizes ecommerce-specific workflows. It pulls data from your store to create hyper-personalized messages, such as welcome series or post-purchase upsells. Over 70,000 brands use it, including Gymshark and Lacoste, proving its scalability for small shops to enterprises.

Key differentiator: Omnichannel support. You can send emails, SMS, web push notifications, and popups from one dashboard, ensuring consistent messaging across channels.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs robust features that go beyond basic email sending. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Automation Workflows

Automation is Omnisend's strongest suit. Pre-built workflows include:
- **Welcome series**: Automate onboarding emails to convert new subscribers.
- **Abandoned cart recovery**: Triggers emails/SMS when shoppers leave items behind, with dynamic product blocks showing exact items.
- **Back-in-stock alerts**: Notifies customers when products restock.
- **Win-back campaigns**: Re-engages inactive subscribers.

Practical advice: Customize triggers based on behavior. For example, set a 1-hour delay for first cart abandonment reminder, then SMS at 24 hours. This combo recovers up to 15-20% of lost sales, per industry benchmarks.

### List Segmentation and Personalization

Segment contacts by purchase history, behavior, or custom attributes. Use tags, RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis, or predictive segmentation powered by AI.

Tip: Build segments like "VIP customers" (high spenders) for exclusive discounts. Personalize with merge tags for first name, last order value, or recommended products via product recommendations engine.

### SMS and Push Notifications

SMS open rates hover at 98%, making it ideal for time-sensitive promos. Omnisend handles compliance (TCPA) with opt-in tools.

Push notifications work for app users or browsers. Advice: Use for flash sales—"50% off ends in 2 hours!"—but limit to 2-3/week to avoid fatigue.

### Popups and Forms

Drag-and-drop builders for exit-intent popups, spin wheels, or banners. Collect emails with incentives like 10% off coupons.

Pro tip: A/B test popup timing and copy. Place on high-traffic pages like product listings, aiming for 5-10% conversion rates.

### Reporting and Analytics

Track revenue per recipient, ROI, and campaign performance. Heatmaps show engagement hotspots.

Integrate with Google Analytics for full-funnel insights.

## Omnisend Pricing Plans

Omnisend offers transparent, contact-based pricing—no hidden fees.

- **Free Plan**: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month. Great for testing.
- **Standard ($16/mo)**: Unlimited emails, basic automation, SMS add-on ($0.02/message).
- **Pro ($59/mo)**: Advanced segmentation, A/B testing, priority support.
- **Custom (Enterprise)**: For 150k+ contacts, dedicated manager.

All plans include a 14-day trial. SMS/push billed separately. Compared to Klaviyo ($20+ for basics), it's cost-effective for growing stores.

Practical advice: Start with Free to import contacts and test automations. Upgrade when hitting limits—scales well without data migration hassles.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-focused: Deep Shopify/Woo integrations sync orders real-time.
- User-friendly interface: No coding needed; templates are mobile-responsive.
- High deliverability: 99%+ rates via dedicated IPs on higher plans.
- Excellent support: 24/7 chat, knowledge base, and free migration service.
- ROI-focused: Built-in revenue tracking ties campaigns to sales.

### Cons
- Limited non-ecommerce use: Not ideal for B2B or service businesses.
- SMS costs add up: $0.015-$0.02 per message; budget accordingly.
- Learning curve for advanced automations: Takes time to master branching logic.
- No native landing pages: Rely on integrations like Unbounce.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Perfect for DTC ecommerce brands with 500+ contacts. If you sell physical/digital products via Shopify or similar, it's a top choice. Avoid if you're a blogger or non-store business—opt for ConvertKit instead.

Audience fit: Small to mid-sized stores wanting automation without complexity. Scales to $1M+ revenue brands.

## Practical Tips to Get Started with Omnisend

1. **Setup in 30 Minutes**: Connect your store, import subscribers, activate cart recovery.
2. **Optimize Lists**: Clean bounces weekly; use double opt-in for quality.
3. **Test Campaigns**: Send to small segments first; monitor open/click rates (aim 20-30%).
4. **Compliance Check**: Add unsubscribe links; honor preferences.
5. **A/B Testing**: Rotate subject lines like "Your Cart Misses You" vs. "Forgot Something?"
6. **Integrate Tools**: Link with Google Tag Manager for tracking, Zapier for custom flows.

Real example: A fashion store used Omnisend's cart abandonment flow, recovering 12% of carts in month 1, adding $5K revenue.
