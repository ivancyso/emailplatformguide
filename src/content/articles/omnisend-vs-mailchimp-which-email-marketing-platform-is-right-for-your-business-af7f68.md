# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: The Ultimate Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With thousands of subscribers relying on tools like Omnisend and Mailchimp, it's crucial to understand their differences. This comparison dives deep into Omnisend vs Mailchimp, focusing on pricing, features, usability, and more. Whether you're running an online store or a service-based business, we'll help you decide.

## Overview of Omnisend and Mailchimp

**Mailchimp** has been a staple since 2001, known for its beginner-friendly interface and robust free plan. It serves over 12 million users worldwide, excelling in email campaigns, basic automations, and audience segmentation.

**Omnisend**, launched in 2014, targets ecommerce brands with omnichannel marketing (email, SMS, push notifications). It's popular among Shopify and WooCommerce users for its pre-built workflows like abandoned cart recovery.

Both platforms integrate with popular CRMs and ecom tools, but Omnisend shines in sales-driven automations while Mailchimp offers broader marketing tools.

## Pricing Breakdown: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a key factor when scaling your list. Here's a practical comparison based on current plans (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates).

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 monthly sends, basic features.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/mo for 500 contacts (up to $75 for 50k), includes A/B testing and basic automation.
- **Standard**: From $20/mo for 500 contacts, adds behavioral targeting and custom events.
- **Premium**: $350+/mo for advanced reporting and automation.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts and sends overages. It's cost-effective for small lists but scales expensively.

### Omnisend Pricing
- **Free**: 250 contacts, 500 emails/mo, full access to features like popups.
- **Standard**: $16/mo for 500 contacts (scales to $59 for 10k), unlimited emails, SMS add-on.
- **Pro**: $59/mo for 500 contacts (up to $1,022 for 150k), unlimited SMS, advanced segmentation.

Omnisend bills by contacts only, with fair usage for sends. SMS starts at $0.016/message. For ecommerce stores sending high volumes, Omnisend often proves cheaper long-term.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your costs using their pricing calculators. If under 500 contacts, start with Mailchimp's free tier. For 1k+ ecommerce contacts, Omnisend's unlimited sends save money.

## Features Face-Off: Key Capabilities Compared

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp's is intuitive with 100+ templates, mobile-optimized. Omnisend provides ecommerce-specific templates (product blocks, dynamic content) and a content AI assistant for faster creation.

**Winner**: Omnisend for product-focused emails.

### Automation Workflows
Mailchimp supports single, classic, and customer journey automations (Standard+). Triggers include tags and purchases.

Omnisend excels with 250+ pre-built workflows: cart abandonment (recovers 13-30% revenue on average), win-back, cross-sell. Multi-channel (email + SMS) boosts open rates by 30%.

**Practical Tip**: Test Omnisend's abandoned cart flow if you have an online store—set it up in 10 minutes via Shopify integration.

### SMS and Omnichannel
Mailchimp added SMS in 2023 (Premium only, extra cost). Omnisend includes SMS from Standard, web push, and popups natively.

### Integrations
Mailchimp: 300+ (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).
Omnisend: 160+ focused on ecommerce (Shopify, BigCommerce, Klaviyo alternatives).

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp: Revenue tracking, cohort analysis (Premium).
Omnisend: Built-in ROI calculator, customer lifetime value (CLV) insights from day one.

**Pro Tip**: Use Omnisend's dashboard to track SMS ROI—combine with UTM tags for Google Analytics accuracy.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is famously simple, ideal for non-marketers. Onboarding wizards guide list imports and first campaigns.

Omnisend has a steeper learning curve for beginners but ecommerce wizards (e.g., Shopify sync) make setup effortless. Mobile apps for both are solid.

**Advice for New Users**: Mailchimp if you're solo; Omnisend if you have a store—its templates reduce design time by 50%.

## Customer Support and Resources

Mailchimp: 24/7 email/chat (paid plans), extensive academy, forums.
Omnisend: Priority chat/email (Pro), live chat (all), dedicated onboarding calls, 100+ tutorials.

Both score high (4.5+ on G2/Capterra). Omnisend edges out for ecommerce-specific help.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Mailchimp if**:
- You're a blogger, agency, or small service business.
- Need free entry and simple newsletters.
- Prioritize audience growth tools like landing pages.

**Pick Omnisend if**:
- Running Shopify/WooCommerce store.
- Focus on revenue recovery (abandoned carts average $18/order).
- Want SMS/email synergy (open rates 98% vs 20%).

**Migration Advice**: Export CSV from one, import to the other. Tag subscribers during switch to maintain segments. Test campaigns on 10% list first.

## Final Verdict: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Neither is 'best' universally—Mailchimp wins for beginners and general use; Omnisend dominates ecommerce with superior automations and value.

For most growing online stores, **Omnisend** is the smarter choice in 2024, potentially boosting revenue 20-50% via workflows. Start with their free trial to import your Mailchimp list and see results.
