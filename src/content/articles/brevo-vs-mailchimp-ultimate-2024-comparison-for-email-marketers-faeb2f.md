# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool is crucial for businesses looking to nurture leads, boost engagement, and drive sales. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp are two popular platforms, each with strengths that appeal to different users. Brevo excels in transactional emails, SMS, and CRM integration, while Mailchimp is renowned for its user-friendly interface and creative templates.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, deliverability, support, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business, or scaling enterprise, this guide provides practical advice to make an informed decision. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first factor when selecting an email tool. Both offer free plans, but they differ significantly in limits and scaling.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Ideal for testing but restrictive for growth.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/month for 5,000 contacts (includes basic automation).
- **Standard**: $20/month for 5,000 contacts (advanced automation, A/B testing).
- **Premium**: $350/month for 10,000+ contacts (multivariate testing, optimization).

Mailchimp uses contact-based pricing, which can get expensive as your list grows. Overages add costs quickly.

### Brevo Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails per day (no monthly cap). Great for low-volume senders.
- **Starter**: €25/month (~$27) for 20,000 emails/month.
- **Business**: €65/month for 20,000 emails + advanced features like A/B testing.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high volume.

Brevo's email-volume-based pricing is more cost-effective for growing lists. No contact limits mean you pay for usage, not subscribers.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your monthly sends. If under 10k emails with many contacts, Brevo saves money. For 5k+ contacts with low volume, Mailchimp's free tier might suffice initially. Use both free plans to test before committing.

## Ease of Use and Interface

### Mailchimp: Beginner-Friendly
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with a vibrant interface and pre-built templates. Setup takes minutes—import contacts, design, and send. It's perfect for non-tech users but can feel cluttered for power users.

### Brevo: Streamlined for Efficiency
Brevo's interface is clean and modular, with a focus on workflows. The editor is capable but less visually oriented than Mailchimp's. It's faster for automation setups.

**Practical Advice**: Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing. Switch to Brevo if you need quick scaling without a steep learning curve. Both have mobile apps for on-the-go management.

## Key Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
- **Mailchimp**: 100+ responsive templates, high customization. Excellent for visually stunning campaigns.
- **Brevo**: 40+ templates, block-based editor. Solid but fewer options; strong for transactional emails.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Classic automations (welcome series, abandoned cart). Standard plan unlocks behavioral triggers.
- **Brevo**: Advanced automation with triggers, waits, and conditions. Free plan includes basics; excels in multi-channel (email + SMS).

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both segment by behavior, tags, and custom fields. Brevo edges out with real-time list syncing and CRM data.

### Additional Tools
| Feature | Mailchimp | Brevo |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| SMS Marketing | Premium only ($350+/mo) | Included in most plans |
| Landing Pages | Yes, all plans | Yes, drag-and-drop |
| Forms & Popups | Yes | Yes, with A/B testing |
| CRM | Basic (add-on) | Built-in, unlimited users |
| E-commerce | Integrates with Shopify | Native cart abandonment |

**Practical Advice**: For e-commerce, Brevo's SMS and cart recovery shine. Use Mailchimp for content-heavy newsletters. Test automations: Set up a welcome series in both to compare setup time (Brevo ~10 mins, Mailchimp ~15 mins).

## Deliverability and Analytics

Deliverability rates are high for both (~95%+), but Mailchimp's established IP reputation helps new users. Brevo performs well in Europe and offers dedicated IPs on higher plans.

Analytics: Mailchimp provides opens, clicks, revenue tracking. Brevo adds heatmaps, bounce details, and revenue per email.

**Practical Advice**: Warm up your IP gradually (start with 100 sends/day). Monitor spam complaints. Use Brevo's deliverability tools for international lists.

## Customer Support and Integrations

- **Support**: Mailchimp offers email/chat (paid plans); priority for Premium. Brevo provides 24/7 chat/email, phone for Business+.
- **Integrations**: Both connect to 100+ apps (Zapier, Shopify, WordPress). Brevo has native WhatsApp and Facebook leads.

**Practical Advice**: If support matters, Brevo's always-on chat wins. For integrations, check Zapier limits—Brevo's free plan has more tasks.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros
- Polished templates and editor
- Strong community resources
- Excellent for creative campaigns

Cons:
- Expensive at scale
- Limited free sends
- SMS is costly

### Brevo Pros
- Affordable scaling
- Multi-channel (SMS, chat)
- Unlimited contacts free

Cons:
- Fewer templates
- Less brand recognition

## Who Should Choose Brevo vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp** if: You're a beginner, prioritize design, or run U.S.-focused newsletters under 5k contacts.
- **Choose Brevo** if: You need SMS/automation on a budget, have growing lists, or want CRM integration.

**Migration Tips**: Export CSV from one, import to the other. Rebuild automations first. Test sends to a small segment.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp comes down to your needs: Mailchimp for ease and creativity, Brevo for value and multichannel power. For most growing businesses, Brevo offers better long-term ROI with its pricing and features.
