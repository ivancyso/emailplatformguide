# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose in 2024?

Choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit can be tough when you're building your email list and scaling your business. Both are popular for creators, bloggers, and small businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. MailerLite shines with affordability and simplicity, while ConvertKit focuses on advanced creator tools like tagging and sequences.

In this 2024 comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur on a budget or a podcaster needing commerce features, you'll find practical advice to decide. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Breakdown: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms scale with subscribers, but their structures differ.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite offers a generous free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—perfect for testing without commitment. Paid plans start at $10/month for the Growing Business plan (up to 500 subs, unlimited emails). Advanced features like A/B testing and web push notifications unlock at $20/month (Advanced plan).

No contracts, monthly billing, and they occasionally discount annual plans by 2 months free. Practical tip: Start free, upgrade as your list grows. It's ideal if you're bootstrapping.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit has no free plan but offers a 14-day trial. The Free plan (reintroduced) covers up to 300 subs with basic features. Creator plan starts at $15/month (300 subs), scaling to $29/month for 1,000 subs. Higher tiers add commerce and advanced reporting.

Annual billing saves 20%. They emphasize value for creators with one-time payments and subscriptions built-in. Tip: If you sell digital products, the commerce features justify the cost from day one.

**Winner?** MailerLite for budgets under $20/month; ConvertKit for premium creator tools.

## Feature Face-Off: Core Capabilities

Both handle emails, automations, and forms, but depth varies.

### Email Templates and Editor

MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 80+ responsive templates. Classic and blocks editors suit beginners. Embed products easily.

ConvertKit's visual builder is plain-text friendly for high deliverability, with 100+ templates optimized for creators. It auto-saves and previews across devices.

**Practical advice:** Use MailerLite for quick, pretty designs; ConvertKit for clean, mobile-first emails that convert.

### Automation and Sequences

MailerLite supports multi-step automations with triggers like sign-ups or tags. RSS-to-email and A/B testing (paid) are straightforward.

ConvertKit excels here—visual automations with sequences (drip campaigns) and tagging over lists for segmentation. Rules-based triggers make it powerful for nurturing leads.

**Tip:** Map your customer journey first. ConvertKit suits complex funnels; MailerLite for simple workflows like welcome series.

### Segmentation and Tagging

MailerLite uses groups, segments, and tags. Build dynamic segments by behavior or custom fields.

ConvertKit's tag system is its superpower—subscribe to multiple tags, automate based on actions. No lists needed, reducing complexity.

**Pro tip:** If you have overlapping audiences (e.g., blog vs podcast subs), ConvertKit's tags prevent duplicates and boost personalization.

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

MailerLite includes unlimited landing pages, popups, and forms on all plans. Drag-and-drop, with exit-intent popups and countdown timers.

ConvertKit offers landing pages and forms (pre-fill enabled), plus subscription incentives. Commerce landing pages sell directly.

**Advice:** Test popups on your site. MailerLite's variety wins for lead gen; ConvertKit for sales pages.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

MailerLite's interface is clean and beginner-friendly. Import lists quickly, with guided setup wizards. Mobile app for monitoring.

ConvertKit feels more pro, with a steeper curve for tags/sequences but excellent Creator Network resources (tutorials, courses).

**Practical steps to start:**
1. Sign up and import CSV.
2. Build a welcome automation.
3. A/B test subject lines.

MailerLite edges out for non-techies; ConvertKit rewards learning.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

MailerLite connects to 250+ apps via Zapier, WordPress, Shopify. Native integrations for Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel.

ConvertKit integrates with 100+ via Zapier, plus Gumroad, Teachable, WooCommerce. Strong for creator tools like Patreon.

**Tip:** Check Zapier limits on free plans. Both work well, but ConvertKit pairs better with digital product stacks.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

MailerLite: Email/chat support (paid plans get priority), knowledge base, Facebook group. High deliverability (SPF/DKIM setup guided).

ConvertKit: 24/7 email, chat (paid), proactive support. Excellent inbox placement, with dedicated IP on enterprise.

**Advice:** Verify domains early. Both score 90%+ deliverability; test with GlockApps.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite

**Pros:**
- Free plan
- Affordable scaling
- Easy editor/popups
- Unlimited images/emails

**Cons:**
- Fewer creator-specific tools
- Automation less advanced
- No native commerce

### ConvertKit

**Pros:**
- Powerful tagging/sequences
- Built-in selling
- Creator-focused resources
- High customization

**Cons:**
- Higher cost
- No free plan (limited)
- Steeper learning curve

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

- Budget-conscious startups
- Beginners needing quick setup
- Businesses focused on lead magnets/popups
- Agencies managing multiple clients (white-label coming)

**Migration tip:** Export from competitors via CSV; remap tags to groups.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Bloggers/podcasters with audiences
- Digital product sellers
- Complex nurturing needed
- Willing to invest in growth

**Tip:** Use their growth course to optimize post-signup.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

**MailerLite wins for affordability and simplicity**—great if you're starting or keeping costs low. **ConvertKit takes the crown for creators** needing automation depth and monetization.

Test both: MailerLite free, ConvertKit trial. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%), clicks (2-5%), and unsubscribes (<0.5%).

Whichever you pick, focus on value-driven emails: Solve problems, segment smartly, automate consistently. Your list is your asset—nurture it right.
