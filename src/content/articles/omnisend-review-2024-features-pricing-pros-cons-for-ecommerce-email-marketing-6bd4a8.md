# Omnisend Review 2024: Is It the Right Email Marketing Platform for Your Ecommerce Store?

If you're running an ecommerce business and looking to level up your customer communication, you've likely come across Omnisend. As a specialized email marketing tool tailored for online stores, Omnisend stands out by combining email, SMS, and push notifications into one platform. ## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform designed primarily for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it focuses on helping stores recover lost sales, nurture leads, and retain customers via email, SMS, and web push notifications. Unlike generalist tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend integrates deeply with ecommerce platforms such as Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, and Magento.

Key differentiator: Its pre-built ecommerce workflows, like abandoned cart recovery and post-purchase upsells, which reportedly recover up to 15% of lost sales (based on Omnisend's case studies). It's user-friendly for non-marketers, with drag-and-drop editors and AI-powered content suggestions.

Practical advice: Start by connecting your store's data. Omnisend pulls customer info, orders, and products automatically, saving hours of manual import.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs robust features into an intuitive dashboard. Here's a breakdown:

### Drag-and-Drop Email Builder

Create professional emails without coding. The builder offers 250+ responsive templates optimized for mobile ecommerce. Customize with dynamic blocks that pull product images, prices, and recommendations from your store.

**Practical tip:** Use product blocks for personalized recommendations. Segment by purchase history (e.g., "viewed but not bought") to boost open rates by 20-30%. Test subject lines with A/B splits—Omnisend makes this one-click.

### Advanced Automations

Omnisend shines here with 10+ pre-built workflows:
- **Welcome series:** Engages new subscribers with a 3-5 email sequence.
- **Abandoned cart:** Sends timed reminders with cart contents.
- **Win-back campaigns:** Re-engages inactive customers.
- **Cross-sell/upsell:** Post-purchase flows.

**Step-by-step setup for abandoned cart:**
1. Go to Automations > Create Workflow > Abandoned Cart.
2. Set triggers (e.g., cart value > $50).
3. Design emails: Email 1 at 1 hour ("Forgot something?"), Email 2 at 24 hours (discount code), SMS at 48 hours.
4. Add segments for high-value carts.
5. Launch and monitor recovery rates in analytics.

Users report 10-20% recovery rates with proper timing.

### SMS and Push Notifications

Not just email—send SMS for high-ROI messages (up to 98% open rates). Push notifications work for app-less web visitors. Combine channels in one campaign.

**Advice:** Use SMS sparingly for urgency (e.g., flash sales). Comply with TCPA by getting opt-ins via popups. Track ROI: SMS often yields $6-10 per $1 spent.

### Popups and Forms

Capture leads with customizable popups, signup forms, and wheels. Use gamification like spin-to-win for 5-10x opt-in rates.

**Pro tip:** Exit-intent popups convert 8-12% of leaving visitors. Tag new subscribers automatically for segmentation.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Hyper-segment by behavior: page views, purchases, location. RFM analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) is built-in.

Analytics dashboard shows revenue per email, unsubscribe rates, and attribution. Heatmaps reveal click hotspots.
