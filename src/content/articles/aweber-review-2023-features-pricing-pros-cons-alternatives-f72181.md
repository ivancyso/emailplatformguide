# AWeber Review 2023: Is It Still Worth It for Your Email Marketing?

Choosing the right email marketing platform can make or break your campaigns. With so many options like Mailchimp, ConvertKit, and ActiveCampaign, AWeber stands out for its longevity and reliability. Established in 1998, AWeber has powered millions of emails for small businesses, bloggers, and e-commerce stores. In this AWeber review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, usability, and whether it's the best fit for you in 2023.

We'll cover practical setup tips, real-world performance, and comparisons to help you decide. Whether you're a beginner or scaling up, this guide provides actionable advice based on AWeber's official specs and user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra.

## What is AWeber?

AWeber is a cloud-based email marketing service focused on automation, list management, and campaign delivery. It's designed for users who want straightforward tools without overwhelming complexity. Key highlights include:

- **High deliverability rates**: AWeber reports inbox placement above 99%, thanks to its sender reputation management.
- **User base**: Over 100,000 customers worldwide, from solopreneurs to enterprises.
- **Integrations**: Connects with 6,000+ apps via Zapier, WordPress plugins, Shopify, and more.

Practical advice: Start by importing your existing list via CSV. Use AWeber's list segmentation to tag subscribers by behavior (e.g., opened last email), which improves engagement from day one.

## Key Features of AWeber

AWeber packs essential tools for modern email marketing. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Automation and Workflows

AWeber's drag-and-drop builder lets you create autoresponders and drip campaigns easily. Set up a welcome series in minutes:
1. Create a new campaign.
2. Add triggers like "subscribed to list."
3. Drag in email templates and delays (e.g., send email 2 on day 3).

Advanced users love the tagging system for behavioral automation. For example, tag buyers and send upsell sequences. Tip: Test delays—AWeber data shows 2-3 day gaps boost open rates by 20%.

### Landing Pages and Sign-Up Forms

Build unlimited pop-ups, inline forms, and landing pages without coding. Customize with 150+ responsive templates. Practical tip: Use A/B testing on headlines—"Get Your Free Guide" vs. "Download Now"—to lift conversions up to 30%.

### Templates and Design Tools

Over 700 mobile-responsive templates for newsletters, promotions, and blogs. The editor is intuitive but lacks some advanced customization (no custom HTML blocks like in ActiveCampaign). Advice: Stick to templates for speed; they're optimized for deliverability.

### Analytics and Reporting

Track opens, clicks, bounces, and revenue per email. Heatmaps show click hotspots. Pro tip: Set up custom reports for ROI—link UTM tags to Google Analytics for full-funnel insights.

### Integrations and E-commerce

Seamless with WooCommerce, Etsy, and PayPal. For e-commerce, use product recommendation tags to personalize sends.

## AWeber Pricing: Transparent but Tiered

AWeber uses subscriber-based pricing with a 30-day free trial (no card required). Plans as of 2023:

| Subscribers | Monthly Price | Features |
|-------------|---------------|----------|
| Up to 500   | $19/mo       | All core features |
| 501-2,500  | $29/mo       | + Webinars |
| 2,501-5,000| $49/mo       | + More automation |
| 5,001-25,000| $69+ /mo   | Enterprise add-ons |

Annual billing saves 15-30%. No free plan post-trial, unlike Mailchimp. Practical advice: Calculate costs with their pricing calculator. For 1,000 subs, it's competitive at $29/mo vs. Mailchimp's $59.

Add-ons like premium templates ($10/mo) or dedicated IP ($50+/mo) for high-volume senders.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

AWeber's interface feels dated (think 2010s dashboard) but is beginner-friendly. Navigation: Lists > Campaigns > Automations. Onboarding includes video tutorials and live chat setup help.

User ratings: 4.5/5 on G2 for ease of use. Tip for new users: Complete the setup wizard—it auto-configures SPF/DKIM for better delivery.

Common pain points: Steeper learning for complex automations compared to Klaviyo's visual builder.

## Customer Support and Deliverability

24/7 email/ticket support, plus live chat (business hours US). Phone for higher plans. Knowledge base has 1,000+ articles.

Deliverability shines: Independent tests (e.g., Mail-Tester) score 9.5/10. Advice: Warm up your IP by starting with 50 emails/day, ramping to 1,000 over weeks.

## Pros and Cons of AWeber

### Pros
- Reliable deliverability and uptime.
- Strong automation for the price.
- Excellent for e-commerce integrations.
- Free migration service for lists from competitors.

### Cons
- Dated UI lacks modern flair.
- Pricing jumps quickly past 5K subs.
- Limited advanced segmentation (no revenue-based scoring).
- No native SMS or push notifications.
