Kit (ConvertKit) and AWeber are both established email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences with different philosophies. This comparison breaks down the key differences to help you choose.

## Quick Overview

| Feature | Kit (ConvertKit) | AWeber |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| **Founded** | 2013 | 1998 |
| **Approach** | Subscriber-centric (tags) | List-based |
| **Best for** | Creators, bloggers | Small businesses |
| **Email templates** | 9 | 700+ |
| **Automation** | Excellent | Good |
| **Free plan** | 1,000 subscribers | 500 subscribers |
| **Starting price** | $29/mo | $19/mo |

## Subscriber Management

This is the fundamental difference between the two platforms.

**Kit** uses a subscriber-centric model (read our [Kit review](/convertkit-review) for a deep dive). Each person exists once in your account, tagged by interests and behavior. You never have duplicate subscribers, and segmentation is based on tags and custom fields.

**AWeber** uses a traditional list-based model. Subscribers join specific mailing lists, and the same person can exist on multiple lists. This is familiar but can lead to duplicate charges and messy data.

**Winner:** Kit. The tag-based approach is more flexible and prevents duplicates.

## Email Design

**AWeber** dominates here with 700+ templates covering every industry and occasion (see our [AWeber review](/aweber-review) for more). The Smart Designer feature auto-creates branded templates from your website. If email design matters to you, AWeber delivers.

**Kit** offers just 9 templates and leans toward plain-text styled emails. The philosophy is that simpler emails feel more personal and get higher engagement — which is true for creators, but limiting for businesses that need branded designs.

**Winner:** AWeber, by far.

## Automation

**Kit's** visual automation builder is one of the best in the industry. You can create sophisticated workflows with conditional logic, event triggers, and multi-path sequences. For course launches, welcome funnels, and tag-based content delivery, Kit excels.

**AWeber** offers automation through campaigns and tag-triggered workflows. It's capable but not as visual or intuitive as Kit's builder. For standard email sequences, it works fine; for complex multi-path automations, Kit is better.

**Winner:** Kit.

## Deliverability

Both platforms have strong deliverability reputations, but AWeber's 25+ year track record gives it a slight edge. AWeber has long-established relationships with ISPs and actively manages sender reputation.

Kit's deliverability is also good and has improved significantly over the years. Both platforms use authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) and provide tools to maintain list hygiene.

**Winner:** Slight edge to AWeber.
