# MailerLite vs ConvertKit 2024: The Ultimate Comparison for Email Marketers

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your audience engagement and revenue streams. For creators, bloggers, and small business owners, **MailerLite vs ConvertKit** is a common debate. Both tools offer powerful features for building lists, sending newsletters, and automating workflows without needing advanced technical skills.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, user experience, and real-world use cases. Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling to thousands of subscribers, you'll get practical advice to decide which fits your needs. Data draws from official sites, user reviews on G2 and Capterra, and common benchmarks as of 2024.

## Quick Overview: MailerLite and ConvertKit

**MailerLite** is known for its simplicity and affordability. Launched in 2010, it's popular among solopreneurs and small teams for drag-and-drop editors, popups, and websites. It supports unlimited emails on paid plans and has a generous free tier.

**ConvertKit** (rebranded from ConvertKit to Kit in some contexts but still widely known as ConvertKit) targets creators like podcasters, writers, and course sellers. It excels in tagging, sequences, and commerce tools, with a focus on monetization.

Both have free plans up to 1,000 subscribers (MailerLite: 12,000 emails/month; ConvertKit: limited sends), making them accessible for testing.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is a key decider in **MailerLite vs ConvertKit**.

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month.
- **Growing Business**: $10/month for 1,000 subs (unlimited emails), scales to $20 for 2,500.
- **Advanced**: $21/month for 1,000 subs, adds A/B testing, web push.
- Annual discounts: 30% off.

Practical tip: Start free and upgrade seamlessly—no data loss. Ideal if email volume is high early on.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 1,000 subs (forms, broadcasts, landing pages).
- **Creator**: $15/month for up to 300 subs, $29 for 1,000 (unlimited sends).
- **Creator Pro**: $25/month for 300 subs, adds advanced automations, segments.
- Annual: 20% off.

ConvertKit bills by subscriber tiers, so costs rise faster for larger lists. Tip: Use the free plan for basic sequences; upgrade for selling products.

**Winner**: MailerLite for budget-conscious users; ConvertKit if you need pro commerce features.

## Ease of Use: Onboarding and Interface

Both platforms prioritize intuitive designs, but differences shine in daily workflows.

MailerLite's editor is block-based and mobile-responsive out-of-the-box. New users report setup in under 10 minutes. Embed forms easily on WordPress or custom sites.

ConvertKit uses a visual builder with tagging logic. It's creator-friendly but has a slight learning curve for complex automations. Dashboard focuses on subscriber journeys.

**Practical Advice**: If you're non-technical, MailerLite's drag-and-drop wins. Test both free trials—import a CSV list to simulate your workflow.

## Core Features Comparison

### Email Templates and Design

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, custom HTML, photo editor. Strong for newsletters and promos.

ConvertKit: Clean, minimalist templates optimized for creators. Fewer options but highly customizable via CSS.

Tip: MailerLite for visual variety; ConvertKit for branded simplicity.

### Automation and Workflows

**H3: MailerLite Automations**
Single-trigger automations (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart). Drag-and-drop builder connects actions like tags and sends.

**H3: ConvertKit Sequences and Automations**
Rule-based automations with if/then logic, tagging for segmentation. Pre-built sequences for lead magnets.

ConvertKit edges out for behavioral triggers (e.g., "if opens X, send Y"). Advice: Map your customer journey first—use ConvertKit for nurture sequences, MailerLite for simple drip campaigns.

### Forms, Popups, and Landing Pages

Both offer unlimited popups/forms/landing pages.

MailerLite: Exit-intent popups, A/B testing on free plan (advanced), embedded widgets.

ConvertKit: Inline forms, flyout subscribe, one-click upsells on landing pages.

Tip: A/B test headlines on popups—expect 20-50% opt-in boosts with personalization.

### Segmentation and Analytics

MailerLite: Groups, tags, click maps, open rates.

ConvertKit: Advanced tagging, subscriber scores, revenue tracking per sequence.

ConvertKit shines for revenue attribution. Practical: Segment by engagement (e.g., "active" vs "cold") to boost deliverability.

## Integrations and E-commerce

MailerLite: 250+ integrations (Zapier, Google Analytics, Stripe, WooCommerce). Built-in surveys.

ConvertKit: Native Gumroad/Patreon, Stripe for paid newsletters. Strong with WordPress plugins.

**Advice for Creators**: ConvertKit if selling courses/ebooks directly; MailerLite for broader e-com like Shopify.

## Customer Support and Community

MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email support, knowledge base, Facebook group.

ConvertKit: Email/ticket support (priority on Pro), extensive creator resources, podcast.

Both score 4.5+ on review sites. Tip: Join communities—MailerLite's forum for quick tips, ConvertKit's for monetization strategies.

## Conclusion: Your Next Steps

In **MailerLite vs ConvertKit**, there's no one-size-fits-all. MailerLite offers unbeatable value and simplicity for growing lists, while ConvertKit empowers creators with automation depth and sales tools. Start with free plans: sign up, send a test campaign, and track metrics like open rates (aim for 30%+).

For most small businesses, MailerLite's affordability wins. Creators prioritizing sequences? Go ConvertKit. Whichever you choose, focus on consistent value-driven emails—list growth follows.
