# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: 2024 Comparison Guide for Email Marketers

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful platforms, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a long-standing favorite for beginners and small businesses with its intuitive interface and free tier. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines for ecommerce brands with advanced omnichannel features like SMS and push notifications.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur or running an online store, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, launched in 2001, is one of the most recognized names in email marketing. It serves over 12 million users worldwide and offers a freemium model that's perfect for startups testing the waters.

**Key Strengths:**
- User-friendly drag-and-drop editor.
- Robust audience segmentation.
- Basic automation for welcome series and abandoned carts.

Practical tip: If you're just starting, use Mailchimp's free plan (up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month) to build your list without upfront costs. Focus on their pre-built templates to launch campaigns quickly.

However, as your list grows, costs can escalate, and advanced ecommerce features require higher tiers.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend is a newer player (founded 2014) designed specifically for ecommerce. It integrates seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce, emphasizing omnichannel marketing.

**Key Strengths:**n- Omnichannel campaigns (email, SMS, web push).
- Advanced automation workflows.
- Pop-up forms and list-building tools optimized for stores.

Practical advice: For Shopify users, connect Omnisend directly to sync customer data and trigger hyper-personalized flows like post-purchase upsells. Start with their free plan (up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month) to test SMS add-ons.

Omnisend's focus on revenue recovery makes it ideal for DTC brands aiming to boost repeat sales.

Pricing is a major factor when choosing an email marketing tool. Here's a side-by-side breakdown based on current plans (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates).

## Features Head-to-Head

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp offers solid automations like tag-based triggers and behavioral series, but they're linear and less visual.

Omnisend excels with a drag-and-drop workflow builder supporting splits, A/B testing, and multi-channel paths (e.g., email → SMS fallback).

**Tip:** For cart abandonment, Omnisend's pre-built flows recover 20-30% more revenue on average (based on their case studies). Test simple automations in both to see which fits your tech comfort level.

### Email Templates and Design

Both have drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp's library is vast (hundreds of themes), mobile-responsive by default.

Omnisend focuses on product-focused blocks (dynamic product feeds from your store), ideal for personalized recommendations.

**Practical Advice:** Import your logo and branding once. Use Mailchimp for content-heavy newsletters; Omnisend for shoppable emails with buy buttons.

### Integrations

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).

Omnisend has 100+ but deeper ecommerce ties (Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives, Google Ads).

**Tip:** Prioritize your stack. If you're all-in on Shopify, Omnisend's native sync saves hours vs. Mailchimp's API setup.

### Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp provides opens, clicks, revenue tracking (paid plans).

Omnisend adds SMS/push metrics, customer lifetime value (CLV), and revenue per channel.

**Advice:** Track ROI with UTM parameters in both. Omnisend's dashboard is better for attributing sales across channels.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is beginner-friendly, with tutorials everywhere.

Omnisend has a steeper curve for workflows but offers 24/7 chat/email support (Pro+ plans).

Mailchimp support is email/ticket-based (paid) or self-serve.

**Practical Tip:** Spend 30 minutes in each free trial. If you're non-technical, stick with Mailchimp. For growth-focused teams, Omnisend's support accelerates setup.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros:**
- Free plan.
- Intuitive for non-marketers.
- Large template library.

**Cons:**
- Pricing jumps with contacts.
- Limited omnichannel.
- Automation lacks depth for complex flows.

### Omnisend
**Pros:**
- Ecommerce-centric automations.
- Cost-effective SMS.
- Unlimited sends in paid plans.

**Cons:**n- Smaller free tier.
- Less general-purpose.
- Interface can overwhelm beginners.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Mailchimp if:**
- You're a blogger, service business, or small team under 1,000 contacts.
- Budget is tight initially.
- You need simple newsletters and basic segments.

**Pick Omnisend if:**
- You run an ecommerce store (Shopify/Woo).
- Want SMS/push for higher engagement.
- Focus on automation-driven revenue (abandoned carts, win-back).

**Migration Tip:** Both allow CSV imports. Test parallel campaigns for 1-2 months before switching to minimize disruption.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce marketers seeking omnichannel power and ROI-focused tools, while Mailchimp remains the go-to for beginners and generalists. Evaluate based on your audience size, channels, and goals—start with free trials to gather real data.

Ultimately, the "best" depends on your business. For most online stores, Omnisend's specialized features deliver better long-term value. Ready to choose? Sign up for trials today and track your first campaign's performance.
