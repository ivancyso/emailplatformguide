# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive sales. For solopreneurs, small businesses, and content creators deciding between MailerLite and ConvertKit, this head-to-head comparison breaks it down. We'll cover pricing, features, automation, ease of use, integrations, and more, with practical advice to help you pick the winner for your needs.

Both platforms are popular for their user-friendly interfaces and powerful automation, but they cater to slightly different audiences. MailerLite shines for beginners and budget-conscious users with its free plan and drag-and-drop simplicity. ConvertKit, on the other hand, is tailored for creators like bloggers and podcasters, emphasizing sequences and tagging for long-term audience growth.

Let's dive into the details.

## MailerLite Overview

MailerLite is an all-in-one email marketing platform known for affordability and ease. Launched in 2010, it serves over 2 million users worldwide. Key strengths include a generous free plan (up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month), intuitive drag-and-drop editor, and built-in tools like landing pages, popups, and surveys.

**Practical Advice:** If you're just starting with email marketing and have under 1,000 subscribers, MailerLite's free tier lets you test automations and campaigns without cost. Use their popup builder to grow your list organically on your website.

## ConvertKit Overview

ConvertKit (formerly ConvertKit) targets creators with tools for selling digital products, courses, and memberships. It features a tagging system for advanced segmentation, visual automations called "Sequences," and commerce integrations. There's a 14-day free trial, and a free plan for up to 10,000 subscribers (with limited features like broadcasts only, no sequences).

**Practical Advice:** ConvertKit excels for newsletters and funnels. If you run a blog or podcast, leverage their subscriber tagging to send personalized follow-ups, like recommending content based on opens.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major factor when choosing between MailerLite and ConvertKit. Here's a side-by-side as of 2024:

| Subscribers | MailerLite | ConvertKit |

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your monthly sends and subscriber growth. For 500 subs sending weekly newsletters (4,000 emails), MailerLite's $10 plan suffices. Budget over $30/mo? ConvertKit's pro features may justify it for e-commerce.

## Feature Comparison

### Automation and Workflows

Both offer automation, but styles differ:
- **MailerLite:** Visual workflow builder with triggers like sign-ups or purchases. Supports A/B testing and RSS campaigns.
- **ConvertKit:** Sequence automations for drip campaigns. Tag-based triggers allow complex paths, e.g., "if opened email X, tag as engaged and send Y."

**Practical Advice:** For simple welcome series, MailerLite's drag-and-drop is faster. Use ConvertKit for multi-step funnels, like a 7-day course delivery.

### Email Templates and Design

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, full drag-and-drop editor with blocks for images, buttons, and custom HTML.
ConvertKit: Clean, mobile-optimized templates focused on text-heavy newsletters. Less visual editing; more code-friendly.

**Practical Advice:** Designers prefer MailerLite for pixel-perfect emails. Writers stick with ConvertKit's simplicity to focus on copy.

### Segmentation and Analytics

- **MailerLite:** Groups, tags, and click/activity segmentation. Detailed reports on opens, clicks, revenue tracking.
- **ConvertKit:** Advanced tagging and scoring. Strong analytics for subscriber growth and revenue per subscriber.

**Practical Advice:** Segment lists early. In MailerLite, create groups for "VIPs" based on purchases. In ConvertKit, tag for behaviors like "podcast listener" to boost engagement rates above 30%.

### Forms, Landing Pages, and Popups

Both have these, but:
- MailerLite: High-converting popups (exit-intent, scroll-triggered), unlimited landing pages.
- ConvertKit: Inline forms, landing pages with commerce, but fewer popup options.

**Practical Advice:** Boost sign-ups 20-50% with MailerLite popups on high-traffic pages. Use ConvertKit forms for gated content like lead magnets.

### Integrations

MailerLite: 250+ including WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics.
ConvertKit: Strong with creator tools like Gumroad, Teachable, Zapier.

**Practical Advice:** Check Zapier compatibility. Sync MailerLite with WooCommerce for abandoned cart emails; ConvertKit with Patreon for member perks.

## Ease of Use

MailerLite scores higher for beginners with its no-code editor and quick setup (under 10 minutes). ConvertKit has a steeper curve due to tagging but intuitive once learned.

**Practical Advice:** Start a free MailerLite account and build a campaign today. Spend ConvertKit's trial mapping your audience tags.

## Customer Support

- MailerLite: 24/7 live chat, email, extensive help center, video tutorials.
- ConvertKit: Email support (priority for paid), Creator Network community, knowledge base.

**Practical Advice:** Need instant help? MailerLite's chat resolves issues fast. Join ConvertKit's community for peer tips.

## Who Should Choose Which?

**Pick MailerLite if:** You're a small business, e-commerce store, or beginner on a budget needing landing pages and popups. Ideal for lists under 5,000 subs.

**Pick ConvertKit if:** You're a creator building long-term relationships with sequences and selling products. Suits bloggers/podcasters with 1,000+ engaged subs.

**Practical Advice:** Audit your needs: High-volume sends? MailerLite. Personalized nurturing? ConvertKit. Test both free options.

## Conclusion

MailerLite edges out for affordability and versatility, making it ideal for most users starting or scaling email marketing. ConvertKit dominates for creators prioritizing automations and monetization.

Ultimately, the best choice depends on your goals, budget, and tech comfort. Sign up for both trials, import a test list, and run a campaign. Track metrics like open rates (aim 25-40%) and decide. Whichever you choose, consistent value-driven emails will grow your business.
