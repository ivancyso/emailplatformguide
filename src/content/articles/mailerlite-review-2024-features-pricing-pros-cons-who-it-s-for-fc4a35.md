# MailerLite Review 2024: Features, Pricing, Pros, Cons & Who It's For

If you're searching for an **email marketing platform** that's affordable, user-friendly, and packed with essential features, you've likely come across MailerLite. In this in-depth **MailerLite review**, we'll dive into its capabilities, pricing structure, strengths, and limitations to help you decide if it's the right choice for your business or side hustle.

MailerLite has built a reputation for serving small to medium-sized businesses, bloggers, and e-commerce stores with straightforward tools that don't overwhelm beginners. Founded in 2010, it powers over 2 million users worldwide and emphasizes simplicity without skimping on functionality. But does it live up to the hype? Let's break it down.

## What is MailerLite?

MailerLite is a Lithuania-based email marketing service designed for creators and small teams who want to grow their audience through newsletters, automations, and website popups. Unlike enterprise giants like Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign, MailerLite focuses on affordability and ease of use, making it accessible for solopreneurs.

Key highlights include:
- A free forever plan for up to 1,000 subscribers.
- Drag-and-drop email builder.
- Automation workflows.
- Landing pages, popups, and forms.
- Basic e-commerce integrations.

It's GDPR-compliant and offers high deliverability rates, which is crucial for ensuring your emails reach inboxes rather than spam folders.

## MailerLite Key Features

MailerLite shines in its core feature set. Here's a practical look at what it offers:

### Drag-and-Drop Email Editor

The visual editor is intuitive, even for non-designers. You can build responsive emails with blocks for text, images, buttons, and dividers. Practical tip: Use the photo manager to organize assets and maintain brand consistency. It supports dynamic content, like personalizing emails with subscriber data (e.g., first name), which boosts open rates by up to 20% according to industry benchmarks.

Test it by starting with a template—over 80 are available, categorized by industry like e-commerce or nonprofits.

### Automation Workflows

Automations are a standout. Create sequences for welcome series, abandoned carts, or re-engagement campaigns. The builder uses a flowchart interface: trigger (e.g., signup), wait steps, and actions (send email).

**Practical advice**: For a welcome automation, set a 2-day delay between emails to nurture leads without overwhelming them. Integrate with e-commerce platforms like Shopify for cart recovery—track revenue directly in MailerLite.

Advanced users can use JSON for custom triggers, but most stick to the no-code builder.

### Website Tools: Popups, Forms, and Landing Pages

Build popups (fixed, floating, fullscreen) with A/B testing to capture leads. Forms embed easily on sites, and landing pages come with 100+ templates.

**Pro tip**: Use exit-intent popups on high-traffic pages to reduce bounce rates. Track conversions via Google Analytics integration for data-driven tweaks.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Tag subscribers or use custom fields for segmentation. Analytics show opens, clicks, bounces, and revenue per email.

**Advice**: Segment by engagement (e.g., opened last 30 days) to send targeted re-engagement campaigns, improving deliverability.

### Integrations

Connects with 100+ apps: WooCommerce, WordPress, Zapier, Google Analytics, Facebook Lead Ads. No native CRM, but Zapier bridges gaps.

## MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite's pricing is transparent and subscriber-based:

| Plan | Subscribers | Emails/Month | Price/Month (Annual) |
|------|-------------|--------------|----------------------|
| Free | Up to 1,000 | 12,000      | $0                  |
| Growing Business | Up to 2,500 | Unlimited  | $9                  |
| Advanced | Up to 10,000 | Unlimited | $18 (up to 500)     |

Higher tiers scale up, billed annually for 15-20% savings. Add-ons like premium templates ($10/month) or web push notifications ($5/month) are optional.

**Practical note**: Start free to test. Upgrade when exceeding limits—monthly billing available at a premium.

No contracts; cancel anytime. Compared to Mailchimp (free up to 500 subs, then $13+), MailerLite offers better value for growing lists.

## Pros and Cons of MailerLite

### Pros
- **Affordable**: Cheapest unlimited sends.
- **User-Friendly**: 4.8/5 on Capterra; quick onboarding.
- **High Deliverability**: 99%+ inbox rates reported.
- **Feature-Rich Free Plan**: Rare in the industry.
- **Responsive Support**: 24/7 chat, email, knowledge base.

### Cons
- **Limited Advanced Features**: No deep behavioral tracking or sales funnels like ActiveCampaign.
- **Basic Reporting**: Lacks cohort analysis.
- **E-commerce Light**: Good for basics, not complex stores.
- **No Phone Support**: Email/chat only.

## Who Should Use MailerLite?

**Ideal for**:
- Bloggers and content creators sending newsletters.
- Small e-commerce shops (under 10k subs).
- Nonprofits or agencies on budgets.

**Not for**:
- Enterprises needing complex automations.
- High-volume transactional emails (use SendGrid add-on).

**Practical decision framework**:
1. List size <5k? MailerLite wins on price.
2. Need popups/landing pages? Yes.
3. Heavy automation? Consider ActiveCampaign.

## MailerLite vs. Alternatives

- **vs. Mailchimp**: MailerLite cheaper post-free; simpler UI.
- **vs. ConvertKit**: Similar for creators, but MailerLite has popups.
- **vs. ActiveCampaign**: MailerLite easier/cheaper; AC more powerful.

**Migration tip**: Use CSV import; MailerLite offers free migration help.
