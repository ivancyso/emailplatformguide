# Mailchimp vs ConvertKit: The Ultimate Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you connect with your audience. With so many options, pitting Mailchimp against ConvertKit makes sense for many users. Mailchimp has long been a staple for small businesses and e-commerce, while ConvertKit targets creators, bloggers, and course sellers with its creator-centric features.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're starting from scratch or switching platforms, you'll get practical advice to decide which fits your workflow. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, launched in 2001, powers millions of campaigns worldwide. It's known for its user-friendly interface and robust free plan. Ideal for e-commerce stores, nonprofits, and beginners, it offers everything from basic newsletters to advanced automations.

Key strengths include:
- Drag-and-drop email builder
- Extensive template library
- Built-in CRM and audience segmentation
- E-commerce integrations like Shopify and WooCommerce

However, as your list grows, costs can rise quickly, and advanced features require higher tiers.

## Overview of ConvertKit

ConvertKit, formerly Landing Page Monkey, rebranded to focus on creators. It's designed for bloggers, podcasters, YouTellers, and online course creators. The platform emphasizes automation sequences, tagging, and subscriber journeys over flashy designs.

Standout features:
- Visual automation builder
- Forms, landing pages, and checkout pages
- Native tagging for segmentation
- Creator-specific tools like digital product sales

ConvertKit shines in simplicity for content creators but may feel limited for complex e-commerce needs.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major decision factor. Both offer free plans, but structures differ.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic features.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/month (500 contacts), adds A/B testing, custom branding.
- **Standard**: $20/month (500 contacts), includes automations and behavioral targeting.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for large lists and advanced reporting.

Costs scale with contacts; overages can add up. No annual discounts on lower plans.

### ConvertKit Pricing
- **Free**: Up to 10,000 subscribers (limited to basic broadcasts and forms—no automations).
- **Creator**: $15/month (300 subscribers), unlocks automations, landing pages.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month (300 subscribers), adds advanced reporting, subscriber scoring.
- Enterprise options for massive lists.

ConvertKit offers annual billing discounts (20% off) and migrates free users seamlessly. For creators with growing lists, it's often more affordable long-term.

**Practical Tip**: Calculate your current list size and growth rate. Use Mailchimp's free tier for testing small lists; switch to ConvertKit if you're a solopreneur expecting 1k+ subs.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive with 100+ mobile-responsive templates. ConvertKit's editor is simpler, focusing on content blocks with fewer templates (but commerce-optimized ones). **Winner**: Mailchimp for design flexibility.

### Automations
ConvertKit excels here with a visual flowchart builder for sequences like welcome series or abandoned cart. Mailchimp's automations (Standard+) are tag-based but require more setup. **Winner**: ConvertKit for creators.

### Segmentation and Tagging
Both segment by behavior, but ConvertKit's tagging is superior—auto-apply tags based on actions (e.g., "opened email X"). Mailchimp uses groups and tags but less dynamically. **Practical Advice**: If personalization drives your strategy, ConvertKit saves time.

### Forms and Landing Pages
ConvertKit includes unlimited forms, landing pages, and checkouts natively. Mailchimp requires popups/forms but landing pages are premium or via integrations. **Winner**: ConvertKit.

### Integrations
Mailchimp leads with 300+ (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). ConvertKit has 100+ focused on creator tools (Teachable, Gumroad, WordPress). Both support Zapier for custom workflows.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp offers detailed opens/clicks/revenue tracking. ConvertKit provides subscriber value, revenue per subscriber, and open rates by tag. Tie, depending on e-com vs creator focus.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is polished for beginners—wizard-guided setup. ConvertKit is straightforward but assumes some marketing knowledge; its automation tutorials are excellent.

**Practical Tip**: Start with Mailchimp if you're new to email marketing. ConvertKit's learning curve pays off for repeat sequences.

Onboarding: Mailchimp has video tours; ConvertKit offers migration services (free import from Mailchimp).

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket support (priority on paid plans), extensive help center, no live chat on free.
ConvertKit: 9-5 CST chat/email, dedicated onboarding calls for Pro users, creator-focused resources.

**Winner**: ConvertKit for responsive, niche support.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- Generous free plan
- Stunning templates
- E-commerce powerhouse

**Cons**:
- Expensive scaling
- Automations locked behind paywalls
- Less creator-focused

### ConvertKit
**Pros**:
- Creator tools (selling products)
- Powerful automations
- Affordable for lists up to 10k

**Cons**:
- Basic design options
- Fewer integrations
- Free plan lacks automations

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- E-commerce businesses (Shopify users)
- Agencies managing multiple clients
- Beginners needing templates and ads

**Migration Tip**: Export CSV from current tool; Mailchimp's importer handles segments.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Content creators (bloggers, YouTubers)
- Course sellers and membership sites
- Those prioritizing automations over design

**Switching Advice**: Use ConvertKit's importer—preserves tags. Test automations on a small segment first.

## Final Verdict and Conclusion

Neither is "best" overall—it depends on your goals. Mailchimp suits visual, e-commerce campaigns with broad appeal. ConvertKit empowers creators with smart automations and monetization.

For most solopreneurs and small teams (our audience), **ConvertKit edges out** due to cost-effectiveness and automation depth. If you're under 500 subs and love designs, start with Mailchimp's free plan.
