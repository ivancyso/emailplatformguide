# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive sales. In this MailerLite vs ConvertKit showdown, we'll break down everything you need to know to pick the winner for your needs. Whether you're a solopreneur, blogger, or small business owner, this guide compares pricing, features, ease of use, and more based on real user experiences and official platform details.

## Why Compare MailerLite and ConvertKit?

Both MailerLite and ConvertKit are popular for their creator-friendly designs and powerful automation. MailerLite appeals to budget-conscious users with its free plan and intuitive interface, while ConvertKit targets content creators with advanced tagging and commerce tools. If you're deciding between them, consider your list size, technical skills, and growth goals.

MailerLite serves over 1 million users worldwide, emphasizing simplicity and affordability. ConvertKit, used by 30,000+ creators, focuses on monetization through emails, courses, and subscriptions.

## Pricing Breakdown: MailerLite vs ConvertKit

Pricing is often the first tiebreaker. Here's a practical look at costs as of 2024.

### MailerLite Pricing

- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month. Includes basic automation, forms, and landing pages—perfect for testing.
- **Growing Business**: $10/month for up to 500 subs (billed annually), scaling to $20 for 1,000 subs. Features unlimited emails.
- **Advanced**: $21/month+ for 500 subs, adding A/B testing, web push notifications, and priority support.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for large lists.

MailerLite's transparency shines—no hidden fees, and annual billing saves 25%. Practical tip: Start free and upgrade seamlessly as your list grows.

### ConvertKit Pricing

- **Free Trial**: 14 days, no card required.
- **Creator**: $9/month for up to 300 subs (annual), $15 monthly. Unlimited emails, basic automations.
- **Creator Pro**: $29/month for 300 subs (annual), adding advanced automations, subscriber scoring, and integrations.
- **Higher Tiers**: Scales to $2,500+/month for massive lists.

ConvertKit lacks a perpetual free plan, which can be a hurdle for bootstrappers. Pro features justify the cost for sellers. Advice: If you sell digital products, the commerce tools pay off quickly.

**Winner on Price**: MailerLite for small lists and startups; ConvertKit for established creators.

## Ease of Use: Which is More Beginner-Friendly?

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

MailerLite's editor is clean and mobile-responsive. Build campaigns in minutes with 80+ templates. Navigation is straightforward—no steep learning curve. Users praise its pop-up builder for lead gen without coding.

Practical advice: Use the website builder for quick landing pages. Integrate with WordPress via a simple plugin.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Dashboard

ConvertKit uses visual automations (like flowcharts) and tagging over segments, which clicks for podcasters and course creators. The editor is solid but less template-heavy than MailerLite's.

Tip: Leverage the "broadcast" for newsletters and "sequences" for drip campaigns. It's intuitive once you grasp tagging.

**Ease Winner**: MailerLite for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for those comfortable with logic-based flows.

## Feature Face-Off

### Email Templates and Design

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, custom HTML, RSS-to-email. Strong for visual newsletters.

ConvertKit: Fewer templates (focused on clean, text-heavy designs), but excellent personalization like subscriber previews.

### Automation and Segmentation

MailerLite: Time-based, behavior-triggered automations. Segments by clicks, opens, custom fields. New: Multi-step workflows.

ConvertKit: Superior tagging system (e.g., tag based on page views). Sequences for evergreen content. Pro: One-click upsells.

Advice: For abandoned cart recovery, both work, but ConvertKit edges for e-commerce.

### Landing Pages and Forms

MailerLite: Unlimited landing pages, pop-ups, surveys on all plans. High-converting templates.

ConvertKit: Forms and landing pages included, with better A/B testing in Pro.

### Integrations and Deliverability

Both integrate with Zapier, Shopify, WordPress. MailerLite: 250+ native. ConvertKit: Strong with Teachable, Gumroad.

Deliverability: Both ~99%, but ConvertKit's creator focus yields higher opens for engaged lists.

### Analytics and Support

MailerLite: Detailed reports, heatmaps. Email/chat support.

ConvertKit: Revenue tracking, subscriber value scores. 24/7 chat in Pro.

**Features Winner**: Tie—MailerLite for websites/forms, ConvertKit for sales funnels.

## Real User Scenarios: Who Should Choose What?

- **Bootstrapping Blogger (under 1k subs)**: MailerLite. Grow free, add pop-ups for leads.
- **Course Creator**: ConvertKit. Sequences nurture, sell directly.
- **E-commerce Store**: Test both—MailerLite for basics, ConvertKit for upsells.
- **Agency**: MailerLite's white-label and teams features.

Practical tip: Export lists easily from either (CSV), so switch if needed. Track ROI with UTM links.

## Migrating Between Platforms

Both offer import tools. MailerLite's migration wizard is user-friendly. Expect 1-2 hours for small lists. Tip: Clean your list first—remove inactives for better deliverability.

## Conclusion: MailerLite vs ConvertKit—Your Verdict

MailerLite wins for affordability, ease, and websites—ideal if you're starting or value simplicity. ConvertKit triumphs for creators monetizing content with deep automations and sales tools.

Assess your needs: Budget under $10/month? MailerLite. Selling products? ConvertKit. Try MailerLite's free plan or ConvertKit's trial today.
