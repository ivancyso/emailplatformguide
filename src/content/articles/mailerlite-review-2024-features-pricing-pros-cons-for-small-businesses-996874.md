# MailerLite Review 2024: Features, Pricing, Pros & Cons for Small Businesses

If you're a small business owner, freelancer, or blogger hunting for an email marketing platform that's easy on the wallet and simple to use, MailerLite might have crossed your radar. In this comprehensive MailerLite review, we'll dive deep into its features, pricing structure, real-world performance, and whether it stacks up for your needs. With a focus on users choosing between email marketing platforms, we'll provide practical advice to help you decide if MailerLite is right for you.

MailerLite positions itself as a user-friendly alternative to giants like Mailchimp or ConvertKit, emphasizing drag-and-drop simplicity, automation, and high deliverability rates. Launched in 2010, it serves over 2 million users worldwide. But does it deliver on its promises? Let's break it down.

## What is MailerLite and Who is It For?

MailerLite is an all-in-one email marketing platform designed for solopreneurs, small teams, and growing businesses. It's particularly appealing if you're just starting out or managing lists under 10,000 subscribers. Unlike enterprise-level tools with steep learning curves, MailerLite prioritizes intuitiveness.

**Ideal users:**
- Bloggers and content creators sending newsletters.
- E-commerce stores running promotions.
- Non-profits or agencies needing basic automations.

If you're scaling to complex customer journeys or need advanced segmentation, you might outgrow it—but for 80% of small operations, it's a solid fit.

## Key Features of MailerLite

MailerLite packs a punch in core areas without overwhelming beginners. Here's a practical look at its standout features.

### Drag-and-Drop Email Builder

The email editor is one of MailerLite's strongest suits. It's a true drag-and-drop interface with pre-built blocks for text, images, buttons, and dividers. No coding required.

**Practical advice:** Start with responsive templates optimized for mobile (over 50% of opens are mobile). Customize with your branding—upload logos and set colors in seconds. Test variations using A/B testing for subject lines and content blocks to boost open rates by 20-30%.

Pro tip: Use the plain-text editor for high-deliverability newsletters; it mimics personal emails and avoids spam filters.

### Automation Workflows

Automations are trigger-based and visual, like a flowchart builder. Set up welcome series, abandoned cart emails, or re-engagement campaigns effortlessly.

**Practical advice:** For a welcome sequence, trigger on signup → send email 1 (intro) → wait 2 days → email 2 (value tip) → wait 3 days → email 3 (offer). This nurtures leads without manual work. Integrate with e-commerce via webhooks for real-time triggers.

Limitations: No advanced branching based on clicks (yet), so keep flows linear for best results.

### Landing Pages, Popups, and Forms

Build unlimited landing pages, popups, and subscription forms. These convert visitors into subscribers seamlessly.

**Practical advice:** Embed popups on exit-intent or scroll depth (e.g., 50%). A/B test headlines like "Get 20% Off" vs. "Free Guide Inside" to lift conversions. Use forms on websites with custom fields for segmentation (e.g., "Interest: Podcasts").

Bonus: One-click website builder for simple microsites, great for lead magnets.

### Integrations and Analytics

Connects to 100+ apps like WordPress, Shopify, Google Analytics, and Zapier. Track opens, clicks, bounces, and revenue with detailed reports.

**Practical advice:** Link to Google Analytics for UTM tracking. Monitor unsubscribe rates—aim under 0.5% by cleaning lists monthly via segmentation (inactive >90 days).
