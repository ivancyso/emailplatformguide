# MailerLite vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against Mailchimp—the two popular choices for small businesses and creators—helps narrow it down. This comparison dives into pricing, features, usability, and real-world performance to guide your decision.

Whether you're a solopreneur starting with a free plan or scaling a growing list, we'll break it down with practical advice based on official specs and user feedback from sources like G2 and Capterra.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free tiers, but they differ significantly.

### MailerLite Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes drag-and-drop editor, basic automations, landing pages, and popups.
- **Growing Plan**: Starts at $10/month (billed annually) for up to 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 1,000, $30 for 2,500, etc. Unlimited emails, advanced automations, A/B testing, and webhooks.
- **Advanced Plan**: From $21/month for 500 subs, adds dedicated IP, 24/7 support, and custom HTML.

MailerLite bills based on subscribers only—no extra charges for emails sent, making it cost-effective for high-volume senders.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month (500 emails daily). Basic editor, templates, and audience insights.
- **Essentials**: $13/month for 500 contacts (up to 10,000 emails), single-step automations.
- **Standard**: $20/month for 500 contacts, multi-step automations, A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for advanced segmentation and multivariate testing.

Mailchimp charges based on contacts *and* sends, which can inflate costs quickly. For example, sending to 10,000 contacts monthly on Essentials exceeds the base price.

**Practical Advice**: Calculate your needs using their pricing calculators. If under 1,000 subs with moderate sends, MailerLite's free plan wins. For 500+ subs with high engagement, MailerLite stays cheaper long-term.

## Feature Comparison: Core Capabilities

Both platforms excel in email marketing essentials, but nuances matter.

### Email Builder and Templates
- **MailerLite**: Intuitive drag-and-drop builder with 80+ responsive templates. Unlimited images and blocks. JSON feed integration for dynamic content.
- **Mailchimp**: Classic and new builders with 100+ templates. Strong content optimizer AI suggests improvements.

**Winner**: Tie for beginners; Mailchimp edges for advanced customization.

### Automation and Workflows

### h3: MailerLite Automations
Trigger-based workflows like welcome series, abandoned cart (with integrations), and RSS campaigns. Visual builder, no coding needed. Unlimited in paid plans.

### h3: Mailchimp Automations
Customer journey builder in higher plans. Single-step free; multi-step from Standard ($20+). Behavioral triggers shine.

**Practical Tip**: Test automations with a small segment first. MailerLite's simplicity suits quick setups; Mailchimp for complex funnels.

### Landing Pages and Forms
- MailerLite: Unlimited pages/forms in all plans. High-converting templates, embedded popups, and exit-intent tech.
- Mailchimp: Basic pages in free; unlimited in paid. Strong form customization.

MailerLite offers more free landing page features.

### Integrations and Analytics
Both integrate with 1,000+ apps via Zapier. Mailchimp has native e-commerce (Shopify, WooCommerce). Analytics: Open rates, clicks, revenue tracking similar. MailerLite provides heatmaps; Mailchimp has predictive insights.

## Ease of Use and Support

MailerLite's interface is clean and beginner-friendly—faster onboarding per reviews (4.8/5 on G2). Mailchimp's dashboard feels busier but powerful.

Support:
- MailerLite: Email/chat (paid), extensive knowledge base, live chat 24/7 on Advanced.
- Mailchimp: 24/7 email/phone on paid plans; free users get help center only.

**Advice for New Users**: Start with MailerLite's 30-second signup and tours. Migrate via CSV import—both support it seamlessly.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Both maintain 99%+ deliverability. MailerLite uses Amazon SES; Mailchimp its own infrastructure. GDPR tools standard. Authentication (DKIM/SPF) easy to set up.

**Pro Tip**: Warm up your IP by starting with 50% list sends, gradually increasing to boost inbox rates.

## Who Should Choose MailerLite?

Ideal for bloggers, freelancers, and small teams (under 5k subs) prioritizing affordability and speed. If you send newsletters weekly without complex sales funnels, it's perfect.

**Migration Tip**: Export from Mailchimp, import to MailerLite—test a campaign on 10% list first.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

Best for e-commerce stores, agencies, or teams needing customer journeys and deep analytics. If integrations are key, its ecosystem shines.

Test both free plans side-by-side. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and unsubscribes (<0.5%). Whichever boosts engagement without hassle is your winner.
