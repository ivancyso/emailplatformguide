# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. In this MailerLite vs ConvertKit showdown, we'll break down key aspects like pricing, features, automation, and usability to help you decide. Both tools cater to creators, bloggers, and small businesses, but they shine in different areas. Whether you're starting with a free plan or scaling up, this guide provides practical insights based on real user experiences and official specs.

## Pricing: Affordability vs Value

Pricing is often the first hurdle. MailerLite offers a generous **free plan** for up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails/month—perfect for bootstrappers testing the waters. Paid plans start at $10/month (Growing Business) for 500 subscribers, scaling to $20 for 1,000, with no cap on sends within limits. Features like automation and landing pages unlock at higher tiers ($30+/month for Advanced).

ConvertKit (rebranded as Kit) has no perpetual free plan, just a 14-day trial. The **Creator plan** starts at $15/month for 300 subscribers and unlimited emails, jumping to $29 for 1,000. The **Creator Pro** at $29+ adds advanced segmentation and reporting. Annual billing saves 20%, but costs rise quickly with list growth—e.g., $79/month for 5,000 subs.

**Practical Advice**: If your list is under 1,000, start with MailerLite's free tier to validate your strategy without commitment. For established creators sending daily newsletters, ConvertKit's unlimited sends justify the price. Calculate your needs: use their pricing calculators and factor in integrations (both support Zapier).

|---------|------------|------------|
| Free Plan | Yes (1k subs) | Trial only |
| Starting Paid | $10/mo | $15/mo |
| Unlimited Sends | No (tiered) | Yes |

## Ease of Use: Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

MailerLite wins for beginners with its **intuitive drag-and-drop editor**. Building campaigns takes minutes—no coding needed. The interface is clean, with A/B testing built-in on all plans. Navigation is straightforward: dashboard → campaigns → design.

ConvertKit emphasizes **creator workflows**, using visual automations (called Sequences) and a tagging system over traditional lists. It's simple once learned but has a steeper curve for non-creators. The editor is block-based, great for newsletters, but less flexible for complex designs.

**Practical Advice**: Test both trials. Import a sample list (CSV works seamlessly) and send a test campaign. MailerLite suits e-commerce stores needing quick popups; ConvertKit excels for content creators automating welcome series.

## Core Features Breakdown

### Email Templates and Design

MailerLite boasts 80+ responsive templates, customizable with HTML access. Includes photo editing tools—unique for non-designers.

ConvertKit offers clean, mobile-optimized templates focused on text-heavy newsletters. Commerce features like paid subscriptions integrate natively.

**Winner**: MailerLite for variety; ConvertKit for newsletters.

### Automation and Segmentation

ConvertKit's **tagging system** is powerful: tag subscribers by behavior (e.g., "opened email X"), triggering one-click automations. Ideal for personalized journeys.

MailerLite uses **automation workflows** with triggers like "subscribed to form" or "clicked link." Segmentation by groups, custom fields, or behaviors. Lacks ConvertKit's tag depth but adds RSS campaigns.

**Practical Advice**: Map your funnel. For simple autoresponders, MailerLite suffices. Creators with podcasts/blogs? ConvertKit's subscriber scoring and segments reduce churn by 20-30% (per user reports).

### Landing Pages, Forms, and Popups

Both excel here. MailerLite includes unlimited landing pages/forms/popups on paid plans, with A/B testing and countdown timers.

ConvertKit has **native landing pages**, opt-in forms, and link pages (great for product launches). Pro adds checkout pages.

**Tip**: Embed forms on WordPress/Webflow via plugins. Track conversions with UTM parameters.

### Integrations and Deliverability

MailerLite integrates with 1,000+ apps (Stripe, Google Analytics). Deliverability ~99%, with dedicated IPs on Enterprise.

ConvertKit connects to 100+ (Teachable, Gumroad). Strong deliverability (98%+ inbox rate) via authentication best practices.

**Advice**: Verify domains/SPF/DKIM on both. Monitor with Google Postmaster Tools.

## Analytics and Support

MailerLite provides **open/click rates, heatmaps, revenue tracking**. 24/7 live chat, email support; knowledge base with videos.

ConvertKit offers **subscriber activity feeds, revenue per subscriber**. Priority email support; Creator Network community.

**Practical Tip**: Use analytics to optimize send times (MailerLite auto-suggests). Join communities: MailerLite Facebook group vs ConvertKit's Slack.
