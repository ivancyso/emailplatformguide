# 10 Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Free & Paid Email Marketing Platforms

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation in lower tiers, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're dealing with bloated fees, clunky interfaces, or missing features like advanced segmentation, it's time to explore better options.

In this roundup, we'll cover the top 10 Mailchimp alternatives tailored for small businesses, creators, e-commerce stores, and agencies. Each includes real pros, cons, pricing (as of 2024), and practical tips for switching. Whether you need free plans, robust automations, or ecommerce integrations, there's a platform here for you. Let's dive in and help you choose the right one.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans jump to $13/month for Essentials, excluding advanced automations until Pro ($350+/month). Users often complain about deliverability dips and forced upgrades. Alternatives offer:

- **Better value**: Unlimited sends or lower per-contact pricing.
- **Advanced features**: AI-driven campaigns, deeper analytics, and no-code builders.
- **Easier migrations**: CSV imports and template transfers.

**Practical tip**: Before switching, export your Mailchimp audience via CSV (Audience > Export Audience). Test deliverability with a small segment on the new platform.

## Top Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign shines for marketing automation. With site tracking, predictive sending, and 900+ integrations, it's ideal for complex funnels.

**Key Features**:
- Advanced automations (if/then branches).
- CRM built-in for sales pipelines.
- Split testing and machine learning content.

**Pricing**: Starts at $29/month (Starter, 500 contacts). Free trial.

**Pros**: Superior automation; high deliverability (99%+).
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

**Practical Advice**: Use the automation blueprint library to replicate Mailchimp journeys. Integrate with Shopify or WordPress for seamless tracking. Migrate via their importer tool—takes under 30 minutes.

### 2. ConvertKit (Now Kit)

Perfect for creators and bloggers. Focuses on simplicity with tagging over segments.

**Key Features**:
- Visual email builder.
- Creator network for collaborations.
- Subscription forms and landing pages.

**Pricing**: Free up to 300 subscribers; Creator at $29/month.

**Pros**: Intuitive for solopreneurs; unlimited emails.
**Cons**: Less ecommerce-focused.

**Practical Advice**: Tag subscribers by behavior (e.g., "opened welcome"). A/B test subject lines—ConvertKit's editor makes it drag-and-drop easy.

### 3. Klaviyo

Ecommerce powerhouse, especially for Shopify users.

**Key Features**n- Dynamic product recommendations.
- Flow builder for abandoned carts.
- Revenue tracking per campaign.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts; scales with sends ($20+/month).

**Pros**: ROI-focused analytics; 1B+ emails/month deliverability.
**Cons**: Overkill for non-stores.

**Practical Advice**: Connect Shopify in one click. Set up "won't buy" filters to exclude low-value contacts, boosting open rates 20-30%.

### 4. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)

Affordable all-in-one with SMS and chat.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts.
- Transactional emails.
- Sales CRM.

**Pricing**: Free (300/day); Starter $25/month.

**Pros**: Generous free tier; multi-channel.
**Cons**: Interface feels dated.

**Practical Advice**: Use automation workflows for re-engagement. Comply with GDPR via built-in consent tools.

### 5. MailerLite

Budget-friendly with drag-and-drop ease.

**Key Features**:
- Popups and forms.
- A/B testing.
- Website builder.

**Pricing**: Free up to 1,000 subscribers; Growing Business $10/month.

**Pros**: Simple; high deliverability.
**Cons**: Basic automations.

**Practical Advice**: Embed forms on WordPress. Segment by engagement score to prioritize warm leads.

### 6. GetResponse

Webinar-focused with funnels.

**Key Features**:
- Landing pages and funnels.
- AI email generator.
- Ecommerce tools.

**Pricing**: Free trial; Email Marketing $19/month.

**Pros**: Conversion funnels.
**Cons**: Support varies.

**Practical Advice**: Build webinar sequences. Use conversion funnels to track ROI beyond opens.

### 7. AWeber

Reliable for small businesses.

**Key Features**:
- RSS-to-email.
- Tag-based automation.
- Ecommerce integration.

**Pricing**: Lite $12.50/month (500 subs).

**Pros**: 99% deliverability; easy.
**Cons**: Fewer advanced features.

**Practical Advice**: Automate RSS newsletters. Test mobile previews rigorously.

### 8. Constant Contact

Beginner-friendly with events.

**Key Features**:
- Event management.
- Social ads.
- AI subject lines.

**Pricing**: Lite $12/month.

**Pros**: Templates galore.
**Cons**: Per-contact pricing.

**Practical Advice**: Use for local businesses with events. Personalize with merge tags.

### 9. Omnisend

Ecommerce SMS + email.

**Key Features**:
- Omnichannel flows.
- Product picker.
- Pre-built workflows.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts; Standard $16/month.

**Pros**: SMS boosts revenue 26%.
**Cons**: Ecom-only vibe.

**Practical Advice**: Time SMS for cart abandonment (e.g., 1hr post-abandon).

### 10. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free CRM-integrated.

**Key Features**:
- Free up to 2M emails/month.
- Personalization tokens.
- Forms and CTAs.

**Pricing**: Free; Marketing Hub $20/month.

**Pros**: Scales with CRM.
**Cons**: Upsell-heavy.

**Practical Advice**: Use free tier for startups. Track lifecycle stages.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: Ecommerce? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Creators? ConvertKit.
2. **Budget**: Free tiers: Brevo, MailerLite, HubSpot.
3. **Scale**: ActiveCampaign for growth.
4. **Test**: Use trials. Send to 10% list first.
5. **Migrate Smart**: Map tags/segments. Notify subs of change.

**Pro Tip**: Check deliverability via Mail-Tester.com post-migration. Aim for 9/10 score.

## Conclusion

Ditching Mailchimp doesn't mean starting over—these alternatives offer superior features at better prices. ActiveCampaign leads for power users, while MailerLite suits bootstrappers. Start with a free trial, import your list, and watch engagement soar.

Ready to switch? Pick one based on your niche and test today. Your inbox (and wallet) will thank you.
