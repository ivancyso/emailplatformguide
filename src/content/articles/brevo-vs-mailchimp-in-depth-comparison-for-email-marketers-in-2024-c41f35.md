# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Should You Choose in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can make or break your campaigns. With thousands of subscribers to nurture and sales to drive, platforms like Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp dominate the space. Both offer drag-and-drop editors, automation, and analytics, but they cater to different needs.

Brevo shines for businesses wanting transactional emails alongside marketing, with affordable scaling. Mailchimp excels in user-friendly templates and creative tools for beginners. This comparison dives into pricing, features, deliverability, and real-world use cases to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur or growing a team, we'll break it down with practical advice based on official docs and user benchmarks.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo is an all-in-one CRM platform focused on email, SMS, chat, and more. Launched as Sendinblue in 2012, it rebranded to emphasize growth tools. Key strengths:

- **Unlimited contacts** on free plan (300 emails/day).
- Strong transactional email support (e.g., order confirmations).
- Multi-channel: Email, SMS, WhatsApp, ads.

Practical tip: Use Brevo's API for e-commerce integrations like WooCommerce. Set up webhooks for real-time transactional sends—ideal if you run an online store.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, founded in 2001, is the OG of email marketing. It's famous for its quirky design and ease for non-techies. Highlights:

- Free plan for up to 500 contacts (1,000 sends/month).
- Extensive template library (hundreds pre-built).
- Audience insights via behavioral data.

Pro advice: Leverage Mailchimp's 'Customer Journey Builder' for simple automations. Start with pop-up forms to grow your list organically—conversion rates often hit 5-10% with A/B testing.

## Pricing Comparison: Brevo vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a top decider. Here's a side-by-side (as of 2024; check sites for updates):

| Feature | Brevo | Mailchimp |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Free Plan** | Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day | 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month |
| **Starter** | $25/mo (20k emails) | $13/mo (5k contacts, 10k sends) |
| **Growth** | $65/mo (20k emails + automation) | $20/mo (25k contacts) |
| **Advanced** | $119/mo (SMS, A/B) | $299+/mo (enterprise) |

**Brevo wins for scaling**: Pay per email volume, not contacts. Great for lists over 10k without jumping tiers.

**Mailchimp for small lists**: Cheaper entry, but costs spike with growth (e.g., 50k contacts = $75/mo).

Practical advice: Calculate your needs. If sending 50k emails/month to 100k contacts, Brevo's $65 plan beats Mailchimp's $299. Use their calculators for quotes.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Ease of Use and Editor

Both have drag-and-drop builders, but Mailchimp's feels more intuitive for designers—responsive previews and content blocks galore. Brevo's editor is clean but less flashy; better for text-heavy newsletters.

**Tip**: Test both free trials. Mailchimp suits visual brands (e.g., fashion); Brevo for service pros (e.g., SaaS).

### Email Templates and Design

Mailchimp: 100+ free templates, theme builder. Custom HTML easy.
Brevo: 50+ templates, multipurpose. Strong mobile optimization.

Advice: Import your logo/CSS in both. A/B test subject lines—Mailchimp's tool auto-rotates; Brevo requires manual setup.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp's automations are beginner-friendly: Welcome series, abandoned cart in clicks.
Brevo offers advanced branching (if/then logic) and transactional triggers.

**Pro tip**: For e-com, Brevo's cart recovery integrates natively with Shopify. Mailchimp needs Zapier ($20/mo extra).

### Deliverability and Analytics

Both claim 95%+ rates. Mailchimp edges with dedicated IP on higher plans; Brevo's strong on EU compliance (GDPR).
Analytics: Mailchimp's heatmaps show click paths; Brevo tracks revenue per email.

Advice: Warm up IPs gradually (start 100/day). Monitor bounce rates—aim <2%. Use Brevo for global sends; Mailchimp for US-centric.

### Integrations and E-commerce

Mailchimp: 300+ native (Google Analytics, Facebook).
Brevo: 300+ via app center, plus native Woo/Shopify.

Tip: Connect to Google Sheets for list imports. Automate leads from Typeform to either.

### SMS and Multi-Channel

Brevo: Built-in SMS (pay-per-use, ~$0.01/msg).
Mailchimp: SMS add-on ($10+/mo).

For omnichannel, Brevo pulls ahead.

- Beginners/solopreneurs.
- Creative agencies.
- Small lists (<10k).
- Design-focused campaigns.

**Migration note**: Brevo's importer handles Mailchimp directly.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp? **Brevo for scalability and value**—perfect if you're expanding or need SMS/transactionals. **Mailchimp for simplicity**—ideal starters prioritizing design.

No one-size-fits-all. Start free trials: Build a sample campaign, import 100 contacts, run an automation. Measure costs against your volume (e.g., 10k sends/mo).

For most small businesses, Brevo edges out long-term. Track ROI: Emails should deliver 40x return. Whichever you pick, segment lists (e.g., by engagement) and personalize—boost opens 26%.
