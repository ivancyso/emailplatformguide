# Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Scale Your Email Marketing

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its pricing tiers, limited automation for free users, and recent changes have many users searching for alternatives. If you're hitting limits on contacts, needing advanced automations, or seeking better value, this roundup covers the best Mailchimp alternatives. We've focused on platforms suitable for solopreneurs, small businesses, and growing teams choosing email marketing tools.

These picks are based on real user feedback from sites like G2 and Capterra, official pricing pages, and feature comparisons as of 2024. Each offers free plans or trials, so test them yourself.

## Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month, with paid plans starting at $13/month but escalating quickly (e.g., $299/month for 10k contacts). Advanced features like deep automations or e-commerce integrations often require higher tiers.

Alternatives shine in:
- **Affordable scaling**: Flat or lower per-contact pricing.
- **Superior automation**: Behavioral triggers, A/B testing, and CRM syncs.
- **Niche focus**: Creator tools, e-com, or agencies.

Practical tip: Audit your current campaigns. Need RSS feeds? E-com tracking? List size? This guides your choice.

## 1. ActiveCampaign

### Key Features
Advanced email automation, site tracking, machine learning for send times, built-in CRM, and 900+ integrations (e.g., Shopify, WordPress).

### Pricing
Free trial; starts at $29/month (1k contacts), scales to $149 for 10k.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Powerful automations save hours; excellent support. **Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

**Best for**: E-commerce and sales teams needing CRM. Migrate via CSV import—test automations first.

## 2. Klaviyo

### Key Features
E-commerce focused: Revenue tracking, dynamic product feeds, flow builder for abandoned carts, SMS integration.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; $20/month for 500 contacts, scales with usage.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Shopify native, high ROI for stores. **Cons**: Less ideal for non-ecom.

**Best for**: Online stores. Practical advice: Connect your store in minutes; use pre-built flows to boost revenue 20-30% per benchmarks.

## 3. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

### Key Features
Unlimited contacts, transactional emails, SMS/chat, automation workflows, landing pages.

### Pricing
Free forever (300 emails/day); $25/month for 20k emails.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Cost-effective for high volume; EU-based GDPR compliant. **Cons**: Interface feels dated.

**Best for**: Budget-conscious marketers. Tip: Use transactional APIs for confirmations alongside marketing.

## 4. ConvertKit (now Kit)

### Key Features
Creator-focused: Visual automations, tagging/subscriber scoring, forms, commerce for digital products.

### Pricing
Free up to 300 subscribers; $29/month for 1k.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Simple for newsletters; one-click upsells. **Cons**: Limited reporting.

**Best for**: Bloggers/content creators. Advice: Segment by tag behavior for personalized sequences.

## 5. GetResponse

### Key Features
Email, webinars, funnels, AI email generator, e-com cart abandonment.

### Pricing
Free trial; $19/month for 1k contacts.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: All-in-one with webinars. **Cons**: Overwhelming for beginners.

**Best for**: Agencies. Tip: Host webinars to nurture leads—integrates seamlessly.

## 6. MailerLite

### Key Features
Drag-drop editor, popups, automation, A/B testing, website builder.

### Pricing
Free up to 1k subscribers/12k emails; $10/month for 500 more.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Beginner-friendly, cheap. **Cons**: Fewer advanced automations.

**Best for**: Startups. Practical: Use popups ethically to grow lists 10-20%.

## 7. Constant Contact

### Key Features
Event management, social ads, AI recommendations, robust templates.

### Pricing
$12/month for 500 contacts.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Easy for non-tech users. **Cons**: Pricier per contact.

**Best for**: Non-profits/small biz. Advice: Leverage events for engagement.

## 8. Omnisend

### Key Features
Omnichannel (email/SMS/push), pre-built workflows, product picker.

### Pricing
Free up to 250 contacts/500 emails; $16/month.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: E-com powerhouse. **Cons**: SMS costs extra.

**Best for**: Multichannel retail. Tip: Automate post-purchase for loyalty.

## 9. AWeber

### Key Features
Tag-based automation, landing pages, ecommerce integrations.

### Pricing
Free trial; $19/month first 500 contacts.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Reliable, great support. **Cons**: Basic design.

**Best for**: Long-term users. Advice: Use tags for hyper-personalization.

## 10. Drip

### Key Features
Behavioral ecommerce automation, workflows, revenue tracking.

### Pricing
$39/month for 1k subscribers.

### Pros & Cons
**Pros**: Deep customer journey mapping. **Cons**: E-com only.

**Best for**: High-ticket DTC. Tip: Score leads by actions for timely nurtures.

## How to Choose and Switch to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Match your needs**: E-com? Klaviyo/Omnisend. Creators? Kit. Volume? Brevo.
2. **Budget check**: Calculate costs for your list size (e.g., Brevo wins for 10k+).
3. **Trial run**: Export Mailchimp CSV, import, recreate top campaigns.
4. **Migration tips**: Use Zapier for syncs; notify subscribers of changes; A/B test new vs. old.
5. **Key metrics**: Delivery rates (all >95%), open rates (20-30% avg), compliance (CAN-SPAM).

Practical checklist:
- Free plan limits?
- Automation depth?
- Integrations (CRM, site)?
- Support quality?
