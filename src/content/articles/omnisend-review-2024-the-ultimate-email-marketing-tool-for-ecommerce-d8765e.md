# Omnisend Review 2024: The Ultimate Email Marketing Tool for Ecommerce

If you're running an online store and looking for a robust email marketing platform, you've likely come across Omnisend. This **Omnisend review** dives deep into its features, pricing, strengths, and limitations to help you decide if it's the right fit for your business. Tailored for ecommerce brands, Omnisend stands out with its omnichannel approach, combining email, SMS, and web push notifications seamlessly.

In this review, we'll cover everything from setup to advanced automations, backed by real user experiences and practical tips. Whether you're on Shopify, WooCommerce, or another platform, read on to see why Omnisend powers over 70,000 stores worldwide.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an ecommerce-focused marketing automation platform launched in 2014. Unlike general-purpose tools like Mailchimp, it's built specifically for online retailers. It emphasizes omnichannel campaigns, allowing you to engage customers via email, SMS, push notifications, and even Facebook Messenger from a single dashboard.

Key highlights:
- **Integrations**: Native support for Shopify, BigCommerce, WooCommerce, Magento, and more.
- **User base**: Popular among DTC brands and growing stores.
- **Free plan**: Available for up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month.

Practical advice: Start with the free plan to test integrations. Connect your store in under 5 minutes via a simple API or app store plugin. This syncs customer data, orders, and products automatically, eliminating manual imports.

Omnisend uses a contact-based pricing model, which is straightforward for ecommerce users. Here's the breakdown as of 2024:

| Plan | Price (billed annually) | Contacts | Key Features |

No hidden fees, and you can downgrade anytime. Compared to Klaviyo (starts at $20 for 250 contacts), Omnisend offers more channels at a similar price point.

## Key Features of Omnisend

### Email Builder and Templates

Omnisend's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 250+ ecommerce-optimized templates. Product blocks pull live inventory data, showing stock levels and prices dynamically.

**Pro tip**: Use the "Product Picker" to create personalized recommendations. For abandoned carts, embed product images with buy buttons—boosting recovery rates by 20-30% based on Omnisend benchmarks.

### Automation Workflows

Pre-built workflows for welcome series, abandoned carts, win-back, and cross-sells save hours. Trigger based on behavior like page views or purchases.

**Practical setup guide**:
1. Go to Automations > Create Workflow.
2. Select "Cart Abandonment" template.
3. Set delay (e.g., 1 hour) and add SMS fallback.
4. Segment by cart value >$50 for high-impact sends.

Users report 15-25% cart recovery rates. Customize with conditional splits—if no open after email 1, send SMS.

### Advanced Segmentation

Segment lists using 360° customer views: purchase history, browsing behavior, tags. Create dynamic segments that update in real-time.

**Example**: Target "VIPs" as customers with >3 purchases and average order value >$100. Practical advice: Combine with RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) scoring for hyper-targeted campaigns, increasing open rates by 40%.

### Omnichannel: SMS and Push

SMS open rates hit 98%. Integrate with Twilio for global delivery. Web push works for app-less notifications.

**Best practice**: Use SMS for urgency (e.g., "Your cart expires in 1 hour") and email for nurturing. A/B test channels—Omnisend reports 3x ROI on SMS.

### Reporting and Analytics

Track revenue per email, ROI, and attribution across channels. Heatmaps show engagement hotspots.

**Tip**: Set up custom dashboards for top metrics like CLV (Customer Lifetime Value) to justify spend to stakeholders.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-centric design with deep store integrations.
- Generous free plan and affordable scaling.
- Excellent automation library reduces setup time.
- 24/7 support via chat/email (Pro plan gets priority).
- High deliverability (99%+ inbox placement).

### Cons
- Limited to ecommerce—no B2B CRM features.
- SMS costs add up for high-volume senders.
- Learning curve for advanced segments (but tutorials help).
- No native landing pages (use integrations like Unbounce).

From G2 and Capterra reviews (4.7/5 average), users praise ease-of-use but note occasional sync delays with non-Shopify platforms.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for:
- Shopify/WooCommerce stores with 500+ contacts.
- Brands wanting omnichannel without multiple tools.
- DTC ecommerce focused on automation ROI.

Not for: Enterprise needing white-label or agencies managing multiple clients (limited user seats).

**Case study tip**: Brands like Gymshark use similar setups for 40% revenue from email/SMS. Replicate by starting with 3 core automations: cart recovery, welcome, post-purchase.

## Omnisend Alternatives

- **Klaviyo**: More advanced analytics, but pricier ($20+).
- **Klipfolio**: Cheaper, but fewer channels.
- **ActiveCampaign**: Great for CRM, less ecommerce focus.

Choose Omnisend if omnichannel and ease matter most.
