# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses scaling their email marketing. Both are popular platforms with robust features, but they cater to slightly different needs. This in-depth comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, support, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're a small business owner, freelancer, or marketing team lead, we'll provide practical advice based on real user experiences and official platform data. No hype—just facts to guide your choice.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo is an all-in-one marketing platform emphasizing transactional emails, SMS, and CRM integration. Launched as Sendinblue in 2012, it rebranded to Brevo in 2023. It's ideal for e-commerce stores and agencies needing multi-channel communication.

**Key Strengths:**
- Unlimited contacts on most plans.
- Built-in SMS and WhatsApp marketing.
- Strong automation workflows.

Brevo's free plan allows 300 emails per day with no contact limit, making it generous for startups testing waters.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, founded in 2001, is a veteran in email marketing known for its user-friendly drag-and-drop editor and creative templates. It's great for content-driven campaigns like newsletters.

**Key Strengths:**
- Intuitive interface for beginners.
- Extensive template library.
- Advanced analytics and A/B testing.

The free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends, but scales up quickly for growing lists.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is a major decision factor. Here's a side-by-side look at current plans (as of 2024; always check official sites for updates).

### Brevo Pricing
- **Free:** 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts, basic features.
- **Starter:** $25/mo (20K emails/mo, SMS add-on $10/1K).
- **Business:** $65/mo (20K emails, advanced automation, A/B testing).
- **Enterprise:** Custom (dedicated IP, SSO).

Brevo bills by emails sent, not contacts, which saves money for large lists with low volume.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free:** 500 contacts, 1K sends/mo.
- **Essentials:** $13/mo (5K contacts, 50K sends).
- **Standard:** $20/mo (5K contacts, behavioral targeting).
- **Premium:** $350/mo (10K+ contacts, advanced segmentation).

Mailchimp charges based on contacts, which can get expensive as your list grows.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your monthly sends and list size. Use Brevo if you have 10K+ contacts but send sporadically. Opt for Mailchimp if under 5K contacts with high engagement needs. Test free plans first—import a sample list and simulate campaigns.

## Feature Comparison

Both platforms offer core email tools, but differences shine in specifics.

### Email Builder and Templates
- **Brevo:** Drag-and-drop editor with responsive templates. Fewer pre-built designs but customizable blocks. Good for transactional emails.
- **Mailchimp:** Superior library of 100+ industry-specific templates. Creative Studio for custom graphics.

**Winner:** Mailchimp for visual campaigns; Brevo for functional ones.

### Automation and Workflows

### H3: Brevo Automations
Brevo excels with event-based triggers (e.g., cart abandonment via webhooks). Multi-step journeys include SMS branching. No workflow limits on paid plans.

### H3: Mailchimp Automations
Customer Journey Builder offers branching based on behavior. Includes pop-ups and forms. Limited to 5 active automations on Standard plan.

**Practical Advice:** Map your customer lifecycle. For e-commerce (abandonment, re-engagement), Brevo's flexibility wins. For nurture sequences, Mailchimp's templates speed setup.

### Landing Pages and Forms
- Brevo: Unlimited pages/forms on paid plans, basic customization.
- Mailchimp: High-converting pages with A/B testing, integrates seamlessly.

### SMS and Multi-Channel
Brevo natively supports SMS/WhatsApp (pay-per-use). Mailchimp requires integrations like Twilio.

### Integrations
Both connect to 100+ apps (Shopify, WordPress, Zapier). Brevo has stronger transactional API; Mailchimp leads in creative tools (Canva-like).

**Analytics and Deliverability**
Brevo: 99%+ deliverability, real-time stats. Mailchimp: Detailed reports, predictive insights on Premium.

## Ease of Use

Mailchimp's interface feels like a design tool—perfect for non-techies. Onboarding wizards guide setup.

Brevo is straightforward but more dashboard-heavy, suiting users comfortable with CRMs.

**Practical Advice:** Spend 30 minutes on each free trial. If you're solo, Mailchimp's simplicity saves time. Teams handling sales/CRM prefer Brevo's unified view.

## Customer Support

- **Brevo:** Email/chat 24/7 on paid plans; knowledge base solid.
- **Mailchimp:** Priority support on Standard+; extensive academy/videos.

Both score 4.5+ on G2/Capterra. Brevo edges for multilingual support.

**Practical Advice:** Check response times via live chat during trials. For global teams, Brevo's 24/7 wins.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo
**Pros:**
- Cost-effective for large lists.
- Multi-channel (SMS included).
- Unlimited contacts.

**Cons:**
- Fewer templates.
- Steeper learning for beginners.

### Mailchimp
**Pros:**
- Beginner-friendly.
- Creative tools.
- Strong community.

**Cons:**
- Expensive at scale.
- Contact-based billing.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?
E-commerce brands, agencies, or high-volume senders with 5K+ contacts. Practical tip: Migrate if using transactional emails—Brevo's API is developer-friendly. Export CSV from current tool, import, and remap automations.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
Bloggers, nonprofits, or small teams under 5K contacts focused on engaging newsletters. Tip: Leverage free resources like templates to boost open rates without design skills.

## Migration Tips

Switching platforms? 
1. Export subscriber lists (CSV).
2. Recreate top campaigns.
3. Test automations with small segments.
4. Update DNS for custom domains (both support DKIM/SPF).
5. Monitor deliverability post-switch.

Tools like Zapier ease data transfer.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your scale and channels. Brevo offers better value for growing lists and SMS needs, while Mailchimp shines for ease and creativity in smaller operations. Start with free trials, align features to goals, and scale smartly.

For most mid-sized businesses, Brevo's pricing and versatility make it the 2024 pick. But if design drives your strategy, stick with Mailchimp. Whichever you choose, focus on list hygiene (remove bounces) and value-driven content for 20-30% open rates.
