# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools popular among small businesses, e-commerce stores, and marketers. Mailchimp has long been a household name for its beginner-friendly interface and free tier, while Omnisend shines in omnichannel marketing, especially for online stores. This in-depth comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, support, and more to help you decide based on your needs.

Whether you're a solopreneur starting out or scaling an e-commerce brand, we'll provide practical advice on features, real-world use cases, and when to pick one over the other.

## Quick Overview of Each Platform

### Mailchimp
Mailchimp, launched in 2001, powers over 13 million users worldwide. It's known for its drag-and-drop email builder, vast template library, and robust audience management. Ideal for newsletters, basic automations, and general marketing. It offers a forever-free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends per month.

### Omnisend
Omnisend, founded in 2014, focuses on e-commerce with seamless integrations for Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce. It excels in pre-built workflows for abandoned carts, welcome series, and cross-channel campaigns (email, SMS, web push). Free plan supports up to 250 contacts and 500 emails monthly.

## Pricing Breakdown: Value for Money

Pricing is a key decider. Both offer free tiers, but scales differently as you grow.

### Mailchimp Pricing Tiers (as of 2024)
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month. Basic features, no automation.
- **Essentials**: $13/mo (500 contacts) – Basic automation, A/B testing.
- **Standard**: $20/mo (500 contacts) – Advanced automation, custom branding.
- **Premium**: $350/mo (500 contacts) – Multivariate testing, optimization.

Mailchimp bills based on contacts and sends. Overages add costs quickly for high-volume senders.

### Omnisend Pricing Tiers
- **Free**: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month. Full automation access.
- **Standard**: $16/mo (500 contacts) – Unlimited emails, SMS add-on.
- **Pro**: $59/mo (500 contacts) – Advanced segmentation, A/B testing.
- **Enterprise**: Custom – Dedicated support, high-volume.

**Practical Tip**: For e-commerce with under 500 contacts, Omnisend's free plan gives more value with automations. Mailchimp suits low-volume newsletters better. Calculate your needs: Use Omnisend's pricing calculator for SMS-inclusive costs; Mailchimp for pure email.

## Feature Comparison: Head-to-Head

### Email Templates and Design
Mailchimp offers 100+ responsive templates with a intuitive drag-and-drop builder. Customization is straightforward for non-designers.

Omnisend provides 250+ e-commerce-specific templates, including product blocks that pull dynamic data. Its builder supports conditional content for personalized designs.

**Winner**: Tie – Mailchimp for general use; Omnisend for shops.

### Automation Workflows
Mailchimp's automations (Standard+ plans) include customer journeys, tag-based triggers, and RSS-to-email.

Omnisend dominates with 200+ pre-built workflows: abandoned cart (recovers 20-30% typically), back-in-stock, birthday flows. Visual builder is ecommerce-optimized.

**Practical Advice**: If recovering sales is key, Omnisend's cart abandonment automation integrates directly with your store—no coding needed. Test Mailchimp's basic triggers for lead nurturing.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both segment by behavior, location, purchases. Mailchimp uses tags/merge fields; Omnisend leverages product data for hyper-personalization (e.g., "recommended products").

Omnisend's behavioral segmentation (page views, cart adds) is superior for e-com.

### Integrations
Mailchimp: 300+ (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).
Omnisend: 140+ focused on e-com (Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives, Google Ads). Both support Zapier.

**Tip**: For Shopify users, Omnisend syncs orders/inventory automatically—saves hours vs. Mailchimp's manual imports.

### Omnichannel: SMS and Push Notifications
Mailchimp added SMS recently (Premium), but it's pricey ($10/1,000 credits).

Omnisend bundles SMS/push natively (Standard+), with high open rates (SMS: 98% vs. email 20-30%).

**Pro Tip**: Start SMS with Omnisend for cart recovery—ROI often 5-10x.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp: Revenue tracking, click maps, comparative reports.
Omnisend: E-com ROI calculator, cohort analysis, message impact on sales.

Both integrate Google Analytics.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is polished for beginners—setup in minutes. Guided tours help.

Omnisend's dashboard prioritizes e-com metrics; slight learning curve for non-shop users but intuitive workflows.

**User Feedback**: G2 rates Mailchimp 4.4/5 (ease), Omnisend 4.7/5. Beginners: Mailchimp. E-com pros: Omnisend.

**Advice**: Sign up for free trials. Import a test list and build one automation to feel the difference.

## Customer Support Options

Mailchimp: Email/ticket (paid), chat (higher tiers), vast knowledge base.

Omnisend: 24/7 email/chat (Pro+), live chat (all paid), academy with video tutorials.

**Tip**: Omnisend's support resolves tickets faster (under 2 hours reported); great for urgent campaigns.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- Generous free plan
- Excellent for non-ecom (blogs, agencies)
- Huge template/community resources

**Cons**:
- Automations locked behind paywalls
- Pricing jumps with contacts
- Weaker e-com tracking

### Omnisend
**Pros**:
- E-com powerhouse (cart recovery)
- Omnichannel included
- Better free automation access

**Cons**:
- Smaller free contact limit
- Less general marketing templates
- SMS costs extra

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

**Pick Mailchimp if**:
- You're a blogger, service business, or non-ecom with <1,000 contacts.
- Need simple newsletters and basic CRM.
- Budget for free forever plan.

**Pick Omnisend if**:
- Running an online store (Shopify/Woo).
- Want SMS/push for higher engagement.
- Focus on revenue-driven automations.

**Real-World Scenario**: A dropshipping store with 1,000 orders/mo saw 15% revenue lift switching to Omnisend's workflows. A newsletter author stuck with Mailchimp for its audience insights.

## Practical Migration Tips

Switching platforms? 
1. Export CSV from current tool (contacts, tags).
2. Map segments accurately.
3. Recreate top workflows first.
4. Use Zapier for one-time data sync.
5. A/B test new vs. old campaigns for 2 weeks.

Both support easy imports—no data loss.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for e-commerce with superior automations, omnichannel, and sales focus—perfect if revenue is your goal. Mailchimp remains the go-to for versatile, beginner-friendly email marketing without e-com needs.

Assess your business: E-com scale? Go Omnisend. General marketing? Mailchimp. Both offer free trials—test with your data today. The right choice boosts opens, clicks, and conversions.
