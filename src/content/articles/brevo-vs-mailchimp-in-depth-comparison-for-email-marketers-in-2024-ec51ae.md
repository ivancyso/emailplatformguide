# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses scaling their email marketing. Both platforms offer robust tools for newsletters, automations, and customer engagement, but they cater to different needs. This comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, deliverability, and support to help you decide.

Whether you're a solopreneur, small business, or growing e-commerce store, we'll provide practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for enterprise features like advanced automation and A/B testing.

- **Pros**: Predictable monthly billing; generous free tier for beginners.
- **Cons**: Contacts-based pricing gets expensive quickly (e.g., 10,000 contacts ~$100+/month). No unlimited contacts on lower tiers.

Practical tip: If your list is under 2,000 and you send infrequently, Mailchimp's free plan works well. Upgrade only when needing behavioral automations.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts but caps at 300 emails/day. Paid plans start at $25/month (Starter) for 20,000 emails/month, with higher tiers up to $65/month for 100,000 emails (no contact limits).

- **Pros**: Email-volume based pricing favors growing lists; includes SMS and chat in higher plans.
- **Cons**: Daily send limits on free plan; transactional emails billed separately.

Practical tip: Ideal for high-volume senders like e-commerce with flash sales. Calculate your monthly sends first—Brevo saves money beyond 5,000 contacts.

**Winner**: Brevo for scaling businesses; Mailchimp for small, low-volume lists.

## Features: Core Capabilities Compared

Both platforms excel in email marketing, but feature depth varies.

### Email Builder and Templates

Mailchimp offers a drag-and-drop builder with 100+ responsive templates, mobile-optimized out of the box. Brevo's builder is intuitive with 40+ templates, plus custom HTML options and a multi-channel editor for email + SMS.

Practical advice: Test templates on mobile—Mailchimp's preview tools are superior for quick iterations.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp shines with behavioral triggers (e.g., cart abandonment, purchase series) and single/multiple-step automations on paid plans. Brevo provides similar journeys, plus API-triggered transactional emails and wait nodes for time delays.

- **Mailchimp**: Classic automations free on higher tiers.
- **Brevo**: Unlimited automations even on free plan.

Tip: For e-commerce, Brevo's transactional focus integrates better with Shopify/WooCommerce for post-purchase flows.

### Landing Pages and Forms

Mailchimp includes basic landing pages and pop-up forms. Brevo offers more advanced pages with A/B testing and sign-up forms that convert better via exit-intent tech.

### Analytics and Reporting

Both track opens, clicks, and unsubscribes. Mailchimp's dashboard visualizes revenue per email; Brevo emphasizes deliverability stats and bounce management.

**Winner**: Tie—Mailchimp for creative campaigns, Brevo for transactional + multi-channel.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is beginner-friendly with guided tours and content optimizer AI suggesting subject lines. Brevo feels more technical but has a clean dashboard and quick-setup wizards.

Practical advice for new users:
- Start with Mailchimp if you're non-technical—its templates reduce design time by 50%.
- Choose Brevo if comfortable with APIs; import contacts via CSV in minutes.

Migration tip: Both support imports from each other. Export Mailchimp lists as CSV and map fields in Brevo to avoid data loss.

## Deliverability and Compliance

High deliverability prevents spam folder woes. Mailchimp boasts 99%+ rates with domain authentication tools. Brevo excels here too, with dedicated IPs on premium plans and strong EU compliance (GDPR built-in).

- **Brevo**: Better for international sends; automatic list cleaning.
- **Mailchimp**: Robust suppression lists.

Tip: Always verify domains (DKIM/SPF) on both. Monitor reputation via Google Postmaster Tools alongside platform analytics.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Zapier, WordPress). Brevo supports 300+ too, with strengths in e-commerce (BigCommerce, PrestaShop) and CRM (HubSpot).

For scalability:
- Mailchimp suits content creators.
- Brevo handles high-volume transactional emails.

## Customer Support Options

Mailchimp offers email/ticket support (priority on paid); chat for Premium. Brevo provides 24/7 chat/email, phone for higher tiers, and a knowledge base with video tutorials.

Practical tip: Use Brevo's live chat for quick SMTP setup issues—responses average under 2 minutes.

## Pros and Cons Summary

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- Intuitive for beginners
- Excellent templates and AI tools
- Strong community resources

**Cons**:
- Pricing scales poorly
- Limited free automations

### Brevo
**Pros**:
- Cost-effective for volume
- Multi-channel (SMS, chat)
- Unlimited contacts free

**Cons**:
- Steeper learning for advanced features
- Fewer design templates

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick Mailchimp** if: You're a blogger, agency, or small team prioritizing ease and design. Best for <5,000 contacts.
- **Pick Brevo** if: E-commerce, transactional needs, or high sends. Scales affordably to 50,000+.

Test both free plans: Send a sample campaign to 100 contacts and track metrics.

## Conclusion

Brevo edges out for cost-conscious growers needing transactional power, while Mailchimp wins for user-friendly campaigns. Evaluate based on your send volume, list size, and integrations. In 2024, Brevo's multi-channel edge makes it future-proof for omnichannel marketing.

Ready to switch? Start with Brevo's free unlimited contacts or Mailchimp's essentials. Whichever you choose, focus on list hygiene and personalized content for 20-30% open rate boosts.
