# Omnisend Review 2024: Features, Pricing, Pros & Cons for Ecommerce

If you're running an online store and looking to boost sales through email marketing, you've likely come across Omnisend. This platform specializes in ecommerce, offering tools for email, SMS, and push notifications. In this Omnisend review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, usability, and whether it's the right fit for your business. We'll base everything on real capabilities, user experiences, and practical advice to help you decide.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform designed primarily for ecommerce brands. Launched in 2014, it integrates seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce. Unlike general email tools like Mailchimp, Omnisend focuses on sales-driven campaigns, using customer data to personalize messages across channels.

It's trusted by over 70,000 ecommerce stores, with claims of up to 73% open rates on welcome emails (based on their benchmarks). For audiences choosing email platforms, Omnisend stands out for its no-coding-required automations and ecommerce-specific templates.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs robust features tailored for ecommerce. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Automation Workflows

Omnisend's automation builder is drag-and-drop, making it beginner-friendly. Pre-built workflows include:
- **Welcome series**: Captures new subscribers with segmented emails.
- **Abandoned cart recovery**: Triggers emails/SMS when shoppers leave items behind, with dynamic product blocks.
- **Back-in-stock alerts**: Notifies customers when items restock.

**Practical advice**: Start with the abandoned cart workflow. Connect your store, customize the delay (e.g., 1 hour first email, 24 hours second), and add urgency like "Your cart is waiting!". Test on a small segment first to monitor revenue lift—users report 10-30% recovery rates.

### Omnichannel Messaging

Beyond email, Omnisend supports SMS and web push notifications. Send SMS for high-urgency promos (e.g., flash sales) or push for cart reminders.

**Pro tip**: Use the "All Channels" campaign to let customers choose preferences. Compliance is built-in with opt-in tracking for SMS (TCPA compliant in the US).

### Personalization and Segmentation

Hyper-personalization uses purchase history, behavior, and custom events. Segments include 'VIP customers' (high spenders) or 'lapsed buyers' (no purchase in 90 days).

**How to implement**: In the dashboard, go to Contacts > Segments. Create a segment like "Viewed product but didn't buy" using behavioral data. Practical advice: A/B test personalized subject lines (e.g., "Hey [Name], your favorites are 20% off")—this can boost opens by 20-30%.

### Product Recommendations

AI-powered recs pull from your catalog for upsells/cross-sells. Types: Related products, frequently bought together.

**Advice**: Embed in emails via dynamic blocks. For Shopify users, sync is automatic. Track performance in reports to refine algorithms.

### Popups and Forms

Wheel of Fortune, spin-to-win, or exit-intent popups collect emails. All are mobile-optimized.

**Setup tip**: Place a popup on high-traffic pages like homepage. Set to trigger after 10 seconds, offering 10% off for email signup. Monitor signup rates vs. bounce impact.

### Analytics and Reporting

Detailed reports cover revenue per email, ROI, and subscriber growth. Integrate with Google Analytics for full-funnel tracking.

## Omnisend Pricing

Omnisend uses a freemium model:

| Plan | Price | Contacts | Key Limits |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| Free | $0/mo | Up to 250 | 500 emails/mo, 15 workflows |
| Standard | $16/mo (500 contacts) | Unlimited emails | All features, SMS extra |
| Pro | $59/mo (500 contacts) | Advanced reporting, API access | Custom workflows |

Pricing scales with contacts (e.g., Standard: $59 for 5k contacts). SMS costs ~$0.02/message. No contracts; cancel anytime.

**Practical advice**: Begin with Free to test integrations. Upgrade to Standard once hitting 500 emails/mo. Calculate ROI: If one campaign brings $500 revenue, it pays for itself.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-focused: Deep Shopify/Woo integrations.
- User-friendly interface: No steep learning curve.
- Strong automation: Saves time on repetitive tasks.
- Excellent support: 24/7 chat, knowledge base.
- High deliverability: 99%+ rates reported.

### Cons
- SMS/push add costs: Not included in base price.
- Limited for non-ecommerce: Less B2B features.
- Advanced segmentation needs Pro plan.
- No native landing pages (use integrations).

User reviews (from G2/Capterra): 4.6/5 average, praising ease but noting pricing for large lists.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for:
- Shopify store owners (seamless sync).
- SMBs/DTC brands scaling to $1M+ revenue.
- Marketers wanting omnichannel without complexity.

Not for: Enterprise needing custom dev or non-ecommerce like SaaS.

**Advice for choosers**: If 70%+ traffic is ecommerce, pick Omnisend. Compare trial data: Track open rates, revenue per recipient.

- **Vs. Klaviyo**: Omnisend cheaper for small lists; Klaviyo better for advanced flows.

- **Vs. ActiveCampaign**: Omnisend omnichannel wins; AC stronger CRM.

Test via free trials.

Omnisend earns its spot as a top email marketing tool for ecommerce, with powerful automations, personalization, and omnichannel reach. It's not perfect—costs add up for SMS-heavy users—but delivers real ROI for most stores. Start with the free plan, set up cart abandonment, and scale from there. For those choosing platforms, Omnisend's ecommerce focus makes it a smart, practical choice.
