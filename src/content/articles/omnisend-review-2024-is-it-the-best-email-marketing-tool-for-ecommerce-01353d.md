# Omnisend Review 2024: Features, Pricing, and Real-User Insights

If you're running an online store and struggling to engage customers through email, Omnisend might be the solution. This platform specializes in ecommerce marketing, combining email, SMS, and push notifications into one dashboard. In this Omnisend review, we'll dive into its capabilities, pricing, strengths, weaknesses, and whether it's right for your business. Designed for Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce users, Omnisend emphasizes automation and segmentation to boost conversions.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an omnichannel marketing automation platform launched in 2014, tailored for ecommerce brands. Unlike generalists like Mailchimp, it focuses on sales-driven campaigns. Key differentiators include pre-built ecommerce workflows (e.g., abandoned cart recovery) and seamless integrations with major platforms.

Over 70,000 stores use it, with claims of average revenue increases up to 37% from automations—based on their case studies. It's not just emails; SMS and web push extend reach to mobile shoppers.

**Practical tip:** Start by connecting your store via API or native apps. This auto-syncs customer data, products, and orders, saving hours of manual imports.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs robust tools without overwhelming beginners. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Campaigns and Templates

Drag-and-drop builder with 250+ responsive templates optimized for mobile. Customize with dynamic blocks pulling product images, prices, and recommendations from your store.

**Pro advice:** Use product-based personalization. Segment by purchase history to recommend 'frequently bought together' items—tests show 20-30% open rate lifts.

### Automations

No-code workflows for welcome series, post-purchase upsells, win-back campaigns, and cart abandonment. Triggers include site behavior, tags, and custom events.

**Example setup:**
1. Go to Automations > Create Workflow > Abandoned Checkout.
2. Set delay (e.g., 1 hour) and add SMS fallback if email fails.
3. A/B test subject lines for 15-25% recovery rates.

Hyperbolic claims aside, real users report 10-15% recovery on average with proper segmentation.

### SMS and Push Notifications

Send MMS with product images or links. Costs are pay-per-message (around $0.015/US), but ROI is high for urgency (e.g., 'Your cart expires in 1 hour'). Web push works on browsers without apps.

**Best practice:** Limit SMS to high-intent moments like abandoned carts to avoid opt-outs. Compliance tools handle TCPA/CTIA rules.

### Popups, Forms, and Landing Pages

Wheel of fortune, spin-to-win, or exit-intent popups capture emails with 20-40% conversion rates. Embed forms on thank-you pages.

**Tip:** A/B test popup timing and incentives. Offer 10% off for emails vs. 15% for SMS signups to prioritize high-value leads.

### Segmentation and List Management

Advanced segments based on behavior (page views, purchases), RFM (recency, frequency, monetary), or custom attributes. No contact limits on segments.

**Practical segmentation example:**
- VIPs: Spent >$100 last 30 days.
- Lapsed: No purchase in 90 days, opened last email.

This drives targeted sends, reducing unsubscribes by 50% per user reports.

### Reporting and Analytics

Track revenue per email/SMS, ROI, and attribution across channels. Heatmaps show click hotspots.

**Insight:** Use the Revenue Chart to justify budgets—link campaigns to tracked sales.

## Omnisend Pricing Plans

Transparent tiers billed monthly/annually (20% discount yearly):

| Plan | Price (500 contacts) | Key Limits |
|------|----------------------|------------|
| Free | $0 | 250 contacts, 500 emails/mo, no SMS/automations |
| Standard | $16/mo | Unlimited emails, 6 automations, basic reporting |
| Pro | $59/mo | Unlimited everything, advanced segmentation, API access |

SMS/push extra: ~$0.015/message. Scales by contacts (e.g., Standard 10k: $59).

**Advice for choosers:** Free plan tests basics. Upgrade to Standard for automations if under 2k contacts. Pro suits scaling stores needing custom events.

No contracts; 14-day trial on paid plans.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-first: Deep store integrations auto-sync data.
- Omnichannel: Email + SMS boosts open rates (SMS: 98% vs. email 20%).
- User-friendly: Intuitive UI, quick setup (under 30 mins).
- Strong support: 24/7 chat, knowledge base, onboarding calls.
- ROI-focused: Built-in revenue tracking.

### Cons
- SMS costs add up for high-volume.
- Free plan limited (no automations).
- Reporting depth lags Klaviyo in Pro.
- No native Facebook/Instagram ads integration.

User reviews (G2/Capterra: 4.6/5) praise ease but note learning curve for advanced segments.

## Who Should Use Omnisend?

Ideal for:
- Shopify/WooCommerce stores (500-50k contacts).
- Brands prioritizing automation over design flair.
- SMBs wanting email+SMS without agencies.

Skip if: Heavy enterprise needs (try Klaviyo/ActiveCampaign) or budget under $20/mo.

**Comparison snippet:** Vs. Mailchimp: Omnisend wins on ecommerce automations (no RFM in free Mailchimp). Vs. Klaviyo: Cheaper entry, but less Shopify-native flows.

## Getting Started: Step-by-Step Guide

1. **Sign up:** Free account, connect store.
2. **Import contacts:** Auto or CSV (hygiene tools clean lists).
3. **Build first automation:** Welcome series—email 1: Brand story; email 2: 10% off.
4. **Launch popup:** High-traffic pages first.
5. **Analyze:** Check dashboard weekly, tweak based on revenue.

**Pro tip:** Enable double opt-in for compliance and quality lists. Test on 10% segments before full sends.
