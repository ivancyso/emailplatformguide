# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business or content creation efforts. With so many options, pitting MailerLite against ConvertKit makes sense for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams. Both platforms offer powerful features for building audiences, sending newsletters, and driving sales—but they cater to slightly different needs.

MailerLite emphasizes affordability and simplicity, ideal for beginners scaling up. ConvertKit, built for creators, shines in advanced automations and monetization. In this head-to-head comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, usability, and more with practical advice to help you decide.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### MailerLite Pricing

MailerLite keeps it straightforward and budget-friendly:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month. Includes basic automation, forms, and landing pages—perfect for testing.
- **Growing Business Plan**: Starts at $10/month (billed monthly) for up to 500 subscribers. Scales to $20 for 1,000, $30 for 2,500, etc. Annual billing saves 10%.
- **Advanced Plan**: From $21/month for 500 subs, adding A/B testing, custom HTML, and priority support.

**Practical Tip**: If you're under 1,000 subs, stick to free. Upgrade only when hitting limits—many users report excellent deliverability even on free.

### ConvertKit Pricing

ConvertKit focuses on creators with tiered plans:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 1,000 subscribers (unlimited emails). Basic forms, broadcasts, and segments.
- **Creator Plan**: Starts at $15/month for 300-1,000 subs, rising to $29 for 1,000-3,000. Includes unlimited automations and landing pages.
- **Creator Pro**: From $25/month for 300 subs, adding advanced reporting, one-click upsells, and subscriber scoring.

**Practical Tip**: ConvertKit's free plan suits growing lists well, but costs jump faster for larger audiences. Calculate based on your projected growth—use their pricing calculator.

**Winner**: MailerLite for pure affordability, especially post-1,000 subs. ConvertKit if you need pro features early.

## Ease of Use: Beginner-Friendly Interfaces

Both platforms prioritize intuitive designs, but let's dive deeper.

### MailerLite's Drag-and-Drop Simplicity

MailerLite's editor is a standout: classic or drag-and-drop builders with photo blocks, columns, and dynamic content. No coding needed. Dashboard is clean, with quick access to campaigns, automations, and sites.

**Practical Advice**: Import contacts via CSV in minutes. Use pre-built templates for newsletters—customize colors and fonts easily. Ideal for non-tech users; setup takes under 30 minutes.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Centric Dashboard

ConvertKit uses a visual automation builder and clean email composer. Tagging system simplifies segmentation. It's straightforward but assumes some familiarity with funnels.

**Practical Advice**: Start with their setup wizard for broadcasts. The inbox preview ensures mobile optimization. Great for Apple users with native integrations.

**Winner**: Tie—MailerLite edges for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for those comfortable with workflows.

## Core Features Breakdown

### Email Builder and Templates

MailerLite: 80+ responsive templates, RSS-to-email, and photo-focused designs. Embed videos easily.

ConvertKit: Simpler builder with blocks for text, images, buttons. Strong for text-heavy newsletters like Substack-style.

**Tip**: Test deliverability—both score high (MailerLite ~99%, ConvertKit similar via SendGrid).

### Automations and Workflows

#### MailerLite Automations
Workflows trigger on tags, opens, clicks. Includes welcome series, abandoned cart. Visual editor connects actions easily.

**Practical Example**: Set a 5-email nurture sequence for new subs—tag 'lead', send day 1 intro, day 3 value, day 7 offer.

#### ConvertKit Sequences
Excels here: Pre-built sequences for courses, launches. Tag-based triggers power sophisticated funnels.

**Practical Example**: Automate a book launch—tag buyers, upsell audiobook instantly.

**Winner**: ConvertKit for complex automations.

### Segmentation and List Management

MailerLite: Groups, tags, custom fields. Behavior-based segments (e.g., opened last 30 days).

ConvertKit: Tag-anything model—subscribers get multiple tags for hyper-personalization.

**Tip**: Clean lists regularly; both support re-engagement campaigns to boost opens.

### Landing Pages and Forms

Both offer popups, forms, and sites:
- MailerLite: Unlimited landing pages on paid, A/B testing.
- ConvertKit: Native pages with checkouts, better for selling digital products.

**Practical Advice**: Use MailerLite for lead magnets (freebies); ConvertKit for course sales pages.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

MailerLite: 1,000+ via Zapier, native WordPress, Google Analytics, Stripe.

ConvertKit: Deep creator integrations—Teachable, Gumroad, Patreon, WooCommerce.

**Tip**: If on Shopify, both work; ConvertKit wins for podcast tools like Transistor.

## Analytics and Reporting

MailerLite: Open rates, clicks, revenue tracking, heatmap.

ConvertKit: Subscriber growth, revenue per sub, A/B insights on Pro.

**Practical Tip**: Track ROI—link UTM parameters to Google Analytics for full-funnel view.

## Customer Support and Resources

MailerLite: 24/7 chat, email, extensive academy, migration service.

ConvertKit: Email support (chat on higher plans), creator-focused guides, podcast.

Both have high satisfaction (4.8+ on G2/Capterra).

- Budget-conscious small businesses
- Bloggers needing simple newsletters/popups
- Agencies managing multiple clients (white-label)

**Advice**: Start free, build to 1k subs, then scale affordably.

## Who Should Choose ConvertKit?

- Course creators, podcasters, authors
- Those prioritizing automations and sales
- Apple ecosystem users

**Advice**: If revenue >$5k/mo from email, invest here.

## Final Verdict and Next Steps

Neither is 'best' universally—MailerLite wins on cost and simplicity (great for 80% of users starting out). ConvertKit dominates for serious creators monetizing audiences.
