# Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2023: Top 8 Picks for Email Marketing Success

If you're searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**, you're not alone. Mailchimp has been a staple in email marketing for years, but recent pricing changes, limited free plans, and feature gaps have many users looking elsewhere. With costs rising—especially for larger lists—and automation options feeling basic compared to competitors, businesses, creators, and marketers are exploring better fits.

This roundup focuses on proven **Mailchimp alternatives** tailored for audiences choosing email platforms. We'll cover key features, pricing, pros/cons, and practical advice to help you switch smoothly. Whether you need advanced automations, e-commerce focus, or creator-friendly tools, there's an option here. All info is based on official sites and user reports as of 2023.

## Why Consider Mailchimp Alternatives?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 subscribers and 1,000 emails/month, with paid plans starting at $13/month for Essentials. Advanced features like deep automations require the $76/month Standard plan. Users often cite:

- **Pricing hikes**: Legacy plans ended, forcing upgrades.
- **Limited segmentation**: Basic compared to rivals.
- **Automation constraints**: No multi-branch journeys without premium tiers.
- **Deliverability issues**: Some report higher spam rates.

Switching can save 20-50% on costs while unlocking better tools. For example, small businesses might prefer unlimited sends, while e-com stores need sales-focused automations.

Practical tip: Audit your current Mailchimp usage. Export lists via CSV, check integrations (e.g., Shopify, WordPress), and prioritize needs like A/B testing or CRM.

## Top 8 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. ActiveCampaign

**Best for**: Advanced automations and CRM integration.

ActiveCampaign shines with site tracking, lead scoring, and machine learning predictions. Build complex customer journeys with if/then branches—far beyond Mailchimp's basics.

**Pricing**: Lite ($9/month, 500 contacts), Plus ($49), Professional ($149). 14-day free trial, no free plan.

**Pros**:
- 900+ integrations (e.g., Shopify, Salesforce).
- High deliverability (95%+).
- Built-in CRM.

**Cons**:
- Steep learning curve.
- No forever-free tier.

**Who it's for**: Agencies or e-com with 1K+ subscribers. Migrate by mapping automations 1:1; expect 2-4 hours setup.

### 2. ConvertKit (now Kit)

**Best for**: Creators, bloggers, and newsletters.

ConvertKit focuses on simplicity with visual automations, tagging, and subscriber segments. Native commerce via Gumroad integration.

**Pricing**: Free (up to 300 subscribers), Creator ($29/month, 1K subs), Creator Pro ($59). Unlimited emails.

**Pros**:
- Creator-friendly forms/landing pages.
- One-click upsells.
- Excellent support.

**Cons**:
- Less robust for large teams.
- E-com features basic.

Practical advice: Use for newsletters—import Mailchimp list, recreate sequences. Great for solopreneurs; scales to 10K subs affordably.

### 3. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for**: SMBs wanting all-in-one marketing.

Brevo offers transactional emails, SMS, chat, and CRM alongside marketing automation. Unlimited contacts on paid plans.

**Pricing**: Free (300 emails/day), Starter ($25/month), Business ($65). Pay-as-you-go SMS.

**Pros**:
- Generous free tier.
- Multichannel (email + SMS).
- EU-based for GDPR ease.

**Cons**:
- Interface dated.
- Daily send limits on free.

**Tip**: Ideal Mailchimp swap for transactional needs. Test deliverability with their inbox preview tool.

### 4. Klaviyo

**Best for**: E-commerce and Shopify users.

Klaviyo excels in revenue-focused flows like abandoned cart (averages 15% recovery). Predictive analytics and dynamic product recommendations.

**Pricing**: Free (250 contacts, 500 sends), up to $20K/month revenue tiers.

**Pros**:
- Shopify native.
- A/B testing on steroids.
- ROI tracking.

**Cons**:
- E-com only (no general newsletters).
- Expensive at scale.

Practical: Connect Shopify, auto-sync lists. Users report 30% revenue lift post-Mailchimp migration.

### 5. GetResponse

**Best for**: Webinars and sales funnels.

Features unlimited emails, autoresponders, and built-in landing pages. AI email generator and conversion funnels.

**Pricing**: Free (500 subs), Email Marketing ($19/month), Marketing Automation ($59).

**Pros**:
- Webinar tool included.
- Strong A/B testing.
- 99% deliverability.

**Cons**:
- Overwhelming dashboard.
- Upsells during signup.

**Advice**: Use for lead gen. Export Mailchimp campaigns, rebuild in funnels—quick win for courses.

### 6. AWeber

**Best for**: Beginners and small lists.

Veteran platform with drag-and-drop editor, tagging, and RSS-to-email. Loyal user base since 1998.

**Pricing**: Free (500 subs), Lite ($19/month), Unlimited ($149).

**Pros**:
- Simple interface.
- 24/7 support.
- E-com add-ons.

**Cons**:
- Automations basic.
- Fewer integrations.

**Tip**: Perfect Mailchimp free plan upgrade. One-click import preserves opens/clicks.

### 7. Moosend

**Best for**: Affordable automations.

Unlimited emails, advanced segmentation, and page builder. E-com automations rival Klaviyo at lower cost.

**Pricing**: Free (1K subs), Pro ($9/month), Enterprise (custom).

**Pros**:
- Unlimited sends.
- Heatmaps/A/B.
- White-label option.

**Cons**:
- Newer player.
- Support email-only on free.

Practical: Budget pick—migrate lists, test cart recovery flows.

### 8. Drip

**Best for**: Advanced e-com personalization.

Customer journey builder with workflows, revenue tracking, and 500+ integrations.

**Pricing**: Starts $39/month (500 subs). 14-day trial.

**Pros**:
- Deep segmentation.
- Visual builder.
- High ROI tools.

**Cons**:
- Pricey for starters.
- Complex setup.

**Who**: DTC brands. Focus on zero-party data collection post-migration.

## How to Choose the Right Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Budget**: Free tiers? (Brevo, ConvertKit). Under $20? (ActiveCampaign Lite, Moosend).
2. **List Size**: Unlimited contacts? (Brevo, Moosend).
3. **Features**: Automations (ActiveCampaign), e-com (Klaviyo), creators (ConvertKit).
4. **Integrations**: Check Zapier/App listings.
5. **Migration**: All support CSV import. Use tools like PieSync for sync. Test sends first.

**Practical Steps**:
- Sign up for trials (most 14 days).
- Import 10% list subset.
- Recreate 2-3 top campaigns.
- Monitor deliverability (use GlockApps).
- Train team via docs/videos.

Expect 1-2 weeks full switch; notify subscribers of change.
