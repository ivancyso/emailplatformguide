# Omnisend Review 2023: Features, Pricing, Pros & Cons for Ecommerce Stores

If you're running an ecommerce store and looking for a robust email marketing platform, you've likely come across **Omnisend**. This Omnisend review dives deep into its capabilities, helping you decide if it's the right fit for your business. Designed specifically for ecommerce, Omnisend stands out with its omnichannel approach, combining email, SMS, and web push notifications into one platform.

In this review, we'll cover everything from core features and pricing to real-world use cases and alternatives. Whether you're migrating from Mailchimp or starting fresh, read on for practical insights.

## What is Omnisend?

Omnisend is an ecommerce-focused marketing automation platform launched in 2014. It caters to online stores by integrating seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce. Unlike general-purpose tools, Omnisend emphasizes omnichannel campaigns—email, SMS, and push notifications—to drive revenue.

Key differentiators include pre-built workflows for abandoned carts, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells. It serves over 70,000 brands, with a focus on mid-sized ecommerce businesses generating $1M+ in revenue.

**Practical tip**: Start by connecting your store via a one-click integration. Omnisend pulls customer data automatically, segmenting lists based on purchase history without manual CSV uploads.

## Key Features of Omnisend

Omnisend packs a punch in features tailored for ecommerce growth. Here's a breakdown:

### Email Automation and Workflows

Omnisend's automation builder is drag-and-drop simple yet powerful. Pre-built templates handle common scenarios:
- **Abandoned cart recovery**: Triggers emails/SMS 1-3 hours after cart abandonment, recovering up to 15-20% of lost sales (based on industry benchmarks).
- **Welcome series**: Nurtures new subscribers with personalized content.
- **Win-back campaigns**: Re-engages inactive customers.

**Practical advice**: Customize triggers using behavioral data like 'viewed product' or 'browsed category'. Test A/B subject lines—Omnisend reports show 20-30% open rate lifts from personalization like {{first_name}}.

### Omnichannel Marketing (SMS & Push)

Go beyond email with SMS and web push:
- **SMS campaigns**: High open rates (98% average). Use for flash sales or shipping updates.
- **Web push notifications**: Opt-in via browser, ideal for cart reminders without email fatigue.

Integrate all channels in one workflow: Email first, SMS follow-up if no response. **Pro tip**: Comply with TCPA regulations by getting explicit opt-ins and including unsubscribe links.

### Segmentation and Personalization

Dynamic segments based on:
- Purchase history
- Behavior (page views, clicks)
- Demographics

Use Product Picker to insert dynamic product recommendations. **Advice**: Segment 'VIP customers' (top 20% spenders) for exclusive offers—increase LTV by 25% per Omnisend case studies.

### Reporting and Analytics

Track revenue per email, ROI, and attribution. Custom dashboards show campaign performance across channels.

**Practical tip**: Set up revenue tracking with UTM parameters. Focus on 'Revenue per Recipient' metric to justify ad spend.

### Integrations

Native apps for 140+ tools: Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives, Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel. API access for custom needs.

## Pros and Cons of Omnisend

### Pros
- Ecommerce-centric: Superior product recommendations and revenue tracking.
- Omnichannel: Boosts engagement (SMS open rates crush email).
- User-friendly: Intuitive editor, no coding needed.
- Strong support: 24/7 chat, onboarding calls.
- High deliverability: 95%+ inbox rates.

### Cons
- Limited free plan: Scales poorly beyond 250 contacts.
- SMS pricing adds up for high-volume.
- No native landing pages (use integrations).
- Steeper learning for non-ecommerce users.

**Advice**: If SMS is core, budget accordingly. For pure email, it might overlap with cheaper tools.
