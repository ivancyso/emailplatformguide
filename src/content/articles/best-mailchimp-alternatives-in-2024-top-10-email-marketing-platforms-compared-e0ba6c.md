# Best Mailchimp Alternatives in 2024: Top 10 Email Marketing Platforms Compared

Mailchimp has long been a go-to for email marketing, but its recent pricing changes, limited automation for free users, and steeper costs for growing lists have many users searching for **Mailchimp alternatives**. If you're a small business owner, freelancer, or marketer tired of escalating fees or basic features, this guide compares the best options.

We'll break down 10 solid alternatives based on real user needs: ease of use, automation capabilities, pricing, deliverability, and integrations. Whether you need advanced segmentation, e-commerce focus, or budget-friendly plans, there's something here. All recommendations draw from official pricing pages and common user feedback as of 2024—no hype, just facts.

## Why Switch from Mailchimp?

Mailchimp's free plan caps at 500 contacts with basic features, and paid plans start at $13/month but jump quickly (e.g., $75+ for 5,000 contacts). Users often complain about poor deliverability for cold lists and rigid templates. Alternatives offer:

- **Better pricing transparency**: Flat rates or pay-per-contact models.
- **Advanced automations**: Without needing enterprise tiers.
- **Superior analytics**: Revenue tracking and A/B testing.
- **Easier migrations**: CSV imports and template transfers.

Practical tip: Before switching, export your Mailchimp audience via CSV, test deliverability with a small segment, and check integration compatibility (e.g., Shopify, WordPress).

## Top 10 Mailchimp Alternatives

### 1. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)
Brevo stands out for transactional emails alongside marketing. Pricing starts free (300 emails/day), then $25/month for 20,000 emails.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts.
- Drag-and-drop editor like Mailchimp.
- SMS and chat integrations.

**Pros**: High deliverability (98%+ claimed), affordable scaling.
**Cons**: Automation workflows can feel clunky for beginners.

Best for: E-commerce stores sending order confirmations. Migrate by mapping custom fields directly.

### 2. ActiveCampaign
A powerhouse for automation. Starts at $29/month for 500 contacts.

**Key Features**:
- Machine learning for split testing.
- 700+ integrations (e.g., Salesforce).
- Site tracking for behavioral emails.

**Pros**: Deep CRM features beat Mailchimp's basics.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

Practical advice: Use conditional content for personalized campaigns—e.g., "If opened last email, show discount."

### 3. ConvertKit (now Kit)
Creator-focused, free up to 300 subscribers, then $29/month.

**Key Features**n- Visual automations.
- Tagging over lists.
- Native landing pages.

**Pros**: Simple for newsletters.
**Cons**: Limited reporting.

Ideal for bloggers. Tip: Segment by tag behavior for re-engagement sequences.

### 4. GetResponse
All-in-one with webinars. Free trial, plans from $19/month (1,000 contacts).

**Key Features**:
- Conversion funnels.
- E-commerce tools.
- A/B testing.

**Pros**: Built-in webinar hosting.
**Cons**: Interface feels dated.

Switch if you host events—import lists and recreate funnels in hours.

### 5. Klaviyo
E-commerce specialist, free up to 250 contacts/$0 revenue, then revenue-based (e.g., 2-3% of tracked sales).

**Key Features**:
- Predictive analytics.
- Flow builder for abandoned carts.
- Shopify native.

**Pros**: ROI-focused metrics.
**Cons**: Not for non-ecom.

Pro tip: Install pixel tracking first for accurate revenue attribution.

### 6. AWeber
Veteran tool, free up to 500 subscribers, $19/month after.

**Key Features**:
- RSS-to-email.
- Landing page builder.
- Strong support.

**Pros**: Reliable deliverability.
**Cons**: Fewer modern automations.

Great for solopreneurs—easy tag-based segmentation.

### 7. Constant Contact
User-friendly for non-techies. $12/month starter (500 contacts).

**Key Features**:
- Event management.
- Social ads integration.

**Pros**: AI content generator.
**Cons**: Pricey for large lists.

Advice: Use their migration service for seamless transfer.

### 8. MailerLite
Budget king, free to 1,000 subscribers, $10/month after.

**Key Features**:
- Pop-up forms.
- Automation blocks.
- Website builder.

**Pros**: Intuitive editor.
**Cons**: Basic analytics.

Perfect starter alternative—test with free plan before committing.

### 9. Omnisend
Omnichannel for e-com. Free to 250 contacts, $16/month pro.

**Key Features**:
- SMS/push notifications.
- Product pickers in emails.

**Pros**: Unified inbox.
**Cons**: E-com only focus.

Tip: Layer SMS on email flows for 30%+ open boosts.

### 10. Drip
Advanced for DTC brands. Starts at $39/month (500 contacts).

**Key Features**:
- Workflow timelines.
- Customer data platform.

**Pros**: Granular scoring.
**Cons**: Complex setup.

For growth hackers—use UTM tracking for precise attribution.

## How to Choose and Migrate to a Mailchimp Alternative

1. **Assess Needs**: List size? E-com? Automations? Match to "best for" above.
2. **Trial Everything**: Most offer 14-30 day trials—import a test list.
3. **Check Deliverability**: Use tools like Mail-Tester.com post-migration.
4. **Pricing Calc**: Factor contacts + emails sent. E.g., Brevo saves 50%+ on volume.
5. **Integrations**: Zapier covers gaps, but native is best.
6. **Migration Steps**:
   - Export Mailchimp CSV.
   - Map fields (name, email, tags).
   - Recreate top campaigns.
   - Update signup forms/links.
   - Monitor first sends for bounces.

Common pitfall: Forgetting custom domains—reauthenticate SPF/DKIM.
