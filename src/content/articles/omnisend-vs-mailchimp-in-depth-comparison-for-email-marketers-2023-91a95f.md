# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can be tough when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp is a veteran in the space, known for its beginner-friendly interface and broad appeal. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines for ecommerce businesses with its omnichannel capabilities like SMS and push notifications.

In this comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide. Whether you're a small business owner or scaling an online store, this guide provides practical advice based on real user experiences and platform capabilities.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp has been around since 2001 and powers millions of campaigns. It's ideal for solopreneurs, bloggers, and small teams starting with email marketing.

**Key Strengths:**
- Intuitive drag-and-drop editor for emails.
- Free plan for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month.
- Built-in audience insights and A/B testing.

**Best For:** General marketing, newsletters, and basic automations. If you're new to email, Mailchimp's templates and tutorials make onboarding smooth.

Practical tip: Start with their free plan to test basic journeys like welcome series. Upgrade only when you hit limits.

## Overview of Omnisend

Launched more recently, Omnisend focuses on ecommerce. It integrates email, SMS, and web push in one dashboard, making it perfect for online stores.

**Key Strengths:**
- Advanced ecommerce automations (e.g., abandoned cart recovery).
- Free plan for up to 250 contacts with unlimited emails.
- Pop-up builder for list growth.

**Best For:** Shopify or WooCommerce users needing multichannel marketing. Omnisend's pre-built workflows save time for cart abandonment and post-purchase upsells.

Practical tip: Use their 1-click integrations with platforms like Shopify to sync customer data automatically—no manual imports needed.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major factor. Here's a side-by-side (billed annually, as of 2023):

| Feature/Plan | Mailchimp | Omnisend |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| Free | Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/mo | Up to 250 contacts, unlimited emails/SMS credits |
| Essentials/Standard | $13/mo (500 contacts) | $16/mo (500 contacts) |
| Premium/Pro | $350+/mo (50k+ contacts) | $59/mo (10k contacts), scales to $142/mo (50k) |

Mailchimp's free tier is more generous for sends, but Omnisend offers unlimited emails on free—great for low-volume ecommerce. Omnisend is often cheaper at scale for high-volume senders due to no overage fees on emails.

**Practical Advice:** Calculate your monthly sends. If under 1,000, Mailchimp free works. For ecommerce with SMS, Omnisend's pay-per-use SMS (from $0.01/msg) adds value without commitment.

## Feature Comparison

### Automation Workflows
Mailchimp offers solid automations like tag-based triggers and customer journeys. Omnisend excels with ecommerce-specific ones: abandoned cart (recovers 10-20% typically), back-in-stock alerts, and cross-sells.

**Winner:** Omnisend for stores; Mailchimp for general use.

Tip: In Omnisend, set up a cart abandonment flow in 5 minutes—add product images dynamically for higher opens.

### Email Templates and Design
Both have drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp's library is vast (hundreds of themes). Omnisend's are ecommerce-optimized with product blocks.

**Practical Advice:** Test mobile responsiveness. Omnisend auto-optimizes for AMP emails, boosting deliverability.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Mailchimp uses tags and segments based on behavior. Omnisend adds product-based segmentation (e.g., "viewed shoes but bought bag").

Tip: Use Omnisend's dynamic discount codes in segments for personalized offers—track ROI easily.

### Multichannel Marketing
Omnisend supports SMS, push notifications, and Facebook Messenger. Mailchimp focuses on email with add-ons for SMS (via Intuit).

**Winner:** Omnisend for omnichannel.

## Ease of Use: Which is More Beginner-Friendly?

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners—its interface feels like a simple app. Omnisend has a slight learning curve for multichannel but includes video guides.

**Practical Advice:** Spend 30 minutes in each free trial. Mailchimp for quick newsletters; Omnisend if you sell products online.

## Integrations and Apps

Mailchimp: 300+ integrations (Google Analytics, WordPress, Zapier).
Omnisend: Deep ecommerce ties (Shopify, BigCommerce, Klaviyo alternatives) plus Zapier.

Tip: If on Shopify, Omnisend's native app syncs orders real-time—set up in-app.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for paid; self-serve for free.
Omnisend: 24/7 live chat, priority for paid, extensive knowledge base.

**Practical Advice:** Test support during trial. Omnisend's chat resolves automation issues faster.

## Pros and Cons

**Mailchimp Pros:**
- Established brand, reliable deliverability.
- Great analytics dashboard.

**Cons:**
- Expensive at scale.
- Limited ecommerce automations.

**Omnisend Pros:**
- Omnichannel powerhouse.
- Cost-effective for growing lists.

**Cons:**
- Smaller template library.
- SMS costs add up.

## Which One Should You Choose?

- **Choose Mailchimp if:** You're a blogger, service business, or beginner needing simple emails. Free plan scales well initially.
- **Choose Omnisend if:** You run an ecommerce store. Multichannel + automations drive revenue (e.g., 15-30% recovery on carts).

Practical decision framework:
1. List size <1k? Try free tiers.
2. Ecommerce? Omnisend.
3. Budget tight at scale? Compare quotes.
4. Migrate data via CSV/Zapier.

## Conclusion

Omnisend edges out for ecommerce due to specialized features, while Mailchimp remains a safe, versatile choice. Test both free plans to see what fits your workflow. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and conversions post-launch.
