# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp are two leading tools, each with strengths that appeal to different users. This Brevo vs Mailchimp guide breaks down pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide.

Whether you're a small business owner, freelancer, or marketer scaling campaigns, understanding these platforms' differences is key. We'll provide practical advice based on real-world use cases, without hype.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free tiers, but their structures differ significantly.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Paid plans start at $13/month (Essentials) for 500 contacts, scaling to $350+/month for enterprise features like advanced automation and unlimited sends.

Key tiers:
- **Essentials**: Basic automation, landing pages.
- **Standard**: Behavioral targeting, custom reports.
- **Premium**: A/B testing, multivariate testing.

Overages for extra contacts can add up quickly. For example, 5,000 contacts on Standard costs around $75/month. Mailchimp bills monthly, with annual discounts up to 15%.

**Practical Tip**: If you're under 500 contacts, start free. Monitor send volume closely to avoid surprise fees.

### Brevo Pricing

Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts but caps at 300 daily emails. Paid plans begin at $25/month (Starter) for 20,000 monthly emails, with no contact limits on higher tiers.

Key tiers:
- **Starter**: Basic automation, transactional emails.
- **Business**: Advanced segmentation, SMS, retargeting ads.
- **Enterprise**: Custom solutions, dedicated IP.

Brevo shines for growing lists—pay based on emails sent, not contacts. A 50,000-email plan is $65/month. Annual billing saves 10-20%.

**Practical Tip**: Ideal for high-volume senders with large lists. Use the free plan to test deliverability before upgrading.

**Winner**: Brevo for scalability; Mailchimp for tiny lists.

## Features: Automation, Templates, and Integrations

Both platforms excel in core email marketing, but nuances matter.

### Automation and Workflows

Mailchimp offers robust single-step and multi-step automations, like welcome series or abandoned cart emails. Premium plans unlock customer journey builder.

Brevo provides similar automations plus transactional emails (e.g., order confirmations) integrated seamlessly. Its workflow builder supports webhooks and API triggers.

**Practical Advice**: For e-commerce, Brevo's transactional focus reduces tool sprawl. Test automations with small segments first to refine triggers.

### Email Templates and Design

Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, with 100+ responsive templates. Code your own HTML if needed.

Brevo's editor matches this, emphasizing mobile-first designs. It includes multipurpose templates and a template marketplace.

**Tip**: Customize templates with your branding. Use A/B testing (available on mid-tier plans for both) to optimize open rates—aim for subject lines under 50 characters.

### Segmentation and Analytics

Mailchimp segments by behavior, tags, and purchases. Reports cover opens, clicks, revenue.

Brevo segments dynamically (e.g., by engagement score) and tracks SMS/WhatsApp too. Analytics include heatmaps.

**Practical Advice**: Build segments like "inactive subscribers last 90 days" to re-engage. Track ROI by attributing revenue to campaigns.

### Integrations

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Shopify, Google Analytics, Zapier).

Brevo integrates with 300+ (WooCommerce, WordPress, HubSpot) and offers native CRM.

**Tip**: Prioritize platforms you already use. Zapier bridges gaps for custom workflows.

**Winner**: Tie—Mailchimp for design polish, Brevo for multichannel.

## Ease of Use and Onboarding

Mailchimp's interface is beginner-friendly, with guided setup wizards and extensive tutorials.

Brevo is clean and straightforward, with a slight learning curve for advanced features like API.

**Practical Advice**: Spend 30 minutes on demos. Import contacts via CSV—clean data first (remove duplicates using built-in tools). Set up double opt-in to comply with GDPR/CAN-SPAM.

Both score high on mobile apps for on-the-go management.

## Deliverability and Compliance

Deliverability impacts inbox placement. Mailchimp boasts 99%+ rates, aided by authentication tools (DKIM, SPF).

Brevo, EU-based, excels here too, with dedicated IPs on enterprise plans. Strong for international sends.

**Practical Tip**: Warm up new domains gradually (start with 50-100 sends/day). Monitor bounce rates—keep under 2%. Use both's compliance checklists for legal sends.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp: Email/ticket for paid users; 24/7 chat on higher plans.

Brevo: Live chat, email, phone (business+); extensive knowledge base.

**Tip**: Free users rely on docs—forums like Reddit's r/MailChimp help too.

## Pros and Cons

### Mailchimp Pros
- Polished UI
- Strong free plan
- Excellent templates

Cons:
- Contact-based pricing
- Costly for large lists

### Brevo Pros
- Unlimited contacts
- Transactional + marketing
- Multichannel (SMS, chat)

Cons:
- Daily email limit on free
- Fewer design templates

## Who Should Choose What?

- **Pick Mailchimp** if: Small teams, design-focused, under 5,000 contacts, need quick setup.
- **Pick Brevo** if: Growing lists, e-commerce, budget-conscious, multichannel needs.

**Practical Migration Advice**: Export lists from one (CSV), import to the other. Recreate automations step-by-step. Run parallel campaigns for 1-2 weeks to compare performance.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your scale and needs. Mailchimp suits creative beginners; Brevo empowers scalable operations with better long-term value. Test free plans—track metrics like open rates (target 20-30%) and conversions.

For most growing businesses, Brevo edges out due to flexible pricing. Whichever you choose, focus on list hygiene, valuable content, and consistent sending. Start today and watch engagement soar.
