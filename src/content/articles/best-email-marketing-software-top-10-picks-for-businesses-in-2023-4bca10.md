# Best Email Marketing Software: Top 10 Picks for Businesses in 2023

Choosing the right email marketing software is crucial for businesses looking to nurture leads, boost sales, and engage customers effectively. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to pick the best email marketing software for your needs. This roundup reviews the top 10 platforms based on real user feedback, key features, pricing, and scalability. Whether you're a solopreneur, small business, or enterprise, we'll help you decide.

## What Makes the Best Email Marketing Software?

Before diving into the list, understand the essential criteria for evaluating email marketing tools:

### Key Features to Prioritize
- **Automation Workflows**: Drag-and-drop builders for welcome series, abandoned cart emails, and re-engagement campaigns.
- **Deliverability**: High inbox placement rates, SPF/DKIM support, and dedicated IP options.
- **Templates and Design**: Mobile-responsive templates with customization options.
- **Analytics**: Open rates, click-through rates, revenue tracking, and A/B testing.
- **Integrations**: Seamless connections to CRM like HubSpot, e-commerce like Shopify, and analytics like Google Analytics.
- **List Management**: Segmentation, tagging, and compliance with GDPR/CAN-SPAM.

### Pricing Considerations
Look for free tiers for startups, scalable plans, and pay-as-you-grow models. Avoid hidden fees for extra contacts or sends.

### Ease of Use and Support
Intuitive interfaces for non-tech users and 24/7 support via chat, email, or phone.

Practical tip: Start with free trials (most offer 14-30 days) to test imports, campaigns, and support before committing.

## Top 10 Best Email Marketing Software Compared

Here's our curated list of the best email marketing software, ranked by versatility, value, and performance.

### 1. Mailchimp
Mailchimp remains a leader for its user-friendly interface and all-in-one marketing hub.

**Key Features**:
- Free plan up to 500 contacts.
- Automation, landing pages, and ads management.
- Extensive template library.

**Pricing**: Free; Essentials $13/mo (500 contacts); Standard $20/mo; Premium $350+/mo.

**Pros**: Beginner-friendly, strong integrations (500+ apps).
**Cons**: Advanced automation locked behind higher tiers; support limited on free plan.

Ideal for: Small businesses starting out.

### 2. ActiveCampaign
Best for advanced automation and CRM integration.

**Key Features**:
- Machine learning for predictive sending.
- Deep CRM with deal pipelines.
- Site tracking and conditional content.

**Pricing**: Lite $29/mo (1,000 contacts); Plus $49/mo; Professional $149/mo; Enterprise custom.

**Pros**: Powerful automations save time; excellent for e-commerce.
**Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

Practical advice: Use its split testing to optimize subject lines—increase opens by 20-30% typically.

### 3. ConvertKit (Now Kit)
Tailored for creators and bloggers.

**Key Features**:
- Visual automations.
- Tagging and subscriber scoring.
- Native landing pages and forms.

**Pricing**: Free up to 300 subscribers; Creator $29/mo; Creator Pro $49/mo.

**Pros**: Simple for newsletters; commerce tools for selling digital products.
**Cons**: Limited reporting compared to enterprise tools.

Ideal for: Content creators monetizing audiences.

### 4. GetResponse
All-in-one with webinar and e-commerce focus.

**Key Features**:
- Conversion funnels.
- Webinars for up to 1,000 attendees.
- Abandoned cart recovery.

**Pricing**: Email Marketing $19/mo (1,000 contacts); Marketing Automation $59/mo; Ecommerce $119/mo.

**Pros**: Affordable webinars; strong deliverability.
**Cons**: Interface feels dated.

Tip: Leverage funnels for lead nurturing—map customer journeys visually.

### 5. Brevo (Formerly Sendinblue)
Great for transactional + marketing emails.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited contacts on paid plans.
- SMS and chat integrations.
- Sales CRM included.

**Pricing**: Free (300 emails/day); Starter $25/mo; Business $65/mo.

**Pros**: Cost-effective for high-volume sends.
**Cons**: Automation limits on lower plans.

Practical: Combine transactional emails (order confirmations) with marketing for higher engagement.

### 6. Constant Contact
Reliable for nonprofits and small businesses.

**Key Features**:
- Event management.
- Social media tools.
- AI-powered content suggestions.

**Pricing**: Lite $12/mo (500 contacts); Standard $35/mo; Premium $80/mo.

**Pros**: Excellent customer support; easy list imports.
**Cons**: No advanced automations.

### 7. AWeber
Veteran platform with strong deliverability.

**Key Features**:
- Tag-based automation.
- Landing pages.
- E-commerce integrations.

**Pricing**: Free up to 500 subscribers; Lite $19/mo; Plus $29/mo; Unlimited custom.

**Pros**: Loyal user base; spam testing tools.
**Cons**: Design options basic.

Tip: Use A/B testing religiously—test send times for your audience's peak opens.

### 8. Klaviyo
E-commerce powerhouse.

**Key Features**:
- Revenue tracking per flow.
- Dynamic product recommendations.
- Shopify/WooCommerce sync.

**Pricing**: Free up to 250 contacts; scales with sends (e.g., $20/mo for 500).

**Pros**: ROI-focused analytics.
**Cons**: Best for stores, not general newsletters.

### 9. HubSpot Email Marketing
Free CRM-integrated option.

**Key Features**:
- Full CRM.
- Personalization tokens.
- A/B testing.

**Pricing**: Free; Marketing Hub Starter $20/mo.

**Pros**: Grows with your business.
**Cons**: Advanced features pricey.

### 10. Drip
Behavioral automation for e-commerce.

**Key Features**:
- Customer journey builder.
- Workflow analytics.

**Pricing**: Starts at $39/mo (2,500 subscribers).

**Pros**: Granular segmentation.
**Cons**: Higher cost.

## How to Choose the Best Email Marketing Software for Your Business

1. **Assess Your Needs**: Newsletters? E-commerce? Choose accordingly (e.g., Klaviyo for shops).
2. **Budget Check**: Factor contacts + sends. Calculate ROI: aim for $38 return per $1 spent (industry avg).
3. **Test Deliverability**: Use tools like Mail-Tester during trials.
4. **Scale Potential**: Ensure it handles growth without price jumps.
5. **Compliance**: Built-in unsubscribe and consent tools.

Practical advice: Import a test list, build a sample automation, and run a campaign. Track metrics like 20-30% open rates as benchmarks. Integrate with your website for pop-ups to grow lists organically.

## Conclusion

The best email marketing software depends on your goals—Mailchimp for simplicity, ActiveCampaign for power, Klaviyo for sales. All offer free trials, so experiment.
