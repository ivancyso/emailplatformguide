# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp makes sense for many users. Both platforms cater to businesses of all sizes, from solopreneurs to enterprises, but they differ in pricing, features, and scalability. This comparison dives deep into key areas to help you decide based on your specific needs.

## Pricing: Value for Money Breakdown

Pricing is often the first hurdle when selecting an email marketing platform. Both Brevo and Mailchimp offer free plans, but their structures vary significantly.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo uses a contact-based pricing model with daily email limits on lower tiers. The free plan allows 300 emails per day to unlimited contacts, making it generous for testing. Paid plans start at $25/month for 20,000 emails/month (Starter), scaling to $65/month for 20,000 emails with more features (Business). Enterprise plans are custom. Additional emails cost extra, but there's no hard contact limit, which suits growing lists.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp charges based on contacts and sends. The free Essentials plan covers up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month. Paid tiers begin at $13/month for 500 contacts (Essentials), jumping to $20+ for Standard (automation features) and higher for Premium. Costs rise quickly with audience size—e.g., 10,000 contacts on Standard can exceed $100/month. Overages apply for extra sends.

**Practical Advice:** For startups with small lists (<1,000 contacts), Brevo's free plan offers more sends. Scaling businesses may prefer Brevo's predictability over Mailchimp's steep per-contact fees. Calculate your monthly volume: if under 20k sends, Brevo saves money.

## Ease of Use and User Interface

Intuitive design speeds up campaigns, crucial for non-technical users.

Brevo's dashboard is clean and modern, with a drag-and-drop editor similar to advanced tools. Navigation is straightforward, but the learning curve steepens for complex automations.

Mailchimp excels here with its polished, beginner-friendly interface. The editor is highly visual, with pre-built templates and A/B testing built-in. It's ideal for quick setups.

**Practical Advice:** Test both free trials. Mailchimp suits visual creators; Brevo appeals to users wanting transactional + marketing emails in one place.

## Core Features Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp has 100+ responsive templates with strong customization. Brevo provides 40+ templates, focusing on clean, professional designs, plus multiproduction for multi-channel (email + SMS).

### Automation and Workflows
Mailchimp's automation is robust in Standard/Premium: customer journeys, behavioral triggers. Brevo shines with advanced workflows, including sales CRM integration and wait nodes.

### List Management and Segmentation
Mailchimp's audience dashboard offers tags, groups, and predictive segmentation. Brevo uses attributes for dynamic segments, with built-in CRM for lead scoring.

### Additional Tools
- **Landing Pages:** Mailchimp includes basic ones; Brevo offers more with forms and pop-ups.
- **SMS and Chat:** Brevo bundles SMS/WhatsApp; Mailchimp adds via integrations.
- **E-commerce:** Both support, but Brevo's transactional API is superior for receipts/abandonment.

**Practical Advice:** For e-commerce, use Brevo's API for high-volume transactional emails. Content marketers love Mailchimp's creative tools.

## Deliverability and Analytics

High deliverability ensures emails reach inboxes. Both claim 99%+ rates, but Mailchimp's legacy boosts reputation. Brevo's EU base aids GDPR compliance, with strong authentication (DKIM/SPF).

Analytics: Mailchimp provides opens, clicks, revenue tracking. Brevo adds heatmaps, link tracking, and A/B insights.

**Practical Advice:** Warm up domains gradually on either. Monitor bounce rates—aim <2%. Use Brevo for global sends; Mailchimp for US-focused lists.

## Integrations and Scalability

Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Shopify, Google Analytics). Brevo supports 300+, plus native WhatsApp and its own API for developers.

Scalability: Mailchimp handles millions but pricier. Brevo scales seamlessly without contact caps.

**Practical Advice:** Check Zapier for gaps. Developers: Brevo's API docs are developer-friendly.

## Customer Support

Mailchimp offers 24/7 email/chat for paid; free is community-only. Brevo provides priority support from Business plan, with phone/email.

**Practical Advice:** Paid users get faster help. Forums suffice for basics.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo
**Pros:** Affordable scaling, multi-channel, unlimited contacts.
**Cons:** Fewer templates, steeper automation curve.

### Mailchimp
**Pros:** User-friendly, creative tools, strong analytics.
**Cons:** Expensive at scale, send limits.

## Who Should Choose Brevo?
Small-medium businesses, e-commerce stores, international teams needing SMS/CRM. Saves 30-50% vs Mailchimp at 10k+ contacts.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?
Beginners, agencies, creative marketers prioritizing design. Best for <5k lists.

## Conclusion

Brevo edges out for cost-effective growth and versatility; Mailchimp wins for simplicity. Audit your needs: volume, channels, budget. Start with free plans—migrate data easily via CSV. Track ROI post-switch. The right tool boosts opens by 20-30% through better fit. Whichever you pick, consistent testing drives success.
