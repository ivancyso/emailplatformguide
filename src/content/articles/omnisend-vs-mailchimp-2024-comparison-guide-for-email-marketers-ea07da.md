# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Omnisend and Mailchimp can feel overwhelming when you're building your email marketing strategy. Both are powerful tools trusted by thousands of businesses, but they cater to slightly different needs. Mailchimp has long been a go-to for beginners and small businesses with its user-friendly interface and free plan. Omnisend, on the other hand, shines for ecommerce brands with advanced omnichannel features like SMS and web push notifications.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur, ecommerce store owner, or marketing team lead, this guide provides practical advice to help you decide. Let's dive in.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both platforms offer free tiers, but they scale differently based on contacts and features.

### Mailchimp Pricing

Mailchimp's structure is straightforward:
- **Free Plan**: Up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends. Includes basic automation and templates.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/month for 500 contacts (billed annually), scaling to $350+ for larger lists.
- **Standard**: From $20/month, adds advanced automation and A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350+/month for high-volume senders with priority support.

Practical tip: If you're under 500 contacts and testing the waters, start free. Watch for send limits—overages can add up quickly.

### Omnisend Pricing

Omnisend focuses on ecommerce with generous limits:
- **Free Plan**: 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, unlimited popups and SMS credits.
- **Standard**: $16/month for 500 contacts (annual billing), includes automation workflows.
- **Pro**: $59/month, unlocks advanced segmentation, A/B testing, and SMS.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for 100k+ contacts.

Omnisend bills by contacts, not sends, making it predictable for growing lists. Pro tip: Ecommerce users save with built-in SMS (starts at $0.018/message).

**Winner?** Mailchimp for tiny lists; Omnisend for ecommerce scaling without send caps.

## Features Breakdown: Head-to-Head

### Automation Workflows

Mailchimp offers single-step automations on free plans, upgrading to multi-step journeys (e.g., welcome series, abandoned cart) on Standard+. It's visual but limited without Premium.

Omnisend excels here with drag-and-drop workflows tailored for ecommerce: cart recovery, browse abandonment, back-in-stock alerts. Includes SMS/push integration out-of-the-box.

**Advice**: Use Omnisend if 70%+ of your revenue is online sales; Mailchimp suffices for newsletters.

### Email Templates and Design

Both have drag-and-drop builders. Mailchimp's library has 100+ responsive templates, strong for blogs/social. Omnisend's 250+ templates are ecommerce-optimized (product blocks, dynamic content).

Practical: Test mobile rendering—Omnisend's product feeds auto-populate from Shopify/WooCommerce.

### Audience Segmentation and Personalization

Mailchimp segments by tags, behavior, or custom fields. Advanced on Premium.

Omnisend's hyper-segmentation uses purchase history, predicted CLV, and RFM scoring—free on Pro.

**Tip**: For personalized recommendations, Omnisend pulls real product data; boosts open rates 20-30% per their benchmarks.

### Integrations and Apps

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier). Ecommerce via official Shopify/Woo apps.

Omnisend natively integrates with 140+ platforms, strongest in ecommerce (Shopify, BigCommerce, Klaviyo imports). Plus SMS gateways.

**Pro Advice**: Audit your stack—Omnisend for Shopify-heavy; Mailchimp for broad CRM.

### Analytics and Reporting

Mailchimp tracks opens, clicks, revenue per email. Ecommerce tracking on paid plans.

Omnisend's dashboard shows ROI, attributed revenue, campaign comparisons. SMS/push metrics included.

**Practical**: Export CSV for Google Sheets analysis; Omnisend's revenue tracking justifies spends.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is intuitive for non-techies—onboard in minutes. Support: Email/chat on paid; community forums free.

Omnisend's ecommerce focus means a slight learning curve for workflows, but tutorials abound. 24/7 chat/email on Pro+; knowledge base is robust.

**User Tip**: Beginners pick Mailchimp; power users Omnisend after 1-2 hours setup.

## Pros and Cons

### Omnisend Pros
- Ecommerce-first automations
- Omnichannel (email + SMS/push)
- No send limits
- Strong segmentation

**Cons**
- Free plan contact-limited
- Less ideal for non-ecom

### Mailchimp Pros
- Generous free tier
- Beginner-friendly
- Vast template library
- All-in-one (ads, landing pages)

**Cons**
- Send limits on free
- Expensive at scale
- Weaker ecommerce tracking

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Omnisend if**: Running an online store (Shopify, etc.), need SMS/push, prioritize revenue automation. Ideal for DTC brands scaling to 10k+ contacts.
- **Choose Mailchimp if**: General marketing, blogs, nonprofits. Budget under $20/month, or need free forever.

**Migration Advice**: Export lists via CSV. Test both free tiers with 100 contacts. Track 30-day metrics: opens, revenue.

Switching? Omnisend imports Mailchimp automations; Mailchimp has migration tools.

## Conclusion

Neither is 'best' universally—Omnisend edges for ecommerce growth (better ROI tracking, omnichannel), while Mailchimp wins for simplicity and free access. Assess your audience: if 50%+ ecommerce buyers, go Omnisend. For broad newsletters, stick with Mailchimp.

Start with free trials. Practical next step: Build one automation (e.g., welcome series) in each and compare setup time/results.
