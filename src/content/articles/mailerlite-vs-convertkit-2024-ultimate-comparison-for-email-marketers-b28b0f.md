# MailerLite vs ConvertKit: Which Email Platform Should You Choose?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform how you engage your audience, nurture leads, and drive revenue. MailerLite and ConvertKit are two popular platforms, each catering to slightly different needs. MailerLite shines with its affordability and beginner-friendly interface, while ConvertKit targets creators like bloggers and podcasters with powerful automation for long-term audience building.

In this in-depth comparison, we'll break down pricing, features, ease of use, integrations, support, and more. Whether you're a solopreneur starting out or scaling a content business, you'll get practical advice to decide. Data is based on official sites as of 2024.

## Pricing Breakdown: Affordability Face-Off

Pricing is often the first hurdle. Both offer free plans, but they differ significantly.

### MailerLite Pricing
MailerLite's free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month—ideal for testing without commitment. Paid plans (monthly billing):
- Starter: $9 for 500 subs, $15 for 1,000, scaling to $20 for 2,500 subs.
- Advanced: Starts at $18 for 500 subs, adding features like automation and popups.
- Enterprise: Custom for high volumes.

Annual billing saves 30%. No subscriber caps on higher plans; costs scale with list size. Practical tip: If under 1,000 subs, stick to free and upgrade seamlessly—no data migration needed.

### ConvertKit Pricing
ConvertKit's free plan covers up to 10,000 subscribers (unlimited emails for broadcasts and sequences)—a huge win for growing lists. Paid tiers:
- Creator: $15/mo (annually) for up to 300 subs, $25 for 500-1,000.
- Creator Pro: $50/mo for advanced selling features.
- Enterprise: Custom.

Monthly billing is 20% higher. ConvertKit emphasizes value for creators with unlimited sends on paid plans. Advice: Use free for audience growth via content upgrades; upgrade when needing commerce tools.

**Winner?** MailerLite for bootstrappers (cheaper entry), ConvertKit for high-volume free users.

## Ease of Use: User Interface and Onboarding

### MailerLite's Intuitive Design
Drag-and-drop editor is simple—no coding required. Dashboard is clean, with quick access to campaigns, automations, and sites. Onboarding includes video tutorials and templates. New users can send their first email in under 10 minutes. Pro tip: Use the A/B testing for subject lines right away to boost open rates by 20-30% typically.

### ConvertKit's Creator-Focused Flow
Slightly steeper curve due to tagging system (vs lists). Visual automation builder is intuitive once learned. Onboarding wizard guides subscriber import and first sequence. Best for those familiar with funnels. Advice: Start with a welcome sequence template to automate onboarding—saves hours weekly.

**Winner:** MailerLite for absolute beginners; ConvertKit for those prioritizing automations.

## Core Features Comparison

### Email Builder and Templates
Both offer responsive, drag-and-drop builders.
- **MailerLite:** 80+ templates, photo editor integration, RSS campaigns. Embed forms easily.
- **ConvertKit:** Commerce-focused templates, inline content upgrades. Simpler but powerful for newsletters.

Practical advice: MailerLite for visually rich emails; ConvertKit for text-heavy creator newsletters.

### Automation and Workflows

#### MailerLite Automations
Trigger-based: welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement. JSON triggers for advanced users. Limit on free plan.

#### ConvertKit Sequences and Automations
Standout feature: Visual builder with tags/actions. Infinite sequences, subscriber scoring. Pro plan unlocks more.

Tip: In ConvertKit, tag subscribers by behavior (e.g., 'read podcast') for hyper-personalized flows—increases conversions 15-25%.

### Segmentation and Tagging
- **MailerLite:** Groups, segments by behavior/custom fields. Simple clicks to build.
- **ConvertKit:** Tag-based (e.g., 'lead-magnet-downloader'). More flexible for creators avoiding list fatigue.

Advice: ConvertKit's tags reduce unsubscribe rates by treating subscribers as individuals.

### Landing Pages and Forms
- **MailerLite:** Unlimited popups, forms, landing pages (paid). Website builder included.
- **ConvertKit:** Forms, landing pages, checkout pages. Strong for digital products.

Pro tip: Use MailerLite popups for exit-intent to capture 5-10% more leads.

### Analytics and Reporting
Both track opens, clicks, revenue. MailerLite adds heatmaps; ConvertKit reports per sequence revenue. Advice: Review revenue per subscriber monthly to refine strategies.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

MailerLite: 100+ native (WordPress, Shopify, Zapier). Easy API.
ConvertKit: Deep with Teachable, Gumroad, podcast hosts. Zapier-heavy.

Practical: If on Shopify, both work; ConvertKit edges for Patreon/Kajabi creators.

## Customer Support and Resources

- **MailerLite:** 24/7 live chat, email, knowledge base, academy courses.
- **ConvertKit:** Email support (priority on Pro), extensive creator resources, podcast.

Winner: MailerLite for instant help.

## Pros and Cons

### MailerLite
**Pros:** Cheap/free, all-in-one (sites/popups), fast support.
**Cons:** Automation limits on free, less creator-specific.

### ConvertKit
**Pros:** Unlimited sends, tagging power, creator tools.
**Cons:** Pricier for small lists, no popups.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Pick MailerLite if:** Small business, e-commerce, need popups/websites, budget-tight. Great for beginners scaling to 10k subs.
- **Pick ConvertKit if:** Blogger/podcaster/creator, focus on sequences/digital sales, okay with $15+ monthly.

Test both free plans: Import 100 subs, build a sequence, send a test campaign. Track setup time and deliverability (both high: 99%+).

## Conclusion

MailerLite vs ConvertKit boils down to your stage and style. MailerLite offers unbeatable value and simplicity for most users, especially under 5k subs. ConvertKit excels for serious creators investing in lifetime value through automations.

No one-size-fits-all—start free on both, migrate if needed (both export CSV). Whichever you choose, consistent value-driven emails yield 40x ROI. Ready to grow? Sign up today and focus on content, not tech.
