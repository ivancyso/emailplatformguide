# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: The Ultimate Comparison Guide for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's customer engagement and revenue. With **Omnisend vs Mailchimp** debates heating up, especially for ecommerce stores, this guide breaks down everything you need to know. We'll compare pricing, features, ease of use, and more to help you decide which tool aligns with your goals.

Whether you're a small business owner scaling up or an established store optimizing workflows, understanding these platforms' strengths is key. Both offer robust email capabilities, but Omnisend shines in omnichannel ecommerce, while Mailchimp remains a beginner-friendly staple. Let's dive in.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, launched in 2001, is one of the most recognized names in email marketing. It serves over 12 million users worldwide and powers campaigns for brands from solopreneurs to enterprises like Spotify and Nike.

### Key Strengths
- **User-Friendly Interface**: Drag-and-drop editor makes designing emails intuitive, even for non-designers.
- **Free Plan Availability**: Ideal for startups with up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends.
- **All-in-One Marketing Hub**: Includes landing pages, ads, and social media tools.

Mailchimp's audience dashboard segments contacts based on behavior, opens, and clicks. Practical tip: Use the "Groups" feature to tag subscribers by purchase history—start by importing your CSV list and applying tags during signup forms.

However, advanced ecommerce automations require higher tiers, and SMS integration is limited without add-ons.

## Overview of Omnisend

Omnisend, founded in 2014, targets ecommerce specifically. It supports over 70,000 stores, integrating seamlessly with Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce. Its omnichannel approach combines email, SMS, and web push notifications.

### Key Strengths
- **Ecommerce-Focused Automations**: Pre-built workflows for abandoned carts, welcome series, and post-purchase upsells.
- **Free Plan for Ecommerce**: Up to 250 contacts and 500 emails/month, with full popup and automation access.
- **Visual Workflow Builder**: Drag-and-drop automation maps rival complex tools.

Practical advice: Connect Omnisend to your store in under 5 minutes via Shopify app. Set up an abandoned cart flow by selecting the trigger (cart abandonment), adding a delay (1 hour), and inserting dynamic product blocks that pull real cart data.

Omnisend's strength lies in revenue recovery—users report 20-30% cart recovery rates with optimized flows, based on platform case studies.

## Pricing Comparison: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a major factor in the **Omnisend vs Mailchimp** showdown. Both scale with contacts, but structures differ.

### Mailchimp Pricing (as of 2024)
| Plan | Monthly Price (500 contacts) | Key Features |
|------|--------------------------------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | Basic emails, 1,000 sends/mo |
| Essentials | $13 | Autoresponders, A/B testing |
| Standard | $20 | Automation sequences, custom landing pages |
| Premium | $350+ | Advanced reporting, unlimited seats |

Overages apply for extra contacts/emails. Annual billing saves 15-20%.

### Omnisend Pricing (as of 2024)
| Plan | Monthly Price (500 contacts) | Key Features |
|------|--------------------------------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | 500 emails/mo, full automations, SMS credits |
| Standard | $16 | 6,000 emails/mo, unlimited popups |
| Pro | $59 | 60,000 emails/mo, advanced segmentation |
| Enterprise | Custom | Priority support, high-volume sends |

**Practical Advice**: Calculate costs using their pricing calculators. For a 1,000-contact list sending weekly newsletters, Mailchimp Essentials (~$20/mo) edges out Omnisend Standard (~$32/mo for equivalent volume). But Omnisend's free tier includes more ecommerce tools.

Winner: Omnisend for low-volume ecommerce; Mailchimp for general marketing under 5,000 contacts.

## Features Head-to-Head: Omnisend vs Mailchimp

### Ease of Use and Templates
Both offer 100+ responsive templates. Mailchimp's editor feels more playful with content blocks; Omnisend emphasizes product-focused designs.

**Tip**: Test templates by duplicating campaigns. In Mailchimp, merge tags like *|FNAME|* personalize easily. Omnisend auto-pulls product images via integrations.

### Automation and Workflows
- **Mailchimp**: Single-step automations on Standard plan; journeys on Premium.
- **Omnisend**: Unlimited multi-step workflows on all paid plans, including SMS branching.

Practical setup: In Omnisend, build a win-back flow—trigger inactive 90 days > send email > if no open, SMS > if click, tag as re-engaged.

### Integrations and Ecommerce Tools
Mailchimp integrates with 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook). Omnisend excels with 140+ ecommerce apps, plus built-in popups/forms for list growth.

**Pro Tip**: Use Omnisend's list-building popups with exit-intent triggers to capture 10-15% more emails. Mailchimp requires third-party tools like OptinMonster.

### Analytics and Reporting
Both track opens, clicks, revenue. Omnisend's dashboard highlights attributed revenue per campaign; Mailchimp focuses on engagement metrics.

### Deliverability and Support
Mailchimp boasts 99%+ deliverability; Omnisend similar with ecommerce IP pools. Support: Mailchimp chat/email; Omnisend 24/7 live chat on Pro+.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp** if: You're a blogger, service business, or beginner needing simple emails. Practical for nonprofits or B2B with its free tier and ad integrations.
- **Choose Omnisend** if: You run an online store (Shopify/Woo). Its automations recover lost sales—set up cart abandonment to boost revenue by 10-20% immediately.

**Migration Tip**: Export CSV from one, import to the other. Test with a small segment first to verify tags and automations.

## Conclusion

In the **Omnisend vs Mailchimp** battle, there's no one-size-fits-all winner—it depends on your business. Mailchimp suits versatile, low-stakes marketing with its polish and free access. Omnisend dominates for ecommerce with targeted automations and omnichannel power, often delivering higher ROI through recovery flows.

Assess your needs: Prioritize ecommerce? Go Omnisend. Need broad tools cheaply? Mailchimp. Start with free trials—both offer 14-30 days. Track metrics like open rates (aim 20-30%) and revenue per email post-switch.
