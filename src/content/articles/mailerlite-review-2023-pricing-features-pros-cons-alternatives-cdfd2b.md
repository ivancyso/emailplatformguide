# MailerLite Review 2023: Pricing, Features, Pros, Cons & Alternatives

If you're a small business owner, freelancer, or blogger looking for an email marketing platform that's easy to use and won't break the bank, MailerLite might be on your radar. In this in-depth MailerLite review, we'll dive into its features, pricing, ease of use, customer support, and whether it's the right choice for your needs. With a focus on simplicity and affordability, MailerLite has gained popularity among users who want powerful tools without complexity.

MailerLite positions itself as a drag-and-drop email builder with automation capabilities, landing pages, and popups—all starting with a generous free plan. But does it live up to the hype? Let's break it down step by step.

## MailerLite Pricing: Transparent and Affordable

One of MailerLite's biggest draws is its straightforward pricing. Unlike some competitors with hidden fees or complex tiers, MailerLite keeps it simple.

### Free Plan
- Up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month.
- Includes core features like email campaigns, forms, and basic automation.
- No credit card required—perfect for testing.

### Paid Plans (as of 2023)

| Plan | Subscribers | Emails/Month | Price (Monthly) | Price (Annual) |
|------|-------------|--------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Growing Business | Up to 2,500 | Unlimited | $9 | $7.50 |
| Advanced | Up to 5,000 | Unlimited | $18 | $15 |
| Enterprise | 500,000+ | Unlimited | Custom | Custom |

**Practical Advice:** Start with the free plan to import your list and send a few campaigns. Upgrade only when you hit limits. Annual billing saves 20%, so commit if you're sure. Watch for add-ons like premium templates ($10/month extra).

No contracts, cancel anytime. Compared to Mailchimp (free up to 500 subs) or ConvertKit (starts at $15), MailerLite offers better value for growing lists.

## Key Features of MailerLite

MailerLite packs a punch for its price. Here's what you get:

### Email Campaigns and Builder
- Drag-and-drop editor with 80+ responsive templates.
- RSS-to-email campaigns for bloggers.
- A/B testing for subjects, content, and send times.

**Tip:** Use the editor's plain-text mode for higher deliverability. Always preview on mobile—90% of emails are opened there.

### Automation Workflows
- Visual builder for welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement.

#### Example Workflow Setup:
1. Trigger: Subscriber joins list via popup.
2. Action: Send welcome email Day 1.
3. Wait 3 days, send value-packed content.
4. If no open, trigger re-engagement.

**Practical Advice:** Automate your nurture sequence first. Track open rates; aim for 20-30% benchmarks. MailerLite's triggers include clicks, purchases (via integrations), and custom JSON events.

### Landing Pages and Popups
- Unlimited landing pages with drag-and-drop.
- Popups, fixed bars, and embedded forms (exit-intent, scroll-triggered).

**Pro Tip:** A/B test popup copy. "Get 20% off" converts better than generic signups. Embed forms on WordPress via plugin.

### Websites and E-commerce
- Build simple websites or one-page sites.
- Stripe/PayPal integration for payments.

### Analytics and Segmentation
- Detailed reports: opens, clicks, bounces.
- Tag-based segmentation (e.g., "VIP customers").

**Advice:** Segment by engagement. Send personalized broadcasts to "active" vs. "inactive" lists to boost opens by 15-20%.

### Integrations
- 100+ including Shopify, WooCommerce, WordPress, Zapier, Google Analytics.

MailerLite lacks native CRM like ActiveCampaign but shines in basics.
