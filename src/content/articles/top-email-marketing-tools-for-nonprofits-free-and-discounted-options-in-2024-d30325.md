# Top Email Marketing Tools for Nonprofits: Free and Discounted Options in 2024

Email marketing remains one of the most cost-effective ways for nonprofits to connect with donors, volunteers, and supporters. With tight budgets, nonprofits need platforms that offer robust features without breaking the bank. Many popular tools provide free plans, generous discounts for verified 501(c)(3) organizations, or scaled pricing that fits small teams. This roundup focuses on the top options tailored for nonprofit needs like donor segmentation, compliance, and integration with fundraising CRMs.

Whether you're sending newsletters, campaign updates, or thank-you notes, the right tool can increase open rates, donations, and retention. We'll cover key features, platform breakdowns, a comparison, and practical advice to help you choose.

## Why Email Marketing is Essential for Nonprofits

Nonprofits rely on email to nurture relationships and drive action. According to benchmarks from platforms like Mailchimp, nonprofit emails achieve average open rates of 25-30%, higher than commercial averages due to genuine audience interest.

Key benefits include:
- **Personalization**: Address donors by name and tailor content based on giving history.
- **Automation**: Welcome series for new subscribers or lapsed donor re-engagement.
- **Measurable ROI**: Track opens, clicks, and conversions to donations.
- **Low Cost**: Far cheaper than direct mail, with potential returns of $42 per $1 spent (DMA data).

In 2024, with rising data privacy laws like CCPA and GDPR, compliance tools are non-negotiable.

## Key Features to Prioritize in Nonprofit Email Tools

When evaluating platforms, look for:

### Donor Segmentation and List Management
Divide lists by donor level (major vs. one-time), location, or engagement. This ensures relevant messaging, like major donor events vs. volunteer opportunities.

### Automation Workflows
Set up drip campaigns: post-donation thank-yous, monthly newsletters, or year-end appeals.

### Integrations
Connect with CRMs like Salesforce Nonprofit Cloud, Bloomerang, or DonorPerfect for seamless data sync.

### Nonprofit-Specific Perks
Free sends up to certain volumes, discounted rates (10-50% off), or templates for fundraising.

### Deliverability and Compliance
High inbox placement, easy unsubscribe, CAN-SPAM compliance, and A/B testing.

### Reporting
Metrics on revenue per email, donor lifetime value, and campaign performance.

## Top 7 Email Marketing Platforms for Nonprofits

Here are the leading tools, selected for affordability, features, and nonprofit suitability. All offer free trials or tiers.

### 1. Mailchimp

Mailchimp is a nonprofit favorite with its user-friendly drag-and-drop editor and nonprofit program. Verified 501(c)(3)s get up to 20% off monthly plans or free access for small lists.

**Key Features**:
- Free plan: 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month.
- Automation for donor journeys.
- Integrations: 300+, including Classy and EveryAction.
- Templates: 100+ nonprofit-specific.

**Pricing for Nonprofits**: Starts free; Essentials at $13/month discounted.

**Pros**: Intuitive, excellent analytics. **Cons**: Automation limited on free plan.

**Practical Advice**: Use behavioral segmentation to target lapsed donors with personalized appeals, boosting reactivation by 15-20% per industry stats.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

Brevo shines with unlimited contacts on paid plans and a robust free tier—300 emails/day.

**Key Features**:
- Advanced automation and transactional emails.
- SMS integration for urgent appeals.
- CRM built-in for donor tracking.

**Pricing for Nonprofits**: Free forever; Starter at $25/month (25k emails).

**Pros**: No contact limits, high deliverability. **Cons**: Steeper learning curve.

**Practical Advice**: Automate post-event follow-ups with SMS/email combos to increase attendance for future events by 25%.

### 3. Constant Contact

Tailored for small nonprofits with event management and donation forms.

**Key Features**:
- 10-30% nonprofit discounts.
- Mobile-optimized templates.
- Social media ads integration.

**Pricing for Nonprofits**: Lite $12/month discounted (500 contacts).

**Pros**: Easy signup forms. **Cons**: No free plan beyond trial.

**Practical Advice**: Embed donation links in every email footer and A/B test subject lines like "Your Gift Doubled" vs. "Support Our Cause" for 10-15% lift.

### 4. ActiveCampaign

Powerhouse for automation, ideal for mid-sized nonprofits.

**Key Features**:
- Site tracking for personalized emails.
- 250+ integrations.
- Machine learning for send times.

**Pricing for Nonprofits**: Lite $29/month (request discount).

**Pros**: Deep personalization. **Cons**: Overkill for beginners.

**Practical Advice**: Build "if/then" automations, e.g., if donor gives $100+, trigger VIP update series.

### 5. HubSpot Email Marketing

Free with its CRM, perfect for growing nonprofits.

**Key Features**:
- Unlimited users/emails on free plan (2k sends/month).
- Smart content personalization.
- Full CRM integration.

**Pricing for Nonprofits**: Free; Marketing Hub from $20/month.

**Pros**: All-in-one. **Cons**: Limited free sends.

**Practical Advice**: Use free CRM to score leads and nurture high-potential donors automatically.

### 6. ConvertKit

Great for storytelling newsletters.

**Key Features**:
- Creator-focused with tagging.
- Landing pages for petitions.

**Pricing for Nonprofits**: Free up to 300 subscribers; $15/month after.

**Pros**: Simple. **Cons**: Basic reporting.

**Practical Advice**: Tag subscribers by interest (e.g., education, environment) for hyper-targeted sends.

### 7. Moosend

Affordable with unlimited emails.

**Key Features**:
- AI optimization.
- Ecommerce for merchandise.

**Pricing for Nonprofits**: Free up to 1k subscribers; Pro $9/month.

**Pros**: Value-packed. **Cons**: Newer player.

**Practical Advice**: Leverage pop-ups on your site to grow lists ethically.

## How to Choose the Right Email Marketing Tool for Your Nonprofit

1. **Assess Your List Size**: Under 1k? Stick to free tiers.
2. **Budget Check**: Apply for discounts via TechSoup or direct verification.
3. **Test Integrations**: Ensure CRM compatibility.
4. **Trial Run**: Send test campaigns to 100 contacts.
5. **Scalability**: Plan for growth to 10k+ lists.

## Practical Implementation Tips

- **Build Your List Legally**: Use double opt-in and clear privacy policies.
- **Content Strategy**: 80/20 rule—80% value (stories, impact), 20% asks.
- **Timing**: Send Tuesdays-Thursdays, 8-10 AM donor timezone.
- **Mobile First**: 60%+ opens on mobile.
- **A/B Test Everything**: Subjects, CTAs, images.
- **Clean Lists**: Remove bounces quarterly to protect sender reputation.
- **Measure Success**: Track donation revenue, not just opens.

Start small: One newsletter/month, automate welcomes, analyze results.

## Conclusion

For nonprofits, email marketing tools like Mailchimp, Brevo, and HubSpot offer the best balance of features, affordability, and ease. Prioritize platforms with nonprofit discounts and strong automation to maximize donor engagement without straining resources. Begin with a free trial, integrate with your CRM, and focus on value-driven content. With consistent effort, expect 20-50% improvements in fundraising metrics. Choose based on your scale—small teams love Mailchimp, growing orgs need ActiveCampaign. Verify eligibility for discounts today and start building meaningful connections.
