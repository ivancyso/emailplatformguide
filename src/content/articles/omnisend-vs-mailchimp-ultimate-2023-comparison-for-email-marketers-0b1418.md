# Omnisend vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2023?

Choosing the right email marketing tool can transform your business's communication strategy. With thousands of subscribers relying on consistent, targeted emails, platforms like Omnisend and Mailchimp stand out. Both offer robust features for automation, segmentation, and analytics, but they cater to slightly different needs. Omnisend excels in ecommerce with multichannel support including SMS, while Mailchimp is a versatile all-in-one marketing hub.

This in-depth comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide. Whether you're a small business owner scaling up or an ecommerce store optimizing conversions, we'll provide practical advice based on real user experiences and official platform details.

## Quick Overview: Omnisend and Mailchimp

### What is Mailchimp?
Mailchimp, launched in 2001, is one of the most recognized names in email marketing. It serves over 12 million users worldwide, offering email campaigns, automations, landing pages, and social media ads. Its free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly sends (recently updated), making it accessible for beginners. Mailchimp shines for general marketing needs, content creators, and small teams needing an intuitive drag-and-drop editor.

### What is Omnisend?
Omnisend, founded in 2014, focuses on ecommerce brands. It integrates seamlessly with platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and WooCommerce. Key strengths include advanced automation workflows, popups for list building, and omnichannel messaging (email + SMS + push). The free plan allows 250 contacts and 500 emails per month, ideal for testing ecommerce flows without upfront costs.

## Pricing Comparison: Value for Money

Pricing is a top concern when selecting an email tool. Both platforms use tiered plans based on contacts/subscribers, but Omnisend often proves more cost-effective for ecommerce users due to higher deliverability and built-in features.

### Mailchimp Pricing (as of 2023)
- **Free**: Up to 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month, basic features.
- **Essentials**: Starts at $13/month (500 contacts), unlimited sends, but no advanced automation.
- **Standard**: $20/month (500 contacts), includes automations and A/B testing.
- **Premium**: $350+/month (10,000+ contacts), full features like multivariate testing.

Mailchimp charges per 500 contacts billed monthly, with overages for sends. It's pricier for larger lists—e.g., 10,000 contacts on Premium costs around $350/month.

### Omnisend Pricing (as of 2023)
- **Free**: 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, full access to core features.
- **Standard**: $16/month (500 contacts), unlimited emails, SMS add-on.
- **Pro**: $59/month (500 contacts), advanced segmentation, A/B testing.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing for high-volume needs.

Omnisend bills per 500 contacts too, but unlimited emails on paid plans reduce costs. For 10,000 contacts, Pro is about $400/month but includes SMS credits and deeper ecommerce integrations.

**Practical Tip**: Calculate your monthly sends and contacts. Use Mailchimp's pricing calculator or Omnisend's for accuracy. For ecommerce with high-volume abandoned cart emails, Omnisend saves money long-term.

## Feature Face-Off: Core Capabilities

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp's drag-and-drop editor is beginner-friendly with 100+ responsive templates. Customization is straightforward, but advanced coding requires HTML knowledge.

Omnisend offers a similar editor but with ecommerce-specific blocks like product carousels and dynamic content. It pulls real-time data from your store, making personalized emails easier.

**Advice**: Test templates with your branding. Omnisend wins for dynamic product feeds.

### Automation Workflows
Mailchimp's Standard plan unlocks customer journeys, tag-based triggers, and behavioral automations. It's solid for welcome series or re-engagement.

Omnisend's automations are ecommerce powerhouses: abandoned cart (up to 3 emails), back-in-stock, cross-sell. Pre-built workflows save hours, and SMS integration boosts recovery rates by 20-30% (based on Omnisend case studies).

**Practical Advice**: Map your customer journey first. Start with Omnisend's 250+ templates if selling online; use Mailchimp for nurture sequences in services.

### Segmentation and Personalization
Both segment by behavior, location, and tags. Mailchimp uses merge tags; Omnisend leverages ecommerce data for hyper-personalization (e.g., 'viewed products').

Omnisend edges out with RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis out-of-the-box.

### Integrations and Apps
Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps (Google Analytics, Facebook, Zapier).
Omnisend focuses on 140+ ecommerce (Shopify, Klaviyo alternatives) plus general tools.

**Tip**: Check your stack. Shopify users: Omnisend installs in minutes.

### Analytics and Reporting
Mailchimp provides opens, clicks, revenue tracking. Omnisend adds ecommerce ROI, SMS performance, and popup analytics.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is polished for non-techies, with tutorials everywhere. Support is email/ticket-based; paid plans get priority chat.

Omnisend's dashboard is clean, ecommerce-oriented. 24/7 live chat on paid plans, extensive knowledge base, and migration services.

**User Feedback**: G2 rates Mailchimp 4.4/5 (ease of use), Omnisend 4.6/5. Beginners prefer Mailchimp; scaling ecommerce loves Omnisend.

**Advice**: Sign up for free trials. Import a test list to simulate your workflow.

## Who Should Choose Omnisend vs Mailchimp?

- **Choose Mailchimp if**: You're a blogger, consultant, or small biz with under 5,000 contacts needing simple emails and social tools. Great for nonprofits or events.

- **Choose Omnisend if**: Running an online store (Shopify/Woo). High cart abandonment? Omnisend's workflows recover lost sales effectively.

**Practical Decision Framework**:
1. List your must-haves (e.g., SMS? Ecom sync?).
2. Estimate contacts/sends.
3. Trial both for 14 days.
4. Review analytics post-test campaign.

## Migrating Between Platforms

Switching? Export CSV from one, import to the other. Mailchimp to Omnisend: Use their migration tool for lists and suppressions. Omnisend to Mailchimp: Manual but straightforward.

**Steps**:
1. Backup lists/tags.
2. Recreate automations.
3. Update signup forms/popups.
4. Test sends.

Omnisend offers free migration help on Pro+ plans.

## Conclusion

Omnisend vs Mailchimp boils down to your business type. Mailchimp is the safe, versatile choice for general marketing with its established ecosystem. Omnisend dominates for ecommerce, delivering higher engagement through targeted automations and multichannel tactics.

For most online stores, Omnisend provides better value and results—especially if revenue tracking matters. Start with free plans to test. Whichever you pick, focus on list hygiene, compliant practices (GDPR/CAN-SPAM), and A/B testing for 20-40% open rate improvements.
