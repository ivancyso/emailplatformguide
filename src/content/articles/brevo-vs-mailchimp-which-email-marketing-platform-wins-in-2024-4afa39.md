# Brevo vs Mailchimp: In-Depth Comparison for 2024

Choosing the right email marketing platform can transform your business's communication strategy. With so many options, pitting Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) against Mailchimp—the industry giant—helps narrow it down. This Brevo vs Mailchimp guide targets businesses deciding between affordability and robust features. We'll break down pricing, ease of use, automation, integrations, and more, based on real user experiences and official specs as of 2024.

Whether you're a solopreneur starting with a free plan or a growing team needing advanced segmentation, read on for practical insights to make an informed choice.

## Overview of Brevo

Brevo is an all-in-one CRM platform emphasizing transactional emails, marketing automation, SMS, and chat. It's popular among e-commerce stores and SMBs for its generous free tier: unlimited contacts with 300 emails per day. Paid plans start at $25/month for 20,000 emails.

Key strengths include:
- Multi-channel support (email, SMS, WhatsApp).
- Advanced automation workflows.
- Built-in sales CRM.

Brevo shines for users wanting scalability without breaking the bank. It's EU-based, prioritizing GDPR compliance, which appeals to privacy-focused businesses.

## Overview of Mailchimp

Mailchimp, launched in 2001, is synonymous with email marketing. It offers drag-and-drop editors, audience insights, and e-commerce integrations. The free plan covers up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month, but features taper off quickly.

Paid Essentials plan starts at $13/month for 500 contacts. It's ideal for beginners with its intuitive interface and vast template library.

Strengths:
- Excellent analytics and A/B testing.
- Creative Studio for landing pages and ads.
- Huge ecosystem of third-party apps.

However, pricing scales steeply with audience size.

## Pricing Comparison: Brevo vs Mailchimp

Pricing is a top decider in Brevo vs Mailchimp debates. Here's a practical breakdown:

### Brevo Pricing
- **Free**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day.
- **Starter**: $25/month (20k emails, removes Brevo branding).
- **Business**: $65/month (unlimited automation, SMS).
- **Enterprise**: Custom (dedicated IP, SSO).

Brevo bills by emails sent, not contacts, making it cost-effective for large lists with infrequent sends.

### Mailchimp Pricing
- **Free**: 500 contacts, 1k sends/month.
- **Essentials**: $13/month (500 contacts, basic automation).
- **Standard**: $20/month (5k contacts, dynamic content).
- **Premium**: $350+/month (advanced segmentation).

Mailchimp charges per contacts, so costs rise fast—e.g., 10k contacts on Standard is $110/month.

**Practical Advice**: For <5k contacts and high-volume sends, Brevo saves money (e.g., $25 vs Mailchimp's $75+). Test free tiers: Brevo for daily emails, Mailchimp for occasional campaigns.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp edges out with 100+ customizable templates and a superior drag-and-drop editor. Brevo's builder is functional but less polished—great for simple newsletters.

**Tip**: Use Mailchimp for visually stunning campaigns; Brevo for transactional receipts.

### Automation and Workflows
Brevo excels here with no-contact limits on automations (e.g., abandoned cart series). Mailchimp requires Standard plan ($20+) for branching logic.

**Practical Workflow Example**:
1. Trigger: New subscriber.
2. Brevo: Send welcome + tag for nurture sequence (free).
3. Mailchimp: Basic welcome only on free; full drip on paid.

### Segmentation and Analytics
Both offer list segmentation. Mailchimp's behavioral tracking (opens, clicks) is intuitive. Brevo adds real-time reporting and heatmaps.

Brevo integrates SMS for higher engagement (open rates ~98% vs email's 20-30%).

### Integrations
Mailchimp: 300+ (Shopify, WordPress, Google Analytics).
Brevo: 300+ including Zapier, but stronger native e-com (WooCommerce).

**Advice**: If you're on Shopify, both work seamlessly. For custom CRMs, Brevo's API is developer-friendly.

## Ease of Use: Brevo vs Mailchimp

Mailchimp wins for absolute beginners—its interface feels like Canva. Onboarding wizards guide list imports and first campaigns.

Brevo has a steeper curve due to its CRM dashboard but pays off with multi-channel views. Mobile apps are solid for both.

**User Tip**: Start with Mailchimp if non-technical; migrate to Brevo as needs grow.

## Customer Support and Scalability

Mailchimp: Email/ticket support (priority on higher plans), extensive knowledge base, 24/7 chat on Premium.
Brevo: 24/7 live chat/email from Starter, phone for Business+. Faster responses reported.

For scalability, Brevo handles millions of emails with deliverability >99% (via dedicated IPs). Mailchimp suits <50k contacts best.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo
**Pros**:

- Unlimited contacts free.

**Cons**:
- Editor less intuitive.
- Fewer templates.

### Mailchimp
**Pros**:
- Beginner-friendly.
- Superior design tools.
- Robust analytics.

**Cons**:
- Expensive scaling.
- Free plan limits.

## Who Should Choose Brevo? Who Mailchimp?

**Pick Brevo if**:
- Budget-conscious with growing lists.
- Need SMS or transactional emails.
- E-commerce focused (automation saves time).

**Pick Mailchimp if**:
- Prioritize design and ease.
- Small audience (<5k).
- Want all-in-one content tools.

**Migration Tip**: Export CSV from one, import to other. Test parallel campaigns for 1-2 months.

In the Brevo vs Mailchimp showdown, there's no one-size-fits-all. Brevo offers better value for scaling businesses (especially >5k contacts), while Mailchimp delights with user-friendliness and creativity. Start with free trials—Brevo for cost-savers, Mailchimp for quick wins.
