# Brevo vs Mailchimp: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins in 2024?

Choosing between Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and Mailchimp can be tough for businesses scaling their email marketing. Both are popular platforms with robust features, but they cater to slightly different needs. Brevo excels in affordability and multichannel marketing, while Mailchimp shines in user-friendly design and creative tools. This comparison breaks down pricing, features, usability, and more to help you decide.

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is often the first factor when selecting an email marketing tool. Both offer free plans, but their structures differ significantly.

### Brevo Pricing
Brevo uses a contact-based pricing model with credits for emails, SMS, and more. The free plan allows unlimited contacts but limits you to 300 emails per day. Paid plans start at $25/month for 20,000 emails/month (Starter plan), scaling up to Enterprise at custom pricing. This is ideal for high-volume senders on a budget, as you only pay for what you use.

Practical advice: If your list grows beyond 500 subscribers, Brevo's free tier remains viable longer than Mailchimp's, but monitor daily limits to avoid disruptions.

### Mailchimp Pricing
Mailchimp charges based on contacts and features. The free Essentials plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 sends/month, with basic features. Paid tiers begin at $13/month for 500 contacts (Essentials), up to Premium at $350+/month for 200,000+ contacts. Automation and advanced segmentation require higher plans.

Practical advice: Mailchimp's pricing jumps quickly with list growth. Calculate your monthly sends and contacts using their pricing calculator to avoid surprises.

**Verdict**: Brevo is more cost-effective for growing lists and transactional emails; Mailchimp suits small teams with low-volume creative campaigns.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Email Builder and Templates
Mailchimp offers a drag-and-drop builder with hundreds of customizable templates, perfect for visually appealing newsletters. It's beginner-friendly with mobile previews.

Brevo's builder is also intuitive but leans toward simplicity, with responsive templates optimized for e-commerce. It includes dynamic content blocks for personalization.

Practical advice: Test both builders with a sample campaign. Mailchimp for design-heavy emails; Brevo for quick, conversion-focused setups.

### Automation and Workflows
Both platforms support automations, but Mailchimp's journey builder allows complex customer journeys with A/B testing at scale. Brevo offers event-based automations, including abandoned cart recovery, integrated with SMS/WhatsApp.

Practical advice: For e-commerce, set up Brevo's cart abandonment flows—they trigger faster. Use Mailchimp for nurture sequences in B2B.

### Segmentation and Analytics
Mailchimp provides behavioral segmentation (e.g., opened emails, clicked links) and detailed reports on revenue per email.

Brevo shines in real-time analytics and predictive segmentation based on engagement.

Practical advice: Export data to Google Analytics from both. Brevo's heatmaps help refine subject lines effectively.

### Integrations and Multichannel
Brevo integrates seamlessly with e-commerce (Shopify, WooCommerce) and offers SMS, chat, and ads in one dashboard.

Mailchimp connects to 300+ apps via Zapier/Make, strong in CRM (Salesforce) and social media.

Practical advice: If you use SMS for promotions, Brevo saves time. Mailchimp pairs best with content tools like Canva.

## Ease of Use and Customer Support

Mailchimp's interface is polished and intuitive, ideal for non-marketers. Onboarding includes tutorials.

Brevo is straightforward but has a slight learning curve for advanced multichannel features. Both have 24/7 chat/email support; Mailchimp adds phone for higher plans.

Practical advice: Start with free trials. Migrate lists using built-in importers—Brevo handles CSV uploads smoothly.

## Pros and Cons

### Brevo Pros:
- Affordable scaling
- Multichannel (email + SMS + chat)
- Unlimited contacts on free plan
- Strong transactional emails

### Brevo Cons:
- Daily email limits on free
- Fewer design templates

### Mailchimp Pros:
- Excellent templates and builder
- Robust analytics
- Large template library
- Beginner-friendly

### Mailchimp Cons:
- Expensive for large lists
- Limited free plan
- Less multichannel focus

## Who Should Choose Brevo?

- E-commerce stores needing SMS/cart recovery
- Budget-conscious teams with growing lists
- Businesses sending transactional + marketing emails

Practical tip: Use Brevo if your open rates are high but costs are rising elsewhere.

## Who Should Choose Mailchimp?

- Creative agencies or bloggers
- Small businesses prioritizing design
- Teams needing advanced reporting

Practical tip: Stick with Mailchimp if your audience loves visually rich content.

## Migration and Getting Started

Switching platforms? Both offer import tools. Export from one, clean your list (remove bounces), and import. Test sends to verify deliverability.

Practical advice: Use Brevo's API for seamless data sync if tech-savvy. Mailchimp's templates ease the transition for designers.

## Conclusion

Brevo vs Mailchimp boils down to your priorities: Brevo for cost-efficiency and multichannel reach, Mailchimp for creative ease and analytics depth. For most small-to-medium businesses, start with free trials—send a test campaign to 100 contacts and track engagement. If scaling affordably with SMS, go Brevo. For polished newsletters, choose Mailchimp.

Ultimately, the best platform aligns with your workflow. Reassess every 6 months as features evolve. Whichever you pick, focus on list hygiene, compliant practices (GDPR/CAN-SPAM), and value-driven content for ROI.
